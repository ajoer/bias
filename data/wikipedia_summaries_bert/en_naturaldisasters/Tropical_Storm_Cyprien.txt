Severe Tropical Storm Cyprien (JTWC designation: 08S) was a short-lived tropical cyclone that caused minor damage in Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on December 30, 2001, Cyprien quickly strengthened in a slightly favorable environment. [CLS] [SEP] By January 2, the storm attained its peak intensity with winds of 100 km/h (65 mph) according to Météo-France and 95 km/h (60 mph) according to the Joint Typhoon Warning Center. [CLS] [SEP] Later that day the storm made landfall in Morombe, at a weaker intensity, and dissipated early on January 3. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the affected areas, Cyprien destroyed 957 structures, leaving 1.16 billion Malagasy franc (2002 MGF; US$181,000) in damages. [CLS] [SEP] No fatalities were reported; however two people were listed as missing. [CLS] [SEP]
