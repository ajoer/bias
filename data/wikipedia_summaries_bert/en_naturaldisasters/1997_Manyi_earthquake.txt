The 1997 Manyi earthquake (Chinese: 玛尼地震) occurred on November 8 at 10:02 UTC. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was in Nagqu Prefecture in northern Tibet, China. [CLS] [SEP] The focal mechanism indicates a lift-lateral strike-slip movement. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake had a surface rupture of 17 km (11 mi) long with up to 7 m (23 ft) of left-lateral slip along the Manyi fault, a westward continuation of the Kunlun fault, offset about 100 km (62 mi) to the south. [CLS] [SEP] Normally, the continental crust is about 35 km (22 mi) thick, but it reaches 70 km (43 mi) thick under the Tibetan Plateau. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake ruptured up to 20 km (12 mi) of the top part of the local continental crust. [CLS] [SEP]
