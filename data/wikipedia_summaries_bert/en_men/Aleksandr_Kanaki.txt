Aleksandr Spiridonovich Kanaki (Russian: Александр Спиридонович Канаки, 29 March 1912 – 1995) was a Russian athlete. [CLS] [SEP] He initially competed in decathlon and placed third at the 1936 Soviet Championships. [CLS] [SEP] During World War II his right hand was irreversibly injured in action. [CLS] [SEP] Hence after the war Kanaki changed to hammer throw, where his handicap was less impeding than in other throwing events. [CLS] [SEP] He set multiple records in hammer throw in the late 1940s and placed fifth at the 1950 European Championships. [CLS] [SEP]
