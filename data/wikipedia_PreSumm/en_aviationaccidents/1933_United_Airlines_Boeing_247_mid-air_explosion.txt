[CLS] On October 10, 1933, a Boeing 247 airliner operated by United Airlines and registered as NC13304 crashed near Chesterton, Indiana. [SEP] [CLS] The transcontinental flight carried three crew and four passengers and originated in Newark, New Jersey, with its final destination in Oakland, California. [SEP] [CLS] It had already landed in Cleveland and was headed to its next stop in Chicago when it exploded en route. [SEP] [CLS] All aboard died in the crash, which was caused by an on-board explosive device. [SEP] [CLS] Eyewitnesses on the ground reported hearing an explosion shortly after 9 p.m. [SEP] [CLS] and seeing the aircraft in flames at an altitude of about 1,000 feet (300 m). [SEP] [CLS] A second explosion followed after the aircraft crashed. [SEP] [CLS] The crash scene was adjacent to a gravel road about 5 miles (8 km) outside of Chesterton, centered in a wooded area on the Jackson Township farm of James Smiley.Investigators combed through the debris and were confronted with unusual evidence: the toilet and baggage compartment had been smashed into fragments. [SEP] [CLS] Shards of metal riddled the inside of the toilet door, while the other side of the door was free of the metal fragments. [SEP] [CLS] The tail section had been severed just aft of the toilet and was found mostly intact almost a mile away from the main wreckage. [SEP] [CLS] On November 16, 2017, the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) declassified 324 documents related to the investigation. [SEP] [CLS]
