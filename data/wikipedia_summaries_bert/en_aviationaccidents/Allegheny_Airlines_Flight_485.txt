Allegheny Airlines Flight 485 was a regularly scheduled domestic passenger flight between Washington, D.C. [CLS] [SEP] and Newport News, Virginia, United States, with three stop-overs, two in Connecticut and a third in Pennsylvania. [CLS] [SEP] On June 7, 1971, the Allegheny Airlines Convair CV-580 operating the flight crashed on approach to Tweed New Haven Regional Airport, New Haven County, Connecticut. [CLS] [SEP] The accident was notable in that all but one person survived the initial impact, however 27 people died in the subsequent fire, after failing to open the emergency exit. [CLS] [SEP] Only the first officer and 2 passengers survived. [CLS] [SEP] Poor visibility in the cabin, a lack of emergency lighting, unclear emergency door instructions, and a lack of additional crew personnel to assist in evacuation were all cited as factors in the high death toll.
