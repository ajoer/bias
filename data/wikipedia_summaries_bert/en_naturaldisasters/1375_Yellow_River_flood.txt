[CLS] The 1375 Yellow River flood was a natural disaster affecting the area around Kaifeng, China during the early Ming dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] Contemporary sources mentioned a death toll of between 15,000 and 25 people, most of them farmers. [SEP] [CLS] The philosopher Wang Yangming mentioned the 1375 Yellow River flood as an example of how even a virtuous man can be destroyed by the power of nature. [SEP] [CLS]
