The name Jebi has been used to name four tropical cyclones in the northwestern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] In addition, the variant Chebi was used in 2001 and 2006 before the spelling was corrected by the WMO Typhoon Committee. [CLS] [SEP] The name was submitted by South Korea and is a Korean word for the barn swallow (Hirundo rustica). [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Chebi (2001) (T0102, 04W, Emong) – made landfall in the People's Republic of China. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Chebi (2006) (T0620, 23W, Queenie) – killed one in the Philippines. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Jebi (2013) (T1309, 09W, Jolina) – struck the Philippines, mainland China and Vietnam. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Jebi (2018) (T1821, 25W, Maymay) – a strong Category 5 typhoon that made landfall in west Japan. [CLS] [SEP]
