The Montreal flood of 1987 happened on July 14 of that year when a series of strong thunderstorms crossed the island of Montreal, Canada, between the noon hour and 2:30 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] Over 100 millimetres (3.9 in) of rain fell during this very short period of time. [CLS] [SEP] The sewer systems were overwhelmed by the deluge and the city was paralyzed by the flooded roads. [CLS] [SEP] Autoroute 15, a sunken highway also known as the Decarie Expressway, soon filled with water, trapping motorists. [CLS] [SEP] Some 350,000 houses lost electricity, and tens of thousands had flooded basements. [CLS] [SEP] Two people died, one in a submerged car and another who was electrocuted. [CLS] [SEP]
