The 1978 Tabas earthquake occurred on September 16 at 19:05:55 local time in central Iran. [CLS] [SEP] The shock measured 7.4 on the moment magnitude scale and had a maximum Mercalli intensity of IX+ (Violent). [CLS] [SEP] The death toll was in the range of 15,000–25,000 with severe effects in the town of Tabas. [CLS] [SEP] Eighty percent of the loss of life occurred in Tabas, but a total of 85 villages were affected. [CLS] [SEP] This shock was felt in Tehran, about 610 kilometers (380 mi) away. [CLS] [SEP] About 55–85 km (34–53 mi) of ground deformation was observed, with about 1.7 meters (5 ft 7 in) of maximum slip. [CLS] [SEP] Only one significant M5 aftershock occurred. [CLS] [SEP]
