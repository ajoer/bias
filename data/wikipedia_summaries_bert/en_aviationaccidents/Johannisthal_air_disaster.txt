The Johannisthal air disaster was one of the first multiple-fatality air disasters in history. [CLS] [SEP] It involved the Imperial German Navy's L 2 airship manufactured by Luftschiffbau Zeppelin as LZ 18. [CLS] [SEP] Its test flight resulted in the death of all 28 passengers and crew on board. [CLS] [SEP] On 17 October 1913, at approximately 10:30am local time, hydrogen gas which was being vented was sucked into the forward engine and ignited causing the airship to explode and burn. [CLS] [SEP] It crashed near Johannisthal Air Field about 10 miles southeast of Berlin. [CLS] [SEP] This accident occurred a little over a month after the Helgoland Island Air Disaster. [CLS] [SEP]
