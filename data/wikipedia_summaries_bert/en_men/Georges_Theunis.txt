[CLS] Georges (George) Emile Léonard Theunis (28 February 1873 – 4 January 1966) was the 24th Prime Minister of Belgium from 16 December 1921 to 13 May 1925 and again from 20 November 1934 to 25 March 1935. [SEP] [CLS] He was governor of the National Bank of Belgium (NBB) from 1941 until 1944. [SEP] [CLS] He received a military training and was also trained as an engineer. [SEP] [CLS] Georges Theunis started his career in the Empain group, where he was an administrator and later the president of the board of ACEC. [SEP] [CLS] During World War I, he headed the Belgian Wartime Provisions Commission in London. [SEP] [CLS] After the war he was involved in the Paris Peace Conference, 1919 and served as the Belgian delegate to the Reparations Commission. [SEP] [CLS] From 1926 until 1927 he chaired the International Economic Conference in Geneva. [SEP] [CLS] In 1926 he joined the newly formed council of regency of the National Bank, together with Emile Francqui, and remained a member until the war, except for two breaks during his ministerial duties. [SEP] [CLS] As regents of the NBB, both Theunis and Francqui represented the power which the large private banks had gained since 1926. [SEP] [CLS] During World War II he served as a special ambassador to the United States of America. [SEP] [CLS] In 1941, he was appointed governor of the National Bank of Belgium by the Pierlot government in exile at London instead of Albert Goffin who had been appointed by Secretary General Charles Plisnier. [SEP] [CLS] On his return to Belgium after the war, he resigned as governor of the NBB. [SEP] [CLS]
