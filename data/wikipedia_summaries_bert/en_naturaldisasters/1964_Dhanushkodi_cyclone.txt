The 1964 Rameswaram cyclone (also known as the Dhanushkodi cyclone) was regarded as one of the most powerful storms to ever strike India on record. [CLS] [SEP] The system was first identified as an area of low pressure over the Andaman Sea on December 15. [CLS] [SEP] Following interaction with a tropical wave, it began to develop and became a depression by December 18. [CLS] [SEP] Increasingly rapid intensification ensued over the following days with the cyclone attaining hurricane-force winds around 5Â°N the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Early on December 23, the storm struck Norther Ceylon and India's Pamban Island with winds estimated at 240 km/h (150 mph), ranking it as a modern-day super cyclonic storm. [CLS] [SEP] Weakening somewhat, the storm soon struck Tamil Nadu. [CLS] [SEP] Rapid weakening followed once the cyclone was onshore and it degenerated into a depression on December 24 as it emerged over the Arabian Sea. [CLS] [SEP] The system later dissipated on December 26 over open water. [CLS] [SEP]
