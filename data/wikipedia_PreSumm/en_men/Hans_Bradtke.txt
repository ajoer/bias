[CLS] "Summer Wind" is a 1965 song, originally released in Germany as "Der Sommerwind" and written by Heinz Meier and German language lyrics by Hans Bradtke. [SEP] [CLS] Johnny Mercer re-wrote the song into English along the same themes as the original, which talked of the changing of the seasons using the Southern European sirocco wind as a metaphor. [SEP] [CLS] In America, it was first recorded by Wayne Newton and subsequently by Bobby Vinton and Perry Como. [SEP] [CLS] The song is best known for a 1966 recording by Frank Sinatra which peaked at number 25 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and number one on the Easy Listening chart. [SEP] [CLS] The Sinatra version originally appeared on his album, Strangers in the Night.
