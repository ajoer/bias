[CLS] Tropical Storm Larry was the twelfth tropical storm in the 2003 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] It was one of eight storms to impact Mexico from either the Pacific or Atlantic Oceans in the season, a near-record. [SEP] [CLS] Larry formed in early October from an extratropical storm in the Bay of Campeche, and reached a peak intensity of 65 mph (100 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] Due to weak steering currents, the storm moved southward, which resulted in the storm hitting the Tabasco coastline. [SEP] [CLS] The storm was the first Tabascan landfall since Tropical Storm Brenda in 1973.Larry drifted across the Isthmus of Tehuantepec, dropping heavy rainfall of over 9 in (229 mm) in places. [SEP] [CLS] The rainfall led to flooding and mudslides, causing damage to thousands of houses. [SEP] [CLS] The flooding killed five people and resulted in $53.6 million (2003 USD) in damage. [SEP] [CLS] Larry was one of three tropical cyclones to hit Mexico in a short period of time, including Tropical Depression Nora and Tropical Storm Olaf in the eastern Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS]
