[CLS] The Winter Hill air disaster occurred on 27 February 1958, when the Silver City Airways Bristol 170 Freighter G-AICS, traveling from the Isle of Man to Manchester, England, crashed into Winter Hill (also known as Rivington Moor) at a point several hundred yards from the Independent Television Authority's Winter Hill transmitting station.Thirty-five people died and seven were injured. [SEP] [CLS] The ICAO report stated that the accident occurred at 9:15 AM. [SEP] [CLS] At the time, the weather was so severe that none of the engineers working in the ITA transmitting station were aware of the crash. [SEP] [CLS] Several feet of snow hampered rescue efforts, and a snow cat vehicle had to be diverted from the A6 to cut a path for emergency vehicles, even though the track had been cleared by people using spades just previously.
