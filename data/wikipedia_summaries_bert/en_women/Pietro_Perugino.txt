Pietro Perugino (US: , Italian: [ˈpjɛːtro peruˈdʒiːno]; c. [CLS] [SEP] 1446/1452 – 1523), born Pietro Vannucci, was an Italian Renaissance painter of the Umbrian school, who developed some of the qualities that found classic expression in the High Renaissance. [CLS] [SEP] Raphael was his most famous pupil. [CLS] [SEP]
