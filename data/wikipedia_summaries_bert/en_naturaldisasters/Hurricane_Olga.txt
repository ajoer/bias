[CLS] Hurricane Olga was the largest tropical cyclone by diameter of gale-force winds on record in the Atlantic. [SEP] [CLS] The fifteenth named storm, ninth and final hurricane of the 2001 Atlantic hurricane season, Olga formed as a subtropical cyclone on November 24. [SEP] [CLS] After acquiring tropical characteristics later that day, Olga meandered westward, and eventually reached hurricane status on November 26. [SEP] [CLS] Olga peaked as a 90 mph (150 km/h) Category 1 hurricane before the storm turned southwestward and weakening back into a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] On November 30 it deteriorated further to a tropical depression, although it re-intensified two days later to tropical storm intensity. [SEP] [CLS] Olga then dissipated as a tropical cyclone on December 4 east of the Bahamas. [SEP] [CLS] Its damaging effects were limited to ships at sea. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone's remnants produced heavy rainfall across the Bahamas and Florida. [SEP] [CLS] It was a relatively rare storm to exist in December, which is outside of the normal Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS]
