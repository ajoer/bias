Vangipurapu Venkata Sai Laxman (pronunciation ; born 1 November 1974), commonly known as VVS Laxman or just VVS, is a former Indian international cricketer and a current cricket commentator and pundit. [CLS] [SEP] A right-hand batsman known for his elegant stroke play, Laxman played as a middle-order batsman in Test cricket. [CLS] [SEP] Laxman is one of the few players to have played 100 Test matches, without ever appearing in a One-Day Cricket World Cup. [CLS] [SEP] Despite being a relatively slow runner between wickets, Laxman compensated this with his masterful stroke play and fast scoring. [CLS] [SEP] Even after playing many great innings, consistently, and in spite of him being revered all over the world as a great batsman, he had to fight a unique, career-long battle to prove himself over and over again to find a place in the national team. [CLS] [SEP] In 2002, he was named one of Wisden's five Cricketers of the Year. [CLS] [SEP] In domestic cricket, Laxman represented Hyderabad. [CLS] [SEP] He also played for Lancashire in the English county cricket. [CLS] [SEP] He was also the captain of the Deccan Chargers team in the Indian Premier League during its inaugural season. [CLS] [SEP] Later, he played for the Kochi Tuskers IPL team. [CLS] [SEP] He is currently a mentor of the Sunrisers Hyderabad. [CLS] [SEP] In 2011, Laxman was awarded the Padma Shri award, India's fourth highest civilian award.. [CLS] [SEP] In 2012, Laxman retired from international cricket. [CLS] [SEP]
