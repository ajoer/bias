Peter the Great (Russian: Пётр Вели́кий, tr. [CLS] [SEP] Pyotr Velikiy, IPA: [ˈpʲɵtr vʲɪˈlʲikʲɪj]), Peter I (Russian: Пётр Первый, tr. [CLS] [SEP] Pyotr Pyerviy, IPA: [ˈpʲɵtr ˈpʲɛrvɨj]) or Pyotr Alekseevich (Russian: Пётр Алексе́евич, IPA: [ˈpʲɵtr ɐlʲɪˈksʲejɪvʲɪtɕ]; 9 June [O.S. [CLS] [SEP] 30 May] 1672 – 8 February [O.S. [CLS] [SEP] 28 January] 1725) ruled the Tsardom of Russia and later the Russian Empire from 7 May [O.S. [CLS] [SEP] 27 April] 1682 until his death in 1725, jointly ruling before 1696 with his elder half-brother, Ivan V. [CLS] [SEP] Through a number of successful wars, he expanded the Tsardom into a much larger empire that became a major European power and also laid the groundwork for the Imperial Russian Navy after capturing ports at Azov and the Baltic Sea. [CLS] [SEP] He led a cultural revolution that replaced some of the traditionalist and medieval social and political systems with ones that were modern, scientific, Westernised and based on the Enlightenment. [CLS] [SEP] Peter's reforms had a lasting impact on Russia, and many institutions of the Russian government trace their origins to his reign. [CLS] [SEP] He is also known for founding and developing the city of Saint Petersburg, which remained the capital of Russia until 1917. [CLS] [SEP]
