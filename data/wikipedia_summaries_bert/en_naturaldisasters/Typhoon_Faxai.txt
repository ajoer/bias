[CLS] Typhoon Faxai, also known as the Reiwa 1 Bōsō Peninsula Typhoon (令和元年房総半島台風, Reiwa Gannen Bōsō-hantō Taifū), was the first typhoon to strike the Kantō region since Mindulle in 2016, and the strongest typhoon to hit the region since Ma-on in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] It was also the worst to hit the region since Talas in 2011, until the region was hit by more destructive Typhoon Hagibis less than a month later. [SEP] [CLS] Forming as the fifteenth named storm of the 2019 Pacific typhoon season, the precursor to Faxai was first noted as a weak tropical depression to the east of the International Dateline on August 29. [SEP] [CLS] The depression then entered the West Pacific basin on August 30. [SEP] [CLS] After moving in a general westward direction, the system strengthened into a named tropical storm by September 5. [SEP] [CLS] Faxai then strengthened into the sixth typhoon of the season the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Two days later, Faxai reached its peak strength as a Category 4 typhoon just before making landfall in mainland Japan. [SEP] [CLS] Turning northeastward, Faxai rapidly weakened and became extratropical on September 10. [SEP] [CLS] Three people were killed and 147 others were injured. [SEP] [CLS] More than 390,000 people were urged to be evacuated. [SEP] [CLS] Faxai left 934,000 households without power. [SEP] [CLS] Train services in JR East were cancelled due to the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Two people died from heatstroke because of the power outage. [SEP] [CLS] Total damages from the typhoon in Japan are currently estimated at US$8.12 billion. [SEP] [CLS]
