[CLS] Arfa Abdul Karim Randhawa (Urdu: عرفه کریم رندھاوا‎‎; 2 February 1995 – 14 January 2012) was a Pakistani student and computer prodigy who became the youngest Microsoft Certified Professional (MCP) in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] She was submitted to the Guinness Book of World Records for her achievement. [SEP] [CLS] Randhawa kept the title until 2008 and went on to represent Pakistan on various international forums, including the TechEd Developers Conference. [SEP] [CLS] She received the President's Award for Pride of Performance by General Pervez Musharraf in 2005. [SEP] [CLS] A science park in Lahore, the Arfa Software Technology Park, was named in her honor. [SEP] [CLS] When she was 10, Randhawa was invited by Bill Gates to visit the Microsoft headquarters in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] She died on 14 January 2012, aged 16, from a cardiac arrest.
