Barbara Rittner (born 25 April 1973) is a former professional tennis player who comes from Germany. [CLS] [SEP] She currently is the captain of the German Fed Cup team. [CLS] [SEP] Her career-high singles ranking was No. [CLS] [SEP] 24 in the world, achieved on 1 February 1993. [CLS] [SEP] As a junior, she won the 1991 Wimbledon Championships. [CLS] [SEP] She won her first WTA Tour title in 1992, and almost nine years later, she won her second at the Belgian Open in Antwerp. [CLS] [SEP] This marked the second longest time between singles titles in the Open Era (behind Marcie Louie, who captured her second title nine and half years after her first one). [CLS] [SEP] She also won three doubles titles with three different partners. [CLS] [SEP] She reached the fourth round of a Grand Slam tournament twice in her professional career; once at the French Open in 1996, and again at the Australian Open in 2001. [CLS] [SEP] Her best performances at Wimbledon and the US Open are the third round in both. [CLS] [SEP] At 's-Hertogenbosch in 2003, as a main-draw alternate, she recorded the best win of her career over the then-sixth ranked Amélie Mauresmo before losing to Kim Clijsters. [CLS] [SEP] Earlier in the year, she achieved her first career top-ten win over Jelena Dokić at Indian Wells. [CLS] [SEP] In January 2005, she became captain of the German Fed Cup team. [CLS] [SEP]
