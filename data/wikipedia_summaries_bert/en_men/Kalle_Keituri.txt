Kalle Keituri (born 25 April 1984) is a Finnish former ski jumper who competed from 2002 to 2014. [CLS] [SEP] He debuted in the Continental Cup in 2002 after winning a bronze medal at the 2002 Junior World Championship in Schonach. [CLS] [SEP] On 1 March 2002 he made his World Cup debut in Lahti, finishing 19th and gaining 12 points; his overall position at the end of the season was 66th. [CLS] [SEP] In the following season he finished 26th in Lahti. [CLS] [SEP] In 2004 he did not participate in the World Cup. [CLS] [SEP] The next year was Keituri's best as he reached 9th position in the overall Continental Cup standings, with 540 points; he also competed in five World Cup events. [CLS] [SEP] In Lahti he was 22nd, followed by 29th Lillehammer and 22nd in Planica; this gained him 22 points in the overall World Cup. [CLS] [SEP] During the 2005–06 World Cup season his best performance was ninth in Titisee-Neustadt on 22 January 2006, but his final Continental Cup position was only 67th. [CLS] [SEP] In the 2007–08 World Cup season he achieved ninth in Kuusamo, which remains his personal best finish. [CLS] [SEP] The largest jump in his career was 205.5 metres in Planica. [CLS] [SEP] At the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, Keituri finished fourth in the team large hill event and 22nd on the individual normal hill. [CLS] [SEP]
