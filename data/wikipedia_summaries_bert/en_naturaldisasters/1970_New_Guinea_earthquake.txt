On October 31 at 17:53 UTC (November 1 at 03:53 AEST) the island of New Guinea was shaken by an earthquake of magnitude 6.9 Mw that particularly affected the city of Madang on the north coast of Papua New Guinea. [CLS] [SEP] Causing between five and eighteen fatalities, it triggered landslides that ran down steep hills into poorly reinforced wooden huts. [CLS] [SEP] The area that experienced the most powerful intensity extended 20 kilometers (12 mi) out from the epicenter. [CLS] [SEP] Underwater landslides caused minor tsunami over about 100 km of coast and severed underwater cables in several places. [CLS] [SEP]
