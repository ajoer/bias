The Brighton tornado is the strongest storm recorded in Melbourne to date. [CLS] [SEP] On the afternoon of 2 February 1918, with prevailing north-westerly winds and a heat wave (typical conditions for Melbourne thunderstorms). [CLS] [SEP] After a severe storm formed and moved off Port Phillip, two tornadoes struck Brighton beach simultaneously at approximately 5:45 pm and proceeded inland, converging near the junction of Halifax and Church Streets. [CLS] [SEP] Five minutes later, a third tornado struck. [CLS] [SEP] The tornadoes then tracked east over open fields. [CLS] [SEP] Damage retrospectively rated F3 on the Fujita scale was observed in places. [CLS] [SEP] Two people were killed, a man and a boy, while the drowning of a woman at St Kilda beach is believed to be related to the same storm cell. [CLS] [SEP] Over 6 were injured in the Brighton area.The tornado completely destroyed the Hawthorn Road Methodist church, which was later rebuilt. [CLS] [SEP] Numerous homes were demolished. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado badly damaged the Brighton Baths, tore the roof off Royal Terminus Hotel and destroyed the verandah of Grimley's Hotel. [CLS] [SEP] Extensive damage was incurred to infrastructure on the Sandringham railway line. [CLS] [SEP] Several community and sporting facilities were destroyed including the cricket club grandstand and a bandstand. [CLS] [SEP] It also damaged the burial monument of Adam Lindsay Gordon in the Brighton general cemetery. [CLS] [SEP]
