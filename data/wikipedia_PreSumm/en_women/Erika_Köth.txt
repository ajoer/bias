[CLS] Erika Köth (15 September 1925 in Darmstadt – 20 February 1989 in Speyer) was a German operatic high coloratura soprano, particularly associated with the roles of Zerbinetta and Zerlina. [SEP] [CLS] Köth began a musical studies in Darmstadt with Elsa Blank in 1942, and after an interruption resumed them in 1945. [SEP] [CLS] She made her stage debut in Kaiserslautern as Philine in Mignon, in 1948, and then sang in Karlsruhe (1950–53). [SEP] [CLS] She made her debut at the Munich State Opera and the Vienna State Opera in 1953, and at the Berlin State Opera in 1961. [SEP] [CLS] She appeared regularly at the Salzburg Festival (1955–64), as the Queen of the Night and Konstanze and Sophie, and in Bayreuth (1965–68), as the Woodbird. [SEP] [CLS] She also made guest appearances in Milan, Paris, London, etc. [SEP] [CLS] Her repertory included: Susanna, Zerlina, Sophie, Despina, Queen of the Night, Lucia, Gilda, Stravinsky's Le Rossignol, and The Rake's Progress. [SEP] [CLS] She had a small but piercing voice of great agility with a range extending remarkably high. [SEP] [CLS] She can be seen on black-and-white video in the role of Rosina in a complete German performance of The Barber of Seville with Fritz Wunderlich, Hermann Prey, and Hans Hotter, and in a German performance of Don Giovanni, with Dietrich Fischer-Dieskau, Donald Grobe, Walter Berry, and Josef Greindl. [SEP] [CLS] Her discography also includes many operetta and lieder recordings. [SEP] [CLS]
