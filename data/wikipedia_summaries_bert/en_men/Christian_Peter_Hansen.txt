Ekke Nekkepenn (also Eke Nekepen, besides other various spellings) is a North German legendary figure. [CLS] [SEP] In the middle of the 19th century the local researcher, folklorist, and graphic artist Christian Peter Hansen (1803-1879) portrayed Ekke Nekkepenn as a merman who lives with his wife Rahn at the bottom of the North Sea and plays mischievous tricks on seafarers and residents of the North Frisian Islands. [CLS] [SEP] In Theodor Storm’s 1866 published fairy tale “The Rain Maiden” a little man of fire with the name Eckeneckenpen appears and casts evil spells to make the fields wither. [CLS] [SEP]
