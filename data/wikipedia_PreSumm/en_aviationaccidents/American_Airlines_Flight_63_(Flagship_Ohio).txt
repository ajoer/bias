[CLS] On July 28, 1943 American Airlines Flight 63 was flown by a Douglas DC-3, named Flagship Ohio, routing Cleveland-Columbus-Dayton-Cincinnati-Louisville-Nashville-Memphis, that crashed on the Louisville-Nashville sector about 1.6 miles (2.6 km) west of Trammel, Kentucky. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft descended from 200 feet (61 m) until it struck trees, then slid across an open field and stopped in an upright position. [SEP] [CLS] Of the 22 people on board, 20 died. [SEP] [CLS] The cause of the crash was loss of control due to severe turbulence and violent downdrafts. [SEP] [CLS]
