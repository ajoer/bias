[CLS] The 856 Damghan earthquake or the 856 Qumis earthquake occurred on 22 December 856 (242 AH). [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake had an estimated magnitude of 7.9, and a maximum intensity of X (Extreme) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] The meizoseismal area (area of maximum damage) extended for about 350 kilometres (220 mi) along the southern edge of the eastern Alborz mountains of present-day Iran including parts of Tabaristan and Gorgan. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake's epicenter is estimated to be close to the city of Damghan, which was then the capital of the Persian province of Qumis. [SEP] [CLS] It caused approximately 200,000 deaths and is listed by the USGS as the sixth deadliest earthquake in recorded history. [SEP] [CLS]
