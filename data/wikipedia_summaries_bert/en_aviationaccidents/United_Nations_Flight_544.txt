United Nations Flight 544 was a civilian helicopter operated by Nizhnevartovskavia that on 21 December 2012 was shot down by the South Sudanese army (SPLA) near Likuangole, South Sudan, killing all four people on board. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 544 was performing a reconnaissance mission for the United Nations Mission in South Sudan (UNMISS) to evaluate overland routes and landing areas for delivery of humanitarian aid. [CLS] [SEP] The flight had originated in Juba. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly after taking off from a stopover at Pibor, the helicopter took fire from SPLA ground-based anti-aircraft artillery and crashed. [CLS] [SEP] The crash site was near the front of ongoing fighting between the SPLA and Murle rebels led by David Yau Yau. [CLS] [SEP] The four victims on board were Russian nationals employed by Nizhnevartovskavia. [CLS] [SEP]
