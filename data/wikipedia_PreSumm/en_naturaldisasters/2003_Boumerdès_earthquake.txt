[CLS] The 2003 Boumerdès earthquake occurred on May 21 at 19:44:21 local time in northern Algeria. [SEP] [CLS] The shock had a moment magnitude of 6.8 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of X (Extreme). [SEP] [CLS] The epicentre of the earthquake was located near the town of Thénia in Boumerdès Province, approximately 60 km east of the capital Algiers. [SEP] [CLS] The quake was the strongest to hit Algeria in more than twenty years – since 1980, when a magnitude 7.1 earthquake resulted in at least 2,633 deaths. [SEP] [CLS]
