[CLS] Paul Otto Schlesinger (8 February 1878 – 25 or 30 November 1943) was a German film actor and director. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Berlin, he began a qualification as a retail merchant and made his actor's debut at the age of 17. [SEP] [CLS] Otto worked at Theaters in Halle, Wiesbaden and Hanover before he returned to Berlin about 1906. [SEP] [CLS] He first appeared in the silent film Ringkampf Konkurrenz in 1910 and in Arsène Lupin contra Sherlock Holmes (1910-11, five episode film serial) next to Viggo Larsen. [SEP] [CLS] In 1912 Otto directed his first own film Selbstgerichtet. [SEP] [CLS] In the beginning of the 1930s he also appeared in successful Sound movies like Der Hauptmann von Köpenick. [SEP] [CLS] After 1933, Otto returned to theaterstages and worked at the Deutsches Theater Berlin and the Kammerspiele Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] In 1937 he was awarded a Staatsschauspieler - title and in 1942 Joseph Goebbels promoted him to the head of the stagecouncil at the Reichskulturkammer. [SEP] [CLS] In September 1943 his Jewish descent was discovered. [SEP] [CLS] To avoid his deportation Otto committed suicide on 30 November 1943, together with his wife Charlotte Klinder-Otto at his home in Berlin. [SEP] [CLS]
