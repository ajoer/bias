[CLS] Roman Herzog (German: [ˈʁoːman ˈhɛʁt͡soːk] (listen); 5 April 1934 – 10 January 2017) was a German politician, judge and legal scholar, who served as President of Germany from 1994 to 1999. [SEP] [CLS] A member of the Christian Democratic Union (CDU), he was the first president to be elected after the reunification of Germany. [SEP] [CLS] He previously served as a judge of the Federal Constitutional Court, and he was the President of the court 1987–1994. [SEP] [CLS] Before his appointment as a judge he was a professor of law. [SEP] [CLS] He received the 1997 Charlemagne Prize. [SEP] [CLS]
