[CLS] The November 2011 Bering Sea cyclone was one of the most powerful extratropical cyclones to affect Alaska on record. [SEP] [CLS] On November 8, the National Weather Service (NWS) began issuing severe weather warnings, saying that this was a near-record (or record) storm in the Bering Sea. [SEP] [CLS] It rapidly deepened from 973 mb (28.7 inHg) to 948 mb (28.0 inHg) in just 24 hours before bottoming out at 943 mbar (hPa; 27.85 inHg), roughly comparable to a Category 3 or 4 hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] The storm had been deemed life-threatening by many people. [SEP] [CLS] The storm had a forward speed of at least 60 mph (97 km/h) before it had reached Alaska. [SEP] [CLS] The storm began affecting Alaska in the late hours of November 8, 2011. [SEP] [CLS] The highest gust recorded was 93 mph (150 km/h) on Little Diomede Island. [SEP] [CLS] One person was reported missing after being swept into the Bering Sea, and he was later pronounced dead. [SEP] [CLS]
