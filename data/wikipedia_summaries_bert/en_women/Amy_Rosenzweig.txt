Amy C. [CLS] [SEP] Rosenzweig is a professor of Chemistry and Molecular Biosciences at Northwestern University. [CLS] [SEP] She was born in 1967 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. [CLS] [SEP] She received her BA in chemistry from Amherst College in 1988, and her Ph.D. [CLS] [SEP] from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1994. [CLS] [SEP] At MIT, Rosenzweig worked under the supervision of Stephen J. [CLS] [SEP] Lippard where she pioneered the structural studies of the hydroxylase component of methane monooxygenase from methyloccous capsulatus. [CLS] [SEP] Her current research interests include structural biology and bioinorganic chemistry, metal uptake and transport, oxygen activation by metalloenzymes, and characterization of membrane protein. [CLS] [SEP] For her work, she has been recognized by a number of national and international awards, including the MacArthur "Genius" Award in 2003. [CLS] [SEP]
