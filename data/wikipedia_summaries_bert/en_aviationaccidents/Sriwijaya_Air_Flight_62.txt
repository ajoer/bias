Sriwijaya Air Flight 62 (SJ62/SSY62) was a scheduled domestic passenger flight, operated by Indonesian airliner Sriwijaya Air from Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, Jakarta to Sultan Thaha Airport, Jambi. [CLS] [SEP] On 27 August 2008, the aircraft operating the flight, a Boeing 737 series 200 registered as PK-CJG, overran the runway and crashed onto a house during its landing attempt at Jambi's Sultan Thaha Airport. [CLS] [SEP] Due to the accident, 26 people were injured, including 3 people on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] One person later succumbed to his injuries. [CLS] [SEP] Everyone on board the aircraft survived the crash. [CLS] [SEP] It was the first fatal crash in Sriwijaya Air's operational history. [CLS] [SEP] Investigation carried out by the Indonesian National Transportation Safety Committee (NTSC) concluded that hydraulics failure were the cause of the crash of Flight 62. [CLS] [SEP] According to the NTSC, the failure of the hydraulics system A caused several systems on the aircraft to be inoperative. [CLS] [SEP] No definitive cause of the failure of the hydraulics were found by the NTSC. [CLS] [SEP] The NTSC also noted about the CRM failure of the crew on board. [CLS] [SEP]
