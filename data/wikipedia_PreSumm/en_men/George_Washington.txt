[SEP] [CLS] Previously, he led Patriot forces to victory in the nation's War for Independence. [SEP] [CLS] He presided at the Constitutional Convention of 1787, which established the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Constitution and a federal government. [SEP] [CLS] Washington has been called the "Father of His Country" for his manifold leadership in the formative days of the new nation. [SEP] [CLS] Washington received his initial military training and command with the Virginia Regiment during the French and Indian War. [SEP] [CLS] He was later elected to the Virginia House of Burgesses and was named a delegate to the Continental Congress, where he was appointed Commanding General of the Continental Army. [SEP] [CLS] He commanded American forces, allied with France, in the defeat and surrender of the British during the Siege of Yorktown. [SEP] [CLS] He resigned his commission after the Treaty of Paris in 1783. [SEP] [CLS] Washington played a key role in adopting and ratifying the Constitution and was then elected president (twice) by the Electoral College. [SEP] [CLS] He implemented a strong, well-financed national government while remaining impartial in a fierce rivalry between cabinet members Thomas Jefferson and Alexander Hamilton. [SEP] [CLS] During the French Revolution, he proclaimed a policy of neutrality while sanctioning the Jay Treaty. [SEP] [CLS] He set enduring precedents for the office of president, including the title "Mr. [SEP] [CLS] President", and his Farewell Address is widely regarded as a pre-eminent statement on republicanism. [SEP] [CLS] Washington owned slaves, and, in order to preserve national unity, he supported measures passed by Congress to protect slavery. [SEP] [CLS] He later became troubled with the institution of slavery and freed his slaves in a 1799 will. [SEP] [CLS] He endeavored to assimilate Native Americans into Anglo-American culture but combated indigenous resistance during occasions of violent conflict. [SEP] [CLS] He was a member of the Anglican Church and the Freemasons, and he urged broad religious freedom in his roles as general and president. [SEP] [CLS] Upon his death, he was eulogized as "first in war, first in peace, and first in the hearts of his countrymen". [SEP] [CLS] He has been memorialized by monuments, art, geographical locations, stamps, and currency, and many scholars and polls rank him among the greatest U.S. [SEP] [CLS] presidents.
