[CLS] Hurricane Tara was one of the deadliest Pacific hurricanes on record. [SEP] [CLS] The final tropical cyclone of the 1961 Pacific hurricane season, Tara formed on November 10 about 230 mi (365 km) off the coast of Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] It strengthened to reach maximum sustained winds of 85 mph (140 km/h) before making landfall in the Mexican state of Guerrero near Zihuatanejo. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Tara dissipated on November 12, bringing heavy rainfall and strong winds to locations inundated by 10 days of precipitation. [SEP] [CLS] Damage was light in the major port city of Acapulco, though further west along the coast, the effects of Tara were much worse.
