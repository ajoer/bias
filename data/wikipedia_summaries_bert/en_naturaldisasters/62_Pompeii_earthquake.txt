[CLS] The AD 62 Pompeii earthquake occurred on 5 February AD 62. [SEP] [CLS] It had an estimated magnitude of between 5 and 6 and a maximum intensity of IX or X on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] The towns of Pompeii and Herculaneum were severely damaged. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake may have been a precursor to the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79, which destroyed the same two towns. [SEP] [CLS] The contemporary philosopher and dramatist Seneca the Younger wrote an account of the earthquake in the sixth book of his Naturales quaestiones, entitled De Terrae Motu (Concerning Earthquakes).
