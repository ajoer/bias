[CLS] William McLean Hamilton, (February 23, 1919 – June 7, 1989) was a Canadian politician. [SEP] [CLS] He was born in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. [SEP] [CLS] His father, Ernest Hamilton, was a lacrosse player who won a gold medal at the 1908 Summer Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] He was a student at the Montreal High School and he took a B.Sc from Sir George Williams University in business administration. [SEP] [CLS] He sat on Montreal City Council and the Executive Council of Montreal. [SEP] [CLS] He was elected to parliament in the 1953 election as a Progressive Conservative for the riding of Notre-Dame-de-Grâce. [SEP] [CLS] In his maiden speech to Parliament in November he accused the very conservative Liberal government led by Louis St. [SEP] [CLS] Laurent of being socialists. [SEP] [CLS] His criticisms were based on the government being corporatistic and taking an interest in private business activities. [SEP] [CLS] Following the 1957 election, he was appointed Postmaster General of Canada by John Diefenbaker. [SEP] [CLS] In that office he annoyed many of his colleagues by not permitting the post office to be used a reward for party supporters. [SEP] [CLS] He also drew ire by attempting to close many small and inefficient post offices. [SEP] [CLS] Despite his staunch fiscal conservatism and anti-communism, he was a supporter of the Quebec Liberal Party over the Union Nationale. [SEP] [CLS] Hamilton disliked the corruption of the Union Nationale and their infusion of strict Catholicism into Quebec politics. [SEP] [CLS] Hamilton was an Anglican. [SEP] [CLS] He resigned as Postmaster General on July 12, 1962 after he was defeated in the 1962 election by Edmund Tobin Asselin. [SEP] [CLS] Hamilton could not speak French, and it was a more important issue in 1962 than it had been previously. [SEP] [CLS] After his defeat Hamilton left Montreal for Vancouver where he ran a business. [SEP] [CLS] In 1978 he was made an Officer of the Order of Canada. [SEP] [CLS]
