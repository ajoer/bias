[CLS] Louise Nevelson (September 23, 1899 â€“ April 17, 1988) was an American sculptor known for her monumental, monochromatic, wooden wall pieces and outdoor sculptures. [SEP] [CLS] Born in the Poltava Governorate of the Russian Empire (present-day Ukraine), she emigrated with her family to the United States in the early 20th century. [SEP] [CLS] Nevelson learned English at school, as she spoke Yiddish at home. [SEP] [CLS] By the early 1930's she was attending art classes at the Art Students League of New York, and in 1941 she had her first solo exhibition. [SEP] [CLS] A student of Hans Hofmann and Chaim Gross, Nevelson experimented with early conceptual art using found objects, and dabbled in painting and printing before dedicating her lifework to sculpture. [SEP] [CLS] Usually created out of wood, her sculptures appear puzzle-like, with multiple intricately cut pieces placed into wall sculptures or independently standing pieces, often 3-D. [SEP] [CLS] One unique feature of her work is that her figures are often painted in monochromatic black or white. [SEP] [CLS] A figure in the international art scene, Nevelson was showcased at the 31st Venice Biennale. [SEP] [CLS] Her work is seen in major collections in museums and corporations. [SEP] [CLS] Nevelson remains one of the most important figures in 20th-century American sculpture. [SEP] [CLS]
