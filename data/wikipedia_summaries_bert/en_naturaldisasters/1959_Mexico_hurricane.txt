The 1959 Mexico hurricane was the deadliest Pacific hurricane on record. [CLS] [SEP] First observed south of Mexico on October 23, the cyclone tracked northwestward. [CLS] [SEP] It intensified into a Category 3 hurricane on October 25 and reached Category 4 intensity on the following day. [CLS] [SEP] After turning toward the northeast, the hurricane made landfall near Manzanillo, Mexico at peak intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The system continued on that trajectory before dissipating on the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Impact from the hurricane was severe and widespread. [CLS] [SEP] Initially forecast to remain offshore, the system curved northeast and moved ashore, becoming one of Mexico's worst natural disasters at the time. [CLS] [SEP] Up to 150 boats were submerged. [CLS] [SEP] Countless homes in Colima and Jalisco were damaged or destroyed, large portions of the states were inaccessible by flash flooding, and hundreds of residents were stranded. [CLS] [SEP] All coconut plantations were blown down during the storm, leaving thousands without work and instating fear that it would take the economy years to recover. [CLS] [SEP] Torrential rainfall across mountain terrain contributed to numerous mudslides that caused hundreds of fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] In the aftermath of the cyclone, convoys delivering aid were hindered by the destruction. [CLS] [SEP] Residents were vaccinated to prevent the spread of disease. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, the hurricane inflicted at least $280 million (1959 USD) in damage. [CLS] [SEP]
