[CLS] The 1941 Cabo San Lucas hurricane is considered one of the worst tropical cyclones on record to affect Cabo San Lucas. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane was first reported on September 8 off the coast of Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] It slowly moved northwestward while intensifying. [SEP] [CLS] After peaking in intensity, it entered the Gulf of California, and weakened rapidly. [SEP] [CLS] It dissipated on September 13. [SEP] [CLS] This system brought winds and heavy rain to the southern tip of the Baja California Peninsula. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane destroyed poorer sections of La Paz and its nearby villages. [SEP] [CLS] Two villages were completely destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Furthermore, Cabo San Lucas was devastated. [SEP] [CLS] The torrential rains damaged roads and left thousands homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Initially following the storm, Cabo San Lucas was abandoned, but after World War II, most of the destroyed buildings were rebuilt. [SEP] [CLS] The remnants of the storm later brought rains to California and Arizona, peaking at 3.54 in (90 mm) at Mormon Lake. [SEP] [CLS]
