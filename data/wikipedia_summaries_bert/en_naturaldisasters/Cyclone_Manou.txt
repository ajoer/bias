Tropical Cyclone Manou was a relatively rare May tropical cyclone that affected southeastern Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP] After an initial strengthening phase, the storm weakened but later re-intensified as it approached Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP] Manou developed a well-defined eye and reached peak winds only 19 km (12 mi) from the eastern Madagascar coastline. [CLS] [SEP] For about 12 hours, the cyclone stalled before turning to the south and weakening. [CLS] [SEP] Manou became extratropical on May 10 and dissipated three days later. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in Madagascar was heaviest near Vatomandry, where 85% of buildings were destroyed and 23 people were killed. [CLS] [SEP] Manou destroyed about 24,500 houses nationwide, leaving 114,480 people homeless. [CLS] [SEP] The storm destroyed large areas of crops and disrupted transportation, including damaging the road between Vatomandry and Brickaville. [CLS] [SEP] Manou injured 85 and killed 89 people throughout Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP]
