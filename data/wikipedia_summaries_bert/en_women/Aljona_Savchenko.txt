Aljona Savchenko (Ukrainian: Олена Валентинівна Савченко, Olena Valentynivna Savčenko; German Romanization: Aljona Sawtschenko, sometimes Aliona Savchenko; born 19 January 1984) is a Ukrainian-born German pair skater. [CLS] [SEP] One of the most decorated pair skaters, she is the 2018 Olympic Champion and two-time Olympic bronze medalist (2010, 2014), a six-time World Champion, a four-time European Champion and a five-time Grand Prix Final champion. [CLS] [SEP] With partner Bruno Massot, she is the 2018 Olympic champion, the 2018 World Champion, a two-time European silver medalist, the 2017–18 Grand Prix Final champion, and a two-time German national champion (2016, 2018). [CLS] [SEP] The pair hold the world record for best free skate score and best total score, and they are the first pair skaters to break 160 points in the free skate. [CLS] [SEP] With former partner Robin Szolkowy, she is the 2010 and 2014 Olympic bronze medalist, a five-time World champion (2008, 2009, 2011, 2012, 2014), a four-time European champion (2007–2009, 2011), a four-time Grand Prix Final champion (2007, 2010, 2011, 2013), and an eight-time German national champion (2004–2009, 2011, 2014). [CLS] [SEP] Savchenko and Szolkowy received the first 10.0 component scores ever given by a judge under the ISU Judging System.Savchenko originally represented Ukraine with Dmitri Boyenko and later Stanislav Morozov. [CLS] [SEP] With Morozov, she was the 2000 World Junior champion, the 1999–2000 Junior Grand Prix Final champion, and a two-time Ukrainian national champion. [CLS] [SEP]
