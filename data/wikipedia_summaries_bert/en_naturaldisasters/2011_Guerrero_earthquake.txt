The 2011 Guerrero earthquake struck with a moment magnitude of 5.7 in southern Mexico at 08:24 local time on 5 May. [CLS] [SEP] It was positioned west of Ometepec, Guerrero, with a focal depth of 24 km (14.9 mi), and was lightly felt in many adjacent areas. [CLS] [SEP] Buildings swayed with the tremor in Mexico City, prompting evacuations and causing panic among many. [CLS] [SEP] Following the quake, police patrolled city streets for safety reasons and damage assessments were carried out across the affected region. [CLS] [SEP] There were no casualties, though two local police stations suffered slight damage. [CLS] [SEP] A number of light aftershocks succeeded the main event, of which the strongest measured a magnitude of 4.1 (ML). [CLS] [SEP]
