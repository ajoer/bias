Tropical Storm Tess known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Welpring was the second of three tropical cyclones to directly impact the Philippines in a two-week time frame in 1988. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather near the Philippines was first observed on November 1. [CLS] [SEP] Following an increase in organization, the disturbance was designated as a tropical cyclone on November 4. [CLS] [SEP] Moving west, Tess steadily strengthened due to favorable conditions aloft. [CLS] [SEP] During the evening of November 5, Tess was estimated to have achieved its highest intensity, with winds of 115 km/h (70 mph). [CLS] [SEP] Rapid weakening then ensured as Tess neared Vietnam, and after making landfall in the country on November 6, Tess dissipated the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Tess and its precursor disturbance brought extensive flooding to much of the central Philippines. [CLS] [SEP] In Cebu, eight people were killed, and many homes and bridges were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] The province of Palawan, a geographically isolated island from the rest of the country, suffered severe damage due to mudslides and flooding. [CLS] [SEP] There, 75 people were confirmed to have died, and 600 others were later presumed to have perished. [CLS] [SEP] In the suburbs of the capital city of Manila, 3,000 people were forced to flee their homes due to rising floodwaters. [CLS] [SEP] In all, there were a total of 686 deaths in the country. [CLS] [SEP] Nationwide, 144,136 people were evacuated to shelter, and 86 homes were destroyed, with 430 others receiving damage. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in the country totaled $11.38 million (1988 USD). [CLS] [SEP] In addition to the impact on the Philippines, Tess killed 37 people and inflicted $11 million in damage in Vietnam. [CLS] [SEP]
