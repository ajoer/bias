On Friday, 16 December 1960 a United Airlines Douglas DC-8, bound for Idlewild Airport (now John F. [CLS] [SEP] Kennedy International Airport) in New York City, collided in midair with a TWA Lockheed L-1049 Super Constellation descending into the city's LaGuardia Airport. [CLS] [SEP] The Constellation crashed on Miller Field in Staten Island and the DC-8 into Park Slope, Brooklyn, killing all 128 people on the two aircraft and six people on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] It was the deadliest aviation disaster in the world at the time. [CLS] [SEP] The death toll would not be surpassed until a Lockheed C-130B Hercules was shot down in May 1968, killing 155 people. [CLS] [SEP] In terms of commercial aviation, the death toll would not be surpassed until the crash of Viasa Flight 742, which crashed on takeoff and killed all 84 people onboard the aircraft, as well as 71 people on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] The accident became known as the Park Slope plane crash or the Miller Field crash, after the crash sites of each plane respectively. [CLS] [SEP] The accident was also the first hull loss and first fatal accident involving a Douglas DC-8. [CLS] [SEP]
