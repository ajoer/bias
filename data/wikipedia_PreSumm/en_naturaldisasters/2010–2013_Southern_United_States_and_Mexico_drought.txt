[CLS] The 2010–2013 Southern United States and Mexico drought was a severe to extreme drought that plagued the Southern United States, including parts of Texas, Louisiana, Arkansas, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Oklahoma; the Southwestern States, including Kansas, Colorado, New Mexico, and Arizona; as well as large parts of Mexico, in a three-year pattern from 2010 to 2013. [SEP] [CLS] The worst effects were in Texas, which experienced the brunt of the drought and its driest August–July (12-month) period on record from 2010-11. [SEP] [CLS] The dry spell in May 2011 was also said to be the worst period of drought in Texas since 1895. [SEP] [CLS] The U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Drought Monitor reported that Lubbock, Texas has experienced the nation's worst average level of drought since the beginning of 2011. [SEP] [CLS] McAllen, Harlingen, Brownsville and Corpus Christi also ranked among the nine U.S. [SEP] [CLS] cities most affected by extreme drought. [SEP] [CLS] The drought in Texas caused an estimated $7.62 billion in crop and livestock losses, surpassing the previous record loss of $4.1 billion set in 2006. [SEP] [CLS]
