[CLS] The name Gordon has been used for five tropical cyclones in the Atlantic Ocean since 1988, when it replaced the name Gilbert on the list of hurricane names. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Gordon (1994) – killed 1,122 in Haiti, and 23 in other nations. [SEP] [CLS] Damage in the United States was $400 million, and damage in Haiti and Cuba was severe. [SEP] [CLS] Despite the high death toll and damages it caused, however, the name was not retired. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Gordon (2000) – formed near Guatemala, cut across the Yucatán Peninsula and later hit Florida as a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Killed 23 in Guatemala and one in Florida, and $10.8 million damage there (no figure for Guatemala). [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Gordon (2006) – formed in the central North Atlantic, tracked north and east while becoming a Category 3 major hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] Crossed the Azores as a Category 1 storm before dissipating over western Europe. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Gordon (2012) – a strong Category 2 hurricane which passed over the eastern Azores as a Category 1. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Gordon (2018) – formed near the Florida Keys and affected South Florida, killing two. [SEP] [CLS] Then made landfall west of the Alabama-Mississippi border as a strong tropical storm, causing moderate damage.The name Gordon was also used for four tropical cyclones in the western Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Gordon (1979) (T7910, 07W, Herming) – Strong tropical storm which made landfall in China. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Gordon (1982) (T8216, 16W) – Category 3 typhoon with no known effects on land. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Gordon (1985) (T8527, 24W) – Weak tropical storm which made landfall in Vietnam. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Gordon (1989) (T8908, 08W, Goring) – Powerful Category 5 super typhoon which crossed extreme northern Luzon at peak intensity before making landfall southwest of Hong Kong as a strong tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] 306 people were killed by Gordon, and 120,000 were left homeless in the Philippines.The name Gordon has also been used for one tropical cyclone in the southwestern Pacific Ocean.
