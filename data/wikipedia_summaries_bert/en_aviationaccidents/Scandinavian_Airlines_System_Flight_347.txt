Scandinavian Airlines System Flight 347 was a scheduled domestic flight which, on 3 November 1994, was hijacked shortly after take-off. [CLS] [SEP] The flight, from Bardufoss Airport via Bodø Airport to Oslo Airport, Fornebu in Norway, was operated by a McDonnell Douglas MD-82 belonging to Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS). [CLS] [SEP] The hijacker was Haris Keč, a Bosnian living in Norway, who made demands that Norwegian authorities help to stop the humanitarian suffering in his home country caused by the Bosnian War. [CLS] [SEP] No one was injured in the incident. [CLS] [SEP] Keč hijacked the aircraft with 122 passengers and a crew of six in mid-air after leaving Bardufoss. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft landed as scheduled at Bodø, where all women, children and seniors were let off, along with two of the cabin crew. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft then departed Bodø with 77 passengers and a crew of four. [CLS] [SEP] It was diverted to Gardermoen, where Keč made his demands. [CLS] [SEP] He surrendered at about 21:00, seven hours after take-off from Bardufoss, after some of his demands had been met. [CLS] [SEP] He was sentenced to four years prison for the incident.
