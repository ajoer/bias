[CLS] Georg Leo Graf von Caprivi de Caprera de Montecuccoli (English: Count George Leo of Caprivi, Caprera, and Montecuccoli), born Georg Leo von Caprivi, (24 February 1831 â€“ 6 February 1899) was a German general and statesman who succeeded Otto von Bismarck as Chancellor of Germany. [SEP] [CLS] Caprivi served as German Chancellor from March 1890 to October 1894. [SEP] [CLS] Caprivi promoted industrial and commercial development, and concluded numerous bilateral treaties for reduction of tariff barriers. [SEP] [CLS] However, this movement toward free trade angered the conservative agrarian interests, especially the Junkers. [SEP] [CLS] He promised the Catholic Center party educational reforms that would increase their influence, but failed to deliver. [SEP] [CLS] As part of Kaiser Wilhelm's "new course" in foreign policy, Caprivi abandoned Bismarck's military, economic, and ideological cooperation with the Russian Empire, and was unable to forge a close relationship with the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland. [SEP] [CLS] He successfully promoted the reorganization of the German military. [SEP] [CLS]
