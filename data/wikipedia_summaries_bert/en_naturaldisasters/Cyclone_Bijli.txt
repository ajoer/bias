Cyclone Bijli (JTWC designation: 01B, also known as Cyclonic Storm Bijli), was the first tropical cyclone to form during the 2009 North Indian Ocean cyclone season. [CLS] [SEP] Bijli formed from an area of Low Pressure on April 14. [CLS] [SEP] Later that evening, RSMC New Delhi upgraded the low pressure area to a Depression and designated it as BOB 01. [CLS] [SEP] The Joint Typhoon Warning Center (JTWC) then issued a Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert for the system and soon after designated it as Tropical Depression 01B. [CLS] [SEP] On the evening of April 15, both RSMC New Delhi and the JTWC reported that the system had intensified into a tropical storm, with the former naming it Bilji. [CLS] [SEP] Soon after, Bilji reached its peak intensity as it approached the coast of Bangladesh. [CLS] [SEP] However, on the morning of April 17, Bijli weakened to a deep depression due to land interaction, before making landfall just south of Chittagong. [CLS] [SEP] The remnants of Bilji continued to weaken as they tracked across northern Myanmar, before RSMC New Delhi issued their last advisory on April 18. [CLS] [SEP] The word Bijli refers to lightning in Hindi. [CLS] [SEP]
