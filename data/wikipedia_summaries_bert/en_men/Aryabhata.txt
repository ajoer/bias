[CLS] Aryabhata (Sanskrit: आर्यभट, ISO: Āryabhaṭa) or Aryabhata I (476–550 CE) was the first of the major mathematician-astronomers from the classical age of Indian mathematics and Indian astronomy. [SEP] [CLS] His works include the Āryabhaṭīya (which mentions that in 3600 Kaliyuga, 499 CE, he was 23 years old) and the Arya-siddhanta. [SEP] [CLS] For his explicit mention of the relativity of motion, he also qualifies as a major early physicist. [SEP] [CLS]
