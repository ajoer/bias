The 1721 Tabriz earthquake occurred on April 26, with an epicenter near the city of Tabriz, Iran. [CLS] [SEP] It leveled some three-quarters of the city, including many prominent mosques and schools in the city, and resulted in the deaths of tens of thousands of people. [CLS] [SEP] The total number of casualties caused by the earthquake is between 8,000 and 250,000; it was most likely approximately 80,000. [CLS] [SEP] At the time that it occurred, the earthquake was popularly interpreted as an omen of misfortune, or a demonstration of godly wrath. [CLS] [SEP] The destruction that the earthquake caused was a significant factor in the successful Ottoman takeover of Tabriz in 1722, as well as contributing to Tabriz's economic difficulties during that period. [CLS] [SEP] It also caused the destruction of some of the city's significant historical monuments. [CLS] [SEP] Accounts of the earthquake are often confused with descriptions of the 1727 Tabriz earthquake. [CLS] [SEP]
