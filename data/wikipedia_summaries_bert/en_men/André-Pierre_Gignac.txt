[CLS] André-Pierre Christian Gignac (French pronunciation: ​[ɑ̃dʁe pjɛʁ ʒiɲak]; born 5 December 1985) is a French professional footballer who plays for Liga MX club Tigres UANL. [SEP] [CLS] He primarily plays as a striker whose influence can extend out to the wings. [SEP] [CLS] Gignac is described as a "powerful and dangerous" striker who is known for his "aerial presence". [SEP] [CLS] He is known personally by teammates and friends as simply Dédé.Gignac began his career in his home department of Bouches-du-Rhône, playing for local club Fos and Martigues. [SEP] [CLS] In 2002, he ventured to the Brittany region to join professional club Lorient. [SEP] [CLS] He starred for the club for two seasons, which included a loan stint at amateur club Pau. [SEP] [CLS] He then joined Toulouse in 2007, amid controversial circumstances. [SEP] [CLS] After early struggles Gignac reached prominence during the 2008–09 season, becoming the league's top scorer with 24 goals. [SEP] [CLS] After a sub-par 2009–10 season with Toulouse, Gignac joined the defending champions Marseille in August 2010 on a five-year contract. [SEP] [CLS] He scored 77 goals in 186 matches across all competitions for OM, winning two consecutive Coupe de la Ligue titles and the 2011 Trophée des Champions. [SEP] [CLS] After his contract expired he moved to Mexico to play for Tigres UANL, where he became the club’s all-time top scorer in all competitions.Gignac is a France international and made his debut with the team in April 2009 against Lithuania as a result of his good form with Toulouse. [SEP] [CLS] He scored his first international goal five months later against the Faroe Islands. [SEP] [CLS] Gignac made his first major international appearance for his country at the 2010 FIFA World Cup, featuring in all three matches the team contested. [SEP] [CLS] He also represented his country at UEFA Euro 2016, in which they were runners-up. [SEP] [CLS]
