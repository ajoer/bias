[CLS] The 1956 Amorgos earthquake occurred at 03:11 UTC on July 9. [SEP] [CLS] It had a magnitude of 7.7 on the moment magnitude scale and a maximum perceived intensity of IX on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] The epicentre was to the south of the island of Amorgos, the easternmost island of the Cyclades in the Aegean Sea. [SEP] [CLS] There was significant damage on Amorgos and the neighbouring island of Santorini. [SEP] [CLS] It was the largest earthquake in Greece in the 20th century. [SEP] [CLS] It was followed 13 minutes later by a magnitude 7.2 earthquake near Santorini. [SEP] [CLS] It triggered a major tsunami with a maximum run-up of 30 m. [SEP] [CLS] The combined effects of the earthquake shaking and the tsunami caused the deaths of 53 people with a further 100 injured. [SEP] [CLS]
