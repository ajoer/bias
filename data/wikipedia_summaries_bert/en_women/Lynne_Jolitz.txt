[CLS] Lynne Greer Jolitz (born June 30, 1961) is a figure in free software and founder of many startups in Silicon Valley, along with her husband William. [SEP] [CLS] Lynne Jolitz, along with her husband, is probably most famous for her pioneering work to port and to free the 386BSD operating system. [SEP] [CLS] She has founded startup companies that involve workstations to companies in Internet multimedia. [SEP] [CLS] Lynne is also a noted author and authority on operating systems and networking issues. [SEP] [CLS] Lynne is an internet news media commentator discussing events in the computer industry and wrote frequently for BYTE. [SEP] [CLS] She also holds patents in internet technologies and semiconductor memory innovations, and writes technical papers and articles. [SEP] [CLS] She is currently an adviser to CoolClip network, an internet startup that uses the server-based video production engine that Lynne originally designed and tested at the University of California, Berkeley.Lynne has appeared on the Oracle E-Business Network and was presented with their Geek of the Week award for her years of work in high-speed networking and operating systems design. [SEP] [CLS] Lynne also has appeared on Dvorak's RealComputing discussing the impact of Internet broadband. [SEP] [CLS] She received an alumni award from the Physics Department at Berkeley for her work in alumni outreach with the department. [SEP] [CLS] She also is active in women's entrepreneur and technology networking groups and mentoring girls in science and technology. [SEP] [CLS] She and her husband reside in Los Gatos, California with their children Rebecca, Benjamin and Sarah. [SEP] [CLS]
