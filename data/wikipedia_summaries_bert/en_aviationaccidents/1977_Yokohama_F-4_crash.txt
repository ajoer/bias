[CLS] The 1977 Yokohama F-4 crash (横浜米軍機墜落事件, Yokohama Beigunki Tsuiraku Jiken, lit. [SEP] [CLS] "Yokohama American Military Aircraft Crash Incident") occurred on 27 September 1977, in Yokohama, Japan. [SEP] [CLS] In the crash, a United States Marine Corps RF-4B-41-MC, BuNo 157344, c/n 3717, 'RF611', of VMFP-3, a (reconnaissance variant of the McDonnell Douglas F-4 Phantom II) flown by a United States Marine Corps crew based at nearby Naval Air Facility Atsugi, en route to USS Midway in Sagami Bay, suffered a mechanical malfunction, the port engine caught fire, and crashed into a residential neighborhood. [SEP] [CLS] The crash killed two boys, ages 1 and 3, and injured seven others, several seriously. [SEP] [CLS] The two-man crew of the aircraft, Capt. [SEP] [CLS] J. [SEP] [CLS] E. [SEP] [CLS] Miller, of Mendota, Illinois, and 1st Lt. [SEP] [CLS] D. [SEP] [CLS] R. [SEP] [CLS] Durbin, of Natchitoches, Louisiana, ejected and were not seriously injured.The crash, which occurred near present-day Eda Station, destroyed several houses. [SEP] [CLS] The two young boys initially survived the crash into their home, but died later from severe burns. [SEP] [CLS] The boys' mother, Kazue Doshida, was also severely burned. [SEP] [CLS] Due to the fear that she would be adversely affected during her recovery by the shock, she was not told until 29 January 1979, that her sons had died. [SEP] [CLS] Upon hearing of their deaths, Doshida responded that she wanted to hold them one more time. [SEP] [CLS] Doshida died in 1982, aged 31, from complications related to her injuries.
