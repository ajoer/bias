In southeastern Pennsylvania on April 4, 1991, a Piper Aerostar propeller-driven aircraft collided in mid-air with a Bell 412 helicopter over Merion Elementary School in Lower Merion Township, a suburb northwest of Philadelphia. [CLS] [SEP] All five people in both aircraft were killed including United States Senator John Heinz, the sole passenger of the Piper. [CLS] [SEP] Two school children on the ground were also killed by falling debris. [CLS] [SEP] In addition to the seven fatalities, five more people on the ground were injured. [CLS] [SEP] An investigation discovered the cause of the accident to be poor judgment and pilot error of the crews from both aircraft. [CLS] [SEP]
