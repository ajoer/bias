Katja Thater (German pronunciation: [ˈkatja ˈtaːtɐ]; born 14 July 1966 in Hamburg) is a German professional poker player and horse breeder. [CLS] [SEP] She is married to another poker professional, Jan von Halle. [CLS] [SEP] Her popularity in Germany comes from the wide coverage on local television of her participation in the Poker Nations Cup and Women's Poker Open. [CLS] [SEP] Before becoming a professional poker player, Thater was the director of an event and marketing company in Hamburg and competed in dressage tournaments at horse shows. [CLS] [SEP] She continues competing in her spare time. [CLS] [SEP] Thater represented Germany as a member of the national team in the 2006 Poker Nations Cup in Cardiff, participated in the Women's Poker Open in London. [CLS] [SEP] She was also a member of PokerStars Team Pro. [CLS] [SEP] She won her first World Series of Poker bracelet in 2007 in the $1,500 Razz event. [CLS] [SEP] Other major tournament finishes include fourth place in the 2002 Austrian Classics and fifth place in the $1,000 World Championship Ladies no limit Texas hold 'em event in 2007. [CLS] [SEP] In 2010, she won a $1,000 buy-in 8-Game Mix Event at the PokerStars Caribbean Adventure in the Bahamas for over $10,000. [CLS] [SEP] In 2016, Thater continued her success at the PokerStars Caribbean Adventure, finishing second to Donald McCalmont in the $1,100 7-Card Stud Hi/Lo Split Eight-or-Better event.As of 2010, Thater's total live tournament winnings exceed $350,000.
