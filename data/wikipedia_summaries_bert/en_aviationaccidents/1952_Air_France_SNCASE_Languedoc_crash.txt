The 1952 Air France SNCASE Languedoc crash occurred on 3 March 1952 when a SNCASE SE.161/P7 Languedoc aircraft of Air France crashed on take-off from Nice Airport for Le Bourget Airport, Paris, killing all 38 people on board. [CLS] [SEP] The cause of the accident was that the aileron controls had jammed, which in itself was contributed to by a design fault. [CLS] [SEP] The accident was the third-deadliest in France at the time and is the deadliest involving the SNCASE Languedoc. [CLS] [SEP]
