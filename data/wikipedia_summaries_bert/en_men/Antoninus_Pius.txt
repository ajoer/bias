[CLS] Antoninus Pius (; Titus Aelius Hadrianus Antoninus Augustus Pius; 19 September 86 – 7 March 161) was Roman emperor from 138 to 161. [SEP] [CLS] He was one of the Five Good Emperors in the Nerva–Antonine dynasty.He was born Titus Aurelius Fulvus Boionius Arrius Antoninus as the son of Titus Aurelius Fulvus. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus' paternal grandfather had been a senator. [SEP] [CLS] His father was a Roman consul in the year 89, but died shortly afterwards. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus was then raised by his maternal grandfather, Gnaeus Arrius Antoninus. [SEP] [CLS] In the first part of the 110s Antoninus married Annia Galeria Faustina, a member of the Nerva-Antonine dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] Faustina's uncle Hadrian became emperor in 117. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus held various offices during the reign of Hadrian, who adopted Antoninus as his son and successor shortly before his death. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus acquired the cognomen Pius after his accession to the throne, either because he compelled the Senate to deify his adoptive father, or because he had saved senators sentenced to death by Hadrian in his later years. [SEP] [CLS] His reign is notable for the peaceful state of the Empire, with no major revolts or military incursions during this time, and for his governing without ever leaving Italy. [SEP] [CLS] A successful military campaign in southern Scotland early in his reign resulted in the construction of the Antonine Wall. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus was an effective administrator, leaving his successors a large surplus in the treasury, expanding free access to drinking water throughout the Empire, encouraging legal conformity, and facilitating the enfranchisement of freed slaves. [SEP] [CLS] He died of illness in 161 and was succeeded by his adopted sons Marcus Aurelius and Lucius Verus as co-emperors. [SEP] [CLS]
