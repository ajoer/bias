[CLS] Hurricane Abby was the only tropical cyclone in the Caribbean Sea during the 1960 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The second tropical cyclone and first named storm of the season, Abby developed on July 10 from a tropical wave in the vicinity of the Lesser Antilles. [SEP] [CLS] Abby rapidly intensified into a hurricane after being a tropical storm for less than six hours. [SEP] [CLS] It briefly peaked as a category 2 hurricane before weakening back. [SEP] [CLS] Abby rapidly weakened to a minimal tropical storm a few days thereafter. [SEP] [CLS] The storm re-strengthened into a hurricane as it began to parallel the coast of Honduras. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Abby made landfall in British Honduras (present-day Belize) on July 15. [SEP] [CLS] Abby dissipated over Mexico later the next day. [SEP] [CLS] The remnants of Abby ultimately became Hurricane Celeste in the Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] Despite passing through or near several countries, Hurricane Abby had a relatively light impact on land, resulting in just $640,500 (1960 USD, $5.57 million 2020 USD) in damage and six fatalities.
