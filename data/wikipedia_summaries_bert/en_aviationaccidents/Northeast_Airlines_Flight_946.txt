[CLS] Northeast Airlines Flight 946 was a domestic U.S. [SEP] [CLS] flight from Boston, Massachusetts, to Montpelier, Vermont, with a scheduled stop in Lebanon, New Hampshire, operated by Northeast Airlines. [SEP] [CLS] On October 25, 1968, some time during the evening, the Fairchild Hiller FH-227 aircraft crashed on Moose Mountain while descending on approach. [SEP] [CLS] The crash killed 32 of 42 passengers and crew. [SEP] [CLS] Of the fatalities, four were employees from the National Life Insurance Company who were returning from a business trip. [SEP] [CLS] The fatalities also included a reporter for the Barre Daily Times and six social workers of the Vermont Head Start Supplementary Training Program on a conference trip. [SEP] [CLS] Ten passengers survived the crash with minor or moderate injuries. [SEP] [CLS] After the crash, Northeast Airlines continued flight service until its merger with Delta Air Lines in the early 1970s. [SEP] [CLS]
