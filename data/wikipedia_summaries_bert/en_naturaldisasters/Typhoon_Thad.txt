Typhoon Thad, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Openg, was considered the worst storm to affect Japan in two years. [CLS] [SEP] Originating from a monsoon trough, Typhoon Thad was first classified on August 15, 1981 and was upgraded into a tropical storm the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Meanwhile, Thad moved north and northeast and attained typhoon intensity midday on August 18. [CLS] [SEP] The next day, the storm reached its peak intensity of 80 mph (130 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] On August 22, Thad accelerated northward, striking eastern Japan the next day just before weakening to a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] After passing through the country, the cyclone transitioned into an extratropical cyclone on August 23. [CLS] [SEP] Forty-one persons perished in Japan due to the typhoon and 173 others were hurt. [CLS] [SEP] Thad flooded 31,082 houses and demolished 4,401 others. [CLS] [SEP] Roughly 27,000 people were left homeless because of Thad. [CLS] [SEP] Moreover, 111,500 homeowners were without power during the height of the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Furthermore, the storm also destroyed 449 roads, and was responsible for 499 landslides. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone inundated 417,400 acres (168,900 hectares) of farm land. [CLS] [SEP] Train service was interrupted in 22 lines. [CLS] [SEP]
