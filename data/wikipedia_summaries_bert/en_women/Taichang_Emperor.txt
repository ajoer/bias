The Taichang Emperor (Chinese: 泰昌; pinyin: Tàichāng; 28 August 1582 – 26 September 1620), personal name Zhu Changluo (Chinese: 朱常洛), was the 15th Emperor of the Ming dynasty. [CLS] [SEP] He was the eldest son of the Wanli Emperor and succeeded his father as emperor in 1620. [CLS] [SEP] However, his reign came to an abrupt end less than one month after his coronation when he was found dead one morning in the palace following a bout of diarrhea. [CLS] [SEP] He was succeeded by his son, Zhu Youjiao, who was enthroned as the Tianqi Emperor. [CLS] [SEP] His era name, Taichang, means "grand prosperity." His reign was the shortest in Ming history. [CLS] [SEP]
