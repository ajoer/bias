[CLS] Tina Maze (Slovene: [ˈtiːna ˈmaːzɛ]; born 2 May 1983) is a retired Slovenian World Cup alpine ski racer. [SEP] [CLS] Maze is the most successful Slovenian ski racer in history with a career that culminated with two gold medals at the 2014 Winter Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] She was awarded the title of the Slovenian Sportswoman of the Year in 2005, 2010, 2011, 2013, 2014 and 2015, and with her four medals she is the most decorated Slovenian athlete at the Winter Olympics.Maze started her career as a giant slalom specialist, but later competed in all five alpine skiing disciplines. [SEP] [CLS] She is one of seven female racers who has won in all five World Cup disciplines and one of three to do it in a single season. [SEP] [CLS] Maze is the 2014 Olympic champion in downhill and giant slalom and the 2015 world champion in downhill and combined. [SEP] [CLS] She was also the world champion in giant slalom in 2011 and super-G in 2013. [SEP] [CLS] Maze won a total of 26 World Cup races during her career, and won the World Cup overall title in 2013. [SEP] [CLS] In that season, she won 11 races and tallied a record number of World Cup points – 2414, beating the previous record of Hermann Maier of 2000 points from the 2000 season. [SEP] [CLS] She also won the super-G and giant slalom titles, and finished first in the combined event (although no globe was awarded) and finished second in slalom and downhill. [SEP] [CLS] Maze also improved Maier's previous record of podium finishes in a single season (22) with 24 podiums. [SEP] [CLS]
