[CLS] The 1997–98 El Niño was regarded as one of the most powerful El Niño–Southern Oscillation events in recorded history, resulting in widespread droughts, flooding and other natural disasters across the globe. [SEP] [CLS] It caused an estimated 16% of the world's reef systems to die, and temporarily warmed air temperature by 1.5 °C, compared to the usual increase of 0.25 °C associated with El Niño events.It led to a severe outbreak of Rift Valley fever after extreme rainfall in north-eastern Kenya and southern Somalia. [SEP] [CLS] It also led to record rainfalls in California during the water season of 1997–98 and one of Indonesia's worst droughts on record. [SEP] [CLS] 1998 ultimately became the warmest year in recorded history (up until then). [SEP] [CLS]
