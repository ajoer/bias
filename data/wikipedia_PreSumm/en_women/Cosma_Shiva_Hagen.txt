[CLS] Cosma Shiva Hagen (German: [ˈkɔs.ma ˈʃiː.va ˈhaːɡn̩] (listen); born May 17, 1981 in Los Angeles, California) is a German-American actress and the daughter of the German new wave/punk singer Nina Hagen and the Dutch musician Ferdinand Karmelk. [SEP] [CLS] Her grandmother is actress Eva-Maria Hagen, and her step-grandfather is the East German dissident writer Wolf Biermann. [SEP] [CLS] Eva-Maria Hagen was allowed to emigrate to West Germany during the 1970s. [SEP] [CLS] Shiva's unusual name was picked by her mother, who claimed she saw a UFO while pregnant. [SEP] [CLS] "Cosma" is a reference to Cosmos, and "Shiva" is a reference to the Hindu God Shiva. [SEP] [CLS] Shiva is largely unknown outside of German-speaking countries. [SEP] [CLS] Although she speaks English, her acting roles have been largely confined to German language films and television productions. [SEP] [CLS] She also starred in an Irish film called Short Order (2005). [SEP] [CLS] Born in Los Angeles, California, Shiva speaks German, English, French and Spanish. [SEP] [CLS] As a child, Cosma Shiva lived in London, Berlin, Paris, Ibiza, a boarding school in Lüneburg and her current home, Hamburg. [SEP] [CLS]
