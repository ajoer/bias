The 1909 Wabash River earthquake occurred at 04:45 local time on September 27 with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VII (Very strong). [CLS] [SEP] It measured 5.1 on a seismic scale that is based on an isoseismal map or the event's felt area. [CLS] [SEP] With moderate damage in the Wabash River Valley, it is currently the strongest earthquake recorded in the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] state of Indiana. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake occurred somewhere along a fault within the Wabash Valley Seismic Zone. [CLS] [SEP]
