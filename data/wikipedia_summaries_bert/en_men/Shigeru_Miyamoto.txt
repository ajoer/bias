Shigeru Miyamoto (Japanese: 宮本 茂, Hepburn: Miyamoto Shigeru, pronounced [mijamoto ɕiɡeɾɯ]; born November 16, 1952) is a Japanese video game designer, producer and game director at Nintendo, where he serves as one of its representative directors. [CLS] [SEP] He is the creator of some of the most acclaimed and best-selling game franchises of all time, including Mario and The Legend of Zelda. [CLS] [SEP] Born in Sonobe, Japan, he graduated from Kanazawa Municipal College of Industrial Arts. [CLS] [SEP] He originally sought a career as a manga artist, until developing an interest in video games. [CLS] [SEP] With the help of his father, joined Nintendo in 1977 after impressing then-president Hiroshi Yamauchi with his toys. [CLS] [SEP] He became the company's first artist and helped create art for the arcade game Sheriff, and was later tasked with creating a new arcade unit for the company. [CLS] [SEP] This eventually led to the 1981 game Donkey Kong. [CLS] [SEP] Miyamoto went on to create both Super Mario Bros. [CLS] [SEP] and The Legend of Zelda, which became massive successes for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). [CLS] [SEP] The games helped Nintendo and the NES to dominate the console game market, especially after the video game crash of 1983. [CLS] [SEP] His games have been flagships of every Nintendo video game console, from the arcade machines of the late 1970s to the present day. [CLS] [SEP] He managed Nintendo's Entertainment Analysis & Development software division, which developed many of the company's first-party titles. [CLS] [SEP] As a result of Nintendo president Satoru Iwata's death in July 2015, Miyamoto took on the role of acting president alongside Genyo Takeda until being formally appointed as the company's "Creative Fellow" a few months later.
