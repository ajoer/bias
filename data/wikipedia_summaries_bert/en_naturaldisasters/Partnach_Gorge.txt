The Partnach Gorge (German: Partnachklamm) is a deep gorge that has been incised by a mountain stream, the Partnach, in the Reintal valley near the south German town of Garmisch-Partenkirchen. [CLS] [SEP] The gorge is 702 metres (2,303 ft) long and, in places, over 80 metres (260 ft) deep. [CLS] [SEP] It was designated a natural monument in 1912. [CLS] [SEP]
