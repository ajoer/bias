Agni Air Flight 101 was a regional flight between Kathmandu and Lukla, Nepal, that crashed on 24 August 2010, killing all 14 people on board. [CLS] [SEP] Twenty minutes after take-off, the flight crew had reported a technical problem. [CLS] [SEP] Contact with the aircraft was lost shortly after. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft crashed 50 miles (80 km) south of Kathmandu. [CLS] [SEP] The investigation into the accident found that pilot fell victim to spatial disorientation following the loss of flight instruments after both generators failed. [CLS] [SEP] Backup battery power was exhausted prematurely due to the crew using an outdated checklist. [CLS] [SEP]
