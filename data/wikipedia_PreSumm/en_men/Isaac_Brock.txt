[SEP] [CLS] Brock was assigned to Lower Canada in 1802. [SEP] [CLS] Despite facing desertions and near-mutinies, he commanded his regiment in Upper Canada (part of present-day Ontario) successfully for many years. [SEP] [CLS] He was promoted to major general, and became responsible for defending Upper Canada against the United States. [SEP] [CLS] While many in Canada and Britain believed war could be averted, Brock began to ready the army and militia for what was to come. [SEP] [CLS] When the War of 1812 broke out, the populace was prepared, and quick victories at Fort Mackinac and Detroit defeated American invasion efforts. [SEP] [CLS] Brock's actions, particularly his success at Detroit, earned him accolades including a knighthood in the Order of the Bath and the sobriquet "The Hero of Upper Canada". [SEP] [CLS] His name is often linked with that of the Native American leader Tecumseh, although the two men collaborated in person only for a few days. [SEP] [CLS] Brock died at the Battle of Queenston Heights, which the British won. [SEP] [CLS]
