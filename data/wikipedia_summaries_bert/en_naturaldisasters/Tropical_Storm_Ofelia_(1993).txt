Tropical Storm Ofelia, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Luming, was an early-season tropical cyclone that passed south Japan during July 1993. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather developed from the Western Pacific monsoon trough in late July 1993. [CLS] [SEP] The disturbance organized into a tropical depression on July 24, and the next day developed into a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] Tracking west-northwestward, Ofelia slowly deepened and attained its peak intensity of 80 km/h (50 mph) and a minimum barometric pressure of 994 mbar (29.4 inHg) at noon on July 26. [CLS] [SEP] On the next day, the storm made landfall shorty before weakening to a tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] On July 27, Ofelia transitioned into an extratropical cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] Across Tokushima Prefecture, 3 people were killed and 44 homes were damaged while 25 other homes were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Elsewhere, across Kochi Prefecture, 142 homes were damaged and an additional 452 were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Three people were also killed and another was injured. [CLS] [SEP] The storm dropped heavy rains in Ehime Prefecture for a 36 hour period, resulting in 17 landslides and the cancellation of 16 flights. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the prefecture, 119 houses were damaged and 10 others were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] In Oita Prefecture, a landslide destroyed a home in Honyabakei, killing three. [CLS] [SEP] In Hiroshima Prefecture, 18,060 households lost power after 934 power lines were downed while 713 homes were damaged and 54 were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 22 railtracks and 1,165 roads were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] Three people were also hurt. [CLS] [SEP] In Yamaguchi Prefecture, four people were killed.
