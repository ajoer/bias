[CLS] Ilke Wyludda (born 28 March 1969) is a discus thrower from Germany. [SEP] [CLS] She set eleven junior records at discus throw (and also two at shot put), and became junior world champion. [SEP] [CLS] Between 1989 and 1991 she recorded 41 successive wins until beaten by Tsvetanka Khristova at the 1991 World Championships. [SEP] [CLS] Wyludda never won the world championships, but she became Olympic champion in 1996. [SEP] [CLS] In early January 2011 Wyludda revealed in Bild that she had to have her right leg amputated because of Sepsis. [SEP] [CLS] After losing her leg she returned to athletics and began entering para-sport competitions. [SEP] [CLS] In 2012, she represented Germany at the London Paralympics becoming the first German athlete to have represented her country at both Olympic and Paralympic Games. [SEP] [CLS] In 2014, she entered the IPC European Championships in Swansea, taking the bronze medal in the F57 discus and silver in the shot put. [SEP] [CLS]
