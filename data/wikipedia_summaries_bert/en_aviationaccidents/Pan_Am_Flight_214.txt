Pan Am Flight 214 was a scheduled flight of Pan American World Airways from San Juan, Puerto Rico, to Baltimore, Maryland, and Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. [CLS] [SEP] On December 8, 1963, the Boeing 707 serving the flight crashed near Elkton, Maryland, while flying from Baltimore to Philadelphia, after being hit by lightning. [CLS] [SEP] All 81 occupants of the plane were killed. [CLS] [SEP] It was the first fatal accident on a Pan Am jet aircraft since the company had taken its first delivery of the type five years earlier. [CLS] [SEP] An investigation by the Civil Aeronautics Board concluded that the cause of the crash was a lightning strike that had ignited fuel vapors in one of the aircraft's fuel tanks, causing an explosion that destroyed one of the wings. [CLS] [SEP] The exact way that the lightning had ignited the fumes was never determined. [CLS] [SEP] However, the investigation revealed various ways that lightning can damage aircraft in flight, which led to new safety regulations. [CLS] [SEP] The crash also led to research into the safety of different types of aviation fuel and research into methods of reducing dangerous fuel-tank vapors. [CLS] [SEP]
