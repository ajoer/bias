[CLS] Typhoon Gerald, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Neneng, affected the Philippines, Taiwan, and China during September 1987. [SEP] [CLS] A tropical depression developed on September 4, and within 24 hours, intensified into a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] After initially moving erratically within the Philippine Sea, Gerald moved west-northwest and then northwest while steadily deepening. [SEP] [CLS] Gerald obtained typhoon intensity on September 8, and the following day, attained maximum intensity. [SEP] [CLS] Shortly thereafter, the typhoon skirted southwestern Taiwan, which resulted in a steady weakening trend. [SEP] [CLS] On September 10, Gerald moved ashore north of Hong Kong near Amoy. [SEP] [CLS] Gerald dissipated the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Across the Philippines, the storm caused $4.48 million (1987 USD) in damage but no deaths. [SEP] [CLS] Although the system stayed offshore Taiwan, the storm inflicted widespread flooding across the island that took five lives. [SEP] [CLS] There, 5,000 families were left without power, and damage exceeded $10 million. [SEP] [CLS] Although Gerald weakened considerably prior to making landfall, the storm was still responsible for extensive damage in the Fujian Province, where 76 people perished, and 31 others suffered injuries. [SEP] [CLS] In the neighboring Zhejiang Province, 28 people were killed and 71 others sustained injuries. [SEP] [CLS] Across China, 4,900 homes were destroyed and 122 fatalities were reported. [SEP] [CLS] Damage in the country amounted to $120 million. [SEP] [CLS]
