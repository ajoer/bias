The 2017 Chiapas earthquake struck at 23:49 CDT on 7 September (local time; 04:49 on the 8th UTC) in the Gulf of Tehuantepec off the southern coast of Mexico, near state of Chiapas, approximately 87 kilometres (54 mi) southwest of Pijijiapan (alternately, 101 kilometres (63 mi) south-southwest of Tres Picos), with a Mercalli intensity of IX (Violent). [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude was estimated to be Mw8.2.The earthquake caused all of Mexico City to tremble, prompting people to evacuate after the early warning system was triggered. [CLS] [SEP] It also generated a tsunami with waves 1.75 metres (5 ft 9 in) above tide level; and tsunami alerts were issued for surrounding areas. [CLS] [SEP] Mexico's president called it the strongest earthquake recorded in the country, in a century. [CLS] [SEP] It was also the second strongest recorded in the country's history, behind the magnitude 8.6 earthquake in 1787, and the largest recorded globally in 2017. [CLS] [SEP]
