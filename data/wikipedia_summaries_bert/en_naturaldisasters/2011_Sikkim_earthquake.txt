The 2011 Sikkim earthquake (also known as the 2011 Himalayan earthquake) occurred with a moment magnitude of 6.9 and was centered within the Kanchenjunga Conservation Area, near the border of Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, at 18:10 IST on Sunday, 18 September. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was felt across northeastern India, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh and southern Tibet. [CLS] [SEP] At least 111 people were killed in the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] Most of the deaths occurred in Sikkim, with reports of fatalities in and near Singtam in the East Sikkim district. [CLS] [SEP] Several buildings collapsed in Gangtok. [CLS] [SEP] Eleven are reported dead in Nepal, including three killed when a wall collapsed in the British Embassy in Kathmandu. [CLS] [SEP] Elsewhere, structural damage occurred in Bangladesh, Bhutan, and across Tibet; another seven fatalities were confirmed in the latter region. [CLS] [SEP] The quake came just a few days after an earthquake of 4.2 magnitude hit Haryana's Sonipat district, sending tremors in New Delhi. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was the fourth significant earthquake in India of September 2011.Exactly a year after the original earthquake at 5:55 pm on 18 September 2012, another earthquake of magnitude 4.1 struck Sikkim, sparking panic among the people observing the anniversary of the original quake.
