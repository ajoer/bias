Harry Kurt Victor Mulisch (pronunciation [ˈɦɑriː ˈmʏlɪʃ]; 29 July 1927 – 30 October 2010) was a Dutch writer. [CLS] [SEP] He wrote more than eighty novels, plays, essays, poems, and philosophical reflections. [CLS] [SEP] Mulisch's works have been translated into over thirty languages.Along with Willem Frederik Hermans and Gerard Reve, Mulisch is considered one of the "Great Three" (De Grote Drie) of Dutch postwar literature. [CLS] [SEP] His novel The Assault (1982) was adapted into a film that won both a Golden Globe and an Academy Award. [CLS] [SEP] Mulisch's work is also popular among the country's public: a 2007 poll of NRC Handelsblad readers voted his novel The Discovery of Heaven (1992) the greatest Dutch book ever written. [CLS] [SEP] He was regularly mentioned as a possible future Nobel laureate. [CLS] [SEP]
