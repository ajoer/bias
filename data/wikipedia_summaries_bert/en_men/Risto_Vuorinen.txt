[CLS] "Sleeping Sun" is a power ballad and the fifth single by Finnish symphonic metal band Nightwish. [SEP] [CLS] The song was written by Tuomas Holopainen and produced by Tero Kinnunen. [SEP] [CLS] It was released as a maxi single with three other songs on 2 August 1999, to coincide with the total solar eclipse that would occur on 11 August. [SEP] [CLS] Later that same year, the band's 1998 album Oceanborn was reissued with the song added to the track listing, and it has been on every release ever since. [SEP] [CLS] In 2004, it was included on Nightwish's first compilation album Tales from the Elvenpath, and in 2005, a re-recorded version was released to promote Highest Hopes: The Best of Nightwish, another compilation released on 27 September. [SEP] [CLS] "Sleeping Sun" was certified with Gold Disc in Finland with more than 5.000 copies sold, and also in Germany with more than 15.000 copies. [SEP] [CLS] It reached the number-two spot in the Finnish charts, and was #69 in the German Official Singles Charts. [SEP] [CLS] The song was used as the theme tune to the 2000 television film Britannic. [SEP] [CLS]
