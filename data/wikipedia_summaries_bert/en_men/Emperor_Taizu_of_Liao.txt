[CLS] Abaoji, posthumously known as Emperor Taizu of Liao, was a Khitan leader and founder of the Liao dynasty (907–926). [SEP] [CLS] He had a sinicised name, Yelü Yi; some sources suggest that Abaoji's family name, Yelü, was adopted during his lifetime, though there is no unanimity on this point. [SEP] [CLS] Abaoji was born in 872 in Southern Mongolia and had a turbulent childhood. [SEP] [CLS] His grandfather was killed in a conflict between tribes, and his father and uncles fled. [SEP] [CLS] He was hidden by his grandmother for his safety. [SEP] [CLS] He became khagan of the Khitans on 27 February 907, and was subsequently enthroned as emperor of the Khitans in 916, proclaiming his own era name. [SEP] [CLS] He died on 6 September 926. [SEP] [CLS] He was responsible for the conquest and unification of all of Inner Mongolia, northern China and southern Manchuria. [SEP] [CLS] Once the Khitan Empire became the Liao dynasty in 942, he was posthumously considered a Liao emperor. [SEP] [CLS]
