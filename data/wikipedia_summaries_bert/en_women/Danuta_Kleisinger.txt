Danuta Kleisinger (born Danuta Czlapinska; May 29, 1924 – 27 August 2017) was an Austrian Righteous Among the Nations and an honorary Israeli citizen. [CLS] [SEP] In 1942 the Polish Jew Jusek Prezman was with his mother Scheine in the Warsaw Ghetto. [CLS] [SEP] Before the war, Danuta's mother was a friend of Scheine Prezman and Jusek was a friend of Danuta. [CLS] [SEP] After the establishment of the ghetto Danuta smuggled food and money for the family Prezman into the ghetto and therewith endangered their lives. [CLS] [SEP] Daunta's fiancé Ewald Kleisinger served after the occupation of Poland as an officer in the German Wehrmacht at the railway station in Warsaw. [CLS] [SEP] With Ewald's assistance, Danuta provided Jusek and Josef with falsified Aryan papers and driver's permits. [CLS] [SEP] Before the ghetto uprising and its destruction in April 1943 Jusek Prezman and his friend Joseph Kormarzyn fled with the help of Kleisinger to the Aryan side of the city. [CLS] [SEP] Danuta hid them with the knowledge and consent of her fiancé, the officer Ewald Kleisinger, three weeks in her apartment. [CLS] [SEP] She also saved the mother of Jusek, Scheine Prezman. [CLS] [SEP] After that Jusek and Josef stayed at the home of Ewald's parents in Vienna. [CLS] [SEP] They were disguised as Polish Christian agricultural workers. [CLS] [SEP] Provided with food and other essentials, they lived as members of the Kleisinger family until the end of the war. [CLS] [SEP] Ewald Kleisinger also is an Austrian Righteous Among the Nations. [CLS] [SEP]
