Tropical Storm Podul (pronounced [pɔ.dul]), known in the Philippines as Tropical Depression Zoraida, was a weak but destructive tropical cyclone that affected the Philippines shortly after the devastating Typhoon Haiyan. [CLS] [SEP] The 31st named storm of the 2013 Pacific typhoon season, Podul developed as a tropical depression on November 11 between Palau and the Philippine island of Mindanao. [CLS] [SEP] The system moved west-northwestward and struck Davao Oriental in Mindanao on November 12, bringing heavy rainfall that killed two people and disrupted relief efforts following Haiyan. [CLS] [SEP] After crossing the Philippines, the depression intensified into Tropical Storm Podul on November 14. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly thereafter, the storm struck southeastern Vietnam, and its circulation dissipated on November 15. [CLS] [SEP] In Vietnam, Podul produced heavy rainfall that resulted in severe flooding. [CLS] [SEP] The storm damaged or destroyed 427,258 houses, and overall damage was estimated at 1.5 trillion₫ (2013 Vietnamese đồng, $72 million 2013 USD). [CLS] [SEP] Podul killed 42 people in the country and caused 74 injuries. [CLS] [SEP]
