Philip the Arab (Latin: Philippus Arabus) or Philip I (Latin: Marcus Julius Philippus; c. [CLS] [SEP] 204 â€“ September 249) was Roman emperor from February 244 to September 249. [CLS] [SEP] He was born in Aurantis, Arabia, in a city situated in modern-day Syria. [CLS] [SEP] He went on to become a major figure in the Roman Empire. [CLS] [SEP] After the death of Gordian III in February 244, Philip, who had been Praetorian prefect, achieved power. [CLS] [SEP] He quickly negotiated peace with the Persian Sassanid Empire and returned to Rome to be confirmed by the senate. [CLS] [SEP] During his reign, the city of Rome celebrated its millennium. [CLS] [SEP] He also introduced the Actia-Dusaria Festivities in Bostra, capital of Arabia. [CLS] [SEP] Dusaria is Dushara, the main Nabataean deity. [CLS] [SEP] Among early Christian writers, Philip had the reputation of being sympathetic to the Christian faith. [CLS] [SEP] Probably for this reason, it was even claimed by some that he had converted to Christianity, which would have made him the first Christian emperor. [CLS] [SEP] He supposedly tried to celebrate Easter with Christians in Antioch, but the bishop Saint Babylas made him stand with the penitents. [CLS] [SEP] Philip and his wife received letters from Origen.Philip was betrayed and killed at the Battle of Verona in September 249 following a rebellion led by his successor, Gaius Messius Quintus Decius.
