Cyclone Berit (also named Cyclone Xaver by the Free University of Berlin) was a very strong European windstorm that formed as a tropical wave near the Lesser Antilles in mid-November 2011. [CLS] [SEP] The storm began producing heavy rain and snow over Northern Europe on 24 and 25 November. [CLS] [SEP] Scotland saw its first snowfall since March, earlier in the year. [CLS] [SEP] The Faroe Islands also reported winds up to 198 km/h (55 m/s; 123 mph) and excessive damage. [CLS] [SEP] On 25 November, the Norwegian Weather Service named the storm 'Berit'. [CLS] [SEP] Another storm, called Yoda, hit Scotland just a day after Xaver. [CLS] [SEP] The storm Yoda was widely known as Lille-Berit (Little-Berit) in Scandinavia, as the Norwegian Weather Service did not issue it with an official name. [CLS] [SEP]
