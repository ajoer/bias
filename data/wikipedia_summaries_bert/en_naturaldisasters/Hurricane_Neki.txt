Hurricane Neki was the final tropical cyclone of the 2009 Pacific hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] It developed on October 18 as an unusually large disturbance from a trough south of Hawaii. [CLS] [SEP] Moving northwestward, it slowly organized at first due to its large size. [CLS] [SEP] After reaching hurricane status on October 21, Neki intensified at a much faster rate and peaked with winds of 125 mph (205 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] It later turned to the north and north-northeast and weakened due to hostile conditions. [CLS] [SEP] While passing through the Papahānaumokuākea Marine National Monument, Neki was downgraded to a tropical storm after the center became exposed from the deepest convection. [CLS] [SEP] It caused little impact in the island chain. [CLS] [SEP] After stalling and executing a small loop, Neki resumed its northward track and dissipated on October 27. [CLS] [SEP]
