The 1933 Baffin Bay earthquake struck Greenland and the Northwest Territories (now Nunavut), Canada with a moment magnitude of 7.7 at 18:21:35 Eastern Time Zone on November 20. [CLS] [SEP] The main shock epicenter was located in Baffin Bay on the east coast of Baffin Island. [CLS] [SEP] Shaking was only felt at the small town of Upernavik, Greenland. [CLS] [SEP] The event is the largest recorded earthquake to strike the passive margin of North America and is the largest north of the Arctic Circle. [CLS] [SEP] No damage was reported because of its offshore location and the small population of the nearby onshore communities. [CLS] [SEP]
