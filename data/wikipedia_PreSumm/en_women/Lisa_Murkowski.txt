[CLS] Lisa Ann Murkowski (; born May 22, 1957) is an American politician serving as the senior United States Senator from Alaska, having held that seat since 2002. [SEP] [CLS] She is a member of the Republican Party, and is the second most senior Republican woman in the Senate. [SEP] [CLS] Along with Susan Collins from Maine, she is frequently described as one of the most moderate Republicans in the Senate and is a crucial swing voter. [SEP] [CLS] Murkowski is the daughter of former U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Senator and Governor of Alaska, Frank Murkowski. [SEP] [CLS] Before her appointment to the Senate, she served in the Alaska House of Representatives and was eventually elected Majority Leader. [SEP] [CLS] She was appointed to the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Senate by her father, who resigned his seat in December 2002 to become the Governor of Alaska. [SEP] [CLS] She completed her father's unexpired term, which ended in January 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Murkowski ran for and won a full term in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] She ran for a second term in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] After losing the Republican Party primary to Tea Party candidate Joe Miller, Murkowski ran as a write-in candidate and defeated both Miller and Democrat Scott McAdams in the general election; this made her the second U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Senator, the first being Strom Thurmond in 1954, to be elected by write-in vote. [SEP] [CLS] Although Murkowski has won three full terms to the Senate, she has never won a majority of the vote; she won pluralities in each of her three races, with 48.6% of the vote in 2004, 39.5% in 2010, and 44.4% in 2016.
