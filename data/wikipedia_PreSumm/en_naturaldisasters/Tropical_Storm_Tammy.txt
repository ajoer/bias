[CLS] Tropical Storm Tammy was a short lived tropical storm during October in the 2005 Atlantic hurricane season which caused minor damage to the southeastern United States. [SEP] [CLS] More significant, however, were its remnants, which contributed to the Northeast U.S. [SEP] [CLS] flooding of October 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Tammy formed from a non-tropical system off the Florida coast on October 5. [SEP] [CLS] It moved north just offshore before making landfall later that day. [SEP] [CLS] The tropical storm rapidly weakened as it moved overland and dissipated the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Its remnant circulation moved south towards the Gulf of Mexico, while the moisture was absorbed by a northeasterly moving cold front. [SEP] [CLS] There were no fatalities directly related to Tammy; however, ten people were killed by the remnants of the storm in combination with the remnants of Subtropical Depression Twenty-Two. [SEP] [CLS] Total damages from the storm were $30 million. [SEP] [CLS]
