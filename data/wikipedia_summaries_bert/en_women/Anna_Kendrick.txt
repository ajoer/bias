Anna Cooke Kendrick (born August 9, 1985) is an American actress and singer. [CLS] [SEP] She began her career as a child in theater productions. [CLS] [SEP] Her first starring role was in the 1998 Broadway musical High Society, for which she earned a nomination for the Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical. [CLS] [SEP] She made her film debut in the musical comedy Camp (2003), and rose to prominence for her role in The Twilight Saga (2008–2012). [CLS] [SEP] Kendrick achieved further recognition for the comedy-drama film Up in the Air (2009), which earned her a nomination for the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress, and for her starring role in the Pitch Perfect film series (2012–17). [CLS] [SEP] Kendrick also had prominent roles in films such as the action comedy Scott Pilgrim vs. [CLS] [SEP] the World (2010), the comedy drama 50/50 (2011), the crime drama End of Watch (2012), the musical fantasy Into the Woods (2014), the drama Cake (2014), the comedy Mike and Dave Need Wedding Dates (2016), the animated comedy Trolls (2016), the comic thriller A Simple Favor (2018), the fantasy comedy Noelle (2019), and the animated sequel Trolls World Tour (2020). [CLS] [SEP] She also sang on various soundtracks for her films and published a memoir, Scrappy Little Nobody, in 2016. [CLS] [SEP]
