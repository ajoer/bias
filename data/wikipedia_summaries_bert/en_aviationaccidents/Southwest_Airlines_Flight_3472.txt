Southwest Airlines Flight 3472 was a regularly scheduled passenger flight operating from New Orleans International Airport in New Orleans, Louisiana to Orlando International Airport in Orlando, Florida. [CLS] [SEP] On August 27, 2016, the Boeing 737-7H4, with 99 passengers and five crew, 12 minutes after departure from New Orleans, was climbing through 31,000 feet and heading east over the Gulf of Mexico when the aircraft's number one CFM International CFM56-7 engine suffered an uncontained engine failure. [CLS] [SEP] Debris from the engine punctured the left side of the fuselage causing a loss of cabin pressure and damaged the wing and empennage. [CLS] [SEP] Oxygen masks were deployed to passengers while the crew initiated an emergency descent to 10,000 feet. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft then diverted to Pensacola International Airport for a safe landing about 20 minutes later without further incident. [CLS] [SEP] While the aircraft sustained substantial damage, there were no injuries. [CLS] [SEP]
