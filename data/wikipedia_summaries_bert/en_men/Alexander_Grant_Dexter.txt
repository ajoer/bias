[CLS] Alexander Grant Dexter (1896–1961) was one of Canada's most influential journalists in the mid-20th century. [SEP] [CLS] Dexter spent his entire career with the Winnipeg Free Press, which he joined in 1912 at the age of 16. [SEP] [CLS] He served in Lord Strathcona's Horse from 1915 until he was invalided to England in 1917. [SEP] [CLS] For many years (1923–44) he was parliamentary reporter in Ottawa for John Dafoe's Free Press. [SEP] [CLS] During the Second World War he served as a private conduit for the government to Dafoe. [SEP] [CLS] He served as associate editor of the Free Press from 1946 to 1948, then as editor from 1948 to 1954. [SEP] [CLS] He received the Marie Moors Cabot Medal for journalism – the first Canadian so honoured – in 1946. [SEP] [CLS] Dexter was very well-connected to official Ottawa. [SEP] [CLS] He sent detailed memoranda on politics and Ottawa intrigues to his employers. [SEP] [CLS] These have been preserved at Queen's University and a collection of his wartime memoranda has been published. [SEP] [CLS]
