Myanma Airways Flight 635 was a scheduled domestic passenger flight from Yangon to Tachileik in eastern Myanmar that was operated by a Fokker F-27 Friendship (registration XY-AEN) owned by Myanmar's flag carrier Myanma Airways. [CLS] [SEP] On 24 August 1998, during its descent to Tachileik the flight went missing over a hill approximately two miles (3.2 km) from the airport. [CLS] [SEP] Search and rescue team later found the wreckage of the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] The accident killed all 36 passengers and crew. [CLS] [SEP]
