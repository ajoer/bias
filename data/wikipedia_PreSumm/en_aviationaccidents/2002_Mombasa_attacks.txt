[CLS] The 2002 Mombasa attacks was a two-pronged terrorist attack on 28 November 2002 in Mombasa, Kenya against an Israeli-owned hotel and a plane belonging to Arkia Airlines. [SEP] [CLS] An all-terrain vehicle crashed through a barrier outside the Paradise Hotel and blew up, killing 13 and injuring 80. [SEP] [CLS] At the same time, attackers fired two surface-to-air missiles at an Israeli charter plane. [SEP] [CLS] The Paradise Hotel was the only Israeli-owned hotel in the Mombasa area. [SEP] [CLS] The attacks were believed to be orchestrated by al-Qaeda operatives in Somalia in an attempt to disrupt the Israeli tourist industry on the African continent. [SEP] [CLS] Much speculation has occurred as to who the perpetrators are, but no complete list of suspects has been defined. [SEP] [CLS] The attack was the second al-Qaeda terrorist operation in Kenya, following the bombing of the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] embassy in Nairobi in 1998. [SEP] [CLS] Following the attack, the UN Security Council and other nations condemned the bombing. [SEP] [CLS]
