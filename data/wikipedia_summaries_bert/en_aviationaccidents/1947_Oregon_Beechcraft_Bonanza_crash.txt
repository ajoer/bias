[CLS] On October 28, 1947, Oregon Governor Earl Snell; second in line of succession Oregon State Senate President Marshall Cornett, and Oregon Secretary of State Robert S. [SEP] [CLS] Farrell, Jr. [SEP] [CLS] were killed in a crash of a Beechcraft Bonanza in stormy weather southwest of Dog Lake in rural Lake County, Oregon. [SEP] [CLS] The trio died along with the aircraft's pilot, who was taking them to an isolated area to hunt Canada geese. [SEP] [CLS] The tragedy propelled Oregon Speaker of the House John Hubert Hall to the Governorship of Oregon. [SEP] [CLS] The crash site was added to the National Register of Historic Places on October 18, 2018. [SEP] [CLS]
