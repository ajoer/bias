In mid-June 2016, severe weather such as heavy rainfall, thunderstorms and hail began across southern China, triggering deadly floods. [CLS] [SEP] Over the following month, additional rain events exacerbated flooding and affected more of the country. [CLS] [SEP] Areas along the Yangtze River and Huai River have been particularly hard-hit. [CLS] [SEP] An estimated 32 million people across 26 provinces were affected and more than 200 people were killed. [CLS] [SEP] 280,000 hectares (700,000 acres) of cropland was destroyed, with state damage estimates of around US$5.73 billion. [CLS] [SEP] According to Aon Benfield, the damage estimate has reached US$22 billion. [CLS] [SEP] Flooding of this magnitude was last seen in the country in 1998. [CLS] [SEP] As researches has been conducted, more than 23 600 houses have been destroyed and 3 600 refugees homeless in that situation. [CLS] [SEP]
