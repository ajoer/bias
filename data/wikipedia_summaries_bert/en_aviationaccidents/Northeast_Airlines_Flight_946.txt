Northeast Airlines Flight 946 was a domestic U.S. [CLS] [SEP] flight from Boston, Massachusetts, to Montpelier, Vermont, with a scheduled stop in Lebanon, New Hampshire, operated by Northeast Airlines. [CLS] [SEP] On October 25, 1968, some time during the evening, the Fairchild Hiller FH-227 aircraft crashed on Moose Mountain while descending on approach. [CLS] [SEP] The crash killed 32 of 42 passengers and crew. [CLS] [SEP] Of the fatalities, four were employees from the National Life Insurance Company who were returning from a business trip. [CLS] [SEP] The fatalities also included a reporter for the Barre Daily Times and six social workers of the Vermont Head Start Supplementary Training Program on a conference trip. [CLS] [SEP] Ten passengers survived the crash with minor or moderate injuries. [CLS] [SEP] After the crash, Northeast Airlines continued flight service until its merger with Delta Air Lines in the early 1970s. [CLS] [SEP]
