The October 1996 India cyclone (also known as Cyclone 05A) had an unusual and protracted path that spanned much of the northern Indian Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] It originated in a weather disturbance that formed on October 14 in the southern Bay of Bengal, off India's east coast. [CLS] [SEP] Moving westward, it struck Andhra Pradesh on October 17 as a well-defined low pressure area. [CLS] [SEP] It crossed southern India and reorganized in the Arabian Sea off the western coast of India. [CLS] [SEP] The system developed into a depression on October 22 and quickly intensified while moving northward. [CLS] [SEP] By October 24, the cyclone approached hurricane intensity as it developed an eye, reaching peak winds of at least 110 km/h (70 mph). [CLS] [SEP] On October 25, the storm abruptly stalled and weakened off Gujarat, and progressed southwestward as a minimal storm. [CLS] [SEP] It was no longer classifiable as a tropical cyclone by October 28, although its remnants persisted until November 2 when they dissipated east of Somalia. [CLS] [SEP] In southern India, the storm dropped heavy rainfall that caused severe flooding in Andhra Pradesh. [CLS] [SEP] At least 112 reservoirs and dams were breached, killing 200 people in Prakasam district. [CLS] [SEP] The floodwaters ruined about 1,600,000 ha (4,000,000 acres) of crops and damaged around 53,000 houses, leaving thousands homeless. [CLS] [SEP] The floods killed 388 people in southern India and caused US$388 million in damage. [CLS] [SEP] The storm later brushed the west coast of India, stranding 50 boats. [CLS] [SEP] The Indian military helped with relief and rescue efforts. [CLS] [SEP] Another cyclone struck Andhra Pradesh in November, causing additional damage and deaths. [CLS] [SEP]
