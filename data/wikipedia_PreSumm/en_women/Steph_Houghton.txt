[CLS] Stephanie Jayne Houghton (; born 23 April 1988) is an English footballer who both plays for and captains Manchester City and the England national team. [SEP] [CLS] At club level, Houghton started at Sunderland in her native North East England before moving on to Leeds United in 2007 then Arsenal Ladies in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] She broke into the Sunderland team as a striker before moving back into midfield and later into defence. [SEP] [CLS] Since her debut in 2007, Houghton has played over 100 times for the England national team. [SEP] [CLS] She suffered serious injuries immediately before the 2007 World Cup and Euro 2009, but recovered to play in the 2011 World Cup and Euro 2013. [SEP] [CLS] She was made England captain in January 2014. [SEP] [CLS] At the 2012 London Olympics, Houghton scored three goals in Great Britain's four games, including winners against New Zealand and Brazil. [SEP] [CLS] Houghton was appointed Member of the Order of the British Empire (MBE) in the 2016 New Year Honours for services to football. [SEP] [CLS]
