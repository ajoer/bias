[CLS] The 1917 Samoa earthquake occurred on June 26 at 05:49 UTC. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter was located in the southwest of the Samoan Islands. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake had a magnitude of Mw 8.5, or Ms 8.4, one of the strongest earthquakes ever recorded in this region.Many subsidences were reported. [SEP] [CLS] In the mountains, masses of rocks were hurled down. [SEP] [CLS] In Apia, violent shaking lasted for about a minute and half. [SEP] [CLS] In American Samoa, the LDS Church in Pago Pago and the Catholic Church in Leone were partly demolished. [SEP] [CLS] A tsunami was triggered by the earthquake, which affected both American Samoa and German Samoa, which was then under Allied occupation. [SEP] [CLS] The tsunami reached a maximum of 40 feet. [SEP] [CLS] The maximum range of the tsunami in Apia was about 80 cm. [SEP] [CLS] Many houses were destroyed, and a bridge was washed away in Palauli. [SEP] [CLS] The tsunami was also recorded in Honolulu and on the west coast of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Just on May 1, 1917, a large earthquake of magnitude Ms 8.0 occurred in the Kermadec Islands region. [SEP] [CLS] The tsunami caused by the earthquake in May also hit the Samoan Islands.The earthquake occurred to the southwest of the Samoa Islands, along the Tonga Plate boundary. [SEP] [CLS] The focal mechanism of this earthquake was unclear. [SEP] [CLS] This earthquake and the 2009 event were not thought to represent the same repeating process in a same location.
