The Great Mississippi Flood of 1927 was the most destructive river flood in the history of the United States, with 27,000 square miles (70,000 km2) inundated up to a depth of 30 feet (9 m). [CLS] [SEP] To try to prevent future floods, the federal government built the world's longest system of levees and floodways. [CLS] [SEP] Ninety-four percent of more than 630,000 people affected by the flood lived in the states of Arkansas, Mississippi, and Louisiana, most in the Mississippi Delta. [CLS] [SEP] More than 200,000 African Americans were displaced from their homes along the Lower Mississippi River and had to live for lengthy periods in relief camps. [CLS] [SEP] As a result of this disruption, many joined the Great Migration from the south to northern and midwestern industrial cities rather than return to rural agricultural labor. [CLS] [SEP] This massive population movement increased from World War II until 1970. [CLS] [SEP]
