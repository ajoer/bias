The June 2008 Midwestern United States floods were flooding events which affected portions of the Midwestern United States. [CLS] [SEP] After months of heavy precipitation, a number of rivers overflowed their banks for several weeks at a time and broke through levees at numerous locations. [CLS] [SEP] Flooding continued into July. [CLS] [SEP] States affected by the flooding included Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri and Wisconsin. [CLS] [SEP] The American Red Cross assisted the victims of flooding and tornadoes across seven states and the National Guard was mobilized to assist in disaster relief and evacuation. [CLS] [SEP] Flooding continued as long as two weeks with central Iowa and Cedar Rapids being hardest hit. [CLS] [SEP] The upper Mississippi Valley experienced flooding in Missouri and Illinois as the region's estuaries drained the floodwater into the river. [CLS] [SEP] The flood left thirteen dead and damage region-wide was estimated to be in the tens of billions of dollars. [CLS] [SEP]
