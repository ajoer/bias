[CLS] Teunis (Ton) Sijbrands (Amsterdam, December 15, 1949) is a Dutch international draughts player who became world champion in 1972. [SEP] [CLS] The next year, he successfully defended his title in a match against Andris Andreiko. [SEP] [CLS] In 1967, 1969, 1970, 1971, 1973 and 1988 he was the national Dutch champion. [SEP] [CLS] Sijbrands also had a weekly column about draughts in the Dutch national newspaper de Volkskrant and wrote several books about the game. [SEP] [CLS] He also made major achievements in simultaneous blindfold draughts. [SEP] [CLS] In 2002 he won 17 games and played 5 draws from a total 22 games, which equals a score of approximately 88%. [SEP] [CLS] On December 18, 2004 in the Dutch village Lutten Sijbrands improved the world record for blindfold simultaneous draughts. [SEP] [CLS] In this attempt that lasted 24 hours, he won 20 out of 24 games; the other 4 were draws, which equals a score of about 92%. [SEP] [CLS] At his next attempt, on October 5, 2007 at Tilburg University, he broke his own record again. [SEP] [CLS] This time he played 25 games, winning 21 and drawing 4. [SEP] [CLS] After losing the record to his compatriot Erno Prosman, Sijbrands regained it in September 2009 when he played 28 simultaneous games, winning 18, drawing 7 and losing 3. [SEP] [CLS] Prosman recovered the world record on July 7, 2012 in Gouda, Netherlands. [SEP] [CLS] He played 30 games, winning 17, losing 5 with 8 draws, a score of exactly 70%. [SEP] [CLS] Sijbrands regained the world record once more on December 19-21, 2014 in Hilversum, Netherlands. [SEP] [CLS] He played 32 games with 14 wins and 18 draws. [SEP] [CLS]
