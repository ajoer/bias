[CLS] In May 2012, two major earthquakes struck Northern Italy, causing 27 deaths and widespread damage. [SEP] [CLS] The first earthquake, registering magnitude 6.1, struck in the Emilia-Romagna region, about 36 kilometres (22 mi) north of the city of Bologna, on 20 May at 04:03 local time (02:03 UTC). [SEP] [CLS] The epicentre was between Finale Emilia, Bondeno and Sermide. [SEP] [CLS] Two aftershocks of magnitude 5.2 occurred, one approximately an hour after the main event and another approximately eleven hours after the main event. [SEP] [CLS] Seven people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] A magnitude 5.8 earthquake struck the same area nine days later, on 29 May, causing an additional twenty deaths and widespread damage, particularly to buildings already weakened by the 20 May earthquake. [SEP] [CLS] The epicentre was in Medolla: the quake itself occurred at a depth of about 10 kilometres (6.2 mi)
