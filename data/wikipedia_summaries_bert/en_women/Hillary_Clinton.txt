Hillary Diane Rodham Clinton (née Rodham; born October 26, 1947) is an American politician, diplomat, lawyer, writer, and public speaker who served as the 67th United States Secretary of State from 2009 to 2013, as a United States Senator from New York from 2001 to 2009, and as First Lady of the United States from 1993 to 2001. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton became the first woman to be nominated for president of the United States by a major political party when she won the Democratic Party nomination in 2016. [CLS] [SEP] She was the first woman to win the popular vote in an American presidential election, which she lost to Donald Trump. [CLS] [SEP] Raised in the Chicago suburb of Park Ridge, Clinton graduated from Wellesley College in 1969, and earned a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School in 1973. [CLS] [SEP] After serving as a congressional legal counsel, she moved to Arkansas and married future president Bill Clinton in 1975; the two had met at Yale. [CLS] [SEP] In 1977, she co-founded Arkansas Advocates for Children and Families. [CLS] [SEP] She was appointed the first female chair of the Legal Services Corporation in 1978, and became the first female partner at Little Rock's Rose Law Firm the following year. [CLS] [SEP] The National Law Journal twice listed her as one of the hundred most influential lawyers in America. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton was the first lady of Arkansas from 1979 to 1981 and again from 1983 to 1992. [CLS] [SEP] As First Lady of the United States, Clinton advocated for healthcare reform. [CLS] [SEP] In 1994, her major initiative—the Clinton health care plan—failed to gain approval from Congress. [CLS] [SEP] In 1997 and 1999, Clinton played a leading role in advocating the creation of the State Children's Health Insurance Program, the Adoption and Safe Families Act, and the Foster Care Independence Act. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton advocated for gender equality at the 1995 UN conference on women. [CLS] [SEP] Her marital relationship came under public scrutiny during the Lewinsky scandal, which led her to issue a statement that reaffirmed her commitment to the marriage. [CLS] [SEP] In 2000, Clinton was elected as the first female senator from New York. [CLS] [SEP] She was re-elected in 2006. [CLS] [SEP] During her Senate tenure, Clinton advocated for medical benefits for first responders whose health was damaged in the September 11 attacks. [CLS] [SEP] She supported the resolution authorizing the Iraq War in 2002 but opposed the surge of U.S. [CLS] [SEP] troops in 2007. [CLS] [SEP] In 2008, Clinton ran for president but was defeated by eventual winner Barack Obama in the Democratic primaries. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton was U.S. [CLS] [SEP] secretary of state in the Obama Administration from 2009 to 2013. [CLS] [SEP] During her tenure, Clinton responded to the Arab Spring by advocating military intervention in Libya. [CLS] [SEP] She was harshly criticized by Republicans for the failure to prevent or adequately respond to the 2012 Benghazi attack. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton helped to organize a diplomatic isolation and a regime of international sanctions against Iran in an effort to force it to curtail its nuclear program; this effort eventually led to the multinational Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action agreement in 2015. [CLS] [SEP] Her use of a private e-mail server during her time as Secretary of State was the subject of intense scrutiny; while no charges were filed against Clinton, the e-mail controversy was the single most covered topic during the 2016 presidential election. [CLS] [SEP] Upon leaving her Cabinet position after Obama's first term, she wrote her fifth book, and undertook speaking engagements. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton made a second presidential run in 2016. [CLS] [SEP] After winning the Democratic nomination, she ran in the general election with Virginia Senator Tim Kaine as her running mate. [CLS] [SEP] Clinton lost the presidential election to Republican opponent Donald Trump in the Electoral College despite winning a plurality of the popular vote. [CLS] [SEP] Following her loss, she wrote her third memoir, What Happened, and launched Onward Together, a political action organization dedicated to fundraising for progressive political groups. [CLS] [SEP] She is the current chancellor of Queen's University Belfast in Belfast, Northern Ireland. [CLS] [SEP]
