[CLS] The Linate Airport disaster occurred on 8 October 2001 at Linate Airport in Milan, Italy, when Scandinavian Airlines Flight 686, a McDonnell Douglas MD-87 airliner carrying 110 people bound for Copenhagen, Denmark, collided on take-off with a Cessna Citation CJ2 business jet carrying four people bound for Paris, France. [SEP] [CLS] All 114 people on both aircraft were killed, as well as four people on the ground. [SEP] [CLS] The subsequent investigation determined that the collision was caused by a number of nonfunctioning and nonconforming safety systems, standards, and procedures at the airport. [SEP] [CLS] It remains the deadliest accident in Italian aviation history. [SEP] [CLS]
