[CLS] Turkish Airlines Flight 1476 (TK1476) was a Turkish Airlines Boeing 737-4Y0 flying from Tirana to Istanbul that was hijacked by Hakan Ekinci in Greek airspace on 3 October 2006. [SEP] [CLS] Ekinci demanded to go to Rome to speak with the Pope, but Greek and Italian F-16 Fighting Falcon jets intercepted and escorted the aircraft until it landed in Brindisi, Italy. [SEP] [CLS] Nobody was harmed, and Ekinci was jailed in Italy while awaiting trial. [SEP] [CLS] At first, the hijacking incident was linked with the envisaged visit of Pope Benedict XVI to Turkey, but later it was ascertained that Ekinci was seeking to request political asylum from Italy. [SEP] [CLS] On 4 October a Turkish Airlines plane was sent to take the other passengers to Istanbul. [SEP] [CLS]
