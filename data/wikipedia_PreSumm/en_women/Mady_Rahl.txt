[CLS] Mady Rahl (January 3, 1915 – August 29, 2009) was a German stage and film actress. [SEP] [CLS] Born Edith Gertrud Meta Raschke in Neukölln, now part of Berlin, Rahl trained as an actress and dancer. [SEP] [CLS] In 1935 she made her stage debut in Leipzig under the direction of Douglas Sirk and started her film career in 1936 with the movie The Mysterious Mister X. [SEP] [CLS] With her role in the circus drama Truxa (1937) Rahl became known to a wider audience. [SEP] [CLS] She appeared in approximately 90 movies, several of them for UFA. [SEP] [CLS] In later years, she appeared frequently on television, while also pursuing her career in the theatre. [SEP] [CLS] She was also the German voice of many cartoon characters and of Lucille Ball. [SEP] [CLS] Rahl's first marriage was to financier Theodor Reimers, her second was to producer Wilhem Sperber, and her third was to architect Werner Bürkle. [SEP] [CLS] All three marriages ended in divorce, and she had no children. [SEP] [CLS] Late in life she painted in the Impressionist style. [SEP] [CLS] Whilst she died of cancer, Rahl was also almost blind and suffering from dementia. [SEP] [CLS] She lived in a retirement home in Munich, where her caretaker was Thomas Speyerer.Her grave is in the Nordfriedhof Cemetery in Munich (plot 178-U-66), where her sister Ellen had been buried in 1995.
