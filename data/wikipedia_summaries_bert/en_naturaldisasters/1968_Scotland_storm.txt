[CLS] The 1968 Hurricane (or Hurricane Low Q) was a deadly storm that moved through the Central Belt of Scotland during mid January 1968. [SEP] [CLS] It was described as Central Scotland's worst natural disaster since records began and the worst gale in the United Kingdom. [SEP] [CLS] Some said that the damage resembled what happened during the Clydebank Blitz in 1941. [SEP] [CLS] 20 people died from the storm, with 9 dead in Glasgow. [SEP] [CLS] 700 people were left homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Such high wind speeds in an urban area were equivalent to those witnessed in Paris during Cyclone Lothar in 1999.A 134 mph (216 km/h) wind gust was recorded at Great Dun Fell in Cumbria, England. [SEP] [CLS] At the time this was the strongest wind gust ever recorded in the United Kingdom, though this was superseded in 1986 when a 173 mph (278 km/h) gust was recorded at Cairn Gorm.
