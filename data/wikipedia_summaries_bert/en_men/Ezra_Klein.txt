[CLS] Ezra Klein is an American journalist, blogger, and political commentator who co-founded Vox, where he is currently editor-at-large. [SEP] [CLS] He was previously a blogger and columnist for The Washington Post and an associate editor of The American Prospect. [SEP] [CLS] He has served as a contributor to Bloomberg News and MSNBC. [SEP] [CLS] At The Washington Post, he managed a branded blog, Wonkblog, which featured his writing and the writing of other policy reporters. [SEP] [CLS] Issues discussed in the blog included health care and budget policy. [SEP] [CLS] He wrote a primer on policy called Wonkbook, which was delivered by e-mail and on his blog each morning. [SEP] [CLS] In January 2014, Klein left The Washington Post and co-founded Vox, a website for explanatory news owned by Vox Media. [SEP] [CLS] He served as the editor-in-chief of Vox until September 2017, when he transitioned into his current role. [SEP] [CLS] His debut book, Why We're Polarized, was published in January 2020.
