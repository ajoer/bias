[CLS] Graham Taylor, OBE (15 September 1944 – 12 January 2017) was an English football player, manager, pundit and chairman of Watford Football Club. [SEP] [CLS] He was the manager of the England national football team from 1990 to 1993, and also managed Lincoln City, Watford, Aston Villa and Wolverhampton Wanderers. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Worksop, Nottinghamshire, Taylor grew up in Scunthorpe, Lincolnshire, which he regarded as his hometown. [SEP] [CLS] The son of a sports journalist who worked on the Scunthorpe Evening Telegraph, Taylor found his love of football in the stands of the Old Show Ground watching Scunthorpe United. [SEP] [CLS] He became a player, playing at full back for Grimsby Town and Lincoln City. [SEP] [CLS] After retiring as a player through injury in 1972, Taylor became a manager and coach. [SEP] [CLS] He won the Fourth Division title with Lincoln in 1976, before moving to Watford in 1977. [SEP] [CLS] He took Watford from the Fourth Division to the First in five years. [SEP] [CLS] Under Taylor, Watford were First Division runners-up in 1982–83, and FA Cup finalists in 1984. [SEP] [CLS] Taylor took over at Aston Villa in 1987, leading the club to promotion in 1988 and 2nd place in the First Division in 1989–90. [SEP] [CLS] In July 1990, he became the manager of the England team. [SEP] [CLS] England qualified for the 1992 European Championships, but were knocked out in the group stages. [SEP] [CLS] Taylor resigned in November 1993, after England failed to qualify for the 1994 FIFA World Cup in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Taylor faced heavy criticism from fans and media during his tenure as an England manager and earned additional public interest and scrutiny when a television documentary which he had permitted to film the failed campaign from behind the scenes, An Impossible Job, aired in 1994. [SEP] [CLS] Taylor returned to club management in March 1994 with Wolverhampton Wanderers. [SEP] [CLS] After one season at Molineux, he returned to Watford, and led the club to the Premier League in 1999 after back-to-back promotions. [SEP] [CLS] His last managerial role was manager of Aston Villa, to which he returned in 2002. [SEP] [CLS] He left at the end of the 2002–03 season. [SEP] [CLS] Taylor served as Watford's chairman from 2009 until 2012 where he continued to hold the position of honorary life-president. [SEP] [CLS] He also worked as a pundit for BBC Radio Five Live. [SEP] [CLS]
