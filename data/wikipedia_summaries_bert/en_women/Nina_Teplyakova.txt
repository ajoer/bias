Anatol Peresselenzeff (Russian: Анатолий Андреевич Переселенцев; 1889–1956) was a pioneer Russian rower and rowing coach. [CLS] [SEP] Competing in single sculls he won both the Russian (1908, 1909 and 1914) and French championships. [CLS] [SEP] In 1913 he also won the European title in double sculls, rowing with Hermann Barrelet; he was disqualified in the single sculls race, together with Giuseppe Sinigaglia.Peresselenzeff was born in a wealthy family and took up rowing in 1903. [CLS] [SEP] In 1909 his parents sent him to Germany and France to study commerce. [CLS] [SEP] He returned to Russia in 1914 and retired from competitions in 1923, after winning his last Russian title, in the double sculls with Yakov Shestoperov. [CLS] [SEP] He then coached rowers at Spartak Moscow and Russian State University of Physical Education, Sport, Youth and Tourism, using French textbooks which he translated into Russian. [CLS] [SEP] In 1941 he was arrested as part of Stalin's political repressions and released in 1948. [CLS] [SEP] He died in 1956.Peresselenzeff was married twice, first to Nina Teplyakova who was a leading Soviet tennis player, and then to Katya, a woman 15 years his junior. [CLS] [SEP]
