Will Claye (born June 13, 1991) is an American track and field athlete of Sierra Leonean descent who competes in the long jump and triple jump. [CLS] [SEP] He won a bronze medal in 2011 World Championships in Athletics and the gold medals at the 2012 IAAF World Indoor Championships and 2018 IAAF World Indoor Championships. [CLS] [SEP] In his Olympic debut at the 2012 Summer Olympics, Claye won a bronze medal in long jump and a silver medal in triple jump. [CLS] [SEP] He repeated his silver medal in the triple jump four years later. [CLS] [SEP] His personal best of 18.14 m (59 ft 6 in), set at the Jim Bush Southern California USATF Championships in Long Beach on June 29, 2019 ranks him as the No. [CLS] [SEP] 3 triple jumper of all time. [CLS] [SEP] Will was two-time Arizona Interscholastic Association high school champion in the triple jump, establishing a new state record of over 50 feet. [CLS] [SEP] He attended Mountain Pointe High School in Phoenix, Arizona, where he was named to the 2008 USA Today's All-USA Team in both jumps. [CLS] [SEP] Claye enrolled early at the University of Oklahoma, but later transferred to the University of Florida. [CLS] [SEP] Will Claye later went on to record the rap song "IDGAF" with YG.
