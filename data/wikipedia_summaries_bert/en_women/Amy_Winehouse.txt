[CLS] Amy Jade Winehouse (14 September 1983 â€“ 23 July 2011) was an English singer and songwriter. [SEP] [CLS] She was known for her deep, expressive contralto vocals and her eclectic mix of musical genres, including soul, rhythm and blues and jazz. [SEP] [CLS] A member of the National Youth Jazz Orchestra during her youth, Winehouse signed to Simon Fuller's 19 Management in 2002 and soon recorded a number of songs before signing a publishing deal with EMI. [SEP] [CLS] She also formed a working relationship with producer Salaam Remi through these record publishers. [SEP] [CLS] Winehouse's debut album, Frank, was released in 2003. [SEP] [CLS] Many of the album's songs were influenced by jazz and, apart from two covers, were co-written by Winehouse. [SEP] [CLS] Frank was a critical success in the UK and was nominated for the Mercury Prize. [SEP] [CLS] The song "Stronger Than Me" won her the Ivor Novello Award for Best Contemporary Song from the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers, and Authors. [SEP] [CLS] Winehouse released her follow-up album, Back to Black, in 2006, which went on to become an international success and one of the best-selling albums in UK history. [SEP] [CLS] At the 2007 Brit Awards it was nominated for British Album of the Year, and she received the award for British Female Solo Artist. [SEP] [CLS] The song "Rehab" won her a second Ivor Novello Award. [SEP] [CLS] At the 50th Grammy Awards in 2008, she won five awards, tying the then record for the most wins by a female artist in a single night and becoming the first British woman to win five Grammys, including three of the General Field "Big Four" Grammy Awards: Best New Artist, Record of the Year and Song of the Year (for "Rehab"), as well as Best Pop Vocal Album. [SEP] [CLS] Winehouse was plagued by drug and alcohol addiction. [SEP] [CLS] She died of alcohol poisoning on 23 July 2011, at the age of 27. [SEP] [CLS] After her death, Back to Black temporarily became the UK's best-selling album of the 21st century. [SEP] [CLS] VH1 ranked Winehouse 26th on their list of the 100 Greatest Women in Music.
