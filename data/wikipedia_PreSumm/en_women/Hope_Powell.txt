[CLS] Hope Patricia Powell, CBE (born 8 December 1966) is an English former international footballer and women's first-team manager of Brighton & Hove Albion. [SEP] [CLS] She was the coach of the England women's national football team and the Great Britain women's Olympic football team until August 2013. [SEP] [CLS] As a player, Powell won 66 caps for England, mainly as an attacking midfielder, scoring 35 goals. [SEP] [CLS] She made her England debut at the age of 16, and went on to play in the 1995 FIFA Women's World Cup, England's first World Cup appearance. [SEP] [CLS] She was also vice-captain of her country. [SEP] [CLS] At club level Powell played in four FA Women's Cup finals and captained Croydon to a League and Cup double in 1996. [SEP] [CLS] The Football Association (FA) appointed Powell as England's first-ever full-time national coach in 1998. [SEP] [CLS] She led the team at the 2001, 2005, 2009 and 2013 editions of the UEFA Women's Championship. [SEP] [CLS] After failing to qualify in 2003, she guided England to the quarter-finals of the FIFA Women's World Cup in 2007 and 2011. [SEP] [CLS] England's best results, reaching the final of the UEFA Women's Championship in 1984 and 2009, both featured Powell. [SEP] [CLS] She was a player at the former and coach at the latter. [SEP] [CLS] As well as managing the England senior team, Powell oversaw the whole structure from Under-15s to the Under-23s, a coach mentoring scheme and The FA’s National Player Development Centre at Loughborough University. [SEP] [CLS] In May 2009 Powell's administration implemented central contracts, to help players focus on full-time training and playing, without having to fit it around full-time employment. [SEP] [CLS] Initially 17 players signed contracts. [SEP] [CLS] In 2003 Powell became the first woman to achieve the UEFA Pro Licence—the highest coaching qualification available. [SEP] [CLS]
