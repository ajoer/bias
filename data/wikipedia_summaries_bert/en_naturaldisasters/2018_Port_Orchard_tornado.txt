The 2018 Port Orchard tornado was a rare strong tornado that struck the city of Port Orchard, Washington on December 18, 2018. [CLS] [SEP] The National Weather Service office in Seattle rated the tornado as an EF2 following an on-site survey of the storm damage the day after the event. [CLS] [SEP] There were no fatalities or major injuries reported. [CLS] [SEP] This was the first tornado to have touched down in Western Washington since an EF0 tornado in 2017 near Monroe. [CLS] [SEP] Damage was most pronounced near the South Kitsap Regional Park.The tornado was the largest recorded in Kitsap County, and the 12th recorded F2/EF2 tornado to touch down in Washington since 1950. [CLS] [SEP] Washington state averages 2.5 tornadoes annually, but only 0.1 tornadoes in the month of December; the majority of these are rated EF0. [CLS] [SEP]
