The 1833 Kunming earthquake was an 8.0 Mw earthquake that struck the provincial capital Kunming in Yunnan, China on September 6, 1833. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake had its epicenter along the Xiaojiang Fault near the town of Songming, approximately 50 km (31 mi) northeast of Kunming's city centre. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake destroyed many buildings, homes, and temples in Kunming and the nearby countryside. [CLS] [SEP] More than 6,000 people died as a result of the earthquake and another 80,000 were displaced. [CLS] [SEP] The Qing government at the time provided relief to the region and used the event to strengthen its administration in the province. [CLS] [SEP] The 1833 Kunming earthquake was the largest magnitude earthquake in Yunnan's recorded history. [CLS] [SEP]
