Tümen Zasagt Khan (Mongolian: Түмэн засагт хаан, Tümen zasagt xaan; Chinese: 圖們) was a 16th-century Mongol Khagan of the Northern Yuan dynasty based in Mongolia who reigned from 1558 to 1592. [CLS] [SEP] He was the successor of Darayisung Gödeng Khan and had direct rule over the Chahar. [CLS] [SEP] It was during his rule that the Mongols conquered Daur and Evenks. [CLS] [SEP] Unlike his father, he succeeded in uniting the entire Mongol people, including the Western Mongols, with little bloodshed. [CLS] [SEP] Tümen, who was born in 1539, was the first of three sons of Darayisung Gödeng Khan. [CLS] [SEP] By being recognized as Khagan, Altan validated his authority. [CLS] [SEP] However, coordinating Altan Khan's actions, Tümen won over Uriyangkhai and Daur Mongols. [CLS] [SEP] His relatives, Abtai Khan and Khutughtai Sechen Khung Taiji, brought a large portion of the Four Oirats back into the Mongol fold. [CLS] [SEP] In addition to his successful invasions of Ming China, Tümen conquered Koko Nur and appointed his son ruler there. [CLS] [SEP] Tümen and other Mongol princes decided to adopt Tibetan Buddhism. [CLS] [SEP] In 1576, Tümen was converted by Ilduni Sanggiduktshi Garma Lama into Buddhism. [CLS] [SEP] He assembled the Six Tumens, and codified laws. [CLS] [SEP] He made reforms on state laws and exempted the Mongol nobles from some taxes. [CLS] [SEP] He compiled a new code that was supposed to be based on Yekhe Zasag of Genghis Khan. [CLS] [SEP] Thenceforwards he was called Jasagtu, who made peace with the Right Wing Tumens and gave their leaders official titles. [CLS] [SEP] He compelled the three Jurchen tribes such as Jurjis in Manchuria and Yekhe Tungusians, to pay tribute.Zasaghtu Khan died in 1592. [CLS] [SEP]
