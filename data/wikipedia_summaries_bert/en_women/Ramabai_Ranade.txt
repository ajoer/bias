[SEP] [CLS] At the age of 11, she was married to Justice Mahadev Govind Ranade, who was a distinguished Indian scholar and social reformer. [SEP] [CLS] In that era of social inequality, women were not allowed to go to school and become literate. [SEP] [CLS] Ramabai, soon after her marriage, started to learn reading and writing with strong support and encouragement from Mahadev Govind Ranade. [SEP] [CLS] Starting with her native language Marathi, she strove hard to master English and Bengali. [SEP] [CLS] Inspired by her husband, Ramabai started 'Hindu Ladies Social Club' in Mumbai to develop public speaking among women. [SEP] [CLS] Ramabai was also a founder and President of 'Seva Sadan Society' in Pune. [SEP] [CLS] Ramabai devoted her life to the improvement of women's lives. [SEP] [CLS] Ramabai Ranade with her husband and other colleagues established in 1886 the first girls' high school in Pune, the renowned Huzurpaga. [SEP] [CLS]
