[CLS] Emperor Chong of Han (Chinese: 漢冲帝; pinyin: Hàn Chōng Dì; Wade–Giles: Han Ch'ung-ti; 143 – 15 February 145) was an emperor of the Chinese Han Dynasty and the eighth emperor of the Eastern Han period. [SEP] [CLS] Emperor Chong was the only son of Emperor Shun. [SEP] [CLS] He ascended the throne at the age of one and reigned less than six months. [SEP] [CLS] During his reign, Empress Dowager Liang and her brother Liang Ji presided over all government affairs. [SEP] [CLS] While the empress dowager herself appeared to be open-minded and honest, she overly trusted her corrupt brother, and this led to corruptions and as a result the peasants suffered greatly. [SEP] [CLS] Emperor Chong died in 145. [SEP] [CLS] He was just two years old. [SEP] [CLS]
