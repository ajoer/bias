[CLS] Hurricane Ida was the strongest landfalling tropical cyclone during the 2009 Atlantic hurricane season, crossing the coastline of Nicaragua with winds of 80 mph (130 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] The remnants of the storm became a powerful nor'easter that caused widespread damage along coastal areas of the Mid-Atlantic States. [SEP] [CLS] Ida formed on November 4 in the southwestern Caribbean, and within 24 hours struck the Nicaragua coast with winds of 80 mph (130 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] It weakened significantly over land, although it restrengthened in the Yucatán Channel to peak winds of 105 mph (165 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Ida weakened and became an extratropical cyclone in the northern Gulf of Mexico before spreading across the southeastern United States. [SEP] [CLS] The remnants of Ida contributed to the formation of a nor'easter that significantly affected the eastern coast of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Numerous watches and warnings were issued during the hurricane's existence. [SEP] [CLS] Areas from Panama to Maine were affected by either the storm or the nor'easter low. [SEP] [CLS] In Nicaragua, nearly 3,000 people evacuated coastal areas ahead of the storm. [SEP] [CLS] More extensive evacuations in Mexico relocated over 100,000 residents and tourists. [SEP] [CLS] In the United States, several parishes in Louisiana and counties in Alabama and Florida declared a state of emergency because of fear of significant damage from the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Officials issued voluntary evacuations and most schools and non-emergency offices in the region closed. [SEP] [CLS] In Central America, Ida brought heavy rainfall to parts of Costa Rica, Nicaragua and Honduras. [SEP] [CLS] Several people were reported missing in Nicaragua, however post-storm reports denied these claims. [SEP] [CLS] Thousands of buildings collapsed or sustained damage and roughly 40,000 people were left homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Damages from Ida in Nicaragua amounted to at least 46 million córdoba ($2.12 million US$). [SEP] [CLS] Aside from heavy rainfall in Mexico and Cuba, little impact from Ida was reported in either country. [SEP] [CLS] In the United States, the remnants caused substantial damage, mainly in the Mid-Atlantic States. [SEP] [CLS] One person was killed by Ida after drowning in rough seas, while six others lost their lives in various incidents related to the nor'easter. [SEP] [CLS] Widespread heavy rainfall led to numerous reports of flash flooding in areas from Mississippi to Maine. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, the two systems caused nearly $300 million in damage throughout the country. [SEP] [CLS]
