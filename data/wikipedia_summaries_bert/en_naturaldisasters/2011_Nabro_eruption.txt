The 2011 Nabro eruption was an eruption of the Nabro stratovolcano in the Southern Red Sea Region of Eritrea, which began on 12 June 2011 after a series of earthquakes. [CLS] [SEP] The eruption killed seven and possibly a further 31 people and is estimated to be the highest altitude injection of sulfur dioxide (SO2) ever observed by satellite. [CLS] [SEP] The Mount Pinatubo eruption 20 years earlier emitted ten times more SO2. [CLS] [SEP] The ash cloud from the eruption reached altitudes which disrupted airline traffic in the region. [CLS] [SEP] Until the eruption began, the volcano had no records of historical eruptions. [CLS] [SEP]
