[CLS] Ernesto Teodoro Moneta (September 20, 1833 in Milan, Kingdom of Lombardy–Venetia – February 10, 1918) was an Italian journalist, nationalist, revolutionary soldier and later a pacifist and Nobel Peace Prize Laureate. [SEP] [CLS] He adopted the motto In varietate unitas! [SEP] [CLS] which later inspired Motto of the European Union. [SEP] [CLS] At age 15, Moneta participated in the "Five Days of Milan" (1848 uprising against Austrian rule). [SEP] [CLS] He later attended the military academy in Ivrea. [SEP] [CLS] In 1859 he joined Garibaldi's Expedition of the Thousand, and also fought in the ranks of the Italian army against the Austrians in 1866. [SEP] [CLS] Subsequently, he became an international peace activist, despite his strong Italian nationalism. [SEP] [CLS] Between 1867 and 1896 he was editor of the Milan democratic paper Il Secolo, published by Edoardo Sonzogno. [SEP] [CLS] In 1887 he founded the Lombard Association for Peace and Arbitration (Unione Lombarda per la Pace e l'Arbitrato), which called for disarmament and envisaged the creation of a League of Nations and Permanent Court of Arbitration. [SEP] [CLS] He won (with Louis Renault) the Nobel Peace Prize in 1907. [SEP] [CLS] In the last years of his life, however, Moneta's Italian nationalism reasserted itself and got the better of his pacifism. [SEP] [CLS] He expressed public support for both the Italian Conquest of Libya in 1912 and Italy's entry into the First World War in 1915. [SEP] [CLS]
