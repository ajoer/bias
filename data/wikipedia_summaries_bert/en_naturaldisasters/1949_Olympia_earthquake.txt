[CLS] The 1949 Olympia earthquake occurred on April 13 at 11:55:44 local time with a magnitude of 7.1 and a maximum Mercalli Intensity of VIII (Severe). [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was triggered more than 50 kilometres (31 mi) deep. [SEP] [CLS] The shock was located in the area between Olympia and Tacoma, and was felt throughout the state, as well as parts of Oregon, British Columbia, Idaho, and Montana. [SEP] [CLS] It is the largest recorded earthquake to occur in the Puget Sound region of Washington. [SEP] [CLS] Eight people were killed, including young crossing guard Marvin Klegman who shielded a second-grader from falling bricks. [SEP] [CLS] At least 64 people were injured, and the total damage is estimated at $25 million. [SEP] [CLS]
