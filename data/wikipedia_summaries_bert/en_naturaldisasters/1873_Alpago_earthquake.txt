The 1873 Alpago earthquake occurred near the Italian city of Belluno on June 29 in the geologically active Alpago Valley of the Veneto region; the zone is rated as two on a four-degree risk scale (one being the highest). [CLS] [SEP] The 6.3 magnitude quake was rated as IX–X (Violent–Extreme) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] Intensities greater than VII (Very strong) were confined to the provinces of Belluno, Treviso and Pordenone. [CLS] [SEP]
