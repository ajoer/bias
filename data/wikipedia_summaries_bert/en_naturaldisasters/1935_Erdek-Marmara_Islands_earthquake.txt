[CLS] The 1935 Erdek–Marmara Islands earthquake occurred at 16:41:30 local time 4 January on the islands of Marmara and Avşa off Erdek in the Sea of Marmara, Turkey. [SEP] [CLS] It had a moment magnitude of 6.4 and a maximum felt intensity of VIII (Severe) on the Mercalli intensity scale, causing 5 deaths and 30 injuries. [SEP] [CLS] Three aftershocks occurred in conjunction with this destructive earthquake that was felt strongly in Istanbul, Edirne and Izmir. [SEP] [CLS] The villages Gündoğdu, Çınarlı, and Asmalı on Marmara Island were completely flattened, and the island's town center was damaged. [SEP] [CLS] On the neighboring Avşa Island, 128 houses in the Türkeli village and all the houses of Yiğitler village were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] On Paşalimanı Island, the villages Poyraz and Harmanlı were completely destroyed and the villages Paşalimanı and Balıklı were partly damaged. [SEP] [CLS] The foghorn building on Hayırsız Island was demolished and rocks at the shore fell into the sea.The submarine communications cable running across the Sea of Marmara remained intact. [SEP] [CLS] In the villages Narlı and Ocaklar on the Kapıdağ Peninsula, and as well as around Avşa, fountains dried and emitted noise. [SEP] [CLS] Since the earthquake occurred in the daytime, fatalities were few. [SEP] [CLS] Five people were killed and 30 were injured. [SEP] [CLS] Aftershocks continued until March 7, 1935.
