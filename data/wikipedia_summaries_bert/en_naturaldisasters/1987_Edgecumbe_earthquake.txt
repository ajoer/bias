The 1987 Edgecumbe earthquake measured 6.5 on the moment magnitude scale and struck the Bay of Plenty region of New Zealand at 1:42 pm on 2 March. [CLS] [SEP] The hypocentre was at a shallow depth of 8 km. [CLS] [SEP] The epicentre was 2.24 kilometres (1.39 mi) south-south-east of the town of Matata, and 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) north-north-west of Edgecumbe, on the Rangitaiki Plains (the floodplain of the Rangitaiki River, the Tarawera River and the Whakatane River). [CLS] [SEP] It was the most damaging earthquake New Zealand had experienced since the 1968 Inangahua earthquake. [CLS] [SEP]
