[CLS] Mathilde Gerg (born 19 October 1975 in Lenggries, Upper Bavaria) is a German former alpine skier. [SEP] [CLS] She was Olympic Champion in the Slalom at the 1998 Winter Olympics, a highly surprising win as most of her career she was known as predominantly a speed specialist; with 1998 being the one year of her career she was a top slalom contender with 2 wins and numerous podiums on the World Cup, finishing 3rd in points for the season. [SEP] [CLS] At the World Championships she was bronze medallist in Combined and Super-G at Sestriere 1997, Bronze medallist in Super-G at St. [SEP] [CLS] Anton 2001, and gold medallist in Nation Team Event at Bormio in 2005. [SEP] [CLS] In 1994 Gerg was Junior World Champion and in 1997 and 2002 she won the World Cup in her favourite discipline, Super-G. [SEP] [CLS] Her 1997 Super G season title came due to decisive points leader Pernilla Wiberg going off course in the final Super G of the season. [SEP] [CLS] She also has twice won the combined season Crystal Globe, and twice narrowly missed the downhill season title, finishing 2nd in the points in both 2004 and 2005. [SEP] [CLS] She was 2nd in the Overall title standings in 1999, losing the Overall title to Alexandra Meissnitzer. [SEP] [CLS] Gerg retired from professional skiing, because of severe injuries, in November 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Her cousin, Annemarie Gerg, was also a member of the German alpine ski team. [SEP] [CLS]
