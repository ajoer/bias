[CLS] Hurricane Jeanne was a Category 3 hurricane that struck the Caribbean and the Eastern United States in September 2004. [SEP] [CLS] It was the deadliest hurricane in the Atlantic basin since Mitch in 1998. [SEP] [CLS] It was the tenth named storm, the seventh hurricane, and the fifth major hurricane of the season, as well as the third hurricane and fourth named storm of the season to make landfall in Florida. [SEP] [CLS] After wreaking havoc on Hispaniola, Jeanne struggled to reorganize, eventually strengthening and performing a complete loop over the open Atlantic. [SEP] [CLS] It headed westwards, strengthening into a Category 3 hurricane and passing over the islands of Great Abaco and Grand Bahama in the Bahamas on September 25. [SEP] [CLS] Jeanne made landfall later in the day in Florida just two miles (three kilometers) from where Hurricane Frances had struck a mere three weeks earlier. [SEP] [CLS] Building on the rainfall of Frances and Hurricane Ivan, Jeanne brought near-record flood levels as far north as West Virginia and New Jersey before its remnants turned east into the open Atlantic.
