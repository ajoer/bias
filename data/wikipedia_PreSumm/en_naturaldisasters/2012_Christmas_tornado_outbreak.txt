[CLS] Near the end of 2012, a massive storm complex developed that produced both a tornado outbreak and a blizzard across the southern and eastern United States. [SEP] [CLS] On Christmas Day 2012 (December 25), a tornado outbreak occurred across Southern United States. [SEP] [CLS] This severe weather/tornado event affected the United States Gulf Coast and southern East Coast over a two-day span. [SEP] [CLS] It occurred in conjunction with a much larger winter storm event that brought blizzard conditions to much of the interior United States. [SEP] [CLS] In total, 31 tornadoes were confirmed by the National Weather Service in five states from Texas to North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] All but one of the tornadoes that occurred during the outbreak touched down on December 25, with the other occurring the following day in North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] Two of the tornadoes were destructive enough to be rated EF3 on the Enhanced Fujita Scale. [SEP] [CLS] At least 16 people died as a result of the related blizzard, and thousands were without power.
