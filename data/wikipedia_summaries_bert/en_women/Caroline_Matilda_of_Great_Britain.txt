Caroline Matilda of Great Britain (Danish: Caroline Mathilde; 22 July 1751 â€“ 10 May 1775) was by birth a Princess of Great Britain and member of the House of Hanover and by marriage Queen consort of Denmark and Norway from 1766 to 1772. [CLS] [SEP] The youngest and posthumous daughter of Frederick, Prince of Wales, by Princess Augusta of Saxe-Gotha, Caroline Matilda was raised in a secluded family atmosphere away from the royal court. [CLS] [SEP] At the age of fifteen, she was married to her first cousin, King Christian VII of Denmark and Norway, who suffered from a mental illness and was cold to his wife throughout the marriage. [CLS] [SEP] She had two children: the future Frederick VI and Louise Augusta, whose biological father may have been the German physician Johann Friedrich Struensee. [CLS] [SEP] In 1769, Struensee entered the service of the Danish king; initially Caroline Matilda treated him coldly, but he quickly won the Queen's heart and they began a love affair. [CLS] [SEP] Struensee gained more and more power and instituted a series of reforms that Caroline Matilda supported. [CLS] [SEP] Struensee's reforms and his relationship with the Queen generated powerful enemies, who included Christian VII's stepmother Queen Dowager Juliana Maria and her son Prince Frederick. [CLS] [SEP] Juliana Maria directed a plot to overthrow the lovers, which ended with the execution of Struensee and Caroline Matilda's divorce and banishment. [CLS] [SEP] She died in Celle, Hanover, at the age of twenty-three from scarlet fever. [CLS] [SEP]
