[CLS] Cubana de Aviación Flight 389 (CU389/CUB389) was a scheduled domestic passenger flight, flying from Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito to Simón Bolívar International Airport (currently renamed as José Joaquín de Olmedo International Airport) in Guayaquil, operated by Cuban flag carrier Cubana de Aviación. [SEP] [CLS] On 29 August 1998, the aircraft operating the flight, a Tupolev Tu-154M overran the runway, smashing buildings and crashed into a soccer field in Quito while taking off from the airport. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft burst into flames and 70 people on board were killed. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 10 people on the ground, including children, were killed.Investigation committee conducted by the Ecuadorian Dirección General de Aviación Civil, concluded that the crash was caused due to mechanical failure that led to an aborted take off. [SEP] [CLS] The crew forgot to select the switches for the hydraulic valves of the control system. [SEP] [CLS] While rolling for take-off, the plane didn't rotate. [SEP] [CLS] A rejected take off occurred and Flight 389 overran the runway. [SEP] [CLS] The crash remains as the second deadliest crash in Ecuador, after a Boeing 737-200 crashed in Cuenca in 1983.
