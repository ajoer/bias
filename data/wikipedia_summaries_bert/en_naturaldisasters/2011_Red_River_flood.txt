[CLS] The 2011 Red River flood took place along the Red River of the North in Manitoba in Canada and North Dakota and Minnesota in the United States beginning in April 2011. [SEP] [CLS] The flood was, in part, due to high moisture levels in the soil from the previous year, which meant that further accumulation would threaten the flood-prone region. [SEP] [CLS] Flood predictors in Winnipeg were worried that a dual crest of both the Assiniboine River and the Red might crest at the city at the same time. [SEP] [CLS] Beginning around April 8, 50 homes were evacuated and two more were flooded after an ice jam in St. [SEP] [CLS] Andrews, Manitoba caused the river to flood over its banks.Three fatalities in the US were blamed on the flood by April 8. [SEP] [CLS] Through most of Southern Manitoba, the Red River crest level was below major floods of the past, including those of 1979 and 1950. [SEP] [CLS] When the river crested at the James Avenue pumping station in Winnipeg, north of the confluence of the Red and Assiniboine rivers, the measured open-water crest was 19.59 above datum. [SEP] [CLS] The high crest at the James Avenue pumping station was attributed largely to flooding of the Assiniboine River. [SEP] [CLS]
