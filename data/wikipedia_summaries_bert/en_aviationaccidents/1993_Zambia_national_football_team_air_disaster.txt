On the evening of 27 April 1993, a DHC-5 Buffalo transport aircraft of the Zambian Air Force crashed into the Atlantic Ocean shortly after taking off from Libreville, Gabon. [CLS] [SEP] The flight was carrying most of the Zambian national football team to a FIFA World Cup Qualifier against Senegal in Dakar. [CLS] [SEP] All 25 passengers and five crew members were killed. [CLS] [SEP] The official investigation concluded that the pilot had shut down the wrong engine following an engine fire. [CLS] [SEP] It also found that pilot fatigue and a faulty instrument had contributed to the accident. [CLS] [SEP]
