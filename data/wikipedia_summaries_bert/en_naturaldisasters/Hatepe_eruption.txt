[CLS] The Hatepe eruption, named for the Hatepe Plinian pumice tephra layer, sometimes referred to as the Taupo eruption and dated to either around AD 180 or 233 was Taupo Volcano's most recent major eruption. [SEP] [CLS] It is considered New Zealand's largest eruption during the last 20,000 years. [SEP] [CLS] The eruption ejected some 120 km3 (29 cu mi) of material, of which 30 km3 (7.2 cu mi) was ejected in a few minutes. [SEP] [CLS] This makes it one of the most violent eruptions in the last 5000 years, comparable to the Minoan eruption in the 2nd millennium BC, the 946 eruption of Paektu Mountain and the 1815 eruption of Mount Tambora.
