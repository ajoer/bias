[CLS] Typhoon Nepartak, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Butchoy, was the third most intense tropical cyclone worldwide in 2016. [SEP] [CLS] Nepartak severely impacted Taiwan and East China, with 86 confirmed fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] It caused 3 deaths and NT$678 million (US$21 million) of damage in Taiwan. [SEP] [CLS] Moreover, Nepartak also had disastrous effects in Fujian, China, causing 111 deaths and ¥9.99 billion (US$1.49 billion) of damage. [SEP] [CLS] After the season, total damages exceeded up to ¥12.65 billion (US$1.89 billion).The first named storm and typhoon of the annual typhoon season, Nepartak developed into a tropical storm south of Guam on July 3 and ended a record-tied 199-day period without a named storm over the basin, tied with the 1997–1998 interval. [SEP] [CLS] Steadily tracking northwestward on July 4 and becoming a typhoon on the next day, Nepartak reached peak intensity with a pinhole eye on July 6. [SEP] [CLS] Nepartak started to weaken on July 7 and then crossed Taiwan later, before emerging into the Taiwan Strait and weakening into a severe tropical storm on July 8. [SEP] [CLS] It eventually made landfall over Fujian, China on July 9 and dissipated over land one day later. [SEP] [CLS]
