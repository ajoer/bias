On 9 September 1349, an earthquake sequence began in Italy's Apennine Mountains that severely affected the Molise-Latium-Abruzzi regions. [CLS] [SEP] Four moderate-large earthquakes devastated towns and villages across the central Italian peninsula, with damage even reported in Rome. [CLS] [SEP] These earthquakes originated from the Apennine fold and thrust belt fault network, with the first and most destructive shock's epicenter originating from the north-west Campania region. [CLS] [SEP] Paleoseimological data gathered from scarping, fault length, and collapsed sections of Venafro's Roman aqueduct indicates the epicenter of the main shock was likely along the Aquae Iuliae fault. [CLS] [SEP] The fault suspected of causing this earthquake occurred on the Aqua Iuliae fault along the Molise-Campania border. [CLS] [SEP]
