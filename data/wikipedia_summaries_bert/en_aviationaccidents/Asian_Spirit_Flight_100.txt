Asian Spirit Flight 100 was a Let L-410 Turbolet that crashed into a mountainside between the municipalities of Kasibu, Nueva Vizcaya and Cabarroguis, Quirino in the Philippines on December 7, 1999. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was en route to Cauayan City in Isabela from Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila. [CLS] [SEP] All fifteen passengers and two crew aboard the flight died in the incident. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft departed the Manila Domestic Terminal of Ninoy Aquino International Airport at 8:34 am PST, with the pilot, Rolando Salandanan, last making contact with air traffic control at 9:19 am PST as he was approaching Cauayan Airport, with no indication of a problem aboard the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 100 was scheduled to land at Cauayan at 9:37 am PST. [CLS] [SEP] The wreckage of Flight 100 would be found the next day, December 8. [CLS] [SEP]
