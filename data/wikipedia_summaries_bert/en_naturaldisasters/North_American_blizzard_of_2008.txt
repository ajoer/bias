The North American blizzard of 2008 was a winter storm that struck most of southern and eastern North America from March 6 to March 10, 2008. [CLS] [SEP] The storm was most notable for a major winter storm event from Arkansas to Quebec. [CLS] [SEP] It also produced severe weather across the east coast of the United States with heavy rain, damaging winds and tornadoes, causing locally significant damage. [CLS] [SEP] The hardest hit areas by the wintry weather were from the Ohio Valley to southern Quebec where up to a half a meter of snow fell locally including the major cities of Columbus, Ohio, Cleveland, Ohio, and Ottawa, Ontario. [CLS] [SEP] For many areas across portions of the central United States, Ontario and Quebec, it was the worst winter storm in the past several years. [CLS] [SEP] The blizzard and its aftermath caused at least 17 deaths across four US states and three Canadian provinces, while hundreds others were injured mostly in weather-related accidents and tornadoes.
