[CLS] Mohawk Airlines Flight 405, a Fairchild Hiller FH-227 twin-engine turboprop airliner registered N7818M, was a domestic scheduled passenger flight operated by Mohawk Airlines that crashed into a house within the city limits of Albany, New York on March 3, 1972, on final approach to Albany County Airport (now Albany International Airport), New York, killing 17 people. [SEP] [CLS] The intended destination airport lies in the suburban Town of Colonie, about 4 miles north of the crash site. [SEP] [CLS]
