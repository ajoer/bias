The 1964 Machida F-8 crash (町田米軍機墜落事故, lit. [CLS] [SEP] "Machida American Military Aircraft Crash") occurred on 5 April 1964 in Machida, Tokyo, Japan. [CLS] [SEP] A United States Marine Corps Vought RF-8A Crusader, BuNo 146891, which was returning as one half of a two-plane flight of Crusaders from Kadena Air Base, Okinawa to its home base of Naval Air Facility Atsugi, Kanagawa Prefecture, suffered a mechanical malfunction. [CLS] [SEP] It subsequently crashed into a residential neighborhood in the Hara-Machida area of Machida City (near present-day JR Machida Station) in Tokyo, Japan. [CLS] [SEP] The other aircraft landed safely at Atsugi. [CLS] [SEP] The crash killed four people and injured 32 others on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] The stricken aircraft's pilot, Captain R. [CLS] [SEP] L. [CLS] [SEP] Bown of Seattle, Washington, successfully ejected at 5,000 feet and landed on a car, suffering minor bruises. [CLS] [SEP] The accident destroyed seven houses. [CLS] [SEP] Three of the four fatalities were caused by debris from the collapsed houses, and the fourth was from pieces of the destroyed aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] Japanese media questioned why Bown was not able to steer the aircraft away from the residential area before ejecting. [CLS] [SEP]
