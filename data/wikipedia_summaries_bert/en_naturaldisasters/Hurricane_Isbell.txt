Hurricane Isbell was the final hurricane to affect the United States during the 1964 season. [CLS] [SEP] The eleventh tropical storm and sixth hurricane of the season. [CLS] [SEP] Isbell developed from a dissipating cold front in the southwestern Caribbean Sea on October 8. [CLS] [SEP] The depression initially remained disorganized as it track northwestward, but strengthened into Tropical Storm Isbell on October 13. [CLS] [SEP] Re-curving northeastward, Isbell quickly strengthened further and reached hurricane status by later that day. [CLS] [SEP] Late on October 13, Isbell made landfall in the Pinar del RÃ­o Province of Cuba. [CLS] [SEP] The storm continued strengthening and peaked as a Category 3 hurricane on the following day. [CLS] [SEP] Isbell moved northeastward and made landfall near Everglades, Florida, late on October 14. [CLS] [SEP] After reaching the Atlantic on the following day, the storm began to weaken. [CLS] [SEP] Isbell turned northward and continued weakening, before transitioning to an extratropical cyclone while located just offshore eastern North Carolina on October 16. [CLS] [SEP] The storm produced strong winds throughout western Cuba. [CLS] [SEP] Hundreds of homes were destroyed, as were several tobacco warehouses. [CLS] [SEP] There was at least $20 million in damage and four deaths in Cuba, three of them caused by collapsing houses in the Guane area. [CLS] [SEP] Several tornadoes in Florida caused significant damage. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the state, 1 house was destroyed, 33 were severely damaged, and 631 suffered minor impact. [CLS] [SEP] Additionally, 66 trailers were destroyed and 88 were inflicted with major damage. [CLS] [SEP] Three deaths occurred in the state, one due to a heart attack and two from drowning in Florida Keys when their shrimp boat sank. [CLS] [SEP] Because the storm weakened considerably, impact in North Carolina was generally minor. [CLS] [SEP] The storm also spawned at least nine tornadoes in the state, which demolished trailers and unroofed homes and other buildings in several communities. [CLS] [SEP] Damage throughout the United States totaled $10 million. [CLS] [SEP]
