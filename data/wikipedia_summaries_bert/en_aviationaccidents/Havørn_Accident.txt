The Havørn Accident (Norwegian: Havørn-ulykken) was a controlled flight into terrain of a Junkers Ju 52 aircraft into the mountain Lihesten in Hyllestad, Norway on 16 June 1936 at 07:00. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft, operated by Norwegian Air Lines, was en route from Bergen to Tromsø. [CLS] [SEP] The pilots were unaware that they were flying a parallel to the planned course, 15 to 20 kilometers (9.3 to 12.4 mi) further east. [CLS] [SEP] The crew of four and three passengers were all killed in what was the first fatal aviation accident in Norway. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft landed on a shelf on the mountain face. [CLS] [SEP] A first expedition found four bodies, but attempts to reach the shelf with the main part of the aircraft and three more bodies failed. [CLS] [SEP] A second party was sent out two days later, coordinated by Bernt Balchen and led by Boye Schlytter and Henning Tønsberg, saw the successful salvage of the remaining bodies. [CLS] [SEP]
