[CLS] Jacqueline "Jackie" Cochran (May 11, 1906 – August 9, 1980) was an American pilot and business executive. [SEP] [CLS] She pioneered women's aviation as one of the most prominent racing pilots of her generation. [SEP] [CLS] She set numerous records and was the first woman to break the sound barrier on 18 May 1953. [SEP] [CLS] Cochran was the wartime head of the Women Airforce Service Pilots (WASP) (1943–44), which employed about 1000 civilian American women in a non-combat role to ferry planes from factories to port cities.
