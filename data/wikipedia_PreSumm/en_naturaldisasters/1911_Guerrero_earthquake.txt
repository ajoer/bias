[CLS] The 1911 Guerrero earthquake occurred on December 16 at 12:14 local time near the coast of Guerrero, Mexico along the Mexican subduction zone. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake's energy was calculated using several different magnitude scales with an estimated moment magnitude of 7.6 and a surface wave magnitude of 7.8. [SEP] [CLS] Following the event, telegraphy reports came in from a broad area of Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] Cities from Guadalajara, Mérida (on the Yucatán Peninsula), and Tapachula (near Guatemala) all reported the tremors. [SEP] [CLS] The duration of the shaking was just over two minutes and the death toll was placed at 28. [SEP] [CLS]
