[CLS] Typhoon Thad, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Openg, was considered the worst storm to affect Japan in two years. [SEP] [CLS] Originating from a monsoon trough, Typhoon Thad was first classified on August 15, 1981 and was upgraded into a tropical storm the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Meanwhile, Thad moved north and northeast and attained typhoon intensity midday on August 18. [SEP] [CLS] The next day, the storm reached its peak intensity of 80 mph (130 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] On August 22, Thad accelerated northward, striking eastern Japan the next day just before weakening to a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] After passing through the country, the cyclone transitioned into an extratropical cyclone on August 23. [SEP] [CLS] Forty-one persons perished in Japan due to the typhoon and 173 others were hurt. [SEP] [CLS] Thad flooded 31,082 houses and demolished 4,401 others. [SEP] [CLS] Roughly 27,000 people were left homeless because of Thad. [SEP] [CLS] Moreover, 111,500 homeowners were without power during the height of the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Furthermore, the storm also destroyed 449 roads, and was responsible for 499 landslides. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone inundated 417,400 acres (168,900 hectares) of farm land. [SEP] [CLS] Train service was interrupted in 22 lines. [SEP] [CLS]
