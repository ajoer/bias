[CLS] Katharina Hacker (born 11 January 1967) is a German author best known for her award-winning novel Die Habenichtse (The Have-Nots). [SEP] [CLS] Hacker studied philosophy, history and Jewish studies at the University of Freiburg and the University of Jerusalem. [SEP] [CLS] Her studies in Israel have been seen as an attempt to redo the strong anti Semitic feelings of her Silesian grandmother. [SEP] [CLS] She did not finish her studies with an academic degree. [SEP] [CLS] Since 1996 she has been living as a freelance writer in Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] In 2006 she was the second writer to be awarded the German Book Prize for Die Habenichtse. [SEP] [CLS] In this and other works, Hacker examines the consequences of globalization and neoliberalism on the working life, social relations, and family interactions of her German protagonists.
