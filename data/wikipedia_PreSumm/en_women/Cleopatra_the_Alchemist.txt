[CLS] Cleopatra the Alchemist (fl. [SEP] [CLS] c. [SEP] [CLS] 3rd century AD) was a Greek alchemist, author, and philosopher. [SEP] [CLS] She experimented with practical alchemy but is also credited as one of the four female alchemists that could produce the Philosopher's stone. [SEP] [CLS] Some writers consider her to be the inventor of the alembic, a distillation apparatus.Cleopatra the Alchemist appears to have been active in Alexandria in the 3rd century or 4th century A.D. [SEP] [CLS] She is associated with the school of alchemy typified by Mary the Jewess and Comarius. [SEP] [CLS] These alchemists used complex apparatus for distillation and sublimation. [SEP] [CLS]
