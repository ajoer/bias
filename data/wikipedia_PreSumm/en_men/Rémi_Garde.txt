[CLS] Rémi Garde (French pronunciation: ​[ʁemi ɡaʁd], born 3 April 1966) is a former French professional footballer. [SEP] [CLS] He had a short stint as a coach with Premier League club Aston Villa. [SEP] [CLS] He was most recently the head coach of Montreal Impact in Major League Soccer. [SEP] [CLS] He played as a defender and defensive midfielder, spending most of his career with his first club Lyon, with whom he won the 1988–89 French Division 2. [SEP] [CLS] In 1993, he moved to Strasbourg, reaching the Coupe de France final and winning the UEFA Intertoto Cup in his second season. [SEP] [CLS] He joined Arsenal in 1996, where he won the 1997–98 FA Premier League before retiring through injury a year later. [SEP] [CLS] Garde was capped by the France national team, featuring in their squad at UEFA Euro 1992.Upon retirement as a player, he worked as a coach and assistant manager at Lyon, before taking the managerial position there in 2011. [SEP] [CLS] He won the Coupe de France and Trophée des Champions the following year, and left in 2014 for personal reasons. [SEP] [CLS] He was hired by Aston Villa in November 2015, and left five months later. [SEP] [CLS] He was hired by Montreal Impact in Major League Soccer in November 2017, and was sacked in August 2019. [SEP] [CLS]
