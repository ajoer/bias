[CLS] Lidia Zamenhof (Esperanto: Lidja; 29 January 1904–1942) was a Polish writer, publisher, translator and the youngest daughter of Klara (Silbernik) and L. [SEP] [CLS] L. [SEP] [CLS] Zamenhof, the creator of Esperanto. [SEP] [CLS] She was an active promoter of Esperanto as well as of Homaranismo, a form of religious humanism first defined by her father. [SEP] [CLS] Around 1925 she became a member of the Baháʼí Faith. [SEP] [CLS] In late 1937 she went to the United States to teach that religion as well as Esperanto. [SEP] [CLS] In December 1938 she returned to Poland, where she continued to teach and translated many Baháʼí writings.
