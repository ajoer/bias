Willem Duys (17 August 1928 – 2 June 2011) was a Dutch radio and television presenter, commentator, tennis player and music producer. [CLS] [SEP] Duys was born in Bussum. [CLS] [SEP] He first started a career as a tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] Having made the final NK Older Youth (Van Lennep Cup) in 1946, he won the Spieghel Hilversum and Heide B tournament, took the semi-finals Plaswijck Kralingen and won the B tournament in Rotterdam. [CLS] [SEP] He also reached the final three men's doubles Flehite A with Peter Scholtz and was 1956 men's singles champion Unilever (following Jan de Soet). [CLS] [SEP] After retiring from tennis in mid 1956 Duys began writing for various music magazines and was invited to act as an announcer on AVRO in 1956. [CLS] [SEP] He became a television presenter in 1959. [CLS] [SEP] From 1963 until 1979 he presented the TV program "Voor de Vuist Weg", generally considered to be the first talk show on Dutch television. [CLS] [SEP] In 1974 he received the Gouden Televizierring for this program. [CLS] [SEP] He regularly presented the Grand Gala du Disque and was a commentator for large tennis matches. [CLS] [SEP] For eight years (1962, 1963, 1974–1976, 1978, 1979 and 1983) he was the regular Eurovision Song Contest Commentator for Dutch television viewers. [CLS] [SEP] Duys also presented the Sunday morning radio show "Muziek Mozaïek" for four decades. [CLS] [SEP] A TV version of that show ran for six seasons. [CLS] [SEP] In 1999, after a cerebral infarction, he retired. [CLS] [SEP] He worked for the AVRO for more than forty years. [CLS] [SEP] Willem Duys lived alternately in Saint-Paul, Alpes-Maritimes and Naarden and thereafter in Hilversum. [CLS] [SEP] He died in Hilversum on 2 June 2011 from a lung infection. [CLS] [SEP]
