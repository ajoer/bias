Theobald of Étampes (Latin: Theobaldus Stampensis; French: Thibaud or Thibault d'Étampes; born before 1080, died after 1120) was a medieval schoolmaster and theologian hostile to priestly celibacy. [CLS] [SEP] He is the first scholar known to have lectured at Oxford and is considered a forerunner of Oxford University. [CLS] [SEP]
