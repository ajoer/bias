On 28 June 2018, at about 1:15 pm, a Beechcraft C90 King Air aircraft chartered from UY Aviation Pvt Ltd with the registration VT-UPZ crashed at Jagruti Nagar in the suburb of Ghatkopar in Mumbai. [CLS] [SEP] The 12-seater aircraft carried 4 people including the pilot. [CLS] [SEP] All people on board plus one person on the ground are confirmed dead.As a result of the crash, a fire broke out in the built-up area. [CLS] [SEP] Local firefighters and police responded to the accident. [CLS] [SEP] It is believed that there are no further casualties.The aircraft had departed from Juhu Aerodrome for a test flight. [CLS] [SEP] Five people lost their lives in this accident; 2 pilots, 1 Technician, 1 Aircraft Maintenance Engineer, and 1 civilian. [CLS] [SEP] The cause of the accident is unknown as of July 2018.The Aircraft Accident Investigation Board released the final investigation report in Dec 2018. [CLS] [SEP] The probable cause of the accident as per the report was a stall caused by lack of situational awareness due to spatial disorientation triggered by deteriorating weather, a transition from ILS(IMC) to visual flying (Partial VMC) and unexpected bank owing to differential engine power. [CLS] [SEP] The report indicts the Indian DGCA for violation of regulations in granting the Air Operator Permit. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft VT-UPZ was previously owned by the Uttar Pradesh government. [CLS] [SEP] In 2014 the aircraft met with an accident in Allahabad in which there was substantial damage but the DGCA investigation termed it as a minor incident. [CLS] [SEP] Taking advantage of this report, the certificate of airworthiness was not cancelled. [CLS] [SEP] Over the next decade, the aircraft changed ownership and was transported to Mumbai where the repair work was carried out under the supervision of the DGCA. [CLS] [SEP] Capt. [CLS] [SEP] Amit Singh FRAeS an aviation safety and human factors blogger carried out a detailed independent analysis of the accident and submitted the report to the Secretary, Ministry of Civil Aviation, India. [CLS] [SEP] Capt. [CLS] [SEP] Amit Singh has stated that the AAIB investigation is manipulated and does not correctly identify the cause of the accident, therefore, needs an investigation by a court of inquiry. [CLS] [SEP] The investigation has completely shielded the operator who was granted an Air Operator Permit by the Indian DGCA without having filled the mandatory post of the Chief of Safety. [CLS] [SEP] As per the technical report, both engines malfunctioned in flight probably due to which the aircraft crashed. [CLS] [SEP]
