[CLS] Emperor Renzong (1124–1193), born Li Renxiao 李仁孝, was the 5th emperor of the Western Xia (reigned 1139–1193). [SEP] [CLS] Li Renxiao was the eldest son of Emperor Chongzong, and succeeded him at the age of sixteen. [SEP] [CLS] After ascending into the throne, Renzong made friendly overtures to the Jin Dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] In domestic politics, Renzong created many schools and used examinations to choose his officials. [SEP] [CLS] He respected Confucianism, and built many temples worshipping Confucius. [SEP] [CLS] During era Tian Sheng, Renzong hired a Tibet lama as a religious advisor and printed many copies of Buddhist teachings. [SEP] [CLS] In 1170, Renzong discovered a plot to kill him. [SEP] [CLS] He executed the generals who were behind the plot. [SEP] [CLS] As a result, Renzong distrusted his army generals and the army began to fall into incompetence. [SEP] [CLS] During his later years, Western Xia began to fight wars against various enemies. [SEP] [CLS] Renzong's reign was the peak of Western Xia Dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] Many tribes to the north and west became vassal states of Western Xia, and Renzong's focus on internal politics allowed the central government to be more efficient. [SEP] [CLS] His reign coincides with the peak of the Southern Song and the Jin Dynasties, and there were relatively few conflicts between these three countries. [SEP] [CLS] He died in 1193 having reigned for over half a century like his father before him. [SEP] [CLS]
