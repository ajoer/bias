The 1615 Arica earthquake was a major earthquake centered near Arica in the Spanish colonial Viceroyalty of Peru, within the present day Arica y Parinacota Region of northwestern Chile. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused considerable damage to the infrastructure of the city with the Iglesia Mayor. [CLS] [SEP] The city's fort collapsed, and cracks opened in the floor of the royal quicksilver storage facility. [CLS] [SEP] No human was reported dead but three people suffered injuries.
