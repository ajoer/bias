The name Halong has been used to name four tropical cyclones in the northwestern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] The name was contributed by Vietnam after Halong Bay, a UNESCO World Heritage site and popular tourist attraction in northern Vietnam. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Halong (2002) (T0207, 10W, Inday) – struck Japan. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Halong (2008) (T0804, 05W, Cosme) Typhoon Halong (2014) (T1411, 11W, Jose) – a Category 5-equivalent super typhoon which formed in an active July. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Halong (2019) (T1923, 24W) – a Category 5-equivalent super typhoon, the most intense tropical cyclone worldwide 2019. [CLS] [SEP]
