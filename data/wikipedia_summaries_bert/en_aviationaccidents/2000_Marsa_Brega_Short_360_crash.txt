The 2000 Marsa Brega Short 360 crash occurred on 13 January 2000 when a fully loaded Swiss-registered Short 360 leased by Avisto ditched into the sea near Marsa Brega in Libya. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was chartered by Sirte Oil Company to transport oil workers to the city. [CLS] [SEP] It was carrying 41 people (38 passengers and three crew). [CLS] [SEP] Most of the passengers were foreigners. [CLS] [SEP] As both engines failed in mid-flight, the crew chose to ditch the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] 21 people died in the crash. [CLS] [SEP] An official investigation concluded that the de-icing device on the aircraft was not activated. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft flew into an adverse weather condition and an icing condition occurred in mid-flight. [CLS] [SEP] The ice melted and flooded the engine, causing both engines to fail. [CLS] [SEP] The Libyan Civil Aviation Authority also blamed the pilots for conducting conversation unrelated to the flight. [CLS] [SEP]
