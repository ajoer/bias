[CLS] In mid-July 1951, heavy rains led to a great rise of water in the Kansas River, Missouri River, and other surrounding areas of the central United States. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding occurred in the Kansas, Neosho, Marais Des Cygnes, and Verdigris river basins. [SEP] [CLS] The damage in June and July 1951 across eastern Kansas and Missouri exceeded $935 million (equivalent to $9.21 billion in 2019). [SEP] [CLS] The flooding killed 17 people and displaced 518,000 more. [SEP] [CLS]
