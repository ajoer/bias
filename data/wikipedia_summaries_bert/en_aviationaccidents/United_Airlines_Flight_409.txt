[CLS] United Airlines Flight 409 was a scheduled flight which originated in New York, New York. [SEP] [CLS] The final flight destination was San Francisco, California, with stops in Chicago, Denver and Salt Lake City. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft operating the service, a Douglas DC-4 propliner, registration N30062, crashed into Medicine Bow Peak, near Laramie, Wyoming, killing all 66 people on board (63 passengers, 3 crew members). [SEP] [CLS] The victims included five female members of the Mormon Tabernacle Choir and military personnel. [SEP] [CLS] At the time, this was the deadliest airline crash in the history of American commercial aviation. [SEP] [CLS] Another 66 lives had been lost earlier that year in the March 22 crash in Hawaii of a United States Navy Douglas R6D-1 Liftmaster military transport aircraft, and 66 had also died in the mid-air collision of two United States Air Force C-119G Flying Boxcars over West Germany on August 11, placing the three crashes in a three-way tie as the deadliest aviation incidents in 1955.
