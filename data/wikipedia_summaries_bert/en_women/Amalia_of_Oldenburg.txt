[CLS] Amalia of Oldenburg (Greek: Αμαλία; 21 December 1818 – 20 May 1875) was queen consort of Greece from 1836 to 1862 as the spouse of King Otto (1815–1867). [SEP] [CLS] As the daughter of Duke Paul Frederick Augustus of Oldenburg (later Grand Duke of Oldenburg) and Princess Adelheid of Anhalt-Bernburg-Schaumburg-Hoym. [SEP] [CLS] She was born a Duchess of Oldenburg, though that title was never used in Greece. [SEP] [CLS] When she arrived in Greece in 1837, she at first won the hearts of the Greeks with her refreshing beauty. [SEP] [CLS] After the Queen became more politically involved, however, she became the target of harsh attacks — and her image suffered further as she proved unable to provide an heir. [SEP] [CLS] She and her husband were expelled from Greece in 1862, after an uprising. [SEP] [CLS] She spent the rest of her years in exile in Bavaria. [SEP] [CLS]
