Elena Kagan (; born April 28, 1960) is an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States. [CLS] [SEP] She was nominated by President Barack Obama on May 10, 2010 and has served since August 7, 2010. [CLS] [SEP] Kagan is the fourth woman to become a member of the Court. [CLS] [SEP] Kagan was born and raised in New York City. [CLS] [SEP] After graduating from Princeton University, the University of Oxford, and Harvard Law School, she clerked for a federal Court of Appeals judge and for Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall. [CLS] [SEP] She began her career as a professor at the University of Chicago Law School, leaving to serve as Associate White House Counsel, and later as policy adviser under President Bill Clinton. [CLS] [SEP] After a nomination to the United States Court of Appeals for the D.C. [CLS] [SEP] Circuit, which expired without action, she became a professor at Harvard Law School and was later named its first female dean. [CLS] [SEP] In 2009, Kagan became the first female Solicitor General of the United States. [CLS] [SEP] President Obama nominated her to the Supreme Court to fill the vacancy arising from the impending retirement of Justice John Paul Stevens. [CLS] [SEP] The United States Senate confirmed her nomination by a vote of 63 to 37. [CLS] [SEP] She is considered part of the Court's liberal wing but tends to be one of the more moderate justices of that group. [CLS] [SEP] She wrote the majority opinion in Cooper v. [CLS] [SEP] Harris, a landmark case restricting the permissible uses of race in drawing congressional districts. [CLS] [SEP]
