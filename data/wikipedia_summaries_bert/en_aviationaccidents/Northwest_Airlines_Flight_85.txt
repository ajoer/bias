Northwest Airlines Flight 85 was a scheduled international passenger flight from Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport in the United States to Narita International Airport in Japan. [CLS] [SEP] On October 9, 2002, the Boeing 747-400 carrying out the flight experienced a lower rudder hardover event, when the flight was over the Bering Sea. [CLS] [SEP] A rudder hardover is when the aircraft's rudder deflects to its travel limit without crew input. [CLS] [SEP] The 747's hardover gave full left lower rudder, requiring the pilots to use full right upper rudder and right aileron to maintain attitude and course. [CLS] [SEP] The flight diverted to Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport. [CLS] [SEP] No passengers or crew were injured, but the incident resulted in an airworthiness directive to prevent the possibility of a future accident. [CLS] [SEP]
