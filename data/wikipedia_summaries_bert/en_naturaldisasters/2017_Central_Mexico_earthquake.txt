The 2017 Puebla earthquake struck at 13:14 CDT (18:14 UTC) on 19 September 2017 with an estimated magnitude of Mw7.1 and strong shaking for about 20 seconds. [CLS] [SEP] Its epicenter was about 55 km (34 mi) south of the city of Puebla. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused damage in the Mexican states of Puebla and Morelos and in the Greater Mexico City area, including the collapse of more than 40 buildings. [CLS] [SEP] 370 people were killed by the earthquake and related building collapses, including 228 in Mexico City, and more than 6,000 were injured.The quake coincidentally occurred on the 32nd anniversary of the 1985 Mexico City earthquake, which killed around 10,000 people. [CLS] [SEP] The 1985 quake was commemorated, and a national earthquake drill was held, at 11 a.m. [CLS] [SEP] local time, just two hours before the 2017 earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] Twelve days earlier, the even larger 2017 Chiapas earthquake struck 650 km (400 mi) away, off the coast of the state of Chiapas. [CLS] [SEP]
