The 1732 Irpinia earthquake was a seismic event with a magnitude of 6.6 that affected Irpinia and part of Sannio. [CLS] [SEP] It occurred on 29 November 1732 at 8:40 AM local time (UTC+1). [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located in the Campanian Apennines, in the area of the Ufita Valley, which is part of the modern-day Province of Avellino. [CLS] [SEP] Around twenty populated areas were destroyed entirely or in part and tens of others were significantly damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The number of deaths was estimated to be 1,940. [CLS] [SEP] Damage from the earthquake was classified as "severe" (indicating damage between $5 and US$24 million), and the number of homes destroyed as classified as "many" (indicating between 101 and 1,000 homes). [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake had a rating on the modified Mercalli intensity scale of X (extreme).Among the most devastated communities were Mirabella Eclano (which was razed to the ground), Carife, Grottaminarda, and Ariano Irpino. [CLS] [SEP] Damage was serious in the provincial capital of Avellino, while in Benevento, there were mainly partial collapses of buildings. [CLS] [SEP]
