[CLS] Maria Cunitz or Maria Cunitia (other versions of surname include: Cunicia, Cunitzin, Kunic, Cunitiae, Kunicia, Kunicka) (1610 in Wołów, Silesia – August 22, 1664, in Byczyna, Silesia) was an accomplished Silesian astronomer, and the most notable female astronomer of the early modern era. [SEP] [CLS] She authored a book Urania propitia, in which she provided new tables, new ephemera, and a simpler working solution to Kepler's Area Law for determining the position of a planet on its elliptical path. [SEP] [CLS] The Cunitz crater on Venus is named after her. [SEP] [CLS] The minor planet 12624 Mariacunitia is named in her honour. [SEP] [CLS]
