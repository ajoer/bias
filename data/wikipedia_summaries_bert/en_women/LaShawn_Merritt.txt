LaShawn Merritt (born June 27, 1986) is an American track and field athlete who competes in sprinting events, specializing in the 400 metres. [CLS] [SEP] He is a former Olympic champion over the distance and his personal best of 43.65 seconds makes him the ninth fastest of all time. [CLS] [SEP] He was a successful junior athlete and won the 400 m gold at the 2004 World Junior Championships in Athletics, as well as setting two world junior records in the relays. [CLS] [SEP] He became part of the American 4×400 meter relay team and helped win the event at the 2006 IAAF World Indoor Championships. [CLS] [SEP] He established himself individually in 2007 by winning a silver medal in the 400 m at the 2007 World Championships. [CLS] [SEP] He came out on top of a rivalry with Jeremy Wariner in 2008 by winning in the 2008 Olympic final in a personal best time, and by a record margin of 0.99 secs. [CLS] [SEP] He also broke the Olympic record in the relay with the American team, recording the second fastest time ever. [CLS] [SEP] Merritt established himself as the World Champion with a win at the 2009 World Championships in Athletics in the 400 m and the 4×400 m relay. [CLS] [SEP]
