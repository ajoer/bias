The 1995 Chiapas earthquake occurred on October 20 at 20:38 local time. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located in Ocozocoautla de Espinosa, in the state of Chiapas, Mexico, near Tuxtla Gutiérrez. [CLS] [SEP] It had a magnitude of Mw 7.1, or ML 6.5. [CLS] [SEP] Building damage was reported. [CLS] [SEP] Around 70 people were reported injured. [CLS] [SEP] In Tuxtla Gutiérrez, telephone and electricity services were momentarily interrupted. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake could be felt strongly in Mexico City and in many parts of southern Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] It could also be felt in Guatemala and El Salvador. [CLS] [SEP] The centroid mechanism is of thrust faulting with a small strike-slip component. [CLS] [SEP] The rupture of this earthquake propagated from NW to SE over a distance of about 30 km. [CLS] [SEP] The duration of the rupture was about 17 s. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was resulted from the internal deformation of the Cocos Plate, which is subducting beneath the North American Plate.
