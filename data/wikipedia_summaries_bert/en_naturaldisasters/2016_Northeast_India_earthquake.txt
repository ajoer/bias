[CLS] The 2016 Imphal earthquake struck northeast India in the state of Manipur on January 4 with a moment magnitude of 6.7 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VII (Very strong). [SEP] [CLS] Its epicentre was located in the Tamenglong district; about 30 km west of Imphal. [SEP] [CLS] At least eleven people were killed, 200 others were injured and numerous buildings were damaged. [SEP] [CLS] The quake was also strongly felt in Bangladesh. [SEP] [CLS] It was also extensively felt in eastern and north-eastern India. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake, which hit at 4:35 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] on 4 January local time (23:05 UTC, 3 January), was centered in an isolated area. [SEP] [CLS] Imphal has a population of more than 250,000. [SEP] [CLS] It was one of the most damaging earthquakes in Manipur since 1880 and 1939. [SEP] [CLS]
