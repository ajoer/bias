[CLS] Elisabeth Volkenrath (née Mühlau; 5 September 1919 – 13 December 1945) was a German supervisor at several Nazi concentration camps during World War II. [SEP] [CLS] Volkenrath, née Mühlau, was an ungelernte Hilfskraft (unskilled worker) when she volunteered for service in a concentration camp. [SEP] [CLS] She began in October 1941 at Ravensbrück concentration camp as a simple Aufseherin. [SEP] [CLS] In March 1942, she was sent to Auschwitz Birkenau where she worked in the same function as at Ravensbrück. [SEP] [CLS] At Auschwitz, she met SS-Rottenführer Heinz Volkenrath, who had worked there since 1941 as SS-Blockführer. [SEP] [CLS] The couple married in 1943. [SEP] [CLS] Elisabeth Volkenrath participated in the selection of prisoners for the gas chambers and, in November 1944, was promoted to Oberaufseherin for all camp sections for female prisoners at Auschwitz.Elisabeth Volkenrath was transferred to Bergen-Belsen when Auschwitz was closed. [SEP] [CLS] From February 1945, she was Oberaufseherin (supervising wardress) at Bergen-Belsen. [SEP] [CLS]
