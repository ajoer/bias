The 1783 Calabrian earthquakes were a sequence of five strong earthquakes that hit the region of Calabria in southern Italy (then part of the Kingdom of Naples), the first two of which produced significant tsunamis. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenters form a clear alignment extending nearly 100 km from the Straits of Messina to about 18 km SSW of Catanzaro. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter of the first earthquake occurred in the plain of Palmi. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquakes occurred over a period of nearly two months, all with estimated magnitudes of 5.9 or greater. [CLS] [SEP] Estimates of the total number of deaths lie in the range 32,000 to 50,000. [CLS] [SEP]
