[CLS] Maryam Yusuf Jamal (Arabic: مريم يوسف جمال‎; Oromo: Maryam Yuusuf Jamaal) (born Zenebech Tola) (born 16 September 1984) is a Bahraini middle-distance runner. [SEP] [CLS] She is the first Bahraini athlete to win an Olympic medal, a gold (originally bronze, but later upgraded after two doping violations) in the 1500m women's race, in the 2012 Summer Olympics in London. [SEP] [CLS] This was also the first Olympic medal won by a woman representing a Gulf state. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Ethiopia, 2005 was her first full season. [SEP] [CLS] She gained the national record and ran the fastest 3000 m of the year, with a time of 8:28.87 at a race in Oslo. [SEP] [CLS] Jamal is a two-time world champion in the 1500 m, having won at the 2007 and 2009 World Championships in Athletics. [SEP] [CLS] She represented Bahrain at the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, finishing fifth in the 1500 m final. [SEP] [CLS] Jamal has also had much success at regional competitions: winning two gold medals at the 2006 Asian Games in addition to the Asian Cross Country Championships in both 2007 and 2009.
