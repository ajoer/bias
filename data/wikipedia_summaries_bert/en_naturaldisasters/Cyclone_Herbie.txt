Tropical Cyclone Herbie was the only known tropical system to impact Western Australia during the month of May on record. [CLS] [SEP] The following day, the system was classified as a tropical low by the Australian Bureau of Meteorology and intensified into a Category 1 cyclone later that day. [CLS] [SEP] Several hours after this upgrade, the storm attained its initial peak intensity with winds of 75 km/h (45 mph 10-minute sustained). [CLS] [SEP] Around the same time, the Joint Typhoon Warning Center classified Herbie as Tropical Storm 21S. [CLS] [SEP] On 19 May, the cyclone formed a new low pressure center and relocated roughly 300 km (190 mi) south. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly thereafter, the storm began to accelerate towards the southeast and started to undergo an extratropical transition. [CLS] [SEP] Early on 21 May, Herbie made landfall in Shark Bay before losing its identity the following day over the Great Australian Bight. [CLS] [SEP] Although a weak storm, Herbie brought flooding rains and severe dust storms to portions of Western Australia. [CLS] [SEP] Additionally, a 30,000 ton freighter broke in half amidst rough seas produced by the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Total losses from the storm reached A$20 million (US$15.6 million). [CLS] [SEP] Due to the significant damage wrought by Herbie, the name was retired following its use. [CLS] [SEP]
