Avioimpex Flight 110 was a scheduled international passenger flight operated by Avioimpex that crashed on 20 November 1993 while flying from Geneva to Skopje. [CLS] [SEP] Before the disaster, Flight 110 had deviated from Skopje International Airport to Ohrid Airport due to a blizzard in the Macedonian capital. [CLS] [SEP] The plane, a Yakovlev Yak-42, was carrying 108 passengers and eight crew, and crashed about 7 kilometres (4.3 mi; 3.8 nmi) east of Ohrid Airport. [CLS] [SEP] All 116 people on board were killed as a result of the crash. [CLS] [SEP] One passenger lived for eleven days after the disaster but succumbed to his injuries. [CLS] [SEP] Most of the victims were Yugoslav citizens of Albanian ethnicity. [CLS] [SEP] The crash was Macedonia's third aviation disaster in 16 months, and remains the country's deadliest. [CLS] [SEP] A subsequent investigation established the cause of the accident as pilot error. [CLS] [SEP]
