[SEP] [CLS] Born at the Neues Palais in Potsdam, she was the second child and eldest daughter of Prince Frederick of Prussia, a member of the House of Hohenzollern who became Crown Prince of Prussia in 1861 and German Emperor in 1888. [SEP] [CLS] Through her mother Victoria, Princess Royal, Charlotte was the eldest granddaughter of the British monarch Queen Victoria and her consort Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha. [SEP] [CLS] Princess Charlotte was a difficult child and indifferent student, with a nervous disposition. [SEP] [CLS] Her relationship with her demanding mother was strained. [SEP] [CLS] As she grew older, Charlotte developed a penchant for spreading gossip and causing trouble. [SEP] [CLS] Eager to escape from parental control, at age seventeen, she married Prince Bernhard of Saxe-Meiningen in 1878. [SEP] [CLS] Her husband's weak-willed personality had little effect on her. [SEP] [CLS] Known for spreading gossip and her eccentric personality, Princess Charlotte enjoyed Berlin society while frequently leaving her only child, Princess Feodora, in the care of family members. [SEP] [CLS] Charlotte and Feodora, in turn, also had a difficult relationship. [SEP] [CLS] Charlotte's brother succeeded their father as Emperor Wilhelm II in 1888, increasing her social influence. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout her brother's reign, she was known for her mischief-making, and spent her life in between bouts of illness, in frivolous and extravagant pursuits. [SEP] [CLS] She became Duchess of Saxe-Meiningen in 1914, only for her husband to lose his title with the end of World War I in 1918. [SEP] [CLS] Charlotte died the following year of a heart attack in Baden-Baden. [SEP] [CLS] She had suffered from a lifetime of ill-health. [SEP] [CLS] Most historians now believe she had porphyria, a genetic disease that afflicted other members of the British Royal Family. [SEP] [CLS]
