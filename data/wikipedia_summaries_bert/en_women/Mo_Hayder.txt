[CLS] Mo Hayder (born 1962) is a British author of crime and thriller fiction. [SEP] [CLS] She is the author of ten novels. [SEP] [CLS] Her debut, Birdman, was published in January 2000 and was an international bestseller. [SEP] [CLS] Her second novel, The Treatment, was a Sunday Times bestseller and won the 2002 WH Smith Thumping Good Read award. [SEP] [CLS] Her third novel, Tokyo, was published in May 2004 and was another Sunday Times bestseller. [SEP] [CLS] Tokyo was published as The Devil of Nanking in the United States in March 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Pig Island was her fourth best seller and was published in April 2006. [SEP] [CLS] Pig Island was nominated for both a Barry Award for Best British crime novel, and a CWA dagger. [SEP] [CLS] Her fifth book, Ritual was the first in The Walking Man series, and was nominated for CWA Ian Fleming Steel Dagger award. [SEP] [CLS] Skin is the second book in The Walking Man series, and was released in early 2009. [SEP] [CLS] Gone, the third book in The Walking Man series, was released in February 2011; it won the Edgar Award for Best Novel. [SEP] [CLS] Her novel Hanging Hill was published 2011, and Wolf in 2014. [SEP] [CLS] Hayder claims to have left school at fifteen, (a year ahead of the UK school leaving age at the time) and has worked as a barmaid, security guard, film-maker, hostess in a Tokyo club, educational administrator and a teacher of English as a foreign language. [SEP] [CLS] Hayder has an MA in film from The American University in Washington DC, and an MA in Creative Writing from Bath Spa University. [SEP] [CLS] Her debut novel, Birdman, was published in 2000 to wide acclaim. [SEP] [CLS] Although some reviewers found the novel (which concerns a serial killer with a particularly vicious modus-operandi) too violent and disturbing, the book became an international bestseller. [SEP] [CLS] Birdman introduced readers to DI Jack Caffery, a character who also featured in Hayder's second novel, The Treatment. [SEP] [CLS] The Treatment tackled themes of paedophilia. [SEP] [CLS] DI Caffery returned in Hayder's recent novels Ritual, Skin, Gone, and Wolf, which she calls the Walking Man series. [SEP] [CLS] Mo Hayder lives just outside Bath, England with her partner, Bob Randell (retired police sgt of Avon & Somerset Police Underwater Search Unit), and her daughter. [SEP] [CLS]
