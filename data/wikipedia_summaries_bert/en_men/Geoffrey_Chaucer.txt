[SEP] [CLS] Widely considered the greatest English poet of the Middle Ages, he is best known for The Canterbury Tales. [SEP] [CLS] He has been called the "father of English literature", or, alternatively, the "father of English poetry". [SEP] [CLS] He was the first writer to be buried in what has since come to be called Poets' Corner, in Westminster Abbey. [SEP] [CLS] Chaucer also gained fame as a philosopher and astronomer, composing the scientific A Treatise on the Astrolabe for his 10-year-old son Lewis. [SEP] [CLS] He maintained a career in the civil service as a bureaucrat, courtier, diplomat, and member of parliament. [SEP] [CLS] Among Chaucer's many other works are The Book of the Duchess, The House of Fame, The Legend of Good Women, and Troilus and Criseyde. [SEP] [CLS] He is seen as crucial in legitimising the literary use of Middle English when the dominant literary languages in England were still French and Latin. [SEP] [CLS]
