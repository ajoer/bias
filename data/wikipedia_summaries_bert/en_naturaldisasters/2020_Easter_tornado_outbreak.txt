A widespread and deadly tornado outbreak affected the Southeastern United States on Easter Sunday and Monday, April 12–13, 2020. [CLS] [SEP] Several tornadoes were responsible for prompting tornado emergencies, including the first one to be issued by the National Weather Service in Charleston, South Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] A large squall line formed and tracked through the mid-Atlantic on April 13, prompting more tornado warnings and watches. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 15 watches were produced during the course of the event, two of which were designated Particularly Dangerous Situations. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the two-day outbreak, a total of 140 tornadoes touched down across 10 states, inflicting widespread and locally catastrophic damage. [CLS] [SEP] The strongest tornado was rated high-end EF4 and occurred in Southern Mississippi, producing estimated winds of 190 mph (310 km/h), reaching a width of 2.25 mi (3.62 km), and causing eight deaths. [CLS] [SEP] With a total of 32 tornado-related fatalities, it was the deadliest tornado outbreak since April 27–30, 2014. [CLS] [SEP] To assist with recovery efforts, governors of five states declared a state of emergency. [CLS] [SEP] Relief efforts were complicated by social distancing requirements amid the ongoing coronavirus pandemic. [CLS] [SEP]
