Severe Tropical Cyclone Fran was the third tropical cyclone within four weeks to impact Vanuatu in 1992. [CLS] [SEP] Fran formed on March 4 and then gradually intensified. [CLS] [SEP] Winds reached gale-force on March 5, and hurricane-force a few days later. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone Fran reached the powerful Category 5 equivalent on the Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale near Vanuatu. [CLS] [SEP] The storm weakened somewhat due to land interaction, but briefly re-intensified after moving away from land. [CLS] [SEP] Fran gradually weakened over the next several days over less favorable conditions. [CLS] [SEP] Meanwhile, the storm passed north of New Caledonia. [CLS] [SEP] Eventually, as a Category 2 system on the Australian scale, Fran made landfall on Queensland during March 16. [CLS] [SEP] Severe Tropical Cyclone Fran turned towards the southeast and eventually headed back out to sea. [CLS] [SEP] The system dissipated the next day. [CLS] [SEP] On Efate, over 130 houses lost roofs. [CLS] [SEP] Along Queensland, two rivers sustained major flooding, but no deaths were attributed to this cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] Total damage from the system was AU$8–10 million (1992 AUD). [CLS] [SEP] Moderate damage was also reported in Fiji. [CLS] [SEP] In New Caledonia, the storm brought flooding and landslides. [CLS] [SEP]
