[CLS] The Linton Bushfire was a wildfire that burned through private land and state forests near the township of Linton, Victoria, Australia on 2 December 1998. [SEP] [CLS] Firefighters from the Victorian state government's Department of Natural Resources and Environment and the Country Fire Authority (CFA) were deployed to put out the fire. [SEP] [CLS] At approximately 8.45pm, two firefighting appliances and their crews were entrapped and engulfed in fire following an unexpected wind change. [SEP] [CLS] The bushfire covered a maximum land of 660 hectares of private and public land. [SEP] [CLS]
