[CLS] Cyclone Favio was the first known tropical cyclone that passed south of Madagascar to strike Africa as an intense tropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Early on February 11, 2007, a zone of disturbed weather formed east of Madagascar. [SEP] [CLS] Four days later, Favio was named as intensified to a moderate tropical storm while moving southwest. [SEP] [CLS] On February 18, the storm was upgraded into a severe tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Then, it turned west in the general direction of Mozambique. [SEP] [CLS] Continuing to intensify, Favio was upgraded to a tropical cyclone early on February 19. [SEP] [CLS] Subsequently, the storm began to undergo rapid deepening; the small storm reached intense tropical cyclone status the next day before peaking in intensity. [SEP] [CLS] However, the cyclone had weakened somewhat prior to making landfall on February 22 in the Inhambane Province in Mozambique. [SEP] [CLS] It rapidly weakened over land and dissipated the next day. [SEP] [CLS] While strengthening, Favio brought heavy rains to southern Madagascar. [SEP] [CLS] After making landfall, Intense Tropical Cyclone Favio brought widespread damage to Vilanculos in Mozambique, where the cyclone killed four people and injured at least 70 in the town. [SEP] [CLS] About 80% of the town was destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, a total of ten people were killed by the storm and nearly 100 others were injured, and combined with an earlier flood, the storm caused $71 million (2007 USD) in damage. [SEP] [CLS] Around 130,000 homes suffered damage and 130 schools were leveled by the tropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Across the nation, a total of 33,000 people were left homeless during the system. [SEP] [CLS] Cyclone Favio destroyed 277,000 ha (684,480 acres) of crops. [SEP] [CLS] In Zimbabwe, 400 electricity poles were downed. [SEP] [CLS] During the aftermath of the storm, a number of agencies provided assistance to victims of Cyclone Favio in Mozambique. [SEP] [CLS] One agency donated $626,500, helping transport 50,000 mosquito nets to the devastated area. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 5,500 bars of soap, 1,600 buckets, 50 baths, and over 180 latrines were distributed to the victims of the devastated area. [SEP] [CLS] In all, the government was praised for how well they handled the disaster. [SEP] [CLS]
