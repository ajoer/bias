The 1944 Appalachians tornado outbreak was a deadly tornado outbreak that hit the Midwest and Mid-Atlantic regions of the United States on June 22–23, 1944. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak produced several strong tornadoes in Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and Maryland—areas that were falsely believed to be immune to tornadoes. [CLS] [SEP] Particularly hard hit was the town of Shinnston in Harrison County, West Virginia, which was destroyed by a violent F4 tornado before 9:00 PM EDT on June 23. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 30 people died at Shinnston and at least 104 were killed in the state of West Virginia by this and two other intense tornadoes. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak itself was and still remains the deadliest tornado outbreak ever to hit the state of West Virginia. [CLS] [SEP] The Shinnston tornado was and is the only tornado to produce violent damage in West Virginia. [CLS] [SEP]
