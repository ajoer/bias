The 2008 Santa Catarina floods were floods in the Santa Catarina, Brazil in November 2008. [CLS] [SEP] They occurred after a period of heavy rainfall, most significantly from 20-23 of November. [CLS] [SEP] The state had suffered constant rainfalls for over two months on the coast, which turned the soil wet enough to cause a landslide during the storm that hit the state in late November. [CLS] [SEP] It affected around 60 towns and over 1.5 million people in the state of Santa Catarina in Brazil. [CLS] [SEP] At least 128 people are confirmed to have been killed with over 78,700 being forced to evacuate their homes. [CLS] [SEP] A further 150,000 have been left without electricity, while water rationing is being carried out in at least one town due to purification problems. [CLS] [SEP] Santa Catarina state Governor Luiz Henrique da Silveira had earlier suggested that the final death toll was likely to be over 50, a figure that has proved optimistic. [CLS] [SEP] Several towns in the region have become cut off due to floodwater and landslide debris and on November 23 the Mayor of Blumenau, João Paulo Kleinübing, declared a state of emergency in the city. [CLS] [SEP] Water levels in the Vale do Itajaí have risen to eleven metres above normal. [CLS] [SEP]
