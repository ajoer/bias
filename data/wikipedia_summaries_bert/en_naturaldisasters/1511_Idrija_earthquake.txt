[CLS] The 1511 Idrija earthquake (Slovene: Idrijski potres) occurred on March 26 with a maximum Mercalli intensity of X (Extreme). [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter was around the town of Idrija in present-day Slovenia, although some place it some 15-20 kilometers to the west, between Gemona and Pulfero in Friulian Slovenia. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake affected a large territory between Carinthia, Friuli, present-day Slovenia and Croatia. [SEP] [CLS] An estimated twelve to fifteen thousand people were killed and damage was considered severe. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was felt as far as in Switzerland and present-day Slovakia. [SEP] [CLS] A number of castles and churches were razed to the ground in a large area from Northeastern Italy to western Croatia. [SEP] [CLS] Among the destroyed buildings were the castles of Udine and Škofja Loka, the monastery of the Teutonic Knights in Ljubljana; the Zagreb cathedral was severely damaged. [SEP] [CLS] Blaž Raškaj, commander of the Jajce fortess, in modern Bosnia, reported to the Hungarian Estates that the earthquake had severely damaged the fortifications. [SEP] [CLS] The reconstruction of the destroyed buildings in the following decades is considered the dividing line between Gothic and Renaissance architecture in the art history of the Eastern Alps.
