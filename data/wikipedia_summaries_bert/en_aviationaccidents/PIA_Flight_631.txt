Pakistan International Airlines (Urdu: پاکستان انٹرنیشنل ایئر لائنز‎; abbreviated PIA, Urdu: پی‌آئی‌اے‎) is the national flag carrier of Pakistan under the administrative control of the Secretary to the Government of Pakistan for Aviation. [CLS] [SEP] Its central hub is Karachi's Jinnah International Airport, while Allama Iqbal International Airport in Lahore, and Islamabad International Airport serve as secondary hubs. [CLS] [SEP] PIA was founded on 29 October 1946 as Orient Airways, and was initially based in Calcutta, British India, before shifting operations to the newly independent state of Pakistan in 1947. [CLS] [SEP] Orient Airways was nationalised to form the Pakistan International Airlines Corporation (PIAC). [CLS] [SEP] The new airline commenced international services in 1955 to London, via Cairo and Rome. [CLS] [SEP] PIA became the first Asian airline to operate jet aircraft with the induction of the Boeing 707 into commercial service on 7 March 1960, and later in 1964 became the first non-Communist airline to fly to China. [CLS] [SEP] The airline played a vital role in the establishment of the Emirates airline in 1985. [CLS] [SEP] In 2004, PIA become the launch customer of the Boeing 777-200LR. [CLS] [SEP] On 10 November 2005 used the Boeing 777-200LR to complete the world's longest nonstop flight by a commercial airliner. [CLS] [SEP] This flight lasted 22 hours and 22 minutes on the eastbound route between Hong Kong and London.PIA is Pakistan's largest airline and operates a fleet of more than 30 aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] The airline operates nearly 100 flights daily, servicing 18 domestic destinations and 25 international destinations across Asia, Europe, the Middle East and North America. [CLS] [SEP] In addition to commercial flight operations, PIA also owns The Roosevelt Hotel in New York City, and the Sofitel Paris Scribe Hotel in Paris. [CLS] [SEP] The airline operates a frequent flier programme, PIA Awards +, and has several codeshare and interline agreements. [CLS] [SEP] However, it is not part of any airline alliance. [CLS] [SEP] On 30 June 2020, PIA was banned from flying in European airspace for 6 months, starting on 1 July 2020, after EASA determined that the airline was not capable of certifying and overseeing its operators and aircraft in accordance with applicable international standards. [CLS] [SEP] This decision was made soon after it was revealed that at least a third of all pilot's licenses issued in Pakistan were fraudulent. [CLS] [SEP] By 9 July 2020, the airline was also banned by the UK and the United States. [CLS] [SEP]
