Alexandra Ioana Stan (born 10 June 1989) is a Romanian singer-songwriter. [CLS] [SEP] Born and raised in the city of Constan»õa, she made her worldwide breakthrough with the 2010 single "Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Saxobeat", which was written and produced by Marcel Prodan and Andrei Nemirschi. [CLS] [SEP] They had previously discovered Stan at a karaoke bar in 2009 and signed her to their label, Maan Records. [CLS] [SEP] "Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Saxobeat" had followed the singer's debut single, "Lollipop (Param Pam Pam)" (2009), which brought her moderate fame in Romania. [CLS] [SEP] "Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Saxobeat" quickly achieved commercial success locally and abroad, reaching number one in several countries and gathering various certifications. [CLS] [SEP] Saxobeats, Stan's debut studio album, was released in August 2011 and features the follow-up singles "Get Back (ASAP)" (2011) and "Lemonade" (2012), which fared moderately well in Europe. [CLS] [SEP] An alleged altercation with Prodan in 2013 and a lawsuit against him delayed the release of Stan's second album, Unlocked, which would eventually be issued in August 2014. [CLS] [SEP] Two of its singles, "Dance" (2014) and "Cherry Pop" (2014), were successful in Japan. [CLS] [SEP] Stan's third studio album, Alesta, was released in March 2016 and included a collaboration with Inna and Daddy Yankee on "We Wanna" (2015); the single went on to reach the top 60 in several countries. [CLS] [SEP] Mami, her fourth record, followed in 2018. [CLS] [SEP] Preceding the release of its last single was Stan's feature on Manuel Riva's "Miami" (2018), which peaked within the top ten in Romania and on Billboard's Dance Club Songs chart. [CLS] [SEP] Stan has received a number of awards and nominations, including the European Border Breakers Award, a Japan Gold Disc Award, an MTV Europe Music Award and two Romanian Music Awards. [CLS] [SEP] She is also referred to as one of the most successful Romanian artists alongside Inna. [CLS] [SEP]
