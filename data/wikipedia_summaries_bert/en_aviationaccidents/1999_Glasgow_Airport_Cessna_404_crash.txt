The 1999 Glasgow Airport Cessna 404 crash occurred on 3 September 1999.Edinburgh Air Charter Flight 3W, call sign "Saltire 3 Whisky", was a charter flight from Glasgow to Aberdeen. [CLS] [SEP] A Cessna 404 Titan, G-ILGW, was used for this flight. [CLS] [SEP] The flight had been chartered by Airtours International Airways to transport two pilots and seven flight attendants. [CLS] [SEP] Upon arrival in Aberdeen, the Airtours crewmembers were scheduled to work a Boeing 757 on a charter flight to Palma de Mallorca. [CLS] [SEP] A Cessna 404 Titan, G-ILGW, was used for the flight to Aberdeen. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly after take off, the left engine failed and the commander feathered the right engine. [CLS] [SEP] Instead of attempting a crash landing, the commander attempted to return to Glasgow Airport without engine power and lost control while trying to make a right turn. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft crashed and caught fire approximately one nautical mile from the airport. [CLS] [SEP] The two Edinburgh Air Charter pilots, the Airtours First Officer, and five AirTours flight attendants died in the crash. [CLS] [SEP] The AirTours Captain and two flight attendants survived. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was slightly overweight for the conditions. [CLS] [SEP] A report recommended engine inspections, more crash-worthy seats, and consideration to fitting aircraft like this with CVRs. [CLS] [SEP] As a result of this accident an airworthiness directive was issued by the British Civil Aviation Authority in June 2000, requiring inspections of the starter adapters and crankshaft gears on Continental GTSIO-520 series engines.
