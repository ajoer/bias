Emperor Mo of Jin (died 9 February 1234), personal name Hudun, sinicised name Wanyan Chenglin, was the last emperor of the Jurchen-led Jin dynasty, which ruled northern China between the 12th and 13th centuries. [CLS] [SEP] Originally a military general, he inherited the throne from his predecessor, Emperor Aizong, during the siege of Caizhou. [CLS] [SEP] He was killed in action on the same day he was crowned emperor, when Caizhou fell to the allied forces of the Mongol Empire and Southern Song dynasty. [CLS] [SEP] Having ruled as emperor for less than a day, or maybe even just a few hours, he holds the record for being the shortest-reigning monarch in Chinese history. [CLS] [SEP]
