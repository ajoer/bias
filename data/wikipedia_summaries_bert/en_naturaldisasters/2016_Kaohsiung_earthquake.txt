[CLS] At 03:57 local time on 6 February 2016, an earthquake with a moment magnitude of 6.4 struck 28 km (17 mi) northeast of Pingtung City in southern Taiwan, in the Meinong District of Kaohsiung. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake struck at a depth of around 23 km (14 mi). [SEP] [CLS] Its comparatively shallow depth caused more intense reverberations on the surface. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake had a maximum intensity of VII (Very strong) on the Mercalli intensity scale, causing widespread damage and 116 deaths. [SEP] [CLS] Almost all of the deaths were caused by a collapsed residential building, named Weiguan Jinlong in Yongkang District, except two others, whom were killed in Gueiren District. [SEP] [CLS] Sixty-eight aftershocks have occurred. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was the deadliest earthquake in Taiwan since the 1999 Chi-Chi earthquake. [SEP] [CLS]
