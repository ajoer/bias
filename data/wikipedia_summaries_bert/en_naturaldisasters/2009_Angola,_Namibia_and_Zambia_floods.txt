The 2009 Angola, Namibia and Zambia floods was a natural disaster which began in early March 2009 and resulted in the deaths of at least 131 people and otherwise affected around 445,000 people. [CLS] [SEP] The floods affected seven regions of Namibia, three provinces of Zambia, two regions of Angola and part of Botswana. [CLS] [SEP] The floodwaters damaged buildings and infrastructure and displaced at least 300,000 people. [CLS] [SEP] A state of emergency was declared in northern Namibia and there were fears that a disease epidemic would ensue. [CLS] [SEP] The Red Cross agencies and governments of the two countries responded to the disaster, and aid was distributed by the World Health Organization. [CLS] [SEP]
