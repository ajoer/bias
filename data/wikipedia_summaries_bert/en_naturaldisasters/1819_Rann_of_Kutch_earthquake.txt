The 1819 Rann of Kutch earthquake occurred at about 18:45 to 18:50 local time on 16 June. [CLS] [SEP] It had an estimated magnitude ranging from 7.7 to 8.2 on the moment magnitude scale and a maximum perceived intensity of XI (Extreme) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] It triggered a tsunami and caused at least 1,543 deaths. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused an area of subsidence that formed the Sindri Lake and a local zone of uplift to the north about 80 km long, 6 km wide and 6 m high that dammed the Koree / Kori / Puran / Nara river. [CLS] [SEP] This natural dam was known as the Allah Bund ("Dam of God"). [CLS] [SEP]
