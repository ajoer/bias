[CLS] The 1904 Chappaqua tornado was an intense tornado that struck northern Westchester County, New York during the afternoon of Saturday, July 16, 1904. [SEP] [CLS] As of 2019, this tornado ranks as the strongest tornado to touch down in the county, ranking as F3 on the modern-day Fujita Scale. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado formed around 3:30 pm EST within a severe thunderstorm near Chappaqua, New York. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado quickly began to produce damage in the hamlet, destroying several structures and killing two people. [SEP] [CLS] Homes were knocked off their foundations and rolled over along the tornado's path. [SEP] [CLS] By 4:00 pm EST, the tornado dissipated and left $100,000 (1904 USD; $2.4 million 2009 USD) worth of damage in its wake. [SEP] [CLS] Hail associated with the same storm cell also inflicted damage upon a few structures. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado is known as the worst disaster in the history of Chappaqua. [SEP] [CLS]
