[CLS] The Maxstoke air crash occurred on the 19 August 1918. [SEP] [CLS] A No. [SEP] [CLS] 14 Aircraft Acceptance Park Handley Page O/400 of the Royal Air Force took off from Castle Bromwich Aerodrome. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was taking part of a test flight, testing a dynamo and lighting system. [SEP] [CLS] While flying over North Warwickshire, the pilots lost control of the aircraft and crashed into a field at Maxstoke, North Warwickshire, killing all seven crew on board. [SEP] [CLS] The pilots were Canadian Lt. [SEP] [CLS] Robert Edward Andrew MacBeth and Lt. [SEP] [CLS] Frederick James Bravery. [SEP] [CLS] The other crew were air mechanics. [SEP] [CLS] Simmons were to make up war load to pilot's instructions and G. [SEP] [CLS] Greenland was responsible for the petrol pumps. [SEP] [CLS] MacBeth and Simmons were buried in the Maxstoke cemetery.The cause of the accident was determined to be loss of control due to wing failure when the aircraft lost fabric from a wing. [SEP] [CLS] It was the deadliest accident involving the Royal Air Force at the time. [SEP] [CLS]
