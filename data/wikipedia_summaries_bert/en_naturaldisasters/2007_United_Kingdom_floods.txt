A series of large floods occurred in parts of the United Kingdom during the summer of 2007. [CLS] [SEP] The worst of the flooding occurred across Scotland on 14 June; East Yorkshire and the Midlands on 15 June; Yorkshire, the Midlands, Gloucestershire, Herefordshire and Worcestershire on 25 June; and Gloucestershire, Herefordshire, Worcestershire, Oxfordshire, Berkshire and South Wales on 28 July 2007. [CLS] [SEP] June was one of the wettest months on record in Britain (see List of weather records). [CLS] [SEP] Average rainfall across the country was 5.5 inches (140 mm); more than double the June average. [CLS] [SEP] Some areas received a month's worth of precipitation in 24 hours.
