[CLS] TAM Transportes Aéreos Regionais Flight 402 was a scheduled domestic flight from São Paulo–Congonhas International Airport in São Paulo, Brazil to Recife International Airport in Recife via Santos Dumont Airport in Rio de Janeiro. [SEP] [CLS] On 31 October 1996, at 8:27 (UTC-2), the starboard engine of the Fokker 100 operating the route reversed thrust while the aircraft was climbing away from the runway at Congonhas. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft stalled and rolled beyond control to the right, then struck two buildings and crashed into several houses in a heavily populated area only 25 seconds after takeoff. [SEP] [CLS] All 95 people on board were killed, as well as another 4 on the ground. [SEP] [CLS] It is the fourth deadliest accident in Brazilian aviation history, the second at the time. [SEP] [CLS] It is also the deadliest aviation accident involving a Fokker 100. [SEP] [CLS]
