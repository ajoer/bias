[CLS] Maria of Alania (born Martha; Georgian: მართა; 1053-1118) was Byzantine empress by marriages to emperors Michael VII Doukas and Nikephoros III Botaneiates. [SEP] [CLS] Her status as empress was considered a significant success for a newly unified Kingdom of Georgia, which would achieve regional influence comparable to that of Byzantium only during the reign of Martha's nephew, King David IV, who refused to carry a Byzantine title. [SEP] [CLS] Maria was the only non-Byzantine empress of the eleventh century. [SEP] [CLS]
