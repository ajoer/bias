The 2011 Van earthquakes occurred in eastern Turkey near the city of Van. [CLS] [SEP] The first earthquake happened on 23 October at 13:41 local time. [CLS] [SEP] The shock had a Mww magnitude of 7.1 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe). [CLS] [SEP] It occurred at a shallow depth, causing heavy shaking across much of eastern Turkey and lighter tremors across neighboring parts of the South Caucasus and Levant. [CLS] [SEP] According to Disaster and Emergency Management Presidency on 30 October, the earthquake killed 604 and injured 4,152. [CLS] [SEP] At least 11,232 buildings sustained damage in the region, 6,017 of which were found to be uninhabitable. [CLS] [SEP] The uninhabitable homes left as much as 8,321 households with an average household population of around 7.6 homeless in the province; this could mean that at least around 60,000 people were left homeless. [CLS] [SEP] The other 5,215 have been damaged but are habitable. [CLS] [SEP] A separate earthquake within the same earthquake system happened on 9 November at 21:23 local time (19:23 UTC). [CLS] [SEP] 40 people were killed and 260 people were injured in the 9 November earthquake. [CLS] [SEP]
