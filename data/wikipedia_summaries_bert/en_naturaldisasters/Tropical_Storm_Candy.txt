Tropical Storm Candy produced minor impact in the state of Texas during the 1968 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] The third tropical cyclone of the annual season, it developed from a tropical disturbance in the southwestern Gulf of Mexico on June 22. [CLS] [SEP] Gradual strengthening occurred, with the depression becoming Tropical Storm Candy on the following day. [CLS] [SEP] The storm reached its peak intensity of 70 mph (110 km/h) later that day and made landfall Port Aransas, Texas on June 23. [CLS] [SEP] Candy weakened into a tropical depression only hours after moving inland. [CLS] [SEP] However, the system remained a designated cyclone until June 26, at which time it completed extratropical transition over the state of Michigan. [CLS] [SEP] Due to rainfall from a trough for several days, combined 11 inches (280 mm) in some areas from Candy itself, flooding occurred in eastern Texas; there was minor damage to crops, roads, and bridges. [CLS] [SEP] Agricultural losses alone were slightly less than $2 million (1968 USD). [CLS] [SEP] Storm surge along the coast of Texas caused "cuts" on Padre Island. [CLS] [SEP] The storm spawned 24 tornadoes, though only one caused significant impact. [CLS] [SEP] Candy and its remnants dropped rainfall in 24 other states, reaching as far north as New Hampshire. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, the system caused $2.7 million in damage and no fatalities. [CLS] [SEP]
