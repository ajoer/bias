Hurricane Martha has been the only known tropical cyclone to make landfall in Panama. [CLS] [SEP] The eighteenth named storm and twelfth hurricane of the 1969 Atlantic hurricane season, Martha developed in the southwestern Caribbean Sea on November 21. [CLS] [SEP] Initially, the storm developed with sustained winds of 50 mph (85 km/h), skipping tropical depression status. [CLS] [SEP] It remained stationary and quickly intensified into a hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] Martha attained maximum sustained winds of 90 mph (150 km/h) on November 22. [CLS] [SEP] Subsequently, Martha weakened and drifted southward. [CLS] [SEP] On November 24, Martha made landfall in Veraguas Province, Panama, as a strong tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] The system weakened to a tropical depression and dissipated over land on November 25. [CLS] [SEP] Because the storm weakened prior to landfall, strong winds were not expected or reported in the impacted countries. [CLS] [SEP] In Panama, more than 13 inches (330 mm) of precipitation may have fallen in some areas. [CLS] [SEP] Agricultural land was flooded in Almirante, Bocas del Toro and streets became inundated in low-lying areas of Puerto Armuelles, Chiriquí. [CLS] [SEP] The storm also brought significant rains to Costa Rica. [CLS] [SEP] Flooding and mudslides isolated most of the capital city of San José. [CLS] [SEP] Numerous streets were inundated in Golfito. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in Costa Rica reached $30 million (1969 USD) and 5 deaths were reported. [CLS] [SEP]
