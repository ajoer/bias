[CLS] William Eric Gallas (French pronunciation: ​[wiljam ɡalas]; born 17 August 1977) is a French retired footballer who played as a defender. [SEP] [CLS] He played most of his footballing career in France and England before finishing his career in Australia with A-League club Perth Glory. [SEP] [CLS] Gallas began his career in France, before being signed by English club Chelsea in 2001. [SEP] [CLS] He transferred to Arsenal as part of an exchange deal in 2006. [SEP] [CLS] He then signed for rivals Tottenham Hotspur in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] While his career has been successful, including Premier League titles in 2005 and 2006, Gallas has also had controversy due to on-field behaviour and friction with other personnel. [SEP] [CLS] In 2007, he was named Arsenal's captain, but was removed from the role during the 2008–09 season after criticising teammates in a media interview. [SEP] [CLS] He left Arsenal at the end of the 2009–10 season, when his contract expired, later signing for rivals Tottenham. [SEP] [CLS] He was released at the end of the 2012–13 season when his contract expired. [SEP] [CLS] He retired from professional football on 16 October 2014. [SEP] [CLS]
