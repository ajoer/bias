[CLS] Hurricane Dog was the most intense hurricane in the 1950 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] Prior to reanalysis by the Hurricane Research Division in 2014, it was considered one of the strongest Atlantic hurricanes on record, equivalent to Category 5 status on the modern Saffir-Simpson scale, with winds of 185 miles per hour (298 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] The fourth named storm of the season, Dog developed on August 30 to the east of Antigua; after passing through the northern Lesser Antilles, it turned to the north and intensified into a Category 4 hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] Dog reached its peak intensity with winds of 145 mph (230 km/h) over the open Atlantic, and after weakening it passed within 200 miles (320 km) of Cape Cod, Massachusetts. [SEP] [CLS] The storm became extratropical on September 12. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Dog caused extensive damage to the Leeward Islands, and was considered the most severe hurricane on record in Antigua. [SEP] [CLS] Many buildings were destroyed or severely damaged on the island, with thousands left homeless just weeks after Hurricane Baker caused serious damage there. [SEP] [CLS] In the United States, the hurricane caused moderate coastal damage, including damage to several boats, and resulted in 11 offshore drownings. [SEP] [CLS] Strong winds caused widespread power outages across southeastern New England. [SEP] [CLS] There were twelve people missing and assumed dead offshore Nova Scotia. [SEP] [CLS] Damage across its path totaled about $3 million (1950 USD, $26.8 million 2009 USD). [SEP] [CLS]
