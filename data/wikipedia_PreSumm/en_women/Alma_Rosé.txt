[CLS] Alma Maria Rosé (3 November 1906 – 5 April 1944) was an Austrian violinist of Jewish descent. [SEP] [CLS] Her uncle was the composer Gustav Mahler. [SEP] [CLS] She was deported by the Nazis to the concentration camp at Auschwitz-Birkenau. [SEP] [CLS] There, for 10 months, she directed an orchestra of female prisoners who played for their captors to stay alive. [SEP] [CLS] As director, Rosé held the status of kapo of the music block.Rosé died in the concentration camp of a sudden illness, possibly food poisoning. [SEP] [CLS] Her experiences in the camp were depicted in Playing for Time. [SEP] [CLS]
