[CLS] The 1992 European Community Monitor Mission helicopter downing was an incident that occurred on 7 January 1992, during the Croatian War of Independence, in which a European Community Monitor Mission (ECMM) helicopter carrying five European Community (EC) observers was downed by a Yugoslav Air Force Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-21, in the air space above the village of Podrute, near Novi Marof, Croatia. [SEP] [CLS] An Italian and a French officer and three Italian non-commissioned officers were killed. [SEP] [CLS] Another ECMM helicopter flying in formation with the attacked helicopter made an emergency landing. [SEP] [CLS] The second helicopter carried a crew and a visiting diplomat, all of whom survived. [SEP] [CLS] The incident was condemned by the United Nations Security Council and the EC. [SEP] [CLS] As a result of the incident, the Yugoslav authorities suspended the head of the air force, and the Yugoslav defense minister, General Veljko Kadijević, resigned his post. [SEP] [CLS] The events followed the end of the first stage of the war in Croatia and closely preceded the country's international recognition. [SEP] [CLS] The MiG-21 pilot, Lieutenant Emir Šišić, disappeared after the incident. [SEP] [CLS] He was tried in absentia together with his superiors by Croatian authorities, convicted, and sentenced to extended imprisonment. [SEP] [CLS] Šišić was subsequently arrested in Hungary in 2001 and extradited to Italy, where he was tried, convicted and sentenced to 15 years in prison. [SEP] [CLS] In 2006, he was turned over to Serbia for the remainder of the sentence, but released in 2008. [SEP] [CLS] Two other Yugoslav officers were tried in absentia in Italy and convicted in 2013, while Serbia was ordered to pay monetary damages to the victims' families. [SEP] [CLS] The victims were posthumously decorated by Italy and France, respectively. [SEP] [CLS]
