The 1949 Manchester BEA Douglas DC-3 accident occurred when a twin-engined British European Airways Douglas DC-3 (registration: G-AHCY) crashed on Saddleworth Moor in the Pennines near Oldham, Lancashire, after a flight from Belfast. [CLS] [SEP] The accident killed 24 of the passengers and crew on board. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft had first flown in 1944, and was captained by F. [CLS] [SEP] W. [CLS] [SEP] Pinkerton, a former RAF serviceman who, as a sergeant, had been posted missing during World War II. [CLS] [SEP] The airline was government-owned. [CLS] [SEP]
