[CLS] Yarisley Silva Rodríguez (born 1 June 1987) is a Cuban pole vaulter. [SEP] [CLS] She won the silver medal at the 2012 Summer Olympics – the first Latin American athlete to win an Olympic medal in that event. [SEP] [CLS] Silva became the first Cuban woman to reach a world class standard in the pole vault. [SEP] [CLS] Her personal bests of 4.90 m (16 ft 3⁄4 in) outdoors and 4.78 m (15 ft 8 in) indoors are the Cuban and Central American and Caribbean records for the event. [SEP] [CLS] She emerged at the regional level with a silver medal at the 2006 Central American and Caribbean Games, and a bronze medal at the 2007 Pan American Games. [SEP] [CLS] She then won gold medals at the 2009 Central American and Caribbean Championships in Athletics and the 2011 Pan American Games. [SEP] [CLS] Silva represented Cuba at the 2008 Summer Olympics and came fifth at the 2011 World Championships in Athletics. [SEP] [CLS]
