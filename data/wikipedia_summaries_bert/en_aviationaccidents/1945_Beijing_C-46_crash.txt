[CLS] The 1945 Peip'ing Curtiss C-46 Commando crash occurred on October 12, 1945, when a USAAF Curtiss C-46 Commando aircraft crashed near Nanyuan Airport in Peip'ing (now known as Beijing) while en route to there from Hankou (now part of Wuhan). [SEP] [CLS] All 59 people on board perished. [SEP] [CLS] It was the deadliest plane crash in 1945 and, at the time it occurred, the deadliest one on the soil of the Republic of China. [SEP] [CLS] The crash also is the worst ever involving the C-46.On the day of the accident, the aircraft, carrying 55 Chinese soldiers and a crew of four, neared Peip'ing in unfavorable overcast weather conditions. [SEP] [CLS] In addition, Nanyuan Airport was lacking in radio beacons, forcing the crew to rely on a commercial broadcasting station to navigate. [SEP] [CLS] On approach, the C-46 struck the station's radio antenna and crashed, killing all aboard. [SEP] [CLS]
