Subtropical Storm Alpha (also called Alfa) was an off-season subtropical cyclone that hit Georgia in May 1972. [CLS] [SEP] It developed from a previously non-tropical cyclone in the western Atlantic Ocean, and initially it moved northeastward off the Carolinas. [CLS] [SEP] The storm turned southwestward due to a building ridge, and concurrently it intensified to become Subtropical Storm Alpha. [CLS] [SEP] It later moved ashore near Savannah, and it finally dissipated in the northeast Gulf of Mexico on May 29. [CLS] [SEP] It produced wave action and moderate rainfall along the coast. [CLS] [SEP] Damage totaled over $100,000 (1972 USD), and there were two associated deaths. [CLS] [SEP]
