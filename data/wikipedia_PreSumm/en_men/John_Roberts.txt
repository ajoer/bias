[CLS] John Glover Roberts Jr. [SEP] [CLS] (born January 27, 1955) is an American lawyer and jurist who serves as Chief Justice of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Roberts has authored the majority opinion in several landmark cases, including Shelby County v. [SEP] [CLS] Holder, National Federation of Independent Business v. [SEP] [CLS] Sebelius, King v. [SEP] [CLS] Burwell, and Department of Commerce v. [SEP] [CLS] New York. [SEP] [CLS] He has been described as having a conservative judicial philosophy but has shown a willingness to work with the Supreme Court's liberal bloc, and since the retirement of Anthony Kennedy in 2018 has come to be regarded as a key swing vote on the Court. [SEP] [CLS] Roberts presided over the impeachment trial of President Donald Trump. [SEP] [CLS] Roberts grew up in northwestern Indiana and was educated in Catholic schools. [SEP] [CLS] He studied history at Harvard College and then attended Harvard Law School, where he was managing editor of the Harvard Law Review. [SEP] [CLS] He served as a law clerk for Circuit Judge Henry Friendly and then-associate justice William Rehnquist before taking a position in the attorney general's office during the Reagan Administration. [SEP] [CLS] He went on to serve the Reagan administration and the George H. [SEP] [CLS] W. [SEP] [CLS] Bush administration in the Department of Justice and the Office of the White House Counsel, before spending 14 years in private law practice. [SEP] [CLS] During this time, he argued 39 cases before the Supreme Court. [SEP] [CLS] Notably, he represented 19 states in United States v. [SEP] [CLS] Microsoft Corp.In 2003, Roberts was appointed as a judge of the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Court of Appeals for the D.C. [SEP] [CLS] Circuit by George W. [SEP] [CLS] Bush. [SEP] [CLS] During his two-year tenure on the D.C. [SEP] [CLS] Circuit, Roberts authored 49 opinions, eliciting two dissents from other judges, and authoring three dissents of his own. [SEP] [CLS] In 2005, Roberts was nominated to be an associate justice of the Supreme Court, initially to succeed the retiring Sandra Day O'Connor. [SEP] [CLS] When Rehnquist died before Roberts's confirmation hearings began, Bush instead nominated Roberts for Chief Justice and later appointed Samuel Alito as Associate Justice. [SEP] [CLS]
