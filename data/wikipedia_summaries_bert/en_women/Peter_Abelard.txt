Peter Abelard (; Latin: Petrus Abaelardus or Abailardus; French: Pierre Abélard, pronounced [a.be.laːʁ]; c. [CLS] [SEP] 1079 – 21 April 1142) was a medieval French scholastic philosopher, theologian, and preeminent logician. [CLS] [SEP] His love for, and affair with, Héloïse d'Argenteuil has become legendary. [CLS] [SEP] The Chambers Biographical Dictionary describes him as "the keenest thinker and boldest theologian of the 12th century". [CLS] [SEP]
