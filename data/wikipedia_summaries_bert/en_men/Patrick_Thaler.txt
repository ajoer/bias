[CLS] Patrick Thaler (born March 23, 1978) is a retired World Cup alpine ski racer from northern Italy. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Bolzano, South Tyrol, he specialized in the slalom. [SEP] [CLS] Thaler competed for Italy at the 2006, 2010 and 2014 Winter Olympics but failed to finish. [SEP] [CLS] His best result from Alpine World Ski Championships is a seventh place in Val-d'Isère, France, in 2009. [SEP] [CLS] During his World Cup career, he reached three third-place finishes in the slalom – twice at Kitzbühel, Austria, and once at Val d'Isère. [SEP] [CLS] In addition, he has 25 top-10 finishes. [SEP] [CLS] He is one of the oldest (35 years, 10 months, 2 days) slalom skiers to have finished in the top-3 in the World cup (in 2014) and the oldest (38 years, 11 months, 11 days) to have finished in the top-10 (in 2017).Thaler retired from ski racing on January 23, 2018, after having competed in the World Cup for a record of 22 seasons (1997–2018).
