Dame Valerie Kasanita Adams (formerly Vili; born 6 October 1984) is a New Zealand shot putter. [CLS] [SEP] She is a four-time World champion, four-time World Indoor champion, two-time Olympic, three-time Commonwealth Games champion and twice IAAF Continental Cup winner. [CLS] [SEP] She has a personal best throw of 21.24 metres outdoors and 20.54 m indoors. [CLS] [SEP] These marks are Oceanian, Commonwealth and New Zealand national records. [CLS] [SEP] She also holds the Oceanian junior record (18.93 m) and the Oceanian youth record (17.54 m), as well as the World Championships record, World Indoor Championships record and Commonwealth Games record. [CLS] [SEP] Adams was the third woman to win world championships at the youth, junior, and senior level of an athletics event, following the feats of Yelena Isinbayeva and Jana Pittman. [CLS] [SEP] She was the first woman to win four consecutive individual titles at the IAAF World Championships. [CLS] [SEP] Adams had a winning streak that extended to 56 wins at elite-level competitions, which started in August 2010 and ended in July 2015. [CLS] [SEP] She was the IAAF World Athlete of the Year in 2014 and the Track & Field News Athlete of the Year in 2012 and 2013. [CLS] [SEP] She had the longest shot put performance of the season every year from 2006 to 2014, bar 2008 when she was second to Natallia Mikhnevich (later banned for doping that year). [CLS] [SEP] Adams won silver medals at the 2016 Summer Olympics, 2005 World Championships in Athletics, and the Commonwealth Games in 2002 and 2018. [CLS] [SEP] She was also a bronze medallist at the 2016 IAAF World Indoor Championships. [CLS] [SEP] While still a teenager, Adams was a finalist at the 2003 World Championships in Athletics and the 2004 Summer Olympics. [CLS] [SEP] At national level, she has won fifteen shot put titles at the New Zealand Athletics Championships between 2001 and 2018, as well as having a hammer throw national title in 2003. [CLS] [SEP] Adams also won four times at the Australian Athletics Championships between 2004 and 2008. [CLS] [SEP] From 2006 to 2012 she was chosen as the New Zealand Sportswoman of the Year seven times consecutively and has been awarded the Lonsdale Cup on five occasions in recognition as the leading national athlete in an Olympic sport.
