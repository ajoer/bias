[CLS] Marie Gutheil-Schoder (16 February 1874 – 4 October 1935) was an important German soprano. [SEP] [CLS] Born Marie Schoder in Weimar, she married Gustav Gutheil in 1899, with whom she lived until his death in 1914. [SEP] [CLS] She debuted in the secondary role of the First Lady at the Weimar Court Opera in The Magic Flute in her native city of Weimar in 1891. [SEP] [CLS] Gustav Mahler engaged her for the Vienna State Opera in 1900, where she remained until 1926. [SEP] [CLS] She appeared at the Royal Opera House as Octavian in Der Rosenkavalier in 1913. [SEP] [CLS] One of her famous roles was her portrayal of a "strange, Nietzschean" Carmen. [SEP] [CLS] She was seen in the 1914 Vienna premiere of Richard Strauss's ballet, Josephslegende as Potiphar's Wife. [SEP] [CLS] She created Esmeralda in the world premiere of Franz Schmidt's opera Notre Dame in the same year. [SEP] [CLS] Gutheil-Schoder created the fiercely difficult single role of Arnold Schoenberg's monodrama Erwartung in 1924 in Prague; earlier that year, she performed his Pierrot lunaire. [SEP] [CLS] Mahler termed her "a musical genius," and she was highly regarded as a musician and singing-actress, although she seemed to be, as one Viennese critic wrote, "the singer without a voice." In her later career, she became a stage director of opera. [SEP] [CLS] She was a well-known pedagogue as well, one of her students being the mezzo-soprano Risë Stevens. [SEP] [CLS] She died at the age of 61, in Ilmenau, Germany. [SEP] [CLS]
