John William Coltrane (September 23, 1926 – July 17, 1967) was an American jazz saxophonist and composer. [CLS] [SEP] Working in the bebop and hard bop idioms early in his career, Coltrane helped pioneer the use of modes and was at the forefront of free jazz. [CLS] [SEP] He led at least fifty recording sessions and appeared on many albums by other musicians, including trumpeter Miles Davis and pianist Thelonious Monk. [CLS] [SEP] Over the course of his career, Coltrane's music took on an increasingly spiritual dimension. [CLS] [SEP] He remains one of the most influential saxophonists in music history. [CLS] [SEP] He received numerous posthumous awards, including canonization by the African Orthodox Church and a Pulitzer Prize in 2007. [CLS] [SEP] His second wife was pianist and harpist Alice Coltrane.
