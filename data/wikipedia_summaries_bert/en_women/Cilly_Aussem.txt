[CLS] Cilly Aussem (German pronunciation: [ˈʦiːli̯ə ˈaʊ̯sm]; 4 January 1909 – 22 March 1963) was a German female tennis player. [SEP] [CLS] She was the first German, male or female, to win the singles title at Wimbledon, which she did in 1931. [SEP] [CLS] She also won the women's single titles at the French Championships and German Championships in 1931. [SEP] [CLS] Aussem's coach and mixed doubles partner was Bill Tilden. [SEP] [CLS] They won the mixed doubles title at the 1930 French Championships. [SEP] [CLS] According to A. [SEP] [CLS] Wallis Myers of The Daily Telegraph and the Daily Mail, Aussem was ranked in the world top ten in 1928, 1930, 1931, and 1934, reaching a career high of World No. [SEP] [CLS] 2 in those rankings in 1930 and 1931 behind Helen Wills Moody.
