[CLS] Henri René Albert Guy de Maupassant (UK: , US: ; French: [ɡi d(ə) mopasɑ̃]; 5 August 1850 – 6 July 1893) was a 19th-century French author, remembered as a master of the short story form, and as a representative of the Naturalist school, who depicted human lives and destinies and social forces in disillusioned and often pessimistic terms. [SEP] [CLS] Maupassant was a protégé of Gustave Flaubert and his stories are characterized by economy of style and efficient, seemingly effortless dénouements (outcomes). [SEP] [CLS] Many are set during the Franco-Prussian War of the 1870s, describing the futility of war and the innocent civilians who, caught up in events beyond their control, are permanently changed by their experiences. [SEP] [CLS] He wrote 300 short stories, six novels, three travel books, and one volume of verse. [SEP] [CLS] His first published story, "Boule de Suif" ("The Dumpling", 1880), is often considered his masterpiece. [SEP] [CLS]
