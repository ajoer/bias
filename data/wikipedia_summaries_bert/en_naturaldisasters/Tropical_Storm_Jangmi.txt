[CLS] Tropical Storm Jangmi (pronounced [tɕaŋ.mi]), known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Seniang, was a weak but destructive tropical cyclone that impacted the Philippines during late December 2014. [SEP] [CLS] It produced heavy rainfall which caused serious flooding. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding in Philippines caused 66 deaths and at least $28.3 million damage.The last of twenty-three named storms of the annual typhoon season, the late-season cyclone remained weak throughout most of its lifespan. [SEP] [CLS] The tropical storm reached peak strength on December 29 near Surigao del Sur, Mindanao. [SEP] [CLS] Jangmi dissipated just before the 2015 Pacific typhoon season started. [SEP] [CLS]
