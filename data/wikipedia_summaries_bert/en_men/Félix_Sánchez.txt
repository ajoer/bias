[CLS] Félix Sánchez, (born August 30, 1977) is a retired Dominican-American track and field athlete. [SEP] [CLS] He is of Dominican descent, was born and raised in the United States, and competed for the Dominican Republic, specializing in the 400 meter hurdles. [SEP] [CLS] He is a two-time Olympic gold medallist, winning gold in 2004 and 2012, and was also World Champion in 2001 and 2003. [SEP] [CLS] Just before turning 36, he set the Masters M35 World Record with a time of 48.10. [SEP] [CLS] Sanchez acquired many nicknames: "Super Felix", "the Invincible", "Superman", and "the Dictator".The Félix Sánchez Olympic Stadium, the Dominican Republic’s largest stadium, is named after him. [SEP] [CLS] Sánchez retired in April 2016, citing the recent birth of his son. [SEP] [CLS] He lives in California. [SEP] [CLS]
