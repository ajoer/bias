Typhoon Zola struck Japan during August 1990. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather developed during mid-August to the west of Guam. [CLS] [SEP] The disturbance developed into a tropical depression on August 16 while tracking eastward. [CLS] [SEP] Decreased wind shear aided in intensification, and it is estimated that the depression strengthened into a tropical storm on August 17. [CLS] [SEP] Continuing to intensify, Zola turned northwest in response to a subtropical ridge to its east before obtaining typhoon intensity on August 20. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Zola reached its peak intensity the next day. [CLS] [SEP] After weakening slightly, the storm moved ashore on Honshu. [CLS] [SEP] On August 23, the system transitioned into an extratropical cyclone over the Sea of Japan. [CLS] [SEP] The typhoon dropped heavy rains across much of the Japanese archipelago. [CLS] [SEP] Near where the storm moved over ashore, in Kōchi Prefecture, three people were injured. [CLS] [SEP] Thirty-eight homes were damaged or destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Further north, 7 people were injured in Hiroshima Prefecture, where 30 houses were damaged or destroyed, and 19 others were flooded. [CLS] [SEP] Power lines were cut in 84,000 locations and 2,100 ha (5,190 acres) of farmland were damaged in the prefecture. [CLS] [SEP] Nationwide, six people were killed while twenty-four others were hurt. [CLS] [SEP] A total 67 houses were destroyed while 420 others were flooded. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 16 ships and 4,560 acres (1,845 ha) of farmland were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] Over 250 flights were called off. [CLS] [SEP] In all, damage totaled ¥15.1 billion, equal to US$104 million. [CLS] [SEP]
