Sadeh (Persian: سده‎) or Jashn-e Sadeh (Persian: جشن سده‎), is an Iranian festival celebrating the discovery of fire by Hushang, the second Shah of the mythological Pishdadian dynasty of Persian Empire. [CLS] [SEP] It is celebrated fifty days before the arrival of Nowruz in Iran and parts of Afghanistan and Tajikistan since the Achaemenid Empire. [CLS] [SEP] Sadeh means "centenary" in Persian language. [CLS] [SEP] Sadeh is a mid winter festival that was celebrated with grandeur and magnificence in ancient Iran (Persia). [CLS] [SEP]
