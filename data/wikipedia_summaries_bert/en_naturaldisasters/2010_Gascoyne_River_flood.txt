The 2010 Gascoyne River flood was regarded as the most severe flood to take place along the Gascoyne River in Western Australia on record. [CLS] [SEP] Triggered by record-breaking rainfall, amounting to over 6,000 percent of the monthly mean, 313.6 mm (12.35 in) and 5 mm (0.20 in) respectively, in just four days, the floods caused widespread damage in the region. [CLS] [SEP] By 17 December, the river began to rise in response to the heavy rains, eventually exceeding its banks within two days. [CLS] [SEP] Water levels reached record values at three stations along the river, cresting at 15.53 m (51.0 ft) near Fishy Pool. [CLS] [SEP] Evacuation orders were issued for several towns affected by rising waters. [CLS] [SEP] The most substantial impact was felt in Carnarvon where entire homes were washed away. [CLS] [SEP] Following the disaster, emergency supplies and funds were distributed to affected residents to aid them in restoring their livelihoods. [CLS] [SEP] Though no people died in the event, an estimated two thousand head of cattle perished and damage was estimated at A$100 million. [CLS] [SEP]
