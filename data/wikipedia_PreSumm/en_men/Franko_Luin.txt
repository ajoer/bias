[CLS] Franko Luin (6 April 1941 in Trieste, Italy – 15 September 2005 in Tyresö, Sweden) was a Swedish type designer of Slovene origin. [SEP] [CLS] He studied graphic arts at Grafiska Institutet in Stockholm, where he graduated in 1967. [SEP] [CLS] A graphic designer at the telecom company Ericsson (1967–1989), He started his own design shop Omnibus Typografi in 1989. [SEP] [CLS] Franko Luin had a keen interest in languages, particularly the international auxiliary language Esperanto, and was for many years president of the Swedish Esperanto association SEF. [SEP] [CLS] He wrote poems, translated songs into Esperanto and organized a well renowned homepage, the Kiosk, which had an enormous link list of online newspapers. [SEP] [CLS] In his later years, he collected and digitized many 19th and 20th century works by Slovene classical authors and distributed them on his homepage Beseda ("Word"). [SEP] [CLS]
