China Airlines Flight 642 (operated by Mandarin Airlines) was a flight that crashed at Hong Kong International Airport on 22 August 1999. [CLS] [SEP] It was operating from Bangkok (Bangkok International Airport, now renamed Don Mueang International Airport) to Taipei with a stopover in Hong Kong.The plane, a McDonnell Douglas MD-11 (registration B-150), was operated by subsidiary Mandarin Airlines on behalf of China Airlines. [CLS] [SEP] While landing during a typhoon, it touched down hard, flipped over and caught fire. [CLS] [SEP] Of the 315 people on board, 312 survived and three were killed. [CLS] [SEP] It was the first fatal accident to occur at the new Hong Kong International airport since it opened in 1998.Flight 642 was one of only two hull losses of MD-11s with passenger configuration, the other being Swissair Flight 111, which crashed in 1998 with 229 fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] All other hull losses of MD-11s have been when the aircraft has been serving as a cargo aircraft. [CLS] [SEP]
