[CLS] The 1707 Hōei earthquake (Hōei jishin 宝永地震) struck south-central Japan at 14:00 local time on 28 October. [SEP] [CLS] It was the largest earthquake in Japanese history until the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake surpassed it. [SEP] [CLS] It caused moderate to severe damage throughout southwestern Honshu, Shikoku and southeastern Kyūshū. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake, and the resulting destructive tsunami, caused more than 5,000 casualties. [SEP] [CLS] This event ruptured all of the segments of the Nankai megathrust simultaneously, the only earthquake known to have done this, with an estimated magnitude of 8.6 ML or 8.7 Mw. [SEP] [CLS] It might also have triggered the last eruption of Mount Fuji 49 days later.Hōei (宝永) was the era spanning the years from March 1704 through April 1711. [SEP] [CLS]
