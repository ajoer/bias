Lufthansa Flight 502 was a scheduled flight from Hamburg, Germany to Buenos Aires, Argentina on 11 January 1959. [CLS] [SEP] The flight was being operated by a Lockheed L-1049G Super Constellation (registration D-ALAK). [CLS] [SEP] On the leg between Senegal and Brazil the Super Constellation was on approach to Rio de Janeiro–Galeão International Airport when it crashed near Flecheiras Beach just short of the runway. [CLS] [SEP] All 29 passengers and seven of the ten crew were killed. [CLS] [SEP] It was the first fatal accident involving Lufthansa since it was formed in 1955. [CLS] [SEP]
