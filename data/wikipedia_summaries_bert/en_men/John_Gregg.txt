John Richard Gregg (born September 6, 1954) is an American politician, businessman and attorney who served as the 85th and longest-serving Democratic speaker of the Indiana House of Representatives from 1996 to 2003. [CLS] [SEP] He served in the Indiana House of Representatives from 1986 to 2003. [CLS] [SEP] Gregg served as majority leader from 1990 to 1994 and minority leader from 1994 to 1996. [CLS] [SEP] He was Indiana House speaker from 1996 to 2003. [CLS] [SEP] In 2012, Gregg was the Democratic nominee for governor of Indiana. [CLS] [SEP] He lost to then-Representative Mike Pence in the closest gubernatorial election in 50 years.Gregg won the Democratic nomination for governor again in 2016. [CLS] [SEP] He was critical of Pence's emphasis on social issues, such as his signing of the controversial Religious Freedom Restoration Act, and his policies on public education and Hoosier workers. [CLS] [SEP] Pence withdrew from the election after Donald Trump chose him as his running mate in the 2016 presidential election. [CLS] [SEP] The state Republican party then nominated Lieutenant Governor Eric Holcomb for governor. [CLS] [SEP] Holcomb defeated Gregg, 51.4% to 45.4%. [CLS] [SEP]
