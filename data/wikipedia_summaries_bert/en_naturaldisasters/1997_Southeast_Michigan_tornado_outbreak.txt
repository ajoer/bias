The Southeast Michigan Tornado Outbreak occurred on July 2, 1997, in the built-up area of Detroit, Michigan. [CLS] [SEP] There were 13 tornadoes in total, 3 dragged through neighborhoods and downtown, hitting northern Detroit between I-96 and Eight Mile Road, Hamtramck and Highland Park. [CLS] [SEP] The storms killed 7, caused local flooding, and destroyed houses. [CLS] [SEP] 5 of the fatalities were recorded in Grosse Pointe Farms, Michigan, due to straight-line winds of up to 100 mph that blew a gazebo full of people into Lake St. [CLS] [SEP] Clair. [CLS] [SEP] One tornado formed east of the Detroit River, in Essex County, Ontario, near Windsor, Ontario and caused damage in Windsor and Essex County. [CLS] [SEP] The strongest tornado was listed as an F3. [CLS] [SEP]
