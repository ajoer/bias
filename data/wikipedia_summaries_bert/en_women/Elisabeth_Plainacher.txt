[CLS] [SEP] Elsa Plainacher's parents operated a mill in Pielamund by the Danube. [CLS] [SEP] She was married three times and had children. [CLS] [SEP] When her daughter Margaret died, she became the guardian of her four grandchildren by her. [CLS] [SEP] Three of them soon died, and only one, Anna, was left. [CLS] [SEP] Anna suffered from epilepsy, which was seen as a sign of the Devil. [CLS] [SEP] Elsa was seen as responsible for the sickness of Anna and also for the deaths of her husband and three other grandchildren. [CLS] [SEP] Elsa was arrested and taken to Vienna, where her case was overseen by the Jesuit inquisitor Georg Scherer. [CLS] [SEP] During the interrogations, she confessed to anything under torture. [CLS] [SEP] She was judged guilty and sentenced to be executed by burning. [CLS] [SEP]
