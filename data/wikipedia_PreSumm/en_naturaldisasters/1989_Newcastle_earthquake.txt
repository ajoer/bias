[CLS] The 1989 Newcastle earthquake occurred in Newcastle, New South Wales on Thursday, 28 December. [SEP] [CLS] The shock measured 5.6 on the Richter magnitude scale and was one of Australia's most serious natural disasters, killing 13 people and injuring more than 160. [SEP] [CLS] The damage bill has been estimated at A$4 billion (or $8.3 billion in 2019, adjusted for inflation), including an insured loss of about $1 billion (or $2 billion in 2019, adjusted for inflation).The effects were felt over an area of around 200,000 square kilometres (77,000 sq mi) in the state of New South Wales, with isolated reports of movement in areas up to 800 kilometres (500 mi) from Newcastle. [SEP] [CLS] Damage to buildings and facilities was reported over an area of 9,000 km2 (3,500 sq mi). [SEP] [CLS]
