[CLS] Siberia Airlines Flight 1812 was a commercial flight shot down by the Ukrainian Air Force over the Black Sea on 4 October 2001, en route from Tel Aviv, Israel to Novosibirsk, Russia. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft, a Soviet-made Tupolev Tu-154, carried an estimated 66 passengers and 12 crew members. [SEP] [CLS] Most of the passengers were Israelis visiting relatives in Russia. [SEP] [CLS] There were no survivors. [SEP] [CLS] The crash site is about 190 km west-southwest of the Black Sea resort of Sochi and 140 km north of the Turkish coastal town of Fatsa and 350 km south-southeast of Feodosiya in Crimea. [SEP] [CLS] The accident took place at the time of the combat missile launches during the joint Ukrainian-Russian military air defense exercises. [SEP] [CLS] The exercises were held at the Russian-controlled training ground of the 31st Russian Black Sea Fleet Research center on Opuk cape near the city of Kerch (Crimea). [SEP] [CLS] Ukraine eventually admitted that it might have caused the crash, probably by an errant S-200 missile fired by its armed forces. [SEP] [CLS] Ukraine paid $15 million to surviving family members of the 78 victims ($200,000 per victim). [SEP] [CLS]
