The 2006 Central Pacific cyclone, also known as Invest 91C or Storm 91C, was an unusual weather system that formed in 2006. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on October 30 from a mid-latitude cyclone in the north Pacific mid-latitudes, it moved over waters warmer than normal. [CLS] [SEP] The system acquired some features more typical of subtropical and even tropical cyclones. [CLS] [SEP] However, as it neared western North America, the system fell apart, dissipating soon after landfall, on November 4. [CLS] [SEP] Moisture from the storm's remnants caused substantial rainfall in British Columbia and the Pacific Northwest. [CLS] [SEP] The exact status and nature of this weather event is unknown, with meteorologists and weather agencies having differing opinions. [CLS] [SEP]
