The 1942 Guatemala earthquake occurred at 17:37 local time on August 6 and had ratings of 7.7 on the moment magnitude scale and 7.9 on the surface wave magnitude scale. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located off the southern coast of Guatemala, and it was one of the strongest earthquakes ever recorded there. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused widespread damage in the west-central highlands of Guatemala. [CLS] [SEP] Thirty-eight people died in the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] Landslides caused by the combination of the earthquake and the heavy seasonal rains destroyed roads, the Inter-American Highway, and telegraph lines. [CLS] [SEP] In Tecpán Guatemala, more than 60% of the houses were demolished. [CLS] [SEP] Damage was reported in some buildings in Antigua Guatemala, including the Palacio de Los Capitanes Generales and some catholic churches. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake could also be felt strongly in Mexico and El Salvador.This earthquake was a lower crustal intraplate earthquake with a compressional focal mechanism. [CLS] [SEP] Tensional activity has been dominant along the down-dip edge along the 1942 rupture zone. [CLS] [SEP] It was estimated that earthquakes near the Middle America Trench with magnitudes of about 7.5~8.0 occurred at intervals of 94 ± 54 yrs in southwestern Guatemala. [CLS] [SEP] Such historical earthquakes included the earthquakes in 1765, 1902, and 1942. [CLS] [SEP]
