[CLS] Ricardo Froilán Lagos Escobar (Spanish pronunciation: [riˈkaɾðo fɾojˈlan ˈlaɣos eskoˈβaɾ]; born 2 March 1938) is a Chilean lawyer, economist and social-democratic politician who served as President of Chile from 2000 to 2006. [SEP] [CLS] He won the 1999-2000 presidential election by a narrow margin in a runoff over Independent Democrat Union (UDI) candidate Joaquín Lavín. [SEP] [CLS] Lagos was the third president from the center-left Coalition of Parties for Democracy to have ruled Chile since 1990. [SEP] [CLS] He was succeeded on March 11, 2006 by Socialist Michelle Bachelet, from the same coalition. [SEP] [CLS] Since May 2007 he has served as a Special Envoy on Climate Change for the United Nations Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon. [SEP] [CLS]
