[CLS] West Caribbean Airways Flight 708 was a West Caribbean Airways charter flight which crashed in a mountainous region in northwest Venezuela in the early hours of Tuesday, 16 August 2005, killing all 160 passengers and crew on board. [SEP] [CLS] The plane, a McDonnell Douglas MD-82, registration HK-4374X, was en route from Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City, Panama to Martinique Aimé Césaire International Airport (FDF) in Fort-de-France, Martinique, France. [SEP] [CLS] While flying at 33,000 feet (10,000 m), the aircraft's speed gradually decreased until it entered an aerodynamic stall. [SEP] [CLS] The crew, probably under the mistaken belief that the aircraft had suffered a double engine flame-out, did not take the necessary actions to recover from the stall. [SEP] [CLS] The confusion and lack of action resulted in the crash.All the passengers were French citizens from Martinique, with the exception of one Italian, acting as the tour operator. [SEP] [CLS] The crew was Colombian. [SEP] [CLS] The flight was chartered by the Globe Trotters de Rivière Salée travel agency in Martinique. [SEP] [CLS] Most of the passengers were tourists returning from a week's vacation in Panama. [SEP] [CLS] The 160-person death toll made the accident the deadliest of 2005, the deadliest aviation disaster to occur in Venezuela, and the deadliest involving a McDonnell Douglas MD-82.
