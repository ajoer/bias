Mercedes Valdivieso (March 1, 1924 â€“ August 3, 1993) was a Chilean writer, known since her earliest writings for the subversive nature of her texts. [CLS] [SEP] She was born in Santiago, Chile. [CLS] [SEP] She first wrote La Brecha (Breakthrough) in 1961, which is considered to be a landmark feminist Latin-American novel. [CLS] [SEP] This novel caused dismay from the reactionary segment of society and loud applause from the critics and is considered a revolutionary departure from the traditional treatment of the feminine role in marriage. [CLS] [SEP] Breakthrough is a novel that ends with the heroine's awareness that she didn't really need to depend upon a man in order to lead a fulfilling life. [CLS] [SEP] The book enjoyed an unexpected publishing success and went through five consecutive editions. [CLS] [SEP] Mercedes Valdivieso had the extreme audacity to become an innovator; she bridged the gap between romantic and domestic fiction in a society where women have been viewed as a sexless gender, icons of virtue, and depending on men to meet the necessities of life. [CLS] [SEP] Valdivieso also was founder and director of Adan, a men's magazine, and Breakthrough, a feminist publication, she published articles in newspapers and magazines and she gave many lectures and speeches.
