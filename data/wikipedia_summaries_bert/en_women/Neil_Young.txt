Neil Percival Young (born November 12, 1945) is a Canadian-American singer-songwriter and activist. [CLS] [SEP] After embarking on a music career in the 1960s, he moved to Los Angeles, joining Buffalo Springfield with Stephen Stills, Richie Furay and others. [CLS] [SEP] Since his early solo albums and those with his backing band Crazy Horse, Young has been prolific, recording a steady stream of studio and live albums. [CLS] [SEP] Young has received several Grammy and Juno Awards. [CLS] [SEP] The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inducted him twice: in 1995 as a solo artist and in 1997 as a member of Buffalo Springfield. [CLS] [SEP] In 2000, Rolling Stone named Young the 34th greatest rock 'n roll artist. [CLS] [SEP] His guitar work, deeply personal lyrics and signature tenor singing voice define his long career. [CLS] [SEP] He also plays piano and harmonica on many albums, which frequently combine folk, rock, country and other musical styles. [CLS] [SEP] His often distorted electric guitar playing, especially with Crazy Horse, earned him the nickname "Godfather of Grunge" and led to his 1995 album Mirror Ball with Pearl Jam. [CLS] [SEP] More recently he has been backed by Promise of the Real. [CLS] [SEP] His 22 albums and singles have been certified Gold and Platinum in U.S by RIAA certification with estimated worldwide sales more than 75 million records.Young directed (or co-directed) films using the pseudonym Bernard Shakey, including Journey Through the Past (1973), Rust Never Sleeps (1979), Human Highway (1982), Greendale (2003), and CSNY/Déjà Vu (2008). [CLS] [SEP] He also contributed to the soundtracks of the films Philadelphia (1993) and Dead Man (1995). [CLS] [SEP] Young has lived in California since the 1960s but retains Canadian citizenship. [CLS] [SEP] He was awarded the Order of Manitoba on July 14, 2006, and was made an Officer of the Order of Canada on December 30, 2009. [CLS] [SEP] He became a United States citizen, taking dual citizenship, on January 22, 2020. [CLS] [SEP]
