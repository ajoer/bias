[CLS] Magdalena Wilhelmine of Württemberg (7 November 1677, Stuttgart – 30 October 1742, Karlsburg Castle, Durlach) was a margravine of Baden. [SEP] [CLS] She had a place in the regency during the minority of her grandson in 1738-42. [SEP] [CLS] She was the daughter of Duke William Louis of Württemberg and Landgravine Magdalena Sibylla of Hesse-Darmstadt. [SEP] [CLS] In order to strengthen the ties between Baden and Württemberg, she married on 27 June 1697 the Hereditary Prince of Baden and later Margrave Charles William of Baden-Durlach. [SEP] [CLS] As Magdalena Wilhelmine had a big nose and blemishes, she did not meet the ideals of beauty held by Karl Wilhelm, who loved beautiful women. [SEP] [CLS] After she bore him a son and heir, the couple separated. [SEP] [CLS] When in 1715 Charles William founded his new residence Karlsruhe, he alone moved into the new palace, while his wife remained in the Karlsburg Castle. [SEP] [CLS] After Charles III William died in 1738, Magdalena Wilhelmine held a post in the guardian government for her nine-year-old grandson Charles Frederick. [SEP] [CLS] After her death she was buried in the margraviate tomb in St. [SEP] [CLS] Michael's church in Pforzheim . [SEP] [CLS]
