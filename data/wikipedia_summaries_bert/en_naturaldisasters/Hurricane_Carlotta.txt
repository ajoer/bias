[CLS] Hurricane Carlotta was the easternmost tropical cyclone in the Eastern Pacific to make landfall at hurricane intensity since 1966. [SEP] [CLS] The third tropical cyclone and third named storm of the 2012 Pacific hurricane season, Carlotta developed slowly into a tropical depression from a tropical wave southwest of Central America on June 14. [SEP] [CLS] It moved generally west-northwestward and by the following day, strengthened into tropical storm strength. [SEP] [CLS] Thereafter, gradual intensification occurred and the storm reached hurricane strength on June 15. [SEP] [CLS] Rapid intensification ensued further, as Carlotta peaked as a 110 mph (175 km/h) Category 2 hurricane on the same day. [SEP] [CLS] At 0100 UTC the following day, Carlotta made landfall near Puerto Escondido, the easternmost landfalling Pacific hurricane in recorded history at the time. [SEP] [CLS] The next day the storm began to weaken as it moved onshore Southwestern Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] Carlotta continued to weaken rapidly, eventually dissipating on June 16. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout Mexico, widespread power outages and wind damage was reported, particularly in Oaxaca. [SEP] [CLS] Infrastructural damage was also reported due to winds and rain, as well as damage to crops. [SEP] [CLS] Rainfall from Carlotta peaked at 13.78 in (35.0 cm) in San Juan Bautista Tuxtepec. [SEP] [CLS] Numerous mudslides resulted from the heavy rains, which blocked roads and damaged structures. [SEP] [CLS] At least 29,000 homes and 2,500 businesses sustained damage from Carlotta, mostly in Oaxaca. [SEP] [CLS] Seven people were killed by Carlotta, and the state of Oaxaca requested MX$1.44 billion (US$113 million) for repairs to public infrastructure. [SEP] [CLS]
