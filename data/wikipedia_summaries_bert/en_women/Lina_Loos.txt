Linalool () refers to two enantiomers of a naturally occurring terpene alcohol found in many flowers and spice plants. [CLS] [SEP] These have multiple commercial applications, the majority of which are based on its pleasant scent (floral, with a touch of spiciness).The word linalool is based on linaloe (a type of wood) and the suffix -ol. [CLS] [SEP] It has other names such as Î²-linalool, linalyl alcohol, linaloyl oxide, allo-ocimenol, and 3,7-dimethyl-1,6-octadien-3-ol. [CLS] [SEP]
