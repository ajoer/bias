The Bonneville flood was a catastrophic flooding event in the last ice age, which involved massive amounts of water inundating parts of southern Idaho and eastern Washington along the course of the Snake River. [CLS] [SEP] Unlike the Missoula Floods, which also occurred during the same period in the Pacific Northwest, the Bonneville flood only happened once. [CLS] [SEP] The flood is believed to be the second largest in known geologic history. [CLS] [SEP]
