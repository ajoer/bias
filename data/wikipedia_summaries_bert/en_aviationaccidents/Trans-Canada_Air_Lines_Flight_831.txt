Trans-Canada Air Lines (TCA) Flight 831 was a flight from Montréal–Dorval International Airport to Toronto International Airport on November 29, 1963. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was a four-engine Douglas DC-8-54CF airliner, registered CF-TJN. [CLS] [SEP] About five minutes after takeoff in poor weather, the jet crashed about 20 miles (32 km) north of Montreal, near Ste-Thérèse-de-Blainville, Quebec, Canada, killing all 111 passengers and seven crew members. [CLS] [SEP] The crash was the deadliest in Canadian history at the time. [CLS] [SEP] It was also the deadliest crash of a DC-8 at the time, and, as of 2020, the sixth-deadliest. [CLS] [SEP]
