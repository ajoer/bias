The 1962 Buin Zahra earthquake occurred on September 1 in the area of Buin Zahra, Qazvin Province, Iran. [CLS] [SEP] The shock had a Richter magnitude of 7.1 and resulted in 12,225 fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] Qazvin Province lies in an area of Iran that experiences large earthquakes. [CLS] [SEP] The 1962 event originated on one of many faults in the area, called the Ipak Fault. [CLS] [SEP] Believed to have been reactivated multiple times, the fault is extensive and could still pose a threat to locals. [CLS] [SEP] Iran's building codes, renowned for performing poorly during earthquakes, were recently evaluated by multiple world organizations. [CLS] [SEP] Most hope that the Iranian government will implement a better quality of design, highlighting that Iran is among the most seismically active countries in the world. [CLS] [SEP]
