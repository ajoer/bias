Masashi Kishimoto (岸本 斉史, Kishimoto Masashi, born November 8, 1974) is a Japanese manga artist. [CLS] [SEP] His best known work, Naruto, was in serialization from 1999 to 2014 and has sold over 250 million copies worldwide in 46 countries as of May 2019. [CLS] [SEP] The series has been adapted into three anime and multiple films, video games, and related media. [CLS] [SEP] Besides the Naruto manga, Kishimoto also personally supervised the two canonical anime films, The Last: Naruto the Movie and Boruto: Naruto the Movie, and has written several one-shot stories. [CLS] [SEP] In 2019, Kishimoto started writing the manga Samurai 8: The Tale of Hachimaru. [CLS] [SEP] A reader of manga ever since a young age, Kishimoto showed a desire to write his own manga, citing authors Akira Toriyama and Katsuhiro Otomo as his main inspirations. [CLS] [SEP] As a result, Kishimoto spent several years working to write his own shōnen manga for Weekly Shōnen Jump magazine which he was a fan of. [CLS] [SEP]
