On October 14, 2016, a rare tornado struck the city of Manzanita, Oregon. [CLS] [SEP] A powerful extratropical cyclone traversing the Pacific Ocean produced localized supercell thunderstorms along coastal Oregon. [CLS] [SEP] The Portland branch of the National Weather Service issued a record-breaking ten tornado warnings that morning for their forecast area. [CLS] [SEP] One particular cell spawned an EF2 tornado at 8:18 a.m. [CLS] [SEP] PDT (15:18 UTC) which traveled through the center of Manzanita. [CLS] [SEP] Although it lasted only two minutes, the tornado damaged 128 homes, rendered one uninhabitable, and downed one-third of the city's trees. [CLS] [SEP] No injuries were reported and damage reached $1 million. [CLS] [SEP] A state of emergency was declared for Manzanita immediately after the tornado; however, clean-up operations had to be delayed for two days as another storm system impacted the region. [CLS] [SEP] In the months following the tornado, approximately $70,000 was raised through various donations. [CLS] [SEP]
