[CLS] The tornado outbreak of March 6–7, 2017 was a widespread severe weather and tornado outbreak that affected portions of the Midwestern United States in the overnight hours of March 6–7. [SEP] [CLS] Occurring just days after a deadly and more significant event across similar areas just a week prior, this particular outbreak led to 63 tornadoes within a 9​1⁄2-hour period as a quasi-linear convective system and discrete supercell thunderstorms traversed the region. [SEP] [CLS] The most notable aspect of the outbreak was an EF3 tornado that damaged or destroyed hundreds of structures within Oak Grove, Missouri, injuring 12 people but causing no fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] An EF1 tornado touched down near Bricelyn, the earliest known tornado on record in the state of Minnesota. [SEP] [CLS] Outside of tornadic activity, hundreds of damaging wind reports and a multitude of severe hail reports were documented. [SEP] [CLS]
