[CLS] Didier Squiban (born 23 September 1959 in Ploudalmézeau (Finistère)) is a French pianist and composer. [SEP] [CLS] His musical work is a combination of traditional Breton music, jazz improvisation and classical romanticism and has added the piano to the repertoire of modern Breton music. [SEP] [CLS] He has been influenced by Duke Ellington, Keith Jarrett, Charlie Parker and Bill Evans as well as Debussy, Stravinsky, Erik Satie, Darius Milhaud, Schönberg and Glenn Gould. [SEP] [CLS] In 1993, he worked as the accompanying pianist for the Breton singer Yann-Fañch Kemener in the acclaimed live show Héritage des Celtes (The Heritage of the Celts) and thereby got closer in touch with the music of his home region Brittany. [SEP] [CLS] In 1997, on the island of his ancestors, Molène, he recorded his first Breton solo piano album: "Molène". [SEP] [CLS]
