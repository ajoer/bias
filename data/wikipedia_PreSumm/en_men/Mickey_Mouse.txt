[CLS] Mickey Mouse is a cartoon character and the mascot of The Walt Disney Company. [SEP] [CLS] He was created by Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks at the Walt Disney Studios in 1928. [SEP] [CLS] An anthropomorphic mouse who typically wears red shorts, large yellow shoes, and white gloves, Mickey is one of the world's most recognizable characters. [SEP] [CLS] Created as a replacement for a prior Disney character, Oswald the Lucky Rabbit, Mickey first appeared in the short Plane Crazy, debuting publicly in the short film Steamboat Willie (1928), one of the first sound cartoons. [SEP] [CLS] He went on to appear in over 130 films, including The Band Concert (1935), Brave Little Tailor (1938), and Fantasia (1940). [SEP] [CLS] Mickey appeared primarily in short films, but also occasionally in feature-length films. [SEP] [CLS] Ten of Mickey's cartoons were nominated for the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film, one of which, Lend a Paw, won the award in 1942. [SEP] [CLS] In 1978, Mickey became the first cartoon character to have a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. [SEP] [CLS] Beginning in 1930, Mickey has also been featured extensively as a comic strip character. [SEP] [CLS] The Mickey Mouse comic strip, drawn primarily by Floyd Gottfredson, ran for 45 years. [SEP] [CLS] Mickey has also appeared in comic books such as Disney Italy's Topolino, MM – Mickey Mouse Mystery Magazine, and Wizards of Mickey, and in television series such as The Mickey Mouse Club (1955–1996) and others. [SEP] [CLS] He also appears in other media such as video games as well as merchandising and is a meetable character at the Disney parks. [SEP] [CLS] Mickey generally appears alongside his girlfriend Minnie Mouse, his pet dog Pluto, his friends Donald Duck and Goofy, and his nemesis Pete, among others (see Mickey Mouse universe). [SEP] [CLS] Though originally characterized as a cheeky lovable rogue, Mickey was rebranded over time as a nice guy, usually seen as an honest and bodacious hero. [SEP] [CLS] In 2009, Disney began to rebrand the character again by putting less emphasis on his friendly, well-meaning persona and reintroducing the more menacing and stubborn sides of his personality, beginning with the video game Epic Mickey.
