[CLS] The tornado outbreak of March 15–17, 1942, was a deadly late-winter tornado outbreak which struck a large area of the Central and Southern United States. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado outbreak killed 149 people and injured at least 1,312. [SEP] [CLS] At least five states reported violent tornadoes, from Illinois and Indiana south to Mississippi, beginning with an F4 tornado in the morning in Illinois. [SEP] [CLS] Intense activity spread south to the Gulf Coast and north to the Michigan–Indiana border as the day went on. [SEP] [CLS] Seven violent tornadoes were reported, one of which was a powerful F5 in Illinois. [SEP] [CLS] The outbreak also produced eighteen tornadoes that caused at least one death, one of the highest such totals for a single outbreak. [SEP] [CLS]
