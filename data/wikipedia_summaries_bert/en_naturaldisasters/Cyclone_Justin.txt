Cyclone Justin was a tropical cyclone of the 1996–97 Australian region cyclone season that caused seven deaths and had a major economic impact in northern Queensland, Australia. [CLS] [SEP] It had a long ​3 1⁄2-week life from 6 March to 24 March 1997. [CLS] [SEP] Peaking as a Category 3 cyclone and making landfall as a Category 2, it caused significant damage in the Cairns region, which it approached on two occasions. [CLS] [SEP] Houses were undermined by huge waves, a marina and boats were severely damaged, roads and bridges suffered from flood and landslide damage and huge losses were inflicted on sugar cane, fruit and vegetable crops. [CLS] [SEP] The death toll in Queensland was seven including five on a yacht which sank. [CLS] [SEP] There were 26 who died in Papua New Guinea, which was also severely affected. [CLS] [SEP] Total estimated costs from damages in Australia were $190 million (1997 values). [CLS] [SEP]
