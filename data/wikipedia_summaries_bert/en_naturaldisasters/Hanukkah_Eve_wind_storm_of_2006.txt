[CLS] The Hanukkah Eve windstorm of 2006 was a powerful Pacific Northwest windstorm in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States and southern British Columbia, Canada between December 14, 2006 and December 15, 2006. [SEP] [CLS] The storm produced hurricane-force wind gusts and heavy rainfall, causing hundreds of millions of dollars in damage and leaving over 1.8 million residences and businesses without power. [SEP] [CLS] Eighteen people were killed, most of whom died of carbon monoxide poisoning in the days following the storm because of improper use of barbecue cookers and generators indoors. [SEP] [CLS] The name of the storm was chosen in a contest run by the National Weather Service office in Seattle from about 8,000 entries. [SEP] [CLS]
