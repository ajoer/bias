Cyclonic Storm Nilam was the deadliest tropical cyclone to directly affect South India since Cyclone Jal in 2010. [CLS] [SEP] Originating from an area of low pressure over the Bay of Bengal on October 28, 2012, the system began as a weak depression 550 km (340 mi) northeast of Trincomalee, Sri Lanka. [CLS] [SEP] Over the following few days, the depression gradually intensified into a deep depression, and subsequently a cyclonic storm by October 30. [CLS] [SEP] It made landfall near Mahabalipuram on October 31 as a strong cyclonic storm with peak winds of 85 km/h (50 mph). [CLS] [SEP] In Chennai's Marina Beach, strong winds pushed piles of sand ashore and seawater reached nearly a 100 m (330 ft) inland. [CLS] [SEP] Schools and colleges in the city remained closed for more than three days.More than 3000 people were evacuated around Mahabalipuram in the wake of the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Schools and colleges in Chennai declared holidays until November 1 as 282 schools had been converted into relief centers. [CLS] [SEP] Government offices and private organisations closed their operations by 3 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] to ease traffic congestion. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone shelters had been arranged in Nagapattinam and Cuddalore districts. [CLS] [SEP] Mahabalipuram faced power outages and 90 trees were uprooted and two huts were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] While damages to property were considerable, human casualties were very few. [CLS] [SEP] Nilam was initially estimated to have caused economic losses of around ₹700 million (US$9.8 million) to ₹800 million (US$11 million). [CLS] [SEP] The figures soon went up to ₹100 crore (US$14 million). [CLS] [SEP] Later, as the Andhra Pradesh government conducted their review on the storm, it was revealed that the state suffered huge economic losses of ₹200 crore (US$28 million).In Bangalore, heavy rains and strong winds felled over 86 trees. [CLS] [SEP] One fell on the compound wall of a police commissioner's residence, causing structural damage. [CLS] [SEP] In Basava Road, a tree pulled down power lines, along with six electric poles outing power in the locality. [CLS] [SEP] Over 3,000 tonnes of debris was cleared out using 285 trucks. [CLS] [SEP] 87.5 millimetres (3.44 in) of rainfall was recorded in the city with a maximum sustained windspeed of 47 km/h (29 mph). [CLS] [SEP]
