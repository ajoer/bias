The 2010 Ladakh floods occurred on 6 August 2010 across a large part of the Ladakh region of Indian-administered Kashmir. [CLS] [SEP] 71 towns and villages were damaged, including the main town in the area, Leh. [CLS] [SEP] At least 255 people are reported to have died, six of whom were foreign tourists, after a cloudburst and heavy overnight rains triggered flash floods, mudflows, and debris flows. [CLS] [SEP] 200 people were reported missing in the initial aftermath of the storm, and thousands more were rendered homeless after the flooding caused extensive damage to property and infrastructure. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, 9000 people were directly affected by the event. [CLS] [SEP]
