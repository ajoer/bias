Tan-Sahsa Flight 414 was a scheduled flight from Juan Santamaría International Airport, San José, Costa Rica to Toncontin Airport in Tegucigalpa, Honduras, with a stopover at Augusto C. [CLS] [SEP] Sandino Airport in Managua, Nicaragua on 21 October 1989. [CLS] [SEP] Flown with a Boeing 727-200, the flight crashed into a hill of the mountain range after the pilots failed to follow a special landing procedure required for the arrival to the airport, killing 131 passengers, leaving 15 survivors. [CLS] [SEP] 20 initially survived, but five died before treatment, due to a delay in rescue personnel from the bad weather. [CLS] [SEP] This accident is the deadliest accident in Central American history. [CLS] [SEP]
