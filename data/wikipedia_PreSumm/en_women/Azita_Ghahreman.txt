[CLS] Azita Ghahreman (born 1962 Mashhad, Iran) is an Iranian poet. [SEP] [CLS] She has written six books in Persian and three books in Swedish. [SEP] [CLS] She has also translated American poetry. [SEP] [CLS] She has published four collections of poetry: Eve's Songs (1983), Sculptures of Autumn (1986), Forgetfulness is a Simple Ritual (1992) and The Suburb of Crows (2008), a collection reflecting on her exile in Sweden (she lives in an area called oxie on the outskirts of Malm√∂) that was published in both Swedish and Persian. [SEP] [CLS] A collection of Azita's work was published in Swedish in 2009, alongside the work of Sohrab Rahimi and Kristian Carlsson . [SEP] [CLS] She has also translated a collection of poems by the American poet and cartoonist, Shel Silverstein, into Persian, The Place Where the Sidewalk Ends (2000). [SEP] [CLS] She has edited three volumes of poems by poets from Khorasan, the eastern province of Iran, which has a rich and distinctive history. [SEP] [CLS] Azita's poems have been translated into various languages including English by Poetry Translation Centre. [SEP] [CLS] A new book of poetry, Under Hypnosis in Dr Caligari's Cabinet was published in Sweden in April 2012. [SEP] [CLS]
