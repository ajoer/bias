Paul Frederic Simon (born October 13, 1941) is an American musician, singer, songwriter and actor. [CLS] [SEP] Simon's musical career has spanned over six decades. [CLS] [SEP] He reached fame and commercial success as half of the duo Simon & Garfunkel, formed in 1956 with Art Garfunkel. [CLS] [SEP] Simon wrote nearly all of their songs, including US number-one singles "The Sound of Silence", "Mrs. [CLS] [SEP] Robinson", and "Bridge over Troubled Water".After Simon & Garfunkel split up in 1970, at the height of their popularity, Simon began a successful solo career. [CLS] [SEP] He recorded three acclaimed albums over the following five years. [CLS] [SEP] In 1986, following a career slump, he released Graceland, an album inspired by South African township music, which sold 14 million copies worldwide and remains his most popular solo work. [CLS] [SEP] Simon also wrote and starred in the film One-Trick Pony (1980) and co-wrote the Broadway musical The Capeman (1998) with the poet Derek Walcott. [CLS] [SEP] On June 3, 2016, Simon released his 13th solo album, Stranger to Stranger, which debuted at number one on the Billboard Album Chart and the UK Albums Chart. [CLS] [SEP] Simon has earned sixteen Grammy awards for his solo and collaborative work, including three for Album of the Year (Bridge Over Troubled Water, Still Crazy After All These Years, and Graceland), and a Lifetime Achievement Award. [CLS] [SEP] He is a two-time inductee into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame: first in 1990 as a member of Simon & Garfunkel and again in 2001 for his solo career. [CLS] [SEP] In 2006 he was selected as one of the "100 People Who Shaped the World" by Time. [CLS] [SEP] In 2011, Rolling Stone named Simon one of the 100 greatest guitarists, and in 2015 he was ranked eighth in their list of the 100 Greatest Songwriters of All Time. [CLS] [SEP] Simon was the first recipient of the Library of Congress's Gershwin Prize for Popular Song in 2007. [CLS] [SEP]
