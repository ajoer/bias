[CLS] The 1992 Atlantic hurricane season was a well below average hurricane season that had one of the latest dates on record for the first named storm. [SEP] [CLS] The season officially began on June 1, 1992, and lasted until November 30, 1992. [SEP] [CLS] The first storm, an unnamed subtropical storm, developed in the central Atlantic on April 21, over a month before the official start of hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The most significant storm of the season was Hurricane Andrew, which at the time was the costliest U.S. [SEP] [CLS] hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] After crossing the Bahamas, the hurricane made landfall in Florida and Louisiana. [SEP] [CLS] It caused $27.3 billion (1992 USD) in damage, mostly in Florida, and 65 fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] Andrew was also the strongest hurricane of the season, reaching winds of 175 mph (282 km/h) while approaching Florida. [SEP] [CLS] Unusually, hurricanes Bonnie and Charley produced tropical storm-force winds in the Azores, and the former caused one fatality. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Danielle was one of few tropical cyclones known to make landfall on the Delmarva Peninsula. [SEP] [CLS] The storm caused minor damage and two fatalities in the Mid-Atlantic and New England regions of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] One other hurricane in the season—Frances—did not significantly affect land. [SEP] [CLS] The system developed in the central Atlantic, and tracked well away from land, and brought only light rainfall to Newfoundland. [SEP] [CLS] In addition to the seven storms, there were three non-developing tropical depressions. [SEP] [CLS] The first depression of the season caused flooding in Cuba and Florida while the other two depressions did not affect any land. [SEP] [CLS] Collectively, the storms in the 1992 Atlantic hurricane season caused $27.3 billion in losses and 73 fatalities. [SEP] [CLS]
