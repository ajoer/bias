[CLS] The 2010 Israeli Air Force Sikorsky CH-53 crash refers to an aviation accident which occurred on 26 July 2010, when an Israeli Air Force Sikorsky CH-53 Sea Stallion Yas'ur helicopter crashed during a training flight in the Carpathian mountains, near the city of Brasov in Romania. [SEP] [CLS] The accident took place during a joint Romanian-Israeli aviation military exercise code-named "Blue Sky 2010". [SEP] [CLS] All on board the aircraft died: four Israeli pilots, two Israeli mechanics, and one Romanian liaison officer. [SEP] [CLS] The Romanian authorities sent police and security forces into the mountain the morning after the accident. [SEP] [CLS] In addition, Israel sent a large team to assist in the rescue operation. [SEP] [CLS] After an extensive search in the crash site, on 27 July 2010, the bodies were eventually found. [SEP] [CLS] An investigation into the incident concluded that the crash was most likely due to human error. [SEP] [CLS]
