[CLS] The FIS Ski Jumping Continental Cup is a series of ski jumping competitions arranged yearly by the International Ski Federation. [SEP] [CLS] It is considered the second level of international ski jumping, ranking below the World Cup and not counting Grand Prix which world top class summer competition. [SEP] [CLS] Athletes competing in the Continental Cup are usually juniors and jumpers fighting for a spot on their nation's World Cup team. [SEP] [CLS] Some jumpers alternate between the World Cup and the Continental Cup and therefore, the winner of the Continental Cup is not necessarily the best jumper. [SEP] [CLS] International Ski Federation considers the last two Europa Cup seasons in 1991/92 and 1992/93 where they competed only in Europe and with only European ski jumpers, as first two continental cup season. [SEP] [CLS] However, men officially began first season in 1993/94 spreading, with hosts spreading from Europe to Asian and North American ground. [SEP] [CLS] Competitors from United States, Canada and Asia previously competed in their own Pacific Rum Cup which was canceled, joined with Europeans on the second level of world ski jumping competition. [SEP] [CLS] Summer continental cup event was first time organized in 1996, however those summer events counted together in joined overall winter ranking until 2001/02. [SEP] [CLS] But from the season 2002/03 on, summer events counts in separated men's summer rankings. [SEP] [CLS] The ladies competition was introduced in the 2004/05. [SEP] [CLS] Summer events organized already in first season like with men counted together in joined overall winter ranking until 2007/08. [SEP] [CLS] But from the 2008/09 on, summer events counts in separated ladies' summer rankings. [SEP] [CLS] Until the 2010/11 this was the ladies' top international ski jumping competition and the season later, World Cup for ladies was introduced in 2011/12. [SEP] [CLS] So far only four individual an none of the team events in this competition have been held on ski flying hills: two events in Ironwood (1994) and two events in Vikersund (2004). [SEP] [CLS] Higher competitive circuits are the World Cup and the Summer Grand Prix; the lower circuits include the FIS Cup, the FIS Race and the Alpen Cup. [SEP] [CLS]
