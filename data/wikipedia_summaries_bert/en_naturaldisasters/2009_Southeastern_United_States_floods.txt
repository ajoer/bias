The 2009 Southeastern United States floods were a group of floods that affected several counties throughout northern Georgia, Tennessee, Alabama, Mississippi, and Arkansas. [CLS] [SEP] The worst flooding occurred across the Atlanta metropolitan area. [CLS] [SEP] Continuous rain, spawned by moisture pulled from the Gulf of Mexico, fell faster than the local watersheds could drain the runoff. [CLS] [SEP] Initial damages from around the state were estimated at $250 million. [CLS] [SEP] On September 26, Georgia Insurance Commissioner John Oxendine raised the estimated cost to $500 million with the potential for it to rise. [CLS] [SEP] Some 20,000 homes, businesses and other buildings received major damage and 17 Georgia counties received Federal Disaster Declarations. [CLS] [SEP] The flood is blamed for at least ten deaths.The floods were historic, breaking records that went back more than a century in some locations. [CLS] [SEP] The Chattahoochee River, the largest river in the region, measured water levels at a 500-year flood level. [CLS] [SEP]
