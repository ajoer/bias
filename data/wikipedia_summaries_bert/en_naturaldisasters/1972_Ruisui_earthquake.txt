The 1972 Ruisui earthquake (also known as the 1972 Juisui earthquake) occurred on April 24 at 17:57 local time. [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude of this earthquake was given as Ms 7.2 by the United States Geological Survey and ML 6.9 by the Central Weather Bureau of Taiwan. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located near Ruisui Township, Hualien County, Taiwan. [CLS] [SEP] The intensity was shindo 4 in Taipei and Hualien. [CLS] [SEP] Five people were reported dead. [CLS] [SEP] The Ruisui Bridge (Chinese: 瑞穗大橋) was destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] The water treatment plant in Ruisui was damaged. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake was caused by the Juisui Fault with a vertical movement of 70 centimeters (28 in). [CLS] [SEP] The Juisui Fault is a segment of the 150 kilometers (93 mi) long Longitudinal Valley Fault, which is a left-lateral fault with a reverse component. [CLS] [SEP] The boundary between the Eurasian Plate and the Philippine Plate lies along the Longitudinal Valley Fault. [CLS] [SEP]
