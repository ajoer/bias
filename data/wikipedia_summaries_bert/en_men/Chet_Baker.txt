[SEP] [CLS] Baker earned much attention and critical praise through the 1950s, particularly for albums featuring his vocals (Chet Baker Sings, It Could Happen to You). [SEP] [CLS] Jazz historian Dave Gelly described the promise of Baker's early career as "James Dean, Sinatra, and Bix, rolled into one." His well-publicized drug habit also drove his notoriety and fame. [SEP] [CLS] Baker was in and out of jail frequently before enjoying a career resurgence in the late 1970s and '80s. [SEP] [CLS]
