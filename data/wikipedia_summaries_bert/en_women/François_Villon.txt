François Villon (pronounced [fʁɑ̃swa vijɔ̃] in modern French; in fifteenth-century French, [frɑnswɛ vilɔn], c. [CLS] [SEP] 1431 – c. [CLS] [SEP] 1463) is the best known French poet of the Late Middle Ages. [CLS] [SEP] A ne'er-do-well who was involved in criminal behavior and had multiple encounters with law enforcement authorities, Villon wrote about some of these experiences in his poems.
