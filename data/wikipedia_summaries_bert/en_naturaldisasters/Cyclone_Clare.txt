Severe Tropical Cyclone Clare was a moderately strong cyclone that struck Western Australia in January 2006. [CLS] [SEP] The storm formed as an area of low pressure in the Arafura Sea, and moved westward. [CLS] [SEP] After receiving the name Clare on 7 January, the system ultimately peaked at Category 3 intensity on the Australian tropical cyclone scale. [CLS] [SEP] It moved ashore on the coast of Pilbara and proceeded inland, dissipating on 10 January. [CLS] [SEP] Clare produced winds of 142 km/h (88 mph) at Karratha and triggered widespread torrential rainfall that led to flooding. [CLS] [SEP] Following its usage, the name Clare was retired by the Bureau of Meteorology, and will never be used again for a tropical cyclone in the area. [CLS] [SEP]
