On 18 January 2015, an Antonov An-26 operated by the Syrian Air Force crashed with no survivors while attempting to land at the besieged Abu al-Duhur military airport in Idlib Governorate, Syria. [CLS] [SEP] The plane was carrying troops as well as military equipment and ammunition. [CLS] [SEP] There were 35 people on board, 30 Syrian soldiers and 5 Iranian military experts.Syrian state media and the SOHR said that the crash was due to heavy fog or "technical issues" and that the plane hit an electricity pylon. [CLS] [SEP] However, Al-Qaeda affiliated group Al-Nusra Front claimed that they shot it down.Syrian media provided a list with the names of the 30 Syrian soldiers who were killed. [CLS] [SEP] The commander of the Syrian army division, Colonel Hussein Al-Yousif, was among those listed killed. [CLS] [SEP] Per SOHR, 13 Syrian officers were among the fatalities. [CLS] [SEP]
