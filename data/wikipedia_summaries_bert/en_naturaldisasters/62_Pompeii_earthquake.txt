The AD 62 Pompeii earthquake occurred on 5 February AD 62. [CLS] [SEP] It had an estimated magnitude of between 5 and 6 and a maximum intensity of IX or X on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] The towns of Pompeii and Herculaneum were severely damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake may have been a precursor to the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79, which destroyed the same two towns. [CLS] [SEP] The contemporary philosopher and dramatist Seneca the Younger wrote an account of the earthquake in the sixth book of his Naturales quaestiones, entitled De Terrae Motu (Concerning Earthquakes).
