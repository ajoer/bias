[CLS] Hurricane Tanya was the first named storm to start with a "T" in the Atlantic since naming began in 1950, and the final storm of the very active 1995 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The twenty-first tropical cyclone, nineteenth named storm, and eleventh hurricane of the season, Tanya developed from a tropical wave while well north of the Lesser Antilles on October 26. [SEP] [CLS] The system headed northeastward and strengthened into Tropical Storm Tanya on October 27. [SEP] [CLS] Tanya meandered around the central Atlantic, and further intensified into a hurricane on October 29. [SEP] [CLS] Thereafter, Tanya tracked northeastward before curving to the east-northeast. [SEP] [CLS] After switching to an eastward direction, Tanya weakened to a tropical storm on November 1. [SEP] [CLS] Later that day, Tanya passed through the Azores as it was transitioning into an extratropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the Azores, the extratropical remnants of Tanya produced high winds. [SEP] [CLS] As a result, extensive property damage occurred, which included destroyed or damaged houses, and sunken boats; there were also reports of significant damage to agriculture. [SEP] [CLS] However, an exact damage toll figure in unknown. [SEP] [CLS] In addition, there was one fatality and several injuries. [SEP] [CLS]
