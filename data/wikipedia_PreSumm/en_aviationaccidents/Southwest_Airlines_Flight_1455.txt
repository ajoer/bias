[CLS] Southwest Airlines Flight 1455 was a scheduled passenger flight from McCarran International Airport, Las Vegas, Nevada to Burbank-Glendale-Pasadena Airport, Burbank, California, that overran the runway during landing on March 5, 2000. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft, a Boeing 737-3T5, registration N668SW, came to rest on a city street adjacent to a gas station. [SEP] [CLS] The National Transportation Safety Board found that the incident was due to the pilots attempting to land with excessive speed. [SEP] [CLS] They also found that the air traffic controller placed them in a position from which their only option was a go around. [SEP] [CLS] Two of the passengers were seriously injured, and there were many minor injuries. [SEP] [CLS] As a result of the incident, the airport installed an Engineered Materials Arrestor System (EMAS) at the east end of the incident runway. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was written off, making the incident the 10th hull loss of a Boeing 737-300. [SEP] [CLS] This was the first major accident in the airline's 29-year history. [SEP] [CLS]
