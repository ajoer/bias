[CLS] The 2007 Martinique earthquake took place on November 29 at 15:00:19 local time in the Windward Islands region, underneath the Martinique Passage. [SEP] [CLS] It was a magnitude 7.4 earthquake which occurred from Fort-de-France, Martinique.This earthquake was felt strongly in Martinique, Dominica, St. [SEP] [CLS] Lucia, Guadeloupe, Montserrat, Antigua, St Kitts and Nevis, Anguilla, British Virgin Islands, U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Virgin Islands, and Puerto Rico. [SEP] [CLS] Power outages were reported in Martinique, Dominica and Guadeloupe. [SEP] [CLS] It was also felt in other Eastern Caribbean islands, from Puerto Rico to the north to Trinidad and Tobago to the south. [SEP] [CLS] It could also be felt in part of South America such as in Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. [SEP] [CLS] In Caracas, Venezuela, some people evacuated office buildings. [SEP] [CLS] The intensity reached VI to VII on the EMS98 scale in Martinique and Dominica.In the surrounding region, the South American Plate is subducting beneath the Caribbean Plate. [SEP] [CLS] This earthquake occurred within the South America Plate and was in response to stresses generated by the slow distortion of the plate. [SEP] [CLS]
