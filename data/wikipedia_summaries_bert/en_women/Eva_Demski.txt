[CLS] Fritz Graßhoff (9 December 1913 – 9 February 1997) was a German painter, poet and songwriter. [SEP] [CLS] He was known for hits sung by Lale Andersen, Freddy Quinn and Hans Albers. [SEP] [CLS] As a painter, he participated in important exhibitions; as a writer, he was known for his lyric volume Halunkenpostille and his autobiographical novel Der blaue Heinrich. [SEP] [CLS] He translated poetry by the Ancient Roman Martial and the Swede Carl Michael Bellman. [SEP] [CLS] Many of his writings have been set to music by composers such as James Last, Norbert Schultze and Siegfried Strohbach. [SEP] [CLS]
