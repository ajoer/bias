[CLS] Veikko Kankkonen (born 5 January 1940) is a retired Finnish ski jumper who competed at the 1960, 1964 and 1968 Winter Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] He won two medals in 1964 with a gold in the individual normal hill and a silver in the individual large hill event. [SEP] [CLS] That same year he won the jumping competition at the Holmenkollen ski festival, which also earned him the Holmenkollen medal (shared with Eero Mäntyranta, Georg Thoma, and Halvor Næs). [SEP] [CLS] He also won the Four Hills Tournament and served as the flag bearer for Finland at the 1968 Olympics.Besides skiing Kankkonen played baseball for the Maila-Veikot Lahti club in the national championships of 1963–64. [SEP] [CLS] He also regularly competed in golf at the national level, with the best result of fourth place. [SEP] [CLS] Kankkonen was a turner by trade and later worked as a ski jumping coach. [SEP] [CLS] His son Anssi Kankkonen became a professional golfer, but he also competed in ski jumping and won a national title in 1985. [SEP] [CLS]
