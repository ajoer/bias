Hurricane Charlie was the deadliest tropical cyclone of the 1951 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] The third named storm, second hurricane, and second major hurricane of the season, it developed from a tropical wave east of the Lesser Antilles. [CLS] [SEP] It moved briskly west-northwest, passing between the islands of Dominica and Guadeloupe. [CLS] [SEP] It strengthened to a hurricane in the eastern Caribbean Sea, and it struck Jamaica with winds near Category 3 intensity. [CLS] [SEP] It weakened over land, and it re-strengthened over the Gulf of Mexico before it made a final landfall near Tampico. [CLS] [SEP] In its path, Charlie caused more than 250 deaths. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane produced Jamaica's deadliest natural disaster of the 20th century, causing more than 152 deaths and $50,000,000 in damages. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane was described as Tampico's worst tropical cyclone since 1936, and it caused more than 100 fatalities in the surrounding areas. [CLS] [SEP] Many bodies were not recovered after flooding. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane produced peak gusts of 160 mph (260 km/h) at the airport, and its strongest sustained winds were unmeasured in mainland Mexico, suggesting that it may have been a Category 4 hurricane at landfall.
