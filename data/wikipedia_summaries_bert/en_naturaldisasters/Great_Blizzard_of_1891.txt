The Great Blizzard of 1891 affected southern England between 9 and 13 March of that year. [CLS] [SEP] Strong winds, cold temperatures and snow which drifted up to 15ft high contributed to the deaths of 200 people and 6000 animals. [CLS] [SEP] A merchant vessel, the Bay of Panama was one of the casualties, driven onto rocks on the south coast of Cornwall by the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Twenty-three lives were lost in this incident alone. [CLS] [SEP]
