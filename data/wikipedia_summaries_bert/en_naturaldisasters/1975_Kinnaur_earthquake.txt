The 1975 Kinnaur earthquake occurred in the early afternoon (local time) (08:02 UTC) of 19 January. [CLS] [SEP] It had a magnitude of 6.8 on the surface wave magnitude scale and a maximum perceived intensity of IX (Violent) on the Mercalli intensity scale, causing extensive damage in Himachal Pradesh, in northern India. [CLS] [SEP] Its epicentre was in Kinnaur district in the southeastern part of Himachal Pradesh and caused 47 casualties. [CLS] [SEP] Landslides, rock falls and avalanches caused major damage to the Hindustan-Tibet Road. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake affected many monasteries and buildings in the state and led to an extensive restoration work in the late 1970s and early 1980s in Himachal Pradesh.
