[CLS] The 1994 offshore Sanriku earthquake (Japanese: 三陸はるか沖地震 Sanriku Haruka Okijishin) occurred on December 28, 1994, at 12:19 UTC (21:19 local time). [SEP] [CLS] This was a magnitude Mw 7.7 earthquake with epicenter located in the Pacific Ocean at about 180 km east of Hachinohe, Aomori (haruka-oki means "far offshore"). [SEP] [CLS] Three people were reported dead and more than 200 injured; 48 houses were completely destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Road damage and power outages were reported. [SEP] [CLS] Liquefaction occurred in the Hachinohe Port area. [SEP] [CLS] The intensity reached shindo 6 in Hachinohe, Aomori, about 187.6 km from epicenter. [SEP] [CLS] It could be felt in Tokyo, about 632.9 km from epicenter, with shindo 2. [SEP] [CLS] The Japanese Meteorological Agency put the magnitude at MJMA 7.5. [SEP] [CLS] Slip associated with this earthquake continued for more than a year and it has been termed an 'ultra-slow earthquake'. [SEP] [CLS]
