[CLS] Fara Tanya Franki Merrett MBE (born 25 January 1984), known as Fara Williams, is an English footballer who plays as a central midfielder for Reading and the England national team. [SEP] [CLS] A consistent goalscorer and set-piece specialist, Williams is considered one of England's leading players. [SEP] [CLS] Since making her senior debut in 2002, Williams has accrued 170 caps for the England Womens Team, making her their highest capped player. [SEP] [CLS] She played at the 2005, 2009, 2013 and 2017 European Championships, as well as the World Cups in 2007, 2011 and 2015. [SEP] [CLS] Williams also featured for Team GB at the 2012 London Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] Williams' club career started with Chelsea then she progressed to Charlton Athletic in 2001. [SEP] [CLS] She signed for Everton in 2004 and later captained the club, winning the Premier League Cup in 2008 and the FA Women's Cup in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] After eight years with Everton she signed for local rivals Liverpool in 2012 and won the league title in 2013 and 2014. [SEP] [CLS] Williams was named The Football Association (FA) Young Player of the Year in 2002, FA Players' Player of the Year in 2009 and FA International Player of the Year in both 2007 and 2009.
