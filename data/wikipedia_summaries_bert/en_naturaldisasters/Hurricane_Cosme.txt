The 1995 Pacific hurricane season was the least active Pacific hurricane season since 1979. [CLS] [SEP] Of the eleven tropical cyclones that formed during the season, four affected land, with the most notable storm of the season being Hurricane Ismael, which killed at least 116 people in Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] The strongest hurricane in the season was Hurricane Juliette, which reached peak winds of 150 mph (240 km/h), but did not significantly affect land. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Adolph was an early-season Category 4 hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Henriette brushed the Baja California Peninsula in early September. [CLS] [SEP] The season officially started on May 15, 1995, in the Eastern Pacific, and on June 1, 1995, in the Central Pacific, and lasted until November 30, 1995. [CLS] [SEP] These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical cyclones form in the northeastern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] The season saw eleven tropical cyclones form, of which ten became tropical storms. [CLS] [SEP] Seven of these storms attained hurricane status, three of them becoming major hurricanes. [CLS] [SEP] There were fewer tropical storms than the average of 16, while the number of hurricanes and major hurricanes were slightly below average. [CLS] [SEP] The season marked the beginning of a multi-decade period of low activity in the Eastern Pacific. [CLS] [SEP]
