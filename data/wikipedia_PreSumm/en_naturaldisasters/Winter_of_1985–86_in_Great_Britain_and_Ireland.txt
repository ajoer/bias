[CLS] The Winter of 1985–86 in Great Britain and Ireland contained two periods of notably cold weather. [SEP] [CLS] November 1985 brought an early start to winter with the month being the coldest in the Central England region since 1925, with an average temperature of 4.1 °C (39.4 °F). [SEP] [CLS] However, December was milder than average and January close to average before February became the coldest month of any kind since January 1963 with an average temperature of −1.1 °C (30.0 °F). [SEP] [CLS] March and April were also below average, especially April which was the coldest since 1922 with an average temperature of just 5.8 °C (42.4 °F). [SEP] [CLS]
