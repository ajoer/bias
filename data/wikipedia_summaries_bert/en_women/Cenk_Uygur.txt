Cenk Kadir Uygur (; Turkish: [ˈdʒeɲc kaˈdiɾ ˈujɡuɾ]; born March 21, 1970) is a Turkish-American political commentator, media host, journalist, and attorney. [CLS] [SEP] Uygur is the creator of The Young Turks (TYT), an American progressive sociopolitical news and commentary program. [CLS] [SEP] Before beginning his career as a political commentator, he worked briefly as an associate attorney in Washington, D.C. [CLS] [SEP] and New York City. [CLS] [SEP] As a young man, Uygur supported social conservative positions but as his views changed he began to identify as a progressive. [CLS] [SEP] He is also the cofounder of Justice Democrats. [CLS] [SEP] In addition to hosting The Young Turks, Uygur appeared on MSNBC as a political commentator. [CLS] [SEP] From January to June 2011, he hosted a weeknight commentary show on the network; he was replaced by Al Sharpton. [CLS] [SEP] After leaving MSNBC, Uygur secured another weeknight commentary show on Current TV, which aired from December 5, 2011, to August 15, 2013. [CLS] [SEP] From 2012 to 2013, he was the chief news officer at Current TV, succeeding Keith Olbermann.Uygur was a candidate for California's 25th congressional district in the 2020 special election to succeed former Representative Katie Hill, who resigned in 2019, but was defeated in the primary, coming in fourth with 6.6% of the vote.In early May 2020, Uygur announced his upcoming book, Justice Is Coming - How Progressives Are Going to Take Over the Country and America Is Going to Love It, scheduled for release on January 19, 2021 – the day before Inauguration Day.
