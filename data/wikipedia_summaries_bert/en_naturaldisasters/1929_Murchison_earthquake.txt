The 1929 Murchison earthquake occurred at 10:17 am on 17 June. [CLS] [SEP] It struck the Murchison region of the South Island, with an estimated magnitude of 7.3, and was felt throughout New Zealand. [CLS] [SEP] There were 17 deaths, mostly as a result of landslides triggered by the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] The rumbling sound of the earthquake was loud enough to be heard at New Plymouth, more than 250 km (155 mi) away. [CLS] [SEP]
