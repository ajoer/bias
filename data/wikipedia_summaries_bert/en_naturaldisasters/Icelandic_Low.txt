The Icelandic Low is a semi-permanent centre of low atmospheric pressure found between Iceland and southern Greenland and extending in the Northern Hemisphere winter into the Barents Sea. [CLS] [SEP] In summer it weakens and splits into two centres, one near Davis Strait and the other west of Iceland. [CLS] [SEP] It is a principal centre of action in the atmosphere circulation of the Northern Hemisphere, associated with frequent cyclone activity. [CLS] [SEP] It forms one pole of the North Atlantic oscillation, the other being the Azores High. [CLS] [SEP]
