Severe Tropical Cyclone Ivy (Fiji Meteorological Service designation: 05F, Joint Typhoon Warning Center designation: 13P) was a tropical cyclone that affected about 25% of the population of Vanuatu in February 2004. [CLS] [SEP] It was first classified as a tropical disturbance on February 21 between Vanuatu and Fiji. [CLS] [SEP] The system tracked northwestward, gradually organizing and intensifying. [CLS] [SEP] After attaining tropical storm status on February 23, Ivy strengthened more quickly as it turned southwestward toward Vanuatu. [CLS] [SEP] It attained peak winds of 165 km/h (105 mph) while moving over Vanuatu, making it an intense Category 4 cyclone on the Australian Region Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale. [CLS] [SEP] By the time it passed through Vanuatu, Ivy had turned southeastward, and it gradually weakened while accelerating. [CLS] [SEP] After becoming extratropical on February 28, it passed just east of New Zealand and eventually dissipated on March 2. [CLS] [SEP] The third significant cyclone in 14 months to affect the region, Ivy produced heavy rainfall and high winds in Vanuatu. [CLS] [SEP] Due to advance warning, only two people were killed, and several people were injured. [CLS] [SEP] The high winds damaged about 11,000 houses, leaving many people homeless. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone passed very close to the Vanuatu capital city of Port Vila, forcing the evacuation of about 2,000 people and shutting down the main port. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone Ivy also affected the Solomon Islands and New Caledonia with rainfall, and later it produced high waves in New Zealand that killed two people. [CLS] [SEP] Because of its severe damage on Vanuatu, the name Ivy was retired following its usage. [CLS] [SEP]
