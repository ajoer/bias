The 2014–17 Brazilian drought is a severe drought affecting the southeast of Brazil including the metropolitan areas of São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro. [CLS] [SEP] In São Paulo, it has been described as the worst drought in 100 years. [CLS] [SEP] The metropolis of São Paulo appeared to be affected the most and by the beginning of February many of its residents were subjected to sporadic water cutoffs. [CLS] [SEP] Rain at the end of 2015 and in early 2016 brought relief, however, long term problems in water supply remain in São Paulo state.Minas Gerais and Espírito Santo still being affected by drought in 2016 due to the 2014–16 El Niño event. [CLS] [SEP] In these areas the rains are irregular since 2014 and the drought worsened from 2015. [CLS] [SEP] Over 50% of the Brazil was affected, as the drought spanned sections of all nine northeastern states.
