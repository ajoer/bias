Typhoon Trami, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Paeng, was the second typhoon to affect Japan within a month. [CLS] [SEP] The twenty-fourth tropical storm and tenth typhoon of the annual typhoon season, Trami developed from a low-pressure area southeast of Guam on September 20. [CLS] [SEP] It intensified into a tropical storm on the next day, and intensified into a typhoon on September 22. [CLS] [SEP] Trami steadily intensified and reached its peak intensity late on September 24. [CLS] [SEP] On the following day, Trami slowed and drifted northward. [CLS] [SEP] It began to weaken due to upwelling. [CLS] [SEP] Trami accelerated and turned northeastward on September 29, before it struck Japan on the next day, and became extratropical on October 1. [CLS] [SEP] The extratropical remnants persisted for days until dissipated completely on October 8. [CLS] [SEP] Trami caused additional damage to Japan, while it still recovering from the impacts of Typhoon Jebi. [CLS] [SEP] Transportation was disrupted with several domestic flights were cancelled. [CLS] [SEP] Over 380,000 people were evacuated. [CLS] [SEP] In total, Trami killed 4 people and left hundreds of injures.
