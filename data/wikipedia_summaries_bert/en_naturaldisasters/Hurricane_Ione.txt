Hurricane Ione was a strong, Category 4 hurricane that affected North Carolina in September 1955, bringing high winds and significant rainfall. [CLS] [SEP] It came on the heels of Hurricanes Connie and Diane, and compounded problems already caused by the two earlier hurricanes. [CLS] [SEP] Spawned by a tropical wave which left the African coast on September 6, the system became a tropical depression in the tropical North Atlantic, before turning northwest and developing into a hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] After turning back to the west east of the Bahamas, Ione turned northwest and northward, moving across eastern North Carolina before moving east-northeastward out to sea. [CLS] [SEP] Ione caused $600 million (2005 USD) in damage, much of it to crops across North Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] As a result of Ione's impacts seven people lost their lives. [CLS] [SEP]
