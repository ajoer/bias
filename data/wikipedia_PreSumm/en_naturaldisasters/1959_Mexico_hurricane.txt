[CLS] The 1959 Mexico hurricane was the deadliest Pacific hurricane on record. [SEP] [CLS] First observed south of Mexico on October 23, the cyclone tracked northwestward. [SEP] [CLS] It intensified into a Category 3 hurricane on October 25 and reached Category 4 intensity on the following day. [SEP] [CLS] After turning toward the northeast, the hurricane made landfall near Manzanillo, Mexico at peak intensity. [SEP] [CLS] The system continued on that trajectory before dissipating on the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Impact from the hurricane was severe and widespread. [SEP] [CLS] Initially forecast to remain offshore, the system curved northeast and moved ashore, becoming one of Mexico's worst natural disasters at the time. [SEP] [CLS] Up to 150 boats were submerged. [SEP] [CLS] Countless homes in Colima and Jalisco were damaged or destroyed, large portions of the states were inaccessible by flash flooding, and hundreds of residents were stranded. [SEP] [CLS] All coconut plantations were blown down during the storm, leaving thousands without work and instating fear that it would take the economy years to recover. [SEP] [CLS] Torrential rainfall across mountain terrain contributed to numerous mudslides that caused hundreds of fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] In the aftermath of the cyclone, convoys delivering aid were hindered by the destruction. [SEP] [CLS] Residents were vaccinated to prevent the spread of disease. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, the hurricane inflicted at least $280 million (1959 USD) in damage. [SEP] [CLS]
