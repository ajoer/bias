[CLS] The 2000 Enggano earthquake struck at 23:28 local time on June 4 with a moment magnitude of 7.9 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VI (Strong). [SEP] [CLS] The event occurred off the coast of southern Sumatra, Indonesia near Enggano Island. [SEP] [CLS] There were more than 100 fatalities and up to 2,585 injuries. [SEP] [CLS] Over 730 aftershocks shocked the area afterwards, one just eleven minutes after the mainshock. [SEP] [CLS] This was the first and southernmost in a series of very large to great Sumatran earthquakes in the 2000s to rupture almost the entire western part of the Sunda megathrust, most notably including the 9.1–9.3 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake, but also the 8.7 2005 Nias–Simeulue earthquake, and the 7.9–8.4 September 2007 Sumatra earthquakes.
