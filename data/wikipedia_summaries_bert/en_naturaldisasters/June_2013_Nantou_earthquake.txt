[CLS] The 2013 Nantou earthquake struck central Taiwan with a moment magnitude of 6.2 on 2 June at 1:43 p.m. [SEP] [CLS] local time. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter was located in mountainous terrain in Ren'ai Township, Nantou County, Taiwan, not far from Sun Moon Lake, close to the epicentre of another large earthquake a little over two months earlier. [SEP] [CLS] News reports indicate that five people were killed; three in Nantou County and two in neighbouring Chiayi County. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake could be felt in Hong Kong, Zhejiang, Fujian, and Guangdong, China. [SEP] [CLS]
