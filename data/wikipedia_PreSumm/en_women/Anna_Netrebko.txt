[CLS] Anna Yuryevna Netrebko (Russian: Анна Юрьевна Нетребко; born 18 September 1971) is a Russian-Austrian operatic soprano. [SEP] [CLS] Discovered and promoted by Valery Gergiev, she began her career at the Mariinsky Theatre, collaborating with the conductor in the theater and performances elsewhere. [SEP] [CLS] She was noticed globally after playing Donna Anna in Mozart's Don Giovanni at the 2002 Salzburg Festival. [SEP] [CLS] She had been known for her rendition of lyric and coloratura soprano roles, most notably Donna Anna and Violetta in Verdi's La traviata, and proceeded into heavier 19th-century romantic roles, such as Leonora in Il trovatore and the role of Lady Macbeth in Macbeth. [SEP] [CLS] Since 2016, she has turned her focus to Verismo repertoire. [SEP] [CLS] She has received multiple awards and honors and has substantial discography made primarily under Deutsche Grammophon. [SEP] [CLS] She holds dual Russian and Austrian citizenship and resides in Vienna and New York City. [SEP] [CLS]
