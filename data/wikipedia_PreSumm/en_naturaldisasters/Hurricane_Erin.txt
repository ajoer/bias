[CLS] The name Erin has been used for six tropical cyclones in the Atlantic Ocean: Hurricane Erin (1989), a Category 2 Cape Verde hurricane that stayed away from land in the eastern Atlantic Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Erin (1995), a Category 2 hurricane that made two landfalls on the state of Florida in July and resulted in 6 direct deaths and $700 million damage. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Erin (2001), a Category 3 major hurricane that passed east of Bermuda but stayed away from the East Coast before becoming extratropical near Cape Race. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Erin (2007), formed in the Gulf of Mexico and made landfall in Texas; noted for its unusual restrengthening while inland over Oklahoma. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Erin (2013), stayed away from land in the eastern Atlantic Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Erin (2019), formed off the coast of North Carolina and then moved out into the ocean without affecting land before becoming extratropical.
