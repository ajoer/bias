The 2010 Hosseinabad earthquake occurred on December 20 at 22:11 local time (18:41 UTC) with a moment magnitude of 6.6 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of IX (Violent). [CLS] [SEP] It was located about 20 km from Hosseinabad (spelled variously as Ḩoseynābād or Hosein Abad) (Persian: حسين آباد), Kermān Province in south-eastern Iran, about 100 km from Bam.The earthquake killed eleven people and injured over 100. [CLS] [SEP] Damage was concentrated in about 20 villages in the area of Fahraj. [CLS] [SEP] Casualties were reported in the villages of Chah Qanbar, Tak Seyfeldini, and Sarze Rigan. [CLS] [SEP] Telephone service was interrupted and power outages were reported in the affected area. [CLS] [SEP] The intensity in Bam was IV (Light) and no damage was caused. [CLS] [SEP] It could also be felt in the province of Sistan-Baluchestan. [CLS] [SEP] According to the director of the Red Crescent of Iran, it is difficult to deliver humanitarian aid to some villages in the affected mountainous area. [CLS] [SEP] Some hard-to-reach areas were inaccessible due to being blocked by landslides. [CLS] [SEP] Moderate earthquakes can cause serious damage in the Iranian countryside, where buildings are often made of mud-bricks. [CLS] [SEP]
