The 2018 Trinidad and Tobago floods were a series of over-bank floods and flash-floods occurring in the twin-island Caribbean nation, Trinidad and Tobago. [CLS] [SEP] These events followed consistent rainfall on Friday 19 October 2018 and intermittent rainfall on Saturday 20 October 2018. [CLS] [SEP] Rivers in Caroni, Diego Martin and Maraval breached their banks. [CLS] [SEP] Several communities were evacuated as homes and vehicles were flooded; many areas were inaccessible. [CLS] [SEP] Flooding along the Caroni Plain caused the submersion of a major part of the Uriah Butler Highway. [CLS] [SEP] The south-bound lane of this highway was temporarily closed to vehicular traffic. [CLS] [SEP] The north-bound lane was temporarily used for two-way traffic, permitting only large SUVs and heavy-T vehicles. [CLS] [SEP] Flight services out of Piarco International Airport were temporarily disrupted. [CLS] [SEP]
