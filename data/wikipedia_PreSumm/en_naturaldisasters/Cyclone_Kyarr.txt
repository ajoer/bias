[CLS] Super Cyclonic Storm Kyarr was an extremely powerful tropical cyclone that became the first super cyclonic storm in the North Indian Ocean since Gonu in 2007. [SEP] [CLS] It was also the second strongest tropical cyclone in the Arabian Sea and one of the most intense tropical cyclones in North Indian Ocean history. [SEP] [CLS] The seventh depression, fifth named cyclone, and the first, and only super cyclonic storm of the annual season, Kyarr developed from a low-pressure system near the Equator. [SEP] [CLS] The system organized itself and intensified to a tropical storm on October 24 as it moved eastwards. [SEP] [CLS] The storm underwent rapid intensification and reached super cyclonic storm status on October 27, as it turned westwards. [SEP] [CLS] Despite the immense strength of the storm, and many countries being affected by high tides and storm surges, there were no reported fatalities. [SEP] [CLS]
