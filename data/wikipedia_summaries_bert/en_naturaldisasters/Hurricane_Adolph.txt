Hurricane Adolph of the 2001 Pacific hurricane season was the first and one of only two East Pacific hurricanes in May to reach Category 4 strength on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale since record keeping began in the East Pacific, with the other being Amanda of 2014. [CLS] [SEP] Adolph was the first depression of the season, forming on May 25; it became a hurricane three days later. [CLS] [SEP] After rapidly intensifying, Adolph became the most powerful storm in terms of maximum sustained winds this season, along with Hurricane Juliette. [CLS] [SEP] The storm briefly threatened land before dissipating on June 1, after moving over colder waters. [CLS] [SEP]
