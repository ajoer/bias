[CLS] The 1906 San Francisco earthquake struck the coast of Northern California at 5:12 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] on Wednesday, April 18 with an estimated moment magnitude of 7.9 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of XI (Extreme). [SEP] [CLS] High-intensity shaking was felt from Eureka on the North Coast to the Salinas Valley, an agricultural region to the south of the San Francisco Bay Area. [SEP] [CLS] Devastating fires soon broke out in the city and lasted for several days. [SEP] [CLS] More than 3,000 people died. [SEP] [CLS] Over 80% of the city of San Francisco was destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] The events are remembered as one of the worst and deadliest earthquakes in the history of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] The death toll remains the greatest loss of life from a natural disaster in California's history and high on the lists of American disasters. [SEP] [CLS]
