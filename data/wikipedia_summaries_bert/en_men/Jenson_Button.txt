Jenson Alexander Lyons Button (born 19 January 1980) is a British racing driver and former Formula One racer who won the 2009 Formula One World Championship driving for Brawn GP. [CLS] [SEP] After his Formula One career, he became a champion of the Super GT Series in 2018, driving a Honda car for Team Kunimitsu. [CLS] [SEP] Button began karting at the age of eight and achieved early success, before progressing to car racing in the British Formula Ford Championship and the British Formula 3 Championship. [CLS] [SEP] He first drove in Formula One with Williams for 2000. [CLS] [SEP] The following year he switched to Benetton, which in 2002 became Renault, and then for 2003 he moved to BAR. [CLS] [SEP] In 2004 he finished third in the World Drivers' Championship, falling to ninth in 2005. [CLS] [SEP] BAR was subsequently renamed Honda for 2006, during which Button won his first Grand Prix in Hungary, after 113 races. [CLS] [SEP] Following the withdrawal of Honda from the sport in December 2008, Button was left without a team for the 2009 season. [CLS] [SEP] In February 2009, Ross Brawn led a management buyout of Honda, creating Brawn GP and recruiting Button as a driver. [CLS] [SEP] Button went on to win a record-equalling six of the first seven races of the 2009 season, securing the World Drivers' Championship at the Brazilian Grand Prix, having led on points all season; his success also helped Brawn GP to secure the World Constructors' Championship. [CLS] [SEP] For 2010, he moved to McLaren, partnering fellow British racer and former World Champion Lewis Hamilton. [CLS] [SEP] After finishing fifth for the team in 2010, Button ended the 2011 season as runner-up, before falling to fifth in 2012. [CLS] [SEP] A further four seasons with McLaren resulted in no further victories and his final Formula One race was the 2017 Monaco Grand Prix. [CLS] [SEP] From the 306 races that Button started he won 15, and took 50 podium finishes. [CLS] [SEP]
