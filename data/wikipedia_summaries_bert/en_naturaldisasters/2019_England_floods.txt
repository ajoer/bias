[CLS] Between November 2019 and February 2020, severe winter flooding occurred across the United Kingdom. [SEP] [CLS] The first wave of flooding occurred in November 2019, mainly affecting Yorkshire and the Humber, the East Midlands and the West Midlands. [SEP] [CLS] Further isolated flooding incidents were reported in December and January, before the second main wave of flooding, caused by Storms Ciara and Dennis, occurred in February 2020. [SEP] [CLS] The excessive rainfall led to the wettest February since records began, in 1766, in England and Wales with 169.6 millimetres (6.68 in) beating the record from 1833. [SEP] [CLS]
