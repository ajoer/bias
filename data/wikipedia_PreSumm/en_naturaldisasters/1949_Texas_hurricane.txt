[CLS] The 1949 Texas hurricane was a tropical cyclone of the 1949 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] Forming in the Pacific Ocean on September 27, the storm crossed into the Gulf of Mexico—one of only a handful of known storms to do so—and began to intensify. [SEP] [CLS] It ultimately peaked with winds corresponding to high-end Category 2 status on the modern-day Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale and made landfall near Freeport, Texas, on the morning of October 4. [SEP] [CLS] It rapidly weakened after moving inland and dissipated several days later. [SEP] [CLS] Damage from the storm was moderate, although the hurricane temporarily cut off the city of Galveston from the mainland. [SEP] [CLS] Rice crops suffered extensive damage, with losses estimated at up to $10 million (1949 USD, $107 million 2020 USD). [SEP] [CLS] Two people died due to the hurricane. [SEP] [CLS]
