The 2008 Skåne County earthquake occurred at 06.20am CET (05.20 UTC) on 16 December and affected the southern part of Sweden and eastern parts of Denmark. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was 5 km southwest of Sjöbo and 60 km east of Malmö. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was considered "moderately strong" with a moment magnitude calculated at 4.2–4.3 . [CLS] [SEP] Strong shaking was reported widely in Sweden from Skåne to Östergötland, in Denmark, and in northern Poland. [CLS] [SEP] The Skåne region is known for extremely low seismic activity, with only three small earthquakes (each less than 2.8) detected between 1970 and 2008, and only 14 earthquakes since 1375. [CLS] [SEP] Roadways in Sweden and Denmark were reported with cracks but investigations did not determine if any were caused by the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP]
