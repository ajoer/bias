Lisa Raymond (born August 10, 1973) is an American retired professional tennis player who has achieved notable success in doubles tennis. [CLS] [SEP] Raymond has 11 Grand Slam titles to her name: 6 in women's doubles and 5 in mixed doubles. [CLS] [SEP] On June 12, 2000, she reached the World No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 Ranking in doubles for the first time. [CLS] [SEP] Her career high singles ranking was No. [CLS] [SEP] 15 in October 1997. [CLS] [SEP] Over the course of her career, Raymond won four singles titles and 79 doubles titles, and held the World No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 doubles ranking for a total of 137 weeks. [CLS] [SEP] Earning more than US$10 million in prize money in her career, Raymond reached the quarterfinals in singles at the 2004 Australian Open and the 2000 Wimbledon Championships. [CLS] [SEP] Raymond, who plays right-handed, has wins over former World No. [CLS] [SEP] 1's Venus Williams, Arantxa Sánchez Vicario, Monica Seles, Jennifer Capriati and Martina Hingis, as well as other accomplished former top 10 players such as Magdalena Maleeva, Lori McNeil, Irina Spîrlea, Natasha Zvereva, Conchita Martínez, Marion Bartoli, Svetlana Kuznetsova and Maria Kirilenko. [CLS] [SEP] She is one of the few players to win a career Grand Slam in doubles. [CLS] [SEP] Among her doubles partners are Lindsay Davenport, Martina Navratilova, Rennae Stubbs, Samantha Stosur, Květa Peschke, Cara Black and Liezel Huber. [CLS] [SEP] Raymond is also an Olympic medalist, having won the bronze medal in the mixed doubles competition at the 2012 Summer Olympics for the United States, partnering with Mike Bryan.
