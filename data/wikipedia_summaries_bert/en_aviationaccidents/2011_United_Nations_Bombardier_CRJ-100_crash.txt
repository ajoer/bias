[CLS] On 4 April 2011, a Bombardier CRJ-100 passenger jet of Georgian Airways operating a domestic flight from Kisangani to Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of Congo on behalf of United Nations, crashed while attempting to land in a thunderstorm at Kinshasa Airport. [SEP] [CLS] Of the 33 people on board, only one, a passenger, survived.A subsequent investigation concluded that the aircraft had encountered a microburst (severe downdraft) moments after initiating a go-around, and the resulting loss of altitude could not be arrested before impact with the ground.
