Airlink Flight 8911 was a positioning flight from Durban International Airport to Pietermaritzburg Airport, South Africa, that crashed into the grounds of Merebank Secondary School, Durban shortly after take-off on 24 September 2009, injuring the three occupants of the aircraft and one on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] The captain of the flight subsequently died of his injuries on 7 October 2009. [CLS] [SEP] The first on scene was Brian Govindsamy, who is a local resident. [CLS] [SEP] He allegedly tried to rescue the crew by breaking down the plane door. [CLS] [SEP] It is, however, still not certain how many of the crew he managed to save. [CLS] [SEP]
