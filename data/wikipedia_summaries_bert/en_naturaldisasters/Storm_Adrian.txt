Storm Adrian (also known as Vaia) was an intense Mediterranean storm which brought severe conditions to Northern Italy and surrounding regions. [CLS] [SEP] it was one of the costliest of the 2018-19 named storms, causing £2.9 billion (≥ €3.3 billion) in damages. [CLS] [SEP] It formed over the western Mediterranean Sea October 28, becoming the sixth named storm of the season and the first named storm of the season for Météo-France. [CLS] [SEP] The storm made landfall in Corsica on 29 October with powerful wind gusts in excess of 189 km/h (117 mph), winds the equivalent of a Category 3 hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] The storm made landfall along the French Riviera later that day, bringing high winds, heavy rain, thunderstorms and a severe storm surge along the south coast of France, causing costal erosion in Nice. [CLS] [SEP] The storm's weather front brought similar problems to northern Italy and the adriatic coast. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout Italy, 11 fatalities were reported. [CLS] [SEP] The storm damaged the Basilica of San Marco and left 75% of Venice underwater. [CLS] [SEP] Additionally, as Storm Adrian pulled north, Central France experienced a winter storm with snowfall totals in excess of 50 cm (20 in) in higher elevations. [CLS] [SEP] The wintry weather cut power to 200,000+ and resulted in traffic chaos. [CLS] [SEP]
