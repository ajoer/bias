[CLS] The Saskatchewan Blizzard of 2007 was a winter storm that struck northeastern British Columbia, central Alberta and central Saskatchewan on Wednesday, January 10, 2007. [SEP] [CLS] The storm hit the city of Saskatoon severely and is considered to be one of the worst storms in Saskatchewan's history. [SEP] [CLS] It brought motor vehicle traffic to a standstill, stranded people and shut down many public services. [SEP] [CLS] There were two fatalities from the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Blizzard conditions shut down airports in Prince Albert and Saskatoon. [SEP] [CLS] Road conditions were poor during the storm, and highways around Saskatoon, North Battleford, Melfort and Wynyard were closed. [SEP] [CLS] Unionized highway snowplow operators, who had been on strike at the time, returned to work in the interest of public safety. [SEP] [CLS] City streets in Saskatoon were also clogged with snow, slowing traffic. [SEP] [CLS] Some roads in the city's outer periphery were impassable due to high drifts. [SEP] [CLS] 14 major intersections were closed or deemed impassable.All branches of Saskatoon's public library closed in the afternoon as did city leisure facilities, indoor skating rinks and public schools. [SEP] [CLS] Several businesses allowed their employees to go home early, while the University of Saskatchewan shut down along with SIAST Kelsey campus and government agencies. [SEP] [CLS] Hotels were filled with stranded travellers and city residents who could not drive home because of the inclement conditions. [SEP] [CLS] Some people resorted to taking refuge overnight in businesses such as Costco.Emergency services spent the day responding to accidents, rescuing stranded motorists from stuck vehicles, and closing roads. [SEP] [CLS] SaskTel's cellular telephone network was overloaded with calls, prompting the Saskatoon fire department to urge the public to refrain from using mobile phones in all but emergency situations.
