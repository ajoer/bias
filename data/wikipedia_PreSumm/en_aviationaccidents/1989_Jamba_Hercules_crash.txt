[CLS] The 1989 Jamba Hercules crash was an air accident involving a Lockheed Hercules L-100 aircraft that crashed on final approach to Jamba, Cuando Cubango, Angola on 27 November 1989. [SEP] [CLS] The flight had originated at Kamina Airport, Zaire, and was attempting a low-level approach at night. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was owned by CIA front company Tepper Aviation; it was delivering arms to UNITA. [SEP] [CLS] The crash killed "Bud" Peddy, the head of Tepper Aviation, who was acting as the plane's pilot. [SEP] [CLS] On board were several Americans, two West Germans, and a Briton. [SEP] [CLS] All were killed in the crash. [SEP] [CLS]
