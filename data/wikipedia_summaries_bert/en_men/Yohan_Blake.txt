Yohan Blake (born 26 December 1989), is a Jamaican sprinter of the 100-metre and 200-metre sprint races. [CLS] [SEP] He won gold at the 100 m at the 2011 World Championships as the youngest 100 m world champion ever, and a silver medal in the 2012 Olympic Games in London in the 100 m and 200 m races for the Jamaican team. [CLS] [SEP] Blake is the second fastest man ever in both 100 m and 200 m. [CLS] [SEP] Together with Tyson Gay, he is the joint second fastest man ever over 100 m with a personal best of 9.69 seconds which he ran on 23 August 2012 with less favorable wind conditions than Gay's personal best from three years prior. [CLS] [SEP] Only Usain Bolt has run faster. [CLS] [SEP] His personal best for the 200 m (19.26 seconds) is the second fastest time ever after Bolt. [CLS] [SEP] In this particular race, his reaction time was incredibly slow (0.269 s) and had he made even an average start he would have comfortably set a new world record, bettering the seemingly unreachable Usain Bolt's improbable record. [CLS] [SEP] He holds the Jamaican national junior record for the 100 metres, and was formerly the youngest sprinter to have broken the 10-second barrier (at 19 years, 196 days).He is coached by Glen Mills and his training partners are Usain Bolt and Daniel Bailey.
