Johan Maria Gerardus Vandeurzen (born 2 June 1958) is a Belgian politician, member of Christen-Democratisch en Vlaams (CD&V), a centrist Flemish Christian Democratic party. [CLS] [SEP] Vandeurzen holds a Master of Laws degree from the Catholic University of Leuven. [CLS] [SEP] He works at Geyskens-Vandeurzen in Beringen.He became federal representative in January 1993 as a successor to retiring Luc Dhoore. [CLS] [SEP] In 2001, his party, the CVP, rebranded into CD&V, of which he became secretary-general. [CLS] [SEP] In 2004 he became party leader. [CLS] [SEP] He remained party leader until December 2007, when he became Minister of Justice in the interim federal Verhofstadt III Government. [CLS] [SEP] He became the Belgian Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Justice and Institutional Reforms in the Leterme I Government, which took office on 20 March 2008. [CLS] [SEP] On 19 December 2008 he resigned after a crisis.After the 2009 Flemish regional election, CD&V was the largest party and he received 69,223 preference votes. [CLS] [SEP] When the 2009–2014 Peeters II Government took office, he became the Flemish Minister for Welfare, Public Health and Family. [CLS] [SEP] He continues to hold this office in the 2014–2019 Bourgeois Government. [CLS] [SEP]
