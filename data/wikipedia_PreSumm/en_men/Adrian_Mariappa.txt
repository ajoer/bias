[CLS] Adrian Joseph Mariappa (born 3 October 1986) is a professional footballer who plays as a defender for EFL Championship club Watford. [SEP] [CLS] He progressed through Watford's youth academy, and started playing for the first team in the 2005–06 season. [SEP] [CLS] Although primarily a centre back, he spent some of his early professional career at right back due to competition for places in the middle. [SEP] [CLS] In his first spell at Watford, he made 247 first team appearances, including 19 in the Premier League. [SEP] [CLS] This included a run of 113 consecutive league appearances, the fourth longest streak in Watford's history. [SEP] [CLS] Mariappa captained Watford at under-18, reserve and first-team levels, and was the club's vice captain in his final few years at Vicarage Road. [SEP] [CLS] Mariappa accepted his first call up to Jamaica's international team in May 2012. [SEP] [CLS] He won his first cap against Guyana later that month, and transferred to Premier League side Reading in July 2012. [SEP] [CLS] He made 29 league appearances for the club before joining Premier League side Crystal Palace in September 2013, following Reading's relegation to the Championship. [SEP] [CLS] Returning to Watford before the 2016–17 season, Mariappa made 7 league appearances that season. [SEP] [CLS]
