The 1943 Alahan Panjang earthquakes occurred on June 8 and June 9 UTC (June 9, 1943, local time) in Sumatra, then under Japanese occupation. [CLS] [SEP] This was an earthquake doublet (the shocks occurred at the same location on consecutive days).The first mainshock occurred on June 8 at 20:42 UTC. [CLS] [SEP] It ruptured the Suliti segment of the Sumatran Fault Zone. [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude was given as Mw 7.2, or Ms 7.1.The second mainshock occurred on June 9 at 03:06 UTC. [CLS] [SEP] It ruptured the Sumani segment of the Sumatran Fault Zone and perhaps the northwestern part of the Suliti segment. [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude was given as Mw 7.5, or Ms 7.4.Alahan Panjang was damaged in the earthquakes. [CLS] [SEP] Right lateral offsets were reported near the town of Solok.Near the Sumani segment, earthquake doublets occurred repeatedly. [CLS] [SEP] Similar earthquake doublet nearby include the earthquakes in 1926 and 2007. [CLS] [SEP]
