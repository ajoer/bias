Johanna Schopenhauer (née Trosiener; July 9, 1766 – April 17, 1838) was the first German woman to publish books without a pseudonym, an influential literary salon host, and in the 1820s the most famous female author in Germany. [CLS] [SEP] She is the mother of the philosopher Arthur Schopenhauer, with whom she had a disagreeable relationship. [CLS] [SEP]
