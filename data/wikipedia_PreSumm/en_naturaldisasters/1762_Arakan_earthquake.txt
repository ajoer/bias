[CLS] The 1762 Arakan earthquake occurred at about 17:00 local time on 2 April, with an epicentre somewhere on the coast from Chittagong (modern Bangladesh) to Arakan in modern Burma. [SEP] [CLS] It had an estimated magnitude of as high as 8.8 on the moment magnitude scale and a maximum estimated intensity of XI (Extreme) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] It triggered a local tsunami in the Bay of Bengal and caused at least 200 deaths. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was associated with major areas of both uplift and subsidence. [SEP] [CLS] It is also associated with a change in course of the Brahmaputra River to from east of Dhaka (Old Brahmaputra River) to 150 kilometres (93 mi) to the west via the Jamuna River.
