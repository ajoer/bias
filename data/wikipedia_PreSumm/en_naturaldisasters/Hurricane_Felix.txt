[CLS] Hurricane Felix was the southernmost landfalling Category 5 hurricane in the Atlantic; surpassing Hurricane Edith of 1971. [SEP] [CLS] It was the sixth named storm, second hurricane, and second Category 5 hurricane of the 2007 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] Felix formed from a tropical wave on August 31, passing through the southern Windward Islands on September 1 before strengthening to attain hurricane status. [SEP] [CLS] On the next day, Felix rapidly strengthened into a major hurricane, and early on September 3 it was upgraded to Category 5 status; at 2100 UTC on the same day, the hurricane was downgraded to Category 4 status, but strengthened into a Category 5 hurricane for the second and final time by the morning of September 4. [SEP] [CLS] On September 4, Felix made landfall just south of the border between Nicaragua and Honduras, causing at least 133 deaths and hundreds of millions of dollars in damages in Central America. [SEP] [CLS] Because of its devastating impact on Central America, especially on Nicaragua, its name was retired after the 2007 season. [SEP] [CLS]
