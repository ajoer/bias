[CLS] Adam de la Halle, also known as Adam le Bossu (Adam the Hunchback) (1240–1287) was a French-born trouvère, poet and musician. [SEP] [CLS] Adam's literary and musical works include chansons and jeux-partis (poetic debates) in the style of the trouvères; polyphonic rondel and motets in the style of early liturgical polyphony; and a musical play, "Jeu de Robin et Marion" (c. [SEP] [CLS] 1282–83), which is considered the earliest surviving secular French play with music. [SEP] [CLS] He was a member of the Confrérie des jongleurs et bourgeois d'Arras. [SEP] [CLS]
