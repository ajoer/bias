Dudo, or Dudon, was a Picard historian, and dean of Saint-Quentin, where he was born about 965. [CLS] [SEP] Sent in 986 by Albert I, Count of Vermandois, on an errand to Richard I, Duke of Normandy, he succeeded in his mission, and, having made a very favorable impression at the Norman court, spent some years in that country. [CLS] [SEP] During a second stay in Normandy, Dudo wrote his history of the Normans, a task which Duke Richard had urged him to undertake. [CLS] [SEP] Very little else is known about his life, except that he died before 1043. [CLS] [SEP]
