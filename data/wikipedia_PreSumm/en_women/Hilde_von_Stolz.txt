[CLS] Hilde von Stolz (8 July 1903 in Segesvár, Nagy-Küküllő County, Austria-Hungary, now Romania – 16 December 1973 in Berlin) was an Austrian-German actress. [SEP] [CLS] Von Stolz attended the Max Reinhardt Seminar in Vienna and made her debut at the local Kammerspielen. [SEP] [CLS] She subsequently performed at various theaters in Vienna and in the Theater am Schiffbauerdamm in Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] She made her debut in film in 1928 under the pseudonym "Helen Steels". [SEP] [CLS] That same year, she moved to Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] In her second film role, she played the lead role opposite Reinhold Schünzel in Don Juan in a Girls' School. [SEP] [CLS] Von Stolz began performing under her real name starting in 1933. [SEP] [CLS] She established herself as a major film actress although she had to be satisfied with major supporting roles that usually portrayed elegant ladies and femmes fatales such as the actress Lydia Link in The Dreamer. [SEP] [CLS] Von Stolz had planned to emigrate from Germany but the outbreak of the Second World War in 1939 frustrated those plans. [SEP] [CLS] During the war she worked in a number of Nazi propaganda films, the most widely known of these was her role as the wife of Duke Karl Alexander in Veit Harlan's Jud Süß (1940). [SEP] [CLS] After the war, she acted only rarely in films. [SEP] [CLS] After her death in 1973, she was buried in the family vault. [SEP] [CLS]
