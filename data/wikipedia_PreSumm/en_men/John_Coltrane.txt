[CLS] John William Coltrane (September 23, 1926 – July 17, 1967) was an American jazz saxophonist and composer. [SEP] [CLS] Working in the bebop and hard bop idioms early in his career, Coltrane helped pioneer the use of modes and was at the forefront of free jazz. [SEP] [CLS] He led at least fifty recording sessions and appeared on many albums by other musicians, including trumpeter Miles Davis and pianist Thelonious Monk. [SEP] [CLS] Over the course of his career, Coltrane's music took on an increasingly spiritual dimension. [SEP] [CLS] He remains one of the most influential saxophonists in music history. [SEP] [CLS] He received numerous posthumous awards, including canonization by the African Orthodox Church and a Pulitzer Prize in 2007. [SEP] [CLS] His second wife was pianist and harpist Alice Coltrane. [SEP] [CLS] The couple had three children: John Jr. [SEP] [CLS] (1964–1982), a bassist; Ravi (born 1965), a saxophonist; and Oran (born 1967), also a saxophonist. [SEP] [CLS]
