Luísa Vilar Braamcamp Sobral ComM (Portuguese pronunciation: [luˈizɐ viˈlaɾ bɾɐ̃ˈkɐ̃p suˈβɾaɫ]; born 18 September 1987) is a Portuguese singer and songwriter. [CLS] [SEP] She came to prominence in 2003 after finishing third in the first season of Ídolos, the Portuguese version of Idols television series. [CLS] [SEP] After going on hiatus to attend the Berklee College of Music, she released her debut album, The Cherry on My Cake, in 2011. [CLS] [SEP] She later composed "Amar pelos dois", which won the Eurovision Song Contest 2017 for Portugal and was performed by her brother Salvador Sobral. [CLS] [SEP]
