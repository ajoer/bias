At 04:56 and 06:19 UTC on October 2, 1969 (21:56 and 23:19 PDT October 1), a pair of earthquakes of magnitude 5.6 and 5.7, respectively, struck the city of Santa Rosa, California, killing one person and damaging buildings. [CLS] [SEP] The maximum felt intensity for the two events was VII (Very strong) and VIII (Severe) respectively on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] The $8.35 million cost of the earthquakes went mostly to replace buildings damaged beyond repair. [CLS] [SEP] There were at least 200 aftershocks after the initial pair of earthquakes. [CLS] [SEP] At the time they were the largest earthquakes to affect the northern San Francisco Bay Area since the 1906 San Francisco earthquake. [CLS] [SEP]
