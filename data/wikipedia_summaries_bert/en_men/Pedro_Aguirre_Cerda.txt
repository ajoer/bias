Pedro Aguirre Cerda (American Spanish: [ˈpeðɾo aˈɣire ˈseɾða]; February 6, 1879 – November 25, 1941) was a Chilean political figure. [CLS] [SEP] A member of the Radical Party, he was chosen as the Popular Front's candidate for the 1938 presidential election, and was elected. [CLS] [SEP] He governed Chile until his death in 1941. [CLS] [SEP] Pedro Aguirre Cerda was of Basque descent. [CLS] [SEP]
