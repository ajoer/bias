Daniel Gerhard Brown (born June 22, 1964) is an American author best known for his thriller novels, including the Robert Langdon novels Angels & Demons (2000), The Da Vinci Code (2003), The Lost Symbol (2009), Inferno (2013) and Origin (2017). [CLS] [SEP] His novels are treasure hunts that usually take place over a period of 24 hours. [CLS] [SEP] They feature recurring themes of cryptography, art, and conspiracy theories. [CLS] [SEP] His books have been translated into 57 languages and, as of 2012, have sold over 200 million copies. [CLS] [SEP] Three of them, Angels & Demons, The Da Vinci Code, and Inferno, have been adapted into films. [CLS] [SEP] The Robert Langdon novels are deeply engaged with Christian themes and historical fact, and have generated controversy as a result. [CLS] [SEP] Brown states on his website that his books are not anti-Christian and he is on a "constant spiritual journey" himself. [CLS] [SEP] He claims that his book The Da Vinci Code is simply "an entertaining story that promotes spiritual discussion and debate" and suggests that the book may be used "as a positive catalyst for introspection and exploration of our faith."
