Cyclonic Storm Sagar was the strongest tropical cyclone to make landfall in Somalia in recorded history, and the first named cyclone of the 2018 North Indian Ocean cyclone season. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on May 16 east of the Guardafui Channel, Sagar intensified into a cyclonic storm on the next day, as it gradually organized. [CLS] [SEP] The storm turned to the west-southwest and traversed the entirety of the Gulf of Aden, making landfall over northwestern Somalia on May 19, farther west than any other storm on record in the North Indian Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Sagar weakened into a remnant low on May 20. [CLS] [SEP] The storm first affected Yemen, brushing the coast with heavy rainfall and gusty winds.
