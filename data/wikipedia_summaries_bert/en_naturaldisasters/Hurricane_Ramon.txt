Hurricane Ramon was a very intense Pacific hurricane that generated heavy rains in Southern California. [CLS] [SEP] The 19th named storm and final hurricane of the above-average 1987 Pacific hurricane season, Ramon originated from a tropical disturbance that formed in early October. [CLS] [SEP] On October 5, a tropical storm had developed several hundred miles southwest of Manzanillo, bypassing the tropical depression stage. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Ramon turned to the west-northwest after initially moving west. [CLS] [SEP] It intensified into a hurricane on October 7. [CLS] [SEP] Two days later, Hurricane Ramon peaked in intensity with winds of 140 mph (220 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] After peaking, Ramon turned to the northwest and rapidly weakened over cooler waters. [CLS] [SEP] It weakened into a tropical storm on October 11 and a depression on October 12. [CLS] [SEP] Ramon dissipated shortly thereafter. [CLS] [SEP] While at sea, Ramon brought light rainfall to the Baja California Peninsula. [CLS] [SEP] The remnants of Hurricane Ramon produced heavy rainfall that caused flooding in California, indirectly contributing to five traffic-related fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] Rainfall was reported as far inland as Utah. [CLS] [SEP]
