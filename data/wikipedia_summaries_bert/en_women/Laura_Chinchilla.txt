Laura Chinchilla Miranda (Spanish: [ˈlawɾa tʃinˈtʃiɟa miˈɾanda]; born 28 March 1959) is a Costa Rican politician who was President of Costa Rica from 2010 to 2014. [CLS] [SEP] She was one of Óscar Arias Sánchez's two Vice-Presidents and his administration's Minister of Justice. [CLS] [SEP] She was the governing PLN candidate for president in the 2010 general election, where she won with 46.76% of the vote on 7 February. [CLS] [SEP] She was the eighth woman president of a Latin American country and the first woman to become President of Costa Rica. [CLS] [SEP] She was sworn in as President of Costa Rica on May 8, 2010.She currently teaches at Georgetown University and is co-chair of the Inter-American Dialogue think tank. [CLS] [SEP]
