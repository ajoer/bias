[CLS] Anna Schnidenwind (née Trutt; 1688 in Wyhl – 24 April 1751 in Endingen am Kaiserstuhl), was one of the last people in Germany and in Europe confirmed to have been executed in public for witchcraft. [SEP] [CLS] It was for the devastating fire of Wyhl on 7 March 1751 which became the trigger of the witch trial. [SEP] [CLS] The fire destroyed most of the village. [SEP] [CLS] The 63-year-old peasant was accused of having caused arson through a Devil's pact. [SEP] [CLS] Schnidenwind was judged guilty and sentenced to death. [SEP] [CLS] She was burned on 24 April 1751 in Endingen in Breisgau, after strangulation. [SEP] [CLS] The region was in those times part of Anterior Austria. [SEP] [CLS] It is quite sure, that the government in Vienna did not know about the case. [SEP] [CLS]
