[CLS] Typhoon Ike was the second deadliest tropical cyclone in the 20th century in the Philippines, where it was known as Typhoon Nitang. [SEP] [CLS] Ike originated from an area of disturbed weather southeast of Guam on August 21, 1984, and five days later, developed into a tropical depression. [SEP] [CLS] Following an increase in organization, the depression attained tropical storm intensity on August 27. [SEP] [CLS] Initially tracking west-southwest, the storm gradually gained strength as wind shear resulted relaxed and Ike became a typhoon on August 30. [SEP] [CLS] Continuing to rapidly intensity, Ike turned west and attained peak intensity on September 1, with the Japan Meteorological Agency estimating winds of 170 km/h (105 mph). [SEP] [CLS] At around 14:00 UTC that day, Ike made landfall on the northeastern tip of Mindanao. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone emerged into the South China Sea on September 3 as a tropical storm before re-intensifying into a typhoon and moving onshore Hainan. [SEP] [CLS] Ike then struck the Chinese mainland as a tropical storm in the Guangxi province and dissipated on September 6. [SEP] [CLS] During its formative stages, Ike brushed Guam, although its compact size reduced the extent of damage. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Ike also struck the Philippines a mere four days after Tropical Storm June inundated the northern portion of the Philippines and also was suffering from the nation's worst economic crisis since independence in 1946. [SEP] [CLS] It also left a path of destruction in the Philippines that at the time was unparalleled in its modern history. [SEP] [CLS] Most of the deaths were in the province of Surigao del Norte, where around 1,000 died, 330 others were wounded, and 80% of structures along with 27 towns were flattened. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Ike was considered the worst typhoon to affect the province in 20 years. [SEP] [CLS] Roughly 90% of homes in Surigao City were leveled, leaving 90,000 individuals homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout Negros Island, over 4,000 dwellings were destroyed, resulting in nearly 75,000 people homeless after a river burst its banks. [SEP] [CLS] In the province of Bohol, Ike was the deadliest natural disaster in the province's history, with 198 fatalities in addition to 89,000 homes damaged or destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, 1,426 people were killed as a result of the typhoon in the archipelago. [SEP] [CLS] At the time, Ike was the deadliest typhoon to hit the country during the 20th century, surpassing the previous record of Typhoon Amy in 1951. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 1,856 people were injured. [SEP] [CLS] Furthermore, 142,653 homes were damaged and 108,219 others were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Nationwide, damage was estimated at $230 million, including $76.5 million from crop damage and $111 million from property damage. [SEP] [CLS] Following the storm, Philippines authorities initially distributed $4 million in aid but refused international aid. [SEP] [CLS] However, authorities reversed its decision on September 8 due to lack of local resources and started accepting foreign aid. [SEP] [CLS] In all, over $7.5 million was donated to the country to provide relief. [SEP] [CLS] Ike was the worst tropical cyclone to strike the Guangxi province in China since 1954, where 14 people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] Across the country, around 13,000 structures were damaged or destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Nationwide, 46 people were killed and 12,000 ha (29,651 acres) of sugar cane were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] About 1,315,420 kg (2,900,000 lb) of vegetables were lost. [SEP] [CLS] Elsewhere, two people were killed and seven were listed missing in Thailand due to flash flooding. [SEP] [CLS]
