The 1806 Great Coastal hurricane was a severe and damaging storm along the East Coast of the United States which produced upwards of 36 in (91 cm) of rainfall in parts of Massachusetts. [CLS] [SEP] First observed east of the Lesser Antilles on 17 August, the hurricane arrived at the Bahamas by 19 August. [CLS] [SEP] The disturbance continued to drift northward and made landfall at the mouth of the Cape Fear River in North Carolina on 22 August. [CLS] [SEP] The storm soon moved out to sea as a Category 2-equivalent hurricane on the Saffirâ€“Simpson hurricane wind scale, persisting off of New England before dissipating south of Nova Scotia on 25 August as a markedly weaker storm. [CLS] [SEP] In the Carolinas, salt, sugar, rice, and lumber industries suffered considerably, and several individuals were killed. [CLS] [SEP] Wharves and vessels endured moderate damage, with many ships wrecked on North Carolinan barrier islands. [CLS] [SEP] A majority of the deaths caused by the hurricane occurred aboard the Rose-in-Bloom offshore of Barnegat Inlet, New Jersey, with 21 of the ship's 48 passengers killed and $171,000 (1806 USD) in damage to its cargo. [CLS] [SEP] Upon arriving in New England, reports indicated extreme rainfall, though no deaths were reported; in all, the hurricane killed more than 24 individuals along the entirety of its track.
