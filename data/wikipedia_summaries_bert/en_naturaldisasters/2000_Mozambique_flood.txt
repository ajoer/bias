[CLS] The 2000 Mozambique flood was a natural disaster that occurred in February and March 2000. [SEP] [CLS] The catastrophic flooding was caused by heavy rainfall that lasted for five weeks and made many homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Approximately 700 people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] 1,400 km2 of arable land was affected and 20,000 head of cattle were lost. [SEP] [CLS] It was the worst flood in Mozambique in 50 years.It started in South Africa when heavy rain falls traveled over to Mozambique. [SEP] [CLS] It caused dozens of deaths. [SEP] [CLS] 44,000 were left homeless and many of them had lost relatives of some kind. [SEP] [CLS] Later, Cyclone Eline came and destroyed many more homes and lives. [SEP] [CLS] The women and children were hurrying to shelter and high land. [SEP] [CLS] 800 had died and thousands of livestock were killed. [SEP] [CLS] The government distributed 15 million dollars (2000 USD) to its citizens to account for damage property and loss of income. [SEP] [CLS] As of 2016, people were still living in recovery shelters with fluctuating water supplies. [SEP] [CLS]
