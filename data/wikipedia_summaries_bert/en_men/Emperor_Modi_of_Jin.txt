[CLS] Emperor Mo of Jin (died 9 February 1234), personal name Hudun, sinicised name Wanyan Chenglin, was the last emperor of the Jurchen-led Jin dynasty, which ruled northern China between the 12th and 13th centuries. [SEP] [CLS] Originally a military general, he inherited the throne from his predecessor, Emperor Aizong, during the siege of Caizhou. [SEP] [CLS] He was killed in action on the same day he was crowned emperor, when Caizhou fell to the allied forces of the Mongol Empire and Southern Song dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] Having ruled as emperor for less than a day, or maybe even just a few hours, he holds the record for being the shortest-reigning monarch in Chinese history. [SEP] [CLS]
