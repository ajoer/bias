Andre Romelle Young (born February 18, 1965), known professionally as Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Dre, is an American rapper, record producer, audio engineer, record executive, entrepreneur, and actor. [CLS] [SEP] He is the founder and CEO of Aftermath Entertainment and Beats Electronics, and was previously co-owner of Death Row Records. [CLS] [SEP] Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Dre began his career as a member of the World Class Wreckin' Cru in 1985, and later found fame with the gangsta rap group N.W.A. [CLS] [SEP] The group popularized explicit lyrics in hip hop to detail the violence of street life. [CLS] [SEP] During the early 1990s, Dre was credited as a key figure in the crafting and popularization of West Coast G-funk, a subgenre of hip hop characterized by a synthesizer foundation and slow, heavy beats. [CLS] [SEP] Dre's solo debut studio album The Chronic (1992), released under Death Row Records, made him one of the best-selling American music artists of 1993. [CLS] [SEP] It earned him a Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance for the single "Let Me Ride", as well as several accolades for the single "Nuthin' but a 'G' Thang". [CLS] [SEP] That year, he produced Death Row labelmate Snoop Doggy Dogg's debut album Doggystyle, and mentored producers such as his step-brother Warren G (leading to the multi-platinum debut Regulate...G Funk Era in 1994) and Snoop Dogg's cousin Daz Dillinger (leading to the double-platinum debut Dogg Food by Tha Dogg Pound in 1995). [CLS] [SEP] In 1996, Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Dre left Death Row Records to establish his own label, Aftermath Entertainment. [CLS] [SEP] He produced a compilation album, Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Dre Presents the Aftermath, in 1996, and released a solo album, 2001, in 1999. [CLS] [SEP] During the 2000s, Dre focused on producing other artists, occasionally contributing vocals. [CLS] [SEP] He signed Eminem in 1998 and 50 Cent in 2002, and co-produced their albums. [CLS] [SEP] He has produced albums for and overseen the careers of many other rappers, including 2Pac, The D.O.C., Snoop Dogg, Xzibit, Knoc-turn'al, The Game, Kendrick Lamar, and Anderson Paak. [CLS] [SEP] Dre has also had acting roles in movies such as Set It Off, The Wash and Training Day. [CLS] [SEP] He has won six Grammy Awards, including Producer of the Year; Non-Classical. [CLS] [SEP] Rolling Stone ranked him number 56 on the list of 100 Greatest Artists of All Time. [CLS] [SEP] He was the second-richest figure in hip hop as of 2018 with an estimated net worth of $800 million.Accusations of Dre's violence against women have been widely publicised. [CLS] [SEP] Following his assault of television host Dee Barnes, he was fined $2,500, given two years' probation, ordered to undergo 240 hours of community service, and given a spot on an anti-violence public service announcement on television. [CLS] [SEP] A civil suit was also settled out of court. [CLS] [SEP] In 2015, Michel'le, the mother of one of his children, accused him of subjecting her to domestic violence during their time together as a couple. [CLS] [SEP] Their abusive relationship is portrayed in her 2016 biopic Surviving Compton: Dre, Suge & Michel'le. [CLS] [SEP] Lisa Johnson, the mother of three of Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Dre's children, stated that he beat her many times, including while she was pregnant. [CLS] [SEP] She was granted a restraining order against him. [CLS] [SEP] Former labelmate Tairrie B claimed that he assaulted her at a post-Grammy party in 1990, in response to her track "Ruthless Bitch."
