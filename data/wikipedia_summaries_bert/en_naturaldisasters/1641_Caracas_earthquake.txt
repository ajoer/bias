The 1641 Caracas earthquake took place in Venezuela on 11 June 1641. [CLS] [SEP] It is often known as the San Bernabé earthquake because 11 June is the feast day of Barnabas in the Catholic calendar. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused extensive damage in Caracas and the destruction of La Guaira; the event led the Caracas city council to propose rebuilding its city in what was then the savannah of Chacao, a move that was opposed by the Governor, Ruy Fernández de Fuenmayor.
