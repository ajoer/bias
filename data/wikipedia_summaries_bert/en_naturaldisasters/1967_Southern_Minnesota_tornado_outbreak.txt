A destructive severe weather episode affected portions of the Midwestern and Southern United States from April 30–May 2, 1967. [CLS] [SEP] It consisted of two consecutive tornado outbreaks that generated at least 38 tornadoes, causing 13 fatalities and 90 injuries. [CLS] [SEP] All of the deaths occurred on April 30, which is known as the 1967 Iowa–Minnesota tornado outbreak, or Black Sunday, to residents of Iowa and southern Minnesota. [CLS] [SEP] That day spawned a total of 21 tornadoes, devastating the towns of Albert Lea and Waseca, Minnesota. [CLS] [SEP]
