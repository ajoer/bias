Hurricane Chantal was one of three tropical cyclones to make landfall in Texas during the 1989 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] The third named storm and the first hurricane of the season, Chantal slowly developed on July 30 in the southern Gulf of Mexico from a tropical disturbance that was previously within Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) while near Trinidad and Tobago. [CLS] [SEP] While heading north-northwestward, the depression steadily intensified and was upgraded to Tropical Storm Chantal on the following day. [CLS] [SEP] Thereafter, Chantal quickly strengthen and became a hurricane on August 1. [CLS] [SEP] After intensifying slightly further, Chantal made landfall near High Island, Texas later that day. [CLS] [SEP] The storm quickly weakened upon moving inland and fell to tropical storm intensity a few hours after landfall. [CLS] [SEP] Early on August 2, Chantal weakened to a tropical depression and dissipated over Oklahoma by August 4. [CLS] [SEP] While making landfall in Texas, the storm produced relatively small tides, with most locations reporting waves less than 4 feet (1.2 m) in height. [CLS] [SEP] However, some locations experienced extensive beach erosion. [CLS] [SEP] In addition, there were numerous rescues made by the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Coast Guard. [CLS] [SEP] Due to both rainfall and high winds at least 3,000 homes were damaged, and numerous trees and sign were knocked down. [CLS] [SEP] Two tornadoes were reported, with one causing the destruction of a shed in Crystal Beach, Texas, and the other knocking over several trees and mobile homes in Iota, Louisiana. [CLS] [SEP] Elsewhere, Chantal and its remnants brought light to moderate rainfall to several other states, although affects were minor in other states. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, 13 fatalities occurred, all of which due to drowning, and at least $100 million (1989 USD) in damage was reported. [CLS] [SEP]
