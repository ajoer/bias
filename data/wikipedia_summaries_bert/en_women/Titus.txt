Titus ( TY-təs; Titus Caesar Vespasianus; 30 December 39 – 13 September 81 AD) was Roman emperor from 79 to 81. [CLS] [SEP] A member of the Flavian dynasty, Titus succeeded his father Vespasian upon his death. [CLS] [SEP] Before becoming emperor, Titus gained renown as a military commander, serving under his father in Judea during the First Jewish–Roman War. [CLS] [SEP] The campaign came to a brief halt with the death of emperor Nero in 68, launching Vespasian's bid for the imperial power during the Year of the Four Emperors. [CLS] [SEP] When Vespasian was declared Emperor on 1 July 69, Titus was left in charge of ending the Jewish rebellion. [CLS] [SEP] In 70, he besieged and captured Jerusalem, and destroyed the city and the Second Temple. [CLS] [SEP] For this achievement Titus was awarded a triumph; the Arch of Titus commemorates his victory to this day. [CLS] [SEP] During his father's rule, Titus gained notoriety in Rome serving as prefect of the Praetorian Guard, and for carrying on a controversial relationship with the Jewish queen Berenice. [CLS] [SEP] Despite concerns over his character, Titus ruled to great acclaim following the death of Vespasian in 79, and was considered a good emperor by Suetonius and other contemporary historians. [CLS] [SEP] As emperor, Titus is best known for completing the Colosseum and for his generosity in relieving the suffering caused by two disasters, the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79 and a fire in Rome in 80. [CLS] [SEP] After barely two years in office, Titus died of a fever on 13 September 81. [CLS] [SEP] He was deified by the Roman Senate and succeeded by his younger brother Domitian. [CLS] [SEP]
