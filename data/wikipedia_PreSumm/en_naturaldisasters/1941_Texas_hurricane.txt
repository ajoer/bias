[CLS] The 1941 Texas hurricane, the second storm of the 1941 Atlantic hurricane season, was a large and intense tropical cyclone that struck coastal Texas as a major hurricane in September 1941, causing relatively severe damage. [SEP] [CLS] The storm is estimated to have formed in the eastern Gulf of Mexico on September 16. [SEP] [CLS] After attaining hurricane strength, it completed a clockwise loop and turned northwestward. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane continued to strengthen until it made landfall near East Matagorda Bay, Texas, with winds of 125 miles per hour (201 km/h), but rapidly weakened as it headed inland. [SEP] [CLS] Damage from the storm amounted to about $6.5 million, and crops throughout the region were largely destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] The city of Houston suffered extensive damage as the storm passed to the east. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane disrupted activities related to the Louisiana Maneuvers. [SEP] [CLS] Later, the system became extratropical and passed over Lake Huron, killing three people in Toronto. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, seven people lost their lives due to the cyclone. [SEP] [CLS]
