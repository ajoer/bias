[CLS] The January 2016 United States Blizzard was a crippling and historic nor'easter blizzard that produced up to 3 ft (91 cm) of snow in parts of the Mid-Atlantic and Northeast United States from January 22 to January 24, 2016. [SEP] [CLS] Evolving from a shortwave trough that formed in the Pacific Northwest on January 19, the system consolidated into a defined low-pressure area on January 21 over Texas. [SEP] [CLS] Regarding it as a "potentially historic blizzard", meteorologists indicated the storm could produce more than 2 ft (61 cm) of snow across a wide swath of the Mid-Atlantic region and could "paralyze the eastern third of the nation". [SEP] [CLS] Winter weather expert Paul Kocin described the blizzard as "kind of a top-10 snowstorm".On January 20–22, the governors of eleven states and the mayor of Washington, D.C. [SEP] [CLS] declared a state of emergency in anticipation of significant snowfall and blizzard conditions. [SEP] [CLS] Approximately 103 million people were affected by the storm, with 33 million people under blizzard warnings. [SEP] [CLS] More than 13,000 flights were cancelled in relation to the storm, with effects rippling internationally. [SEP] [CLS] Thousands of National Guardsmen were placed on standby and states deployed millions of gallons of brine and thousands of tons of road salt to lessen the storm's effect on roadways. [SEP] [CLS] A travel ban was instituted for New York City and Newark, New Jersey for January 23–24. [SEP] [CLS] The storm was given various unofficial names, including Winter Storm Jonas, Blizzard of 2016 and Snowzilla. [SEP] [CLS] Seven states observed snowfall in excess of 30 in (76 cm), with accumulations peaking at 42 in (110 cm) in Glengary, West Virginia. [SEP] [CLS] Ice- and snow-covered roads led to hundreds of incidents across the affected region, several of which resulted in deaths and injuries. [SEP] [CLS] At least 55 people were killed in storm-related incidents: 12 in Virginia, 9 in Pennsylvania, 6 in New Jersey, 6 in New York, 6 in North Carolina, 4 in South Carolina, 3 in Maryland, 3 in Washington, D.C., 1 in Arkansas, 1 in Delaware, 1 in Georgia, 1 in Kentucky, 1 in Massachusetts, and 1 in Ohio. [SEP] [CLS] Total economic losses are estimated between $500 million and $3 billion. [SEP] [CLS] The storm ranked as a Category 5 "extreme" event for the Northeast on the Regional Snowfall Index, and a Category 4 for the Southeast. [SEP] [CLS] It is the most recent winter storm to rank as a Category 5, and the first to do so since the 2011 Groundhog Day Blizzard. [SEP] [CLS]
