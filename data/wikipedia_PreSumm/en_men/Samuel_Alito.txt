[CLS] Samuel Anthony Alito Jr. [SEP] [CLS] (; born April 1, 1950) is an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] He was nominated by President George W. [SEP] [CLS] Bush on October 31, 2005, and has served since January 31, 2006.Raised in Hamilton Township, New Jersey, and educated at Princeton University and Yale Law School, Alito served as U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Attorney for the District of New Jersey and a judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit before joining the Supreme Court. [SEP] [CLS] He is the 110th Justice, the second Italian American, and the eleventh Roman Catholic to serve on the court. [SEP] [CLS] Alito is considered "one of the most conservative justices on the Court". [SEP] [CLS] He has described himself as a "practical originalist." Alito's majority opinions in landmark cases include McDonald v. [SEP] [CLS] Chicago, Burwell v. [SEP] [CLS] Hobby Lobby, Murphy v. [SEP] [CLS] NCAA, and Janus v. [SEP] [CLS] AFSCME. [SEP] [CLS]
