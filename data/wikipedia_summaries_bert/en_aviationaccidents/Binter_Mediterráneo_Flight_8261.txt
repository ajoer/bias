Binter Mediterráneo Flight 8261 (Registration EC-FBC), on 29 August 2001, crash-landed next to the N-340 highway, some 200 metres short of runway 32 at Ruiz Picasso International Airport at Málaga, Spain. [CLS] [SEP] The captain reported to Málaga Air Traffic Control, while on final approach, a fire in the aircraft's port engine. [CLS] [SEP] The fire turned out to be a false alarm but, in following the emergency procedures, the First Officer inadvertently shut down both of the aircraft's engines. [CLS] [SEP] The plane descended, hitting the airport approach lights, and stopping next to the N-340. [CLS] [SEP] Four of the 47 people on board were killed, including the captain. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was scrapped. [CLS] [SEP]
