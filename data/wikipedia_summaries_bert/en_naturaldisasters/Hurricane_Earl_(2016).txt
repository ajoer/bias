[CLS] Hurricane Earl (pronounced [uɾaˈkan eˈaɾl]) was the deadliest Atlantic hurricane to impact Mexico since Hurricane Stan in 2005. [SEP] [CLS] The fifth named storm and second hurricane of the 2016 Atlantic hurricane season, Earl formed from a tropical wave south of Jamaica on August 2. [SEP] [CLS] The precursor to Earl brought torrential rainfall and flooding to the Lesser Antilles. [SEP] [CLS] Upon classification, the storm moved westward through the Caribbean Sea, brushing the north coast of Honduras. [SEP] [CLS] Earl strengthened into an 85 mph (140 km/h) hurricane before making landfall on Belize on August 4. [SEP] [CLS] It weakened while moving across the Yucatán Peninsula, but reintensified in the Bay of Campeche and followed the coastline. [SEP] [CLS] On August 6, Earl dissipated after moving ashore Veracruz. [SEP] [CLS] Prior to Earl becoming a tropical cyclone, 13 people died in storm-related incidents across the Dominican Republic. [SEP] [CLS] Striking Belize as a Category 1, the hurricane caused extensive damage; losses to agriculture exceeded US$100 million. [SEP] [CLS] Heavy rain in Mexico triggered many landslides, several of which proved fatal in the states of Puebla, Hidalgo, and Veracruz; at least 81 people died in the country. [SEP] [CLS] Lesser effects were felt in neighboring countries in Central America. [SEP] [CLS]
