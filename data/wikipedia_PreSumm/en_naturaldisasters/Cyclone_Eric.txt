[CLS] Severe Tropical Cyclone Eric was one of two tropical cyclones to affect the island nations of Vanuatu and Fiji within a week during January 1985. [SEP] [CLS] The precursor shallow depression developed within the monsoon trough during January 13, to the west of Espiritu Santo, Vanuatu. [SEP] [CLS] On January 16, the storm developed-hurricane-force winds and Eric began to undergo rapid deepening. [SEP] [CLS] While two different agencies differ on when and how strong Eric was at its peak, it was believed to have peak on January 17 while passing through the Fiji island group. [SEP] [CLS] Shortly after its peak, Eric began to weaken steadily, and by January 20, Severe Tropical Cyclone Eric had ceased to exist as a tropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Cyclone Eric combined with another storm (Cyclone Nigel) brought 25 fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] Eric caused $40 million (1985 USD) worth of damage. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 299 farms were affected as well as the airport in Nadi. [SEP] [CLS] About 30,000 people were left homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Severe crop damage was also reported. [SEP] [CLS] Viti Levu sustained the worst effects from Cyclone Eric. [SEP] [CLS] During the aftermath of the storm, a number of first world countries distributed aid for victims of Eric.
