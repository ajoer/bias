[CLS] Typhoon Haima, known in the Philippines as Super Typhoon Lawin, was the third most intense tropical cyclone worldwide in 2016. [SEP] [CLS] It was the twenty-second named storm and the eleventh typhoon of the annual typhoon season. [SEP] [CLS] Impacting the Philippines less than 3 days after Typhoon Sarika, Haima formed out of a tropical disturbance southwest of Chuuk on October 14, it developed into a tropical storm the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Steady strengthening occurred over the next day or two as it tracked westward towards the Philippines. [SEP] [CLS] After forming an eye shortly after it was upgraded to a typhoon, Haima began to rapidly strengthen and eventually became a super typhoon on October 18. [SEP] [CLS] It later attained its peak intensity as a Category 5-equivalent tropical cyclone before weakening slightly. [SEP] [CLS] Haima later made landfall late on October 19 as a Category 4-equivalent storm. [SEP] [CLS] Rapid weakening occurred as it interacted with the landmasses until it entered the Southern China Sea as a weak typhoon. [SEP] [CLS] It formed a large ragged eye once again and remained steady in intensity until making landfall in China on October 21. [SEP] [CLS] It weakened below typhoon intensity and became extratropical on October 22. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone drifted northeastwards and later eastwards before emerging over water again, but eventually dissipated by October 26. [SEP] [CLS] 19 people were killed by Haima, and damage totals were estimated in excess of US$970 million. [SEP] [CLS] The storm forced several hundred flights in the Philippines, Hong Kong and China to be cancelled. [SEP] [CLS] Ahead of the storm, several shelters were set up in the areas near China by the government to adequate the affected people Flooding and storm surge affected many coastal areas, downing several trees and power lines, leading to power outages. [SEP] [CLS] Due to the damage caused by the storm in the Philippines and China, the names Haima and Lawin were retired from their respective name lists in 2017. [SEP] [CLS]
