The 1933 Tampico hurricane was one of two storms in the 1933 Atlantic hurricane season to reach Category 5 intensity on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale. [CLS] [SEP] It developed on September 16 near the Lesser Antilles, and slowly intensified while moving across the Caribbean Sea. [CLS] [SEP] Becoming a hurricane on September 19, its strengthening rate increased while passing south of Jamaica. [CLS] [SEP] Two days later, the hurricane reached peak winds, estimated at 160 mph (260 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] After weakening, it made landfall on the Yucatán Peninsula, destroying several houses. [CLS] [SEP] One person was killed offshore Progreso, Yucatán during the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Over land, the hurricane weakened to a tropical storm, although it re-intensified slightly in the Gulf of Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] On September 25, it made a second landfall just south of Tampico, Tamaulipas with winds at around 110 mph (180 km/h), and it quickly dissipated over land. [CLS] [SEP] Damage was heaviest there, estimated at $5 million (1933 USD) and there were hundreds of deaths. [CLS] [SEP] About 75% of the houses in Tampico were damaged, including about 50% of houses that had severe to total destruction to their roofs. [CLS] [SEP] The destruction prompted the declaration of martial law, and there was a curfew instated. [CLS] [SEP]
