The 1966 flood of the Arno (Italian: Alluvione di Firenze del 4 novembre 1966) in Florence killed 101 people and damaged or destroyed millions of masterpieces of art and rare books. [CLS] [SEP] It is considered the worst flood in the city's history since 1557. [CLS] [SEP] With the combined effort of Italian and foreign volunteers alike, or angeli del fango ("Mud Angels"), many of these fine works have been restored. [CLS] [SEP] New methods in conservation were devised and restoration laboratories established. [CLS] [SEP] However, even decades later, much work remains to be done. [CLS] [SEP]
