[CLS] Gabriele "Gabi" Zimmer (born 7 May 1955) is a German politician who served as Member of the European Parliament (MEP) for Germany from 2004 to 2019. [SEP] [CLS] She joined the Socialist Unity Party of Germany (SED), ruling party of the German Democratic Republic (East Germany) in 1981. [SEP] [CLS] After its transformation into the Party of Democratic Socialism in 1990, she became the party's leader in the new state of Thuringia, a position she held until 1998. [SEP] [CLS] She was a member of the Landtag of Thuringia, the state parliament, from 1990 until 2004. [SEP] [CLS] She became deputy leader of PDS in 1996, and in October 2000 she was elected leader. [SEP] [CLS] However, after the party failed to pass the 5% electoral threshold in the 2002 federal election, she chose not to stand for re-election, and left the leadership in May 2003. [SEP] [CLS] She remained active in the party, and was elected to the European Parliament in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] In 2007, PDS merged into The Left, and Zimmer was re-elected as an MEP representing The Left in 2009 and 2014. [SEP] [CLS] In 2012, she became the chairwoman of the GUE/NGL group in the European Parliament. [SEP] [CLS] She chose not to stand for re-election in the 2019 European Parliament election.
