The 2000 Baku earthquake occurred on November 25 at 22:09 (18:09 UTC) local time with an epicenter just offshore Baku, Azerbaijan. [CLS] [SEP] It measured 6.8 on the moment magnitude scale and the maximum felt intensity was VII on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] It was followed three minutes later by a quake measuring 5.9. [CLS] [SEP] It was the strongest for almost 160 years, since 1842 in the Baku suburbs and in addition to the capital affected Sumgayit, Shamakhi and neighboring cities. [CLS] [SEP] According to the United States Geological Survey, the epicentre was in the Caspian Sea, 25 km to the south-southeast of Baku. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was felt as far away as e.g. [CLS] [SEP] Tbilisi, 600 km northwest of the epicentre, Makhachkala and the Karabudakh and Isberbas settlements in Dagestan. [CLS] [SEP]
