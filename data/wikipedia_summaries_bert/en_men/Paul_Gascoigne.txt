[CLS] Paul John Gascoigne (, born 27 May 1967) is an English former professional football player and manager. [SEP] [CLS] He is also known by his nickname, Gazza. [SEP] [CLS] He earned 57 caps during his England career. [SEP] [CLS] The National Football Museum stated that he is "widely recognised as the most naturally talented English footballer of his generation".Born and raised in Gateshead, Gascoigne signed schoolboy terms with Newcastle United, before turning professional with the top tier (pre-Premier League creation) club in 1985. [SEP] [CLS] Three years later he was sold on to Tottenham Hotspur for a £2.2 million fee. [SEP] [CLS] He won the FA Cup with Spurs in 1991, before being sold to Italian club Lazio for £5.5 million the following year. [SEP] [CLS] In July 1995, he was transferred to Rangers for £4.3 million and helped the club to two league titles and two trophies. [SEP] [CLS] He returned to England in a £3.4 million move to Middlesbrough in March 1998. [SEP] [CLS] He made his debut in the Premier League in the 1998–99 season, having already featured in the 1998 Football League Cup Final. [SEP] [CLS] He switched to Everton in July 2000, and later had spells with Burnley, Gansu Tianma (China), and Boston United. [SEP] [CLS] Gascoigne was part of the England team that reached fourth place in the 1990 FIFA World Cup, where he famously cried after receiving a yellow card in the semi-final with West Germany which meant he would have been suspended for the final had England won the game. [SEP] [CLS] He also helped the team to the semi-finals of UEFA Euro 1996, which included scoring a goal against Scotland described by The Guardian in 2013 as "one of the most iconic goals in the game's recent history". [SEP] [CLS] He has been involved in a number of high-profile goal celebrations at both club and international level, including the "dentist's chair" celebration from Euro '96, and mimicking playing the flute with Rangers in 1998.In the later parts of his career and especially following retirement, Gascoigne's life became dominated by severe mental and emotional problems, particularly alcoholism. [SEP] [CLS] He has been jailed or sectioned on numerous occasions and his struggles receive regular coverage in the British press. [SEP] [CLS] He has frequently attempted to live without alcohol, though rehabilitation programmes have provided only temporary relief. [SEP] [CLS] His personal issues ended his coaching career, and he has not worked in football since being dismissed as the manager of Kettering Town in 2005. [SEP] [CLS]
