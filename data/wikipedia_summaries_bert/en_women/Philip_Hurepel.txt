Philip I of Boulogne (Philip Hurepel) (1200–1235) was a French prince, Count of Clermont-en-Beauvaisis in his own right, and Count of Boulogne, Mortain, Aumale, and Dammartin-en-Goële jure uxoris. [CLS] [SEP] He was the son of Philip II of France and his controversial third wife Agnes of Merania. [CLS] [SEP] Illegitimacy shadowed his birth and career, but he was legitimated by Pope Innocent III. [CLS] [SEP] He was associated with founding the Tour du Guet in Calais. [CLS] [SEP] He is the first recorded person to bear a differenced version of the arms of France.He was married in c. [CLS] [SEP] 1223 to Matilda II, Countess of Boulogne. [CLS] [SEP] Young Philip, by right of his wife, became Count of Boulogne, Mortain, Aumale, and Dammartin-en-Goële. [CLS] [SEP] He revolted against his sister-in-law Blanche of Castile when his elder half-brother Louis VIII died in 1226. [CLS] [SEP] When Philip died in 1235, Matilda continued to reign and was married to Afonso III of Portugal. [CLS] [SEP] Matilda and Philip had a son Alberic and daughter Joan, who both survived Philip. [CLS] [SEP] Alberic reportedly renounced his rights and went to England, for unknown reasons. [CLS] [SEP] Apparently he survived his mother and died in 1284. [CLS] [SEP] Joan of Boulogne married Gaucher de Châtillon in 1236. [CLS] [SEP]
