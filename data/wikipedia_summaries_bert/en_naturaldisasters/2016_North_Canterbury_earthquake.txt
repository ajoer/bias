The 2016 Kaikoura earthquake was a magnitude 7.8 (Mw) earthquake in the South Island of New Zealand that occurred two minutes after midnight on 14 November 2016 NZDT (11:02 on 13 November UTC). [CLS] [SEP] Ruptures occurred on multiple faults and the earthquake has been described as the "most complex earthquake ever studied".The earthquake started at about 15 kilometres (9 mi) north-east of Culverden and 60 kilometres (37 mi) south-west of the tourist town of Kaikōura and at a depth of approximately 15 kilometres (9 mi). [CLS] [SEP] The complex sequence of ruptures lasted for about two minutes. [CLS] [SEP] The cumulative magnitude of the ruptures was 7.8, with the largest amount of that energy released far to the north of the epicentre.The quake was the second largest New Zealand earthquake since European settlement of the country. [CLS] [SEP] Over $1.8 billion in insurance claims were received. [CLS] [SEP] There were two deaths, in Kaikōura and Mount Lyford. [CLS] [SEP]
