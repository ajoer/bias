Michel Jobert (11 September 1921 – 25 May 2002) was a French politician of the left-wing Gaullist orientation. [CLS] [SEP] He served as Minister of Foreign Affairs under Georges Pompidou, and as Minister of External Commerce under François Mitterrand. [CLS] [SEP] His tenure was marked, in part, by tense relations with the United States as he pursued French independence in the sphere of foreign relations. [CLS] [SEP] This policy at one point led a frustrated Henry Kissinger to call him "an idiot" and a "bad" foreign minister. [CLS] [SEP] Jobert died on 25 May 2002 in Paris, aged 80. [CLS] [SEP]
