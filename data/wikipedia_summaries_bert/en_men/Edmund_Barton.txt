[CLS] Sir Edmund "Toby" Barton, (18 January 1849 â€“ 7 January 1920) was an Australian politician and judge who served as the first Prime Minister of Australia, in office from 1901 to 1903. [SEP] [CLS] He resigned to become a founding member of the High Court of Australia, where he served until his death. [SEP] [CLS] Barton was an early supporter of the federation of the Australian colonies, the goal of which he summarised as "a nation for a continent, and a continent for a nation". [SEP] [CLS] After the retirement of Henry Parkes he came to be seen as the leader of the federation movement in New South Wales. [SEP] [CLS] He was a delegate to the constitutional conventions, playing a key role in the drafting of a national constitution, and was one of the lead campaigners for federation in the subsequent referendums. [SEP] [CLS] In late 1900, despite the initial "Hopetoun Blunder", Barton was commissioned to form a caretaker government as Australia's first prime minister. [SEP] [CLS] His term began on 1 January 1901, the date on which federation occurred. [SEP] [CLS] At the first federal elections in March 1901, Barton and the Protectionists won the most seats, but were well short of a majority. [SEP] [CLS] He was able to continue on as a prime minister by forming an alliance with the fledgling Labor Party, which held the balance of power. [SEP] [CLS] The Barton Government established a number of new national institutions, including the Australian Defence Force and the Commonwealth Public Service. [SEP] [CLS] It introduced nation-wide women's suffrage, and laid the foundations of the White Australia policy with the Immigration Restriction Act 1901. [SEP] [CLS] Barton left politics in 1903 to become one of the three founding members of the High Court, which his government had created. [SEP] [CLS] He was succeeded as prime minister by Alfred Deakin. [SEP] [CLS] On the court, Barton was able to shape the judicial interpretation of the constitution he had helped write. [SEP] [CLS] Although he served 16 years on the High Court and less than three as prime minister, he is chiefly remembered for his political career. [SEP] [CLS]
