Elena Viacheslavovna Dementieva (Russian: Еле́на Вячесла́вовна Деме́нтьева, [jɪˈlʲɛnə dʲɪˈmʲenʲtʲjɪvə] (listen); born 15 October 1981) is a Russian former professional tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] She won the singles gold medal at the 2008 Olympics in Beijing, having previously won the silver medal at the 2000 Olympics in Sydney. [CLS] [SEP] She won 16 WTA singles titles, reached the finals of the 2004 French Open and 2004 US Open and reached seven other Grand Slam semifinals. [CLS] [SEP] Dementieva was also part of the Russian team that won the 2005 Fed Cup. [CLS] [SEP] In doubles, she won the 2002 WTA Championships with Janette Husárová and was the runner-up in two US Open doubles finals – in 2002 with Husárová and in 2005 with Flavia Pennetta. [CLS] [SEP] Dementieva achieved a career-high ranking of world No. [CLS] [SEP] 3, which was accomplished on 6 April 2009. [CLS] [SEP] She announced her retirement on 29 October 2010, after her final match at the 2010 WTA Championships. [CLS] [SEP] Between 2003 and 2010, she only ended one year, in 2007, outside the top 10. [CLS] [SEP] She is considered to be one of the most talented players never to have won a Grand Slam tournament. [CLS] [SEP]
