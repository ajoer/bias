The January 2012 Pacific Northwest snowstorm was a large extratropical cyclone that brought record snowfall to the Pacific Northwest in January 2012. [CLS] [SEP] The storm produced very large snowfall totals, reaching up to 50 inches (1,300 mm) in Oregon. [CLS] [SEP] A 110 mph (180 km/h) wind gust was reported at Otter Rock, Oregon. [CLS] [SEP] A mother and child were killed in Oregon after the car they were in slid into a creek, while a man was killed in the Seattle area. [CLS] [SEP] About 200,000 homes were without power in the Greater Seattle area after the storm. [CLS] [SEP]
