[CLS] Baikal Airlines Flight 130 was a scheduled domestic passenger flight from Irkutsk to Moscow that crashed on 3 January 1994. [SEP] [CLS] The plane involved in the crash was a Tupolev Tu-154 operated by Russian airline Baikal Airlines. [SEP] [CLS] The plane was carrying 115 passengers and 9 crew members and was en route to Moscow when one of the engines suddenly burst into flames. [SEP] [CLS] The crew then tried to return to Irkutsk, but lost control of the plane and crashed into a dairy farm near the town of Mamony. [SEP] [CLS] All 124 people on board and one person on the ground were killed in the crash. [SEP] [CLS]
