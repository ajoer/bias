The 2000 Kipawa earthquake (or 2000 Kipawa "Millennium" earthquake ) struck Quebec and Ontario, Canada with a moment magnitude of 5.2 at 6:22 a.m. [CLS] [SEP] on January 1. [CLS] [SEP] It occurred in the Western Quebec Seismic Zone. [CLS] [SEP] The main shock epicenter was located in Lake Kipawa about 10 km (6 mi) north of Témiscaming in southwestern Quebec and 70 km (43 mi) northeast of North Bay, Ontario. [CLS] [SEP] The shaking was strongest within 50 km (31 mi) of the epicenter. [CLS] [SEP] It was felt in Témiscaming, North Bay and as far away as Toronto, making it one of the most significant earthquakes in Canada in 2000. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was triggered by major thrust faults associated with the Ottawa-Bonnechere Graben.Minor damage was reported during this earthquake, including fallen light objects, a damaged ventilation pipe and fractures in plaster. [CLS] [SEP] Its epicenter was very close to that of the 1935 Timiskaming earthquake and lies in a group of 76 located earthquakes since 1935. [CLS] [SEP] Seventeen aftershocks were recorded.
