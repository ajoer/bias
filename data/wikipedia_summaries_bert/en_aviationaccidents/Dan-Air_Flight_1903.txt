[CLS] Dan-Air Flight 1903 was a de Havilland Comet 4 aircraft operated by Dan Air Services Limited that, on Friday, 3 July 1970, crashed into the wooded slopes of the Serralada del Montseny near Arbúcies (Girona), Catalonia, Spain. [SEP] [CLS] The flight was on a non-scheduled international passenger service from Manchester to Barcelona. [SEP] [CLS] British tour operator Clarksons Holidays had contracted the aircraft to carry a group of holidaymakers who had booked an all-inclusive package holiday with it. [SEP] [CLS] The crash resulted in the aircraft's destruction and the deaths of all 112 on board. [SEP] [CLS] It was the deadliest aviation accident in 1970, and remains the highest death toll of any accident or incident involving the De Havilland Comet anywhere in the world.The crash was Dan-Air's first fatal accident killing fare-paying passengers. [SEP] [CLS] News of the first major accident in the company's eighteenth year of existence came just two days after British tour operator Global Holidays had awarded it a four-year, £2.5 million contract for all Global charter flights from Birmingham, starting in April 1971.
