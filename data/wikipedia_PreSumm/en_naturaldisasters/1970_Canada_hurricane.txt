[CLS] The 1970 Canada hurricane was an unnamed tropical cyclone that brought impact to Bermuda and Newfoundland. [SEP] [CLS] The fourth hurricane and ninth tropical storm of the annual hurricane season, this system developed northeast of the Bahamas as a subtropical depression on October 12. [SEP] [CLS] While tracking northeastward, the system intensified, becoming a subtropical storm on the following day. [SEP] [CLS] The subtropical storm transitioned into a tropical cyclone on October 16, and strengthened into a hurricane about twelve hours later. [SEP] [CLS] It transitioned into an extratropical cyclone early on October 17. [SEP] [CLS] The system produced tropical storm force winds on Bermuda, which caused the suspension of schools, transportation, and interrupted businesses, although minimal structural damage occurred. [SEP] [CLS] Light rainfall was also reported on the island. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane force winds were observed throughout Newfoundland, which caused damage to structures, though mostly limited to broken windows. [SEP] [CLS] Rough seas damaged fishing dories and a fishing ramp on the Atlantic coast of the island. [SEP] [CLS] The cost of damage on the Burin Peninsula was estimated to be in the thousands of dollars, although the specific figure in unknown. [SEP] [CLS] Heavy rainfall was also reported in the region, with precipitation in Quebec reaching nearly 5 inches (130 mm). [SEP] [CLS] On the French Territory of Saint Pierre and Miquelon, several buildings and houses lost their roof due to strong winds. [SEP] [CLS]
