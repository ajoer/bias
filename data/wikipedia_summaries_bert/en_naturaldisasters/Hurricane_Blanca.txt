The name Blanca has been used for ten tropical cyclones in the Eastern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Blanca (1966) never affected land and set a record for the farthest traveling storm that formed in the Eastern Pacific by traveling 4,300 miles. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (1970) dissipated after 3 days and did not make landfall. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (1974) did not make landfall. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (1979) did not make landfall. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Blanca (1985) did not affect any land. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (1991) did not cause any casualties or damages. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (1997) did not cause any major damage or casualties. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (2003) did not have any effects on land. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Blanca (2009) did not make landfall, but contributed to flooding in Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Blanca (2015) was a record-breaking, two time Category 4 hurricane which made landfall in the Baja California Peninsula as a tropical storm, being the earliest storm on record to do so.
