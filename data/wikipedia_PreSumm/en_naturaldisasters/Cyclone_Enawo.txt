[CLS] Intense Tropical Cyclone Enawo was the strongest tropical cyclone to strike Madagascar since Gafilo in 2004, which killed at least 81 people of the country in March 2017. [SEP] [CLS] Forming as a moderate tropical storm on 3 March, Enawo initially drifted and intensified slowly. [SEP] [CLS] It strengthened into a tropical cyclone on 5 March and further an intense tropical cyclone on 6 March. [SEP] [CLS] Enawo made landfall over Sava Region on 7 March just after reaching peak intensity, and it emerged back into the Indian Ocean as a post-tropical depression late on 9 March, before dissipating two days later. [SEP] [CLS] Fifteen municipalities (out of 31) were severely affected, in the two most impacted districts of Antalaha and Maroantsetra. [SEP] [CLS]
