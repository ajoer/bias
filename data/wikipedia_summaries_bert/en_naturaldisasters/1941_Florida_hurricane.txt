The 1941 Florida hurricane was a compact but strong tropical cyclone that affected the Bahamas, Florida, and the southeastern United States in October 1941. [CLS] [SEP] The fifth known storm of the 1941 Atlantic hurricane season, it was first observed to the north of the Virgin Islands on October 3. [CLS] [SEP] The storm tracked generally westward, reaching peak winds of 120 miles per hour (193 km/h) before passing through the Bahamas. [CLS] [SEP] After weakening somewhat, the storm later passed across southern Florida with winds of 100 mph (161 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane then emerged into the Gulf of Mexico as a tropical storm, but regained hurricane intensity and made another landfall along the Florida Panhandle. [CLS] [SEP] Turning northeast, it crossed Georgia and South Carolina, and entered the Atlantic Ocean on October 8. [CLS] [SEP] In advance of the storm, preparations were extensive; residents boarded up homes and businesses, while evacuations were recommended in some coastal areas. [CLS] [SEP] In the Bahamas, where winds reached 104 mph (167 km/h), the storm killed three people. [CLS] [SEP] The city of Nassau was struck particularly hard. [CLS] [SEP] In Florida, damage was relatively severe, and included the deaths of several people. [CLS] [SEP] High winds brought down trees and power lines, though the storm was characterized by highly unusual rainfall patterns. [CLS] [SEP] In the Everglades region, a storm surge flooded local streets. [CLS] [SEP] As the storm progressed northward, the city of Tallahassee suffered widespread power outages and damage to numerous vehicles. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the state, the hurricane inflicted US$675,000 ($11.7 million 2011 USD) in damage. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone later killed one person in Georgia. [CLS] [SEP]
