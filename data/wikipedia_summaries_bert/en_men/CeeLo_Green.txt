[CLS] Thomas DeCarlo Callaway (born 30 May 1975), better known by his stage name CeeLo Green (or Cee Lo Green), is an American singer-songwriter, rapper, record producer, actor, and businessman. [SEP] [CLS] Green is known for his work in soul music, including the hit singles "Crazy" and "Fuck You". [SEP] [CLS] Born in Atlanta, Georgia, Green came to initial prominence as a member of the Southern hip hop group Goodie Mob. [SEP] [CLS] He subsequently began a solo career, releasing two albums on Arista Records: Cee-Lo Green and His Perfect Imperfections (2002) and Cee-Lo Green... [SEP] [CLS] Is the Soul Machine (2004). [SEP] [CLS] Green's next project was Gnarls Barkley, a collaboration with DJ Danger Mouse. [SEP] [CLS] In 2006, the duo scored a worldwide hit with "Crazy", which reached the top five in twenty countries, including the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] and U.K. [SEP] [CLS] With Gnarls Barkley, Green released two albums: 2006's platinum-selling St. [SEP] [CLS] Elsewhere and 2008's The Odd Couple. [SEP] [CLS] In 2010, Green continued his solo career with the album The Lady Killer, which spawned his biggest solo hit, "Fuck You". [SEP] [CLS] The song peaked at number two on the Billboard Hot 100, and reached the top 10 in thirteen countries. [SEP] [CLS] Green has released three further solo albums, Cee Lo's Magic Moment (2012), Heart Blanche (2015), and CeeLo Green Is Thomas Callaway, as well as a reunion album with Goodie Mob, Age Against the Machine (2014). [SEP] [CLS] From 2011 to 2014, Green was a judge and coach on the American reality television singing competition The Voice, appearing on four of its seasons. [SEP] [CLS] He voiced Murray the Mummy in the 2012 animated feature film Hotel Transylvania, and also appeared in a few television programs and films including his own show, CeeLo Green's The Good Life, on TBS.
