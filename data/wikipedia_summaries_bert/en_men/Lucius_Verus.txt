[CLS] Lucius Aurelius Verus (15 December 130 – 23 January 169) was Roman emperor from 161 until his death in 169, alongside his adoptive brother Marcus Aurelius. [SEP] [CLS] He was a member of the Nerva-Antonine dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] Verus' succession together with Marcus Aurelius marked the first time that the Roman Empire was ruled by multiple emperors, an increasingly common occurrence in the later history of the Empire. [SEP] [CLS] The eldest son of Lucius Aelius Caesar, first adopted son and heir to Hadrian, Verus was born and educated in Rome where he held several political offices prior to taking the throne. [SEP] [CLS] After his biological father’s death in 138, he was adopted by Antoninus Pius, who was himself adopted by Hadrian. [SEP] [CLS] Hadrian died later that year, and Antoninus Pius succeeded to the throne. [SEP] [CLS] Antoninus Pius ruled until 161 and was succeeded by Lucius Verus and Marcus Aurelius. [SEP] [CLS] The majority of Verus’s reign was occupied by his direction of the war with Parthia which ended in Roman victory and some territorial gains. [SEP] [CLS] After initial involvement in the Marcomannic Wars, he fell ill and died in 169. [SEP] [CLS] He was deified by the Roman Senate as the Divine Verus (Divus Verus). [SEP] [CLS]
