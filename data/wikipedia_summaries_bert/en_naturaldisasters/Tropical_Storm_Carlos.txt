The 1997 Pacific hurricane season was a very active hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] With hundreds of deaths and hundreds of millions of dollars in damage, this season was one of the costliest and deadliest Pacific hurricane seasons. [CLS] [SEP] This was due to the exceptionally strong 1997–98 El Niño event. [CLS] [SEP] The 1997 Pacific hurricane season officially started on May 15, 1997, in the eastern Pacific, and on June 1, 1997, in the central Pacific, and lasted until November 30, 1997. [CLS] [SEP] These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when almost all tropical cyclones form in the northeastern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Several storms impacted land. [CLS] [SEP] The first was Tropical Storm Andres which killed four people and left another two missing. [CLS] [SEP] In August, Tropical Storm Ignacio took an unusual path, and its extratropical remnants caused minor damage in the Pacific Northwest and California. [CLS] [SEP] Linda became the most intense east Pacific hurricane in recorded history, a record it maintained until it was surpassed by Hurricane Patricia in 2015. [CLS] [SEP] Although it never made landfall, it produced large surf in Southern California and as a result five people had to be rescued. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Nora caused flooding and damage in the Southwestern United States, while Olaf made two landfalls and caused eighteen deaths and several other people were reported missing. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Pauline killed several hundred people and caused record damage in southeastern Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] In addition, Super Typhoons Oliwa and Paka originated in the region before crossing the International Date Line and causing significant damage in the western Pacific. [CLS] [SEP] There were also two Category 5 hurricanes: Linda and Guillermo. [CLS] [SEP] Activity in the season was above average. [CLS] [SEP] The season produced 17 named storms, which was a little above normal. [CLS] [SEP] The average number of named storms per year is 15. [CLS] [SEP] The 1997 season also had 9 hurricanes, compared to the average of 8. [CLS] [SEP] There were also 7 major hurricanes compared to the average of 4. [CLS] [SEP]
