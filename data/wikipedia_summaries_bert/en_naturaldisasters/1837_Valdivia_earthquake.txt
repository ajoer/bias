The 1837 Valdivia earthquake struck south-central Chile on November 17. [CLS] [SEP] Together with earthquakes in 1575 and 1737 the earthquake is among the historical predecessors to the great 1960 earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] The rupture zone was roughly from Valdivia to the south. [CLS] [SEP] It was felt in the cities of Concepción, Valdivia and Ancud. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was also felt by the crew whaling ships in Guafo Island and Chonos Archipelago. [CLS] [SEP] Various landslides were triggered in Chiloé and people are reported to have been thrown to the ground in Valdivia. [CLS] [SEP] In contrast in Concepción the shakings were moderate. [CLS] [SEP] As reported in various coastal localities the ground rose as result of the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] Decades later while surveying southern Chile’s coasts Francisco Vidal Gormaz was told of islands that been submerged and some that had emerged as a consequence of the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused a tsunami that struck Hawaii, what is now French Polynesia and Japan.In Japan the tsunami flooded rice fields, destroyed salmon traps and broke into salt evaporation ponds causing significant economic losses.
