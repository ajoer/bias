"Summer Wind" is a 1965 song, originally released in Germany as "Der Sommerwind" and written by Heinz Meier and German language lyrics by Hans Bradtke. [CLS] [SEP] Johnny Mercer re-wrote the song into English along the same themes as the original, which talked of the changing of the seasons using the Southern European sirocco wind as a metaphor. [CLS] [SEP] In America, it was first recorded by Wayne Newton and subsequently by Bobby Vinton and Perry Como. [CLS] [SEP] The song is best known for a 1966 recording by Frank Sinatra which peaked at number 25 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and number one on the Easy Listening chart. [CLS] [SEP] The Sinatra version originally appeared on his album, Strangers in the Night. [CLS] [SEP] A variety of singers and bands have covered the song since, including Shirley Bassey, Fun Lovin' Criminals, Michael Bubl√© and James Dean Bradfield. [CLS] [SEP] Following Johnny Mercer's centennial in 2009, Scottish singer Todd Gordon recorded a version of the song that included original, addition lyrics penned by the songwriter. [CLS] [SEP] The track was released on the 2012 album, Helping the Heroes, with the Royal Air Force Squadronaires big band. [CLS] [SEP]
