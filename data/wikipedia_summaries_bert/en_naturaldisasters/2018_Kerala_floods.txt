On 16 August 2018, severe floods affected the south Indian state Kerala, due to unusually high rainfall during the monsoon season. [CLS] [SEP] It was the worst flood in Kerala in nearly a century. [CLS] [SEP] Over 483 people died, and 140 are missing. [CLS] [SEP] About a million people were evacuated, mainly from Chengannur, Pandanad, Edanad, Aranmula, Kozhencherry, Ayiroor, Ranni, Pandalam, Kuttanad, Malappuram, Aluva, Chalakudy, Thrissur, Thiruvalla, Eraviperoor, Vallamkulam, North Paravur, Chellanam, Vypin Island and Palakkad. [CLS] [SEP] All 14 districts of the state were placed on red alert. [CLS] [SEP] According to the Kerala government, one-sixth of the total population of Kerala had been directly affected by the floods and related incidents. [CLS] [SEP] The Indian government had declared it a Level 3 Calamity, or "calamity of a severe nature". [CLS] [SEP] It is the worst flood in Kerala after the great flood of 99 that took place in 1924. [CLS] [SEP] Thirty-five out of the fifty-four dams within the state were opened, for the first time in history. [CLS] [SEP] All five overflow gates of the Idukki Dam were opened at the same time, and for the first time in 26 years 5 gates of the Malampuzha dam of Palakkad were opened. [CLS] [SEP] Heavy rains in Wayanad and Idukki have caused severe landslides and have left the hilly districts isolated. [CLS] [SEP] The situation was regularly monitored by the National Crisis Management Committee, which also coordinated the rescue and relief operations. [CLS] [SEP]
