[CLS] The 1978 Thessaloniki earthquake (Greek: Μεγάλος Σεισμός της Θεσσαλονίκης) occurred on 20 June at 23:03 local time. [SEP] [CLS] The shock registered 6.5 on the moment magnitude scale, had a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe), and was felt throughout northern Greece, Yugoslavia and Bulgaria. [SEP] [CLS] It was the largest event in the area since the 1932 Ierissos earthquake.It was the first earthquake that hit a big city in Greece. [SEP] [CLS] It is estimated that 49 people died, 37 of which were living in the same block of flats in the city center that collapsed. [SEP] [CLS] More than 220 people were injured and many thousands were left homeless. [SEP] [CLS] 3170 buildings (4.5%) were found to have severe damages (red label), 13918 buildings (21.0%) had moderate damages (orange label), and 49071 buildings (74.5%) were found to have no damages (green label), as per the assessment of the Greek authorities. [SEP] [CLS] There have also been some recorded damages in archaeological monuments like the Arch of Galerius and Rotunda and the Church of the Acheiropoietos. [SEP] [CLS]
