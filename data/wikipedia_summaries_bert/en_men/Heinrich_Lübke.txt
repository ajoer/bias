[CLS] Karl Heinrich Lübke (German: [ˈhaɪnʁɪç ˈlʏpkə]; 14 October 1894 – 6 April 1972) was a German politician who was the second President of the Federal Republic of Germany (West Germany) from 1959 to 1969. [SEP] [CLS] Prior to his presidency he served as Federal Minister for Agriculture. [SEP] [CLS] Besides agriculture he was interested in the state of developing countries. [SEP] [CLS] The moderate conservative suffered from deteriorating health towards the end of his career, and is known for a series of embarrassing incidents that may have resulted from his health issues. [SEP] [CLS] Lübke resigned three months before the scheduled end of his second term. [SEP] [CLS]
