[CLS] A magnitude 5.7 earthquake struck India 20 km (12 mi) east north-east of Ambassa in the state of Tripura on 3 January 2017 with a maximum observed intensity of 6-7 EMS. [SEP] [CLS] It struck at 2:39 pm local time (09:09 UTC), and was centered in an isolated area. [SEP] [CLS] The estimated depth was 32.0 km.One person died and five others were injured in India. [SEP] [CLS] At least 50 houses were damaged due to landslides that occurred in Dhalai district, while roads were blocked after trees were uprooted. [SEP] [CLS] According to the Tripura State Disaster Management Authority, at least 6,727 buildings were damaged in Tripura in the districts of Dhalai and Unakoti. [SEP] [CLS] Shaking was felt in many parts of north-eastern India including as far as Kolkata. [SEP] [CLS] The tremor was also felt in neighboring Bangladesh, where two people died and three others were injured. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake caused liquefaction on the banks of the Manu river in Tripura and along the Dhalai river in adjacent parts of Bangladesh, in particular in the Kamalganj area. [SEP] [CLS]
