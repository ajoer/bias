[CLS] Lambert of Ardres (active 1194–1203) was a chronicler in the twelfth-century Kingdom of France, from on the frontiers of the County of Flanders.By 1194, Lambert was the parish priest of Ardres. [SEP] [CLS] He was related to the Counts of Guînes, for whom he wrote a Historia comitum Ghisnensium, begun around 1196 and left unfinished in 1203. [SEP] [CLS] It is a mixture of history and folklore. [SEP] [CLS] It also contains a contemporary description of a donjon. [SEP] [CLS] A chapter of Georges Duby's The Knight, The Lady, and the Priest is dedicated to Lambert's Historia.Lambert's Historia has been published in a number of editions: Godfrey de Ménilglaise (ed.), Chronique de Guines et d'Ardres par Lambert, curé d'Ardres (Paris, 1855).
