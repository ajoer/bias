Yelena Gadzhievna Isinbayeva (Russian: Елена Гаджиевна Исинбаева, IPA: [jɪˈlʲɛnə gɐˈdʐɨjɪvnə ɪsʲɪnˈbajɪvə]; born 3 June 1982) is a Russian former pole vaulter. [CLS] [SEP] She is a two-time Olympic gold medalist (2004 and 2008), a three-time World Champion (2005, 2007 and 2013), the current world record holder in the event, and is widely considered the greatest female pole-vaulter of all time. [CLS] [SEP] Isinbayeva was banned from the 2016 Rio Olympics after the appearance of an independent report about an extensive state-sponsored doping program in Russia, thus dashing her hopes of a grand retirement winning the Olympic gold medal. [CLS] [SEP] She retired from athletics in August 2016 after being elected to serve an 8-year term on the IOC's Athletes' Commission.Isinbayeva has been a major champion on nine occasions (Olympic, World outdoor and indoor champion and European outdoor and indoor champion). [CLS] [SEP] She was also the jackpot winner of the IAAF Golden League series in 2007 and 2009. [CLS] [SEP] After poor performances at the world championships in 2009 and 2010, she took a year-long break from the sport. [CLS] [SEP] She became the first woman to clear the five-metre barrier in 2005. [CLS] [SEP] Her current world record is 5.06 m outdoors, set in Zurich in August 2009. [CLS] [SEP] Her 5.01 m indoors was the world record for just over a year. [CLS] [SEP] The latter was Isinbayeva's twenty-eighth pole vault world record. [CLS] [SEP] On 2 March 2013, Jenn Suhr joined Isinbayeva as the only women who have cleared 5 metres. [CLS] [SEP] In the process, Suhr took Isinbayeva's indoor world record. [CLS] [SEP] Isinbayeva was named Female Athlete of the Year by the IAAF in 2004, 2005 and 2008, and World Sportswoman of the Year by Laureus in 2007 and 2009. [CLS] [SEP] In 2007 she entered in the FICTS "Hall of Fame" and was awarded with "Excellence Guirlande D'Honneur". [CLS] [SEP] She was given the Prince of Asturias Award for Sports in 2009. [CLS] [SEP] She is one of only nine athletes (along with Valerie Adams, Usain Bolt, Veronica Campbell-Brown, Jacques Freitag, Kirani James, Jana Pittman, Dani Samuels, and David Storl) to win world championships at the youth, junior, and senior level of an athletic event.
