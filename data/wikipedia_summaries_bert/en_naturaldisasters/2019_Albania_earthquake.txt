Northwestern Albania was struck by a strong 6.4-magnitude earthquake with an epicentre 16 kilometres (9.9 mi) west-southwest of Mamurras, at 03:54 CET (UTC+1) on 26 November 2019. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake lasted at least 50 seconds and was felt in Albania's capital Tirana, and in places as far away as Taranto and Belgrade, 370 kilometres (230 mi) northeast of the epicentre. [CLS] [SEP] The maximum felt intensity was VIII (Severe) on the Modified Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 51 people were killed in the earthquake, with about 3,000 injured. [CLS] [SEP] It was the second earthquake to strike the region in the space of three months. [CLS] [SEP] It was the strongest earthquake to hit Albania in more than 40 years, its deadliest earthquake in 99 years and the world's deadliest earthquake in 2019. [CLS] [SEP]
