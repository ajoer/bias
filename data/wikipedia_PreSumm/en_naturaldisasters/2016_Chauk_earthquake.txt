[CLS] A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Myanmar 25 km (16 mi) west of Chauk on 24 August 2016 with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VI (Strong). [SEP] [CLS] It struck at 5:04 pm local time (10:34 UTC), and was centered in an isolated area. [SEP] [CLS] The estimated depth was 84.1 km. [SEP] [CLS] Tremors from the earthquake were felt in Yangon, in the eastern cities of Patna, Guwahati, and Kolkata in India, in Bangkok in Thailand and in Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh. [SEP] [CLS] According to reports, several temples in the nearby ancient city of Bagan were damaged and four people were reported dead. [SEP] [CLS]
