[CLS] The 2010 Elazığ earthquake was a 6.1 Mw earthquake that occurred on 8 March 2010 at 02:32 UTC (04:32 local time). [SEP] [CLS] The epicentre was Başyurt in Elazığ Province, in eastern Turkey. [SEP] [CLS] Initial reports in global media said as many as 57 people had died. [SEP] [CLS] By 10 March, reports in the Turkish media placed the death toll at 41 and later, the death toll rose to 42. [SEP] [CLS] Another 74 were injured, many after falling and jumping from buildings. [SEP] [CLS] A stampede through the streets led to further injuries.The earthquake came one week to the day after the Turkish Chamber of Civil Engineers sent a report to parliament detailing inadequate building projects and the possibility that Istanbul would be destroyed by an earthquake, which could kill tens of thousands of people, at some point in the next three decades.
