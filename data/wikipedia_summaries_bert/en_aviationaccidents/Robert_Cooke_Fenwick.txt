Robert Cooke Fenwick (6 October 1882—13 August 1912) was a British aircraft pioneer and designer. [CLS] [SEP] Fenwick was educated at South Shields High School between 1893-1895, leaving the school to go to school in Jersey. [CLS] [SEP] After school, he became an aircraft designer. [CLS] [SEP] He worked at Planes Limited and worked on the Handley Page Type B, later redesigning it into the Planes Limited Biplane. [CLS] [SEP] He is known for designing and building the Mersey Monoplane, which competed in trials at the British Military Aeroplane Competition, on Salisbury Plain in 1912. [CLS] [SEP] He was granted his aviator’s certificate, No. [CLS] [SEP] 35, on 29 November 1910. [CLS] [SEP] On 13 August 1912, Fenwick lost control of his Mersey monoplane and it crashed, killing him in the process. [CLS] [SEP]
