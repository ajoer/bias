On April 26, 1991, a large tornado outbreak struck the central United States. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak produced an F5 tornado that heavily damaged the town of Andover, Kansas, as well as numerous less destructive tornadoes throughout the region. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak killed 21 people, 17 of which were from the Andover tornado. [CLS] [SEP] A news team filming a tornado in the outbreak sought shelter under a highway overpass, causing a misconception that overpasses can provide adequate shelter during a tornado. [CLS] [SEP]
