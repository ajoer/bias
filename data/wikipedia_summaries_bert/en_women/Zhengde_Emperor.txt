The Zhengde Emperor (Chinese: 正德帝; pinyin: Zhèngdé Dì; 27 October 1491 – 20 April 1521) was the 11th Emperor of the Ming dynasty, reigned from 1505 to 1521. [CLS] [SEP] Born Zhu Houzhao, he was the Hongzhi Emperor's eldest son. [CLS] [SEP] Zhu Houzhao took the throne at only 14 with the era name Zhengde meaning "Right virtue" or "Rectification of virtue". [CLS] [SEP] He was known for favoring eunuchs such as Liu Jin and became infamous for his childlike behavior. [CLS] [SEP] He eventually died at age 29 from an illness he contracted after drunkenly falling off a boat into the Yellow River. [CLS] [SEP] He left behind no sons and was succeeded by his first cousin Zhu Houcong. [CLS] [SEP]
