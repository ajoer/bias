[CLS] Pertinax (; Publius Helvius Pertinax; 1 August 126 – 28 March 193) was a Roman soldier and politician who ruled as Roman emperor for the first three months of 193. [SEP] [CLS] He succeeded Commodus to become the first emperor during the tumultuous Year of the Five Emperors. [SEP] [CLS] Born the son of a freed slave, Pertinax became an officer in the army. [SEP] [CLS] He fought in the Roman–Parthian War of 161–166, where his success led him to be promoted to higher-ranking positions in both the military and political spheres. [SEP] [CLS] He achieved the rank of provincial governor and urban prefect. [SEP] [CLS] He was a member of the Roman Senate, serving at the same time as the historian Cassius Dio. [SEP] [CLS] Following the death of Commodus, Pertinax was acclaimed emperor. [SEP] [CLS] He attempted to institute several reform measures, although the short length of his time as emperor prevented the success of those attempts. [SEP] [CLS] One of those reforms, the restoration of discipline among the Praetorian Guard, led to conflict that eventually culminated in Pertinax's assassination by the Guard. [SEP] [CLS] Pertinax would be deified by the emperor Septimius Severus. [SEP] [CLS] His historical reputation has largely been a positive one, in line with Cassius Dio's assessment. [SEP] [CLS]
