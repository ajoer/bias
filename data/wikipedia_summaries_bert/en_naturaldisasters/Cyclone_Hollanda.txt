[CLS] Cyclone Hollanda was the worst tropical cyclone in Mauritius in 19 years. [SEP] [CLS] It formed on February 6, 1994, in the central Indian Ocean in the southern hemisphere. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone moved southwestward for much of its duration, striking Mauritius on February 10 at peak intensity with winds of 218 km/h (135 mph). [SEP] [CLS] It later passed just southeast of Réunion before turning to the south and weakening. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone became extratropical on February 14 in the southern Indian Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] On Mauritius, Hollanda destroyed or severely damaged 450 houses, which left at least 1,500 people homeless. [SEP] [CLS] High winds left half of the island without power, and also caused severe crop damage; nearly half of the island's sugar crop was destroyed, which necessitated for the government to assist in replanting efforts. [SEP] [CLS] Hollanda killed 2 people and caused $135 million in damage on Mauritius. [SEP] [CLS] About a week after the cyclone struck, Cyclone Ivy also affected the area, although to a lesser degree. [SEP] [CLS] The highest rainfall from the cyclone fell on Réunion, with 741 mm (29.2 in) recorded at Grand Coude. [SEP] [CLS]
