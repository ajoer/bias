[CLS] Severe Tropical Cyclone Fran was the third tropical cyclone within four weeks to impact Vanuatu in 1992. [SEP] [CLS] Fran formed on March 4 and then gradually intensified. [SEP] [CLS] Winds reached gale-force on March 5, and hurricane-force a few days later. [SEP] [CLS] Cyclone Fran reached the powerful Category 5 equivalent on the Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale near Vanuatu. [SEP] [CLS] The storm weakened somewhat due to land interaction, but briefly re-intensified after moving away from land. [SEP] [CLS] Fran gradually weakened over the next several days over less favorable conditions. [SEP] [CLS] Meanwhile, the storm passed north of New Caledonia. [SEP] [CLS] Eventually, as a Category 2 system on the Australian scale, Fran made landfall on Queensland during March 16. [SEP] [CLS] Severe Tropical Cyclone Fran turned towards the southeast and eventually headed back out to sea. [SEP] [CLS] The system dissipated the next day. [SEP] [CLS] On Efate, over 130 houses lost roofs. [SEP] [CLS] Along Queensland, two rivers sustained major flooding, but no deaths were attributed to this cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Total damage from the system was AU$8–10 million (1992 AUD). [SEP] [CLS] Moderate damage was also reported in Fiji. [SEP] [CLS] In New Caledonia, the storm brought flooding and landslides. [SEP] [CLS]
