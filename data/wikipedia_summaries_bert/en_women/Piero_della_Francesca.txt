Piero della Francesca (, also US: , Italian: [ˈpjɛːro della franˈtʃeska] (listen); c. [CLS] [SEP] 1415 – 12 October 1492), originally named Piero di Benedetto, was an Italian painter of the Early Renaissance. [CLS] [SEP] To contemporaries he was also known as a mathematician and geometer. [CLS] [SEP] Nowadays Piero della Francesca is chiefly appreciated for his art. [CLS] [SEP] His painting is characterized by its serene humanism, its use of geometric forms and perspective. [CLS] [SEP] His most famous work is the cycle of frescoes The History of the True Cross in the church of San Francesco in the Tuscan town of Arezzo. [CLS] [SEP]
