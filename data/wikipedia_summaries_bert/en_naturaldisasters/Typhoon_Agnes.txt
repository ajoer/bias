Typhoon Agnes, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Undang, was the final tropical cyclone to affect the Philippines during the 1984 Pacific typhoon season. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather developed near the equator and the Caroline Islands on October 28. [CLS] [SEP] Following an increase in organization, the cyclone was designated a tropical storm on October 31. [CLS] [SEP] After tracking northwest initially, Agnes turned west-northwest the next day, a course that the system would maintain for the rest of its lifetime. [CLS] [SEP] Agnes intensified into a typhoon on November 2, and on November 4, attained peak intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The storm then made landfall on Samar Island, part of the Philippines, at that intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The storm weakened over land, but re-intensified over the South China Sea; Agnes obtained a secondary peak intensity on November 6. [CLS] [SEP] The next day, Typhoon Agnes moved ashore Vietnam while still at typhoon intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The storm rapidly deteriorated over land and dissipated by November 8 over Thailand. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Agnes was the final of a series of tropical cyclones to affect the Philippines that season, following June, Ike, and Warren. [CLS] [SEP] Agnes brought widespread damage to several provinces across the central section of the archipelago. [CLS] [SEP] In the Iloilo province, 230 people were killed and four others were injured. [CLS] [SEP] Provincewide, a total of 21 homes were flattened, resulting in 1,326 homeless people. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in the province amounted to at least $5 million. [CLS] [SEP] Approximately 20% of the rice crop was destroyed in Iloilo. [CLS] [SEP] In the Capiz province, 265 people died and property damage amounted $25 million (1984 USD). [CLS] [SEP] Around 80% of houses in Roxas, located in Capiz, were damaged and 100 people were killed. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout Panay Island, four hundred forty-eight fatalities were reported, at least eight people were injured, five bridges were destroyed, and thirty-five schools were demolished. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 4,014 homes were destroyed; consequently, over 24,000 people lost their homes. [CLS] [SEP] Across the Philippines, 895 people lost their lives due to Agnes, with 272 people listed missing and 2,526 others wounded. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 491,968 people were evacuated to shelter in 27 provinces. [CLS] [SEP] More than 200,000 homes were destroyed while 163,056 other dwellings were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in the country totaled $90.7 million, with $19.1 million from crops and $56.9 million from public infrastructure. [CLS] [SEP] In Vietnam, the typhoon severely affected the rice harvest and winter crop cultivation. [CLS] [SEP] The Nam Định Province suffered the brunt of the typhoon, where the 134 people died and 289 sustained injuries. [CLS] [SEP] There, 400 schools and 230 nurseries were flattened. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the country, the storm destroyed 271,000 ha (669,655 acres) of farmland. [CLS] [SEP]
