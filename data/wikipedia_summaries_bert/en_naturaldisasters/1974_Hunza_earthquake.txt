The 1974 Hunza earthquake occurred in the rugged and isolated Hunza, Hazara and Swat districts of northern Pakistan at 12:11 UTC [17:11 PST] on 28 December. [CLS] [SEP] The epicentre was located at 35.0 degrees north and 72.8 degrees east. [CLS] [SEP] The 6.2 magnitude quake had a shallow focal depth and was followed by numerous aftershocks. [CLS] [SEP] An official estimate of the number killed was 5,300 with approximately 17,000 injured. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 97,000 were reported affected by the tremor. [CLS] [SEP] Most of the destruction was centered on the village of Pattan, located about 100 miles (160 km) north of the capital city of Islamabad. [CLS] [SEP] The village was almost completely destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] The epicentral region is characterized by steep-walled narrow canyons and valleys. [CLS] [SEP] Most of the population was concentrated along the rivers. [CLS] [SEP] Much of the destruction was caused by the numerous landslides and rockfalls which came tumbling down from high above. [CLS] [SEP] The main road leading into the area was blocked for about 25 miles (40 km) by landslides and rockfalls, hampering relief efforts. [CLS] [SEP] The government flew in emergency supplies by helicopter until the roads were reopened on 13 January. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake, which reached MMI V in Kabul, Afghanistan, affected some 1,000 square miles (2,600 km2) of the Indus Valley region. [CLS] [SEP] Several nations contributed money and supplies to aid the inhabitants of the stricken area. [CLS] [SEP]
