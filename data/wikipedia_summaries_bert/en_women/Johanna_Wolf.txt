Johanna Wolf (1 June 1900 – 5 June 1985) was Adolf Hitler's chief secretary. [CLS] [SEP] Wolf joined Hitler's personal secretariat in the autumn of 1929 as a typist, at which time she also became a member of the Nazi Party. [CLS] [SEP] Wolf went on to serve as Hitler's chief secretary until the night of 21–22 April 1945, when she was ordered to fly out of Berlin to safety. [CLS] [SEP] She died on 5 June 1985.
