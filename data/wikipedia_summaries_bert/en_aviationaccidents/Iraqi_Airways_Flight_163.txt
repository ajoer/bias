Iraqi Airways Flight 163 was a Boeing 737-270C, registered YI-AGJ, that was hijacked in 1986. [CLS] [SEP] On 25 December 1986, en route from Baghdad's Saddam International Airport to Amman, Jordan, Flight 163 was hijacked by four men. [CLS] [SEP] Iraqi Airways security personnel tried to stop the hijackers, but a hand grenade was detonated in the passenger cabin, forcing the crew to initiate an emergency descent. [CLS] [SEP] Another hand grenade exploded in the cockpit, causing the aircraft to crash near Arar, Saudi Arabia where it broke in two and caught fire. [CLS] [SEP] There were 106 people on board, and 60 passengers and 3 crew members died. [CLS] [SEP] The surviving passengers were able to tell authorities what transpired on the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] The hijacking was one of the deadliest ever, and was one of many in 1985 and 1986. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly after the hijacking, the pro-Iranian group Islamic Jihad Organization (a widely used name for Hezbollah) claimed responsibility. [CLS] [SEP] One of the dead hijackers was later identified by the Central Intelligence Agency as a Lebanese national named Ribal Khalil Jallul, whose passport photo was matched to a Hezbollah martyr poster found near a mosque in Beirut. [CLS] [SEP] Iraq accused Iran of being behind the attack. [CLS] [SEP]
