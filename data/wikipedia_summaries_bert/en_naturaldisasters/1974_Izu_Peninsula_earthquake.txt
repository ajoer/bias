The 1974 Izu Peninsula earthquake (Japanese: 1974年伊豆半島沖地震 Sen-kyūhyaku-nanajūyo-nen Izu-hantō-oki Jishin) occurred on May 9 at 08:33 local time. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located off the Izu Peninsula, Japan. [CLS] [SEP] 25 people were reported dead. [CLS] [SEP] Landslides and damage of roads, buildings, and infrastructure were reported. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake triggered a small tsunami. [CLS] [SEP] The intensity in Tokyo reached shindo 3. [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude of this earthquake was put at Ms 6.5, or MJMA 6.9. [CLS] [SEP]
