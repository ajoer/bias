Elke Winkens, née Fischer (born 25 March 1970 in Linnich) is an Austrian-German actress. [CLS] [SEP] She grew up in Germany in the district Ratheim in Hückelhoven. [CLS] [SEP] Because of her father's work the family moved to the Netherlands and Belgium; for some time she also resided in Africa. [CLS] [SEP] At the age of six she took ballet classes and at the age of nine she started performing on stage. [CLS] [SEP] Afterwards she performed in many dance and theater acts. [CLS] [SEP] From 1978 to 1986 she was a professional gymnast and won several titles in floor exercise.At the age of eighteen she got a scholarship at the London Studio Center School in London, where she was stationed between 1989 and 1991. [CLS] [SEP] Then she moved to Vienna and participated in the Musical School at the Theater Vienna where she studied song, dance and acting. [CLS] [SEP] Later she joined the cabaret performance group Die Hektiker, this is where Winkens got her first attention from media. [CLS] [SEP] She got her first TV acting job in the series Cell-O-Fun which was broadcast between 1996 and 1997, and then the series called One in 1998. [CLS] [SEP] In 2002, she acted in the crime series Kommisar Rex where she played the character Niki Herzog. [CLS] [SEP] In 2003, Winkens posed for the German edition of Playboy magazine. [CLS] [SEP] And in 2008, she participated in the celebrity dancing show Dancing Stars which was broadcast on ORF. [CLS] [SEP] She was voted off just one week from the final. [CLS] [SEP]
