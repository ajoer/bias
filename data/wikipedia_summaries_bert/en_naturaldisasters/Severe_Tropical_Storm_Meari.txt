[CLS] The 2011 Pacific typhoon season was a slightly below average season that produced a total of 21 named storms, 8 typhoons, and four super typhoons. [SEP] [CLS] This season was much more active than the previous season, although both seasons were below the Pacific typhoon average of 26. [SEP] [CLS] The season ran throughout 2011, though most tropical cyclone tend to develop between May and October. [SEP] [CLS] The season's first named storm, Aere, developed on May 7 while the season's last named storm, Washi dissipated on December 19. [SEP] [CLS] The season was also much deadlier and destructive than the previous season. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Muifa affected many countries during August. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Talas and Typhoon Roke made landfall over in Japan and were the most destructive since 2009. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Nesat was the most powerful to strike China since 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Washi, a late but weak cyclone, affected southern Philippines and killed 2546 people. [SEP] [CLS] Within the Northwestern Pacific Ocean, there are two separate agencies who assign names to tropical cyclones which can often result in a cyclone having two names. [SEP] [CLS] The Japan Meteorological Agency will name a tropical cyclone should it be judged to have 10-minute sustained wind speeds of at least 65 km/h, (40 mph) anywhere in the basin.
