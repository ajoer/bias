A historic snowstorm struck the Ohio Valley of the United States, as well as Ontario in Canada, on December 22 and December 23 and is not the same storm that led to snow in Texas on Christmas Eve. [CLS] [SEP] It lasted roughly 30 hours, and brought snowfall amounts up to 29 inches (74 cm) to portions of the Midwestern United States. [CLS] [SEP] Damages from the storm totaled US$900 million (2004 dollars). [CLS] [SEP] A total of 18 died during the storm, one from Canada, mainly due to car accidents. [CLS] [SEP]
