On 15 July 1960, at 09:04, Ethiopian Air Lines Flight 372, a C-47 Skytrain registered as ET-T-18, took off from Bulki, Ethiopia, on a short-haul flight to Aba Segud Airport, Jimma, Ethiopia. [CLS] [SEP] There were eight passengers, three crew and a cargo of coffee on board. [CLS] [SEP] At 09:40, the pilot requested the activation of the Jimma non-directional beacon (NDB) to assist his navigation. [CLS] [SEP] There was no further contact with the flight. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was found to have crashed at 9,400 feet into the side of a mountain 27.5 km (17.1 mi) south of Jimma, killing one of the pilots and leaving the passengers and remaining crew injured. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was damaged beyond repair. [CLS] [SEP]
