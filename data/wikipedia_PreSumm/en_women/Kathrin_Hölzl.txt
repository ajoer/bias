[CLS] Kathrin Hölzl (born 18 July 1984) is a retired World Cup alpine ski racer from Germany. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Berchtesgaden, Bavaria, she was the gold medalist in the giant slalom at the 2009 World Championships. [SEP] [CLS] Hölzl made her World Cup debut in December 2001 in Val d'Isère, France. [SEP] [CLS] Following her world championship in February 2009, she scored her first World Cup victory in a giant slalom at Aspen in November, followed by another win in December at Lienz. [SEP] [CLS] She made two additional podiums and won the World Cup giant slalom title for the 2010 season. [SEP] [CLS] In October 2013 Hölzl announced her retirement from the sport after struggling with injuries and illness for several years, having last competed in a World Cup race in December 2011.
