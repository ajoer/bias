Extremely Severe Cyclonic Storm Hudhud was a strong tropical cyclone that caused extensive damage and loss of life in eastern India and Nepal during October 2014. [CLS] [SEP] Hudhud originated from a low pressure system that formed under the influence of an upper-air cyclonic circulation in the Andaman Sea on October 6. [CLS] [SEP] Hudhud intensified into a cyclonic storm on October 8 and as a Severe Cyclonic Storm on October 9. [CLS] [SEP] Hudhud underwent rapid deepening in the following days and was classified as a Very Severe Cyclonic Storm by the IMD. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly before landfall near Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, on October 12, Hudhud reached its peak strength with three-minute wind speeds of 185 km/h (115 mph) and a minimum central pressure of 960 mbar (28.35 inHg). [CLS] [SEP] The system then drifted northwards towards Uttar Pradesh and Nepal, causing widespread rains in both areas and heavy snowfall in the latter. [CLS] [SEP] Hudhud caused extensive damage to the city of Visakhapatnam and the neighbouring districts of Vizianagaram and Srikakulam of Andhra Pradesh.
