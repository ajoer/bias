[CLS] [SEP] Born poor in 1886 in Sichuan, he was adopted by a wealthy uncle at age nine; this prosperity provided him a superior early education that led to his admission into a military academy. [CLS] [SEP] After his time at the academy, he joined a rebel army and soon became a warlord. [CLS] [SEP] It was after this period that he adopted communism. [CLS] [SEP] He ascended through the ranks of the Chinese Red Army as it closed in on securing the nation. [CLS] [SEP] By the time China was under Mao's control, Zhu was a high-ranking official within the Communist Party of China. [CLS] [SEP] He served as Commander-in-Chief of the Eighth Route Army during the Second Sino-Japanese War. [CLS] [SEP] In 1955 he became one of the Ten Marshals of the People's Liberation Army, of which he is regarded as the principal founder. [CLS] [SEP] Zhu remained a prominent political figure until his death in 1976. [CLS] [SEP] As the chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress from 1975 to 1976, Zhu was the head of state of the People's Republic of China. [CLS] [SEP]
