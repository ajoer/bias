[CLS] Marlon Francis King (born 26 April 1980) is a former professional footballer who played as a striker. [SEP] [CLS] Born and raised in south London, he started his career in non-League football with Dulwich Hamlet. [SEP] [CLS] He played for Barnet, Gillingham, Leeds United and Nottingham Forest before moving to Watford in 2005. [SEP] [CLS] At Watford he was the Football League Championship top scorer, and was voted Watford player of the season as the club earned promotion to the Premier League. [SEP] [CLS] King joined Wigan Athletic in 2008, and subsequently spent loan spells at Hull City and Middlesbrough. [SEP] [CLS] He joined Coventry City in September 2010, following his release from prison, and made 29 appearances for the club before controversially switching to Birmingham City in June 2011.
