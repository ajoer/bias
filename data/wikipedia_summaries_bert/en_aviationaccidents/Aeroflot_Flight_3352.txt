[CLS] Aeroflot Flight 3352 was a Tupolev Tu-154 airline flight on a domestic route from Krasnodar to Novosibirsk, with an intermediate landing in Omsk. [SEP] [CLS] While landing at Omsk Airport on Thursday, 11 October 1984, the aircraft crashed into maintenance vehicles on the runway, killing 174 people on board and 4 on the ground. [SEP] [CLS] While a chain of mistakes in airport operations contributed to the accident, its major cause was an air traffic controller falling asleep on duty. [SEP] [CLS] As of 2020, this remains the deadliest aviation accident on Russian territory. [SEP] [CLS] It was also the deadliest aviation accident involving a Tupolev Tu-154 at the time until the crash of Aeroflot Flight 7425 9 months later; as of 2020, it still ranks the second-deadliest accident involving a Tupolev Tu-154.
