[CLS] Gisela Jacob (née Gisela Arendt; 5 November 1918 – 18 February 1969) was a German swimmer who won three medals at the 1934 European Aquatics Championships and two medals at the 1936 Summer Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] She also competed at the 1952 Olympics and finished seventh in the 4 × 100 m freestyle relay.She won eight national titles in the 100 m freestyle (1933–1937, 1939, 1949) and 100 m backstroke (1934), competing as Arendt before World War II and as Jacob after the war.Her brother, Heinz Arendt, and son, Rainer Jacob, (b. [SEP] [CLS] 1946) were both Olympics swimmers, and Heinz competed alongside Gisela at the 1936 Olympics. [SEP] [CLS]
