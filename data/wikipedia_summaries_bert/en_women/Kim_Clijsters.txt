Kim Antonie Lode Clijsters (Dutch pronunciation: [kɪm ˈklɛistərs] (listen); born 8 June 1983) is a Belgian professional tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] Clijsters has been a world No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 in both singles and doubles, having held both rankings simultaneously in 2003. [CLS] [SEP] She has won a total of six Grand Slam tournament titles, four in singles and two in doubles. [CLS] [SEP] Clijsters has competed professionally from 1997 in an era in which her primary rivals were compatriot Justine Henin and 23-time Grand Slam singles champion Serena Williams. [CLS] [SEP] Coming from a country with limited success in men's or women's tennis, Clijsters became the first Belgian player to attain the No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 ranking. [CLS] [SEP] Together with Henin, she established Belgium as a leading force in women's tennis as the two of them led their country to their first Fed Cup crown in 2001 and were the top two players in the world in late 2003. [CLS] [SEP] Individually, Clijsters has won 41 singles titles and 11 doubles titles on the Women's Tennis Association (WTA) Tour. [CLS] [SEP] She was a three-time winner of the WTA Tour Championships. [CLS] [SEP] Between singles and doubles, she has been a champion at all four Grand Slam tournaments, winning the US Open and the Australian Open in singles and Wimbledon and the French Open in doubles with Ai Sugiyama. [CLS] [SEP] Her success at majors has been highlighted by winning three consecutive appearances at the US Open. [CLS] [SEP] Plagued by injuries and having lost some of her desire to compete, Clijsters initially retired from tennis in 2007 at the age of 23 to get married and have a daughter. [CLS] [SEP] She returned to the sport two years later and won her second US Open title as an unranked player in just her third tournament back. [CLS] [SEP] She defended her title the following year and then won the Australian Open in 2011 en route to becoming the first mother to be ranked No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 by the WTA. [CLS] [SEP] Along with Margaret Court, she also holds the record for most Grand Slam singles titles won as a mother, with three such titles, and was the first to win one since Evonne Goolagong Cawley in 1980. [CLS] [SEP] Clijsters retired again following the 2012 US Open. [CLS] [SEP] In 2020, after seven years of retirement from professional tennis, Clijsters began her second comeback at the Dubai Tennis Championships. [CLS] [SEP] Clijsters was born to athletic parents with backgrounds in professional football and gymnastics. [CLS] [SEP] She was renowned for her athleticism, which was highlighted by her ability to perform splits on court in the middle of points. [CLS] [SEP] She built the offensive side of her game around controlled aggression while also using her exceptional movement to become an elite defensive player. [CLS] [SEP] Clijsters was very popular and well-liked as a player, having won the Karen Krantzcke Sportsmanship Award eight times. [CLS] [SEP] She was inducted into the International Tennis Hall of Fame in 2017. [CLS] [SEP]
