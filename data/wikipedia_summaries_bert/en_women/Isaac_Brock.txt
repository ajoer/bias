Major-General Sir Isaac Brock KB (6 October 1769 â€“ 13 October 1812) was a British Army officer and colonial administrator from Guernsey. [CLS] [SEP] Brock was assigned to Lower Canada in 1802. [CLS] [SEP] Despite facing desertions and near-mutinies, he commanded his regiment in Upper Canada (part of present-day Ontario) successfully for many years. [CLS] [SEP] He was promoted to major general, and became responsible for defending Upper Canada against the United States. [CLS] [SEP] While many in Canada and Britain believed war could be averted, Brock began to ready the army and militia for what was to come. [CLS] [SEP] When the War of 1812 broke out, the populace was prepared, and quick victories at Fort Mackinac and Detroit defeated American invasion efforts. [CLS] [SEP] Brock's actions, particularly his success at Detroit, earned him accolades including a knighthood in the Order of the Bath and the sobriquet "The Hero of Upper Canada". [CLS] [SEP] His name is often linked with that of the Native American leader Tecumseh, although the two men collaborated in person only for a few days. [CLS] [SEP] Brock died at the Battle of Queenston Heights, which the British won. [CLS] [SEP]
