Kaisa Sere (3 June 1954 – 5 December 2012) was a Finnish computer scientist, specialising in research into formal methods.Kaisa Sere was born 3 June 1954 in Gamlakarleby (present-day Kokkola, Finland). [CLS] [SEP] She received an MSc in mathematics in 1979 and a PhD in computer science in 1990, both from Åbo Akademi University in Turku, southern Finland. [CLS] [SEP] She undertook formal methods research in action systems, distributed systems, hardware design, neural networks, and program refinement. [CLS] [SEP] She undertook joint research with Ralph-Johan Back and was involved in many collaborative European research projects. [CLS] [SEP] She also supervised 19 PhD students.During 1984–5, Sere was a lecturer at Ohio State University in the United States. [CLS] [SEP] During 1991–2, she was a post-doctoral researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the Utrecht University in the Netherlands. [CLS] [SEP] During 1993–8, she held an Associate Professorship at the Department of Computer Science and Applied Mathematics in the University of Kuopio in Finland. [CLS] [SEP] In 1997, she became a Docent of Computer Science in the same department in 1997. [CLS] [SEP] During 1998–9, she held a senior research professorship funded by the Academy of Finland. [CLS] [SEP] In 1998, she became a full professor of Computer Science and Engineering in the Department of Information Technology at Åbo Akademi University. [CLS] [SEP] She held a senior researcher position from the Academy of Finland during 2010–11. [CLS] [SEP] Sere was also affiliated with the Turku Center for Computer Science (TUCS) and a member of the Research Council for Natural Sciences and Engineering for the Academy of Finland.
