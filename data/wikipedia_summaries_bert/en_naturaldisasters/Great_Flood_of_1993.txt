The Great Flood of 1993 (or Great Mississippi and Missouri Rivers Flood of 1993) was a flood that occurred in the Midwestern United States, along the Mississippi and Missouri rivers and their tributaries, from April to October 1993. [CLS] [SEP] The flood was among the most costly and devastating to ever occur in the United States, with $15 billion in damages (approx. [CLS] [SEP] $26 billion in 2018 dollars). [CLS] [SEP] The hydrographic basin affected over around 745 miles (1,199 km) in length and 435 miles (700 km) in width, totaling about 320,000 square miles (830,000 km2). [CLS] [SEP] Within this zone, the flooded area totaled around 30,000 square miles (78,000 km2) and was the worst such U.S. [CLS] [SEP] disaster since the Great Mississippi Flood of 1927, as measured by duration, area inundated, persons displaced, crop and property damage, and number of record river levels. [CLS] [SEP] In some categories, the 1993 flood even surpassed the 1927 flood, at the time the largest flood ever recorded on the Mississippi. [CLS] [SEP]
