Typhoon Krosa, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Vinta, was a typhoon that made landfall in the northern Philippines in late October 2013. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on October 27 near Guam, the storm slowly intensified while moving westward. [CLS] [SEP] Krosa developed an eye and became a typhoon before striking Luzon on October 31. [CLS] [SEP] The storm weakened over land, but re-intensified over the South China Sea, reaching peak winds of 150 km/h (90 mph) on November 2 off the southeast coast of China. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Krosa stalled and encountered unfavorable conditions, resulting in quick weakening. [CLS] [SEP] By November 3, it had weakened to tropical storm status, and was no longer being warned on by the next day. [CLS] [SEP] In northern Luzon, Krosa damaged 32,000 houses, including 3,000 that were destroyed, and caused four fatalities.
