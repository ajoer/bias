[CLS] Hurricane Doria was an unusual and erratic hurricane that existed during September 1967. [SEP] [CLS] The fourth named storm and hurricane of the 1967 Atlantic hurricane season, Doria developed on September 8 off the east coast of Florida. [SEP] [CLS] It meandered until attaining tropical storm status, at which point the storm accelerated towards the northeast. [SEP] [CLS] On September 10, Doria intensified into a Category 1 hurricane on the modern-day Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale. [SEP] [CLS] After moving out to sea, the storm turned westward towards the United States. [SEP] [CLS] A compact cyclone, Doria weakened to a tropical storm shortly before moving ashore in the Mid-Atlantic States. [SEP] [CLS] The storm ultimately dissipated on September 21. [SEP] [CLS] The storm, which ultimately made landfall near the Virginia–North Carolina border, produced high winds along the coast from New Jersey through North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] A small boat sank off the coast of New Jersey, killing three of its occupants. [SEP] [CLS] Overall damage was estimated around $150,000 (1967 USD), although the storm overall was considered beneficial. [SEP] [CLS]
