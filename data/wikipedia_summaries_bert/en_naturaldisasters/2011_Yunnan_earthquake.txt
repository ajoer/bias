The 2011 Yunnan earthquake was a 5.4 magnitude earthquake that occurred on 10 March 2011 at 12:58 CST, with its epicenter in Yingjiang County, Yunnan, People's Republic of China, near the Burmese border. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 26 people died and 313 were injured with 133 in serious condition. [CLS] [SEP] China's Xinhua reports that up to seven aftershocks, measuring up to a magnitude of 4.7, followed the initial quake, which caused a total of 127,000 people to be evacuated to nearby shelters. [CLS] [SEP] It joined over 1,000 other minor tremors that affected the region in the two preceding months. [CLS] [SEP] Following damage surveys, officials reported that 1,039 buildings were destroyed and 4,994 more were seriously damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake occurred one day before a much larger earthquake struck Japan that also formed a tsunami. [CLS] [SEP]
