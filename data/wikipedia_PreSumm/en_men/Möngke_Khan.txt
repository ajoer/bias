[CLS] Möngke (Mongolian: ᠮᠥᠩᠬᠡ Möngke / Мөнх Mönkh; Chinese: 蒙哥; pinyin: Ménggē; 11 January 1209 – 11 August 1259) was the fourth khagan of the Mongol Empire, ruling from 1 July 1251, to 11 August 1259. [SEP] [CLS] He was the first Khagan from the Toluid line, and made significant reforms to improve the administration of the Empire during his reign. [SEP] [CLS] Under Möngke, the Mongols conquered Iraq and Syria as well as the kingdom of Dali (modern Yunnan). [SEP] [CLS]
