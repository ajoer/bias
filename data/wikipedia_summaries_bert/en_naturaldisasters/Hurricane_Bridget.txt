Hurricane Bridget of June 1971 was one of the worst hurricanes to strike the Mexican city of Acapulco. [CLS] [SEP] It formed on June 14 as a tropical depression, which is a minimal tropical cyclone with winds less than gale force. [CLS] [SEP] However, it was soon upgraded to a tropical storm, and Bridget steadily intensified to become a hurricane on June 15. [CLS] [SEP] After peaking at Category 2 intensity, it weakened to a tropical storm on June 17, then made landfall in Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] Hours later, however, it turned offshore as a tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] Bridget dissipated on June 20 after leaving heavy damage and 17 deaths in the Acapulco area. [CLS] [SEP]
