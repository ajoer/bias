[CLS] Therese Krones (7 October 1801 – 28 December 1830) was an Austrian actress of much ability and grace in comic parts. [SEP] [CLS] She was born at Freudenthal of parents who were engaged in the theatrical business. [SEP] [CLS] After several provincial tours she appeared at the Leopoldstädter Theater in Vienna, where she played with Ferdinand Raimund, who greatly influenced her technique. [SEP] [CLS] In 1827, she retired from the stage for a time, being unjustly accused of complicity in the murder of Johann Conrad Blank by Severin von Jaroszynski. [SEP] [CLS] She wrote several plays: Sylphide, das See-Fräulein (1828), Der Branntweinbrenner und der Nebelgeist (1829), and Kleopatra (1830). [SEP] [CLS] Krones died in Vienna after a short but serious illness, aged only 29. [SEP] [CLS] She was buried at the St. [SEP] [CLS] Marx Cemetery, but in 1930 was exhumed and reburied in an honorary grave in the Vienna Zentralfriedhof. [SEP] [CLS]
