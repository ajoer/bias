Daniel Morgan (unknown 1735-36 – July 6, 1802) was an American pioneer, soldier, and politician from Virginia. [CLS] [SEP] One of the most respected battlefield tacticians of the American Revolutionary War of 1775–1783, he later commanded troops during the suppression of the Whiskey Rebellion of 1791–1794. [CLS] [SEP] Born in New Jersey to Welsh immigrants, Morgan settled in Winchester, Virginia. [CLS] [SEP] He became an officer of the Virginia militia and recruited a company of riflemen at the start of the Revolutionary War. [CLS] [SEP] Early in the war, Morgan served in Benedict Arnold's expedition to Quebec and in the Saratoga campaign. [CLS] [SEP] He also served in the Philadelphia campaign before resigning from the army in 1779. [CLS] [SEP] Morgan returned to the army after the Battle of Camden, and led the Continental Army to victory in the Battle of Cowpens. [CLS] [SEP] After the war, Morgan retired from the army again and developed a large estate. [CLS] [SEP] He was recalled to duty in 1794 to help suppress the Whiskey Rebellion, and commanded a portion of the army that remained in Western Pennsylvania after the rebellion. [CLS] [SEP] A member of the Federalist Party, Morgan twice ran for the United States House of Representatives, winning election to the House in 1796. [CLS] [SEP] He retired from Congress in 1799 and died in 1802. [CLS] [SEP]
