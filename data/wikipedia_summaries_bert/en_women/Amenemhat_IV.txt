See Amenemhat, for other individuals with this name.Amenemhat IV (also Amenemhet IV) was the seventh and penultimate pharaoh of the 12th Dynasty of Egypt (c. [CLS] [SEP] 1990–1800 BC) during the late Middle Kingdom period (c. [CLS] [SEP] 2050–1710 BC), ruling for over nine years in the late 19th century BC or the early 18th century BC.Amenemhat IV may have been the son, grandson or step-son of his predecessor, the powerful Amenemhat III. [CLS] [SEP] His reign started with a two-year coregency with Amenemhat III and was seemingly peaceful. [CLS] [SEP] He undertook expeditions in the Sinai for turquoise, in Upper Egypt for amethyst and to the Land of Punt. [CLS] [SEP] He also maintained trade relations with Byblos as well as the Egyptian presence in Nubia. [CLS] [SEP] Amenemhat IV built some parts of the temple of Hathor at Serabit el-Khadim in the Sinai and constructed the well-preserved temple of Renenutet in Medinet Madi. [CLS] [SEP] Amenemhat IV's tomb has not been identified, although the Southern Mazghuna pyramid is a possibility. [CLS] [SEP] He was succeeded by Sobekneferu, possibly his sister or step-sister, whose short reign marked the end of the 12th Dynasty and the beginning of the Middle Kingdom's decline into the Second Intermediate Period.
