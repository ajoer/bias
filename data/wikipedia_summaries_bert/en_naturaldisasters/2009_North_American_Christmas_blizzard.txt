The 2009 North American Christmas blizzard was a powerful winter storm and severe weather event that affected the Midwestern United States, Great Plains, Southeastern United States, the Eastern Seaboard, and parts of Ontario. [CLS] [SEP] The storm began to develop on December 22 before intensifying to produce extreme winds and precipitation by the morning of December 24. [CLS] [SEP] The storm's rapid development made it difficult for forecasters to predict. [CLS] [SEP] The blizzard was reported to have claimed at least 21 lives, and disrupted air travel during the Christmas travel season.
