Luigi Cherubini ( KERR-uu-BEE-nee, Italian: [luˈiːdʒi keruˈbiːni]; 8 or 14 September 1760 – 15 March 1842) was an Italian Classical and Romantic composer. [CLS] [SEP] His most significant compositions are operas and sacred music. [CLS] [SEP] Beethoven regarded Cherubini as the greatest of his contemporaries. [CLS] [SEP] His operas were heavily praised and interpreted by Rossini. [CLS] [SEP]
