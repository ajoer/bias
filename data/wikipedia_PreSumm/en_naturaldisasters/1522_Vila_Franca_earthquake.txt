[CLS] The 1522 Vila Franca earthquake, also known as the 1522 Vila Franca landslide (Portuguese: Subversão de Vila Franca or Terramoto de Vila Franca) refers to the earthquake and landslides that occurred on October 21–22, 1522, in the municipality of Vila Franca do Campo. [SEP] [CLS] Vila Franco do Campo was then the provincial capital and is located on São Miguel Island in the Portuguese archipelago of the Azores. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter of the earthquake was situated several kilometers north-northwest of Vila Franca and contained a maximum intensity of X (i.e., "Very destructive") on the European macroseismic scale. [SEP] [CLS] They caused landslides and lahars that moved 6,750,000 cubic metres (238,000,000 cu ft) of material down the surrounding slopes, destroying buildings. [SEP] [CLS] The movement of debris into the settlement caused the deaths of 3,000 to 5,000 people. [SEP] [CLS] In addition to the destruction of Vila Franca, the earthquake affected the neighboring settlements of Ponta Garça, Maia and Porto Formoso where thousands died as well. [SEP] [CLS] A tsunami formed by the lahar destroyed several boats that were located near the islet of Vila Franca and the deaths of almost a hundred people. [SEP] [CLS] Gaspar Frutuoso, writing 70 years following the destruction, provided a complete record of these events, called "Romance de Vila Franca."
