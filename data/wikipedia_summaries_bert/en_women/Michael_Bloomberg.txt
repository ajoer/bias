Michael Rubens Bloomberg (born February 14, 1942) is an American businessman, politician, philanthropist, and author. [CLS] [SEP] He is the majority owner and co-founder of Bloomberg L.P.. [CLS] [SEP] He was the mayor of New York City from 2002 to 2013, and was briefly a candidate in the 2020 Democratic presidential primaries. [CLS] [SEP] Bloomberg grew up in Medford, Massachusetts and graduated from Johns Hopkins University and Harvard Business School. [CLS] [SEP] He began his career at the securities brokerage Salomon Brothers before forming his own company in 1981. [CLS] [SEP] That company, Bloomberg L.P., is a financial information, software and media firm that is known for its Bloomberg Terminal. [CLS] [SEP] Bloomberg spent the next twenty years as its chairman and CEO. [CLS] [SEP] In 2020, Forbes ranked him as the sixteenth-richest person in the world, with an estimated net worth of $48 billion as of 7 April 2020 and as of 21 July 2020, Bloomberg ranked 8th in Forbes 400 with net worth $60.1 billion . [CLS] [SEP] Since signing The Giving Pledge, Bloomberg has given away $8.2 billion. [CLS] [SEP] Bloomberg was elected the 108th mayor of New York City. [CLS] [SEP] First elected in 2001, he held office for three consecutive terms, winning re-election in 2005 and in 2009. [CLS] [SEP] Pursuing socially liberal and fiscally moderate policies, Bloomberg developed a technocratic managerial style. [CLS] [SEP] After a brief stint as a full-time philanthropist, he re-assumed the position of CEO at Bloomberg L.P. [CLS] [SEP] by the end of 2014. [CLS] [SEP] As mayor of New York, Bloomberg established public charter schools, rebuilt urban infrastructure, and supported gun control, public health initiatives, and environmental protections. [CLS] [SEP] He also led a rezoning of large areas of the city, which facilitated massive and widespread new commercial and residential construction after the September 11 attacks. [CLS] [SEP] Bloomberg is considered to have had far-reaching influence on the politics, business sector, and culture of New York City during his three terms as mayor. [CLS] [SEP] He has also faced significant criticism for his expansion of the city's stop and frisk program, support for which he reversed with an apology before his 2020 presidential run. [CLS] [SEP] In November 2019, Bloomberg officially launched his campaign for the Democratic nomination for president of the United States in the 2020 election. [CLS] [SEP] He ended his campaign in March 2020, after having won only 61 delegates. [CLS] [SEP] Bloomberg self-funded $935 million on the primary campaign, setting the record for the most expensive U.S. [CLS] [SEP] presidential primary campaign. [CLS] [SEP]
