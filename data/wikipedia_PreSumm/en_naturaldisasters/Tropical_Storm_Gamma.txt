[CLS] Tropical Storm Gamma was the 24th tropical storm of the 2005 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] Gamma formed on November 18 from a tropical wave which had left the coast of Africa on November 3. [SEP] [CLS] Between November 13 and November 16 the system was designated Tropical Depression Twenty-Seven and moved westward through the Windward Islands into the Caribbean. [SEP] [CLS] Although its winds were not of tropical storm force, the storm brought damagingly heavy rainfall to Trinidad and to St. [SEP] [CLS] Vincent and the Grenadines. [SEP] [CLS] On November 18, after a period of disorganization during which it appeared to be dissipating, the system strengthened and was upgraded to a tropical storm off the coast of Honduras and given the name Gamma. [SEP] [CLS] There, its torrential rainfall triggered deadly landslides. [SEP] [CLS] Gamma's winds and rains also reached into Belize, where they were less deadly. [SEP] [CLS] The storm dissipated on November 22, having killed 39 people. [SEP] [CLS]
