[CLS] Anna Seghers (German: [ˈana ˈzeːɡɛʁs] (listen); 19 November 1900 – 1 June 1983) was a German writer famous for depicting the moral experience of the Second World War. [SEP] [CLS] After living in Mexico City (1941–47) and West Berlin (1947-50), Anna Seghers eventually settled in the GDR. [SEP] [CLS] The pseudonym Anna Seghers was apparently based on the surname of the Dutch painter and printmaker Hercules Pieterszoon Seghers or Segers (c. [SEP] [CLS] 1589 – c. [SEP] [CLS] 1638).
