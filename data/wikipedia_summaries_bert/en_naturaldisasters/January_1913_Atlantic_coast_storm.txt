[CLS] The January 1913 Atlantic coast storm was a strong extratropical cyclone that affected the eastern coast of the United States on January 3, 1913. [SEP] [CLS] It resulted in heavy damage due to the high winds and produced record low pressure readings. [SEP] [CLS] The lowest confirmed barometric pressure reading, 955.0 mb (28.20 inHg), for a non-tropical system in the continental United States (CONUS) was recorded during this storm at Canton, New York. [SEP] [CLS] This broke the record low of this type set by the January 1886 Blizzard. [SEP] [CLS] The lowest pressure reading of this type was later equalled on March 7, 1932 at Block Island, Rhode Island. [SEP] [CLS] The next lowest record, 955.2 mb (28.21 inHg), was during the October 2010 North American storm complex on October 26, 2010 at Bigfork, Minnesota. [SEP] [CLS]
