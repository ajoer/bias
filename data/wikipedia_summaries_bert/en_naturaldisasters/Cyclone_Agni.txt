Severe Cyclonic Storm Agni was a tropical cyclone of the 2004 North Indian Ocean cyclone season notable for its record proximity to the equator. [CLS] [SEP] It was the second North Indian Ocean cyclone to receive a name, after Onil earlier in the year. [CLS] [SEP] Agni formed on November 28 well to the southwest of India in the Arabian Sea, and steadily intensified as it tracked northwestward. [CLS] [SEP] The Joint Typhoon Warning Center (JTWC) estimated peak 1 minute sustained winds of 120 km/h (75 mph), while the India Meteorological Department (IMD) estimated peak 3 minute sustained winds of 100 km/h (65 mph); the IMD is the official warning center for the north Indian Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] After peaking, it weakened due to wind shear, dry air, and cooler waters, and the JTWC issued its final advisory on December 3 as it approached the coast of Somalia. [CLS] [SEP] The remnants of Agni moved along the Somalian coastline until dissipating on December 5. [CLS] [SEP]
