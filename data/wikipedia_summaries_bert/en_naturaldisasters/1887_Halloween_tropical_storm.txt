[CLS] The 1887 Halloween tropical storm was a late-season tropical cyclone that caused significant damage along the East Coast of the United States during Halloween of 1887. [SEP] [CLS] The sixteenth tropical storm of the annual hurricane season, it formed from an area of disturbed weather over the Gulf of Mexico on October 29. [SEP] [CLS] The storm later came ashore along the west coast of Florida. [SEP] [CLS] After crossing the state, it produced severe thunderstorms along the North Carolina–Virginia coastline before becoming extratropical on November 1. [SEP] [CLS] The extratropical system intensified into the equivalent of a Category 1 hurricane on the modern-day Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale. [SEP] [CLS] It eventually dissipated on November 6, shortly after hitting northwest France. [SEP] [CLS] The storm affected the town of Norfolk, where it became the most damaging storm since 1879. [SEP] [CLS] Despite the damage inland, the storm is best known for the unusually high number of shipwrecks and maritime incidents it caused. [SEP] [CLS] One ship, a schooner called the Manantico, capsized, killing the captain and one of its crew members. [SEP] [CLS] Three other ships were driven ashore on Virginia beaches from Dam Rock to Cape Henry, and numerous others were put in danger. [SEP] [CLS]
