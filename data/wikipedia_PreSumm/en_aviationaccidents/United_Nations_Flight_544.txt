[CLS] United Nations Flight 544 was a civilian helicopter operated by Nizhnevartovskavia that on 21 December 2012 was shot down by the South Sudanese army (SPLA) near Likuangole, South Sudan, killing all four people on board. [SEP] [CLS] Flight 544 was performing a reconnaissance mission for the United Nations Mission in South Sudan (UNMISS) to evaluate overland routes and landing areas for delivery of humanitarian aid. [SEP] [CLS] The flight had originated in Juba. [SEP] [CLS] Shortly after taking off from a stopover at Pibor, the helicopter took fire from SPLA ground-based anti-aircraft artillery and crashed. [SEP] [CLS] The crash site was near the front of ongoing fighting between the SPLA and Murle rebels led by David Yau Yau. [SEP] [CLS] The four victims on board were Russian nationals employed by Nizhnevartovskavia. [SEP] [CLS]
