[CLS] On 21 May 2000, a British Aerospace BAe-3101 Jetstream 3101 operated by East Coast Aviation Services (known operationally as Executive Airlines) crashed into mountainous terrain in Bear Creek Township, Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania. [SEP] [CLS] The plane was carrying 17 professional gamblers returning home from Caesar's Palace Casino in Atlantic City, New Jersey, along with 2 crew members. [SEP] [CLS] It was chartered by Caesar's Palace (New Jersey). [SEP] [CLS] All 19 passengers and crews on board were killed instantly. [SEP] [CLS] An investigation was conducted by the National Transportation Safety Board. [SEP] [CLS] It found that while the aircraft was approaching Scranton International Airport, it ran out of fuel. [SEP] [CLS] The investigation found that the crews were supposed to refill the aircraft with a total of 180 gallons of fuel. [SEP] [CLS] Instead, they refilled with 90 gallons. [SEP] [CLS] Although the final report concluded that fuel exhaustion was the cause of the crash, it was not received warmly by some relatives of the victims. [SEP] [CLS] Several of them filled lawsuits against Executive Airlines and British Aerospace. [SEP] [CLS] Executive Airlines chief executive Michael Peragine questioned the NTSB report, claiming that it dismissed several other factors that might have been beneficial to the investigation.
