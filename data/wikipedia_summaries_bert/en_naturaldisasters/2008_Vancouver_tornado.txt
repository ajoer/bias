The 2008 Vancouver tornado was a rare tornado that occurred in the city of Vancouver, Washington on January 10, 2008. [CLS] [SEP] The NWS rated this storm EF1 (wind speeds of 90-110 mph).The National Weather Service briefly posted a Tornado Warning. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado was immediately accompanied by several funnel clouds, and an advisory issued stated that more funnel clouds were possible. [CLS] [SEP] A funnel cloud was seen to the south of the tornado in Gresham, Oregon. [CLS] [SEP] The storm was a rare event in the Pacific Northwest. [CLS] [SEP] Damage reports showed widespread downed power lines, malfunctioning traffic lights, and roofing that had been ripped off. [CLS] [SEP] The majority of the damage was light in nature. [CLS] [SEP] Vancouver Lake Crew's facilities on the east shore of Vancouver Lake were demolished in the storm. [CLS] [SEP] At least 126,000 customers had lost power because of the storm.
