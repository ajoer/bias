[CLS] Anna Lowenstein or Löwenstein (born 1951) is a British Esperantist. [SEP] [CLS] She worked for the World Esperanto Association 1977–1981. [SEP] [CLS] Under the name Anna Brennan she founded and was editor of the feminist magazine Sekso kaj Egaleco 1979–1988, and she edited the 'easy language' section of Kontakto 1983–1986. [SEP] [CLS] She has written some non-fiction, and two novels. [SEP] [CLS] Her historical novel The Stone City (La Ŝtona Urbo), was first published in English and Esperanto in 1999, and has since been translated into French (2010) and Hungarian (2014). [SEP] [CLS] Her second novel Morto de artisto (2008) was published in Esperanto. [SEP] [CLS] She is well known as a journalist, teacher and activist in the Esperanto movement, and has been a member of the Academy of Esperanto since 2001. [SEP] [CLS] She is married to Renato Corsetti, a former president of the World Esperanto Association. [SEP] [CLS] The couple lived together in Italy from 1981, but since 2015 they have been living in the UK. [SEP] [CLS]
