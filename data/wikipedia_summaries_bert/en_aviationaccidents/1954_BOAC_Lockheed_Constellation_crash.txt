A British Overseas Airways Corporation (BOAC) Lockheed L-749A Constellation crashed and caught fire as it attempted to land at Kallang Airport on 13 March 1954, killing 33 of the 40 passengers and crew. [CLS] [SEP] The accident occurred when the aircraft struck a seawall on approach to the runway. [CLS] [SEP] The inquiry attributed this to crew tiredness, noting that the captain had been on duty for over 21 hours. [CLS] [SEP] It also criticised the response of the airport fire unit. [CLS] [SEP] This is the highest death toll of any aviation accident in Singapore. [CLS] [SEP]
