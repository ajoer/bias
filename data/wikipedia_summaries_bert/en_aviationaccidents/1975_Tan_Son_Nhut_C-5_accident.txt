On 4 April 1975, a Lockheed C-5A Galaxy participating in the first mission of Operation Babylift crashed on approach during an emergency landing at Tan Son Nhut Air Base, South Vietnam. [CLS] [SEP] The cause was ascribed to loss of flight control due to explosive decompression and structural failure. [CLS] [SEP] The accident marked the second operational loss and first fatal crash for the C-5 Galaxy fleet, and is the third deadliest accident involving a U.S. [CLS] [SEP] military aircraft after the 1968 Kham Duc C-130 shootdown and Arrow Air Flight 1285.
