[CLS] In Greek mythology, Asterion (; Greek: Ἀστερίων, gen.: Ἀστερίωνος, literally "starry") or Asterius (; Ἀστέριος) may refer to the following figures: Asterion, one of the Potamoi. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius, one of the Giants. [SEP] [CLS] Asterion, an attendant of the starry-god Astraeus. [SEP] [CLS] Asterion or Asterius, king of Crete. [SEP] [CLS] Asterion or Asterius, name of the Minotaur. [SEP] [CLS] Asterion, son of Zeus and Idaea, the real name of Minos. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius, son of Minos and Androgenia, a girl from the Cretan city of Phaestus. [SEP] [CLS] He was the commander of Cretans who joined the god Dionysus in his Indian War. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius never returned to his homeland but instead settled among the Colchians and named them Asterians. [SEP] [CLS] There Asterius fathered Miletus, Caunus, and Byblis. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius, a king of Anactoria (Miletus) and son of Anax, son of Gaia. [SEP] [CLS] He was a slain by the hero Miletus who named after himself the newly conquered lands. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius, according to Hyginus one of the Sons of Aegyptus, who married Cleo, daughter of Danaus. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius, a prince of Pylos and son of King Neleus by Chloris, daughter of Amphion of Orchomenus. [SEP] [CLS] He was the brother to Pero, Asterius, Pylaon, Deimachus, Eurybius, Epilaus, Evagoras, Phrasius, Eurymenes, Alastor, Nestor and Periclymenus. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius was slain along with his brothers, except Nestor, by Heracles when the hero took revenge to Neleus who refused to cleanse him from blood-debt. [SEP] [CLS] Asterion or Asterius, an Argonaut from Piresia in Thessaly. [SEP] [CLS] Asterius or Asterion, an Argonaut from the Achaean city of Pellene. [SEP] [CLS] He was the son of Hyperasius, descendant of Pelles, son of King Phorbas of Argos. [SEP] [CLS] In two separate accounts, Asterius and his brother Amphion were called the children of Hypso while Hippasus was said to be their father. [SEP] [CLS]
