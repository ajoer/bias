Ramón Freire Serrano (American Spanish: [raˈmoɱ ˈfɾejɾe]; November 29, 1787 – December 9, 1851) was a Chilean political figure. [CLS] [SEP] He was head of state on several occasions, and enjoyed a numerous following until the War of the Confederation. [CLS] [SEP] Ramón Freire was one of the principal leaders of the liberal Pipiolo movement. [CLS] [SEP] He has been praised by historian Gabriel Salazar as the most democratic leader of the early republican period in Chile. [CLS] [SEP]
