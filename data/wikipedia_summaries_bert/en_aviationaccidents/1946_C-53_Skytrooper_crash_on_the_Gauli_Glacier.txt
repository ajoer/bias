The C-53 Crash on the Gauli Glacier in the Bernese Alps, (Switzerland) on 19 November 1946 was a turning point in alpine rescue and an international media event. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft, coming from Tulln, Austria (near Vienna), bound for Pisa, Italy, collided with the Gauli Glacier in poor visibility. [CLS] [SEP] On board were eight passengers (among them two high-ranking officers of the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] armed forces, four women, and one 11-year-old girl) and four crew. [CLS] [SEP] Several people were injured, but there were no fatalities. [CLS] [SEP]
