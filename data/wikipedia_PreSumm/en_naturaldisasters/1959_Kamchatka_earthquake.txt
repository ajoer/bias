[CLS] The 1959 Kamchatka earthquake occurred on May 4 at 19:15 local time with a moment magnitude of 8.0, and a surface wave magnitude of 8.2. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter was near the Kamchatka Peninsula, Russian SFSR, USSR. [SEP] [CLS] Building damage was reported in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky. [SEP] [CLS] The maximum intensity was VIII (Damaging) on the Medvedev–Sponheuer–Karnik scale. [SEP] [CLS] The intensity in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky was about VIII MSK. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake triggered a tsunami with .2 meters (7.9 in) of runup that was recorded in Massacre Bay, Alaska, in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Subduction is active along about the southern half of the eastern coast of Kamchatka Peninsula, between its junctions with the Aleutian Islands and the Kuril Islands. [SEP] [CLS]
