2004 Doğubayazıt earthquake was a 5.1 Mw or 5.2 Mw earthquake that rocked Doğubayazıt, Ağrı, Turkey on 2 July 2004 at 01.30 local time. [CLS] [SEP] 18 people were killed and 32 were injured.The greatest damage was at Yığınçal village, while Kutlubulak and Sağlıksuyu villages were also affected. [CLS] [SEP] Kandilli Observatory stated that around 1000 buildings were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was felt from Ağrı, Iğdır, Kars, and areas near Iran-Turkey border. [CLS] [SEP] The intensity of the shock was reported as VII, while the depth was reported as 5 km. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake happened during the local mountain pasture season where villagers were in the mountains, which prevented a higher number of casualties. [CLS] [SEP] Various organizations sent relief to the area. [CLS] [SEP] Three ministers of Turkish government visited the area, Greece offered help and France sent messages of solidarity.
