[CLS] The name Josephine has been used for six tropical cyclones in the Atlantic Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Josephine (1984) - stayed far away from U.S. [SEP] [CLS] coast, but was so large that tropical storm-force winds were felt at Cape Hatteras, North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Josephine (1990) - wandered around Atlantic, performing a full loop at one point. [SEP] [CLS] Never threatened land. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Josephine (1996) - formed in the Gulf of Mexico, made landfall in Florida and moved over the eastern states, causing $130 million in damage. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Josephine (2002) - short-lived storm that remained in the open sea. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Josephine (2008) - storm that remained in the open sea. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Josephine (2020) - earliest tenth named storm on record, dissipated north of the Lesser Antilles without affecting land. [SEP] [CLS]
