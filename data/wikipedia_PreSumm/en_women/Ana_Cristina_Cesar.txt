[CLS] Ana Cristina César (June 2, 1952 – October 29, 1983) was a poet, literary critic and translator from Rio de Janeiro. [SEP] [CLS] She came from a middle-class Protestant background and was usually known as "Ana C." She had written since childhood and developed a strong interest in English literature. [SEP] [CLS] She spent some time in England in 1968 and, on returning to Brazil, she became a published author of note. [SEP] [CLS] The 1970s and early 1980s were the peak of her poetic career. [SEP] [CLS] She returned to England in 1983. [SEP] [CLS] One of the authors she admired was Sylvia Plath. [SEP] [CLS] She shared some commonalities with her in temperament and fate. [SEP] [CLS] She died in 1983 by jumping out of a window at her parents´ apartment, in Rio de Janeiro. [SEP] [CLS]
