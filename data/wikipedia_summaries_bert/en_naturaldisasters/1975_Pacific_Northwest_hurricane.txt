The 1975 Pacific Northwest hurricane was an unusual Pacific tropical cyclone that attained hurricane status farther north than any other Pacific hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] It was officially unnamed, with the cargo ship Transcolorado providing vital meteorological data in assessing the storm. [CLS] [SEP] The twelfth tropical cyclone of the 1975 Pacific hurricane season, it developed from a cold-core upper-level low merging with the remnants of a tropical cyclone on August 31, well to the northeast of Hawaii. [CLS] [SEP] Convection increased as the circulation became better defined, and by early on September 2 it became a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] Turning to the northeast through an area of warm water temperatures, the storm quickly strengthened, and, after developing an eye, it attained hurricane status late on September 3, while located about 1,200 miles (1,950 km) south of Alaska. [CLS] [SEP] After maintaining peak winds for about 18 hours, the storm rapidly weakened, as it interacted with an approaching cold front. [CLS] [SEP] Early on September 5, it lost its identity near the coast of Alaska. [CLS] [SEP]
