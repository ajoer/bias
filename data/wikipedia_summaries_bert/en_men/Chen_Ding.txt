[CLS] Ding-Shinn Chen (Chinese: 陳定信; pinyin: Chén Dìngxìn; 6 July 1943 – 24 June 2020) was a Taiwanese hepatologist. [SEP] [CLS] He was born on 6 July 1943, in what became Yingge District, New Taipei City. [SEP] [CLS] Chen's mother became sick when he was a child, and needed surgical intervention. [SEP] [CLS] As such, Chen was around medical professionals at a young age, which sparked his interest in the field. [SEP] [CLS] Upon high school graduation, Chen was accepted into the National Taiwan University College of Medicine. [SEP] [CLS] During his fourth year of medical school, Chen's father died of liver cancer, inspiring him to study hepatology. [SEP] [CLS] He completed medical studies in 1968. [SEP] [CLS] Chen became a resident at National Taiwan University Hospital and later joined the NTU medical faculty, working closely with Sung Juei-low. [SEP] [CLS] Chen and Sung advocated for widespread preventative measures against hepatitis B, resulting in the establishment of a mass vaccination program in 1984. [SEP] [CLS] Chen specialized in liver disease research, namely hepatitis. [SEP] [CLS] His research earned Chen the nickname Liver King (肝帝; gāndì), which, pronounced in Mandarin, is homophonous to Gandhi.In 1991, Chen was elected a member of Academia Sinica. [SEP] [CLS] In 2001 he was appointed dean of NTU's College of Medicine. [SEP] [CLS] That same year, he became a fellow of The World Academy of Sciences. [SEP] [CLS] In 2002, Chen traveled to Malawi to deliver medical textbooks. [SEP] [CLS] During the 2003 SARS outbreak, Chen worked closely with Michael M. [SEP] [CLS] C. [SEP] [CLS] Lai on research into vaccinations against the disease. [SEP] [CLS] In 2005, Chen was elected a foreign associate of the United States National Academy of Sciences. [SEP] [CLS] Chen stepped down as dean in 2007. [SEP] [CLS] He was the 2009 recipient of the International Recognition Award presented by the European Association for the Study of the Liver. [SEP] [CLS] Chen won the 2010 Nikkei Asia Prize in science and technology. [SEP] [CLS] In 2012, Chen spoke in support of decriminalizing medical malpractice. [SEP] [CLS] He favored granting Chen Shui-bian medical parole in 2014. [SEP] [CLS] In 2018, Chen was awarded Order of Brilliant Star with Grand Cordon. [SEP] [CLS] That same year, Chen received the Baruch S. [SEP] [CLS] Blumberg Prize from the Hepatitis B Foundation.Chen died at the National Taiwan University Hospital on 24 June 2020 from pancreatic cancer, age 76. [SEP] [CLS]
