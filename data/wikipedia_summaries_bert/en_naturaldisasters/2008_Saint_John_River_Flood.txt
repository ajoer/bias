[CLS] The 2008 Saint John River flood was considered the worst flooding of the Saint John River in 35 years. [SEP] [CLS] Flood levels reached 27 feet and 4 inches in Fredericton on May 1, almost reaching the previous record of 28 feet and 3 inches, set in 1973. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding was attributed to the melting of record snowfall from the 2007-2008 winter and heavy rain during the melting season. [SEP] [CLS] Fifty streets in Fredericton area were closed as a result of the flooding. [SEP] [CLS] The flooding caused severe damage in areas of northern Maine and western New Brunswick including international bridges and hundreds of homes and buildings. [SEP] [CLS] By May 3, the Canadian Red Cross had registered 680 residents who had been forced to evacuate from their homes. [SEP] [CLS] An additional 600 residences were evacuated in Maine.
