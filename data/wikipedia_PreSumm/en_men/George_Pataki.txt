[CLS] George Elmer Pataki (; born June 24, 1945) is an American lawyer and Republican politician who served as the 53rd Governor of New York (1995â€“2006). [SEP] [CLS] An attorney by profession, Pataki was elected mayor of his hometown of Peekskill, New York and went on to be elected to the State Assembly and the State Senate. [SEP] [CLS] In 1994, Pataki ran for Governor of New York against three-term incumbent Mario Cuomo, defeating him by a margin of more than three points as part of the Republican Revolution of 1994. [SEP] [CLS] Pataki would himself be elected to three consecutive terms, and was the third Republican Governor of New York elected since 1923 (the other two were Govs. [SEP] [CLS] Thomas E. [SEP] [CLS] Dewey and Nelson Rockefeller). [SEP] [CLS] As of 2020, Pataki is the most recent Republican to hold any statewide office in New York. [SEP] [CLS] In early 2015, Pataki began exploring a candidacy for the Republican nomination for President of the United States in 2016; he announced his candidacy on May 28, 2015. [SEP] [CLS] On December 29, 2015, Pataki ended his campaign before the Republican presidential primaries had begun. [SEP] [CLS]
