[CLS] The 1933 Texas tropical storm produced record rainfall in the south-central United States in July of the 1933 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] It was the third storm of the season, developing on July 14 near the Lesser Antilles. [SEP] [CLS] While moving westward through the Caribbean Sea, the cyclone passed just south of Jamaica on July 16. [SEP] [CLS] The storm dropped heavy rainfall on the island that caused flooding and road washouts. [SEP] [CLS] On July 18, the storm struck Belize and later moved across the Yucatán Peninsula. [SEP] [CLS] Initially it was believed that the storm continued into Mexico and dissipated while another storm formed to its northeast, but it was discovered in 2012 that the storm followed one continuous track. [SEP] [CLS] On July 23, the storm struck southeastern Texas at its peak intensity of 50 mph (85 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] It moved inland and transitioned into an extratropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Turning eastward, the storm dropped torrential rainfall in eastern Texas and western Louisiana, peaking at 21.3 in (540 mm) in Logansport, Louisiana. [SEP] [CLS] Several stations reported record rainfall, including Shreveport, Louisiana where its 24‑hour amount remained the highest daily total as of 2008. [SEP] [CLS] High rains left about $1.5 million in crop damage between two Texas counties. [SEP] [CLS] The rains caused rivers to exceed their banks, forcing evacuations and road closures.
