[CLS] Helen Iglauer Glueck (1907–1995) was an American physician known for her research in blood chemistry that linked bleeding disorders in newborns with lack of Vitamin K in breast milk. [SEP] [CLS] Dr. [SEP] [CLS] Glueck graduated from Walnut Hills High School in Cincinnati, Ohio in 1925. [SEP] [CLS] She obtained her BA from the University of Wisconsin–Madison and her MD from the University of Cincinnati College of Medicine. [SEP] [CLS] She directed the University of Cincinnati Student Health Services (1945-1959) and then became Director of the Coagulation Laboratory at the University. [SEP] [CLS]
