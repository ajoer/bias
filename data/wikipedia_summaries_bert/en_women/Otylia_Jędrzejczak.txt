[CLS] Otylia Jędrzejczak (Polish: [ɔˈtɨlja jɛndˈʐɛjtʂak] (listen); born 13 December 1983) is a Polish swimmer. [SEP] [CLS] She was the Olympic champion from 2004 Athens in the 200 metre butterfly. [SEP] [CLS] She finished 4th in this event in the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, and also swam at the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney. [SEP] [CLS] In Athens 2004 she also silvered in 400 m freestyle and 100 m butterfly. [SEP] [CLS] Three times she broke the world record in the women's 200 m butterfly (once in the 25-metre pool). [SEP] [CLS] Jędrzejczak was born in Ruda Śląska, Silesian Voivodship, Poland. [SEP] [CLS] She started swimming at the age of six as a measure to correct a slight curvature of the spine. [SEP] [CLS] At first she hated the sport. [SEP] [CLS] Her attitude towards it changed after she had won her first prize in a competition in Germany at the age of eight. [SEP] [CLS] Jędrzejczak took up swimming seriously in high school. [SEP] [CLS] Led by coach Maria Jakóbik, she won her first titles in the Junior European Championships in 1999. [SEP] [CLS] Medals in the Senior European Championships in 1999 and 2000 opened the way to her first Olympic appearance. [SEP] [CLS] Jędrzejczak studied at the Academy of Physical Education in Warsaw (Akademia Wychowania Fizycznego w Warszawie). [SEP] [CLS] Her coach is Paweł Słomiński. [SEP] [CLS] She stands 187 cm (6 ft 1½ in) tall and wears a size 47 (EU)/14 (US) shoe. [SEP] [CLS] For her sport achievements, she received the Knight's Cross of the Order of Polonia Restituta (5th Class) in 2004. [SEP] [CLS]
