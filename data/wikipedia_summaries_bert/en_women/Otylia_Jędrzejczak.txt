Otylia Jędrzejczak (Polish: [ɔˈtɨlja jɛndˈʐɛjtʂak] (listen); born 13 December 1983) is a Polish swimmer. [CLS] [SEP] She was the Olympic champion from 2004 Athens in the 200 metre butterfly. [CLS] [SEP] She finished 4th in this event in the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, and also swam at the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney. [CLS] [SEP] In Athens 2004 she also silvered in 400 m freestyle and 100 m butterfly. [CLS] [SEP] Three times she broke the world record in the women's 200 m butterfly (once in the 25-metre pool). [CLS] [SEP] Jędrzejczak was born in Ruda Śląska, Silesian Voivodship, Poland. [CLS] [SEP] She started swimming at the age of six as a measure to correct a slight curvature of the spine. [CLS] [SEP] At first she hated the sport. [CLS] [SEP] Her attitude towards it changed after she had won her first prize in a competition in Germany at the age of eight. [CLS] [SEP] Jędrzejczak took up swimming seriously in high school. [CLS] [SEP] Led by coach Maria Jakóbik, she won her first titles in the Junior European Championships in 1999. [CLS] [SEP] Medals in the Senior European Championships in 1999 and 2000 opened the way to her first Olympic appearance. [CLS] [SEP] Jędrzejczak studied at the Academy of Physical Education in Warsaw (Akademia Wychowania Fizycznego w Warszawie). [CLS] [SEP] Her coach is Paweł Słomiński. [CLS] [SEP] She stands 187 cm (6 ft 1½ in) tall and wears a size 47 (EU)/14 (US) shoe. [CLS] [SEP] For her sport achievements, she received the Knight's Cross of the Order of Polonia Restituta (5th Class) in 2004. [CLS] [SEP]
