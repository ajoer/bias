The Helgoland Island air disaster occurred on 9 September 1913 after the airship Zeppelin LZ 14 had been transferred to the Imperial German Navy on 7 October 1912. [CLS] [SEP] As the first airship owned by the Navy, it was given the serial number L-1. [CLS] [SEP] Ordered to participate in manoeuvers, it departed the mainland in bad weather. [CLS] [SEP] With 20 people on board, L-1 flew into a gale, and, while 18 miles from its destination, the cold rain caused its gas to contract, causing it to settle 32 km; 17 nmi (20 mi) north of Heligoland into the North Sea, breaking in two. [CLS] [SEP] The control car sank, drowning 13 of its occupants. [CLS] [SEP] Seven were rescued by motor torpedo boats. [CLS] [SEP]
