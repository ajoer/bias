[CLS] Erik Kynard Jr. [SEP] [CLS] (born February 3, 1991) is an American track and field athlete who competes in the high jump. [SEP] [CLS] In his Olympic debut at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London as a 21-year-old, he won a silver medal in the men's high jump. [SEP] [CLS] Kynard was born in 1991, the son of Erik Kynard and Brandynn Adams. [SEP] [CLS] He is a 2009 graduate of Rogers High School in Toledo, Ohio, and a graduate of Kansas State University where he trains under Cliff Rovelto. [SEP] [CLS] He jumps off his right leg. [SEP] [CLS] At the 2012 United States Olympic Trials, Kynard made the Olympic team by placing second behind Jamie Nieto with a height of 2.28 m. [SEP] [CLS] At the Olympics, Kynard won silver behind Russian Ivan Ukhov with a height of 2.33 m, the first major international medal of his career. [SEP] [CLS] Ukhov won the competition with a height of 2.38 m. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the high jump competition, Kynard was noticed by his American-themed tube socks. [SEP] [CLS] He will likely get gold after the disqualification of Ukhov for doping in 2019. [SEP] [CLS] At the start of the 2013 outdoor season, he cleared a world-leading mark of 2.34 m at the Mt SAC Relays. [SEP] [CLS] He won the high jump title at the United States Outdoor National Championships in 2014 and 2015: in the latter he tied his personal best, and the Meet record, of 2.37m (7' 9-1/4").
