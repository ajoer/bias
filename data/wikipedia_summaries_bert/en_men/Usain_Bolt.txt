[CLS] Usain St Leo Bolt, (; born 21 August 1986) is a Jamaican former sprinter, widely considered to be the greatest sprinter of all time. [SEP] [CLS] He is a world record holder in the 100 metres, 200 metres and 4 × 100 metres relay. [SEP] [CLS] An eight-time Olympic gold medallist, Bolt is the only sprinter to win Olympic 100 m and 200 m titles at three consecutive Olympics (2008, 2012 and 2016). [SEP] [CLS] He also won two 4 × 100 relay gold medals. [SEP] [CLS] He gained worldwide fame for his double sprint victory in world record times at the 2008 Beijing Olympics, which made him the first person to hold both records since fully automatic time became mandatory. [SEP] [CLS] An eleven-time World Champion, he won consecutive World Championship 100 m, 200 m and 4 × 100 metres relay gold medals from 2009 to 2015, with the exception of a 100 m false start in 2011. [SEP] [CLS] He is the most successful athlete of the World Championships. [SEP] [CLS] Bolt is the first athlete to win four World Championship titles in the 200 m and is one of the most successful in the 100 m with three titles. [SEP] [CLS] Bolt improved upon his second 100 m world record of 9.69 with 9.58 seconds in 2009 – the biggest improvement since the start of electronic timing. [SEP] [CLS] He has twice broken the 200 metres world record, setting 19.30 in 2008 and 19.19 in 2009. [SEP] [CLS] He has helped Jamaica to three 4 × 100 metres relay world records, with the current record being 36.84 seconds set in 2012. [SEP] [CLS] Bolt's most successful event is the 200 m, with three Olympic and four World titles. [SEP] [CLS] The 2008 Olympics was his international debut over 100 m; he had earlier won numerous 200 m medals (including 2007 World Championship silver) and holds the world under-20 and world under-18 records for the event. [SEP] [CLS] His achievements as a sprinter have earned him the media nickname "Lightning Bolt", and his awards include the IAAF World Athlete of the Year, Track & Field Athlete of the Year, BBC Overseas Sports Personality of the Year (three times) and Laureus World Sportsman of the Year (four times). [SEP] [CLS] Bolt retired after the 2017 World Championships, when he finished third in his last solo 100 m race, opted out of the 200 m, and pulled up in the 4×100 m relay final. [SEP] [CLS] Stating that it was his "dream" to play professional football, in August 2018, Bolt began training with A-League club the Central Coast Mariners as a left-winger. [SEP] [CLS] On 12 October 2018, Bolt scored twice for the team in a friendly match. [SEP] [CLS] He left the club the following month, and in January 2019 chose not to pursue a career in football.
