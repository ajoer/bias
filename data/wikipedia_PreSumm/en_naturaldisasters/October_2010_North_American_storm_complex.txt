[CLS] The October 2010 North American storm complex is the name given to a historic extratropical cyclone that impacted North America. [SEP] [CLS] The massive storm complex caused a wide range of weather events including a major serial derecho stretching from the Gulf Coast to the Great Lakes, a widespread tornado outbreak across the Southeast United States and Midwest and a blizzard across portions of the Canadian Prairies and the Dakotas. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone's lowest minimum pressure of 955.2 mb (28.21 inHg) made it the second most intense non-tropical system recorded in the continental United States (CONUS). [SEP] [CLS] The lowest confirmed pressure for a non-tropical system in the continental United States was set by a January 1913 Atlantic coast storm. [SEP] [CLS]
