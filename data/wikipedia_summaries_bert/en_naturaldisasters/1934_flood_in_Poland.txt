1934 flood in Poland (Polish: Powódź w Polsce, 1934) was the biggest flood in the Second Polish Republic. [CLS] [SEP] It began with heavy rains in the Dunajec river basin, which took place between 13 and 17 July 1934. [CLS] [SEP] In the following days, the flood spread to the basins of the Raba, Wisłoka, and Skawa, all of which are tributaries to the Vistula. [CLS] [SEP] The disaster took the lives of 55 people and caused damages estimated at 60 million interbellum Polish zlotys. [CLS] [SEP]
