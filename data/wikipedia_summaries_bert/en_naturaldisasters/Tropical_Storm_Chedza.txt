Severe Tropical Storm Chedza was a deadly tropical cyclone that struck Madagascar in January 2015. [CLS] [SEP] It formed from the intertropical convergence zone and moved over Mozambique, After moving open waters, the system began organizing on January 14, becoming Tropical Storm Chedza two days later. [CLS] [SEP] It quickly intensified over the Mozambique Channel due to warm waters and favorable conditions, and the storm attained peak 10 minute sustained winds of 100 km/h (65 mph) on January 16. [CLS] [SEP] That day, Chedza moved ashore western Madagascar between Belo sur Mer and Morondava, and it quickly crossed the island while weakening. [CLS] [SEP] The storm briefly re-intensified, passing southwest of Réunion before turning to the southeast. [CLS] [SEP] Chedza became extratropical on January 19, and was last noted two days later. [CLS] [SEP] The formative stages of Chedza brought rainfall to an already flooded region across southeastern Africa. [CLS] [SEP] Weeks of heavy rainfall killed 117 people in Mozambique and 104 in neighboring Malawi, where it was the worst floods in 24 years. [CLS] [SEP] In Madagascar, Chedza struck after weeks of heavy rainfall, causing rivers to increase and flooding widespread areas of crop fields. [CLS] [SEP] In the capital city of Antananarivo, the deluge damaged the main water pump that controlled water levels in the region. [CLS] [SEP] The rainfall caused mudslides and damaged roads. [CLS] [SEP] Across the country, flooding from Chedza displaced 54,792 people, after destroying 4,430 houses and flooding another 3,442, mostly in Vatovavy-Fitovinany in the southeastern portion. [CLS] [SEP] Chedza killed 80 people and caused about $40 million in damage (2015 USD). [CLS] [SEP] Later, the storm brought heavy rainfall and strong winds to the mountainous peaks of Réunion.
