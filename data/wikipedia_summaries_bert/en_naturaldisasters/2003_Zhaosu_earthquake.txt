The 2003 Zhaosu earthquake, also known as the Syumbinskoe earthquake, occurred on December 1 at 01:38 UTC. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located in the Almaty Region, Kazakhstan, near the Sino–Kazakh border. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake had a magnitude of Mw 6.0 and had a maximum observed intensity of VII (Very strong) on the Medvedev–Sponheuer–Karnik scale. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was close to the Zhaosu County, Xinjiang, where 10 people were reported dead, 73 people injured, and more than 800 buildings collapsed. [CLS] [SEP] Some people were evacuated in Zhaosu. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake occurred in the cold winter, and the 30 cm ground snow covered the roads in the mountainous region and hindered the relief work. [CLS] [SEP]
