Northwest Airlines Flight 188 was a regularly scheduled flight from San Diego, California, to Minneapolis, Minnesota on October 21, 2009. [CLS] [SEP] The flight landed over one hour late in Minneapolis after overshooting its destination by over 150 miles (240 km) because of pilot errors. [CLS] [SEP] As a result of this incident, the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) revoked the pilot certificates of the involved pilots and the National Transportation Safety Board issued recommendations to air traffic control procedures and changes in the rules for cockpit crew and air traffic controllers. [CLS] [SEP] The incident also caused American lawmakers to move to prevent pilots on U.S. [CLS] [SEP] airliners from using electronic devices while taxiing or flying. [CLS] [SEP] Changes to flight deck automation have also been suggested as a result of the incident and prototype designs that could mitigate errors leading to similar incidents have been described.
