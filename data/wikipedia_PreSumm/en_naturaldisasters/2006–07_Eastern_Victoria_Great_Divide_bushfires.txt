[CLS] The Eastern Victoria Great Divide bushfires, also known as the Great Divide Complex, were a series of bushfires that commenced in the Victorian Alps in Australia on 1 December 2006 due to lightning strikes and continued for 69 days. [SEP] [CLS] They were the longest running bushfires in the state's history. [SEP] [CLS] Between 1.2 and 1.3 million hectares were burnt, most of it public land. [SEP] [CLS] Victorian fire personnel were assisted by others from all states and territories of Australia, as well as teams from New Zealand, the United States and Canada. [SEP] [CLS]
