[SEP] [CLS] She was born on December 15, 1901, in Stamford, Connecticut, the oldest of three children of Frances Bancroft and William J. [SEP] [CLS] Long. [SEP] [CLS] She graduated from Vassar College. [SEP] [CLS] Long had worked at Vogue and Vanity Fair before finding fame at The New Yorker. [SEP] [CLS] Harold Ross hired her to write a column on New York nightlife. [SEP] [CLS] Under the name of Lipstick, Lois Long chronicled her nightly escapades of drinking, dining, and dancing. [SEP] [CLS] She wrote of the decadence of the decade with an air of aplomb, wit, and satire, becoming quite a celebrity. [SEP] [CLS] Because her readers did not know who she was, Long often jested in her columns about being a "short squat maiden of forty" or a "kindly, old, bearded gentleman." However, in the announcement of her marriage to The New Yorker cartoonist Peter Arno, she revealed her true identity. [SEP] [CLS] She remained with The New Yorker as a columnist until 1968. [SEP] [CLS] She died in 1974. [SEP] [CLS]
