[CLS] The 1977 Oman cyclone was the deadliest tropical cyclone on record to strike Oman. [SEP] [CLS] The storm formed off the west coast of India in the Arabian Sea, and curved westward to reach peak winds of 110 km/h (70 mph). [SEP] [CLS] The storm struck Masirah Island and later southern Oman on June 13, before dissipating the next day over Saudi Arabia. [SEP] [CLS] Producing wind gusts to 230 km/h (140 mph), the storm was the strongest cyclone on record to hit the Arabian Peninsula until Cyclone Gonu hit in 2007. [SEP] [CLS] About 95% of Marisah Island was damaged by the strong winds, including much of the military base. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone dropped 430.6 mm (16.95 in) of rainfall over a 24 hour period on Marisah, which was the highest daily total in the country as of 2003. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, the storm killed at least 105 people and left 50,000 homeless. [SEP] [CLS]
