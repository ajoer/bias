Hurricane Gaston was a minimal hurricane that made landfall in South Carolina on August 29, 2004. [CLS] [SEP] It then crossed North Carolina and Virginia before exiting to the northeast and dissipating. [CLS] [SEP] The storm killed nine people – eight of them directly – and caused $130 million (2004 USD) in damage. [CLS] [SEP] Gaston produced torrential downpours that inundated Richmond, Virginia. [CLS] [SEP] Although originally designated a tropical storm, Gaston was reclassified as a hurricane when post-storm analysis revealed it had maximum sustained winds of 75 mph (120 km/h).
