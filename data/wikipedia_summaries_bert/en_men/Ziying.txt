[CLS] Ziying, King of Qin (Chinese: 秦王子嬰; pinyin: Qín-wáng Zǐyīng, died January 206 BC) was the third and last ruler of the Qin dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] He ruled over a fragmented Qin Empire for 46 days from mid-October to early December in 207 BC. [SEP] [CLS] He is referred to in some sources with the posthumous name Emperor Shang of Qin (秦殤帝), despite Qin abolishing the practice of posthumous names. [SEP] [CLS] (In Chinese tradition, even someone who never held a ruling title while alive might be given the posthumous title "emperor" after his death.)
