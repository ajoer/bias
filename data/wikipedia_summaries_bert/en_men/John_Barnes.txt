John Charles Bryan Barnes MBE (born 7 November 1963) is a Jamaican-born English international former professional footballer and manager. [CLS] [SEP] He currently works as a commentator and pundit for ESPN and SuperSport. [CLS] [SEP] Initially a quick, skilful left winger, he moved to central midfield later in his career. [CLS] [SEP] He won two league titles with Liverpool with whom he also won two cup finals at Wembley. [CLS] [SEP] He was also a FA Cup runner-up with Watford, Liverpool and Newcastle United. [CLS] [SEP] He earned 79 caps for England. [CLS] [SEP] Barnes was born and initially raised in Jamaica as the son of a military officer from Trinidad and Tobago and a Jamaican mother. [CLS] [SEP] He moved to London with his family when he was 12 years old. [CLS] [SEP] Barnes joined Watford aged 17 in 1981 before playing 296 competitive games for them scoring 85 goals. [CLS] [SEP] He was a Watford 1984 FA Cup Final runner-up. [CLS] [SEP] He debuted for England in 1983 and in 1987 joined Liverpool for £900,000. [CLS] [SEP] In his 10 seasons there Liverpool won the then top-flight First Division twice and the FA Cup twice. [CLS] [SEP] He scored 106 goals in 403 matches. [CLS] [SEP] By the time of his last cap in 1995, he had more caps than any other black England player. [CLS] [SEP] After two years at Newcastle United, he ended his playing career at Charlton Athletic in 1999. [CLS] [SEP] Barnes was the PFA Players' Player of the Year once (in 1987–88) and the Football Writers' Association Footballer of the Year twice (in 1987–88 and 1989–90). [CLS] [SEP] In the run-up to England's 1990 FIFA World Cup campaign he recorded a rap for the official team song, New Order's "World in Motion". [CLS] [SEP] In 2005, he was inducted into the English Football Hall of Fame. [CLS] [SEP] In 2006, in a poll of Liverpool fans' favourite players, Barnes came fifth; a year later, FourFourTwo magazine named him Liverpool's best all time player. [CLS] [SEP] In 2016, The Times readers voted him England's greatest ever left-footed player.Barnes had 8 months as Celtic head coach when his former Liverpool manager Kenny Dalglish was director of football. [CLS] [SEP] Barnes has since managed the Jamaica national team in 2008–09 and English club Tranmere Rovers for four months in 2009. [CLS] [SEP]
