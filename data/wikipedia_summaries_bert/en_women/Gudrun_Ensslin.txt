Gudrun Ensslin (German: [ˈɡuːdʁuːn ˈɛnsliːn]; 15 August 1940 – 18 October 1977) was a founder of the West German far-left militant group Red Army Faction (Rote Armee Fraktion, or RAF, also known as the Baader-Meinhof Gang). [CLS] [SEP] After becoming involved with co-founder Andreas Baader, Ensslin was influential in the politicization of his anarchist beliefs. [CLS] [SEP] Ensslin was perhaps the intellectual head of the RAF. [CLS] [SEP] She was involved in five bomb attacks, with four deaths, was arrested in 1972 and died on 18 October 1977 in what has been called Stammheim Prison's Death Night. [CLS] [SEP]
