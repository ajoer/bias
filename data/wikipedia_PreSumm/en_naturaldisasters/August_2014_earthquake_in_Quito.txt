[CLS] On August 12, 2014 a magnitude 5.1 earthquake struck Ecuador 22 km (14 mi) west-southwest of Cayambe at a depth of 11.9 km (7.4 mi). [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake shook Ecuador's capitol Quito 32 km (20 mi) away causing large dust clouds and prompting the temporary shutdown of Quito's Mariscal Sucre International Airport. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake killed at least 4 people including two men who were buried by a landslide in a local quarry. [SEP] [CLS] It also injured at least 8 others. [SEP] [CLS] Earthquakes are commonplace in Ecuador because of its position along the "Ring of Fire," an area of high seismic activity that encircles the Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS]
