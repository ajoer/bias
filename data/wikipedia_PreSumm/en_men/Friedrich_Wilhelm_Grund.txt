[CLS] Friedrich Wilhelm Grund (7 October 1791, Hamburg, Germany – 24 November 1874, Hamburg, Germany) was a German composer, conductor and teacher. [SEP] [CLS] He studied with his father (piano, violin, cello and contrabass) and with the Hamburg cantor Christian Friedrich Gottlieb Schwencke. [SEP] [CLS] In 1819, he abandoned his career as a concert virtuoso because of the nerve disease of his right hand and he started to compose and teach. [SEP] [CLS] In the same year, he co-founded and led the Gesellschaft der Freunde des religiösen Gesangs (later the Hamburger Singakademie). [SEP] [CLS] He also co-founded the Hamburger Tonkünstlervereins. [SEP] [CLS]
