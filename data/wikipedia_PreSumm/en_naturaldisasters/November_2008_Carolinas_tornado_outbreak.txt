[CLS] The November 2008 Carolinas tornado outbreak was a brief but deadly tornado outbreak which began shortly after midnight (local time), while many people were sleeping. [SEP] [CLS] Most of the eight tornadoes that touched down were produced by two supercell thunderstorms over North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] At 12:25 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] EST, the National Weather Service issued a tornado watch for most of eastern North Carolina as the risk of tornadoes increased. [SEP] [CLS] Not long after, the first tornado of the outbreak, an EF2, touched down in South Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] Almost an hour later, the second tornado touched down in Robeson County, North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] Three other minor tornadoes, two EF0 and an EF1, touched down over the next two hours. [SEP] [CLS] Around 3:10 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] EST, the first of two killer tornadoes touched down near Kenly, North Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] The EF2 tornado destroyed a few homes and damaged several others. [SEP] [CLS] Roughly 20 minutes later, an EF3 tornado touched down in Wilson County. [SEP] [CLS] This tornado killed one person and injured a few others after destroying several homes. [SEP] [CLS] Total damages from the outbreak amounted to $2.5 million, about half of which was a result of the EF3 tornado. [SEP] [CLS]
