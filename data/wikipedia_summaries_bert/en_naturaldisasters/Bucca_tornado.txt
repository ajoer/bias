The Bucca tornado was one of the most violent tornadoes ever observed in Australia, being the first Australian tornado to be officially rated F4 on the Fujita scale. [CLS] [SEP] It occurred near the township of Bucca (near Bundaberg) in Queensland on 29 November 1992 at around 2:20pm AEST. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado was accompanied by cricket-ball sized hail across Bucca and Bullyard regions. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado damaged or destroyed nine houses, some flattened to the ground. [CLS] [SEP] Trees were snapped and stones were found embedded into tree trunks. [CLS] [SEP] Several tree saplings were speared into the walls of homes. [CLS] [SEP] A refrigerator from one home was blown away and never found. [CLS] [SEP] A 3-ton truck was also thrown 300 metres (980 ft) and approximately 20 cattle were killed.The weather system that spawned the Bucca tornado was also responsible for an F3 tornado at Oakhurst.
