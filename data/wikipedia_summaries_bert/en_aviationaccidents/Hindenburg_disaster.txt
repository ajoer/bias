The Hindenburg disaster occurred on May 6, 1937, in Manchester Township, New Jersey, United States. [CLS] [SEP] The German passenger airship LZ 129 Hindenburg caught fire and was destroyed during its attempt to dock with its mooring mast at Naval Air Station Lakehurst. [CLS] [SEP] There were 35 fatalities (13 passengers and 22 crewmen) from the 97 people on board (36 passengers and 61 crewmen), and an additional fatality on the ground. [CLS] [SEP] The disaster was the subject of newsreel coverage, photographs, and Herbert Morrison's recorded radio eyewitness reports from the landing field, which were broadcast the next day. [CLS] [SEP] A variety of hypotheses have been put forward for both the cause of ignition and the initial fuel for the ensuing fire. [CLS] [SEP] The event shattered public confidence in the giant, passenger-carrying rigid airship and marked the abrupt end of the airship era. [CLS] [SEP]
