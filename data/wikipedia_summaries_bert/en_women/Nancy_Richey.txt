Nancy Richey (born August 23, 1942) is a former tennis player from the United States, who won two Grand Slam singles titles (1967 Australian Championships and 1968 French Open) and four Grand Slam women's doubles titles (1965 U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Championships and 1966 Australian, Wimbledon, and U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Championships). [CLS] [SEP] She was ranked World No. [CLS] [SEP] 2 in singles at year-end in 1969. [CLS] [SEP] Richey won 69 singles titles during her career and helped the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] win the Federation Cup in 1969. [CLS] [SEP] She won the singles title at the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Women's Clay Court Championships a record six consecutive years, from 1963 through 1968. [CLS] [SEP] Richey married Kenneth S. [CLS] [SEP] Gunter on December 15, 1970. [CLS] [SEP] They were divorced on December 28, 1976, and Richey reverted to her maiden name. [CLS] [SEP] She is the sister of American tennis player Cliff Richey. [CLS] [SEP] They were the first brother-sister combination to both be concurrently ranked in the USA Top Ten. [CLS] [SEP] They were ranked in the Top Three concurrently in 1965, 1967, 1969 and 1970. [CLS] [SEP] Nancy Richey was inducted into the International Tennis Hall of Fame in 2003. [CLS] [SEP]
