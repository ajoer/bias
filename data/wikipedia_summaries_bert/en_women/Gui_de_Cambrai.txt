Gui de Cambrai (born in the second half of the 12th century, died in the 13th century), was a medieval writer from Northern France who used vernacular French rather than Latin language. [CLS] [SEP] He wrote Le Vengement Alixandre (Alexander revenged), an 1806-line epic poem (c. [CLS] [SEP] 1190). [CLS] [SEP] A French version of Barlaam and Josaphat (c. [CLS] [SEP] 1220-1225) has also been tentatively attributed to de Cambrai. [CLS] [SEP]
