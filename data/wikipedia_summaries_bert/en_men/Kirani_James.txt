[CLS] Kirani James (born 1 September 1992) is a Grenadian sprinter who specializes in the 200 and 400 metres. [SEP] [CLS] He won the 400 m at the World Championships in 2011 and the 2012 London Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] He is Grenada's first and only Olympic medalist.Prodigious from a young age, he ran the fastest 400 m times ever by a 14-year-old and a 15-year-old. [SEP] [CLS] He won a series of gold medals at the CARIFTA Games and the Commonwealth Youth Games and rose on the international stage with 400 m silver medals at the 2007 World Youth and 2008 World Junior Championships. [SEP] [CLS] James became the first athlete to run a 200/400 double at the 2009 World Youth Championships and was the 2010 World Junior Champion. [SEP] [CLS] James received an athletic scholarship at the University of Alabama and won back-to-back NCAA Outdoor Championship titles in his first two years. [SEP] [CLS] He is the third fastest of all-time indoors (44.80 seconds) and ran a personal best of 43.74 at a 2014 Diamond League event in Lausanne. [SEP] [CLS] James is one of only nine athletes (along with Valerie Adams, Usain Bolt, Veronica Campbell-Brown, Jacques Freitag, Yelena Isinbayeva, Jana Pittman, Dani Samuels and David Storl) to win world championships at the youth, junior, and senior level of an athletic event.
