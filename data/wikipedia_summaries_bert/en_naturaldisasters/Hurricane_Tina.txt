Hurricane Tina was the strongest and longest-lived storm of the 1992 Pacific hurricane season, which also threatened land for a brief period. [CLS] [SEP] The twenty-fourth tropical cyclone, twenty-second tropical storm, fourteenth hurricane, and eighth major hurricane of the record breaking 1992 season, Tina formed from a tropical wave on September 17. [CLS] [SEP] The storm moved towards the west and strengthened into a hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] A breakdown in a ridge and to the north and a trough then re-curved Tina to the northeast and towards land, still moving slowly and gradually slowing down. [CLS] [SEP] The trough broke down and was replaced by a strong ridge. [CLS] [SEP] Tina then changed direction again and headed out to sea. [CLS] [SEP] It intensified into a Category 4 storm with winds of 150 mph (240 km/h) and a central pressure of 932 millibars. [CLS] [SEP] Tina then slowly weakened as it turned to the north. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Depression Tina dissipated on October 11, shortly after entering the Central Pacific Hurricane Center's area of responsibility. [CLS] [SEP] Although the tropical cyclone never made landfall, heavy rains were recorded across western Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] While at peak intensity, the storm also displayed annular characteristics.
