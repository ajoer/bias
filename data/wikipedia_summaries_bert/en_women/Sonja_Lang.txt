Toki Pona is a philosophical, artistic, and constructed language known for its small vocabulary. [CLS] [SEP] It was created by Canadian linguist and translator Sonja Lang for the purpose of simplifying thoughts and communication. [CLS] [SEP] It was first published online in 2001 as a draft, and later in complete form in the book Toki Pona: The Language of Good in 2014. [CLS] [SEP] A small community of speakers developed in the early 2000s. [CLS] [SEP] While activity mostly takes place online in chat rooms, on social media, and in other groups, there were a few organized in-person meetings during the 2000s and 2010s. [CLS] [SEP] The underlying feature of Toki Pona is minimalism. [CLS] [SEP] It focuses on simple universal concepts, making use of very little to express the most. [CLS] [SEP] The language is isolating and has around 123 root words and 14 phonemes that are easy to pronounce across different languages. [CLS] [SEP] However, it was not created to be an international auxiliary language.
