Tropical Storm Pabuk, also referred to as Cyclonic Storm Pabuk, was a weak storm that struck the Malay Peninsula in January 2019. [CLS] [SEP] It was also the earliest-forming storm in both the Northwestern Pacific Ocean and North Indian Ocean basins on record. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on the last day of 2018, Pabuk persisted into 2019, spanning two calendar years, and crossed into the North Indian Ocean basin several days later. [CLS] [SEP] The first tropical cyclone and named storm of the 2019 Pacific typhoon and North Indian Ocean cyclone seasons, Pabuk originated as a tropical disturbance in the South China Sea on December 28, 2018, which organized into a tropical depression on December 31. [CLS] [SEP] A day later, on January 1, 2019, the system intensified into a tropical storm and was named Pabuk. [CLS] [SEP] Pabuk made landfall in Thailand on January 4, emerging into the Bay of Bengal in the North Indian Ocean basin shortly afterward. [CLS] [SEP] Pabuk weakened after it entered the North Indian Ocean, eventually degenerating into a remnant low on January 7, before dissipating on the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Pabuk killed a total of 10 people, and the storm caused an estimated total of US$157 million in damages. [CLS] [SEP] In Thailand, eight people were killed and the economic losses were estimated at $156 million. [CLS] [SEP] Pabuk also caused one death each in Vietnam and Malaysia. [CLS] [SEP]
