[CLS] Hurricane Waldo was a Pacific hurricane that caused significant flooding in Kansas during October 1985. [SEP] [CLS] After developing into a tropical depression on October 7, it steadily intensified, becoming a tropical storm within a day. [SEP] [CLS] Waldo reached hurricane intensity on October 8. [SEP] [CLS] After peaking as a moderate Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale, it re-curved to the east, making landfall at peak intensity near Culiacán. [SEP] [CLS] Afterward, it rapidly dissipated. [SEP] [CLS] In all, Waldo caused moderate damage in Sonora. [SEP] [CLS] The remnants of the storm combined with a cold front over the Great Plains. [SEP] [CLS] Significant flooding and one death was recorded in Kansas. [SEP] [CLS] Many rivers and creeks overflowed its banks. [SEP] [CLS]
