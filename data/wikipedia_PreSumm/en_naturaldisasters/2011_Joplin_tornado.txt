[CLS] The 2011 Joplin tornado was a powerful and catastrophic EF5-rated multiple-vortex tornado that struck Joplin, Missouri, on the evening of Sunday, May 22, 2011. [SEP] [CLS] It was part of a larger late-May tornado outbreak and reached a maximum width of nearly one mile (1.6 km) during its path through the southern part of the city. [SEP] [CLS] This particular tornado was unusual in that it intensified in strength and grew larger in size at a very fast rate. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado tracked eastward across the city, and then continued eastward across Interstate 44 into rural portions of Jasper County and Newton County. [SEP] [CLS] It was the third tornado to strike Joplin since May 1971. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, the tornado killed 158 people (with an additional eight indirect deaths), injured some 1,150 others, and caused damages amounting to a total of $2.8 billion. [SEP] [CLS] It was the deadliest tornado to strike the United States since the 1947 Glazier–Higgins–Woodward tornadoes, and the seventh-deadliest overall. [SEP] [CLS] It also ranks as the costliest single tornado in U.S. [SEP] [CLS] history. [SEP] [CLS] Insurance payout was $2.8 billion, the highest in Missouri history, with the previous record of $2 billion being the April 10, 2001 hail storm. [SEP] [CLS]
