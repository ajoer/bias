[CLS] [SEP] 992 | other_post = Abbot of AbingdonBishop of Ramsbury | death_date = 16 November 1005 | death_place = Canterbury, Kent, England | buried = Canterbury Cathedral | feast_day = 16 November | venerated = Roman Catholic Church, Anglican Communion, Eastern Orthodox Church | canonized_date = Pre-Congregation }} Ælfric of Abingdon (died 16 November 1005) was a late 10th-century Archbishop of Canterbury. [CLS] [SEP] He previously held the offices of abbot of St Albans Abbey and Bishop of Ramsbury, as well as likely being the abbot of Abingdon Abbey. [CLS] [SEP] After his election to Canterbury, he continued to hold the bishopric of Ramsbury along with the archbishopric of Canterbury until his death in 1005. [CLS] [SEP] Ælfric may have altered the composition of Canterbury's cathedral chapter by changing the clergy serving in the cathedral from secular clergy to monks. [CLS] [SEP] In his will he left a ship to King Æthelred II of England as well as more ships to other legatees. [CLS] [SEP]
