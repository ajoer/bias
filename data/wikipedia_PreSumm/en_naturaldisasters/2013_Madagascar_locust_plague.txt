[CLS] In 2012, Madagascar had an upsurge in the size of its Malagasy migratory locust (Locusta migratoria capito) populations. [SEP] [CLS] In November of that year, the government issued a locust alert, saying that conditions were right for swarming of the pest insects. [SEP] [CLS] In February 2013, Cyclone Haruna struck the country, creating optimal conditions for locust breeding. [SEP] [CLS] By late March 2013, approximately 50% of the country was infested by swarms of locusts, with each swarm consisting of more than one billion insects. [SEP] [CLS] The authorities changed the situation to plague status. [SEP] [CLS] According to one eyewitness,"You don't see anything except locusts. [SEP] [CLS] You turn around, there are locusts everywhere".News of the infestation went global the week before Passover, inspiring comparisons with the Biblical Plagues of Egypt, one of which was a swarm of locusts. [SEP] [CLS] It is the worst locust outbreak in Madagascar since a 17-year-long outbreak which began during the 1950s. [SEP] [CLS]
