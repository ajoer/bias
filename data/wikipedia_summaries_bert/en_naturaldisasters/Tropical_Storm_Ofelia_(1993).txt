[CLS] Tropical Storm Ofelia, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Luming, was an early-season tropical cyclone that passed south Japan during July 1993. [SEP] [CLS] An area of disturbed weather developed from the Western Pacific monsoon trough in late July 1993. [SEP] [CLS] The disturbance organized into a tropical depression on July 24, and the next day developed into a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Tracking west-northwestward, Ofelia slowly deepened and attained its peak intensity of 80 km/h (50 mph) and a minimum barometric pressure of 994 mbar (29.4 inHg) at noon on July 26. [SEP] [CLS] On the next day, the storm made landfall shorty before weakening to a tropical depression. [SEP] [CLS] On July 27, Ofelia transitioned into an extratropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Across Tokushima Prefecture, 3 people were killed and 44 homes were damaged while 25 other homes were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Elsewhere, across Kochi Prefecture, 142 homes were damaged and an additional 452 were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Three people were also killed and another was injured. [SEP] [CLS] The storm dropped heavy rains in Ehime Prefecture for a 36 hour period, resulting in 17 landslides and the cancellation of 16 flights. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the prefecture, 119 houses were damaged and 10 others were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] In Oita Prefecture, a landslide destroyed a home in Honyabakei, killing three. [SEP] [CLS] In Hiroshima Prefecture, 18,060 households lost power after 934 power lines were downed while 713 homes were damaged and 54 were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 22 railtracks and 1,165 roads were damaged. [SEP] [CLS] Three people were also hurt. [SEP] [CLS] In Yamaguchi Prefecture, four people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] Moreover, 1,312 homes were damaged and 84 others were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, 13 people were killed and damage was estimated at 21.9 billion yen (US$197 million). [SEP] [CLS]
