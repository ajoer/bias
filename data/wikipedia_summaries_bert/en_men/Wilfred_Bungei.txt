[CLS] Wilfred Kipkemboi Bungei (born 24 July 1980) is a Kenyan retired Middle-distance runner, who won the 800 m gold medal at the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing. [SEP] [CLS] He also won at the World indoor Championships in Moscow 2006 the 800 metres title, defeating Mbulaeni Mulaudzi and Olympic Champion Yuriy Borzakovskiy in the course of the race. [SEP] [CLS] Bungei was ranked No.1 in the world over 800 m in 2002 and 2003. [SEP] [CLS] He has a personal best of 1:42.34 minutes (Rieti 2002). [SEP] [CLS] At the 2001 World Championships in Athletics in Edmonton he won a silver medal over 800 m, finishing behind Andre Bucher. [SEP] [CLS] While in school, he focused on sprints and decathlon, before concentrating on 800 metres running. [SEP] [CLS] He graduated from Samoei High School in 1998. [SEP] [CLS] At the 1998 World Junior Championships in Athletics he won a silver medal. [SEP] [CLS] Bungei represented his native country at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece. [SEP] [CLS] Bungei is from Kabirirsang, a village near Kapsabet. [SEP] [CLS] Several of his relatives are runners, including his brother Sammy Kurgat, who won the 2008 Cologne Marathon. [SEP] [CLS] He is a second cousin to Kenyan-born Danish former athlete Wilson Kipketer, while his mum is a cousin of Henry Rono.Bungei is based near Verona, Italy during track season. [SEP] [CLS] He is married to Priscah Bungei with two sons (as of 2008).He was part of the 4 x 800 metres relay team who currently holds the world record. [SEP] [CLS]
