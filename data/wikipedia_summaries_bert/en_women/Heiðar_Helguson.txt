Heiðar Helguson (born 22 August 1977) is an Icelandic former professional footballer who played as a striker. [CLS] [SEP] Heiðar started his career in the Icelandic league system, with UMFS Dalvík and Þróttur. [CLS] [SEP] Following a one-season spell in Norway with Lillestrøm SK, Heiðar moved to England to play for Watford in 1999, with Lillestrøm receiving a transfer fee of £1.5 million. [CLS] [SEP] The English media anglicised his name to Heidar when he joined Watford – it is by this given name that he is commonly known in England. [CLS] [SEP] Although Watford were relegated from the Premier League during his first season at the club, Heiðar was a prolific goalscorer, finishing as the team's top scorer in the 1999–2000, 2002–03 and 2004–05 seasons, before being sold to Fulham in 2005. [CLS] [SEP] He played Premier League for them between 2005 and 2007, and Bolton until 2009, before transferring to Championship side QPR in 2009. [CLS] [SEP] After a return to Watford on loan in the 2009–10 season, Heiðar played regularly for QPR in 2010–11, as they secured promotion to the Premier League as champions. [CLS] [SEP] In QPR's first Premier League season since 1995–96, Heiðar finished as the team's top scorer, helping them to secure Premier League survival on the last day of the 2011–12 season. [CLS] [SEP] He subsequently joined Cardiff City. [CLS] [SEP] Heiðar played and scored for his country at under-19 and under-21 levels, before making his debut for the senior team in 1999. [CLS] [SEP] He earned 55 caps for Iceland, scoring 13 goals, before retiring from international football in 2011. [CLS] [SEP]
