[CLS] Aeroflot Flight 3739 was a Soviet domestic passenger flight from Irkutsk to Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg) with a stopover in Kurgan. [SEP] [CLS] On March 8, 1988, after the Tupolev Tu-154 operating the flight had left Kurgan for Leningrad, it was hijacked by the Ovechkin family, whose members sought an escape from the Soviet Union. [SEP] [CLS] They were born in Sosnovka (now Perm Krai). [SEP] [CLS] The hijackers demanded the crew fly the aircraft to London. [SEP] [CLS] The flight engineer persuaded the hijackers to allow a stopover in Finland for refueling. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft in fact landed at the Soviet military airbase Veshchevo, near the Finnish border, where it was stormed by the incident response team of the Soviet interior ministry. [SEP] [CLS] During the incident four hostages died and five hijackers committed suicide. [SEP] [CLS] Two surviving prosecutable members of the Ovechkin family were sentenced to eight and six years in prison, respectively. [SEP] [CLS] One of the crew was awarded the Order of the Red Banner as a result of the incident. [SEP] [CLS]
