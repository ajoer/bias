Adil Rami (French pronunciation: ​[adil ʁami]; Arabic: عادل رامي‎; born 27 December 1985) is a French professional footballer who plays as a central defender. [CLS] [SEP] Rami began his football career playing for amateur club Étoile Fréjus Saint-Raphaël, then known as ES Fréjus. [CLS] [SEP] Due to only playing the sport as a hobby, he spent three seasons at the club playing in the Championnat de France amateur, the fourth division of French football. [CLS] [SEP] In 2006, Rami ascended three divisions after signing with Ligue 1 club Lille. [CLS] [SEP] At Lille, Rami developed into a starter, making his professional debut in May 2007. [CLS] [SEP] He is nicknamed "Shrek" by teammates and Lille supporters. [CLS] [SEP] In January 2011, Rami joined Spanish club Valencia on a four-year contract. [CLS] [SEP] However, he was immediately loaned back to his previous club and was a part of the Lille team which won the league and cup double. [CLS] [SEP] Despite constant early inquiries from Morocco prior to his first selection, Rami chose to play for his home country of France. [CLS] [SEP] He was in the preliminary list to play for France at the 2010 FIFA World Cup finals but did not make the final squad. [CLS] [SEP] Rami made his senior debut on 11 August 2010 in a friendly match against Norway. [CLS] [SEP] Rami was a member of the France squad that won the 2018 FIFA World Cup. [CLS] [SEP]
