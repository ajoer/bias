The 1911 Michoacán earthquake occurred on June 7 at 04:26 local time (11:02 UTC). [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located near the coast of Michoacán, Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake had a magnitude of 7.6 on the moment magnitude scale. [CLS] [SEP] 45 people were reported dead. [CLS] [SEP] In Mexico City, 119 houses were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Cracks were reported in Palacio Nacional, Escuela Normal para Maestros, Escuela Preparatoria, Inspección de Policía, and Instituto Geológico. [CLS] [SEP] Ciudad Guzmán, the seat of Zapotlán el Grande, Jalisco, suffered great damage.The earthquake occurred hours before the revolutionary Francisco I. [CLS] [SEP] Madero entered Mexico City on the same day, and it was also known as "temblor maderista". [CLS] [SEP] On June 7, 2011, a ceremony was held in Ciudad Guzmán commemorating the centenary of this earthquake.This earthquake was a megathrust earthquake along the Middle America Trench (MAT), a major subduction zone.
