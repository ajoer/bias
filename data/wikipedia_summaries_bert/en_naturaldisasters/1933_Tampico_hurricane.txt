[CLS] The 1933 Tampico hurricane was one of two storms in the 1933 Atlantic hurricane season to reach Category 5 intensity on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale. [SEP] [CLS] It developed on September 16 near the Lesser Antilles, and slowly intensified while moving across the Caribbean Sea. [SEP] [CLS] Becoming a hurricane on September 19, its strengthening rate increased while passing south of Jamaica. [SEP] [CLS] Two days later, the hurricane reached peak winds, estimated at 160 mph (260 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] After weakening, it made landfall on the Yucatán Peninsula, destroying several houses. [SEP] [CLS] One person was killed offshore Progreso, Yucatán during the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Over land, the hurricane weakened to a tropical storm, although it re-intensified slightly in the Gulf of Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] On September 25, it made a second landfall just south of Tampico, Tamaulipas with winds at around 110 mph (180 km/h), and it quickly dissipated over land. [SEP] [CLS] Damage was heaviest there, estimated at $5 million (1933 USD) and there were hundreds of deaths. [SEP] [CLS] About 75% of the houses in Tampico were damaged, including about 50% of houses that had severe to total destruction to their roofs. [SEP] [CLS] The destruction prompted the declaration of martial law, and there was a curfew instated. [SEP] [CLS]
