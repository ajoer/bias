The 1898 Georgia hurricane was a major hurricane that hit the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] state of Georgia, as well as the strongest on record in the state. [CLS] [SEP] It was first observed on September 29, although modern researchers estimated that it developed four days earlier to the east of the Lesser Antilles. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane maintained a general northwest track throughout its duration, and it reached peak winds of 135 mph (215 km/h) on October 2. [CLS] [SEP] That day, it made landfall on Cumberland Island in Camden County, Georgia, causing record storm surge flooding. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane caused heavy damage throughout the region, and killed at least 179 people. [CLS] [SEP] Impact was most severe in Brunswick, where a 16 ft (4.9 m) storm surge was recorded. [CLS] [SEP] Overall damage was estimated at $1.5 million (1898 USD), most of which occurred in Georgia. [CLS] [SEP] In extreme northeastern Florida, strong winds nearly destroyed the city of Fernandina, while light crop damage was reported in southern South Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] After moving ashore, the hurricane quickly weakened and traversed much of North America; it continued northwestward until reaching the Ohio Valley and turning northeastward, and it was last observed on October 6 near Newfoundland.
