[CLS] Auguste Marie Joseph Jean Léon Jaurès (3 September 1859 – 31 July 1914), commonly referred to as Jean Jaurès (French: [ʒɑ̃ ʒɔʁɛs]), was a French Socialist leader. [SEP] [CLS] Initially a moderate republican, he was later one of the first social democrats, becoming the leader, in 1902, of the French Socialist Party, which opposed Jules Guesde's revolutionary Socialist Party of France. [SEP] [CLS] The two parties merged in 1905 in the French Section of the Workers' International (SFIO). [SEP] [CLS] An antimilitarist, Jaurès was assassinated at the outbreak of World War I, and remains one of the main historical figures of the French Left. [SEP] [CLS]
