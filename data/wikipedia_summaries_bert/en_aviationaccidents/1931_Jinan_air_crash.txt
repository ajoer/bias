[CLS] 1931 Jinan Air Crash occurred on November 19, 1931, when a Stinson Detroiter mail plane carrying one passenger and two pilots crashed into a mountainous area in Jinan, Shandong Province, China. [SEP] [CLS] All 3 people on board, including the only passenger, the famous Chinese poet Xu Zhimo, died in the crash. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was operated by China Airways Federal in the Republic of China and was contracted by Chunghwa Post. [SEP] [CLS] It departed from Ming Gugong Airport in Nanking, heading to Nanyuan Airport in Beiping (now Beijing). [SEP] [CLS] The passenger, Xu Zhimo, was intending to attend an architectural lecture given by the well-known architect Lin Huiyin in Beiping. [SEP] [CLS] Because of Xu's fame as a poet, the crash shocked the literary community in China. [SEP] [CLS]
