[CLS] The 1928 Haiti hurricane was considered the worst tropical cyclone in Haiti since the 1886 Indianola hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] The second tropical cyclone and second hurricane of the season, the storm developed from a tropical wave near Tobago on August 7. [SEP] [CLS] Steadily intensifying as it moved northwestward, the system passed through the southern Windward Islands. [SEP] [CLS] Upon entering the Caribbean Sea early on August 8, the tropical depression strengthened into a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] On August 9, the storm strengthened to the equivalent of a Category 1 hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] The next day, the hurricane peaked with winds of 90 mph (150 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] After striking the Tiburon Peninsula of Haiti, the cyclone began weakening and fell to tropical storm intensity on August 12. [SEP] [CLS] By midday on the following day, the storm made landfall near Cienfuegos, Cuba. [SEP] [CLS] Upon emerging into the Straits of Florida, the storm began to re-strengthen. [SEP] [CLS] Early on August 13, it struck Big Pine Key, Florida, as a strong tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Weakening slowly while moving north-northwestward, the system made another landfall near St. [SEP] [CLS] George Island. [SEP] [CLS] After moving inland, the tropical storm slowly deteriorated and dissipated over West Virginia on August 17. [SEP] [CLS] In Haiti, the storm completely wiped out livestock and many crops, particularly coffee, cocoa, and sugar. [SEP] [CLS] Several villages were also destroyed, rendering approximately 10,000 people homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Damage reached $1 million and there were at least 200 deaths. [SEP] [CLS] The only impact in Cuba was downed banana trees. [SEP] [CLS] In Florida, the storm left minor wind damage along the coast. [SEP] [CLS] A Seaboard Air Line Railroad station was destroyed in Boca Grande, while signs, trees, and telephone poles were knocked down in Sarasota. [SEP] [CLS] Several streets in St. [SEP] [CLS] Petersburg were closed due to flooding or debris. [SEP] [CLS] Between Cedar Key and the Florida Panhandle, several vessels capsized. [SEP] [CLS] Water washed up along the side of roads and in wooded areas. [SEP] [CLS] The storm contributed to flooding onset by the previous hurricane, with rainfall peaking at 13.5 in (340 mm) in Caesars Head, South Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] The worst impact from flooding occurred in North Carolina, where several houses were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Six people were killed in the state, of which four due to flooding. [SEP] [CLS] Property damage in the state totaled over $1 million. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, the storm caused at least $2 million in damage and 210 fatalities. [SEP] [CLS]
