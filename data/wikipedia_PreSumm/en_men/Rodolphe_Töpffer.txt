[CLS] Rodolphe Töpffer ( TOP-fər, French: [ʁɔdɔlf tœpfɛʁ]; 31 January or 1 February 1799 – 8 June 1846) was a Swiss teacher, author, painter, cartoonist, and caricaturist. [SEP] [CLS] He is best known for his illustrated books (littérature en estampes, "graphic literature"), which are possibly the earliest European comics. [SEP] [CLS] He is known as the father of comic strips and has been credited as the "first comics artist in history."Paris-educated, Töpffer worked as a schoolteacher and ran a boarding school, where he entertained students with his caricatures. [SEP] [CLS] In 1837, he published Histoire de M. [SEP] [CLS] Vieux Bois (published in the United States in 1842 as The Adventures of Obadiah Oldbuck). [SEP] [CLS] Each page of the book had one to six captioned cartoon panels, much like modern comics. [SEP] [CLS] Töpffer published several more of these books, and wrote theoretical essays on the form. [SEP] [CLS]
