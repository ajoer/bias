See Amenemhat, for other individuals with this name.Amenemhat III, also spelled Amenemhet III, was a pharaoh of the Twelfth Dynasty of Egypt. [CLS] [SEP] He ruled from c. [CLS] [SEP] 1860 BC to c. [CLS] [SEP] 1814 BC, the highest known date being found in a papyrus dated to Regnal Year 46, I Akhet 22 of his rule. [CLS] [SEP] His reign is regarded as the golden age of the Middle Kingdom. [CLS] [SEP] He may have had a long coregency (of 20 years) with his father, Senusret III. [CLS] [SEP] Toward the end of his reign he instituted a coregency with his successor Amenemhet IV, as recorded in a now damaged rock inscription at Konosso in Nubia, which equates Year 1 of Amenemhet IV to either Year 46, 47, or 48 of his reign. [CLS] [SEP] His daughter, Sobekneferu, later succeeded Amenemhat IV, as the last ruler of the twelfth dynasty. [CLS] [SEP] Amenemhat III's throne name, Nimaatre, means "Belonging to the Justice of Re."
