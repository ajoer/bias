On Monday, August 27, 1990, American musician Stevie Ray Vaughan was killed in a helicopter crash near East Troy, Wisconsin, at age 35. [CLS] [SEP] He was one of the most influential blues guitarists of the 1980s, described by the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as "the second coming of the blues". [CLS] [SEP] Vaughan spent much of his last days performing with his band Double Trouble as the opening act for Eric Clapton at Alpine Valley Music Theatre. [CLS] [SEP] After the concert concluded, he and three members of Clapton's tour entourage boarded a helicopter that crashed into the side of a nearby ski hill shortly after takeoff. [CLS] [SEP] The Civil Air Patrol was notified of the crash at 4:30 a.m., and authorities were called to locate the scene of the accident. [CLS] [SEP] All five people were pronounced dead on arrival. [CLS] [SEP] The autopsy concluded that Vaughan suffered multiple internal injuries and died of exsanguination due to blunt trauma. [CLS] [SEP] At the inquest, the coroner found no evidence of drug or alcohol use and recorded death by misadventure. [CLS] [SEP] The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) concluded that the pilot failed to gain sufficient altitude to avoid rising terrain. [CLS] [SEP] Vaughan was buried at Laurel Land Cemetery in Dallas, Texas, on August 31, 1990. [CLS] [SEP] In 1992, his family filed a wrongful death lawsuit against Omniflight Helicopters that was settled for an undisclosed amount in 1995. [CLS] [SEP]
