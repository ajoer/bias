Irene Epple-Waigel (born 18 June 1957) is a German former alpine skier. [CLS] [SEP] She won a total of 11 Alpine Skiing World Cup races and two World Cups, in giant slalom and combined (both in 1982). [CLS] [SEP] She also won a silver medal at the 1980 Winter Olympic Games in giant slalom. [CLS] [SEP] In the 1978 FIS Alpine Skiing World Championships at Garmisch-Partenkirchen she won the silver medal in the downhill. [CLS] [SEP] On 9 January 1983 she won the first women's World Cup Super-G race, held in Verbier. [CLS] [SEP] In 1992 she completed her medical studies in Munich, and in November 1994 married Theo Waigel, who was the German Finance Minister from 1989 to 1998. [CLS] [SEP] In 1980 she was named the German Sportswoman of the Year. [CLS] [SEP] She is the sister of alpine skier Maria Epple. [CLS] [SEP]
