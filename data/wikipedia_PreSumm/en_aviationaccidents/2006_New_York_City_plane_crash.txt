[CLS] On October 11, 2006, a Cirrus SR20 aircraft crashed into the Belaire Apartments in the Upper East Side of Manhattan, New York City, at about 2:42 p.m. [SEP] [CLS] EDT (18:42 UTC). [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft struck the north side of the building causing a fire in several apartments, which was extinguished within two hours.Both people aboard the aircraft were killed in the accident: New York Yankees pitcher Cory Lidle and his certificated flight instructor. [SEP] [CLS] Twenty-one people were injured, including eleven firefighters. [SEP] [CLS] An apartment resident, Ilana Benhuri, who lived in the building with her husband, was hospitalized for a month with severe burns incurred when the post-impact fire engulfed her apartment.The Cirrus SR20 aircraft, tail number N929CD, was owned by Lidle. [SEP] [CLS] On May 1, 2007, the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) stated that the probable cause of the crash was pilot error. [SEP] [CLS] The NTSB was unable to determine which person was flying the aircraft at the time of the crash. [SEP] [CLS]
