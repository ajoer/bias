The Altaigate Scandal is the name given to the events surrounding the 9 January 2009 disappearance of an Mi-171 helicopter in the south of the Altai Republic. [CLS] [SEP] The helicopter contained several VIP passengers. [CLS] [SEP] The helicopter was found as a result of large-scale rescue operations on 11 January 2009. [CLS] [SEP] It was discovered on the Sailughem mountain ridge 8 kilometers from the border between Russia and Mongolia. [CLS] [SEP] Seven people were killed, 2 severely injured and 2 slightly injured. [CLS] [SEP] Among those dead were the Representative of the Russian President to the State Duma Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Alexander Kosopkin, the Head of the Altai Republic Game Management Agency Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Kaimin, two pilots, and one locally famous musician. [CLS] [SEP] Among the survivors were Deputy Governor of the Altai Republic Mr. [CLS] [SEP] Anatoly Bannykh, the second pilot and two other, one of which was heavily injured. [CLS] [SEP]
