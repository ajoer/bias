Ejei Khongghor or Ejei Khan (Chinese: 额哲; pinyin: Ézhé; ?–1661) was the son of Ligdan Khan, the last in the Borjigin clan of Mongol Khans, who once established the Mongol Empire in the 13th century. [CLS] [SEP] The Northern Yuan dynasty, which existed as remnants of the Yuan dynasty retreating north to the Mongolian homeland after 1368, had formally came to the end at this time. [CLS] [SEP]
