[CLS] Hurricane Paloma was the third-strongest November Atlantic hurricane on record. [SEP] [CLS] It was the sixteenth tropical storm, eighth hurricane and fifth major hurricane of the 2008 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] A late-season hurricane, it set several records for its intensity and formation. [SEP] [CLS] Paloma was the third most powerful November hurricane on record in the Atlantic Basin, behind only a 1932 hurricane and 1999's Lenny. [SEP] [CLS] It also marked the first time that at least one major hurricane formed in every month of the hurricane season from July to November, with only June not having a major hurricane this season. [SEP] [CLS] Paloma developed out of a strong tropical disturbance off the eastern coast of Nicaragua and northern coast of Honduras on November 5. [SEP] [CLS] The disturbance had slowly developed into a tropical depression while hugging the coastline. [SEP] [CLS] The depression strengthened into a tropical storm early on November 6, then a hurricane later that day. [SEP] [CLS] The next day, Paloma intensified into a Category 2 hurricane, then soon afterward into a Category 3 major hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] Early on November 8, Paloma continued to intensify and reached Category 4 intensity, and then weakened rapidly into a Category 2 hurricane before making landfall in Santa Cruz del Sur, Cuba. [SEP] [CLS] Paloma weakened into a tropical storm on November 9 while moving over Cuba, where it stalled out. [SEP] [CLS] It dissipated later that evening. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Paloma caused heavy damage in both the Cayman Islands and Cuba. [SEP] [CLS] Total damage standing at $454.5 million, with one fatality. [SEP] [CLS]
