The Voice of Germany is a German reality talent show created by John de Mol, based on the concept The Voice of Holland and it is part of an international series. [CLS] [SEP] It began airing on ProSieben and Sat.1 on November 24, 2011.There are five different stages to the show: producers' auditions, blind auditions, battle rounds, sign offs, and live shows. [CLS] [SEP] There have been nine winners to date: Ivy Quainoo, Nick Howard, Andreas Kümmert, Charley Ann Schmutzler, Jamie-Lee Kriewitz, Tay Schmedtmann, Natia Todua, Samuel Rösch, and the latest Claudia Emmanuela Santoso. [CLS] [SEP] The show was originally presented by Stefan Gödde. [CLS] [SEP] However, from 2012 onwards Thore Schölermann has presented the show. [CLS] [SEP] From 2015 until 2019, Schölermann together with Lena Gercke hosted the show. [CLS] [SEP] In 2020, Annemarie Carpendale will replace Gercke as the new host. [CLS] [SEP] The coaches for the tenth season will feature Mark Forster, Nico Santos, Stefanie Kloss & Yvonne Catterfeld and Samu Haber & Rea Garvey. [CLS] [SEP] Other coaches from previous seasons include Nena, The BossHoss, Xavier Naidoo, Max Herre, Michi & Smudo, Andreas Bourani, Michael Patrick Kelly, Alice Merton and Sido. [CLS] [SEP] In the ninth season, Nico Santos was featured as an off-screen fifth coach for Comeback Stage contestants. [CLS] [SEP] Michael Schulte took over as the Comeback Stage coach for the tenth season.
