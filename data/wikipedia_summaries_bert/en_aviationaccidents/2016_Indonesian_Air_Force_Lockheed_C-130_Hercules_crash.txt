The Lockheed C-130 Hercules is an American four-engine turboprop military transport aircraft designed and built originally by Lockheed (now Lockheed Martin). [CLS] [SEP] Capable of using unprepared runways for takeoffs and landings, the C-130 was originally designed as a troop, medevac, and cargo transport aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] The versatile airframe has found uses in a variety of other roles, including as a gunship (AC-130), for airborne assault, search and rescue, scientific research support, weather reconnaissance, aerial refueling, maritime patrol, and aerial firefighting. [CLS] [SEP] It is now the main tactical airlifter for many military forces worldwide. [CLS] [SEP] More than 40 variants of the Hercules, including civilian versions marketed as the Lockheed L-100, operate in more than 60 nations. [CLS] [SEP] The C-130 entered service with the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] in 1956, followed by Australia and many other nations. [CLS] [SEP] During its years of service, the Hercules family has participated in numerous military, civilian and humanitarian aid operations. [CLS] [SEP] In 2007, the C-130 became the fifth aircraft to mark 50 years of continuous service with its original primary customer, which for the C-130 is the United States Air Force. [CLS] [SEP] The C-130 Hercules is the longest continuously produced military aircraft at over 60 years, with the updated Lockheed Martin C-130J Super Hercules currently being produced. [CLS] [SEP]
