The Bell UH-1 Iroquois (nicknamed "Huey") is a utility military helicopter powered by a single turboshaft engine, with two-bladed main and tail rotors. [CLS] [SEP] The first member of the prolific Huey family, it was developed by Bell Helicopter to meet a 1952 US Army requirement for a medical evacuation and utility helicopter, and first flew in 1956. [CLS] [SEP] The UH-1 was the first turbine-powered helicopter produced for the United States military, and more than 16,000 have been built since 1960.The Iroquois was originally designated HU-1, hence the Huey nickname, which has remained in common use, despite the official redesignation to UH-1 in 1962. [CLS] [SEP] The UH-1 first saw service in combat operations during the Vietnam War, with around 7,000 helicopters deployed. [CLS] [SEP] The Bell 204 and 205 are Iroquois versions developed for the civil market. [CLS] [SEP]
