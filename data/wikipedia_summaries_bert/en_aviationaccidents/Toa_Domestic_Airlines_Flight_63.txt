Toa Domestic Airlines Flight 63, registration JA8764, was a NAMC YS-11A-217 en route from Chitose Airport near Sapporo, Japan to Hakodate Airport. [CLS] [SEP] On July 3, 1971, the plane left Chitose Airport on a scheduled flight at 08:30. [CLS] [SEP] After arriving in Hakodate airspace, the plane was descending below 1800 metres when it crashed at 09:05 into the south face of Yokotsudake (Yokotsu Mountain). [CLS] [SEP] All 64 passengers and four crew on board perished. [CLS] [SEP] The cause of the crash was determined to be pilot error that followed strong winds pushing the plane off course. [CLS] [SEP] The Aircraft Accident Investigation Commission (AAIC) was formed soon after the crash. [CLS] [SEP]
