[CLS] Temple Neuf (French pronunciation: ​[tɑ̃pl nœf], meaning "New Temple") is a Protestant church in Metz, France. [SEP] [CLS] It is located on place de la Comédie (next to Opéra-Théâtre), at the center of the Jardin d'Amour on the southwestern edge of Île du Petit-Saulcy, which is surrounded by the Moselle. [SEP] [CLS] The church was built by Glod, with the first stone being laid on 25 November 1901 (when Metz was a part of the German Empire), following plans by architect Conrad Wahn. [SEP] [CLS] It was inaugurated as the Neue evangelische Kirche on 14 May 1904 in the presence of Wilhelm II, German Emperor, and his wife Augusta Victoria of Schleswig-Holstein. [SEP] [CLS] The church is an example of Romanesque Revival architecture, and has been a monument historique of France since 1930. [SEP] [CLS]
