[CLS] The 2014 Iquique earthquake struck off the coast of Chile on 1 April, with a moment magnitude of 8.2, at 20:46 local time (23:46 UTC). [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter of the earthquake was approximately 95 kilometres (59 mi) northwest of Iquique. [SEP] [CLS] The mainshock was preceded by a number of moderate to large shocks and was followed by a large number of moderate to very large aftershocks, including a M7.7 event on 3 April. [SEP] [CLS] The megathrust earthquake triggered a tsunami of up to 2.11 metres (6.9 ft) that hit Iquique at 21:05 local time (00:05 UTC, 2 April). [SEP] [CLS] Similar-sized tsunamis were also reported to have hit the coasts of Pisagua and Arica. [SEP] [CLS]
