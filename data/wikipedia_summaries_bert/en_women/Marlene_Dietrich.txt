[CLS] Marie Magdalene "Marlene" Dietrich (, German: [maʁˈleːnə ˈdiːtʁɪç] (listen); 27 December 1901 – 6 May 1992) was a German-American actress and singer. [SEP] [CLS] Her career spanned from the 1910s to the 1980s.In 1920s Berlin, Dietrich performed on the stage and in silent films. [SEP] [CLS] Her performance as Lola-Lola in The Blue Angel (1930), brought her international acclaim and a contract with Paramount Pictures. [SEP] [CLS] Dietrich starred in many Hollywood films such as Morocco (1930), Shanghai Express (1932) and Desire (1936). [SEP] [CLS] She successfully traded on her glamorous persona and "exotic" looks, and became one of the highest-paid actresses of the era. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout World War II, she was a high-profile entertainer in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] Although she still made occasional films after the war such as Witness for the Prosecution (1957), Dietrich spent most of the 1950s to the 1970s touring the world as a marquee live-show performer. [SEP] [CLS] Dietrich was known for her humanitarian efforts during the war, housing German and French exiles, providing financial support and even advocating their American citizenship. [SEP] [CLS] For her work on improving morale on the front lines during the war, she received several honors from the United States, France, Belgium and Israel. [SEP] [CLS] In 1999, the American Film Institute named Dietrich the ninth greatest female screen legend of classic Hollywood cinema. [SEP] [CLS]
