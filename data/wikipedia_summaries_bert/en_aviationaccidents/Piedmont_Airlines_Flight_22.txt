The 1967 Hendersonville mid-air collision occurred when a Piedmont Airlines Boeing 727-22 and a twin-engine Cessna 310 collided on July 19, 1967 over Hendersonville, North Carolina, USA. [CLS] [SEP] Both aircraft were destroyed and all passengers and crew were killed, including John T. [CLS] [SEP] McNaughton, an advisor to Robert McNamara. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft were both operating under instrument flight rules (IFR) and in radio contact with the Asheville control tower, though on different frequencies. [CLS] [SEP] The accident investigation carried out in the aftermath of the crash was the first major investigation ever conducted by the newly created National Transportation Safety Board. [CLS] [SEP] A review of the investigation, conducted 39 years after the crash, upheld the original findings that had placed primary responsibility on the Cessna pilot. [CLS] [SEP]
