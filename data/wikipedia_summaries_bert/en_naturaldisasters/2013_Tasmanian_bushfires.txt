The 2013 Tasmanian bushfires were a series of bushfires which occurred in south-eastern Tasmania, Australia, between November 2012 and late April 2013. [CLS] [SEP] The fires burnt approximately 20,000 hectares (49,000 acres) of mixed resident land and native forest. [CLS] [SEP] It was predicted early on that the 2012-13 fire season had the potential to be worse than usual. [CLS] [SEP] High fuel loads coupled with dry, hot and windy conditions pointed to potential danger. [CLS] [SEP] The Tasmania Fire Service implemented a media campaign intended to increase community preparedness and awareness of what to do if bushfires threatened, however, nobody predicted that the fire season would last for almost 6 months, a duration unprecedented in recorded Tasmanian history.
