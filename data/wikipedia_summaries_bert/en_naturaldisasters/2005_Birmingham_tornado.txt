[CLS] The 2005 Birmingham tornado was one of the strongest tornadoes recorded in the United Kingdom in nearly 30 years, occurring on 28 July 2005 in the suburbs of Birmingham. [SEP] [CLS] It formed on a day when thunderstorms were expected to develop across the Midlands and eastern England. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado struck at approximately 14:30 BST in the Sparkbrook area and also affected King's Heath, Moseley and Balsall Heath as it carved a 7 kilometre-long path through the city. [SEP] [CLS] Its main effects were felt on Ladypool Road, which bore the brunt of the damage. [SEP] [CLS] Ladypool Primary School was extensively damaged and lost its distinctive Martin & Chamberlain tower. [SEP] [CLS] The adjacent St Agatha's Church also suffered some damage. [SEP] [CLS] Christ Church (consecrated in 1867), on the corner of Dolobran Road and Grantham Road in Sparkbrook, was also damaged and has now been demolished.The Met Office and TORRO (The Tornado and Storm Research Organisation) estimated that the tornado had a general T4 rating on the TORRO scale, with a short spell as a T5 tornado, which would indicate wind speeds between 137 and 186 mph (220 and 299 km/h), equivalent to an F2 or F3 tornado on the Fujita scale. [SEP] [CLS] There were no fatalities, although there were approximately 19 injuries, three of which were reported to be serious. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado uprooted an estimated 1,000 trees, removed the roofs of buildings, picked up and deposited cars and caused other damage during its short existence.
