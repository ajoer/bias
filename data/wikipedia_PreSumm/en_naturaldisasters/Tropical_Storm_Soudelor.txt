[CLS] Tropical Storm Soudelor, known in the Philippines as Tropical Depression Gorio, was a weak tropical cyclone that produced deadly flooding in the Philippines, China and Vietnam in July 2009. [SEP] [CLS] Forming out of an area of low pressure on July 9, Soudelor failed to maintain deep convection around its center for the duration of its existence. [SEP] [CLS] On July 10, the depression brushed the northern Philippines and intensified into a tropical storm on July 11. [SEP] [CLS] Later that day, the storm crossed the Leizhou Peninsula. [SEP] [CLS] The last public advisory from the JMA was issued the following day after Soudelor made landfall in southern China. [SEP] [CLS] In the Philippines, Soudelor produced severe flooding that killed one person and resulted in the issuance of a state of calamity. [SEP] [CLS] The storm later killed 15 people in southern China after a group of hikers were washed away in a flash flood on Hainan Island. [SEP] [CLS] In Vietnam, rainfall up to 250 mm (9.8 in) caused widespread flooding. [SEP] [CLS] Lightning triggered by the storm killed two people in the country. [SEP] [CLS]
