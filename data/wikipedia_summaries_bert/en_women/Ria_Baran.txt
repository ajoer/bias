[CLS] Ria Baran (2 November 1922 in Dortmund, Germany – 12 November 1986) was a German pair skater. [SEP] [CLS] She skated together with Paul Falk and twice became World champion and in 1952 Olympic champion. [SEP] [CLS] She was one of the oldest female figure skating Olympic champions. [SEP] [CLS] Ria Baran married Paul Falk during her active international figure skating. [SEP] [CLS] Therefore she is sometime listed as Ria Baran-Falk or Ria Falk. [SEP] [CLS] The pair skated for the club Düsseldorfer EG and had no coach. [SEP] [CLS] Until 1951 Ria Baran and Paul Falk were not able to participate in international competitions because Germany was excluded from the international sport after World War II. [SEP] [CLS] They were the first couple which performed side by side double jumps and they also invented the Lasso-Lift. [SEP] [CLS] Baran and Falk were never defeated in amateur competition. [SEP] [CLS] Between 1950 and 1952 Ria Baran was voted 3 times running as the female athlete of the year in Germany. [SEP] [CLS] After winning the Olympics in 1952 they turned pro and worked for Holiday on Ice. [SEP] [CLS] Baran later worked as a secretary. [SEP] [CLS]
