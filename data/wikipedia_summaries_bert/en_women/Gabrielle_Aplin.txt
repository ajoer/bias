Gabrielle Ann Aplin (born 10 October 1992) is an English singer-songwriter. [CLS] [SEP] After amassing a following for her acoustic music covers on YouTube, Aplin signed a recording deal with Parlophone in February 2012. [CLS] [SEP] She rose to prominence the following November when she was selected to record the soundtrack for a John Lewis television commercial with a cover of Frankie Goes to Hollywood's "The Power of Love", which went on to top the UK Singles Chart. [CLS] [SEP] Her debut album, English Rain, was released in May 2013 to positive reviews. [CLS] [SEP] It debuted at number two on the UK Album Chart and led to more singles: "Please Don't Say You Love Me", "Panic Cord", "Home" and "Salvation". [CLS] [SEP] English Rain has since been certified Gold in the UK, selling over 100,000 copies. [CLS] [SEP] Aplin's second album, Light Up the Dark, was released in September 2015, followed by the release of her third album, Dear Happy, in January 2020. [CLS] [SEP] She has also released multiple extended plays throughout her career. [CLS] [SEP]
