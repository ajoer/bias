[CLS] The 1987 Svaneti avalanches was a series of massive avalanches in the northwest Caucasus highlands of the then-Soviet republic of Georgia, which hit, in January 1987, the country's Svaneti province, namely the Mestia and Lentekhi districts. [SEP] [CLS] The avalanches were brought about by unprecedented snowfall which lasted for 46 days, falling 16 meters thick at several places. [SEP] [CLS] From 9 to 31 January 1987 330 avalanches were registered. [SEP] [CLS] The mountainous villages such as Chuberi, Ushguli, Mulakhi, Kala, and Khaishi were heavily hit. [SEP] [CLS] The village of Zhamushi was completely buried in snow, with 26 killed. [SEP] [CLS] In total, 105 people died in the disaster. [SEP] [CLS] More than 2,000 houses were damaged and about 8,500 people had to be resettled. [SEP] [CLS] The total damage caused was about $300 million. [SEP] [CLS]
