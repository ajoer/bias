[CLS] Janica Kostelić (Croatian pronunciation: [janitsa kǒstelitɕ]; born 5 January 1982) is a former World Cup alpine ski racer and four-time Olympic gold medalist from Croatia. [SEP] [CLS] In addition to the Olympics, she won five gold medals at the World Championships. [SEP] [CLS] In World Cup competition, she won thirty individual races, three overall titles, three slalom titles, and four (unofficial) combined titles. [SEP] [CLS] Kostelic's accomplishments in professional skiing have led some commentators, writers, and fellow ski racers to regard her as the greatest female ski racer of all time. [SEP] [CLS] Kostelić is the only woman to win four gold medals in alpine skiing at the Winter Olympics (in 2002 and 2006), and the only woman to win three alpine skiing gold medals in one Olympics (2002). [SEP] [CLS] Kostelić was the World Cup overall champion in 2001, 2003 and 2006. [SEP] [CLS] On 15 January 2006, Kostelić became the third woman in World Cup history (after Swede Pernilla Wiberg and Austrian Petra Kronberger) to win World Cup races in all of the sport's five disciplines. [SEP] [CLS] On 5 February 2006 Kostelić became the second female skier (after Petra Kronberger) to win all five disciplines in one season. [SEP] [CLS] In the summer of 2006, she decided to not compete in the 2007 season, due to chronic knee and back pain. [SEP] [CLS] She had endured ten knee surgeries and thyroid surgery during her career. [SEP] [CLS] Following a year away from competition, Kostelić announced her retirement from racing in April 2007, at just 25 years old.Since 2016, Kostelić has been the State Secretary for Science, Education and Sports in the Croatian Government.
