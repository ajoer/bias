[CLS] Olga Costa (b. [SEP] [CLS] Leipzig, August 28, 1913 – Guanajuato, June 28, 1993) was a painter and cultural promoter who immigrated to Mexico from Germany when she was twelve. [SEP] [CLS] She began to study art at the Academy of San Carlos but left after only three months to help support her family. [SEP] [CLS] However, she met her husband, artist José Chávez Morado during this time. [SEP] [CLS] Her marriage to him involved her in Mexico's cultural and intellectual scene and she began to develop her ability to paint on her own, with encouragement from her husband. [SEP] [CLS] She had numerous exhibitions of her work in Mexico, with her work also sent to be sold in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] She was also involved in the founding and development of various galleries, cultural societies and three museums in the state of Guanajuato. [SEP] [CLS] She received the Premio Nacional de Ciencias y Artes among others for her work. [SEP] [CLS]
