Shrimad Rajchandra (11 November 1867 – 9 April 1901) was a Jain poet, mystic, philosopher, scholar and reformer. [CLS] [SEP] Born near Morbi, he claimed to have recollection of his past lives at the age of seven. [CLS] [SEP] He performed Avadhāna, a memory retention and recollection test that gained him popularity, but he later discouraged it in favour of his spiritual pursuits. [CLS] [SEP] He wrote much philosophical poetry including Atma Siddhi. [CLS] [SEP] He also wrote many letters and commentaries and translated some religious texts. [CLS] [SEP] He is best known for his teachings on Jainism and his spiritual guidance to Mahatma Gandhi. [CLS] [SEP]
