[CLS] Hurricane Gordon was a long-lived and catastrophic late-season hurricane of the 1994 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The twelfth and final tropical cyclone of the season, Gordon formed in the southwestern Caribbean on November 8. [SEP] [CLS] Without strengthening, the storm made landfall on Nicaragua. [SEP] [CLS] Later on November 10, the storm began to strengthen as it tracked further from land, and it quickly strengthened into Tropical Storm Gordon, the seventh named storm that season. [SEP] [CLS] Gordon also made landfalls in Jamaica and Cuba while a minimal tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] It entered the southwestern Atlantic while resembling a subtropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] By the time it entered the Gulf of Mexico, the storm was fully tropical again. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Gordon later crossed the Florida Keys, and turning to the northeast it made landfall in Fort Myers, Florida. [SEP] [CLS] Gordon strengthened after it re-entered the Atlantic Ocean, becoming a hurricane on November 17. [SEP] [CLS] It briefly threatened North Carolina while turning to the northwest, although it turned to the south and weakened. [SEP] [CLS] Gordon deteriorated into a tropical depression and struck Florida again at that intensity on November 20. [SEP] [CLS] It turned to the north and dissipated the next day over South Carolina. [SEP] [CLS] Gordon first caused flooding in northern Costa Rica that destroyed 700 houses and caused $30 million in damage. [SEP] [CLS] There were six deaths in the country and an additional two deaths in neighboring Panama. [SEP] [CLS] Upon affecting Jamaica, the storm was responsible $11.8 million in damage and four deaths. [SEP] [CLS] Damage was heaviest in Haiti, after a prolonged southwesterly flow dropped 14 in (360 mm) of rainfall in a 24â€‘hour period. [SEP] [CLS] The rains resulted in extensive mudslides and flooding that disrupted transportation and damaged 10,800 houses, with another 3,500 destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] There were 1,122 deaths in the country, partially due to deforested hills, and damage was estimated at $50 million. [SEP] [CLS] In neighboring Dominican Republic, there were five additional deaths, as well as flooding near its capital. [SEP] [CLS] In Cuba, Gordon caused $100 million in damage, and 5,906 houses were damaged or destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Due to large-scale evacuations, there were only two deaths in the country. [SEP] [CLS] In Florida, the storm caused $400 million in damage (1994 USD, equivalent to about $600 million in 2018), much of it agricultural, and there were eleven deaths, eight of them direct. [SEP] [CLS] Gordon later affected North Carolina with high waves, causing beach erosion and destroying five houses. [SEP] [CLS] Overall damage was $594 million due to Gordon (1994 USD, equivalent to about $0.9 billion in 2018). [SEP] [CLS] International governments and agencies through the United Nations sent relief supplies and monetary assistance to Haiti, following Gordon's devastating impact there. [SEP] [CLS] American soldiers were already stationed in the country to restore ousted Jean-Bertrand Aristide to presidency. [SEP] [CLS] The troops helped in rescues and worked to restore a damaged road between Port-au-Prince and Jacmel. [SEP] [CLS] Despite the deaths and damage, the name was not retired following the season. [SEP] [CLS]
