The 1679 Armenia earthquake (also called Yerevan earthquake or Garni earthquake) took place on June 4 in the Yerevan region of Armenia, then part of the Persian Empire.Numerous buildings were destroyed as a result of the earthquake. [CLS] [SEP] In Yerevan most notable structures were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The Yerevan Fortress was destroyed completely, so were the following churches: Poghos-Petros, Katoghike, Zoravor and the Gethsemane Chapel.Furthermore, the nearby Kanaker village was completely destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] The classical Hellenistic Temple of Garni also collapsed. [CLS] [SEP] Among many churches and monasteries that were reduced to ruins were Havuts Tar, Saint Sargis Monastery of Ushi, Hovhannavank, Geghard, and Khor Virap. [CLS] [SEP]
