SANSA Flight 32, a CASA C-212 Aviocar on its way to Palmar Sur Airport crashed into the Cerro Cedral, a mountain in Costa Rica after takeoff from Juan Santamaria International Airport in San Jose on January 15, 1990. [CLS] [SEP] All 20 passengers and 3 crew on board died in the crash. [CLS] [SEP] Pilot fatigue was determined to be the cause. [CLS] [SEP] At the time, it was the worst accident involving a CASA C-212 Aviocar and is currently the fifth worst accident. [CLS] [SEP] It is also currently the second worst accident in Costa Rica.
