Mazie Keiko Hirono (; Japanese name: 広野 慶子, Hirono Keiko; born November 3, 1947) is a Japanese-born American politician serving since 2013 as the junior United States Senator from Hawaii. [CLS] [SEP] She is a member of the Democratic Party. [CLS] [SEP] Hirono served as a member of the Hawaii House of Representatives from 1981 to 1995 and as Hawaii's ninth lieutenant governor from 1994 to 2002, under Ben Cayetano. [CLS] [SEP] The Democratic nominee for governor of Hawaii in 2002, Hirono was defeated by Republican Linda Lingle. [CLS] [SEP] From 2007 to 2013, she served as a member of the United States House of Representatives for Hawaii's 2nd congressional district. [CLS] [SEP] Hirono is the first elected female senator from Hawaii, the first Asian-American woman elected to the Senate, the first U.S. [CLS] [SEP] senator born in Japan, and the nation's first Buddhist senator. [CLS] [SEP] She considers herself a non-practicing Buddhist and is often cited with Hank Johnson as the first Buddhist to serve in the United States Congress. [CLS] [SEP] She is the third woman to be elected to Congress from Hawaii (after Patsy Mink and Pat Saiki). [CLS] [SEP] In 2012, Hirono was the Democratic nominee for the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Senate seat being vacated by the retirement of Daniel Akaka. [CLS] [SEP] Hirono won the election, defeating Lingle in a landslide, 63% to 37%. [CLS] [SEP] She was sworn in on January 3, 2013, by Vice President Joe Biden. [CLS] [SEP] Hirono was the only person of Asian ancestry serving in the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Senate from 2013 until 2017, when senators Tammy Duckworth and Kamala Harris were sworn in, representing Illinois and California, respectively. [CLS] [SEP] Although Brian Schatz joined the Senate a week before Hirono, following the death of Daniel Inouye, making him Hawaii's senior senator, Hirono's three terms in the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] House of Representatives make her the dean, or longest-serving member overall, of Hawaii's congressional delegation.
