[CLS] The name Yanyan was used to name one tropical cyclone in the northwestern Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] The name was contributed by Hong Kong and is a given name (Chinese: 欣欣; pinyin: Xīnxīn; Cantonese Yale: Yān-yān; lit.: 'Joyful'). [SEP] [CLS] It was part of a series of reduplicated female names from Hong Kong, like Tingting, Shanshan and Lingling. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Yanyan (T0301, 01W), 2003During the 2004 ESCAP/WMO Typhoon Committee, Hong Kong requested to retire the name. [SEP] [CLS] Instead, the name was replaced by Dolphin to be used in future seasons. [SEP] [CLS] The name Dolphin has been used to name two tropical cyclones in the northwestern Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] The name, also contributed by Hong Kong, refers to the Chinese white dolphin, a mascot in Kong Kong. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Dolphin (2008) (T0822, 27W, Ulysses) Typhoon Dolphin (2015) (T1507, 07W) — one of the earliest super typhoons of the season. [SEP] [CLS]
