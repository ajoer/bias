Luís Vaz de Camões (Portuguese pronunciation: [luˈiʒ ˈvaʒ dɨ kaˈmõjʃ]; sometimes rendered in English as Camoens or Camoëns, e.g. [CLS] [SEP] by Byron in English Bards and Scotch Reviewers, ; c. [CLS] [SEP] 1524 or 1525 – 20 June [O.S. [CLS] [SEP] 10 June] 1580) is considered Portugal's and the Portuguese language's greatest poet. [CLS] [SEP] His mastery of verse has been compared to that of Shakespeare, Vondel, Homer, Virgil and Dante. [CLS] [SEP] He wrote a considerable amount of lyrical poetry and drama but is best remembered for his epic work Os Lusíadas (The Lusiads). [CLS] [SEP] His collection of poetry The Parnasum of Luís de Camões was lost in his lifetime. [CLS] [SEP] The influence of his masterpiece Os Lusíadas is so profound that Portuguese is sometimes called the "language of Camões". [CLS] [SEP]
