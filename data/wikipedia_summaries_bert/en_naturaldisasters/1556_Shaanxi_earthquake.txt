[CLS] The 1556 Shaanxi earthquake, or Huaxian earthquake (simplified Chinese: 华县大地震; traditional Chinese: 華縣大地震; pinyin: Huáxiàn Dàdìzhèn), or Jiajing earthquake (Chinese: 嘉靖大地震; pinyin: Jiājìng Dàdìzhèn), is the deadliest earthquake in recorded history. [SEP] [CLS] According to imperial records, approximately 830,000 people lost their lives.It occurred on the morning of 23 January 1556 in Shaanxi, during the Ming dynasty. [SEP] [CLS] More than 97 counties in the provinces of Shaanxi, Shanxi, Henan, Gansu, Hebei, Shandong, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangsu and Anhui were affected. [SEP] [CLS] Buildings were damaged slightly in the cities of Beijing, Chengdu and Shanghai. [SEP] [CLS] An 840-kilometre-wide (520 mi) area was destroyed, and in some counties as much as 60% of the population was killed. [SEP] [CLS] Most of the population in the area at the time lived in yaodongs, artificial caves in loess cliffs; these collapsed in great numbers, causing many casualties. [SEP] [CLS]
