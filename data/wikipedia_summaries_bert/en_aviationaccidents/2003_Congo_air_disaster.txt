The 2003 Congo air disaster occurred on 8 May 2003, when a cargo door of an Ilyushin Il-76 owned by Ukrainian Cargo Airways accidentally opened mid-flight while the aircraft was flying over Mbuji-Mayi with more than 160 people on board. [CLS] [SEP] The flight was conducting a civilian transport from Kinshasa in the southwest of the country to Lubumbashi in the southeast of the country. [CLS] [SEP] 17 people were confirmed dead by Congolese Government, while officials estimated that 60 may have lost their lives. [CLS] [SEP] Survivors even claimed a higher number of fatalities, claiming that as many as 200 people were sucked out to their deaths. [CLS] [SEP]
