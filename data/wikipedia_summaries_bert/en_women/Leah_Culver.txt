Leah Culver (born November 5, 1982) is a computer programmer and startup founder. [CLS] [SEP] Culver is a 2006 computer science graduate of the University of Minnesota. [CLS] [SEP] After graduating, she worked at the start ups iLoop Mobile and Instructables. [CLS] [SEP] In 2007, she co-founded the micro-blogging site Pownce, acquired by Six Apart in December 2008. [CLS] [SEP] She left Six Apart in February 2010. [CLS] [SEP] She co-founded Convore, focused on real time chat in 2011. [CLS] [SEP] Convore pivoted into Grove, a chat service for workgroups, which she sold to Revolution Systems in October 2012. [CLS] [SEP] That year, Culver was featured in the documentary The Startup Kids. [CLS] [SEP] From 2013 to 2016, Culver worked as an engineer at Dropbox. [CLS] [SEP] Leah is currently CTO of Breaker, which she co-founded with Erik Berlin in December 2016. [CLS] [SEP] She has authored OAuth and oEmbed API specifications. [CLS] [SEP] In 2019, Culver was a part of Girl Geek X's 30 Female CTOs to Watch list. [CLS] [SEP] Culver was named among the Most Influential Women in Web 2.0 by Fast Company magazine in November 2008. [CLS] [SEP]
