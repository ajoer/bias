Tropical Storm Delta was a late-forming tropical storm of the 2005 Atlantic hurricane season which struck the Canary Islands as a strong extratropical storm, causing significant damage and then crossed over Morocco before dissipating. [CLS] [SEP] It was the twenty-sixth tropical or subtropical storm of the season, making the 2005 season the first to record so many storms. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Delta, like most late-season storms, developed out of an extratropical low. [CLS] [SEP] The storm gradually gained tropical characteristics and was briefly a subtropical storm on November 22. [CLS] [SEP] Delta moved erratically for a few days before moving towards the Canary Islands. [CLS] [SEP] It became extratropical just before it passed to the north of the archipelago. [CLS] [SEP]
