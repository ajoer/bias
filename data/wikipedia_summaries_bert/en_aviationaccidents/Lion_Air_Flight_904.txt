Lion Air Flight 904 was a scheduled domestic passenger flight from Husein Sastranegara International Airport in Bandung to Ngurah Rai International Airport in Bali, Indonesia. [CLS] [SEP] On 13 April 2013, the Boeing 737-800 operating the flight crashed into water short of runway while on final approach to land. [CLS] [SEP] All 101 passengers and 7 crew on board survived the accident. [CLS] [SEP] At 15:10 local time (07:10 UTC), the aircraft crashed approximately 0.6 nautical miles (1.1 km) short of the seawall protecting the threshold of Runway 09. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft's fuselage broke into two and 46 people were injured, 4 of them seriously.Among the findings contained in the final investigation report was that the crew continued the approach in adverse weather conditions beyond the point at which the approved procedure would have required to abort the landing. [CLS] [SEP] The subsequent attempt to go around was made too late to avoid the impact with the sea. [CLS] [SEP] It was also determined that there were no issues with the aircraft and all systems were operating normally. [CLS] [SEP]
