The name Earl has been used for seven tropical cyclones in the Atlantic Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Earl (1980) — drifted over the central Atlantic Ocean without affecting land. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Earl (1986) — drifted over the central Atlantic Ocean without affecting land. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Earl (1992) — headed towards eastern Florida then veered away. [CLS] [SEP] No damage was reported. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Earl (1998) — struck the Florida Panhandle, causing approximately US$79 million in damage and 3 fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Earl (2004) — traveled across the Windward Islands, then dissipated. [CLS] [SEP] Its remnants became Hurricane Frank in the eastern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Earl (2010) — a strong, long-lived category 4 that affected most of the United States east coast and Canada. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Earl (2016) — struck Belize as a minimal hurricane, then made a second landfall near Veracruz, Mexico as a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] The storm caused US$250 million in damage and 106 deaths. [CLS] [SEP]
