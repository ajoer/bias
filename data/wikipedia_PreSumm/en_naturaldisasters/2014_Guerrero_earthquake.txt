[CLS] The 2014 Guerrero earthquake occurred with a moment magnitude of 7.2 that hit the state of Guerrero, close to Acapulco, Mexico, on 18 April at 14:27:26. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter occurred 265 kilometers southwest of Mexico City and at a depth of 24 kilometers. [SEP] [CLS] Thrust motion at shallow depths is what caused the earthquake. [SEP] [CLS] This was broadly consistent with a slip on or near the Guerrero Seismic Gap, a boundary between the Cocos and North American plates along the Pacific Coast approximately 200 kilometers long. [SEP] [CLS] The shaking was felt in states as far away as Puebla and Tlaxcala. [SEP] [CLS]
