Pertinax (; Publius Helvius Pertinax; 1 August 126 – 28 March 193) was a Roman soldier and politician who ruled as Roman emperor for the first three months of 193. [CLS] [SEP] He succeeded Commodus to become the first emperor during the tumultuous Year of the Five Emperors. [CLS] [SEP] Born the son of a freed slave, Pertinax became an officer in the army. [CLS] [SEP] He fought in the Roman–Parthian War of 161–166, where his success led him to be promoted to higher-ranking positions in both the military and political spheres. [CLS] [SEP] He achieved the rank of provincial governor and urban prefect. [CLS] [SEP] He was a member of the Roman Senate, serving at the same time as the historian Cassius Dio. [CLS] [SEP] Following the death of Commodus, Pertinax was acclaimed emperor. [CLS] [SEP] He attempted to institute several reform measures, although the short length of his time as emperor prevented the success of those attempts. [CLS] [SEP] One of those reforms, the restoration of discipline among the Praetorian Guard, led to conflict that eventually culminated in Pertinax's assassination by the Guard. [CLS] [SEP] Pertinax would be deified by the emperor Septimius Severus. [CLS] [SEP] His historical reputation has largely been a positive one, in line with Cassius Dio's assessment. [CLS] [SEP]
