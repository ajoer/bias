Afghanistan Blizzard was a fierce blizzard that struck Afghanistan on the 10th of January 2008. [CLS] [SEP] Temperatures fell to a low of -30 C, with up to 180 centimetres of snow in the more mountainous regions, killing at least 926 people. [CLS] [SEP] Aid organizations and foreign troops distributed several tons of clothing, blankets, food and fuel in provinces throughout the country and in remote, mountainous villages. [CLS] [SEP] The hospitals performed frostbite amputations on at least 100 people across the country, as many walked barefoot in the freezing cold mud and snow. [CLS] [SEP] The weather also claimed more than 100,000 sheep and goats, and nearly 315,000 cattle died. [CLS] [SEP]
