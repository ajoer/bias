Continental Airlines Flight 11, registration N70775, was a Boeing 707 aircraft which exploded in the vicinity of Centerville, Iowa, while en route from O'Hare Airport, Chicago, Illinois, to Kansas City, Missouri, on May 22, 1962. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft crashed in a clover field near Unionville, in Putnam County, Missouri, killing all 45 crew and passengers on board. [CLS] [SEP] The investigation determined the cause of the crash was a suicide bombing committed as insurance fraud. [CLS] [SEP]
