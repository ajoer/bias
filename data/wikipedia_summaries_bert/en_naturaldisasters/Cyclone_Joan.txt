Severe Tropical Cyclone Joan was an intense tropical cyclone that ravaged areas of Western Australia. [CLS] [SEP] Forming out of a tropical low on 30 November 1975, Joan gradually intensified as it tracked towards the west. [CLS] [SEP] After attaining Category 5 intensity on 5 December, the storm abruptly began to track southward and accelerated. [CLS] [SEP] The following day, the cyclone reached its peak intensity with winds of 215 km/h (130 mph) and a barometric pressure of 915 hPa (27.02 inHg). [CLS] [SEP] Joan only weakened slightly before making landfall in the vicinity of Mundabullagana. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone rapidly weakened after landfall before dissipating over Western Australia on 12 December. [CLS] [SEP] Although no fatalities resulted from Joan, the cyclone caused widespread destruction across areas of Western Australia. [CLS] [SEP] In Port Hedland, most of the structures were damaged as a result of the storm. [CLS] [SEP] In addition, 1,000 homes in the town were damaged or destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Further inland, record rainfall caused widespread flooding in many areas, causing many rivers and streams to overflow. [CLS] [SEP] Due to the severity of the storm, the name Joan was retired after the season. [CLS] [SEP]
