[CLS] Hurricane Pauline was one of the deadliest Pacific hurricanes to make landfall in Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] The sixteenth tropical storm, eighth hurricane, and seventh major hurricane of the 1997 Pacific hurricane season, Pauline developed out of a tropical wave from Africa on September 16, 1997, moving across South America and into the Pacific Ocean. [SEP] [CLS] On October 5, the depression intensified into a tropical storm early the next day and by October 7, Pauline had reached hurricane intensity. [SEP] [CLS] It initially moved eastward, then turned northwestward and quickly strengthened to reach peak winds of 135 mph (215 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] It paralleled the Mexican coastline a short distance offshore before weakening and making landfall near Puerto Angel, on October 9, and dissipated the next day. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane Pauline produced torrential rainfall along the Mexican coastline, peaking at 32.62 inches (930 mm) in Puente Jula. [SEP] [CLS] Intense flooding and mudslides in some of the poorest areas of Mexico killed between 230 and 500 people, making it one of the deadliest Eastern Pacific storms in recorded history. [SEP] [CLS] The passage of the hurricane destroyed or damaged tens of thousands of houses, leaving around 300,000 people homeless and causing $447.8 million in damage (1997 USD; $713 million 2020 USD).
