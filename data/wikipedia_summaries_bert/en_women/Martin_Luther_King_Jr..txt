Martin Luther King Jr. [CLS] [SEP] (born Michael King Jr.; January 15, 1929 â€“ April 4, 1968) was an American Christian minister and activist who became the most visible spokesperson and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968. [CLS] [SEP] King is best known for advancing civil rights through nonviolence and civil disobedience, inspired by his Christian beliefs and the nonviolent activism of Mahatma Gandhi. [CLS] [SEP] King led the 1955 Montgomery bus boycott and later became the first president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC). [CLS] [SEP] As president of the SCLC, he then led an unsuccessful 1962 struggle against segregation in Albany, Georgia, and helped organize the nonviolent 1963 protests in Birmingham, Alabama. [CLS] [SEP] He helped organize the 1963 March on Washington, where he delivered his famous "I Have a Dream" speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial. [CLS] [SEP] On October 14, 1964, King won the Nobel Peace Prize for combating racial inequality through nonviolent resistance. [CLS] [SEP] In 1965, he helped organize the Selma to Montgomery marches. [CLS] [SEP] In his final years, he expanded his focus to include opposition towards poverty, capitalism, and the Vietnam War. [CLS] [SEP] FBI Director J. [CLS] [SEP] Edgar Hoover considered him a radical and made him an object of the FBI's COINTELPRO from 1963 on. [CLS] [SEP] FBI agents investigated him for possible communist ties, recorded his extramarital liaisons and reported on them to government officials, and, in 1964, mailed King a threatening anonymous letter, which he interpreted as an attempt to make him commit suicide.King was planning a national occupation of Washington, D.C., to be called the Poor People's Campaign, when he was assassinated on April 4 in Memphis, Tennessee. [CLS] [SEP] His death was followed by riots in many U.S. [CLS] [SEP] cities. [CLS] [SEP] Allegations that James Earl Ray, the man convicted of killing King, had been framed or acted in concert with government agents persisted for decades after the shooting. [CLS] [SEP] King was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal. [CLS] [SEP] Martin Luther King Jr. [CLS] [SEP] Day was established as a holiday in cities and states throughout the United States beginning in 1971; the holiday was enacted at the federal level by legislation signed by President Ronald Reagan in 1986. [CLS] [SEP] Hundreds of streets in the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] have been renamed in his honor, and a county in Washington was rededicated for him. [CLS] [SEP] The Martin Luther King Jr. [CLS] [SEP] Memorial on the National Mall in Washington, D.C., was dedicated in 2011. [CLS] [SEP]
