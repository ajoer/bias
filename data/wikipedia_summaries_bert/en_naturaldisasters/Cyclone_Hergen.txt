Cyclone Hergen was an intense European windstorm that moved across Northern Europe during mid December 2011. [CLS] [SEP] It was first noted over the central North Atlantic Ocean by the Met Office. [CLS] [SEP] It then later reached peak intensity just northwest of Ireland and then crossed the north of Scotland hours later. [CLS] [SEP] It hung around the coast of Norway for the next week before being absorbed by another strong windstorm named Joachim. [CLS] [SEP] Hergen was not the first storm to strike Western Europe during the last week as Cyclone Friedhelm brought widespread damage and very strong winds on 8 December. [CLS] [SEP] Hergen was followed by Joachim a few days later, then Oliver/Patrick (Cato/Dagmar) during the Christmas holiday. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone Ulli then brought major damage and flooding to Northern Europe during the early part of January 2012. [CLS] [SEP]
