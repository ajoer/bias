[CLS] The 1855 Bursa earthquake occurred on 28 February, with an estimated magnitude of 7.02±0.64 Mw A devastating precursor quake that took place in Kemalpaşa, a town of Bursa, Turkey caused severe destruction all over Bursa and other neighboring cities. [SEP] [CLS] 300 people died and thousands of homes and workplaces were wrecked, and some of the historical monuments and buildings including mosques collapsed. [SEP] [CLS] Subsequently, fire spread out in the city, which increased the death toll. [SEP] [CLS] On 11 April 1855, an aftershock of the 28 February earthquake was recorded as Mw 6.65±0.33. [SEP] [CLS] This aftershock affected the region from Gemlik to Mudanya. [SEP] [CLS] 1,300 people died. [SEP] [CLS] Gökmen-zâde Seyyid el-Hâcî Hüseyin Rıfat Efendî Bursavî wrote about these earthquakes in his book İşaret-numa, written in the Ottoman Turkish language. [SEP] [CLS]
