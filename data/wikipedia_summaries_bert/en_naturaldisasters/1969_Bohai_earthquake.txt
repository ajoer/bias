The 1969 Bohai earthquake occurred on July 18, 1969, at 13:24 local time. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located in the Bohai Sea, off the coast of Shandong Province, China. [CLS] [SEP] The magnitude of this earthquake is Ms 7.4. [CLS] [SEP] Areas of maximal intensity were mainly distributed around the estuary of the Yellow River. [CLS] [SEP] Ground cracks and sand boils were reported. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake could be felt in Liaoning, Hebei, Beijing, Tianjin, Shanxi, Shandong, and Jiangsu. [CLS] [SEP] Ten people were reported dead. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake caused ground surface subsidences of about 15.1 cm ~ 15.8 cm in the Yellow River Delta region. [CLS] [SEP] The mechanism of this earthquake was of dextral strike-slip faulting. [CLS] [SEP] Although it was located near the Tancheng-Lujiang fault zone (郯庐断裂带), some researchers think that this earthquake was dominated by the seaward extending part of the Huanghekou-Liaocheng seismotectonic zone (黄河口-聊城地震构造带).
