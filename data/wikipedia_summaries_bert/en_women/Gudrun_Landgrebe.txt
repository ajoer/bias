[CLS] Gudrun Landgrebe (German: [ɡuː.dʁʊn ˈlantˌɡʁeːbə] (listen); born 20 June 1950) is a German actress. [SEP] [CLS] Landgrebe was born in Göttingen, grew up in Bochum, and attended theatre school in Cologne from 1968 until 1971. [SEP] [CLS] In 1971 she made her debut at Stadttheater Bielefeld. [SEP] [CLS] She also appeared in Heimat as the character Klärchen Sisse. [SEP] [CLS] Since 1981 she has frequently appeared in German movies - her first film role was in the comedy Dabbel Trabbel. [SEP] [CLS] In 1983 she gained international fame in the Robert van Ackerens movie Die flambierte Frau. [SEP] [CLS] Further movies such as Istvan Szabo's Oberst Redl (1985), Yerma (1984) and Burkhard Driest's Annas Mutter (1984) followed. [SEP] [CLS] In 1985 she appeared alongside Kevin McNally in Liliana Cavani's The Berlin Affair. [SEP] [CLS] She appeared in 1998 in the TV movie Opera Ball with Heiner Lauterbach and Franka Potente. [SEP] [CLS] In 1997 she was in Rossini – oder die mörderische Frage, wer mit wem schlief, with Mario Adorf, Veronica Ferres, Heiner Lauterbach, Jan Josef Liefers and Götz George, directed by Helmut Dietl. [SEP] [CLS] Landgrebe has been married to Dr. [SEP] [CLS] Ulrich von Nathusius since June 2001 and lives in Hunsrück. [SEP] [CLS]
