[CLS] Severe Tropical Cyclone Narelle was a powerful Category 4 tropical cyclone in early January 2013 that brought light rains to areas in South Australia suffering from a drought and heat wave. [SEP] [CLS] On 4 January, a tropical low pressure developed within a monsoon trough over the Timor Sea. [SEP] [CLS] Over the following several days, the system gradually tracked westward and intensified, being classified Tropical Cyclone Narelle on 8 January. [SEP] [CLS] Turning southward into a region of low wind shear, Narelle intensified into a severe tropical cyclone on 9 January. [SEP] [CLS] Over the following two days, the cyclone's structure fluctuated, temporarily featuring an eye, before it maintained its organisation and intensified further on 11 January. [SEP] [CLS] The storm attained its peak intensity later on 11 January as a Category 4 cyclone with winds of 195 km/h (120 mph). [SEP] [CLS] The following day, Narelle passed approximately 330 km (205 mi) northwest of Exmouth as it moved on a south-southwesterly course. [SEP] [CLS] The system steadily weakened and ultimately fell below tropical cyclone strength on 15 January well to the west of Geraldton. [SEP] [CLS] Early in the storm's existence, Narelle brought strong winds, heavy rain, and high winds to many areas in Indonesia. [SEP] [CLS] More than 10,000 homes were flooded and many others were damaged by thunderstorms. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 14 people were killed by the storm, and 17 others were listed as missing. [SEP] [CLS] In Western Australia, scattered strong thunderstorms caused minor damage and produced a possible tornado. [SEP] [CLS]
