Laocoön (; Ancient Greek: Λαοκόων, IPA: [laokóɔːn]), the son of Acoetes, is a figure in Greek and Roman mythology and the Epic Cycle. [CLS] [SEP] He was a Trojan priest who was attacked, with his two sons, by giant serpents sent by the gods. [CLS] [SEP] The story of Laocoön has been the subject of numerous artists, both in ancient and in more contemporary times. [CLS] [SEP]
