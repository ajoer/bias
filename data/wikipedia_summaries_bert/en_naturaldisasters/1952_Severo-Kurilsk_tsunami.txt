[CLS] The 1952 Severo-Kurilsk earthquake struck off the coast of the Kamchatka Peninsula. [SEP] [CLS] The 9.0 Mw earthquake triggered a major tsunami that hit Severo-Kurilsk, Kuril Islands, Sakhalin Oblast, Russian SFSR, USSR, on 4 November 1952 at about 5 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] This led to the destruction of many settlements in Sakhalin Oblast and Kamchatka Oblast, while the main impact struck the town of Severo-Kurilsk. [SEP] [CLS] It was the sixth most powerful earthquake on record, and to date, the most powerful earthquake in Russian history.The tsunami was generated by a major earthquake in the Pacific Ocean, 130 kilometers (81 mi) from the shore of Kamchatka, with an estimated magnitude of 9.0. [SEP] [CLS] There were three waves about 15–18 meters (49–59 ft) high. [SEP] [CLS] After the earthquake the majority of the Severo-Kurilsk citizens fled to the surrounding hills, where they escaped the first wave. [SEP] [CLS] However, most of them returned to the town and were killed by the second wave. [SEP] [CLS] The third wave was minor. [SEP] [CLS] According to the authorities, out of a population of 6,000 people, 2,336 died. [SEP] [CLS] The survivors were evacuated to continental Russia. [SEP] [CLS] The settlement was then rebuilt in another location.Earthquakes continue to happen in the area including a series of three M8 events that occurred at ~60km depth on 25-27 March 2020.
