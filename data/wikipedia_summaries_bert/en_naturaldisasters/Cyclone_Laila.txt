Cyclonic Storm Laila (IMD designation: BOB 01, JTWC designation: 01B) was the first cyclonic storm to affect southeastern India in May since the 1990 Andhra Pradesh cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] The first tropical cyclone of the 2010 North Indian Ocean cyclone season, Laila developed on May 17 in the Bay of Bengal from a persistent area of convection. [CLS] [SEP] Strengthening as it tracked northwestward, it became a severe cyclonic storm on May 19. [CLS] [SEP] The next day, Laila made landfall in Andhra Pradesh, and it later dissipated over land. [CLS] [SEP] It caused flooding and damage along its path. [CLS] [SEP] Laila is an Urdu/Arabic name, meaning Night. [CLS] [SEP] It was the worst storm to hit Andhra Pradesh in the last 14 years.
