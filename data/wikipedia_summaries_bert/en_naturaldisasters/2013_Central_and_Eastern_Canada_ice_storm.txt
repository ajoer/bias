[CLS] The December 2013 North American storm complex was a significant storm complex that had all sorts of severe weather, including a winter storm, a crippling ice storm and a tornado outbreak that impacted the central and eastern portions of Canada, parts of the Central Great Plains, the Southern United States, and the northeastern United States from 20 to 23 December 2013. [SEP] [CLS] Formed in the South Central United States, the storm headed across the Great Plains towards Canada into Atlantic Canada and northeastern United States where the storm dissipated on 23 December 2013. [SEP] [CLS] The storm produced freezing rain and snow to the affected areas which caused massive damage to electric power transmission and trees. [SEP] [CLS] The storm resulted in 27 deaths, loss of power to over a million residents and over $200 million in damages. [SEP] [CLS] The storm produced similar conditions to the ice storm of 1998 which affected similar areas. [SEP] [CLS]
