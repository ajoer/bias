Typhoon Chataan, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Gloria, was the deadliest natural disaster in the history of Chuuk, a state in the Federated States of Micronesia (FSM). [CLS] [SEP] The typhoon formed on June 28, 2002, near the FSM, and for several days it meandered while producing heavy rainfall across the region. [CLS] [SEP] On Chuuk, the highest 24-hour precipitation total was 506 mm (19.9 in), which was greater than the average monthly total. [CLS] [SEP] The rain produced floods up to 1.5 m (4.9 ft) deep, causing landslides across the island that killed 47 people. [CLS] [SEP] There was also one death on nearby Pohnpei, and damage in the FSM totaled over $100 million.After affecting the FSM, Chataan began a northwest track as an intensifying typhoon. [CLS] [SEP] Its eye passed just north of Guam on July 4, though the eyewall moved across the island and dropped heavy rainfall. [CLS] [SEP] Totals were highest in southern Guam, peaking at 536 mm (21.1 in). [CLS] [SEP] Flooding and landslides from the storm severely damaged or destroyed 1,994 houses. [CLS] [SEP] Damage on the island totaled $60.5 million, and there were 23 injuries. [CLS] [SEP] The typhoon also affected Rota in the Northern Marianas Islands with gusty winds and light rainfall. [CLS] [SEP] Typhoon Chataan attained its peak intensity of 175 km/h (110 mph) on July 8. [CLS] [SEP] It weakened while turning to the north, and after diminishing to a tropical storm Chataan struck eastern Japan on July 10. [CLS] [SEP] High rainfall, peaking at 509 mm (20.0 in), flooded 10,270 houses. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in Japan totaled about $500 million. [CLS] [SEP] The name Chataan means "rainy day" in the Chamorro language, which is spoken on Guam. [CLS] [SEP] The Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) named the storm Gloria while the typhoon was in the vicinity of the country. [CLS] [SEP]
