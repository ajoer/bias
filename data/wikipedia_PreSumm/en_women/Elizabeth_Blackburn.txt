[CLS] Elizabeth Helen Blackburn, (born 26 November 1948) is an Australian-American Nobel laureate who is the former President of the Salk Institute for Biological Studies. [SEP] [CLS] Previously she was a biological researcher at the University of California, San Francisco, who studied the telomere, a structure at the end of chromosomes that protects the chromosome. [SEP] [CLS] In 1984, Blackburn co-discovered telomerase, the enzyme that replenishes the telomere, with Carol W. [SEP] [CLS] Greider. [SEP] [CLS] For this work, she was awarded the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, sharing it with Greider and Jack W. [SEP] [CLS] Szostak, becoming the first Australian woman Nobel laureate. [SEP] [CLS] She also worked in medical ethics, and was controversially dismissed from the Bush Administration's President's Council on Bioethics. [SEP] [CLS]
