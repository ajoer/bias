[CLS] Typhoon Rananim, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Karen, was the strongest typhoon to make landfall on the Chinese province of Zhejiang since 1956. [SEP] [CLS] It formed on August 6, 2004, intensifying into a tropical storm on August 8. [SEP] [CLS] Rananim gradually intensified, initially moving northward before turning to the northwest and attaining typhoon status. [SEP] [CLS] After developing a small eye, the typhoon attained peak winds of 150 km/h (90 mph) as it passed between Taiwan and Okinawa. [SEP] [CLS] On August 12, Rananim moved ashore in China, and it dissipated three days later. [SEP] [CLS] The name Rananim means "hello" in the Chuukese language.Impact outside of China was minimal and largely limited to heavy rains, although one death was reported in Taiwan. [SEP] [CLS] In the country, strong winds and heavy rainfall left heavy damage near the coast, as well as to farms further inland. [SEP] [CLS] Rananim destroyed 64,300 houses and damaged another 125,000.
