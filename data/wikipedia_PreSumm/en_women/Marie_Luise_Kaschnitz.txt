[CLS] Marie Luise Kaschnitz (born Marie Luise von Holzing-Berslett; 31 January 1901 – 10 October 1974) was a German short story writer, novelist, essayist and poet. [SEP] [CLS] She is considered to be one of the leading post-war German poets.She was born in Karlsruhe. [SEP] [CLS] She married archaeologist Guido Freiherr Von Kaschnitz-Weinberg (the author of The Mediterranean Foundations of Ancient Art) in 1925, and travelled with him on archaeological expeditions. [SEP] [CLS] She received high praise for her short stories, many of which were inspired by events in her life, complemented by her personal reminiscences. [SEP] [CLS] These stories were collected in books such as Orte and Engelsbrücke. [SEP] [CLS] She enjoyed travel greatly and her tales make use of diverse settings. [SEP] [CLS] They are thoughtful in nature, rather than eventful, often dealing with particular stages in a woman's life or a relationship. [SEP] [CLS] Her main collection is Lange Schatten ("Long Shadows"). [SEP] [CLS] Her favorite story was 1961's "Das dicke Kind". [SEP] [CLS] Her post-war essay collection in Menschen und Dinge 1945 established her reputation in Germany. [SEP] [CLS] Her poems dealt with the war and the early post-war period, often expressing a yearning for a peaceful past, but also hope for the future. [SEP] [CLS] In the volume Dein Schweigen – meine Stimme she dealt with the death of her husband. [SEP] [CLS] After 1960 she became influenced by Pablo Neruda. [SEP] [CLS] She briefly taught poetics at the University of Frankfurt. [SEP] [CLS] She was a member of PEN. [SEP] [CLS] She won many prizes, including the Georg Büchner Prize in 1955 and the Roswitha Prize in 1973. [SEP] [CLS] She was nominated for the 1967 Nobel Prize in Literature. [SEP] [CLS] She died, aged 73, in Rome. [SEP] [CLS] The Marie Luise Kaschnitz Prize is named in her honor. [SEP] [CLS]
