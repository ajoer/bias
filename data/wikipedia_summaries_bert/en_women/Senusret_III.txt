Khakaure Senusret III (also written as Senwosret III or the hellenised form, Sesostris III) was a pharaoh of Egypt. [CLS] [SEP] He ruled from 1878 BC to 1839 BC during a time of great power and prosperity, and was the fifth king of the Twelfth Dynasty of the Middle Kingdom. [CLS] [SEP] He was a great pharaoh of the Twelfth Dynasty and is considered to be, perhaps, the most powerful Egyptian ruler of the dynasty. [CLS] [SEP] Consequently, he is regarded as one of the sources for the legend about Sesostris. [CLS] [SEP] His military campaigns gave rise to an era of peace and economic prosperity that reduced the power of regional rulers and led to a revival in craftwork, trade, and urban development. [CLS] [SEP] Senusret III was among the few Egyptian kings who were deified and honored with a cult during their own lifetime. [CLS] [SEP]
