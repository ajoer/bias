[CLS] Tropical Storm Kelly, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Daling, was a weak but destructive tropical storm that struck the Philippines in June 1981. [SEP] [CLS] A tropical disturbance organized into a tropical depression east of the Philippines on June 28. [SEP] [CLS] It tracked westward, reaching tropical storm on June 30 before hitting the central Philippines. [SEP] [CLS] Kelly weakened over the islands, but re-strengthened over the South China Sea, nearly attaining typhoon status on July 2. [SEP] [CLS] The system passed through the southern Hainan Island on July 3. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone then crossed the Gulf of Tonkin as a weakening tropical storm, and made landfall in northern Vietnam on July 4 as a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Kelly dissipated two days later inland. [SEP] [CLS] Tropical Storm Kelly was the worst storm to affect the Philippines in eight months. [SEP] [CLS] Nine towns in an 6,920 km (4,300 mi) area near the Mayon Volcano sustained flooding. [SEP] [CLS] Over 1,450 km (900 mi) of railways lines were washed out. [SEP] [CLS] At least 800 homes were destroyed while 3,845 ha (9,500 acres) of rice fields were ruined. [SEP] [CLS] More than 3,600 individuals were displaced. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, damage totaled to $7.8 million (1981 USD). [SEP] [CLS]
