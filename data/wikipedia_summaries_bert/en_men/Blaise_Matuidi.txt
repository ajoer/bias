Blaise Matuidi (French pronunciation: ​[blɛz matɥidi]; born 9 April 1987) is a French professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Major League Soccer club Inter Miami and the France national team. [CLS] [SEP] Matuidi began his football career playing for amateur clubs in the Île-de-France region, such as US Fontenay-sous-Bois and CO Vincennois. [CLS] [SEP] In 1999, he was selected to attend the prestigious Clairefontaine academy. [CLS] [SEP] After departing Clairefontaine, Matuidi joined semi-professional club Créteil and spent three years developing in the club's youth academy. [CLS] [SEP] In 2004, he signed with professional club Troyes and made his professional debut in the 2004–05 season. [CLS] [SEP] After three seasons with Troyes, Matuidi joined Saint-Étienne. [CLS] [SEP] With Saint-Étienne, he played European football for the first time after participating in the 2008–09 edition of the UEFA Cup. [CLS] [SEP] In the 2009–10 season, he was named first-choice captain under manager Alain Perrin. [CLS] [SEP] In July 2011, after four seasons with Saint-Étienne, Matuidi transferred to Paris Saint-Germain on a three-year deal. [CLS] [SEP] He won 16 domestic honours during his time with the club, including four consecutive Ligue 1 titles. [CLS] [SEP] In 2017, he joined Italian side Juventus, winning a domestic double in his first season, followed by two more consecutive league titles over the next two seasons, as well as the 2018 Supercoppa Italiana. [CLS] [SEP] He joined MLS club Inter Miami in the summer of 2020. [CLS] [SEP] Matuidi is a former France youth international, having represented his nation at under-19 and under-21 level. [CLS] [SEP] In August 2010, Matuidi was called up to the senior team for the first time under new manager Laurent Blanc. [CLS] [SEP] He made his international debut in September 2010 in a UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying match against Bosnia and Herzegovina and has since represented his nation at two UEFA European Football Championships and two FIFA World Cups, winning a runners-up medal at Euro 2016, and a winner's medal at the 2018 FIFA World Cup.
