[CLS] The Blizzard of 1999 was a strong winter snowstorm which struck the Midwestern United States and portions of central and eastern Canada, hitting hardest in Iowa, Wisconsin, Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Ohio, Ontario, and Quebec dumping as much as 60 centimetres (2 ft) of snow in many areas. [SEP] [CLS] Chicago received a recorded 21.6 in (55 cm). [SEP] [CLS] The storm hit just after New Year's Day, between January 2 and January 4, 1999. [SEP] [CLS] Travel was severely disrupted throughout the areas and the cities of Chicago and Toronto were also paralyzed.
