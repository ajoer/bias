The 1903 Jamaica hurricane devastated Martinique, Jamaica, and the Cayman Islands in August 1903. [CLS] [SEP] The second tropical cyclone of the season, the storm was first observed well east of the Windward Islands on August 6. [CLS] [SEP] The system moved generally west-northwestward and strengthened into a hurricane on August 7. [CLS] [SEP] It struck Martinique early on August 9, shortly before reaching the Caribbean Sea. [CLS] [SEP] Later that day, the storm became a major hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] Early on August 11, it made landfall near Morant Point, Jamaica, with winds of 120 mph (195 km/h), with would be the hurricane's maximum sustained wind speed. [CLS] [SEP] Early on the following day, the storm brushed Grand Cayman at the same intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The system weakened before landfall near Playa del Carmen, Quintana Roo, early on August 13, with winds of 100 mph (155 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] The system emerging into the Gulf of Mexico early on August 14 after weakening while crossing the Yucatán Peninsula, but failed to re-strengthen. [CLS] [SEP] Around 00:00 UTC on August 16, the cyclone made landfall north of Tampico, Tamaulipas, with winds of 80 mph (130 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane soon weakened to a tropical storm and dissipated over San Luis Potosí late on August 16. [CLS] [SEP] In Martinique, hundreds of homes were deroofed in Fort-de-France, while about 5,000 people were left homeless in the villages of Fond, Fourniols, La Haye, Recluce, and Tivoli, all of which were established after the eruption of Mount Pelée in 1902. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane also left extensive damage to crops and eight fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] In Jamaica, several communities were completely or nearly destroyed, including Manchioneal, Port Antonio, and Port Maria. [CLS] [SEP] Thousands of homes also suffered damage in the capital city of Kingston. [CLS] [SEP] Banana crops were devastated so severely that many growers were forced into bankruptcy. [CLS] [SEP] Numerous ships were wrecked, particularly on the north coast of the island. [CLS] [SEP] There were at least 65 deaths and about $10 million (1903 USD) in damage. [CLS] [SEP] In the Cayman Islands, more than 200 houses and seven of eight churches on Grand Cayman were destroyed or heavily damaged. [CLS] [SEP] Of the 23 ships in the harbor, only the Governor Blake survived. [CLS] [SEP] Most of the crews on board those ships were reported killed, but loss of life onshore was minimal. [CLS] [SEP] The storm also caused heavy damage on the Yucatán Peninsula. [CLS] [SEP] Many ships were wrecked and communications were cut off in several places. [CLS] [SEP] In the Tampico area, there was considerable damage to the port and many ships were sunk or driven ashore. [CLS] [SEP] Much of the land between Tampico and Cárdenas in San Luis Potosí was submerged due to flooding. [CLS] [SEP] In all, the storm is believed to have killed at least 149 people. [CLS] [SEP]
