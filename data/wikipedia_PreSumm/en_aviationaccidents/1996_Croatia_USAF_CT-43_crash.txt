[CLS] On April 3, 1996, a United States Air Force Boeing CT-43A (Flight IFO-21) crashed on approach to Dubrovnik, Croatia, while on an official trade mission. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft, a Boeing 737-200 originally built as T-43A navigational trainer and later converted into a CT-43A executive transport aircraft, was carrying United States Secretary of Commerce Ron Brown and 34 other people, including The New York Times Frankfurt bureau chief Nathaniel C. [SEP] [CLS] Nash. [SEP] [CLS] While attempting an instrument approach to Dubrovnik Airport, the airplane crashed into a mountainside. [SEP] [CLS] An Air Force Technical Sergeant survived the initial impact, but died en route to a hospital. [SEP] [CLS] Everyone else on board died at the scene of the crash.The aircraft was operated by the 76th Airlift Squadron of the 86th Airlift Wing, based at Ramstein Air Base in Germany. [SEP] [CLS] Unlike civilian 737s, the military CT-43A version was not equipped with a flight data recorder nor a cockpit voice recorder. [SEP] [CLS]
