Near the end of 2012, a massive storm complex developed that produced both a tornado outbreak and a blizzard across the southern and eastern United States. [CLS] [SEP] On Christmas Day 2012 (December 25), a tornado outbreak occurred across Southern United States. [CLS] [SEP] This severe weather/tornado event affected the United States Gulf Coast and southern East Coast over a two-day span. [CLS] [SEP] It occurred in conjunction with a much larger winter storm event that brought blizzard conditions to much of the interior United States. [CLS] [SEP] In total, 31 tornadoes were confirmed by the National Weather Service in five states from Texas to North Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] All but one of the tornadoes that occurred during the outbreak touched down on December 25, with the other occurring the following day in North Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] Two of the tornadoes were destructive enough to be rated EF3 on the Enhanced Fujita Scale. [CLS] [SEP] At least 16 people died as a result of the related blizzard, and thousands were without power. [CLS] [SEP] There were 63 preliminary local storm reports received for tornadoes, including 60 in four states on December 25 alone.
