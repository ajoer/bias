Baikal Airlines Flight 130 was a scheduled domestic passenger flight from Irkutsk to Moscow that crashed on 3 January 1994. [CLS] [SEP] The plane involved in the crash was a Tupolev Tu-154 operated by Russian airline Baikal Airlines. [CLS] [SEP] The plane was carrying 115 passengers and 9 crew members and was en route to Moscow when one of the engines suddenly burst into flames. [CLS] [SEP] The crew then tried to return to Irkutsk, but lost control of the plane and crashed into a dairy farm near the town of Mamony. [CLS] [SEP] All 124 people on board and one person on the ground were killed in the crash. [CLS] [SEP]
