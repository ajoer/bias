The 1918 San Fermín earthquake, also known as the Puerto Rico earthquake of 1918, struck the island of Puerto Rico at 10:14:42 local time on October 11. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake measured 7.1 on the moment magnitude scale and IX (Violent) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [CLS] [SEP] The mainshock epicenter occurred off the northwestern coast of the island, somewhere along the Puerto Rico Trench. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake triggered a tsunami with waves measured that swept the west coast of the island. [CLS] [SEP] The combined effects of the earthquake and tsunami made it one of the worst natural disasters that have struck the island. [CLS] [SEP] The losses resulting from the disaster were approximately 76–118 casualties and $4–29 million in property damage. [CLS] [SEP]
