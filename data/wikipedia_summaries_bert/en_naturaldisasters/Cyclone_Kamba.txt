The 2007–08 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season was the third most active tropical cyclone season, along with the 1985–86 season and behind the 1993–94 season and the 2018–19 season, with twelve named tropical cyclones developing in the region. [CLS] [SEP] It began on November 15, 2007, and ended on April 30, 2008, with the exception for Mauritius and the Seychelles, which ended May 15. [CLS] [SEP] These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical cyclones form in the basin, which is west of 90°E and south of the Equator. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical cyclones in this basin are monitored by the Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre in Réunion. [CLS] [SEP]
