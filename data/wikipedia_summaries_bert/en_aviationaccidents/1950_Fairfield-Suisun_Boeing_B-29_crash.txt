Northeast of San Francisco, California, on 5 August 1950, a nuclear-capable United States Air Force Boeing B-29 Superfortress bomber carrying a Mark 4 nuclear bomb crashed shortly after takeoff from Fairfield-Suisun Air Force Base with 20 men on board. [CLS] [SEP] Twelve men were killed in the crash, including the commander of the 9th Bombardment Wing, Brigadier General Robert F. [CLS] [SEP] Travis, and another seven were killed on the ground when the aircraft exploded. [CLS] [SEP] The base was later renamed for Travis. [CLS] [SEP]
