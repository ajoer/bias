[CLS] Rosenhöhe (or Rosenhoehe) is one of the 21 boroughs of Offenbach am Main, Germany. [SEP] [CLS] It is the city’s southern-most borough and the second smallest by population with only 1,396 inhabitants. [SEP] [CLS] Until 2019 it was one of the nine officially recognized boroughs. [SEP] [CLS] When in July 2019 the city council approved a bill to fully divide the city's area into boroughs, Rosenhöhe became the second largest by area after Bieber. [SEP] [CLS] The total area is 7,08 km² (2,73 sq mi). [SEP] [CLS] Rosenhöhe borders the boroughs of Carl-Ulrich-Siedlung and Lauterborn in the north, Buchrain in the west and Tempelsee in the east. [SEP] [CLS] The southern border of Rosenhöhe is shared with the Offenbach city limits, bordering the towns of Neu-Isenburg, Dreieich and Heusenstamm. [SEP] [CLS] Most of its area is occupied by forests and is a landscape preservation area. [SEP] [CLS] Built-up area is only found in the northern tip of the borough. [SEP] [CLS]
