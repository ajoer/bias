Tropical Cyclone Bingiza was the only named storm to make landfall in the inactive 2010–11 South-West Indian Ocean cyclone season. [CLS] [SEP] The second of three storms, Bingiza developed on February 9 to the northeast of Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP] For a few days it meandered generally southwestward, failing to intensify significantly. [CLS] [SEP] On February 12, the storm began a steady westward track as environmental conditions became more favorable. [CLS] [SEP] In a 24‑hour period, Bingiza developed from a moderate tropical storm into an intense tropical cyclone with a well-defined eye. [CLS] [SEP] After attaining peak 10–minute sustained winds of 155 km/h (100 mph), the cyclone moved ashore in northeastern Madagascar on February 14 and quickly weakened as it crossed the country. [CLS] [SEP] Bingiza emerged into the Mozambique Channel as a weak tropical disturbance, and it turned southward to move across western Madagascar. [CLS] [SEP] Bingiza attained tropical storm status before making its final landfall near Morondava, degenerating into a remnant low on February 17, and dissipating two days later. [CLS] [SEP] Across Madagascar, the cyclone killed 34 people and injured 13 others. [CLS] [SEP] High winds destroyed 25,464 houses, which left 25,845 people homeless. [CLS] [SEP] Bingiza first affected the Masoala National Park, destroying half of a village and leaving it isolated. [CLS] [SEP] High winds and heavy rainfall left over 500 km2km (195 mi2) of damaged crops, which increased food prices and threatened the economic livelihoods of the affected people. [CLS] [SEP]
