[CLS] Damon Graham Devereux Hill, (born 17 September 1960) is a British former racing driver. [SEP] [CLS] He is the son of Graham Hill, and, along with Nico Rosberg, one of only two sons of a Formula One world champion to also win the title. [SEP] [CLS] He started racing on motorbikes in 1981, and after minor success moved on to single-seater racing cars. [SEP] [CLS] Despite progressing steadily up the ranks to the International Formula 3000 championship by 1989, and despite being competitive, he never won a race at that level.Hill became a test driver for the Formula One title-winning Williams team in 1992. [SEP] [CLS] He was promoted to the Williams race team the following year after Riccardo Patrese's departure and took the first of his 22 victories at the 1993 Hungarian Grand Prix. [SEP] [CLS] During the mid-1990s, Hill was Michael Schumacher's main rival for the Formula One Drivers' Championship, which saw the two clash several times on and off the track. [SEP] [CLS] Their collision at the 1994 Australian Grand Prix gave Schumacher his first title by a single point. [SEP] [CLS] Hill became champion in 1996 with eight wins, but was dropped by Williams for the following season. [SEP] [CLS] He went on to drive for the less competitive Arrows and Jordan teams, and in 1998 gave Jordan their first win.Hill retired from racing after the 1999 season. [SEP] [CLS] He has since launched several businesses and has made appearances playing the guitar with celebrity bands. [SEP] [CLS] In 2006, he became president of the British Racing Drivers' Club, succeeding Sir Jackie Stewart. [SEP] [CLS] Hill stepped down from the position in 2011 and was succeeded by Derek Warwick. [SEP] [CLS] He presided over the securing of a 17-year contract for Silverstone to hold Formula One races, which enabled the circuit to see extensive renovation work. [SEP] [CLS] Hill currently works as part of the Sky Sports F1 broadcasting team. [SEP] [CLS]
