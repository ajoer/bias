[CLS] [SEP] She was first based in Switzerland, where she received international attention when she appeared in the first performance in German of Menotti's The Consul, in Basel, in 1951. [CLS] [SEP] In 1952, Borkh became a member of the Deutsche Oper Berlin and the Bavarian State Opera in Munich. [CLS] [SEP] She appeared at leading opera houses in Europe and the Americas, and at festivals such as Bayreuth and Salzburg. [CLS] [SEP] Trained first as an actress, she was admired for both singing and stage presence, especially in the Richard Strauss roles Salome and Elektra. [CLS] [SEP] She also performed in contemporary opera, such as the premiere of Josef Tal's Ashmedai in Hamburg in 1971. [CLS] [SEP] Her recordings include complete operas and recitals. [CLS] [SEP] Borkh was awarded the Hans-Reinhart-Ring, the highest honour for theatre professionals in Switzerland. [CLS] [SEP]
