Bafétimbi Gomis (French pronunciation: ​[bafetimbi ɡɔmis]; born 6 August 1985) is a French professional footballer who plays as a striker for Saudi club Al-Hilal. [CLS] [SEP] Gomis played youth football with Saint-Étienne before making his professional debut for the club in 2004, also spending time on loan at Troyes in Ligue 2. [CLS] [SEP] In 2009, he joined Saint-Étienne's Derby du Rhone rivals Lyon for a fee of €13 million. [CLS] [SEP] Over five seasons at the Stade Gerland, he played 244 official matches and scored 95 goals, winning the Coupe de France and the Trophée des Champions in 2012. [CLS] [SEP] In 2014, he moved to Premier League club Swansea City on a free transfer. [CLS] [SEP] After a loan back in France at Marseille, he signed for Galatasaray, where he was top scorer as they won the Süper Lig in 2018. [CLS] [SEP] Gomis made 12 appearances for the French national team from 2008 to 2014, scoring three goals. [CLS] [SEP] He was a member of the squad for UEFA Euro 2008. [CLS] [SEP]
