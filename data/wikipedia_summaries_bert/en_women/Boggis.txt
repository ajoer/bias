Boogie is a repetitive, swung note or shuffle rhythm, "groove" or pattern used in blues which was originally played on the piano in boogie-woogie music. [CLS] [SEP] The characteristic rhythm and feel of the boogie was then adapted to guitar, double bass, and other instruments. [CLS] [SEP] The earliest recorded boogie-woogie song was in 1916. [CLS] [SEP] By the 1930s, Swing bands such as Benny Goodman, Glenn Miller, Tommy Dorsey and Louis Jordan all had boogie hits. [CLS] [SEP] By the 1950s, boogie became incorporated into the emerging rockabilly and rock and roll styles. [CLS] [SEP] In the late 1980s and the early 1990s country bands released country boogies. [CLS] [SEP] Today, the term "boogie" usually refers to dancing to pop, disco, or rock music. [CLS] [SEP]
