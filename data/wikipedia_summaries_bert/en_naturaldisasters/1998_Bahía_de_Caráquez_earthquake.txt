[CLS] The 1998 Bahía de Caráquez earthquake occurred on August 4 at 13:59 local time (18:59 UTC) with a magnitude Mw 7.2. [SEP] [CLS] The epicenter was located at 10 km north of Bahía de Caráquez, about 190 km NNW of Guayaquil, and about 215 km W of Quito, Ecuador. [SEP] [CLS] The intensity in Bahía de Caráquez reached MM VIII. [SEP] [CLS] In Bahía de Caráquez, electricity, telephone, and water services disrupted, and many buildings were damaged. [SEP] [CLS] It was felt strongly in Guayaquil and Quito and could be felt in much of Ecuador and in Cali, Colombia. [SEP] [CLS] An Mw 5.4 foreshock occurred 1 hour and 24 minutes before the main shock and hence alerted many people. [SEP] [CLS] The Nazca Plate is subducting beneath the South American Plate near the Ecuadorian coast. [SEP] [CLS] This earthquake was a shallow thrust earthquake in this subduction zone.Since March 1997, Wawa Pichincha, 12 km to the west of Quito, began a period of unrest. [SEP] [CLS] Since May 1998, a seismic swarm occurred at Wawa Pichincha. [SEP] [CLS] Three days after the Bahía de Caráquez earthquake, on August 7, 1998, a moderate phreatic eruption occurred in Wawa Pichincha. [SEP] [CLS] Wawa Pichincha remained active in 1999, and in 2000 its activities became more moderate. [SEP] [CLS]
