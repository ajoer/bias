Franciszek Karpiński (4 October 1741 – 16 September 1825) was the leading sentimental Polish poet of the Age of Enlightenment. [CLS] [SEP] He is particularly remembered for his religious works later rendered as hymns and carols. [CLS] [SEP] He is also considered one of the most original Polish writers of the early partitions. [CLS] [SEP] In his native Poland he was cherished during the Polish Romantic Period of the early 19th century. [CLS] [SEP]
