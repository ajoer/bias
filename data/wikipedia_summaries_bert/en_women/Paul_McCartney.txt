Sir James Paul McCartney (born 18 June 1942) is an English singer, songwriter, musician, composer, and record and film producer who gained worldwide fame as co-lead vocalist and bassist for the Beatles. [CLS] [SEP] His songwriting partnership with John Lennon remains the most successful in history. [CLS] [SEP] After the group disbanded in 1970, he pursued a solo career and formed the band Wings with his first wife, Linda, and Denny Laine. [CLS] [SEP] A self-taught musician, McCartney is proficient on bass, guitar, keyboards, and drums. [CLS] [SEP] He is known for his melodic approach to bass-playing (mainly playing with a plectrum), his versatile and wide tenor vocal range (spanning over four octaves), and his eclecticism (exploring styles ranging from pre-rock and roll pop to classical and electronica). [CLS] [SEP] McCartney began his career as a member of the Quarrymen in 1957, which evolved into the Beatles in 1960. [CLS] [SEP] Starting with the 1967 album Sgt. [CLS] [SEP] Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band, he gradually became the Beatles' de facto leader, providing the creative impetus for most of their music and film projects. [CLS] [SEP] His Beatles songs "And I Love Her" (1964), "Yesterday" (1965), "Eleanor Rigby" (1966) and "Blackbird" (1968) rank among the most covered songs in history.In 1970, McCartney debuted as a solo artist with the album McCartney. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the 1970s, he led Wings, one of the most successful bands of the decade, with more than a dozen international top 10 singles and albums. [CLS] [SEP] McCartney resumed his solo career in 1980. [CLS] [SEP] Since 1989, he has toured consistently as a solo artist. [CLS] [SEP] In 1993, he formed the music duo the Fireman with Youth of Killing Joke. [CLS] [SEP] Beyond music, he has taken part in projects to promote international charities related to such subjects as animal rights, seal hunting, land mines, vegetarianism, poverty, and music education. [CLS] [SEP] McCartney is one of the most successful composers and performers of all time.
