The 1917 Samoa earthquake occurred on June 26 at 05:49 UTC. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter was located in the southwest of the Samoan Islands. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake had a magnitude of Mw 8.5, or Ms 8.4, one of the strongest earthquakes ever recorded in this region.Many subsidences were reported. [CLS] [SEP] In the mountains, masses of rocks were hurled down. [CLS] [SEP] In Apia, violent shaking lasted for about a minute and half. [CLS] [SEP] In American Samoa, the LDS Church in Pago Pago and the Catholic Church in Leone were partly demolished. [CLS] [SEP] A tsunami was triggered by the earthquake, which affected both American Samoa and German Samoa, which was then under Allied occupation. [CLS] [SEP] The tsunami reached a maximum of 40 feet. [CLS] [SEP] The maximum range of the tsunami in Apia was about 80 cm. [CLS] [SEP] Many houses were destroyed, and a bridge was washed away in Palauli. [CLS] [SEP] The tsunami was also recorded in Honolulu and on the west coast of the United States. [CLS] [SEP] Just on May 1, 1917, a large earthquake of magnitude Ms 8.0 occurred in the Kermadec Islands region. [CLS] [SEP] The tsunami caused by the earthquake in May also hit the Samoan Islands.The earthquake occurred to the southwest of the Samoa Islands, along the Tonga Plate boundary. [CLS] [SEP] The focal mechanism of this earthquake was unclear. [CLS] [SEP] This earthquake and the 2009 event were not thought to represent the same repeating process in a same location.
