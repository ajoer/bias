[CLS] The Tête de l'Obiou accident was an airliner crash in France on 13 November 1950. [SEP] [CLS] Curtiss-Reid Flying Service had been operating a scheduled service between Paris and Rome since 1945. [SEP] [CLS] On 13 November 1950, a Paris-bound Douglas C-54B-1-DC aircraft crashed on the Grande Tête de l'Obiou mountain, 48 km (30 mi) south of Grenoble. [SEP] [CLS] All 51 passengers and 7 crew were killed. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was 50 miles (80 km) off course. [SEP] [CLS] Flight attendant Helen Johnston's body was missing for weeks until it was found in a crevasse on the mountain. [SEP] [CLS]
