[CLS] Tropical Storm Agatha was a weak but catastrophic tropical cyclone that brought widespread floods to much of Central America, and was the deadliest tropical cyclone in the eastern Pacific since Hurricane Pauline in 1997. [SEP] [CLS] The first storm of the 2010 Pacific hurricane season, Agatha originated from the Intertropical Convergence Zone, a region of thunderstorms across the tropics. [SEP] [CLS] It developed into a tropical depression on May 29 and tropical storm later, it was dissipated on May 30, reaching top winds of 45 mph (75 km/h) and a lowest pressure of 1000 mbar (hPa; 29.53 inHg). [SEP] [CLS] In Guatemala, 152 people were killed and 100 left missing by landslides. [SEP] [CLS] Thirteen deaths also occurred in El Salvador. [SEP] [CLS] Agatha soon dissipated over Guatemala. [SEP] [CLS] As of June 15, officials in Guatemala have stated that 165 people were killed and 113 others are missing. [SEP] [CLS] In all, Agatha caused at least 204 fatalities, and roughly $1.1 billion in damage throughout Central America. [SEP] [CLS] After the season the name Agatha was not retired, and therefore was used again in the 2016 season. [SEP] [CLS]
