Tropical Storm Bebinca was a weak but erratic and long-lived tropical cyclone that affected South China and Vietnam in mid-August 2018. [CLS] [SEP] Bebinca originated from a tropical depression over the South China Sea on August 9. [CLS] [SEP] Maintaining this intensity for a few days near the Guangdong coast, the system intensified into a tropical storm south of Jiangmen on August 13. [CLS] [SEP] The storm moved slowly to the east and then curved back on the next day, before making landfall in the Leizhou Peninsula on August 15. [CLS] [SEP] Bebinca crossed the Gulf of Tonkin and made landfall in Vietnam on August 16, before dissipated on the next day. [CLS] [SEP] In China, 6 people were dead and the economic losses by the storm reached ¥2.31 billion (US$333 million). [CLS] [SEP] In Vietnam, Bebinca killed 13 people. [CLS] [SEP] Moderate damage to agriculture and infrastructure were reported in Northern Vietnam, with heavy rains caused multiple landslides and blocked the road. [CLS] [SEP] Total damage in Vietnam were over ₫1.34 trillion (US$57.6 million). [CLS] [SEP]
