[CLS] Hurricane King was the most severe hurricane to strike the city of Miami, Florida since the 1926 Miami hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] It was the eleventh tropical storm and the last of six major hurricanes in the 1950 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone formed in the western Caribbean Sea on October 13, and initially moved northeastward, slowly strengthening. [SEP] [CLS] Hurricane King crossed Cuba on October 17, causing seven deaths and $2 million in damage (1950 USD). [SEP] [CLS] It reached its peak intensity of 130 mph (210 km/h) and subsequently made landfall on downtown Miami. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane damaged 20,861 houses in southern Florida, 580 of them severely, and destroyed a further 248. [SEP] [CLS] Further inland, King caused heavy crop damage, particularly to the citrus industry. [SEP] [CLS] After weakening to a tropical storm, King moved across Georgia, where it caused isolated power outages and minor damage. [SEP] [CLS] Across the United States, the hurricane left four fatalities and $30 million in damage ($316,000,000 in 2014 USD). [SEP] [CLS]
