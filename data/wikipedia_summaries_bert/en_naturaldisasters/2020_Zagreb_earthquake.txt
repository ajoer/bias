[CLS] At approximately 6:24 AM CET on the morning of 22 March 2020, an earthquake of magnitude 5.3 Mw, 5.5 ML, hit Zagreb, Croatia, with an epicenter 7 kilometres (4.3 mi) north of the city centre. [SEP] [CLS] The maximum felt intensity was VII (Very strong) on the Modified Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was followed by numerous aftershocks, the strongest of which with a magnitude of 5.0. [SEP] [CLS] It was the strongest earthquake in Zagreb since the 1880 earthquake and caused substantial damage in the historical city center. [SEP] [CLS] Over 1,900 buildings are reported to have become uninhabitable by the earthquake damage. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake was also felt in Slovenia. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 27 people were injured, of whom one teenage girl died succumbing to her injuries. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake occurred during the coronavirus pandemic and caused problems in enforcement of social distancing measures set out by the Government of Croatia. [SEP] [CLS] It occurred during the Croatian Presidency of the Council of the European Union. [SEP] [CLS]
