[CLS] Typhoon Mangkhut, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Ompong, was an extremely powerful and catastrophic tropical cyclone that caused extensive damage in Guam, the Philippines and South China in September 2018. [SEP] [CLS] It was the strongest typhoon to strike Luzon since Megi in 2010, and the strongest to make landfall anywhere in the Philippines since Haiyan in 2013. [SEP] [CLS] Mangkhut was also the strongest typhoon to affect Hong Kong since Ellen in 1983.Mangkhut was the thirty-second tropical depression, twenty-second tropical storm, ninth typhoon, and fourth super typhoon of the 2018 Pacific typhoon season. [SEP] [CLS] It made landfall in the Philippine province of Cagayan late on September 14, as a Category 5-equivalent super typhoon, and subsequently impacted Hong Kong and southern China. [SEP] [CLS] Mangkhut was also the third-strongest tropical cyclone worldwide in 2018. [SEP] [CLS] Over the course of its existence, Mangkhut left behind a trail of severe destruction in its wake. [SEP] [CLS] The storm caused a total of $3.77 billion (2018 US) in damage across multiple nations, along with at least 134 fatalities: 127 in the Philippines, 6 in mainland China, and 1 in Taiwan.
