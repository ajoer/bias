[CLS] The Willamette Valley flood of 1996 was part of a larger series of floods in the Pacific Northwest of the United States which took place between late January and mid-February 1996. [SEP] [CLS] It was Oregon's largest flood event in terms of fatalities and monetary damage during the 1990s. [SEP] [CLS] The floods spread beyond Oregon's Willamette Valley, extending west to the Oregon Coast and east toward the Cascade Mountains. [SEP] [CLS] Significant flood damage also impacted the American states of Washington, Idaho (particularly the north of the state) and California. [SEP] [CLS] The floods were directly responsible for eight deaths in Oregon, as well as over US$500 million in property damage throughout the Pacific Northwest. [SEP] [CLS] Three thousand residents were displaced from their homes. [SEP] [CLS]
