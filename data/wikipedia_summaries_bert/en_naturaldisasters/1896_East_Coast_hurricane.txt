[CLS] The 1896 East Coast hurricane was a slow-moving tropical cyclone that battered the East Coast of the United States from Florida to New England in mid-October 1896. [SEP] [CLS] The fifth tropical cyclone of the 1896 Atlantic hurricane season, it formed on October 7 in the southern Gulf of Mexico, and caused minor damage in Florida while crossing the state two days later. [SEP] [CLS] From October 10 through 13, the hurricane drifted northeastward along the coast, reaching its peak intensity as the equivalence of a Category 2 hurricane on the modern-day Saffirâ€“Simpson scale. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane subjected many areas along the East Coast to days of high seas and damaging northeasterly winds, which halted shipping operations. [SEP] [CLS] The Mid-Atlantic coastline experienced flooding storm tides that submerged and heavily eroded Cobb's Island, part of the Virginia Barrier Islands. [SEP] [CLS] Hotels and cottages there were extensively damaged, and the hurricane brought about the end of the island's stint as a popular summer resort. [SEP] [CLS] Along the Jersey Shore, low-lying railroads were flooded, boardwalks were destroyed, and many beach houses sustained damage. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane did $200,000 in damage to coastal installations on New York's Coney Island. [SEP] [CLS] To the north, wind gusts as high as 80 mph (130 km/h) affected eastern New England. [SEP] [CLS] Four sailors died in two maritime incidents attributed to the hurricane, and overall damage amounted to $500,000. [SEP] [CLS]
