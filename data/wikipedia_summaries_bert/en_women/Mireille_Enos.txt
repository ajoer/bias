[CLS] Marie Mireille Enos (; born September 22, 1975) is an American actress. [SEP] [CLS] Drawn to acting from a young age, she graduated in performing arts from Brigham Young University, where she was awarded the Irene Ryan Acting Scholarship. [SEP] [CLS] Having made her acting debut in the 1994 television film Without Consent, she has since received nominations for a Tony Award, a Golden Globe Award, and an Emmy Award. [SEP] [CLS] Early in her career, Enos appeared variously as a guest star on such television shows as Sex and the City and The Education of Max Bickford among others. [SEP] [CLS] She made her feature film debut with a minor part in the 2001 romantic comedy Someone Like You, but garnered wider attention for her role as Honey in the 2005 Broadway production of Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?. [SEP] [CLS] Her performance in the latter earned her a nomination for Best Featured Actress at the Tony Awards. [SEP] [CLS] She again ventured into television roles and landed the role of twins Kathy and JoDean Marquart in the HBO drama series Big Love. [SEP] [CLS] Enos' breakout role was on the AMC crime drama series The Killing; she played Sarah Linden, a Seattle-based police officer for the show's four seasons from 2011 to 2014. [SEP] [CLS] Her performance garnered her critical acclaim and earned her nominations for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series at the Primetime Emmy Award and the Golden Globe Award. [SEP] [CLS] Enos starred as Karin Lane in the 2013 disaster film World War Z and Kathleen Hall in the 2014 romantic drama If I Stay; both of the films were blockbuster productions. [SEP] [CLS] She continued to draw praise for her work in independent films like Never Here (2017). [SEP] [CLS] Enos starred as the lead character in the short-lived ABC legal thriller The Catch. [SEP] [CLS] In 2019, she appeared in the Amazon/BBC co-production of Good Omens as Carmine "Red" Zuigiber, a war correspondent who is actually War, one of the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse. [SEP] [CLS] In 2019, she appeared as Marissa Wiegler in action drama web television series Hanna, based on the 2011 film of the same name, on Prime Video. [SEP] [CLS]
