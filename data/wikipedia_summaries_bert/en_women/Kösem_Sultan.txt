Kösem Sultan (Ottoman Turkish: كوسم سلطان‎, IPA: [cœˈsæm suɫˈtan]) (c. [CLS] [SEP] 1589 – 2 September 1651) – also known as Mahpeyker Sultan (Turkish pronunciation: [mahpejˈkæɾ suɫˈtan]; from the Persian compound ماه پيكر Māh-peyker meaning "moon framed") – was one of the most powerful women in Ottoman history. [CLS] [SEP] Kösem Sultan achieved power and influenced the politics of the Ottoman Empire when she became haseki sultan as favourite consort of Ottoman Sultan Ahmed I (r. [CLS] [SEP] 1603–1617) and valide sultan as mother of Murad IV (r. [CLS] [SEP] 1623–1640) and Ibrahim (r. [CLS] [SEP] 1640–1648), and grandmother of Mehmed IV (r. [CLS] [SEP] 1648–1687). [CLS] [SEP] Kösem lived in the Ottoman Empire as a courtier during the reign of six sultans: Ahmed I, Mustafa I, Osman II, Murad IV, Ibrahim, and Mehmed IV. [CLS] [SEP] After her death, she was known by the names "Valide-i Muazzama" (magnificent mother), "Vālide-i Maḳtūle" (murdered mother), and "Vālide-i Şehīde" (martyred mother). [CLS] [SEP]
