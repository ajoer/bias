[CLS] The Great Kantō earthquake (関東大地震, Kantō dai-jishin) struck the Kantō Plain on the main Japanese island of Honshū at 11:58:44 JST (02:58:44 UTC) on Saturday, September 1, 1923. [SEP] [CLS] Varied accounts indicate the duration of the earthquake was between four and ten minutes. [SEP] [CLS] Extensive firestorms and even a fire tornado added to the death toll. [SEP] [CLS] Ethnically-charged civil unrest after the disaster (i.e. [SEP] [CLS] the Kantō Massacre) has been documented. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake had a magnitude of 7.9 on the moment magnitude scale (Mw ), with its focus deep beneath Izu Ōshima Island in Sagami Bay. [SEP] [CLS] The cause was a rupture of part of the convergent boundary where the Philippine Sea Plate is subducting beneath the Okhotsk Plate along the line of the Sagami Trough. [SEP] [CLS]
