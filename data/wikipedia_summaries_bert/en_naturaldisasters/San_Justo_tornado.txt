The San Justo tornado was a powerful tornado which struck San Justo, a town in the province of Santa Fe, Argentina, on January 10, 1973. [CLS] [SEP] At least 63 people were reported dead and 350 were reported injured as it cut a 300 yard wide swath through the town. [CLS] [SEP] It was the most violent tornado ever recorded in South America, and also the entire Southern Hemisphere. [CLS] [SEP] However, the tornado was extremely short-lived, and only lasted for 7 minutes, reaching F5 intensity 2 minutes after it formed. [CLS] [SEP] Multiple factories and more than 500 homes were destroyed or damaged, and some homes were said to have vanished with little or no trace. [CLS] [SEP] Vehicles were thrown hundreds of yards and mangled beyond recognition, and grass was reportedly ripped from the ground. [CLS] [SEP] A newspaper image showed a vehicle motor that was embedded into a concrete wall by the tornado. [CLS] [SEP] A tractor was found in a wooded area 500 meters away from the dealership where it originated, and a pond outside of town was reportedly sucked dry by the tornado. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado is widely considered to have been an F5 on the Fujita Scale. [CLS] [SEP] This tornado was the most violent ever reported in Argentina and Southern Hemisphere and had an economic cost of about $60,000 and was the deadliest tornado in Argentina's history.
