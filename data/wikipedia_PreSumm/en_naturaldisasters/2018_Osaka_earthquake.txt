[CLS] On 18 June 2018, around 7:58 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] Japan Standard Time, an earthquake measuring 5.6 Mw on the moment magnitude scale (preliminary 5.5 Mw) struck in northern Osaka Prefecture, Japan. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake's epicenter was near Takatsuki and occurred at a depth of approximately 13 kilometres (8.1 mi). [SEP] [CLS] The Japan Meteorological Agency registered a magnitude of 6.1 Mj and an intensity of lower 6 on the shindo scale.Shaking from the earthquake was felt strongly in the prefecture and the nearby metropolitan areas of Osaka and Kyoto, temporarily disrupting electrical and gas service to 170,000 homes and buildings. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake struck during rush hour, disrupting train services for several hours, and also damaged water pipes and hundreds of homes. [SEP] [CLS] Four people were killed, 15 serious injuries were reported and 419 people had minor injuries. [SEP] [CLS]
