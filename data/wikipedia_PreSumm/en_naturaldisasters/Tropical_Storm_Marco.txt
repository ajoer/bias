[CLS] Tropical Storm Marco was the smallest tropical cyclone on record. [SEP] [CLS] The thirteenth named storm of the 2008 Atlantic hurricane season, Marco developed out of a broad area of low pressure over the northwestern Caribbean during late September 2008. [SEP] [CLS] Influenced by a tropical wave on October 4, a small low-level circulation center developed over Belize. [SEP] [CLS] Around this time, tropical storm force winds extended 11.5 miles (18.5 km) from the center of the storm, making Marco the smallest tropical cyclone on record. [SEP] [CLS] Around 1200 UTC, Marco made landfall near Misantla, Veracruz. [SEP] [CLS] The storm rapidly weakened after landfall, dissipating later that day. [SEP] [CLS] Due to its small size, Marco caused minimal damage; however, the storm's heavy rains led to floods up to 10 feet (3.05 m) deep that covered highways and damaged homes. [SEP] [CLS]
