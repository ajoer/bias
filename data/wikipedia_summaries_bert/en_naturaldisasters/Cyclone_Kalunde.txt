[CLS] [SEP] The eleventh named storm and sixth cyclone of the season, Kalunde formed on March 4 from an area of disturbed weather east-southeast of Diego Garcia. [CLS] [SEP] The storm steadily strengthened and attained severe tropical storm intensity on March 6. [CLS] [SEP] After starting a phase of rapid deepening, Kalunde attained cyclone intensity the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Kalunde attained its peak intensity on March 8, as an intense tropical cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] It maintained its peak strength for a day; shortly thereafter, the system began to weaken. [CLS] [SEP] After undergoing an eyewall replacement cycle, the storm brushed Rodrigues. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly after doing so, Kalunde weakened into a tropical cyclone and later a severe tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] Two days later, on March 16, the cyclone transitioned into an extratropical cyclone and dissipated the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone Kalunde brought US$3.15 million in damage to Rodrigues Island. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 1,600 homes and 40 boats were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] Severe coastal damage took place across the island; many roads were washed out. [CLS] [SEP] Power outages also occurred across the island, delaying residents access to information pertaining to Kalunde. [CLS] [SEP] About 80 percent of the drinking water was contaminated and the entire food crop was destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] However, no deaths were reported. [CLS] [SEP]
