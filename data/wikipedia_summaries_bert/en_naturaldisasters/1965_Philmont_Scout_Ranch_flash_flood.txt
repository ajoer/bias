Philmont Scout Ranch is a ranch located in Colfax County, New Mexico, near the village of Cimarron; it covers 140,177 acres (219 sq mi; 567 kmÂ²) of wilderness in the Sangre de Cristo Mountains of the Rocky Mountains. [CLS] [SEP] Donated by oil baron Waite Phillips, the ranch is owned and operated by the Boy Scouts of America. [CLS] [SEP] It is a National High Adventure Base where crews of Scouts and Venturers take part in backpacking treks and other outdoor activities. [CLS] [SEP] By land area, it is one of the largest youth camps in the world. [CLS] [SEP] During the season, between June 8 and August 22, an estimated 22,000 Scouts and adult leaders backpack through the Ranch's extensive backcountry. [CLS] [SEP] More than 1,130 seasonal staff are responsible for the Ranch's summer operations. [CLS] [SEP] Philmont is also home to the Philmont Training Center, the National Scouting Museum and the Seton Memorial Library. [CLS] [SEP] The Training Center is the primary location for BSA's national volunteer training programs. [CLS] [SEP] Philmont is a working ranch, maintaining small herds of cattle, horses, burros, and bison. [CLS] [SEP] The only documented Tyrannosaurus rex track in the world was discovered within the camp's boundaries in 1993 in North Ponil Canyon by the Anasazi Trail Camp. [CLS] [SEP] It was formally identified in 1994.There are three other high adventure camps that the BSA owns and maintains: the Northern Tier National High Adventure Bases in Minnesota, as well as Manitoba and Ontario in Canada; Florida National High Adventure Sea Base in the Florida Keys; and Summit Bechtel Family National Scout Reserve in southern West Virginia.
