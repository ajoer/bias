[CLS] The 1940 Canberra air disaster was an aircraft crash that occurred near Canberra, the capital of Australia, on 13 August 1940, during World War II. [SEP] [CLS] All ten people on board were killed: six passengers, including three members of the Australian Cabinet and the Chief of the General Staff; and four crew. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft is believed to have stalled on its landing approach, when it was too low to recover. [SEP] [CLS] The deaths of the three cabinet ministers severely weakened the United Australia Party government of Robert Menzies and contributed to its fall in 1941. [SEP] [CLS]
