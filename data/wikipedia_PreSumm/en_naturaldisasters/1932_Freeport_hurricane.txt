[CLS] The 1932 Freeport hurricane was an intense tropical cyclone that primarily affected areas of the Texas coast in August of the 1932 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] After landfall, the hurricane began to quickly weaken before dissipating over the Texas Panhandle. [SEP] [CLS] The storm caused heavy rainfall inland, peaking at 9.93 in (252 mm) in Angleton. [SEP] [CLS] Record rainfall rates were also reported in some areas. [SEP] [CLS] Power outages were reported in Galveston and San Antonio. [SEP] [CLS] The storm caused $7.5 million in damages, mostly to crops, and 40 deaths. [SEP] [CLS]
