Henri Kontinen (Finnish pronunciation: [ˈhenri ˈkontinen]; born 19 June 1990) is a Finnish tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] Kontinen has won 21 doubles titles on the ATP Tour in his career. [CLS] [SEP] He was formerly ranked as world No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 in doubles after winning the 2017 Australian Open men's doubles championship. [CLS] [SEP] He reached his best singles rankings of world No. [CLS] [SEP] 220 in October 2010. [CLS] [SEP] His brother Micke is a retired tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] After ending his singles career due to injuries he started focusing on doubles. [CLS] [SEP] He reached the quarterfinals of the men's doubles 2016 Wimbledon Championships with John Peers and won the mixed doubles at the same tournament with Heather Watson. [CLS] [SEP] His win in the 2017 Australian Open men's doubles with Peers was his first Grand Slam title in men's doubles. [CLS] [SEP]
