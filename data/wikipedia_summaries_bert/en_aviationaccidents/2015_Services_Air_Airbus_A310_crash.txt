On 24 December 2015, at 08:35, an Airbus A310-304F cargo aircraft, operated by Congolese company Services Air on a domestic flight, ran off the end of the runway and crashed in a residential area while landing at Mbuji-Mayi Airport in the city of Mbuji-Mayi, the capital of Kasai-Oriental province in the Democratic Republic of Congo. [CLS] [SEP] Eight people were killed and nine others were injured, all of whom were people in the residential area. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft suffered substantial damage, but the five crew members were uninjured. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft overshot the runway by about 500 m (1,640 ft) and ploughed into the residential area, destroying several homes. [CLS] [SEP]
