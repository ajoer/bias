[CLS] The 1138 Aleppo earthquake was among the deadliest earthquakes in history. [SEP] [CLS] Its name was taken from the city of Aleppo, in northern Syria, where the most casualties were sustained. [SEP] [CLS] The quake occurred on 11 October 1138 and was preceded by a smaller quake on the 10th. [SEP] [CLS] It is frequently listed as the third deadliest earthquake in history, following on from the Shensi and Tangshan earthquakes in China. [SEP] [CLS] However, the figure of 230,000 dead is based on a historical conflation of this earthquake with earthquakes in November 1137 on the Jazira plain and the large seismic event of 30 September 1139 in the Transcaucasian city of Ganja. [SEP] [CLS] The first mention of a 230,000 death toll was by Ibn Taghribirdi in the fifteenth century. [SEP] [CLS]
