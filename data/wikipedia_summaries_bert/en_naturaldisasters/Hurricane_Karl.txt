[CLS] Hurricane Karl was the most destructive tropical cyclone on record to strike the Mexican state of Veracruz. [SEP] [CLS] The eleventh tropical storm, sixth hurricane, and fifth and final major hurricane of the 2010 Atlantic hurricane season, Karl formed from an area of low pressure which had formed off of the northern coast Venezuela on September 11. [SEP] [CLS] It crossed the Caribbean and was upgraded to Tropical Storm Karl on September 14. [SEP] [CLS] The cyclone made landfall on the Yucatan Peninsula of Mexico as a strong tropical storm, and then rapidly strengthened in the Bay of Campeche before it made landfall near the city of Veracruz, on the central Mexican Gulf coast, as a major hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] This marked the first known time that a major hurricane existed in the Bay of Campeche. [SEP] [CLS] Afterwards, the storm rapidly weakened over the mountains of Mexico and dissipated on September 18. [SEP] [CLS] As of September 23, 22 people have been confirmed killed, most of which were in the state of Veracruz. [SEP] [CLS] Insured losses from the storm are estimated at US$206 million as of January 2011, while total damaged equated to approximately $3.9 billion. [SEP] [CLS]
