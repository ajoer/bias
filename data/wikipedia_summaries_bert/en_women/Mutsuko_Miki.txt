Mutsuko Miki (三木 睦子, Miki Mutsuko, 31 July 1917 – 31 July 2012) was a Japanese activist who advocated on behalf of pacifism, official compensation for comfort women, and improved Japan–North Korea relations. [CLS] [SEP] She was the widow of former Japanese Prime Minister Takeo Miki. [CLS] [SEP] Miki served as the wife of the Prime Minister of Japan, or First Lady, during her husband's two-year tenure from 1974–76. [CLS] [SEP]
