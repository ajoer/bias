This page documents notable tornadoes and tornado outbreaks worldwide in 2008. [CLS] [SEP] Strong and destructive tornadoes form most frequently in the United States, Bangladesh, and Eastern India, but they can occur almost anywhere under the right conditions. [CLS] [SEP] Tornadoes also develop occasionally in southern Canada during the Northern Hemisphere's summer and somewhat regularly at other times of the year across Europe, Asia, and Australia. [CLS] [SEP] Tornadic events are often accompanied with other forms of severe weather, including strong thunderstorms, strong winds, and hail. [CLS] [SEP] There were 1,692 tornadoes confirmed in the United States in 2008, with 126 confirmed fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] This made 2008 the deadliest year in that country since 1998 with 2011 and 2004 having more tornadoes. [CLS] [SEP] Fatalities were also reported elsewhere in the world: three in France, two each in Bangladesh and Poland and one in Russia, and 18 in China. [CLS] [SEP] With 1,692 confirmed tornadoes, 2008 ranked as the third most active US tornado season on record, only 2011 and 2004 have had more tornadoes confirmed with 1,697 and 1,817, respectively. [CLS] [SEP] The US state of Kansas received the most tornadoes in the United States in 2008 with 187. [CLS] [SEP]
