Super Typhoon Doug, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Ritang, was the only Category 5 typhoon in the 1994 Pacific typhoon season. [CLS] [SEP] The 13th storm and the second super typhoon of the season, it formed on the eastern end of a monsoon trough and developed into a tropical depression on August 2. [CLS] [SEP] It moved west and rapidly strengthened. [CLS] [SEP] Doug reached a peak of 925 mbar (27.3 inHg) and winds of 160 mph (260 km/h) before brushing the coast of Taiwan. [CLS] [SEP] Doug made landfall near Shanghai, China as a weak tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] Doug killed 26 people and caused $110 million (1994 USD) damage in Taiwan. [CLS] [SEP] Doug's effects reached Korea. [CLS] [SEP]
