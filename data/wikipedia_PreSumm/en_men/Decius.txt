[SEP] [CLS] A distinguished politician during the reign of Philip the Arab, Decius was proclaimed emperor by his troops after putting down a rebellion in Moesia. [SEP] [CLS] In 249, he defeated and killed Philip near Verona and was recognized as emperor by the Senate afterwards. [SEP] [CLS] During his reign, he attempted to strengthen the Roman state and its religion, leading to the Decian persecution, where a number of prominent Christians (including Pope Fabian) were put to death. [SEP] [CLS] In the last year of his reign, Decius co-ruled with his son Herennius Etruscus, until they were both killed by the Goths in the Battle of Abritus. [SEP] [CLS]
