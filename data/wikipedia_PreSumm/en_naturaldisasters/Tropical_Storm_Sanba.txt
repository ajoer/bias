[CLS] Tropical Storm Sanba, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Basyang, was a weak tropical cyclone that affected southern and central parts of the Philippines in mid-February 2018. [SEP] [CLS] Sanba developed as a tropical depression in the open Pacific Ocean on February 8. [SEP] [CLS] The system moved generally westward while slowly developing, finally attaining tropical storm status on February 11. [SEP] [CLS] Soon after, wind shear caused the system to lose organization and remain as a minimal tropical storm through February 11 and 12. [SEP] [CLS] During this time, Sanba moved westwards then west-northwestwards, making landfall over northeastern Mindanao on February 13. [SEP] [CLS] The system weakened into a tropical depression before making another landfall in southeastern Negros later that day. [SEP] [CLS] After traversing the Philippine Islands, Sanba failed to reorganize significantly in the Sulu Sea and dissipated two days later west of Palawan. [SEP] [CLS] As a developing system, Sanba brought minor flooding to Palau. [SEP] [CLS] From February 12 to 16, Sanba caused heavy rains, floods, and landslides across the southern and central Philippines, affecting over 250,000 people. [SEP] [CLS] About 40,000 people sought shelter in evacuation centers. [SEP] [CLS] More than 1,600 houses were damaged or destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Power outages occurred in five places. [SEP] [CLS] Ports were closed and flights cancelled because of adverse weather conditions, which also caused damage to roads and bridges. [SEP] [CLS] Schools and workplaces were shut as Sanba passed over the Philippines. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 15 people were killed, mostly by flooded rivers and landslides, and 16 others were injured.
