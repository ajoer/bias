[CLS] Amy C. [SEP] [CLS] Rosenzweig is a professor of Chemistry and Molecular Biosciences at Northwestern University. [SEP] [CLS] She was born in 1967 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. [SEP] [CLS] She received her BA in chemistry from Amherst College in 1988, and her Ph.D. [SEP] [CLS] from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1994. [SEP] [CLS] At MIT, Rosenzweig worked under the supervision of Stephen J. [SEP] [CLS] Lippard where she pioneered the structural studies of the hydroxylase component of methane monooxygenase from methyloccous capsulatus. [SEP] [CLS] Her current research interests include structural biology and bioinorganic chemistry, metal uptake and transport, oxygen activation by metalloenzymes, and characterization of membrane protein. [SEP] [CLS] For her work, she has been recognized by a number of national and international awards, including the MacArthur "Genius" Award in 2003. [SEP] [CLS]
