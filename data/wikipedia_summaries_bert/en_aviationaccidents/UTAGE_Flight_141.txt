UTAGE Flight 141 was a chartered international passenger flight operated by Guinean regional airline Union des Transports Aériens de Guinée, flying from Cotonou Airport in Cotonou, Benin, to Kufra Airport in Kufra, Libya. [CLS] [SEP] On 25 December 2003, the aircraft crashed into the Bight of Benin, killing 141 people, most of them Lebanese. [CLS] [SEP] Shortly after the crash, 35 survivors were found by a search and rescue team. [CLS] [SEP] Some of them were pronounced dead upon reaching the hospital. [CLS] [SEP] The final accident report, which was published by France's Bureau d'Enquêtes et d'Analyses pour la Sécurité de l'Aviation Civile (BEA), concluded that the crash occurred due to the airplane being overloaded, as the plane's actual weight exceeded its maximum weight capacity, thus decreasing its performance, especially when taking off. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 141 did take off, but could not gain altitude, hit a localizer building, and plunged into the ocean. [CLS] [SEP] The report also blamed the airport's lack of facilities.The crash was the 102nd aviation accident involving the Boeing 727. [CLS] [SEP] It is the deadliest plane crash in Benin's aviation history, and the 11th deadliest accident involving the Boeing 727. [CLS] [SEP]
