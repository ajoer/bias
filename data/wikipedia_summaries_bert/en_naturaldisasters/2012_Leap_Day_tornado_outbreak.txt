[CLS] The 2012 Leap Day tornado outbreak was a significant and deadly tornado outbreak on February 28 and February 29, 2012. [SEP] [CLS] It caused severe damage in several regions, especially the Great Plains and Ohio Valley regions. [SEP] [CLS] It also resulted in several tornadoes in the Central Plains, a rarity for the time of year. [SEP] [CLS] The most destructive and deadly tornado was a violent early-morning EF4 that hit Harrisburg, Illinois, killing 8 people. [SEP] [CLS] In total, 15 people died in the outbreak. [SEP] [CLS] Just two days later, a larger and deadlier outbreak devastated the Ohio Valley and Southern United States. [SEP] [CLS]
