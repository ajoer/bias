[CLS] Albert II (French: Albert Félix Humbert Théodore Christian Eugène Marie, pronounced [albɛʁ feliks œ̃bɛʁ teodɔʁ kʁistjɑ̃ øʒɛn maʁi]); Dutch: Albert Felix Humbert Theodoor Christiaan Eugène Marie, pronounced [ˈɑlbərt ˈfeːlɪks ˈɦɵmbərt teːjoːˈdoːr ˈkrɪstijaːn øːˈʒɛːn maːˈriː]; German: Albrecht Felix Humbert Theodor Christian Eugen Maria, pronounced [ˈʔalbʁɛkt ˈfeːlɪks ˈhʊmbɛʁt ˈteːodoːɐ̯ ˈkʁɪsti̯an ˈʔɔʏɡn̩ maˈʁiːaː]; born 6 June 1934) reigned as King of the Belgians, from 1993 until his abdication in 2013. [SEP] [CLS] King Albert II is the last living child of King Leopold III and Queen Astrid, born princess of Sweden. [SEP] [CLS] He is the younger brother of Grand Duchess Joséphine-Charlotte of Luxembourg and King Baudouin, whom he succeeded upon Baudouin's death in 1993. [SEP] [CLS] He married Donna Paola Ruffo di Calabria (now Queen Paola), with whom he had three children. [SEP] [CLS] Albert's eldest son, Philippe, is the current King of the Belgians. [SEP] [CLS] On 3 July 2013, King Albert II attended a midday session of the Belgian cabinet. [SEP] [CLS] He then announced that, on 21 July, Belgian National Day, he would abdicate the throne for health reasons. [SEP] [CLS] He was succeeded by his son Philippe on 21 July 2013. [SEP] [CLS] Albert II was the fourth monarch to abdicate in 2013, following Pope Benedict XVI, Queen Beatrix of the Netherlands, and Emir Hamad bin Khalifa of Qatar. [SEP] [CLS] In so doing, he was also the second Belgian monarch to abdicate, following his father Leopold III who abdicated in 1951, albeit under very different circumstances. [SEP] [CLS]
