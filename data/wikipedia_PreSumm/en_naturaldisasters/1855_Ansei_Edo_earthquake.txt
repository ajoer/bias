[CLS] The 1855 Edo earthquake (安政江戸地震, Ansei Edo Jishin), was the third Ansei Great Earthquake, which occurred during the late-Edo period. [SEP] [CLS] It proceeded the 1854 Nankai earthquake, which took place about a year prior. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake occurred at 22:00 local time on 11 November. [SEP] [CLS] It had an epicenter close to Edo (now Tokyo), causing considerable damage in the Kantō region from the shaking and subsequent fires, with a death toll of 7,000–10,000 people and destroyed around 14,000 buildings. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake had a magnitude of 7.0 on the surface wave magnitude scale and reached a maximum intensity of XI (Extreme) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake triggered a minor tsunami. [SEP] [CLS]
