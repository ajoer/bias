TWA Flight 513, registration NC86513, Star of Lisbon, was a Lockheed L-049 Constellation operated by Transcontinental and Western Air on a training flight on July 11, 1946 when it crashed near Reading, Pennsylvania. [CLS] [SEP] Electrical wiring in the baggage compartment arced, starting a fire. [CLS] [SEP] The smoke and intense fire created made it impossible for the pilots to maintain control of the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] Of the six crewmembers aboard, five were killed. [CLS] [SEP] This accident is memorable for grounding all Lockheed Constellations from July 12 until August 23, 1946 when cargo fire detection equipment could be installed. [CLS] [SEP] The crash was referred to in Martin Scorsese's film The Aviator, based on the life of TWA's then-owner, Howard Hughes. [CLS] [SEP]
