Pacific Southwest Airlines (PSA) Flight 182 was a Boeing 727-214 commercial airliner, registration N533PS, that collided with a private Cessna 172 light aircraft, registration N7711G, over San Diego, California, at 9:01 am on Monday, September 25, 1978. [CLS] [SEP] It was Pacific Southwest Airlines' first fatal accident. [CLS] [SEP] Both aircraft crashed into North Park, a San Diego neighborhood. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 182 impacted just north of the intersection of Dwight and Nile, killing all 135 people aboard the aircraft and seven people on the ground in houses, including two children. [CLS] [SEP] The Cessna impacted on Polk Ave. [CLS] [SEP] between 32nd St. [CLS] [SEP] and Iowa St., killing the two on board. [CLS] [SEP] Nine others on the ground were injured and 22 homes were destroyed or damaged by the impact and debris. [CLS] [SEP]
