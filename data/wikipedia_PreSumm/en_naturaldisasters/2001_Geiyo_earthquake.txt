[CLS] The 2001 Geiyo earthquake (2001年芸予地震 Nisen-ichi-nen Gēyo Jishin) occurred with a moment magnitude of 6.7 on March 24 at 15:27 local time near Hiroshima, Japan. [SEP] [CLS] One person in Hiroshima and one person in Ehime were reported dead. [SEP] [CLS] About 3,700 buildings were damaged in the Hiroshima area. [SEP] [CLS] Liquefaction was observed in Hiroshima and Ehime. [SEP] [CLS] Power outage occurred in the prefectures of Hiroshima, Ehime, Okayama, Yamaguchi, and Kōchi. [SEP] [CLS] The maximum intensity was shindo lower 6 in Hiroshima. [SEP] [CLS] This earthquake could be felt along the eastern and southern coasts of South Korea.The released seismic moment of the earthquake was 1.3×1019 Nm. [SEP] [CLS] This earthquake is a normal faulting intraslab event within the subducting Philippine Sea Plate. [SEP] [CLS] The slip of the earthquake was estimated to be about 1.5–2.4 meters (4 ft 11 in–7 ft 10 in). [SEP] [CLS] The locations of aftershocks were distributed roughly in N-S direction. [SEP] [CLS] It has been suggested that this earthquake was related to the dehydration of the Philippine Sea Plate slab.In this region, a strong earthquake occurred in 1905, which was also an intraslab event within the subducting plate.
