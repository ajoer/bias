[CLS] Michael Ballack (German pronunciation: [ˈmɪçaːʔeːl ˈbalak]; born 26 September 1976) is a German retired professional footballer. [SEP] [CLS] He is among the top goal scorers in the history of the German national team. [SEP] [CLS] Ballack wore the number 13 shirt for every team he has played for, except from 1. [SEP] [CLS] FC Kaiserslautern. [SEP] [CLS] He was selected by Pelé as one of FIFA's 100 Greatest Living Players, and as the UEFA Club Midfielder of the Year in 2002. [SEP] [CLS] He won the German Footballer of the Year award three times – in 2002, 2003 and 2005. [SEP] [CLS] Ballack was known for his passing range, powerful shot, physical strength and commanding presence in midfield.Ballack began his career as a youth at Chemnitzer FC, his local team, and made his professional debut in 1995. [SEP] [CLS] On 26 March 1996, Ballack made his debut for Germany's under-21 side. [SEP] [CLS] Although the team were relegated in his first season, his performances in the Regionalliga the following season led to a transfer to 1. [SEP] [CLS] FC Kaiserslautern in 1997. [SEP] [CLS] He won the Bundesliga in his first season at the club, his first major honour. [SEP] [CLS] He became a first team regular in 1998–99 season and also earned his first senior national cap for Germany. [SEP] [CLS] He moved to Bayer Leverkusen for €4.1 million in 1999. [SEP] [CLS] The 2001–02 season saw him win a slew of runners-up medals: Bayer Leverkusen finished second in the Bundesliga, DFB-Pokal, UEFA Champions League and Germany lost to Brazil in the 2002 World Cup Final. [SEP] [CLS] A €12.9 million move to Bayern Munich led to further honours: the team won the Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal double in 2003, 2005 and 2006. [SEP] [CLS] Ballack had become a prolific goalscorer from midfield, scoring 58 goals for Bayern between 2002 and 2006. [SEP] [CLS] He joined Premier League club Chelsea in mid-2006 and won English honours in his first season at the club. [SEP] [CLS] Injury ruled him out of much of 2007, but he returned the following season to help Chelsea reach their first ever Champions League final. [SEP] [CLS] He also went on to win the FA Cup again in 2009 and then once more as part of a league and cup double in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] Internationally, Ballack has played in the UEFA European Football Championship in 2000, 2004 and 2008, and the FIFA World Cup in 2002 and 2006. [SEP] [CLS] Jürgen Klinsmann appointed him captain of the national team in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] Ballack scored consecutive game-winning goals in the quarter-final and semi-final to help his country to the 2002 World Cup final and led his team to the semi-finals of the 2006 World Cup and the Euro 2008 final.
