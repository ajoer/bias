Typhoon Kelly, known as Typhoon Oniang in the Philippines, struck Japan during the middle of October 1987. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather formed along the monsoon trough near Yap on October 6. [CLS] [SEP] Although thunderstorm activity was initially displaced from the center, gradual development occurred nevertheless. [CLS] [SEP] The disturbance became a tropical depression on October 9, and a tropical storm the next day. [CLS] [SEP] While moving generally north-northwest towards Japan, Kelly attained typhoon intensity on October 12. [CLS] [SEP] Continuing to intensify, Typhoon Kelly reached its maximum intensity on October 15, but a weakening trend began thereafter. [CLS] [SEP] The next day, the typhoon passed over the islands of Shikoku and Honshu. [CLS] [SEP] By October 17, Kelly completed its transition into an extratropical cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] Across Japan, the typhoon was responsible for 452 landslides, 20 destroyed bridges, and 165 damaged roads. [CLS] [SEP] A total of 216 homes were destroyed, 24,044 houses were flooded, 99 ships were damaged, and 6,802 ha (16,810 acres) of farmland were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The typhoon killed nine and injured seventeen others. [CLS] [SEP] Damage amounted to $365.6 million (1987 USD). [CLS] [SEP]
