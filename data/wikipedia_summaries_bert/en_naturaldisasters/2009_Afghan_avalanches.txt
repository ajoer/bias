The 2009 Afghan avalanches occurred near Kabul, Afghanistan on 16 January 2009. [CLS] [SEP] At least ten people were killed and twelve vehicles and machinery used to clear the road of snow were swept away when the avalanche struck a highway. [CLS] [SEP] Forty people were rescued, eleven of whom were injured by the avalanches. [CLS] [SEP] The avalanches struck the Salang tunnel, the main highway linking southern and northern Afghanistan in the middle of the Hindu Kush mountains, at an altitude of 4,450 metres (14,600 ft). [CLS] [SEP] Searchers spent the next two days and beyond locating the victims. [CLS] [SEP]
