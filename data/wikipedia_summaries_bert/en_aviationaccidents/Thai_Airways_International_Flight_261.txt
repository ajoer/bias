[CLS] Thai Airways International Flight 261 (TG261/THA261) was a scheduled domestic passenger flight from Bangkok's Don Mueang International Airport, Thailand to Surat Thani International Airport in Surat Thani, Thailand. [SEP] [CLS] The flight was operated by Thai Airways International, the flag carrier of Thailand. [SEP] [CLS] On 11 December 1998, the aircraft, an Airbus A310-204 registered in Thailand as HS-TIA, stalled and crashed onto a swamp during its landing attempt at Surat Thani Airport. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 101 people were killed in the crash.Thailand's Aircraft Accident Investigation Committee (AAIC) opened an investigation into the accident. [SEP] [CLS] The investigation revealed that the crew had become disoriented. [SEP] [CLS] Visibility was limited. [SEP] [CLS] Stress caused the crew to lose control of the aircraft. [SEP] [CLS] The AAIC noted also Surat Thani's minimal lighting and faulty warnings to the aircraft.The accident was the second deadliest plane crash in Thailand, behind Lauda Air Flight 004. [SEP] [CLS] It was the fifth worst accident involving the Airbus A310 and the fourth hull loss of an Airbus A310. [SEP] [CLS]
