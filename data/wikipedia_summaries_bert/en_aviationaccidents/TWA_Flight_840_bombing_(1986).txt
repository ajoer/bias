Trans World Airlines Flight 840 was a regularly scheduled flight from Los Angeles to Cairo via New York City, Rome, and Athens on April 2, 1986. [CLS] [SEP] About 20 minutes before landing in Athens, a bomb was detonated on the aircraft while it was over Argos, Greece, blasting a hole in the plane's starboard side. [CLS] [SEP] Four passengers were killed after being blown out, while another 7 were injured by flying shrapnel and debris. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft then made a successful emergency landing with no further loss of life. [CLS] [SEP]
