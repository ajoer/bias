The 1893 New York hurricane, also known as the Midnight Storm, was a powerful and destructive tropical cyclone that struck the New York City area in August 1893. [CLS] [SEP] First identified as a tropical storm on August 15, over the central Atlantic Ocean, the hurricane moved northwestward for most of its course, ultimately peaking with maximum sustained winds of 115 mph (185 km/h) and a minimum barometric pressure reading of 952 mbar (hPa; 28.11 inHg). [CLS] [SEP] It turned due northward as it approached the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] East Coast and struck western Long Island on August 24. [CLS] [SEP] It moved inland and quickly deteriorated, degenerating the next day. [CLS] [SEP] The storm inflicted severe damage with storm tides as high as 30 ft (9 m). [CLS] [SEP] Trees were brought down, houses were demolished, and Hog Island was largely washed away by the cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] Several areas suffered extensive effects from the hurricane, and at least 34 sailors lost their lives. [CLS] [SEP] The storm is regarded as one of the most severe hurricanes to strike the city. [CLS] [SEP]
