The 2008 Chino Hills earthquake occurred at 11:42:15 a.m. [CLS] [SEP] PDT on July 29 in Southern California. [CLS] [SEP] The epicenter of the magnitude 5.5 earthquake was in Chino Hills, c. [CLS] [SEP] 28 miles (45 km) east-southeast of downtown Los Angeles. [CLS] [SEP] Though no lives were lost, eight people were injured, and it caused considerable damage in numerous structures throughout the area and caused some amusement park facilities to shut down their rides. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake led to increased discussion regarding the possibility of a stronger earthquake in the future. [CLS] [SEP]
