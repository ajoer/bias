[CLS] Antje Harvey (born May 10, 1967 in Magdeburg, as Antje Misersky) is a former German cross-country skier and biathlete. [SEP] [CLS] She began her career as cross country skier and was a member of the East German team that won the 4 × 5 km bronze medal at the 1985 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships in Seefeld. [SEP] [CLS] When her father, Henner Misersky, who worked as a trainer of the East German team until 1985, refused to give steroid substances to his daughter and other team members, he was fired. [SEP] [CLS] Antje Misersky was then put under pressure and had to end her career in the GDR.In 1989, she started biathlon. [SEP] [CLS] At the 1992 Winter Olympics in Albertville, she won the gold medal in 15 km individual and two silvers in the 7.5 km sprint and in the 3 × 7.5 km. [SEP] [CLS] At the 1994 Winter Olympics at Lillehammer she won another relay silver medal (4 × 7.5 km). [SEP] [CLS] Harvey also won two medals in the relay at the World Championships (1995: gold, 1991: bronze). [SEP] [CLS] In the spring of 1993, she married American biathlete Ian Harvey whom she had met 15 months earlier at the Biathlon World Cup in Ruhpolding, Germany. [SEP] [CLS] In 1995, she retired from competitive sports and moved to Utah where she lives with her daughters, Hazel (*1996) and Pearl (*2001). [SEP] [CLS] In 2000, she became an American citizen. [SEP] [CLS] For her refusal to take part in the systematic doping in the GDR, Antje Harvey received the Heidi-Krieger-Medal in 1995, in Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] The Heidi-Krieger-Medal is a prize of the association Doping-Opfer-Hilfe from Germany. [SEP] [CLS]
