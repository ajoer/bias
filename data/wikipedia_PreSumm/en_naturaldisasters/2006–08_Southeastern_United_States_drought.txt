[CLS] The 2006–08 Southeastern United States drought was a crippling drought that struck the southeast of the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Several reasons, including an unusually strong Bermuda high pressure and La Niña in the eastern Pacific Ocean (which causes dry conditions across the southern U.S.) were responsible for the drought. [SEP] [CLS] 2007 was particularly dry across the region, with rivers and lakes dropping to record-low levels and in some areas. [SEP] [CLS]
