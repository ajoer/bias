Franz Josef Jonas (4 October 1899 â€“ 24 April 1974) was an Austrian politician. [CLS] [SEP] He served as President of Austria, between 1965 and 1974. [CLS] [SEP] He was a typesetter by profession and a member of the Social Democratic Party of Austria. [CLS] [SEP] After World War II he got involved in Viennese communal politics and was mayor of Vienna from 1951 to 1965. [CLS] [SEP] From 1965, he was federal president and was re-elected in 1971. [CLS] [SEP] He was a fervent supporter of Esperanto, and starting in 1923, became a long-time instructor of the language. [CLS] [SEP] His address to the 1970 World Congress of Esperanto, which was held in Vienna, was delivered in Esperanto.In 1974, he died in office, the fourth consecutive President to do so. [CLS] [SEP] In Vienna, a large tram station officially called Schottentor which was built when he was mayor is colloquially named after him (Jonas-Reindl, which translates as Jonas bowl). [CLS] [SEP] In 1966 he was awarded the Grand Cross of The Royal Norwegian Order of St. [CLS] [SEP] Olav with Collar, and in 1969 the Pierre de Coubertin medal. [CLS] [SEP]
