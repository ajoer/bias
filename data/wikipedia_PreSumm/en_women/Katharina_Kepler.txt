[CLS] Katharina Kepler (née: Guldenmann; 1546 – 13 April 1622) was a woman from Stuttgart, Württemberg, who was the mother of the famous astronomer Johannes Kepler, and was accused of witchcraft in 1615. [SEP] [CLS] Katharina Kepler was married to Heinrich Kepler and had one daughter and three sons; one of them was Johannes Kepler. [SEP] [CLS] In 1615, a witch trial was initiated by Lutherus Einhorn who in his reign as vogt of the Protestant town of Leonberg (1613 - 1629) accused 15 women of sorcery and executed 8 of them. [SEP] [CLS] He acted in accordance with the will of the government and the public, which had asked for an investigation of sorcery, and issued an arrest of Katharina Kepler in 1615. [SEP] [CLS] Ursula Reinbold had accused Katharina Kepler of giving her a potion after an argument which had made her sick. [SEP] [CLS] Johannes Kepler defended his mother himself, with the assistance of his university in Tübingen. [SEP] [CLS] One of his student friends, Christopher Besoldus, assisted her juridically. [SEP] [CLS] Her son took her away to Linz in December 1616. [SEP] [CLS] When she returned to Leonberg in the summer of 1620, she was arrested and imprisoned for fourteen months. [SEP] [CLS] She was told how she would be tortured, as a means of frightening her, but she refused to confess anything. [SEP] [CLS] In October 1621, Kepler was able to effect her release. [SEP] [CLS] Katharina Kepler died the following year. [SEP] [CLS]
