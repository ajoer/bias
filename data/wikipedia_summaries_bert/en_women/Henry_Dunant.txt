Henry Dunant (born Jean-Henri Dunant; 8 May 1828 – 30 October 1910), also known as Henri Dunant, was a Swiss humanitarian, businessman and social activist. [CLS] [SEP] He was the visionary, promoter and co-founder of the Red Cross. [CLS] [SEP] In 1901 he received the first Nobel Peace Prize together with Frédéric Passy, making Dunant the first Swiss Nobel laureate. [CLS] [SEP] During a business trip in 1859, Dunant was witness to the aftermath of the Battle of Solferino in modern-day Italy. [CLS] [SEP] He recorded his memories and experiences in the book A Memory of Solferino which inspired the creation of the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) in 1863. [CLS] [SEP] The 1864 Geneva Convention was based on Dunant's idea for an independent organisation to care for wounded soldiers. [CLS] [SEP] Dunant was the founder of the Swiss branch of the Young Men's Christian Association YMCA. [CLS] [SEP]
