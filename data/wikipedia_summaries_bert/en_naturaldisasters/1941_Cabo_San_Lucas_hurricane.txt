The 1941 Cabo San Lucas hurricane is considered one of the worst tropical cyclones on record to affect Cabo San Lucas. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane was first reported on September 8 off the coast of Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] It slowly moved northwestward while intensifying. [CLS] [SEP] After peaking in intensity, it entered the Gulf of California, and weakened rapidly. [CLS] [SEP] It dissipated on September 13. [CLS] [SEP] This system brought winds and heavy rain to the southern tip of the Baja California Peninsula. [CLS] [SEP] The hurricane destroyed poorer sections of La Paz and its nearby villages. [CLS] [SEP] Two villages were completely destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Furthermore, Cabo San Lucas was devastated. [CLS] [SEP] The torrential rains damaged roads and left thousands homeless. [CLS] [SEP] Initially following the storm, Cabo San Lucas was abandoned, but after World War II, most of the destroyed buildings were rebuilt. [CLS] [SEP] The remnants of the storm later brought rains to California and Arizona, peaking at 3.54 in (90 mm) at Mormon Lake. [CLS] [SEP]
