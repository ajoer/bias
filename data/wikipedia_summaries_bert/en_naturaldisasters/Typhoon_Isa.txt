Typhoon Isa was the first of eleven super-typhoons to occur during the 1997 Pacific typhoon season. [CLS] [SEP] The second tropical cyclone of the season, Isa developed from a disturbance in the monsoon trough near the Caroline Islands on April 12. [CLS] [SEP] It moved erratically at first, though after attaining tropical storm status it curved westward due to the subtropical ridge to its north. [CLS] [SEP] Isa very gradually intensified, and on April 20 the typhoon reached peak 1-min winds of 270 km/h (165 mph), as reported by the Joint Typhoon Warning Center; Japan Meteorological Agency reported maximum 10-min winds of 155 km/h (100 mph). [CLS] [SEP] After turning northward, it accelerated to the northeast, and merged with a larger extratropical cyclone on April 24. [CLS] [SEP] Early in its duration, Isa caused light rainfall and moderate winds on Pohnpei. [CLS] [SEP] Later, a stationary rainband from the typhoon dropped heavy precipitation on Guam during its dry season. [CLS] [SEP] Damage in the Guam National Weather Service area of responsibility totaled $1 million (1997 USD, $1.59 million 2020 USD), the majority of it from crop damage. [CLS] [SEP] No deaths were reported. [CLS] [SEP]
