[CLS] Hurricane Faith reached the northernmost latitude and had the longest track of any Atlantic tropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] The sixth named storm and fifth hurricane of the 1966 Atlantic hurricane season, Faith developed from an area of disturbed weather between Cape Verde and the west coast of Africa on August 21. [SEP] [CLS] Tracking westward, the depression gradually intensified and became Tropical Storm Faith on the following day. [SEP] [CLS] Moving westward across the Atlantic Ocean, it continued to slowly strengthen, reaching hurricane status early on August 23. [SEP] [CLS] About 42 hours later, Faith reached an initial peak with winds of 105 mph (165 km/h), before weakening slightly on August 26. [SEP] [CLS] Located near the Lesser Antilles, the outer bands of Faith produced gale force winds in the region, especially Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, and Antigua. [SEP] [CLS] Minor coastal damage occurred as far south as Trinidad and Tobago. [SEP] [CLS] By August 28, the storm began to re-intensify, after curving north-northwestward near The Bahamas. [SEP] [CLS] Faith finally weakened while north of Great Britain and became extratropical near the Faroe Islands on September 6.
