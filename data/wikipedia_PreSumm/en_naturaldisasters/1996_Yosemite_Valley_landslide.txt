[CLS] The 1996 Yosemite Valley landslide occurred on July 10, 1996, near the Happy Isles trailhead in Yosemite Valley, Yosemite National Park, Mariposa County, California. [SEP] [CLS] 162,000 tons of rocks and other debris fell to the ground at over 160 miles per hour. [SEP] [CLS] Of the 12 campers/hikers involved in the incident, one was killed. [SEP] [CLS] The landslide competes with the 1997 Merced River flood and the 2013 Rim wildfire for the designation of the worst natural disaster in Yosemite to date. [SEP] [CLS]
