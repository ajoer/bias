The 1138 Aleppo earthquake was among the deadliest earthquakes in history. [CLS] [SEP] Its name was taken from the city of Aleppo, in northern Syria, where the most casualties were sustained. [CLS] [SEP] The quake occurred on 11 October 1138 and was preceded by a smaller quake on the 10th. [CLS] [SEP] It is frequently listed as the third deadliest earthquake in history, following on from the Shensi and Tangshan earthquakes in China. [CLS] [SEP] However, the figure of 230,000 dead is based on a historical conflation of this earthquake with earthquakes in November 1137 on the Jazira plain and the large seismic event of 30 September 1139 in the Transcaucasian city of Ganja. [CLS] [SEP] The first mention of a 230,000 death toll was by Ibn Taghribirdi in the fifteenth century. [CLS] [SEP]
