Typhoon Faxai, also known as the Reiwa 1 Bōsō Peninsula Typhoon (令和元年房総半島台風, Reiwa Gannen Bōsō-hantō Taifū), was the first typhoon to strike the Kantō region since Mindulle in 2016, and the strongest typhoon to hit the region since Ma-on in 2004. [CLS] [SEP] It was also the worst to hit the region since Talas in 2011, until the region was hit by more destructive Typhoon Hagibis less than a month later. [CLS] [SEP] Forming as the fifteenth named storm of the 2019 Pacific typhoon season, the precursor to Faxai was first noted as a weak tropical depression to the east of the International Dateline on August 29. [CLS] [SEP] The depression then entered the West Pacific basin on August 30. [CLS] [SEP] After moving in a general westward direction, the system strengthened into a named tropical storm by September 5. [CLS] [SEP] Faxai then strengthened into the sixth typhoon of the season the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Two days later, Faxai reached its peak strength as a Category 4 typhoon just before making landfall in mainland Japan. [CLS] [SEP] Turning northeastward, Faxai rapidly weakened and became extratropical on September 10. [CLS] [SEP] Three people were killed and 147 others were injured. [CLS] [SEP] More than 390,000 people were urged to be evacuated. [CLS] [SEP] Faxai left 934,000 households without power. [CLS] [SEP] Train services in JR East were cancelled due to the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Two people died from heatstroke because of the power outage. [CLS] [SEP] Total damages from the typhoon in Japan are currently estimated at US$8.12 billion. [CLS] [SEP]
