[CLS] The 2005 Hindu Kush earthquake hit northeastern Afghanistan with a magnitude of 6.5 on December 12 at 21:47 (UTC). [SEP] [CLS] According to the United States Geological Survey's ShakeMap and Did You Feel It? [SEP] [CLS] products, the maximum Mercalli intensity was VI (Strong) at Chitral. [SEP] [CLS] Five people were killed in the Hindu Kush region and landslides blocked several roads near Bagh, Kashmir. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake occurred some 65 miles away from Faizabad, a city in the Hindu Kush mountains, but it could be felt in many neighboring areas. [SEP] [CLS] It could even be felt about 200 miles away in Islamabad, Pakistan. [SEP] [CLS] The quake was strong enough to trigger panic among survivors of October's devastating earthquake, who came out from their makeshift shelters in freezing temperatures. [SEP] [CLS] Although magnitude-6 earthquakes typically cause severe damage, this quake caused relatively little due to the fact that it occurred deep underground (224.6 km (140 mi)). [SEP] [CLS]
