On 1 October 2012, Riama, a 1934 vintage de Havilland DH.84 Dragon passenger aircraft, crashed in Queensland, Australia, while flying from Monto to Caboolture. [CLS] [SEP] Radio contact was lost about an hour after the pilot reported to be in cloud with zero visibility. [CLS] [SEP] The wreckage was found in heavily wooded, hilly terrain two days later. [CLS] [SEP] The pilot and five passengers were killed in the accident. [CLS] [SEP] The investigation by the Australian Transport Safety Bureau (ATSB) found that the pilot most likely fell victim to spatial disorientation and lost control of the aircraft. [CLS] [SEP] He was not qualified for instrument flight and the aircraft was not equipped for such operations. [CLS] [SEP] The weather at the time was reported as low clouds and rain. [CLS] [SEP]
