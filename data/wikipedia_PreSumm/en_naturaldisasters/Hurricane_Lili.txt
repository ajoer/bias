[CLS] Hurricane Lili was the second costliest, deadliest, and strongest hurricane of the 2002 Atlantic hurricane season, only surpassed by Hurricane Isidore, which affected the same areas around a week before Lili. [SEP] [CLS] Lili was the twelfth named storm, fourth hurricane, and second major hurricane of the 2002 Atlantic hurricane season. [SEP] [CLS] The storm developed from a tropical disturbance in the open Atlantic on September 21. [SEP] [CLS] It continued westward, affecting the Lesser Antilles as a tropical storm, then entered the Caribbean. [SEP] [CLS] As it moved west, the storm dissipated while being affected by wind shear south of Cuba, and regenerated when the vertical wind shear weakened. [SEP] [CLS] It turned to the northwest and strengthened up to category 2 strength on October 1. [SEP] [CLS] Lili made two landfalls in western Cuba later that day, and then entered the Gulf of Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] The hurricane rapidly strengthened on October 2, reaching Category 4 strength that afternoon. [SEP] [CLS] It weakened rapidly thereafter, and hit Louisiana as a Category 1 hurricane on October 3. [SEP] [CLS] It moved inland and dissipated on October 6.Lili caused extensive damage through the Caribbean, particularly to crops and poorly built homes. [SEP] [CLS] Mudslides were common on the more mountainous islands, particularly Haiti and Jamaica. [SEP] [CLS] In the United States, the storm cut off the production of oil within the Gulf of Mexico, and caused severe damage in parts of Louisiana. [SEP] [CLS] Lili was also responsible for severe damage to the barrier islands and marshes in the southern portion of the state. [SEP] [CLS] Total damage amounted to $925 million (2002 USD), and the storm killed 15 people during its existence. [SEP] [CLS]
