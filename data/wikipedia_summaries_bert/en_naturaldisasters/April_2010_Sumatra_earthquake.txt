The April 2010 Sumatra earthquake occurred on April 7 at 5:15 AM local time with a moment magnitude of 7.8 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe). [CLS] [SEP] The shock occurred near Banyak Islands, off the island of Sumatra in Indonesia. [CLS] [SEP] A tsunami watch was issued according to the Pacific Tsunami Warning Center in Honolulu which was later canceled. [CLS] [SEP] A 40 cm surge was reported in the Banyak Islands an hour after the quake, along with 62 injuries. [CLS] [SEP] Power outages were reported throughout the province of North Sumatra as well as in Aceh. [CLS] [SEP] This quake is one of the sequence of large earthquakes along the Sunda megathrust in 2000s. [CLS] [SEP] In Simeulue Regency, 21 were hospitalized in Gunung Putih, Teluk Dalam subdistrict, and 41 were injured in Teupah Selatan subdistrict. [CLS] [SEP] Some of the injured were treated at Simeulue general hospital in Sinabang. [CLS] [SEP]
