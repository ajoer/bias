[CLS] Jacksonville State University (JSU) is a public university in Jacksonville, Alabama. [SEP] [CLS] Founded in 1883, Jacksonville State offers programs of study in six academic schools leading to bachelor's, master's, education specialist, and doctorate degrees in addition to certificate programs and continuing education opportunities. [SEP] [CLS] In the Fall semester of 2011, JSU began offering the school's first doctoral degree, Doctor of Science in Emergency Management. [SEP] [CLS] In 2016, the university gained approval to offer its second doctorate, a Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) degree. [SEP] [CLS] The university was founded as Jacksonville State Normal School, and in 1930 the name changed to Jacksonville State Teachers College, and again in 1957 to Jacksonville State College. [SEP] [CLS] The university began operating as Jacksonville State University in 1966. [SEP] [CLS] JSU currently has an enrollment of more than 9,000 students, with nearly 500 faculty members (more than 320 of whom are full-time). [SEP] [CLS] Jacksonville State's Business School was ranked within the nation's 90th percentile by the Princeton Review. [SEP] [CLS] It is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS). [SEP] [CLS] In addition, 40 academic programs (79% of programs that can be accredited) earned specialized programmatic accreditations. [SEP] [CLS] These programs include business, education, applied engineering, nursing, social work, drama, art, music, computer science, family and consumer science, and communication. [SEP] [CLS]
