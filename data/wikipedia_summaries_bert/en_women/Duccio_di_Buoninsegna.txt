Duccio di Buoninsegna (UK: DOO-chee-oh, Italian: [ˈduttʃo di ˌbwɔninˈseɲɲa]; c. [CLS] [SEP] 1255–1260 – c. [CLS] [SEP] 1318–1319) was an Italian painter active in Siena, Tuscany, in the late 13th and early 14th century. [CLS] [SEP] He was hired throughout his life to complete many important works in government and religious buildings around Italy. [CLS] [SEP] Duccio is considered one of the greatest Italian painters of the Middle Ages, and is credited with creating the painting styles of Trecento and the Sienese school. [CLS] [SEP] He also contributed significantly to the Sienese Gothic style. [CLS] [SEP]
