[CLS] Typhoon Cecil, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Rubing, was one of two typhoons to strike Vietnam within a week in 1985. [SEP] [CLS] Cecil originated from an area of convection that tracked west-northwest and passed south of Palau late on October 9. [SEP] [CLS] The disturbance became increasingly defined as it moved through the southern Philippines. [SEP] [CLS] On October 12, a tropical depression developed, and the next day, the depression was upgraded into a tropical storm. [SEP] [CLS] Cecil turned northwest over the open waters of the South China Sea as it steadily strengthened, and was classified as a typhoon on October 14. [SEP] [CLS] After unexpectedly slowing down, Cecil continued to intensify and at noon of October 14, reached its peak intensity of 145 km/h (90 mph). [SEP] [CLS] Land interaction with Vietnam triggered a weakening trend. [SEP] [CLS] After turning west, Cecil moved onshore just north of Huáº¿ at 22:00 UTC on October 15. [SEP] [CLS] At least 7,000 schools were damaged and 1,900 others were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Moreover, 70,000 t (77,160 short tons) of rice were wiped away. [SEP] [CLS] Approximately 3,000 ha (7,400 acres) of farmland were flooded and at least 225,000 others were left homeless. [SEP] [CLS] In all, damage was estimated at US$65 million. [SEP] [CLS] Typhoon Cecil was considered the worst natural disaster in the central portion of the country in history. [SEP] [CLS] Elsewhere, one person was killed in Thailand. [SEP] [CLS]
