Matija Gogala (born December 11, 1937) is a Slovene entomologist, working mainly in the fields of insect bioacoustics, physiology and taxonomy. [CLS] [SEP] He studied biology at the University of Ljubljana where he graduated in 1960 and became an assistant at the Department of biology (Biotechnical faculty). [CLS] [SEP] He received his doctorate in 1964. [CLS] [SEP] Later, he became a docent at the department and began teaching animal physiology. [CLS] [SEP] He obtained full professorship in 1981. [CLS] [SEP] In the meantime, he worked at the National Institute of Biology in Ljubljana, where he served as a director between 1976 and 1979. [CLS] [SEP] In 1987, he began working at Slovenian Museum of Natural History and served also as its director between 1992 and 2001. [CLS] [SEP] In 1991, he became an associate, and in 1999 a full member of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts, currently serving as its vice president. [CLS] [SEP] Gogala is also a member of several international scientific committees and boards and the president of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility board for Slovenia. [CLS] [SEP] His son, Andrej Gogala, is also an entomologist. [CLS] [SEP]
