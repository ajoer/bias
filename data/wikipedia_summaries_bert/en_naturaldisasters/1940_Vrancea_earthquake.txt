The 1940 Vrancea earthquake, also known as the 1940 Bucharest earthquake, (Romanian: Cutremurul din 1940) occurred on Sunday, 10 November 1940, in Romania, at 03:39 (local time), when the majority of the population was at home. [CLS] [SEP] The 1940 earthquake registered a magnitude of 7.7 on the moment magnitude scale, being the strongest earthquake recorded in the 20th century in Romania. [CLS] [SEP] Its epicenter lay in the Vrancea zone at a depth of about 133 km. [CLS] [SEP] The pleistoseist area of this earthquake was of 80,000 km² and the macroseismic effects were felt on a surface of over 2,000,000 km², being reported on vast areas, thus: in the north up to Saint Petersburg, at over 1,300 km, where there have been estimated seismic intensities of IV–V (MCS degrees), in the south, up to Greece, in the east, up to the Kharkov–Moscow line, with estimated intensities of V–VI (MCS degrees), in the west, up to Belgrade, Budapest, Warsaw.
