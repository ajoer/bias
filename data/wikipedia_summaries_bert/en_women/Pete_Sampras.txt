Petros "Pete" Sampras (born August 12, 1971) is an American former professional tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] His professional career began in 1988 and ended at the 2002 US Open, which he won, defeating rival Andre Agassi in the final. [CLS] [SEP] Sampras won 14 Grand Slam singles titles during his career, which was an Open Era record at the time of his retirement: seven Wimbledon, two Australian Opens and a joint Open Era record five US Open titles. [CLS] [SEP] He won 64 singles titles in total. [CLS] [SEP] He first reached world No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 in 1993, and held that position for a total of 286 weeks (second of all time), including an Open Era record of six consecutive year-end No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 rankings from 1993 to 1998. [CLS] [SEP] A right-handed player with a single-handed backhand, his precise and powerful serve earned him the nickname "Pistol Pete". [CLS] [SEP] In 2007, he was inducted into the International Tennis Hall of Fame. [CLS] [SEP]
