The February 2007 North American blizzard was a massive winter storm that affected most of the eastern half of North America, starting on February 12, 2007 and peaking on Valentine's Day, February 14. [CLS] [SEP] The storm produced heavy snowfalls across the midwestern United States from Nebraska to Ohio and produced similar conditions across parts of the northeastern United States, and into Canada in Ontario, Quebec and New Brunswick. [CLS] [SEP] Significant sleet and freezing rain fell across the southern Ohio Valley and affected portions of the east coast of the United States, including the cities of Boston, Baltimore, Washington, D.C., New York City and Philadelphia. [CLS] [SEP] The southern portion of the storm produced severe thunderstorms with numerous tornadoes reported. [CLS] [SEP] One tornado hit a subdivision of New Orleans that was still recovering from the effects of Hurricane Katrina, which hit the region in August 2005. [CLS] [SEP] In total, this storm system was responsible for 37 deaths across 13 U.S. [CLS] [SEP] states and Canadian provinces of New Brunswick, Ontario and Quebec. [CLS] [SEP] The NOAA classified the storm as a Category 3 "Major" storm. [CLS] [SEP] The National Weather Service has determined that this storm was one of the three largest snowstorms to hit the inland areas of the northeastern United States since 1940. [CLS] [SEP]
