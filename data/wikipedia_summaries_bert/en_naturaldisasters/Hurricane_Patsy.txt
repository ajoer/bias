[CLS] The 1959 Pacific hurricane season featured the first Category 5 hurricane ever recorded in the Central Pacific basin. [SEP] [CLS] During the season, 15 storms developed, 5 of those became hurricanes, and 3 of those became major hurricanes. [SEP] [CLS] The strongest of the storms was Hurricane Dot, which was a Category 4 and made landfall on Kauai, Hawaii, as a Category 1. [SEP] [CLS] Patsy reached 175 miles per hour (282 km/h) winds, but had a pressure higher than Dot. [SEP] [CLS] The deadliest storm of the season was Hurricane Fifteen, which made landfall in Mexico at Category 4 and killed 1,800 people. [SEP] [CLS]
