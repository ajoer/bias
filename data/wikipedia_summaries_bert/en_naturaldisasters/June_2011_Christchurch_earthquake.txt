[CLS] The June 2011 Christchurch earthquake was a shallow magnitude 6.0 Mw earthquake that occurred on 13 June 2011 at 14:20 NZST (02:20 UTC). [SEP] [CLS] It was centred at a depth of 7 km (4.3 mi), about 5 km (3 mi) south-east of Christchurch, which had previously been devastated by a magnitude 6.2 MW earthquake in February 2011. [SEP] [CLS] The June quake was preceded by a magnitude 5.9 ML tremor that struck the region at a slightly deeper 8.9 km (5.5 mi). [SEP] [CLS] The United States Geological Survey reported a magnitude of 6.0 Mw and a depth of 9 km (5.6 mi). [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake produced severe shaking, registering at VIII on the Mercalli scale in and around Christchurch. [SEP] [CLS] It destroyed several structures and caused additional damage to many others which had been affected by previous earthquakes. [SEP] [CLS] The damaged tower of the historic Lyttelton Timeball Station collapsed before dismantling work could be completed. [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake downed phone lines and cut off power to 54,000 households. [SEP] [CLS] Restoration costs in Christchurch following the February earthquake were estimated to increase by NZ$6 billion (US$4.8 billion) because of the additional damage from this event. [SEP] [CLS] Forty-six people suffered injuries, two of which critical, and one elderly man died after being knocked unconscious. [SEP] [CLS]
