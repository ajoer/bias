[CLS] Ptolemy X Alexander I (Greek: Πτολεμαῖος Ἀλέξανδρος, Ptolemaĩos Aléxandros) was King of Egypt from 107 BC till his death in 88 BC, in co-regency with Cleopatra III as Ptolemy Philometor Soter until 101 BC, and then with Berenice III as Ptolemy Philadelphus. [SEP] [CLS] He was a son of Ptolemy VIII Physcon and Cleopatra III, and younger brother of Ptolemy IX. [SEP] [CLS] His birth name was probably Alexander. [SEP] [CLS] When Ptolemy VIII died in 116 BC, Ptolemy IX became king with Cleopatra III as his co-regent and Ptolemy X was sent to Cyprus to serve as governor. [SEP] [CLS] However, in 114/13 BC, he declared himself king. [SEP] [CLS] Cleopatra III quarrelled with Ptolemy IX and arranged for Ptolemy X to return to Egypt in 107 BC and replace his brother as co-regent. [SEP] [CLS] The pair fought a war against Ptolemy IX in the Hasmonean kingdom (103-102 BC), in which Ptolemy X successfully prevented his brother from invading Egypt. [SEP] [CLS] In 101 BC, Ptolemy X had his mother murdered and appointed his niece-wife Berenice III as co-regent. [SEP] [CLS] An Egyptian uprising in 91 BC caused him to lose control of the south of the country. [SEP] [CLS] In 88 BC, the people expelled him from Alexandria, recalling Ptolemy IX to the throne. [SEP] [CLS] Ptolemy X raised an army with Roman help and invaded Cyprus, but was killed. [SEP] [CLS]
