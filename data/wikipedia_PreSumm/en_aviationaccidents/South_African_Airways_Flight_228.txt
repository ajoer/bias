[CLS] South African Airways Flight 228 was a scheduled flight from Jan Smuts International Airport in Johannesburg, South Africa, to London Heathrow International Airport in England. [SEP] [CLS] The plane operating the flight, which was only 6 weeks old, flew into the ground soon after take-off after a scheduled stopover in Windhoek, South West Africa (present day Namibia) on 20 April 1968. [SEP] [CLS] Five passengers survived, while 123 people died. [SEP] [CLS] The subsequent investigation determined that the accident was attributable largely to pilot error; the manufacturer subsequently also recognised the lack of a ground proximity warning system in its aircraft. [SEP] [CLS] The accident is the deadliest aviation accident to date in Namibia. [SEP] [CLS]
