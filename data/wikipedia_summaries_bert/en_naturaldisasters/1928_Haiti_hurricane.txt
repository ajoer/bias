The 1928 Haiti hurricane was considered the worst tropical cyclone in Haiti since the 1886 Indianola hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] The second tropical cyclone and second hurricane of the season, the storm developed from a tropical wave near Tobago on August 7. [CLS] [SEP] Steadily intensifying as it moved northwestward, the system passed through the southern Windward Islands. [CLS] [SEP] Upon entering the Caribbean Sea early on August 8, the tropical depression strengthened into a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] On August 9, the storm strengthened to the equivalent of a Category 1 hurricane. [CLS] [SEP] The next day, the hurricane peaked with winds of 90 mph (150 km/h). [CLS] [SEP] After striking the Tiburon Peninsula of Haiti, the cyclone began weakening and fell to tropical storm intensity on August 12. [CLS] [SEP] By midday on the following day, the storm made landfall near Cienfuegos, Cuba. [CLS] [SEP] Upon emerging into the Straits of Florida, the storm began to re-strengthen. [CLS] [SEP] Early on August 13, it struck Big Pine Key, Florida, as a strong tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] Weakening slowly while moving north-northwestward, the system made another landfall near St. [CLS] [SEP] George Island. [CLS] [SEP] After moving inland, the tropical storm slowly deteriorated and dissipated over West Virginia on August 17. [CLS] [SEP] In Haiti, the storm completely wiped out livestock and many crops, particularly coffee, cocoa, and sugar. [CLS] [SEP] Several villages were also destroyed, rendering approximately 10,000 people homeless. [CLS] [SEP] Damage reached $1 million and there were at least 200 deaths. [CLS] [SEP] The only impact in Cuba was downed banana trees. [CLS] [SEP] In Florida, the storm left minor wind damage along the coast. [CLS] [SEP] A Seaboard Air Line Railroad station was destroyed in Boca Grande, while signs, trees, and telephone poles were knocked down in Sarasota. [CLS] [SEP] Several streets in St. [CLS] [SEP] Petersburg were closed due to flooding or debris. [CLS] [SEP] Between Cedar Key and the Florida Panhandle, several vessels capsized. [CLS] [SEP] Water washed up along the side of roads and in wooded areas. [CLS] [SEP] The storm contributed to flooding onset by the previous hurricane, with rainfall peaking at 13.5 in (340 mm) in Caesars Head, South Carolina. [CLS] [SEP] The worst impact from flooding occurred in North Carolina, where several houses were destroyed. [CLS] [SEP] Six people were killed in the state, of which four due to flooding. [CLS] [SEP] Property damage in the state totaled over $1 million. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, the storm caused at least $2 million in damage and 210 fatalities. [CLS] [SEP]
