[CLS] Tropical Airways Flight 1301 (TBG1301/M7 1301) was a domestic short-haul passenger flight, flying from Hugo Chávez International Airport in Cap-Haïtien, Haiti to the commune of Port-de-Paix which crashed onto a sugarcane field less than 10 minutes after take off on the evening of 24 August 2003. [SEP] [CLS] The aircraft was a 19-seater Let L-410 Turbolet carrying 19 passengers and 2 crew. [SEP] [CLS] Witnesses stated that the aircraft caught fire during take-off and exploded when it hit the ground. [SEP] [CLS] All on board were killed. [SEP] [CLS] The crash is currently the second deadliest aircraft crash in Haiti and the second worst accident involving a Let L-410 Turbolet, after Sakha Avia Flight 301. [SEP] [CLS] The Haitian investigative board stated that multiple factors caused the crash, one of which was an opened cargo door during mid-flight. [SEP] [CLS] The pilots were struggling to return to the airport. [SEP] [CLS] Subsequent poor CRM contributed to the crash. [SEP] [CLS]
