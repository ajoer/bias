Alaska's Mount Redoubt volcano began erupting on March 22, 2009, and activity continued for several months. [CLS] [SEP] During the eruptions, which lasted for several months, reports found ash clouds reaching as high as 65,000 feet (20,000 m) above sea level. [CLS] [SEP] In response, the National Weather Service issued a series of ash fall advisories. [CLS] [SEP] The Mat-Su Valley, Anchorage, Valdez and large portions of the Kenai Peninsula all received coatings of tephra.
