The 2005 Logan Airport runway incursion was a near runway collision that occurred at approximately 7:40 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] Eastern Daylight Time on June 9, 2005 between US Airways Flight 1170 (US1170) and Aer Lingus Flight 132 (EI132). [CLS] [SEP] EI132 was an Airbus A330-300 aircraft, owned and operated by the Irish airline Aer Lingus, destined for Shannon, Ireland, and carrying 12 crew members and 260 passengers. [CLS] [SEP] US1170 was a Boeing 737-300 aircraft owned by Wells Fargo Bank Northwest NA and operated by US Airways, destined for Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and carrying 6 crew members and 103 passengers. [CLS] [SEP] The near collision took place on the runway at General Edward Lawrence Logan International Airport (BOS), in Boston, Massachusetts. [CLS] [SEP] In total 381 people were on board the two aircraft. [CLS] [SEP]
