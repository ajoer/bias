[CLS] The Papua New Guinea earthquake was a magnitude 7.5 earthquake that occurred in the Hela Province of Papua New Guinea on 26 February 2018 (25 February UTC), at 3:44 a.m. [SEP] [CLS] local time (25 February, 17:44 UTC). [SEP] [CLS] The earthquake's epicenter was 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) west of the town of Komo. [SEP] [CLS] The maximum felt intensity was IX (Violent) on the Mercalli intensity scale. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 160 people were killed and many others were injured. [SEP] [CLS] An aftershock of M6.0 killed 11 people on 4 March, while another aftershock of M6.7 occurred at 00:13 local time on 7 March, killing at least 25 more. [SEP] [CLS] A 6.3 aftershock killed another 4 people on 7 April, more than a month after the first tremors hit the area. [SEP] [CLS]
