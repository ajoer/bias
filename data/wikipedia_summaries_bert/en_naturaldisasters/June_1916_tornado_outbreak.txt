The June 1916 tornado outbreak was a deadly severe-weather episode that produced at least 35 tornadoes across the Southern United States on June 5–6, 1916. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak killed at least 112 people, 76 of them in the U.S. [CLS] [SEP] state of Arkansas alone. [CLS] [SEP] Unconfirmed reports suggested higher totals in rural areas. [CLS] [SEP] The outbreak was the deadliest June tornado outbreak in the state and one of the largest outbreaks in Arkansas history, with at least 24 significant tornadoes in-state. [CLS] [SEP] The deadliest tornado of the outbreak and the deadliest to strike Arkansas on June 5 was a powerful F4 tornado that hit Heber Springs, killing 25 people. [CLS] [SEP] Other deadly tornadoes struck much of the state and in nearby parts of Missouri and Illinois. [CLS] [SEP] Overnight on June 5–6, tornadoes spread east and south into Louisiana, Tennessee, and Mississippi, with an F3 tornado hitting the northern suburbs of Jackson, Mississippi, killing 13 people there.
