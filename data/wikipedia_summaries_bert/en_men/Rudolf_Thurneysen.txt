[CLS] [SEP] His teachers included Ernst Windisch and Heinrich Zimmer. [CLS] [SEP] He received his promotion (approximating to a doctorate) in 1879 and his habilitation, in Latin and the Celtic languages, followed at the University of Jena in 1882. [CLS] [SEP] From 1885 to 1887 he taught Latin at Jena, then taking up the Chair of Comparative Philology at the University of Freiburg where he replaced Karl Brugmann, a renowned expert in Indo-European studies. [CLS] [SEP] In 1909 Thurneysen published his Handbuch des Alt-Irischen, translated into English as A Grammar of Old Irish by D. [CLS] [SEP] A. [CLS] [SEP] Binchy and Osborn Bergin, and still in print as of 2006. [CLS] [SEP] In 1913 he moved to the University of Bonn. [CLS] [SEP] It is in this period that Thurneysen has been called the greatest living authority on Old Irish. [CLS] [SEP] He retired in 1923 and died in Bonn in 1940. [CLS] [SEP] The Rudolf Thurneysen Memorial Lecture (German: Vortrag in Memoriam Rudolf Thurneysen), given at Bonn, is named in his honour. [CLS] [SEP]
