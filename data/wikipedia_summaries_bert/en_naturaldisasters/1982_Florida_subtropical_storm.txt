The 1982 Florida subtropical storm, officially known as Subtropical Storm One, was the only subtropical cyclone of the inactive 1982 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] The storm originated from two different systems around the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean Sea on June 16. [CLS] [SEP] The systems merged into trough while a circulation began to form off the coast of Florida on June 18. [CLS] [SEP] The depression made landfall in Florida and strengthened into a storm over land. [CLS] [SEP] The storm entered the Atlantic Ocean and headed to the northeast, becoming extratropical on June 20 near Newfoundland. [CLS] [SEP] The storm was the only system of 1982 to affect the eastern half of the United States, and it caused three fatalities and caused $10 million in damage (1982 USD, $21 million 2007 USD).
