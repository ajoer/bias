TransAsia Airways Flight 235 was a TransAsia Airways domestic flight from Taipei to Kinmen (Quemoy), Republic of China. [CLS] [SEP] On 4 February 2015, the aircraft serving the flight, a ten-month-old ATR 72-600, crashed into the Keelung River shortly after takeoff from Taipei Songshan Airport, 5.4 km (3.4 mi; 2.9 nmi) to the east of Songshan in Taiwan. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft had 53 passengers and five crew on board; there were 15 survivors. [CLS] [SEP] Two minutes after takeoff, the pilots reported an engine flameout. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 235 climbed to a maximum height of 1,510 feet (460 m), then descended. [CLS] [SEP] The other engine, still working, was shut down mistakenly. [CLS] [SEP] Immediately before crashing into the river, it banked sharply left and clipped a taxi travelling west on the Huandong Viaduct (causing two more injuries), then the viaduct itself, with its left wing. [CLS] [SEP] Flight 235 was the second fatal accident involving a TransAsia Airways ATR aircraft within seven months: Flight 222 had crashed on 23 July 2014, killing 48 of the 58 on board.
