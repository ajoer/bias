The 1932 Freeport hurricane was an intense tropical cyclone that primarily affected areas of the Texas coast in August of the 1932 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] It was the second storm and first hurricane of the season, developing just off the Yucat√°n Peninsula on August 12. [CLS] [SEP] While moving to the northwest, the storm began to quickly intensify the next day. [CLS] [SEP] It rapidly intensified from a category 1 hurricane to a category 4 with winds estimated at 150 mph (240 km/h) and an estimated central pressure of 935 mbar (27.6 inHg) shortly before making landfall near Freeport, Texas, early on August 14. [CLS] [SEP] After landfall, the hurricane began to quickly weaken before dissipating over the Texas Panhandle. [CLS] [SEP] The storm caused heavy rainfall inland, peaking at 9.93 in (252 mm) in Angleton. [CLS] [SEP] Record rainfall rates were also reported in some areas. [CLS] [SEP] Power outages were reported in Galveston and San Antonio. [CLS] [SEP] The storm caused $7.5 million in damages, mostly to crops, and 40 deaths. [CLS] [SEP]
