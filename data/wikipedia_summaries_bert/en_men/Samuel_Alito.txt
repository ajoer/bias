Samuel Anthony Alito Jr. [CLS] [SEP] (; born April 1, 1950) is an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States. [CLS] [SEP] He was nominated by President George W. [CLS] [SEP] Bush on October 31, 2005, and has served since January 31, 2006.Raised in Hamilton Township, New Jersey, and educated at Princeton University and Yale Law School, Alito served as U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Attorney for the District of New Jersey and a judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit before joining the Supreme Court. [CLS] [SEP] He is the 110th Justice, the second Italian American, and the eleventh Roman Catholic to serve on the court. [CLS] [SEP] Alito is considered "one of the most conservative justices on the Court". [CLS] [SEP] He has described himself as a "practical originalist." Alito's majority opinions in landmark cases include McDonald v. [CLS] [SEP] Chicago, Burwell v. [CLS] [SEP] Hobby Lobby, Murphy v. [CLS] [SEP] NCAA, and Janus v. [CLS] [SEP] AFSCME. [CLS] [SEP]
