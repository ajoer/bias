Cilly Aussem (German pronunciation: [ˈʦiːli̯ə ˈaʊ̯sm]; 4 January 1909 – 22 March 1963) was a German female tennis player. [CLS] [SEP] She was the first German, male or female, to win the singles title at Wimbledon, which she did in 1931. [CLS] [SEP] She also won the women's single titles at the French Championships and German Championships in 1931. [CLS] [SEP] Aussem's coach and mixed doubles partner was Bill Tilden. [CLS] [SEP] They won the mixed doubles title at the 1930 French Championships. [CLS] [SEP] According to A. [CLS] [SEP] Wallis Myers of The Daily Telegraph and the Daily Mail, Aussem was ranked in the world top ten in 1928, 1930, 1931, and 1934, reaching a career high of World No. [CLS] [SEP] 2 in those rankings in 1930 and 1931 behind Helen Wills Moody.
