[CLS] The 1968 Tracy tornado. [SEP] [CLS] was an extremely powerful F5 tornado that struck Tracy, Minnesota on Thursday, June 13, 1968, at around 7 o'clock. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado killed nine people and injured 125 others. [SEP] [CLS] The wind speeds of the tornado reached over 300 mph as it tracked 9 miles (14 km) through Murray, Lyon and Redwood counties in southwestern Minnesota. [SEP] [CLS] It is one of only two official F5 tornadoes that have occurred in Minnesota, although several other tornadoes that occurred before 1950 are estimated to have been F5 strength. [SEP] [CLS] The tornado destroyed 111 homes, caused major damage to 76, and caused minor damage to 114. [SEP] [CLS] Five businesses were destroyed and 15 others were damaged. [SEP] [CLS] Some homes in town only had their foundations left behind. [SEP] [CLS] A few farms outside of town were swept completely away, and extensive ground scouring occurred. [SEP] [CLS] An elementary school and 106 automobiles were destroyed, and a heavy boxcar was thrown more than a block by the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Two other boxcars were thrown 300 yards, and a steel I-beam was carried for two miles on a piece of roof. [SEP] [CLS] Hard rains and hail were also reported. [SEP] [CLS] A total of five tornadoes hit Minnesota on June 13, 1968, most of which were rated F0. [SEP] [CLS]
