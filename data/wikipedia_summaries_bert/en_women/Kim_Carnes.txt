Kim Carnes (born July 20, 1945) is an American singer-songwriter. [CLS] [SEP] Born and raised in Los Angeles, California, she began her career as a songwriter in the 1960s, writing for other artists while performing in local clubs and working as a session background singer with the famed Waters sisters (featured in the documentary 20 Feet from Stardom). [CLS] [SEP] After she signed her first publishing deal with Jimmy Bowen, she released her debut album Rest on Me in 1972. [CLS] [SEP] Carnes' self-titled second album primarily contained self-penned songs, including her first charting single "You're a Part of Me", which reached No. [CLS] [SEP] 35 on the Billboard Adult Contemporary chart in 1975. [CLS] [SEP] In the following year, Carnes released Sailin', which featured "Love Comes from Unexpected Places". [CLS] [SEP] The song won the American Song Festival and the award for Best Composition at the Tokyo Song Festival in 1976. [CLS] [SEP] In her breakthrough year, 1980, Carnes was commissioned by Kenny Rogers to co-write the songs for his concept album Gideon (1980), and their duet "Don't Fall in Love with a Dreamer" hit No. [CLS] [SEP] 4 on Billboard Hot 100, and earned the duo a Grammy Award nomination. [CLS] [SEP] Later that year, her cover of Smokey Robinson's "More Love", from the album Romance Dance (1980), hit No. [CLS] [SEP] 10. [CLS] [SEP] The following year, Carnes released Mistaken Identity, which featured the worldwide hit, "Bette Davis Eyes". [CLS] [SEP] This became the best-selling single of the year in the United States, spending nine weeks at No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 on the Billboard Hot 100, going Gold, and won the Grammy Awards for Record of the Year and Song of the Year. [CLS] [SEP] Mistaken Identity went to No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 on the Billboard 200, was certified Platinum, and was nominated for the Grammy Award for Album of the Year. [CLS] [SEP] Carnes also saw success with the singles "Draw of the Cards" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 28), "Does It Make You Remember" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 36), "Crazy in the Night (Barking at Airplanes)" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 15), "Make No Mistake, He's Mine" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 51), with Barbra Streisand, "What About Me?" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 15), with Kenny Rogers and James Ingram, "I'll Be Here Where the Heart Is", from the Flashdance soundtrack, and the Grammy Award nominated singles "Voyeur" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 29) and "Invisible Hands" (No. [CLS] [SEP] 40). [CLS] [SEP] Her other successes as a songwriter include co-writing the No. [CLS] [SEP] 1 duet "The Heart Won't Lie" with Donna Weiss (who had co-written "Bette Davis Eyes" with Jackie DeShannon), which was recorded by Vince Gill and Reba McEntire. [CLS] [SEP] Her distinctive, raspy vocal style has drawn comparisons to Rod Stewart. [CLS] [SEP] Her most recent studio album is Chasin' Wild Trains (2004). [CLS] [SEP] As of 2017, Carnes was residing in Nashville, Tennessee, where she continues to write music. [CLS] [SEP]
