Extremely Severe Cyclonic Storm Mekunu was the strongest storm to strike Oman's Dhofar Governorate since 1959. [CLS] [SEP] The second named storm of the 2018 North Indian Ocean cyclone season, Mekunu developed out of a low pressure area on May 21. [CLS] [SEP] It gradually intensified, passing east of Socotra on May 23 as a very intense tropical cyclone. [CLS] [SEP] On May 25, Mekunu reached its peak intensity. [CLS] [SEP] The India Meteorological Department estimated 10 minute sustained winds of 175 km/h (110 mph), making Mekunu an extremely severe cyclonic storm. [CLS] [SEP] The American-based Joint Typhoon Warning Center estimated slightly higher 1 minute winds of 185 km/h (115 mph). [CLS] [SEP] While at peak intensity, Mekunu made landfall near Raysut, Oman, on May 25. [CLS] [SEP] The storm rapidly weakened over land, dissipating on May 27. [CLS] [SEP] While passing near Socotra, Cyclone Mekunu dropped heavy rainfall, causing landslides and flooding that killed 20 people. [CLS] [SEP] In the eastern Yemeni mainland, the cyclone caused power outages and flooding, resulting in four fatalities. [CLS] [SEP] In Oman, Cyclone Mekunu killed seven people and caused about US$1.5 billion in damage. [CLS] [SEP] Rainfall from Mekunu reached 617 mm (24.3 in) in Salalah. [CLS] [SEP] The rainfall created desert lakes in the Empty Quarter, or Rub' al Khali, contributing to a locust outbreak that affected 10 countries, including Pakistan where the outbreak led to a state of emergency.
