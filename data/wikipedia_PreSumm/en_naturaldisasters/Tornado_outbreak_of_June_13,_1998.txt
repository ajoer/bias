[CLS] On June 13, 1998, a tornado outbreak occurred across much of the United States. [SEP] [CLS] The day saw 45 tornadoes touchdown primarily across Kansas, Nebraska, and Oklahoma. [SEP] [CLS] The outbreak saw the Storm Prediction Center outline two Moderate Risk areas in parts of Virginia, North Carolina, and South Carolina, and in parts of Kansas and Nebraska. [SEP] [CLS] Additionally, two far more expansive Slight Risk areas were delineated across much of the Central and Eastern United States. [SEP] [CLS] By 06:00 UTC June 14, tornadoes had struck parts of six states, including downtown Sabetha, Kansas and parts of North Oklahoma City and the vicinity. [SEP] [CLS]
