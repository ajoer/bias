[CLS] Tropical Storm Bolaven, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Agaton, was an early-season tropical cyclone that affected southern parts of the Philippines in January 2018. [SEP] [CLS] The first named storm of the 2018 Pacific typhoon season, Bolaven formed as a tropical depression near Palau on December 29, 2017. [SEP] [CLS] The system moved generally westwards without intensifying and made landfall over northeastern Mindanao on January 1, 2018. [SEP] [CLS] The depression spent the next day traversing the Philippines, making four more landfalls in the Visayas and one in Palawan. [SEP] [CLS] The system strengthened into a tropical storm on January 3 as it entered the South China Sea, receiving the name Bolaven. [SEP] [CLS] However, Bolaven weakened back to a tropical depression just a day later amid a marginal environment and dissipated on January 4 east of Vietnam. [SEP] [CLS] Bolaven brought heavy rain to areas that had been recently impacted by tropical storms Kai-tak and Tembin. [SEP] [CLS] More than 230,000 families in the Philippines were affected and at least 11,000 people sought shelter in evacuation centers. [SEP] [CLS] Floods and landslides killed four people across the country, while damaging houses and blocking roads. [SEP] [CLS] Maritime transport was greatly disrupted, with rough seas stranding over 3,600 people in various ports. [SEP] [CLS] Schools across the country were also shut at various points between January 1 and 3.
