[CLS] Cyclone Jokwe was the first tropical cyclone to make landfall in Mozambique since Cyclone Favio struck in the previous year, and was the most recent cyclone to make landfall on Mozambique until Cyclone Dineo in 2017. [SEP] [CLS] It quickly weakened while paralleling the coastline, though the storm restrengthened as it turned southward in the Mozambique Channel. [SEP] [CLS] Late in its duration, it remained nearly stationary for several days, and steadily weakened due to wind shear before dissipating on March 16. [SEP] [CLS] The storm caused minor damage in northern Madagascar. [SEP] [CLS] In Mozambique, the cyclone affected 200,000 people, and left at least sixteen fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] Cyclone Jokwe destroyed over 9,000 houses and damaged over 3,000 more, with the heaviest damage in Angoche and the Island of Mozambique in Nampula Province. [SEP] [CLS] The storm also caused widespread power outages and crop damages. [SEP] [CLS] The name Jokwe was submitted to the World Meteorological Organization by Botswana. [SEP] [CLS]
