[CLS] Cornelia Oschkenat, (née Riefstahl; born 29 October 1961) is a German former track and field athlete who represented East Germany. [SEP] [CLS] She competed at the 1988 Olympic Games in Seoul. [SEP] [CLS] At the 1987 World Championships in Rome, she won a bronze medal in the 100 metres hurdles with a time of 12.46s as well as silver medal as a member of the East German 4 × 100 metres relay team. [SEP] [CLS] She also won the 1987 World Indoor 60 m hurdles title, two World Cup 100 m hurdles titles and three European Indoor 60 m hurdles titles. [SEP] [CLS] Her world indoor record for the 50 metres hurdles of 6.58 secs in 1988, still stands (as of 2019). [SEP] [CLS] Oschkenat won eleven East German individual national titles, taking five titles in the 100 m hurdles between 1984 and 1989 at the East German Athletics Championships, as well as six consecutive titles in the 60 m hurdles at the East German Indoor Athletics Championships over that period.
