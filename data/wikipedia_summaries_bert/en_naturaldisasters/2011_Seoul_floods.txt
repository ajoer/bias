The 2011 Seoul floods were a series of floods in late July 2011 caused by heavy rainfall around Seoul, South Korea. [CLS] [SEP] The intense rain triggered a series of flash floods and landslides that killed at least 49 people by July 27, leaving a total of more than 77 dead or missing. [CLS] [SEP] On July 27, the number of killed rose further to 69. [CLS] [SEP] The floods occurred primarily around the national capital Seoul and nearby Siheung regions. [CLS] [SEP] At least 86 power outages followed the landslides, affecting 125,000 people by July 27. [CLS] [SEP] Over 11,000 South Koreans were forced to evacuate. [CLS] [SEP]
