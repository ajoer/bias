The 2000 Mozambique flood was a natural disaster that occurred in February and March 2000. [CLS] [SEP] The catastrophic flooding was caused by heavy rainfall that lasted for five weeks and made many homeless. [CLS] [SEP] Approximately 700 people were killed. [CLS] [SEP] 1,400 km2 of arable land was affected and 20,000 head of cattle were lost. [CLS] [SEP] It was the worst flood in Mozambique in 50 years.It started in South Africa when heavy rain falls traveled over to Mozambique. [CLS] [SEP] It caused dozens of deaths. [CLS] [SEP] 44,000 were left homeless and many of them had lost relatives of some kind. [CLS] [SEP] Later, Cyclone Eline came and destroyed many more homes and lives. [CLS] [SEP] The women and children were hurrying to shelter and high land. [CLS] [SEP] 800 had died and thousands of livestock were killed. [CLS] [SEP] The government distributed 15 million dollars (2000 USD) to its citizens to account for damage property and loss of income. [CLS] [SEP] As of 2016, people were still living in recovery shelters with fluctuating water supplies. [CLS] [SEP]
