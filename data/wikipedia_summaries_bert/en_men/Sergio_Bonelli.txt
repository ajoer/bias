Sergio Bonelli (2 December 1932 â€“ 26 September 2011) was an Italian comic book writer and publisher. [CLS] [SEP] He is best known as creator of Zagor (1961) and Mister No (1975), as well as a comics publisher through the publishing house Sergio Bonelli Editore. [CLS] [SEP] He was born in Milan, the son of Gian Luigi Bonelli, the creator of Tex Willer and many other Italian comic strips. [CLS] [SEP] In order to distinguish himself from his father, he usually wrote his scripts under the pen name Guido Nolitta. [CLS] [SEP] He made his debut as a scriptwriter in 1957, with the translation into Italian of the Spanish series Verdugo Ranch, for which he wrote the final episode (artwork by Franco Bignotti). [CLS] [SEP] In 1958 Bonelli created his first character Un ragazzo nel Far West ('A boy in the Far West'), also illustrated by Franco Bignotti. [CLS] [SEP] In the late 1950s and early 1960s he wrote several episodes of Piccolo Ranger. [CLS] [SEP] In 1960 Bonelli wrote Il Giudice Bean, a mini-series of six adventures, illustrated by Sergio Tarquinio. [CLS] [SEP] In the same year he met the illustrator Gallieno Ferri, with whom he created Zagor in 1961. [CLS] [SEP] Bonelli wrote almost all Zagor stories until 1980 (issue #182). [CLS] [SEP] In 1975 he created the Mister No series. [CLS] [SEP] In 1977 Bonelli wrote the script of the book L'Uomo del Texas (illustrated by Aurelio Galleppini). [CLS] [SEP] He also wrote numerous Tex Willer stories, starting with issue #183 in the late 1970s. [CLS] [SEP] In 1990 he created the mini-series River Bill (artwork by Francesco Gamba). [CLS] [SEP] He was chairman of Sergio Bonelli Editore (formerly known as CEPIM), one of Italy's major comics publishers (titles include Tex, Dylan Dog, Mister No and many others). [CLS] [SEP] He died in Monza, on 26 September 2011, following a short illness. [CLS] [SEP]
