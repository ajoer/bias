Lazaro Mendez, better known by his stage name DJ Laz (born December 2, 1971 in Hollywood, Florida, United States) is a Cuban-American rapper and DJ from Miami, Florida. [CLS] [SEP] He hosted his weekday radio show in Miami on WPOW 96.5 FM, branded "Power 96", for 22 years until he left the company in April 2012. [CLS] [SEP] Beginning July 4, 2012, the new DJ Laz Morning Show began after arbitration on the newly reformatted WRMA DJ106.7 a Bilingual Dance/Rhythmic formatted station. [CLS] [SEP] He is best known for his albums DJ Laz and Category 6, which charted on U.S. [CLS] [SEP] Billboard album charts. [CLS] [SEP] He is also notable for the singles "Journey Into Bass" (1994) and "Move Shake Drop" (2008), which peaked at #56 on the Billboard Hot 100. [CLS] [SEP] The DJ Laz Morning Show on LA 96.3 of Los Angeles and DJ 106.7 of Miami is known for mysteriously getting their hands on exclusive content. [CLS] [SEP] DJ Laz is also known for his show's prominent appearance on Fox's television show, Dish Nation. [CLS] [SEP] In July 2012, when Justin Bieber made a 911 call to authorities to report paparazzi that were following him, the DJ Laz Morning Show was the first to obtain Bieber's 911 call, airing it on Los Angeles' LA 96.3.On Tuesday, September 11, 2012, the DJ Laz Morning Show aired a pre-recorded interview with President Barack Obama. [CLS] [SEP] Many Obama critics quickly bashed Obama for making no mention to the September 11 attacks in 2001, despite the fact that the interview was pre-recorded. [CLS] [SEP] The White House later made it clear that the call was indeed pre-recorded and was supposed to have been played on Monday, September 10. [CLS] [SEP] On May 5, 2014, DJ Laz was in command of a boat that was stuck on a sand bar in Biscayne Bay. [CLS] [SEP] When bystanders got out to push the boat, DJ Laz increased power to the engine, and a 23-year-old man who had been trying to help was killed by the boat prop.DJ Laz hosted the Morning Show for 97.3 in Miami from August, 2014 until November, 2019. [CLS] [SEP] He left in advance of an acquisition, and can currently be found on Sirius XM on Pitbull's Globalization Channel 13. [CLS] [SEP]
