[CLS] The 1948 Miami hurricane (Air Weather Service designation: Fox) caused no fatalities in Florida, despite moving across the Miami area as a hurricane. [SEP] [CLS] The ninth tropical storm and fifth hurricane of the 1948 season, the storm developed from a large low pressure area over the northwestern Caribbean Sea on October 3. [SEP] [CLS] The storm intensified into a tropical storm early the next day and a hurricane several hours later. [SEP] [CLS] Late on October 5, the hurricane struck Bahia Honda Key, Florida, with winds of 105 mph (165 km/h) and two hours later, hit Flamingo. [SEP] [CLS] Fox emerged into the Atlantic Ocean near Fort Lauderdale early on October 6. [SEP] [CLS] The storm moved northeastward and later curved to the east-northeast. [SEP] [CLS] Late on October 7, Fox made landfall on Bermuda with winds of 105 mph (165 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] Fox weakened over the next several days and later executed a large cyclonic loop. [SEP] [CLS] By October 16, it became extratropical while well east-southeast of Newfoundland. [SEP] [CLS] In Cuba, homes and cattle were swept away by flash flooding. [SEP] [CLS] Eleven deaths and about 300 injuries were attributed to the tropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] Damage in the country reached about $6 million. [SEP] [CLS] The storm brought strong winds to Florida, with a sustained wind speed of 122 mph (196 km/h) at Naval Air Station Key West. [SEP] [CLS] Heavy rainfall exceeding 9.5 in (240 mm) in Miami and three tornadoes also contributed to the damage in South Florida. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the state, 674 homes were severely damaged or destroyed, while 45 other buildings were demolished. [SEP] [CLS] Overall, damage in Florida reached $5.5 million and there were no deaths, but 36 injuries, none of which were serious. [SEP] [CLS] In Bermuda, buildings were unroofed and the sides of some structures were knocked down. [SEP] [CLS] Electrical light wires and telephone lines were toppled across the island. [SEP] [CLS] Damage totaled over $1 million. [SEP] [CLS]
