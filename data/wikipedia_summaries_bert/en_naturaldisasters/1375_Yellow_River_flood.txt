The 1375 Yellow River flood was a natural disaster affecting the area around Kaifeng, China during the early Ming dynasty. [CLS] [SEP] Contemporary sources mentioned a death toll of between 15,000 and 25 people, most of them farmers. [CLS] [SEP] The philosopher Wang Yangming mentioned the 1375 Yellow River flood as an example of how even a virtuous man can be destroyed by the power of nature. [CLS] [SEP]
