[CLS] [SEP] Geens received a Master in Law from the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven as well as a licentiate of economy. [CLS] [SEP] In 1961 Geens, together with Frank Swaelen and Leo Tindemans, was part of a think tank of the CVP. [CLS] [SEP] In 1972 he became a member of the party leadership. [CLS] [SEP] Geens was elected a member of the city council of Winksele in 1970. [CLS] [SEP] After Winksele's fusion with Herent in 1976 he became a member of that city council. [CLS] [SEP] In 1974 in a government led by Tindemans, Geens became Secretary of State for the Budget and Science. [CLS] [SEP] In 1976 he was promoted to minister but with the same competences. [CLS] [SEP] He became Minister of Finance in the second Tindemans cabinet, but this coalition fell over the Egmont pact. [CLS] [SEP] He remained Minister of Finance under Paul Vanden Boeynants and in the first two coalitions led by Wilfried Martens. [CLS] [SEP] During the third government led by Martens he again became minister of the budget. [CLS] [SEP] During Martens IV, Geens became minister-president of the Flemish Executive which he led until 1992. [CLS] [SEP] Geens gave Flanders a new economical identity, by taking the initiative for the Derde Industriële Revolutie in Vlaanderen (Third industrial revolution in Flanders), Flanders Technology and the Flemish Aerospace Group (FLAG). [CLS] [SEP] Gaston Geens was seventy when he died at home due to cardiac arrest. [CLS] [SEP] On 30 May 2009, a statue of Geens was unveiled in Aarschot by Herman Van Rompuy. [CLS] [SEP]
