The Blizzard of January 1881 (17â€“20 January 1881) was one of the most severe blizzards ever to hit the southern parts of the United Kingdom. [CLS] [SEP] On 17 January 1881, a low pressure system rapidly developed in the English Channel. [CLS] [SEP] The snowfalls began on the 17th in the southwest and as the system deepened as it moved through the Channel, a gale force easterly developed over southern parts with heavy blizzards and drifting snow. [CLS] [SEP] The blizzard paralysed all transport, communication, trade and industries. [CLS] [SEP] Hundreds of miles of rail track were blocked by drifting snow, dozens of feet high in places. [CLS] [SEP] Even in central London, there were 3 foot drifts in places. [CLS] [SEP] Around 100 people are believed to have died as a result of the blizzard. [CLS] [SEP] The storm was accompanied by extreme cold. [CLS] [SEP] Comparisons are drawn to the Great Blizzard of 1899 which affected the United States. [CLS] [SEP]
