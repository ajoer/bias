The 1931 Hawke's Bay earthquake, also known as the Napier earthquake, occurred in New Zealand at 10:47 am on 3 February, killing 256, injuring thousands and devastating the Hawke's Bay region. [CLS] [SEP] It remains New Zealand's deadliest natural disaster. [CLS] [SEP] Centred 15 km north of Napier, it lasted for two and a half minutes and had a magnitude of 7.8 Ms (7.7 Mw). [CLS] [SEP] There were 525 aftershocks recorded in the following two weeks, with 597 being recorded by the end of February. [CLS] [SEP] The main shock could be felt in much of New Zealand, with reliable reports coming in from as far south as Timaru, on the east coast of the South Island. [CLS] [SEP]
