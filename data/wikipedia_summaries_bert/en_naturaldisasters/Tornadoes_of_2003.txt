[CLS] This page documents notable tornadoes and tornado outbreaks worldwide in 2003. [SEP] [CLS] Strong and destructive tornadoes form most frequently in the United States, Bangladesh, and Eastern India, but they can occur almost anywhere under the right conditions. [SEP] [CLS] Tornadoes also develop occasionally in southern Canada during the Northern Hemisphere's summer and somewhat regularly at other times of the year across Europe, Asia, and Australia. [SEP] [CLS] Tornadic events are often accompanied with other forms of severe weather, including strong thunderstorms, strong winds, and hail. [SEP] [CLS] There were 1,395 tornadoes reported in the United States in 2003, of which 1,374 were confirmed. [SEP] [CLS] 2003 is currently the seventh-most active year for tornadoes in the United States since reliable record-keeping began in 1950. [SEP] [CLS]
