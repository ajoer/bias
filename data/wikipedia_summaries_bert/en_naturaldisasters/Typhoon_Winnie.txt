Typhoon Winnie, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Ibiang, was the worst tropical cyclone to impact the Chinese provinces of Zhejiang, Fujian, Jiangsu, and Shandong in 200 years. [CLS] [SEP] Originating from an area of low pressure over the Pacific Ocean on August 5, 1997, the system organized into a tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] It headed northwestward, slowly strengthening into a tropical storm on August 9. [CLS] [SEP] Intensification became more rapid as conditions became more favorable, and Winnie reached typhoon strength on August 10. [CLS] [SEP] On August 12, 1997, Winnie attained Super Typhoon status, with peak 1-minute sustained winds of 160 mph. [CLS] [SEP] Winnie then weakened and passed north of Taiwan, before making landfall in Eastern China at Category 1-equivalent typhoon strength on the August 18. [CLS] [SEP] Winnie continued northeast over land while weakening, bringing heavy rainfall before dissipating on August 23. [CLS] [SEP] Winnie is also tied with Typhoon Carmen in 1960 for having the largest eye on record, at 230 mi (370 km) in diameter. [CLS] [SEP]
