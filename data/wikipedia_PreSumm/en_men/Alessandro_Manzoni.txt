[CLS] Alessandro Francesco Tommaso Antonio Manzoni (UK: , US: , Italian: [alesˈsandro manˈdzoːni]; 7 March 1785 – 22 May 1873) was an Italian poet, novelist and philosopher. [SEP] [CLS] He is famous for the novel The Betrothed (orig. [SEP] [CLS] Italian: I promessi sposi) (1827), generally ranked among the masterpieces of world literature. [SEP] [CLS] The novel is also a symbol of the Italian Risorgimento, both for its patriotic message and because it was a fundamental milestone in the development of the modern, unified Italian language. [SEP] [CLS] Manzoni also contributed to the stabilization of the modern Italian language and helped to insure linguistic unity throughout Italy. [SEP] [CLS] He was an influential proponent of Liberal Catholicism in Italy. [SEP] [CLS] His work and thinking has often been contrasted with that of his younger contemporary Giacomo Leopardi by critics. [SEP] [CLS]
