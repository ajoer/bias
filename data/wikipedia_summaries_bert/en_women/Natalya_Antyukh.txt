[CLS] Natalya Nikolayevna Antyukh (Russian: Наталья Николаевна Антюх, born 26 June 1981 in Leningrad) is a Russian athlete who primarily competes in the 400 metres and 400 metre hurdles. [SEP] [CLS] She won the gold medal in 400m hurdles at the 2012 Summer Olympics, after winning a bronze medal in the 400 m dash at the 2004 Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] She is the only woman to win an Olympic medal in both the 400 m hurdles and 400 m dash. [SEP] [CLS] In addition to winning medals in individual contests, she has been a very successful relay runner, winning a silver medal at the 2004 Olympics. [SEP] [CLS] In 2020, Antyukh among four Russian track and field athletes charged with doping offences, facing charges of using a prohibited substance or method. [SEP] [CLS] The Athletics Integrity Unit said the cases were based on an investigation into Russian doping for the World Anti-Doping Agency presented in 2016 by Canadian lawyer Richard McLaren.
