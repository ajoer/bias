[CLS] The 1917 Nueva Gerona hurricane was the most intense tropical cyclone to strike the Florida Panhandle until Hurricane Opal in 1995. [SEP] [CLS] The eighth tropical cyclone and fourth tropical storm of the season, this system was identified as a tropical storm east of the Lesser Antilles on September 20. [SEP] [CLS] After crossing the Lesser Antilles, the system entered the Caribbean Sea and achieved hurricane intensity on September 21. [SEP] [CLS] After becoming a Category 2 hurricane, the storm struck the northern coast of Jamaica on September 23. [SEP] [CLS] Early on September 25, the cyclone reached Category 4 status and attained maximum sustained winds of 150 mph (240 km/h) soon thereafter. [SEP] [CLS] Later that day, the hurricane made landfall in eastern Pinar del Río Province, Cuba. [SEP] [CLS] The system entered the Gulf of Mexico shortly thereafter and weakened slightly. [SEP] [CLS] Recurving to the northeast, the hurricane briefly threatened Louisiana before turning toward Florida. [SEP] [CLS] Early on September 29, the hurricane made landfall near Fort Walton Beach, Florida, with winds of 115 mph (185 km/h). [SEP] [CLS] Once over land, the cyclone rapidly weakened and transitioned into an extratropical cyclone before dissipating on September 30. [SEP] [CLS] Some islands in the Lesser Antilles experienced strong winds and heavy rainfall, including Dominica, Guadeloupe, and Saint Lucia. [SEP] [CLS] In Jamaica, the hurricane caused significant damage to banana and coconut plantations. [SEP] [CLS] Communications from Holland Bay were disrupted when the station was demolished. [SEP] [CLS] The greatest damages were reported from the northern half of the island. [SEP] [CLS] Nine deaths occurred in the city of Port Antonio. [SEP] [CLS] In Nueva Gerona, Cuba, strong winds destroyed well-constructed buildings and all but 10 homes. [SEP] [CLS] The Isla de la Juventud overall experienced about $2 million (1917 USD) in damage and there were at least 20 fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] Orchards and crops were destroyed on the Pinar del Río Province. [SEP] [CLS] In Louisiana and Mississippi, impact was generally limited to damaged crops and timber stands. [SEP] [CLS] Ten deaths from drowning were reported in Louisiana. [SEP] [CLS] Farther east in Mobile, Alabama, portions of roofs, trees, and other debris littered streets. [SEP] [CLS] Communications were severed in Pensacola, Florida. [SEP] [CLS] Several small watercraft washed ashore, and numerous wharves, docks, and boat storages suffered impact. [SEP] [CLS] Total damages were estimated near $170,000 in Pensacola area. [SEP] [CLS] Five deaths were reported in Florida, all of them in Crestview. [SEP] [CLS] The storm and its remnants also produced rainfall in Georgia, North Carolina, and South Carolina. [SEP] [CLS]
