Typhoon Gene, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Oyang, struck Japan during late September 1990. [CLS] [SEP] An area of disturbed weather formed several hundred kilometers south-southeast of Okinawa on September 18. [CLS] [SEP] Gradual development occurred as it tracked generally westward, and on September 22, the disturbance developed into a tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] The depression intensified into a tropical storm the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Continuing to steadily intensify, Gene turned northwest and became a severe tropical storm on September 25. [CLS] [SEP] In the evening, Gene was declared a typhoon, and on September 26, attained its maximum intensity. [CLS] [SEP] Gene leveled off in intensity while recurving towards Japan. [CLS] [SEP] After brushing Kyushu and Shikoku on September 29 and Honshu on September 20, Gene weakened back to a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] On September 30, Gene transitioned into an extratropical cyclone, which was last noted on October 1. [CLS] [SEP] The typhoon hit the Japanese archipelago ten days after Typhoon Flo, which killed 40 people. [CLS] [SEP] The southwestern portion of the country suffered the worst damage from the typhoon. [CLS] [SEP] Nationwide, six fatalities were reported and twenty others sustained injuries. [CLS] [SEP] In Miyazaki Prefecture, three people were killed and ten others were wounded. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout the prefecture, 4,688 houses were damaged and ninety-two others were destroyed, which led to 18,449 homeless. [CLS] [SEP] A total 210 houses were destroyed while 13,318 others were flooded. [CLS] [SEP] Elsewhere, 229 houses were damaged in Kagoshima Prefecture and seventy more were destroyed; consequently, 194 people lost their homes. [CLS] [SEP] There, almost 30,000 homes lost power. [CLS] [SEP] Overall, there were 340 landslides and roads were damaged at 182 locations. [CLS] [SEP] All Nippon Airways cancelled ninety domestic flights and Japan Airlines cancelled ten flights. [CLS] [SEP] Over 200,000 people were affected by cancellation of flights and train services. [CLS] [SEP] Thirty-one ships and 10,560 acres (4,273 ha) of farmland were damaged.
