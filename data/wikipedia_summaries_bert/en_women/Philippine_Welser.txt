[CLS] Philippine Welser (1527 – 24 April 1580) was the morganatic wife of Ferdinand II, Archduke of Austria. [SEP] [CLS] She was granted the titles Baroness of Zinnenburg, Margravine of Burgau, Landgravine of Mellenburg and Countess of Oberhohenberg and Niederhohenberg. [SEP] [CLS] She was born in Augsburg. [SEP] [CLS] Her family, the Welsers of Augsburg, were merchants and financiers of European significance and great wealth. [SEP] [CLS] Her father was Franz (Friedrich) Welser (1497–1572) and her mother was Anna Adler (1507–1572). [SEP] [CLS] Philippine Welser's uncle Bartholomeus V. [SEP] [CLS] Welser received Venezuela as security for a loan from the King of Spain in 1528, and the family thus became rulers of the colony. [SEP] [CLS] Already at a young age, she was renowned for both her beauty and her learning. [SEP] [CLS] She died at Schloss Ambras, Innsbruck in Tyrol. [SEP] [CLS] She was the mother of Margrave Andrew of Burgau, who became a Cardinal, and Charles, Margrave of Burgau, who became a noted General. [SEP] [CLS] After her death, her husband remarried to Anne Juliana Gonzaga and became the father of Anna of Tyrol, who would become Holy Roman Empress. [SEP] [CLS]
