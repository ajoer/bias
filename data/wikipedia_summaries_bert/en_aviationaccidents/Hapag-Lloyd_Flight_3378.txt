Hapag-Lloyd Flight 3378 (HF-3378) was a passenger service operated by Hapag-Lloyd Flug from Chania, on the island of Crete, Greece, to Hannover, Germany. [CLS] [SEP] On 12 July 2000, the aircraft flying the route, an Airbus A310-304, registration D-AHLB, with 143 passengers and 8 crew on board, set off for Hannover leaving the landing gear fully extended, as a precaution, since a malfunction had prevented its proper retraction after take-off. [CLS] [SEP] The plane eventually ran out of fuel while attempting a diversion to Vienna, crash-landing just short of runway 34. [CLS] [SEP] No fatalities resulted, although the aircraft was written off. [CLS] [SEP]
