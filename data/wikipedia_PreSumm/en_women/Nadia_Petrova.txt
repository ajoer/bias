[CLS] Nadezhda Viktorovna Petrova (Russian: Надежда Викторовна Петрова; born 8 June 1982) is a retired Russian tennis player. [SEP] [CLS] In her career, she won 37 titles on the WTA Tour, 13 in singles and 24 in doubles. [SEP] [CLS] In singles, she reached a career-high ranking of world No. [SEP] [CLS] 3 in May 2006, reached two Grand Slam semifinals at the French Open in 2003 and 2005, and reached seven additional Grand Slam quarterfinals across all four major tournaments. [SEP] [CLS] She won the Tier-I tournaments in Charleston and Berlin in 2006 as well as Tokyo 2012. [SEP] [CLS] In doubles, Petrova won the year-ending WTA Championships in 2004 with Meghann Shaughnessy, and in 2012 with Maria Kirilenko. [SEP] [CLS] With Kirilenko, she also won the bronze medal in the doubles competition at the 2012 Summer Olympics. [SEP] [CLS]
