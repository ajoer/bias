Hurricane Henri was a rare tropical cyclone that entered the Gulf of Mexico without having made landfall; it was the second of four times this occurred during the 20th century. [CLS] [SEP] The eighth named storm and fifth hurricane of the 1979 Atlantic hurricane season, it formed on September 14 in the northwestern Caribbean Sea. [CLS] [SEP] Throughout much of its duration, Henri moved erratically and initially maintained a general westward track. [CLS] [SEP] On September 16 it attained tropical storm status, and a day later it reached hurricane status. [CLS] [SEP] By two days later, after experiencing hostile conditions, Henri weakened to tropical depression status as it turned to the northeast, and on September 24 it merged with a frontal low in the northeast Gulf of Mexico. [CLS] [SEP] Due to its slow and erratic motion, the hurricane forced evacuations along the Mexican coastline. [CLS] [SEP] Its remnants brought rainfall and flooding to the Florida Panhandle.
