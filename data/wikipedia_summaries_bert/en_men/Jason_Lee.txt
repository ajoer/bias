Jason Michael Lee (born April 25, 1970) is an American actor and former professional skateboarder. [CLS] [SEP] He played Earl Hickey in the television comedy series My Name Is Earl, for which he was nominated for Best Actor in a Musical or Comedy TV series in 2005 and 2006 by The Golden Globes. [CLS] [SEP] He is also known for his roles in Kevin Smith films such as Mallrats, Chasing Amy, Dogma, Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back, Jersey Girl, Clerks II, and Cop Out. [CLS] [SEP] He starred as David Seville in the live action/CGI Alvin and the Chipmunks film series and achieved leading roles in Kissing a Fool, Almost Famous, Heartbreakers, Stealing Harvard, A Guy Thing. [CLS] [SEP] His voice acting credits include Syndrome in The Incredibles, Bones in Monster House and the title character in Underdog. [CLS] [SEP] A former professional skateboarder, Lee is the co-founder and co-owner of Stereo Skateboards, founding the company in 1992 with fellow skateboarder Chris "Dune" Pastras. [CLS] [SEP] Since 1992, Stereo manufactures and distributes skateboard decks, equipment and apparel; as well as, producing skate videos. [CLS] [SEP]
