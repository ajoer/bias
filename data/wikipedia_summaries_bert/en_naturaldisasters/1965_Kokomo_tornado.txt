During the evening of April 11, 1965, an intense, destructive and long-lived tornado, rated F4 struck Kokomo, Indiana, and adjacent areas. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado was responsible for 17 deaths and 560 injuries. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado was part of a larger weather system that had produced several other tornadoes across the Great Plains over the course of April 11 and 12. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado touched down just northwest of Russiaville at 7:34 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] CDT (19:46 UTC), and stayed on the ground for 43 minutes over a 44-mile (71 km) path, crossing through a heavily populated section of Kokomo. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado was 800 yards wide at its peak. [CLS] [SEP]
