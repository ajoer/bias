[CLS] Marta Hillers (Krefeld May 26, 1911 â€“Basel June 16, 2001) was a German journalist, and the author of the memoir, Eine Frau in Berlin (A Woman in Berlin), published anonymously in 1959 and 2003 in German. [SEP] [CLS] It is the diary of a German woman from 20 April to 22 June 1945, during and after the Battle of Berlin. [SEP] [CLS] The book details the author's rape, in the context of mass rape by the occupying forces, and how she and many other German women chose to take a Soviet officer as a protector. [SEP] [CLS] The book was first published in English in 1954 in the United States. [SEP] [CLS] When it was published in Germany in 1959, the author was accused of "besmirching the honor of German women." Hillers refused to have another edition published in her lifetime. [SEP] [CLS] Having married and moved to Switzerland, Hillers left journalism and did not publish another major work. [SEP] [CLS] She died in 2001. [SEP] [CLS] A new edition of her book was published posthumously in Germany in 2003, again anonymously. [SEP] [CLS] It met wide critical acclaim and was on the bestseller list for weeks. [SEP] [CLS] A controversy broke out when a literary editor revealed the author as Hillers. [SEP] [CLS] No one else has been suggested. [SEP] [CLS] New English editions were published in the United Kingdom and the United States in 2005, as well as in seven other languages. [SEP] [CLS] The book was adapted as a film and released first in 2008 in Germany and Poland. [SEP] [CLS] In the United States it is known as A Woman in Berlin. [SEP] [CLS]
