A devastating earthquake known in scientific literature as the Earthquake of 749 struck on January 18, 749, in areas of the Umayyad Caliphate, with the epicenter in Galilee. [CLS] [SEP] The most severely affected areas were parts of Palestine and western Transjordan. [CLS] [SEP] The cities of Tiberias, Beit She'an Pella, Gadara, and Hippos were largely destroyed while many other cities across the Levant were heavily damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The casualties numbered in the tens of thousands. [CLS] [SEP] In Jewish sources this earthquake is known, in Hebrew, as רעש שביעית, Ra'ash Shevi'it, lit. [CLS] [SEP] "seventh noise", interpreted by scholars to mean The Earthquake of the Sabbatical Year, because the earthquake fell on a sabbatical year, literally "the seventh year" in the Jewish calendar.
