Malaysian Airline System Flight 653 (MH653) was a scheduled domestic flight from Penang to Kuala Lumpur in Malaysia, operated by Malaysian Airline System (MAS). [CLS] [SEP] On the evening of 4 December 1977, the Boeing 737-200 aircraft flying the service crashed at Tanjung Kupang, Johor, Malaysia, while purportedly being diverted by hijackers to Singapore. [CLS] [SEP] It was the first fatal air crash for Malaysia Airlines (as the airline is now known), with all 93 passengers and 7 crew killed. [CLS] [SEP] The flight was apparently hijacked as soon as it reached cruise altitude. [CLS] [SEP] The circumstances in which the hijacking and subsequent crash occurred remain unsolved. [CLS] [SEP] The crash is the deadliest aviation disaster to occur in Malaysia. [CLS] [SEP]
