[CLS] Sébastien Loeb (French pronunciation: ​[sebastjɛ̃ lœb]; born 26 February 1974) is a French professional rally, racing, and rallycross driver. [SEP] [CLS] He competed for the Citroën World Rally Team in the World Rally Championship (WRC) and is the most successful driver in WRC history, having won the world championship a record nine times in a row. [SEP] [CLS] He holds several other WRC records, including most event wins, most podium finishes and most stage wins. [SEP] [CLS] Loeb announced his retirement from World Rallying at the end of the 2012 season. [SEP] [CLS] Participating in selected events in the 2013 WRC season, he raced a full season in the FIA GT Series driving a McLaren MP4-12C before moving on with Citroën to the FIA World Touring Car Championship in 2014. [SEP] [CLS] In the 2018 season he is one of the official drivers of the Team Peugeot Total.Originally a gymnast, Loeb switched to rallying in 1995 and won the Junior World Rally Championship in 2001. [SEP] [CLS] Signed by the Citroën factory team for the 2002 season, he and co-driver Daniel Elena took their maiden WRC win that same year at the Rallye Deutschland. [SEP] [CLS] After finishing runner-up to Petter Solberg by one point in 2003, Loeb took his first drivers' title in 2004. [SEP] [CLS] Continuing with Citroën, he went on to take a record ninth consecutive world title in 2012. [SEP] [CLS] Loeb is a tarmac expert, having won all but three of the WRC rallies on that surface in which he has participated between 2005 and 2013. [SEP] [CLS] Besides his success in rallying, Loeb is a three-time winner at the Race of Champions, after taking home the Henri Toivonen Memorial Trophy and the title "Champion of Champions" in 2003, 2005 and 2008. [SEP] [CLS] In 2004, he won the Nations' Cup for France with Jean Alesi. [SEP] [CLS] In 2006, he finished second in the 24 Hours of Le Mans. [SEP] [CLS] Loeb was named the French Sportsman of the Year in 2007 and 2009, and made knight of the Legion of Honour (Légion d'honneur) in 2009. [SEP] [CLS] In 2012, he won the rallycross final in his first appearance at X Games XVIII. [SEP] [CLS] In 2018, Loeb won the Spanish round of that year's World Rally Championship, in a rare entry six years after his retirement as a full-time rally driver. [SEP] [CLS]
