Helen Iglauer Glueck (1907–1995) was an American physician known for her research in blood chemistry that linked bleeding disorders in newborns with lack of Vitamin K in breast milk. [CLS] [SEP] Dr. [CLS] [SEP] Glueck graduated from Walnut Hills High School in Cincinnati, Ohio in 1925. [CLS] [SEP] She obtained her BA from the University of Wisconsin–Madison and her MD from the University of Cincinnati College of Medicine. [CLS] [SEP] She directed the University of Cincinnati Student Health Services (1945-1959) and then became Director of the Coagulation Laboratory at the University. [CLS] [SEP]
