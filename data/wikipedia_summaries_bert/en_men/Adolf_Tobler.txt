Adolf Tobler (24 May 1835 – 18 March 1910) was a Swiss-German linguist and philologist. [CLS] [SEP] Born in Hirzel in Zürich, Switzerland, he was the brother of linguist Ludwig Tobler (1827–1895). [CLS] [SEP] Adolf Tobler died in Berlin, Germany. [CLS] [SEP] He studied Romance philology at the universities of Zürich and Bonn, receiving his doctorate in 1857. [CLS] [SEP] At Bonn, he was influenced by the teachings of Friedrich Christian Diez and Nicolaus Delius. [CLS] [SEP] After graduation, he worked as a schoolteacher at the Solothurn cantonal school, then at the gymnasium in Bern. [CLS] [SEP] In 1867, he relocated to the University of Berlin, where from 1871 up until his death, he held the chair of Romance philology. [CLS] [SEP] In 1890/91, he served as university rector.The "Tobler-Mussafia law", a grammatical rule applicable to Romance languages is named after Tobler and Austrian philologist Adolf Mussafia.
