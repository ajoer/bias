[CLS] Vikersundbakken or Vikersund Hill is a ski flying hill at Vikersund in Modum, Norway. [SEP] [CLS] It is one of two largest purpose-built ski flying hills in the world. [SEP] [CLS] Nine world records have been set on the hill, with the current of 253.5 meters being set by Stefan Kraft. [SEP] [CLS] The complex also consists of a large hill, a normal hill and several training hills. [SEP] [CLS] The hill originally constructed by Kristian Hovde was opened in 1936 as a large hill. [SEP] [CLS] It was rebuilt to become a ski flying hill in 1964 and has later been converted in 1989, 1999 and 2010. [SEP] [CLS] The large hill was built in 1988. [SEP] [CLS] The hill was the first to receive floodlights in 2006. [SEP] [CLS] It has hosted the FIS Ski Flying World Championships four times, in 1977, 1990, 2000, 2012. [SEP] [CLS]
