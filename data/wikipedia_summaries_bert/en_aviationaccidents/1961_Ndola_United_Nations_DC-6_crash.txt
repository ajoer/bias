The Ndola United Nations DC-6 crash occurred on 18 September 1961 in Northern Rhodesia. [CLS] [SEP] The crash resulted in the deaths of all people onboard including Dag Hammarskjöld, the second Secretary-General of the United Nations, and 15 others. [CLS] [SEP] Hammarskjöld had been en route to cease-fire negotiations with Moise Tshombe during the Congo Crisis. [CLS] [SEP] Three official inquiries failed to determine conclusively the cause of the crash, which set off a succession crisis at the United Nations. [CLS] [SEP]
