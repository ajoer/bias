The 1957 Andreanof Islands earthquake took place on March 9 with a moment magnitude of 8.6 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe). [CLS] [SEP] It occurred south of the Andreanof Islands group, which is part of the Aleutian Islands arc. [CLS] [SEP] The event occurred along the Aleutian Trench, the convergent plate boundary that separates the Pacific Plate and the North American Plates near Alaska. [CLS] [SEP] A basin wide tsunami followed, with effects felt in Alaska and Hawaii. [CLS] [SEP] Total losses were around $5 million. [CLS] [SEP]
