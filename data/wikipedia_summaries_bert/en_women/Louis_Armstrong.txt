Louis Daniel Armstrong (August 4, 1901 â€“ July 6, 1971), nicknamed "Satchmo", "Satch", and "Pops", was an American trumpeter, composer, vocalist, and actor who was among the most influential figures in jazz. [CLS] [SEP] His career spanned five decades, from the 1920s to the 1960s, and different eras in the history of jazz. [CLS] [SEP] In 2017, he was inducted into the Rhythm & Blues Hall of Fame. [CLS] [SEP] Armstrong was born and raised in New Orleans. [CLS] [SEP] Coming to prominence in the 1920s as an inventive trumpet and cornet player, Armstrong was a foundational influence in jazz, shifting the focus of the music from collective improvisation to solo performance. [CLS] [SEP] Around 1922, he followed his mentor, Joe "King" Oliver, to Chicago to play in the Creole Jazz Band. [CLS] [SEP] In Chicago, he spent time with other popular jazz musicians, reconnecting with his friend Bix Beiderbecke and spending time with Hoagy Carmichael and Lil Hardin. [CLS] [SEP] He earned a reputation at "cutting contests", and relocated to New York in order to join Fletcher Henderson's band. [CLS] [SEP] With his instantly recognizable rich, gravelly voice, Armstrong was also an influential singer and skillful improviser, bending the lyrics and melody of a song. [CLS] [SEP] He was also skilled at scat singing. [CLS] [SEP] Armstrong is renowned for his charismatic stage presence and voice as well as his trumpet playing. [CLS] [SEP] By the end of Armstrong's career in the 1960s, his influence had spread to popular music in general. [CLS] [SEP] Armstrong was one of the first popular African-American entertainers to "cross over" to wide popularity with white (and international) audiences. [CLS] [SEP] He rarely publicly politicized his race, to the dismay of fellow African Americans, but took a well-publicized stand for desegregation in the Little Rock crisis. [CLS] [SEP] He was able to access the upper echelons of American society at a time when this was difficult for black men. [CLS] [SEP] Armstrong appeared in films such as High Society (1956) alongside Bing Crosby, Grace Kelly, and Frank Sinatra, and Hello, Dolly! [CLS] [SEP] (1969) starring Barbra Streisand. [CLS] [SEP] He received many accolades including three Grammy Award nominations and a win for his vocal performance of Hello, Dolly! [CLS] [SEP] in 1964. [CLS] [SEP]
