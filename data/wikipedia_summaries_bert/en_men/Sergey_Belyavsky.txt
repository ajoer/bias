Sergey Ivanovich Belyavsky (Russian: Серге́й Ива́нович Беля́вский; December 7, 1883 (Julian calendar: November 25) – October 13, 1953) was a Soviet/Russian astronomer and a discoverer of 36 numbered minor planets.His last name is also alternatively spelled Beljavskij (name under which the Minor Planet Center credits him) or Beljawskij. [CLS] [SEP] His first name is occasionally given as "Sergius". [CLS] [SEP] He was born in St. [CLS] [SEP] Petersburg and was a member of the Academy of Sciences of the Soviet Union. [CLS] [SEP] His field of work included astrophotometry, astrometry, and the study of variable stars. [CLS] [SEP] He died in Leningrad. [CLS] [SEP] He discovered the bright naked-eye comet C/1911 S3 (Beljawsky), also known according to the nomenclature of the time as "Comet 1911 IV" or "Comet 1911g". [CLS] [SEP] Belyavsky observed at Simeiz Observatory (Симеиз) in Crimea. [CLS] [SEP] Between 1937 and 1944, Belyavsky was the seventh director of the Pulkovo Observatory, where he succeeded Boris Gerasimovich. [CLS] [SEP]
