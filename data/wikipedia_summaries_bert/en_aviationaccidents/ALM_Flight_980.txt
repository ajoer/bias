ALM Antillean Airlines Flight 980 was a flight scheduled to fly from John F. [CLS] [SEP] Kennedy International Airport in New York City to Princess Juliana International Airport in St. [CLS] [SEP] Maarten, Netherlands Antilles, on 2 May 1970. [CLS] [SEP] After several unsuccessful landing attempts, the aircraft's fuel was exhausted, and it made a forced water landing (ditching) in the Caribbean Sea 48 km (30 mi; 26 nmi) off St. [CLS] [SEP] Croix, with 23 fatalities and 40 survivors. [CLS] [SEP] The accident is one of a small number of intentional water ditchings of jet airliners. [CLS] [SEP]
