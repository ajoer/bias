The 1903 Florida hurricane was an Atlantic hurricane that caused extensive wind and flood damage on the Florida peninsula and over the adjourning Southeastern United States in early to mid September 1903. [CLS] [SEP] The third tropical cyclone and third hurricane of the season, this storm was first observed near Mayaguana island in the Bahamas early on September 9. [CLS] [SEP] Moving northwestward, it became a hurricane the next day and passed near Nassau. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone then turned to the west-northwest on September 11 and passed just north of the Bimini Islands. [CLS] [SEP] As it crossed the Bahamas, the cyclone produced hurricane-force winds that caused damage to crops and buildings, but no deaths were reported over the island chain. [CLS] [SEP] Late on September 11, the cyclone struck near Fort Lauderdale, Florida. [CLS] [SEP] The Inchulva capsized near Delray Beach, drowning nine of her crew members. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone caused severe wind damage in present-day Broward and Palm Beach counties, although most of the losses were to crops such as sugarcane. [CLS] [SEP] The cyclone weakened to a tropical storm while crossing Florida, but re-intensified into a hurricane over the Gulf of Mexico on September 17. [CLS] [SEP] Additionally, a storm surge caused boats to be blown ashore in the Florida Panhandle. [CLS] [SEP] In all, the storm killed 14 people in Florida and produced $500,000 in damage. [CLS] [SEP] After falling to tropical storm intensity early on September 14, the storm weakened to a tropical depression on September 16, several hours before dissipating over South Carolina.
