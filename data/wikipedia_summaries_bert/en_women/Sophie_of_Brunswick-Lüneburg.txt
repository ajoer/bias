William VI (4 July 1535 – 20 August 1592), called William the Younger (German: Wilhelm der Jüngere), was Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg and Prince of Lüneburg from 1559 until his death. [CLS] [SEP] Until 1569 he ruled together with his brother, Henry of Dannenberg. [CLS] [SEP] William was the son of Ernest I, Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg. [CLS] [SEP] On 12 October 1561 he married Dorothea of Denmark (29 June 1546 Kolding–6 January 1617 Winsen), daughter of Christian III of Denmark and Dorothea of Saxe-Lauenburg. [CLS] [SEP] In 1582, William began suffering from fits of insanity. [CLS] [SEP] These fits caused his wife to flee him in 1584 for her own safety. [CLS] [SEP] After William's death, his wife became regent for their son George. [CLS] [SEP]
