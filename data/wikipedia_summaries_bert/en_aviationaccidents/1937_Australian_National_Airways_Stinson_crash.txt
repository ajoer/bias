The 1937 Airlines of Australia Stinson crash was an accident which occurred on 19 February 1937. [CLS] [SEP] The Airlines of Australia Stinson Model A airliner disappeared during a flight from Brisbane to Sydney, carrying five passengers and two pilots. [CLS] [SEP] Both pilots and two passengers were killed in the crash. [CLS] [SEP] One of the surviving passengers died while attempting to bring help to the other survivors. [CLS] [SEP] The wreckage was found by Bernard O'Reilly of the Lamington Guest House who went looking for the aircraft believing it had failed to cross the border. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft had crashed in the McPherson Range on the border between Queensland and New South Wales. [CLS] [SEP]
