[CLS] The 1986 Jamaica floods killed 50 people in the country, comparable to flooding that occurred in June 1979. [SEP] [CLS] The floods originated as a stationary front on May 24 that produced rainfall across much of the central Caribbean Sea for two weeks. [SEP] [CLS] Rainfall totals in Jamaica reached 635 mm (25.0 in) at Norman Manley International Airport. [SEP] [CLS] The flooding left heavy agriculture damage totaling $22.5 million, and 40,000 residents per day received meals after the event. [SEP] [CLS] Roads and bridges were damaged across the country, and one damaged bridge resulted in eight deaths after a bus crashed. [SEP] [CLS] The floods left 2,000 Jamaicans homeless. [SEP] [CLS] Elsewhere, the floods were the worst in Haiti in decades. [SEP] [CLS] There, a swollen river destroyed several homes in Les Cayes, and there were 21 deaths nationwide. [SEP] [CLS] In neighboring Dominican Republic, flooding isolated several towns and caused mudslides that killed 12 people. [SEP] [CLS] In Cuba, flooding was worst in the easternmost four provinces, and there were five deaths. [SEP] [CLS] The overall system spawned a subtropical cyclone on June 5 that later became Tropical Storm Andrew. [SEP] [CLS]
