Hurricane Olivia was the first tropical cyclone to make landfall on Maui and Lanai in recorded history. [CLS] [SEP] The fifteenth named storm, ninth hurricane, and sixth major hurricane of the 2018 Pacific hurricane season, Olivia formed southwest of Mexico on September 1. [CLS] [SEP] The depression slowly organized and strengthened into Tropical Storm Olivia on the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Olivia then began a period of rapid intensification on September 3, reaching its initial peak on September 5. [CLS] [SEP] Soon after, Olivia began a weakening trend, before re-intensifying on September 6. [CLS] [SEP] On the next day, Olivia peaked as a Category 4 hurricane, with winds of 130 mph (215 km/h) and a minimum central pressure of 951 mbar (28.08 inHg). [CLS] [SEP] Six hours later, Olivia began another weakening trend that resulted in the hurricane being downgraded to Category 1 status on September 8, east of the 140th meridian west. [CLS] [SEP] On September 9, Olivia entered the Central Pacific Basin. [CLS] [SEP] Over the next couple of days, Olivia prompted the issuance of Tropical Storm Watches and Warnings for Hawaii County, Oahu, Maui County, and Kauai County. [CLS] [SEP] Olivia weakened into a tropical storm on September 11, before making brief landfalls in northwest Maui and Lanai on the next day, becoming the first tropical cyclone to impact the islands in recorded history. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical storm-force winds mainly affected Maui County and Oahu. [CLS] [SEP] Torrential rains affected the same area from September 11 to 13, causing flash flooding. [CLS] [SEP] Olivia caused a total of US$25 million in damages. [CLS] [SEP] Olivia was downgraded to a tropical depression on September 13 while continuing to head west. [CLS] [SEP] Due to wind shear disrupting Olivia's convection, the system weakened into a remnant low on September 14. [CLS] [SEP] Olivia crossed into the West Pacific Basin on September 19 as a remnant low, before dissipating later that day. [CLS] [SEP]
