[CLS] Hurricane Tomas was the latest recorded tropical cyclone on a calendar year to strike the Windward Islands. [SEP] [CLS] The nineteenth named storm and twelfth hurricane of the 2010 Atlantic hurricane season, Tomas developed from a tropical wave east of the Windward Islands on October 29. [SEP] [CLS] Quickly intensifying into a hurricane, it moved through the Windward Islands and passed over Saint Lucia. [SEP] [CLS] After reaching Category 2 status on the Saffir-Simpson scale, Tomas quickly weakened to a tropical storm in the central Caribbean Sea, due to strong wind shear and dry air. [SEP] [CLS] Tomas later regained hurricane status as it reorganized near the Windward passage. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the hurricane's path, 71 people are known to have been killed, 14 of whom were in Saint Lucia. [SEP] [CLS] In the wake of the storm in Haiti, flooding intensified a cholera outbreak indirectly causing more fatalities. [SEP] [CLS] However, direct impacts from the hurricane in Haiti were less than anticipated. [SEP] [CLS] Overall damage from the storm was $463.4 million. [SEP] [CLS]
