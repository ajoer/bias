Severe Tropical Cyclone Drena was a powerful tropical cyclone that caused significant damage throughout New Zealand. [CLS] [SEP] A tropical depression formed on January 2, 1997. [CLS] [SEP] After crossing Vanuatu and emerging into the Coral Sea, the storm began to intensify, and by January 6, had attained peak intensity. [CLS] [SEP] After attaining peak intensity, Drena began to weaken. [CLS] [SEP] Cyclone Drena later brushed New Caledonia, bringing some damage. [CLS] [SEP] After transitioning into an extratropical cyclone on January 9, Drena brought impact to New Zealand. [CLS] [SEP] Damage totaled to $6.7 million and 3 people were killed. [CLS] [SEP] Roughly 140 people were evacuated after 44 homes were flooded. [CLS] [SEP] After the season, Drena was retired. [CLS] [SEP]
