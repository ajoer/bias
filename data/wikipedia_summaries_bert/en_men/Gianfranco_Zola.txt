Gianfranco Zola (Italian pronunciation: [dʒaɱˈfraŋko dˈdzɔːla]; born 5 July 1966) is an Italian football manager and former footballer who played predominantly as a forward. [CLS] [SEP] He was most recently the assistant manager of Chelsea. [CLS] [SEP] He spent the first decade of his playing career playing in Italy, most notably with Napoli, alongside Diego Maradona and Careca, where he was able to win the Serie A title, and at Parma, where he won the Italian Super Cup and the UEFA Cup. [CLS] [SEP] He later moved to English side Chelsea, where he was voted the Football Writers' Player of the Year in the 1996–97 season. [CLS] [SEP] During his time at the club, he won the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup, the UEFA Super Cup, two FA Cups, the League Cup, and the Community Shield. [CLS] [SEP] In 2003, he was voted Chelsea's greatest player ever. [CLS] [SEP] He was capped 35 times for Italy from his debut in 1991, appearing at the 1994 World Cup, where Italy finished in second place, and Euro 1996. [CLS] [SEP] After a stint with Italy under-21s, Zola began his club managerial career with West Ham United of the Premier League in 2008 in the Premier League, before being sacked in 2010. [CLS] [SEP] He was manager of Watford from July 2012 until he announced his resignation on 16 December 2013. [CLS] [SEP] From December 2014 to March 2015 he managed Cagliari in Serie A. [CLS] [SEP] He returned to Chelsea as the assistant of new Chelsea manager Maurizio Sarri on 18 July 2018, ahead of the 2018–19 Premier League season. [CLS] [SEP]
