The 2006 Westchester County tornado was the strongest and largest tornado in Westchester County, New York since the 1904 Chappaqua tornado. [CLS] [SEP] It touched down there on Wednesday, July 12, 2006 and traveled 13 miles (21 km) into southwestern Connecticut during a 33-minute span through two states. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado touched down at 3:30 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] EDT (19:30 UTC) on the shore of the Hudson River before becoming a waterspout and traveling 3 mi (5 km) across the river. [CLS] [SEP] Coming ashore, the tornado entered Westchester County and struck the town of Sleepy Hollow at F1 intensity. [CLS] [SEP] After passing through the town, it intensified into an F2 tornado and grew to almost a quarter of a mile (400 m) in diameter. [CLS] [SEP] The tornado continued through the county, damaging numerous structures, until it crossed into Connecticut at 4:01 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] EDT (20:01 UTC). [CLS] [SEP] Not long after entering the state, it dissipated in the town of Greenwich at 4:03 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] EDT (20:03 UTC). [CLS] [SEP] When the tornado entered Westchester County, it was the eighth known tornado to either touch down or enter the county since 1950.Two barns and a warehouse were destroyed, and a large stained-glass window was shattered. [CLS] [SEP] Numerous homes and businesses were damaged and thousands of trees were uprooted. [CLS] [SEP] There were no fatalities and only six minor injuries were associated with the storm. [CLS] [SEP] The cost of damages was estimated at $12.1 million. [CLS] [SEP]
