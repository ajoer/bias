Tropical Storm Gamma was the 24th tropical storm of the 2005 Atlantic hurricane season. [CLS] [SEP] Gamma formed on November 18 from a tropical wave which had left the coast of Africa on November 3. [CLS] [SEP] Between November 13 and November 16 the system was designated Tropical Depression Twenty-Seven and moved westward through the Windward Islands into the Caribbean. [CLS] [SEP] Although its winds were not of tropical storm force, the storm brought damagingly heavy rainfall to Trinidad and to St. [CLS] [SEP] Vincent and the Grenadines. [CLS] [SEP] On November 18, after a period of disorganization during which it appeared to be dissipating, the system strengthened and was upgraded to a tropical storm off the coast of Honduras and given the name Gamma. [CLS] [SEP] There, its torrential rainfall triggered deadly landslides. [CLS] [SEP] Gamma's winds and rains also reached into Belize, where they were less deadly. [CLS] [SEP] The storm dissipated on November 22, having killed 39 people. [CLS] [SEP]
