[CLS] In mid-June 2016, severe weather such as heavy rainfall, thunderstorms and hail began across southern China, triggering deadly floods. [SEP] [CLS] Over the following month, additional rain events exacerbated flooding and affected more of the country. [SEP] [CLS] Areas along the Yangtze River and Huai River have been particularly hard-hit. [SEP] [CLS] An estimated 32 million people across 26 provinces were affected and more than 200 people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] 280,000 hectares (700,000 acres) of cropland was destroyed, with state damage estimates of around US$5.73 billion. [SEP] [CLS] According to Aon Benfield, the damage estimate has reached US$22 billion. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding of this magnitude was last seen in the country in 1998. [SEP] [CLS] As researches has been conducted, more than 23 600 houses have been destroyed and 3 600 refugees homeless in that situation. [SEP] [CLS]
