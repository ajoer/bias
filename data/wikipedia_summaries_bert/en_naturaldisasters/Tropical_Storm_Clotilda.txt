[CLS] Cyclone Clotilda was a destructive tropical cyclone that inundated Réunion in February 1987. [SEP] [CLS] A tropical disturbance first formed between Madagascar and Réunion on February 9 and slowly intensified thereafter. [SEP] [CLS] While meandering, the storm fluctuated in intensity before it reached its peak intensity on February 13, with winds of 110 km/h (70 mph). [SEP] [CLS] After passing near Réunion, it began to weaken. [SEP] [CLS] On February 16, however, Clotilda began to regain strength, and reached its secondary peak on February 17. [SEP] [CLS] Two days later, Clotilda became an extratropical cyclone. [SEP] [CLS] By February 22, Clotilda was no long being tracked by meteorologists. [SEP] [CLS] While active, it brought torrential rains to the island of Réunion during a span of 72 hours. [SEP] [CLS] A total of 1,855 mm (73.0 in) of rain was recorded in La Plaine-des-Palmistes; rainfall totals occasionally exceeded the totals measured during Cyclone Hyacinthe, the last major storm to affect Réunion. [SEP] [CLS] However, the peak total measured in Hyacinthe was lower than the total measured during Clotilda. [SEP] [CLS] Furthermore, about 250 homes were damaged and roughly 120 homes were destroyed. [SEP] [CLS] Eighty-nine trees were also brought down during the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Throughout the island, damage totaled $2 million (1987 USD) and 10 people were killed. [SEP] [CLS] In addition to the destruction on Réunion, 5% of crops on Mauritius were impacted by the storm. [SEP] [CLS] During the aftermath of the cyclone, 1,000 people on Réunion were evacuated to shelters. [SEP] [CLS]
