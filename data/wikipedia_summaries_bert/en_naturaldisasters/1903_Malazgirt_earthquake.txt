The 1903 Manzikert earthquake struck Manzikert of nowadays Muş Province in eastern Turkey on 28 April. [CLS] [SEP] Registering a surface wave magnitude of 7.0, the earthquake originated from a highly active seismic zone. [CLS] [SEP] It killed 3,500 people and 20,000 animals, destroyed 12,000 homes, and is now listed among Turkey's deadliest earthquakes in recorded history. [CLS] [SEP] To this day, Turkey is threatened by major earthquakes – more than 100 earthquakes over 7.0 have taken place in the country's known history. [CLS] [SEP]
