Cubana de Aviación Flight 455 was a Cuban flight from Barbados to Jamaica that was brought down on October 6, 1976 by a terrorist bomb attack. [CLS] [SEP] All 73 people on board the Douglas DC-8 aircraft were killed. [CLS] [SEP] Two time bombs were used, whose explosives have been variously described as dynamite or C-4. [CLS] [SEP] The crash killed every member of the Cuban National Fencing team. [CLS] [SEP] Several CIA-linked anti-Castro Cuban exiles were implicated by the evidence. [CLS] [SEP] Political complications quickly arose when Cuba accused the US government of being an accomplice to the attack. [CLS] [SEP] CIA documents released in 2005 indicate that the agency "had concrete advance intelligence, as early as June 1976, on plans by Cuban exile terrorist groups to bomb a Cubana airliner." Former CIA terrorist operative Luis Posada Carriles denies involvement but provides many details of the incident in his book Caminos del Guerrero (Ways of the Warrior). [CLS] [SEP] The Coordination of United Revolutionary Organizations, of which Carriles was a member, is widely seen as responsible for the bombing.Four men were arrested in connection with the bombing, and a trial was held in Venezuela. [CLS] [SEP] Freddy Lugo and Hernán Ricardo Lozano were each sentenced to 20-year prison terms. [CLS] [SEP] Orlando Bosch was acquitted and later moved to Miami, Florida, where he lived until his death on April 27, 2011. [CLS] [SEP] Luis Posada Carriles was held for eight years while awaiting a final sentence but eventually fled. [CLS] [SEP] He later entered the United States, where he was held on charges of entering the country illegally, but was released on April 19, 2007. [CLS] [SEP]
