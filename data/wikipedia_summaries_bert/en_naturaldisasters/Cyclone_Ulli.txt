Cyclone Ulli (also named Cyclone Emil by the Norwegian Meteorological Institute) was an intense and deadly European windstorm. [CLS] [SEP] Forming on December 31, 2011 off the coast of New Jersey, Ulli began a rapid strengthening phase on January 2 as it sped across the Atlantic. [CLS] [SEP] Ulli was the costliest disaster in January 2012 globally. [CLS] [SEP] The damage from the storm in Glasgow was also compared to a storm in 1968.Ulli was one of many storms to affect Europe during the winter of 2011-2012. [CLS] [SEP] The storm clustering began in late-November when Xaver and Yoda hit the United Kingdom and Norway. [CLS] [SEP] In early to mid-December, Friedhelm, Hergen and Joachim hit northern Europe. [CLS] [SEP] Another storm, Patrick hit Scandinavia on Christmas Day. [CLS] [SEP] Ulli was followed by Andrea which formed the next day and struck northern Europe on 5 January. [CLS] [SEP]
