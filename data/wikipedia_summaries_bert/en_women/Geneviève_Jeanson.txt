[CLS] Geneviève Jeanson (born August 29, 1981) is a former professional bicycle racer from Quebec, Canada. [SEP] [CLS] She won the world junior road and time trial championships in 1999 and the Tour de Snowy in 2000. [SEP] [CLS] Later that year she won La Flèche Wallonne World Cup race. [SEP] [CLS] She joined the Canadian Olympic team that year. [SEP] [CLS] She acknowledged in a documentary on Radio-Canada (the French-language CBC) on September 20, 2007, that she had been administered EPO more or less continuously since she was 16 years old.After residing in Phoenix, Arizona and San Diego, California (where she studied sociology and psychology), Jeanson came back to Lachine, Quebec in 2012 to live with her once estranged parents and complete her college-level education at the Saint-Anne Collégial International. [SEP] [CLS] In autumn 2014, she attended Concordia University, in Montreal, where she studied neuroscience. [SEP] [CLS] Jeanson currently lives with her common law husband and works in the fitness industry. [SEP] [CLS]
