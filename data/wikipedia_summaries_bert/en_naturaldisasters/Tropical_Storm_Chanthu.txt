[CLS] Severe Tropical Storm Chanthu, known in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Gener, was a strong tropical storm that produced deadly flooding in Vietnam and Thailand. [SEP] [CLS] Originating from an area of low pressure on June 5, 2004, Chanthu was first declared a tropical depression near southern Leyte Island in the Philippines. [SEP] [CLS] Tracking west-northwestward, the depression intensified into a tropical storm over the central Philippines before entering the South China Sea. [SEP] [CLS] Once over the warm waters of the sea, the system quickly intensified, attaining its peak 10-minute winds of 110 km/h (70 mph) and 1-minute winds of 140 km/h (85 mph). [SEP] [CLS] On June 12, the storm made landfall in Vietnam before quickly weakening over land. [SEP] [CLS] By June 13, the system had weakened to a tropical depression and dissipated two days later. [SEP] [CLS] In Vietnam, Chanthu wrought substantial damage and killed 38 people. [SEP] [CLS] Damage from the storm was estimated at 125 billion Vietnam dong (US$7.9 million), mostly from agricultural losses. [SEP] [CLS] One person was also killed in Thailand. [SEP] [CLS]
