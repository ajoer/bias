Sandra Day O'Connor (born March 26, 1930) is an American attorney, politician, and jurist who served as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States from 1981 until her retirement in 2006. [CLS] [SEP] She was the first woman to serve on the Court.Prior to O'Connor's tenure on the Court, she was a judge and an elected official in Arizona, serving as the first female Majority Leader of a state senate as the Republican leader in the Arizona Senate. [CLS] [SEP] Upon her nomination to the Court, O'Connor was confirmed unanimously by the Senate. [CLS] [SEP] On July 1, 2005, she announced her intention to retire effective upon the confirmation of a successor. [CLS] [SEP] Samuel Alito was nominated to take her seat in October 2005 and joined the Court on January 31, 2006. [CLS] [SEP] As a moderate Republican, O'Connor tended to approach each case narrowly without seeking to establish sweeping precedents. [CLS] [SEP] She most frequently sided with the Court's conservative bloc, having the swing opinion in many decisions. [CLS] [SEP] She often wrote concurring opinions that limited the reach of the majority holding. [CLS] [SEP] Her majority opinions in landmark cases include Grutter v. [CLS] [SEP] Bollinger and Hamdi v. [CLS] [SEP] Rumsfeld. [CLS] [SEP] She also wrote in part the per curiam majority opinion in Bush v. [CLS] [SEP] Gore, and was one of three co-authors of the lead opinion in Planned Parenthood v. [CLS] [SEP] Casey. [CLS] [SEP] During her time on the court, some publications ranked her among the most powerful women in the world. [CLS] [SEP] On August 12, 2009, she was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama. [CLS] [SEP]
