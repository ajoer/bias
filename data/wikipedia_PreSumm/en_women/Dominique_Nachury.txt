[CLS] Legislative elections were held on 11 and 18 June 2017 (with different dates for voters overseas) to elect the 577 members of the 15th National Assembly of the French Fifth Republic. [SEP] [CLS] They followed the two-round presidential election won by Emmanuel Macron. [SEP] [CLS] The centrist party he founded in 2016, La République En Marche! [SEP] [CLS] (LREM), led an alliance with the centrist Democratic Movement (MoDem); together, the two parties won 350 of the 577 seats – a substantial majority – in the National Assembly, including an outright majority of 308 seats for LREM. [SEP] [CLS] The Socialist Party (PS) was reduced to 30 seats and the Republicans (LR) reduced to 112 seats, and both parties' allies also suffered from a marked drop in support; these were the lowest-ever scores for the centre-left and centre-right in the legislative elections. [SEP] [CLS] The movement founded by Jean-Luc Mélenchon, la France Insoumise (FI), secured 17 seats, enough for a group in the National Assembly. [SEP] [CLS] Among other major parties, the French Communist Party (PCF) secured ten and the National Front (FN) obtained eight seats. [SEP] [CLS] Both rounds of the legislative election were marked by record low turnout.Édouard Philippe, appointed as Prime Minister by Macron following his victory in the presidential election, was reappointed following the second round of the legislative elections and presented his second government by 21 June. [SEP] [CLS] The 15th legislature of the French Fifth Republic commenced on 27 June. [SEP] [CLS]
