[CLS] Susan Polgar (born April 19, 1969, as Polgár Zsuzsanna and often known as Zsuzsa Polgár) is a Hungarian and American chess player. [SEP] [CLS] Polgár was Women's World Champion from 1996 to 1999. [SEP] [CLS] On the FIDE rating list of July 1984, at the age of 15, she became the top-ranked female player in the world. [SEP] [CLS] In 1991 she became the third woman to be awarded the title of Grandmaster by FIDE. [SEP] [CLS] She won twelve medals at the Women's Chess Olympiad (5 gold, 4 silver and 3 bronze). [SEP] [CLS] Also a trainer, writer and promoter, Polgar sponsors various chess tournaments for young players and is the head of the Susan Polgar Institute for Chess Excellence (SPICE) at Webster University. [SEP] [CLS] She served as the Chairperson (or co-chair) of the FIDE Commission for Women's Chess from 2008 until late 2018. [SEP] [CLS]
