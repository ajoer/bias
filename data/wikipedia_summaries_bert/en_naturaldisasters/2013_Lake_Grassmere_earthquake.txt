The 2013 Lake Grassmere earthquake was a magnitude 6.6 earthquake that occurred at 2:31:05 pm (NZST) on Friday 16 August 2013. [CLS] [SEP] The epicentre was located about 10 km south-east of Seddon, under Lake Grassmere, with a focal depth of 8 km. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake caused significant land damage in the local area, with landslips blocking roads, including the main highway between Blenheim and Christchurch. [CLS] [SEP] Buildings in Seddon were damaged, with some being declared uninhabitable. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake was widely felt in both the North and South Islands of New Zealand. [CLS] [SEP]
