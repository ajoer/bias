[CLS] Franck Henry Pierre Ribéry (French pronunciation: ​[fʁɑ̃k ʁibeʁi]; born 7 April 1983) is a French professional footballer who plays for Serie A club Fiorentina. [SEP] [CLS] He primarily plays as a winger, preferably on the left side, and is known for his pace, energy, skill, and precise passing. [SEP] [CLS] Ribéry is described as a player who is fast, tricky and an excellent dribbler, who has great control with the ball at his feet. [SEP] [CLS] Since joining Bayern, he has been recognised on the world stage as one of the best players of his generation. [SEP] [CLS] The previous talisman of the French national team, Zinedine Zidane, once referred to Ribéry as the "jewel of French football".Ribéry's career began in 1989 as a youth player for local hometown club Conti Boulogne. [SEP] [CLS] He left the club after seven years to join professional outfit Lille, but departed the club after three years after having difficulties adjusting. [SEP] [CLS] In 1999, Ribéry joined US Boulogne, where he played for two years. [SEP] [CLS] After spending two more years in the amateur divisions with two clubs (Alès and Brest), in 2004, Ribéry earned a move to Ligue 1 club Metz. [SEP] [CLS] After six months with the club, Ribéry moved to Turkey in January 2005 to join Galatasaray, where he won the Turkish Cup. [SEP] [CLS] After six months at Galatasaray, he departed the club in controversial fashion in order to return to France to join Marseille. [SEP] [CLS] Ribéry spent two seasons at the club, helping the Marseillais reach the final of the Coupe de France in back-to-back seasons. [SEP] [CLS] In 2007, Ribéry joined German club Bayern Munich for a then club-record fee of €25 million. [SEP] [CLS] With Bayern, he won nine Bundesliga titles (a Bundesliga record), six DFB-Pokal, one UEFA Champions League and one FIFA Club World Cup, which include four doubles and one treble, amounting to a club record of 24 titles over twelve seasons. [SEP] [CLS] His form for Bayern in the club's 2012–13 treble winning season saw him nominated alongside Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo on the three-man shortlist for the 2013 FIFA Ballon d'Or. [SEP] [CLS] During his long spell at Bayern, Ribéry was also known for his fruitful partnership with fellow winger Arjen Robben—together they were affectionately referred to by the nickname Robbery. [SEP] [CLS] He left Bayern in summer 2019, and subsequently joined Italian side Fiorentina, while Robben retired from football. [SEP] [CLS] Between 2006 and 2014, Ribéry represented the France national football team 81 times. [SEP] [CLS] Ribéry has represented his nation at two FIFA World Cups (2006, 2010) and two UEFA European Championships (2008, 2012). [SEP] [CLS] He made his international debut in May 2006 against Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] At the 2006 World Cup, Ribéry scored his first international goal against Spain and played in the final match against Italy. [SEP] [CLS] Individually, Ribéry is a three-time winner of the French Player of the Year award and also won the Footballer of the Year in Germany award, becoming the first player to hold both honours. [SEP] [CLS] He has also been named to the UEFA Team of the Year and declared the Young Player of the Year in France. [SEP] [CLS] In 2013, Ribéry won the UEFA Best Player in Europe Award. [SEP] [CLS] In 2013, he was also ranked fourth in The Guardian's list of the best players in the world. [SEP] [CLS]
