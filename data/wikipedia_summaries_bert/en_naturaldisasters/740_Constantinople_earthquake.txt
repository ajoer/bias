The 740 Constantinople earthquake took place on 26 October, 740, in the vicinity of Constantinople and the Sea of Marmara. [CLS] [SEP] In Constantinople, the earthquake caused the collapse of many public buildings. [CLS] [SEP] The Walls of Constantinople were also damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The casualties in Constantinople reportedly included over 1,000 people. [CLS] [SEP] The earthquake reportedly destroyed a number of towns in Thrace. [CLS] [SEP] It also damaged the cities of Nicaea (İznik), Nicomedia (İzmit), and Praenetus. [CLS] [SEP] The historians Theophanes the Confessor (8th-9th century) and George Kedrenos (11th century) reported that the earthquake caused the sea to retire away from the coast in many places. [CLS] [SEP] The sea soon returned, and flooded many towns. [CLS] [SEP] The description given matches that of a seismic sea-wave (tsunami). [CLS] [SEP] The date of the earthquake is recorded by (among others) Anastasius Bibliothecarius (9th century), Joannes Zonaras (12th century), Carolus Sigonius (16th century), Caesar Baronius (16th-17th century), and Sethus Calvisius (16th-17th century).
