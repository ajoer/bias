Tropical Storm Tammy was a short lived tropical storm during October in the 2005 Atlantic hurricane season which caused minor damage to the southeastern United States. [CLS] [SEP] More significant, however, were its remnants, which contributed to the Northeast U.S. [CLS] [SEP] flooding of October 2005. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Tammy formed from a non-tropical system off the Florida coast on October 5. [CLS] [SEP] It moved north just offshore before making landfall later that day. [CLS] [SEP] The tropical storm rapidly weakened as it moved overland and dissipated the next day. [CLS] [SEP] Its remnant circulation moved south towards the Gulf of Mexico, while the moisture was absorbed by a northeasterly moving cold front. [CLS] [SEP] There were no fatalities directly related to Tammy; however, ten people were killed by the remnants of the storm in combination with the remnants of Subtropical Depression Twenty-Two. [CLS] [SEP] Total damages from the storm were $30 million. [CLS] [SEP]
