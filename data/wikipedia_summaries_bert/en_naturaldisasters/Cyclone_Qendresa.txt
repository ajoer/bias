Cyclone Qendresa, also known as Medicane Qendresa, was one of the most intense Mediterranean tropical-like cyclones on record, which struck Malta and the Italian island of Sicily in 2014. [CLS] [SEP] The storm formed on 5 November and rapidly intensified two days later, reaching peak intensity on 7 November, due to a cold-core low aloft. [CLS] [SEP] Qendresa directly hit Malta in the afternoon and then crossed the eastern coast of Sicily on 8 November. [CLS] [SEP] Later, the cyclone weakened significantly and dissipated over Crete on 11 November. [CLS] [SEP] Academic sources indicate that Qendresa transitioned into a subtropical cyclone, prior to reaching peak intensity. [CLS] [SEP] Qendresa caused three fatalities, and at least $250 million (2014 USD) in damages in Italy. [CLS] [SEP]
