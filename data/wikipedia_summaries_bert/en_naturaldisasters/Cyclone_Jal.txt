Severe Cyclonic Storm Jal was the fifth named cyclonic storm and the fourth Severe Cyclonic Storm of the 2010 North Indian Ocean cyclone season. [CLS] [SEP] Jal developed from a low pressure area in the South China Sea that organized into a Tropical Depression on October 28. [CLS] [SEP] Jal is a Sanskrit word, meaning water. [CLS] [SEP] At least 54 people are known to have been killed in India. [CLS] [SEP] As a tropical depression, Jal produced torrential rains over parts of Thailand and Malaysia, triggering severe flooding which killed 59 and four people in the two countries respectively. [CLS] [SEP] In Sri Lanka, heavy rainfall with strong winds have caused flooding affecting around 80,000 people. [CLS] [SEP] In Thailand, Jal was responsible for 78 deaths, being the 4th deadliest tropical cyclone in the country. [CLS] [SEP]
