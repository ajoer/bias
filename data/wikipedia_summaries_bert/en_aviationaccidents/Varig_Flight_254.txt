Varig Flight 254 was a Boeing 737-241, c/n 21006/398, registration PP-VMK, on a scheduled passenger flight from São Paulo, Brazil, to Belém, capital city of the state of Pará in the country's North Region, on 3 September 1989. [CLS] [SEP] The flight had several intermediate stopovers, the last being in Marabá, Pará. [CLS] [SEP] Prior to takeoff from Marabá, the crew entered an incorrect heading into the flight computer, flying deep into a remote area of the Amazon jungle. [CLS] [SEP] Attempts to reach an alternative airport were unsuccessful, and the plane eventually ran out of fuel, making a belly landing in the jungle 1,050 mi (1,690 km) northwest of Rio de Janeiro. [CLS] [SEP] Of the 54 passengers and crew, 12 passengers died and many more sustained serious injuries. [CLS] [SEP] The survivors were rescued two days later. [CLS] [SEP]
