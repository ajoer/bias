[CLS] Kerstin Szymkowiak (born Kerstin Jürgens on 19 December 1977 in Siegen) is a German retired skeleton racer who has competed since 2002. [SEP] [CLS] She won three bronze medals in the women's skeleton event, earning them in 2004, 2008, and 2009. [SEP] [CLS] Her best overall seasonal finish in the women's Skeleton World Cup title was third in 2004-5. [SEP] [CLS] Jürgens married after the 2007-08 Skeleton World Cup season and has been competing since in the 2008-09 Skeleton World Cup season under her married name. [SEP] [CLS] She finished second behind defending world champion Anja Huber of Germany in the season opener at Winterberg on November 28, 2008. [SEP] [CLS] Szymkowiak's nickname is the "Ice Tiger" (Eistiger in German). [SEP] [CLS] She earned the silver medal at the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver. [SEP] [CLS] After retiring from the sport Szymkowiak was appointed an Athlete Role Model for the 2012 Winter Youth Olympics in Innsbruck. [SEP] [CLS] In May 2012 the Swiss bobsleigh and skeleton federation announced that Szymkowiak had been elected as head of their skeleton section.Szymkowiak's husband Philippe is a massage therapist for the Swiss national bobsleigh team. [SEP] [CLS] She gave birth to a daughter, Noalie, in September 2010. [SEP] [CLS]
