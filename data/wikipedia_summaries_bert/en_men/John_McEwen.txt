[CLS] Sir John McEwen, (29 March 1900 â€“ 20 November 1980) was an Australian politician who served as the 18th Prime Minister of Australia, holding office from 19 December 1967 to 10 January 1968 in a caretaker capacity after the disappearance of Harold Holt. [SEP] [CLS] He was the leader of the Country Party from 1958 to 1971. [SEP] [CLS] McEwen was born in Chiltern, Victoria. [SEP] [CLS] He was orphaned at the age of seven and raised by his grandmother, initially in Wangaratta and then in Dandenong. [SEP] [CLS] McEwen left school when he was 13 and joined the Australian Army at the age of 18, but the war ended before his unit was shipped out. [SEP] [CLS] He was nonetheless eligible for a soldier settlement scheme, and selected a property at Stanhope. [SEP] [CLS] He established a dairy farm, but later bought a larger property and farmed beef cattle. [SEP] [CLS] After several previous unsuccessful candidacies, McEwen was elected to the House of Representatives at the 1934 federal election. [SEP] [CLS] He was first elevated to cabinet by Joseph Lyons in 1937. [SEP] [CLS] McEwen became deputy leader of the Country Party in 1940, under Arthur Fadden. [SEP] [CLS] He replaced Fadden as leader in 1958, and remained in the position until his retirement from politics in 1971. [SEP] [CLS] He served in parliament for 36 years in total, spending a record 25 years as a government minister. [SEP] [CLS] The Coalition returned to power in 1949, initially under Robert Menzies and then under Harold Holt. [SEP] [CLS] McEwen came to have a major influence on economic policy, particularly in the areas of agriculture, manufacturing, and trade. [SEP] [CLS] When Holt died in office in December 1967, he was commissioned as caretaker prime minister while the Liberal Party elected a new leader. [SEP] [CLS] He was 67 at the time, the oldest person to become prime minister and only the third from the Country Party. [SEP] [CLS] McEwen ceded power to John Gorton after 23 days in office, and in recognition of his service was appointed deputy prime minister, the first time that position had been formally created.
