British European Airways Flight 411 crashed on approach to Manchester Airport after a flight from Amsterdam Schiphol International Airport on 14 March 1957 and hit a house in Wythenshawe. [CLS] [SEP] All on board, 20 passengers and crew, died in the crash as did two people in the house. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft involved was a Vickers Viscount registration G-ALWE operated by British European Airways. [CLS] [SEP] The cause of the crash was metal fatigue in flaps causing loss of control. [CLS] [SEP]
