The 1961 Holtaheia Vickers Viking crash (Norwegian: Holtaheia-ulykken) was a controlled flight into terrain incident on 9 August 1961 at Holta in Strand, Norway. [CLS] [SEP] The Eagle Airways (later, British Eagle) Vickers 610 Viking 3B Lord Rodney was en route from London to Stavanger Airport, Sola on an AIR Tours charter flight taking a school group for a camping holiday. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was making an instrument landing when it crashed 54 km (34 mi) north east of Stavanger. [CLS] [SEP] The accident killed all 39 people on board. [CLS] [SEP]
