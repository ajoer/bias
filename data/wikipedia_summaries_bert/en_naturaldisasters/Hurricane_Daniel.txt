The name Daniel has been used for eight tropical cyclones in the eastern Pacific Ocean. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Daniel (1978), a Category 3 hurricane that did not affect land. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Daniel (1982), a Category 3 which reached Hawaii as a tropical depression and dissipated in the Alenuihaha Channel between Maui and the Big Island. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Daniel (1988), did not make landfall. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Daniel (1994), no reports of damage or casualties. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Daniel (2000), a Category 3 hurricane that threatened Hawaii for a time while weakening. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Daniel (2006), powerful Category 4 which brought rain to Hawaii as a tropical depression. [CLS] [SEP] Hurricane Daniel (2012), a Category 3 hurricane that did not affect land. [CLS] [SEP] Tropical Storm Daniel (2018), a weak tropical storm that never threatened land. [CLS] [SEP]
