[CLS] Hatem Ben Arfa (Arabic: حاتم بن عرفة‎, French pronunciation: ​[atɛm bɛnaʁfa]; born 7 March 1987) is a French professional footballer who plays as a winger and an attacking midfielder for Spanish club Valladolid. [SEP] [CLS] Known for his dribbling ability, he has been described as "one of the best-rated talents in France" but has been criticised by the media and players alike for lacking discipline.Ben Arfa's career started in the Île-de-France region, where he trained at AC Boulogne-Billancourt and Versailles. [SEP] [CLS] In 1999, he was selected to attend the Clairefontaine academy. [SEP] [CLS] He spent three years there before leaving for Lyon, where he won four Ligue 1 titles. [SEP] [CLS] In his early career at Lyon, he played as a centre forward but moved into a winger role during the 2007–08 season. [SEP] [CLS] In the summer of 2008, Ben Arfa signed with rivals Marseille for €11 million in a move that required the intervention of the Ligue de Football Professionnel. [SEP] [CLS] With Marseille, he won the 2009–10 league title, his fifth overall, as well as the Coupe de la Ligue in 2010. [SEP] [CLS] After two years at Marseille, Ben Arfa joined English club Newcastle United on loan for the 2010–11 season. [SEP] [CLS] The deal was made permanent later in the season.Ben Arfa spent four years at Newcastle, with a loan spell at Hull City in his final season at the club. [SEP] [CLS] He signed for Nice in January 2015 but was unable to make his debut until August, having already represented Newcastle United Reserves and Hull City the previous season. [SEP] [CLS] He scored seventeen goals in thirty-two league appearances for Nice, attracting the interest of several European clubs, and signed for Paris Saint-Germain on 1 July 2016. [SEP] [CLS] After failing to make a competitive appearance in the 2017–18 season, Ben Arfa signed for Rennes, where he won the Coupe de France over Paris Saint-Germain.Ben Arfa is a former French youth international and has played at all levels for France. [SEP] [CLS] At the under-17 level, he was a part of the team that won the 2004 UEFA European Under-17 Championship. [SEP] [CLS] Ben Arfa was called up to the senior team for the first time in October 2007 for a UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying match against the Faroe Islands. [SEP] [CLS] He has made fifteen appearances for France, scoring twice. [SEP] [CLS]
