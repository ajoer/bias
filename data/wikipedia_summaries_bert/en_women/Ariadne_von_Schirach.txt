Ariadne von Schirach (born 1978 in Munich) is a German philosopher, writer, journalist and critic. [CLS] [SEP] She is known as a literary critic for Deutschlandradio Kultur, and as an essayist and columnist for newspapers such as Die Welt and Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung. [CLS] [SEP] She studied philosophy, psychology and sociology at LMU, the Free University of Berlin and the Humboldt University of Berlin. [CLS] [SEP] She teaches philosophy and chinese thinking at the Berlin University of the Arts, the Hochschule für Bildende Künste Hamburg and the Donau-Universität Krems since 2012. [CLS] [SEP] In 2007 she published the book Der Tanz um die Lust, about the consequences of an increasingly sexualized society, which became a bestseller. [CLS] [SEP] In 2014 she published her second book, Du sollst nicht funktionieren: Für eine neue Lebenskunst. [CLS] [SEP] 2016 she published the psychoanalytical textbook Ich und du und Müllers Kuh. [CLS] [SEP] She is a cousin of the lawyer and bestselling crime writer Ferdinand von Schirach and the sister of the novelist Benedict Wells. [CLS] [SEP]
