Annelise Coberger (born 16 September 1971) is a New Zealand former alpine skier. [CLS] [SEP] Born in Christchurch, she became the first person from the Southern Hemisphere to win a medal at the Winter Olympics when she won silver in the slalom at Albertville in France in 1992. [CLS] [SEP] For this success, at the annual Halberg Awards she was awarded the title of New Zealand Sportsman of the Year. [CLS] [SEP] Coberger also competed at the 1994 Winter Olympics in Lillehammer but did not finish her first run of the slalom. [CLS] [SEP] Coberger remained the only Winter Olympic medalist from New Zealand for 26 years until Zoi Sadowski-Synnott won bronze in the women's big air at the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang. [CLS] [SEP] Her other victories include a single World Cup slalom. [CLS] [SEP] In the 1992-93 season, she was the runner-up in the Slalom World Cup with just 6 points behind the winner Vreni Schneider. [CLS] [SEP] Coberger finished competing at an international level a couple of years after her Olympic success. [CLS] [SEP] She then joined the New Zealand Police. [CLS] [SEP]
