[CLS] Anja Rupel (born 19 March 1966) is a Slovene pop singer, songwriter, radio announcer, and journalist. [SEP] [CLS] Her father, Fedja Rupel, is a flautist and a professor at the Academy of Music in Ljubljana, and her uncle is the politician and diplomat Dimitrij Rupel. [SEP] [CLS] Rupel has been involved in music since childhood. [SEP] [CLS] She played the flute for over ten years. [SEP] [CLS] In 1982, she began to perform as lead vocalist with the prominent synthpop group Videosex, which became very popular in the former Yugoslavia. [SEP] [CLS] The group recorded four albums. [SEP] [CLS] Their debut album, Videosex 84 appeared in 1983 and they played with bands such as Ekatarina Velika and Otroci Socializma in Belgrade. [SEP] [CLS] The group broke up in 1992. [SEP] [CLS] Rupel then went on to her solo career and, in 1994 she recorded her first album Odpri oči (Open Your Eyes). [SEP] [CLS] For a while, she performed with the internationally prominent industrial/techno group Laibach from Trbovlje, Slovenia. [SEP] [CLS] In 1988, she sang The Beatles' anthem, Across the Universe and was the first Slovene to be featured on MTV. [SEP] [CLS] She is now recording new album in cooperation with the Slovene musician Rudi Štigel. [SEP] [CLS] She also performed in the 1987 film Hudodelci (The Felons) directed by Franci Slak, and in several other films. [SEP] [CLS] On 11 October 2002, she gave birth to a daughter named Luna. [SEP] [CLS] She is with a musician and singer Aleš Klinar who was a leader of a folk-pop group Agropop. [SEP] [CLS]
