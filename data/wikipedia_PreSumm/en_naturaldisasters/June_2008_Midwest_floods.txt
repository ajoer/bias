[CLS] The June 2008 Midwestern United States floods were flooding events which affected portions of the Midwestern United States. [SEP] [CLS] After months of heavy precipitation, a number of rivers overflowed their banks for several weeks at a time and broke through levees at numerous locations. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding continued into July. [SEP] [CLS] States affected by the flooding included Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri and Wisconsin. [SEP] [CLS] The American Red Cross assisted the victims of flooding and tornadoes across seven states and the National Guard was mobilized to assist in disaster relief and evacuation. [SEP] [CLS] Flooding continued as long as two weeks with central Iowa and Cedar Rapids being hardest hit. [SEP] [CLS] The upper Mississippi Valley experienced flooding in Missouri and Illinois as the region's estuaries drained the floodwater into the river. [SEP] [CLS] The flood left thirteen dead and damage region-wide was estimated to be in the tens of billions of dollars. [SEP] [CLS]
