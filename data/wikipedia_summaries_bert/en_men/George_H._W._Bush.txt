[CLS] George Herbert Walker Bush (June 12, 1924 – November 30, 2018) was an American politician, diplomat, and businessman who served as the 41st president of the United States from 1989 to 1993. [SEP] [CLS] A member of the Republican Party, Bush also served in the U.S. [SEP] [CLS] House of Representatives, as U.S. [SEP] [CLS] Ambassador to the United Nations, as Director of Central Intelligence, and as the 43rd vice president. [SEP] [CLS] Bush was raised in Greenwich, Connecticut and attended Phillips Academy before serving in the United States Navy during World War II. [SEP] [CLS] After the war, he graduated from Yale University and moved to West Texas, where he established a successful oil company. [SEP] [CLS] After an unsuccessful run for the United States Senate, he won election to the 7th congressional district of Texas in 1966. [SEP] [CLS] President Richard Nixon appointed Bush to the position of Ambassador to the United Nations in 1971 and to the position of chairman of the Republican National Committee in 1973. [SEP] [CLS] In 1974, President Gerald Ford appointed him as the Chief of the Liaison Office to the People's Republic of China, and in 1976 Bush became the Director of Central Intelligence. [SEP] [CLS] Bush ran for president in 1980, but was defeated in the Republican presidential primaries by Ronald Reagan. [SEP] [CLS] He was then elected vice president in 1980 and 1984 as Reagan's running mate. [SEP] [CLS] In the 1988 presidential election, Bush defeated Democrat Michael Dukakis, becoming the first incumbent vice president to be elected president since Martin Van Buren in 1836. [SEP] [CLS] Foreign policy drove the Bush presidency, as he navigated the final years of the Cold War and played a key role in the reunification of Germany. [SEP] [CLS] Bush presided over the invasion of Panama and the Gulf War, ending the Iraqi occupation of Kuwait in the latter conflict. [SEP] [CLS] Though the agreement was not ratified until after he left office, Bush negotiated and signed the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), which created a trade bloc consisting of the United States, Canada, and Mexico. [SEP] [CLS] Domestically, Bush reneged on a 1988 campaign promise by signing a bill that increased taxes and helped reduce the federal budget deficit. [SEP] [CLS] He also signed the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 and appointed David Souter and Clarence Thomas to the Supreme Court. [SEP] [CLS] Bush lost the 1992 presidential election to Democrat Bill Clinton following an economic recession and the decreased emphasis of foreign policy in a post–Cold War political climate. [SEP] [CLS] After leaving office in 1993, Bush was active in humanitarian activities, often working alongside Clinton, his former opponent. [SEP] [CLS] With the victory of his son, George W. [SEP] [CLS] Bush, in the 2000 presidential election, the two became the second father–son pair to serve as the nation's president, following John Adams and John Quincy Adams. [SEP] [CLS] Another son, Jeb Bush, unsuccessfully sought the Republican presidential nomination in the 2016 Republican primaries. [SEP] [CLS] After a long battle with vascular Parkinson's disease, Bush died at his home on November 30, 2018. [SEP] [CLS] Historians generally rank Bush as an above average president. [SEP] [CLS]
