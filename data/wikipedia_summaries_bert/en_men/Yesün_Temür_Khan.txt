Yesün Temür (Mongolian: Есөн Төмөр, November 28, 1293 – August 15, 1328) was a great-grandson of Kublai Khan and ruled as emperor of the Yuan dynasty from 1323 to 1328. [CLS] [SEP] Apart from Emperor of China, he is regarded as the 10th Khagan of the Mongol Empire or Mongols, although it was only nominal due to the division of the empire. [CLS] [SEP] In Chinese, Yesün Temür Khan, who was very fond of the traditional ways of the Mongols, is known as the Emperor Taiding of Yuan (Chinese: 元泰定帝) from his era's name. [CLS] [SEP] His name means "nine iron Khan" in the Mongolian language. [CLS] [SEP] He was probably the emperor visited by the Franciscan friar Odoric, who left an excellent record of his travels.
