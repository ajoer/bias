[CLS] Typhoon Tembin, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Vinta, was the deadliest tropical cyclone to strike Mindanao since Typhoon Bopha in 2012. [SEP] [CLS] Following and impacting the Philippines less than a few days after the deadly Tropical Storm Kai-tak, Tembin, which means Libra in Japanese, the twenty-seventh named storm and the eleventh typhoon of the 2017 Pacific typhoon season. [SEP] [CLS] It was first classified as a weak tropical depression on December 16. [SEP] [CLS] The system gradually intensified and consolidated into a tropical storm on December 20. [SEP] [CLS] Tembin made landfall in Mindanao late the next day. [SEP] [CLS] On December 23, Tembin followed a path towards the South China Sea and intensified into a typhoon early the following day. [SEP] [CLS] Quick intensification ensued and Tembin reached its peak intensity as a low-end Category 2 typhoon as assessed by the Joint Typhoon Warning Center on December 24. [SEP] [CLS] Unfavorable conditions enhanced by the winter monsoon caused Tembin to rapidly weaken shortly afterwards, before it ultimately dissipated on December 26 while just south of Vietnam. [SEP] [CLS] Tembin caused a total of 266 deaths, five of which were from a sinking of a ferry due to rough seas caused by the storm. [SEP] [CLS] Due to the high death toll in the Philippines, the names Tembin and Vinta were retired from their respective name lists in early 2018. [SEP] [CLS] They will be replaced by Koinu and Verbena. [SEP] [CLS]
