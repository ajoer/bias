Tomaž Šalamun (July 4, 1941 – December 27, 2014) was a Slovenian poet who was a leading figure of postwar neo-avant-garde poetry in Central Europe and internationally acclaimed absurdist. [CLS] [SEP] His books of Slovene poetry have been translated into twenty-one languages, with nine of his thirty-nine books of poetry published in English. [CLS] [SEP] His work has been called a poetic bridge between old European roots and America. [CLS] [SEP] Šalamun was a member of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts. [CLS] [SEP] He lived in Ljubljana, Slovenia, and was married to the painter Metka Krašovec. [CLS] [SEP]
