Cyclonic Storm Komen was an unusual tropical cyclone that originated near the southern coast of Bangladesh and later struck the same country while drifting over the northern Bay of Bengal. [CLS] [SEP] The second named storm of the 2015 season, Komen brought several days of heavy rainfall to Myanmar, Bangladesh, and India. [CLS] [SEP] It formed as a depression on July 26 over the Ganges delta and moved in a circular motion around the northern Bay of Bengal. [CLS] [SEP] Komen intensified into a 75 km/h (45 mph) cyclonic storm and moved ashore southeastern Bangladesh on July 30. [CLS] [SEP] The system turned westward over land and was last noted over eastern India on August 2. [CLS] [SEP] Across its path, Komen dropped torrential rainfall, primarily in northwestern Myanmar where the precipitation totaled at 840 mm (33 in) in Paletwa. [CLS] [SEP] The rains compounded upon ongoing flooding and contributed to the worst flooding in the country in a century. [CLS] [SEP] About 1.7 million people were forced to evacuate as flood waters inundated houses to their rooftops. [CLS] [SEP] About 510,000 houses in the country were damaged or destroyed, and many residents lost their source of income as 667,221 acres (270,000 ha) of crop fields were damaged. [CLS] [SEP] The floods killed 132 people, of which at least 39 were directly related to Komen. [CLS] [SEP] The government requested assistance from the international community to cope with the disaster, considered the worst in the country since Cyclone Nargis in 2008. [CLS] [SEP] Elsewhere, the storm's flooding damaged 88,900 houses in Bangladesh and covered crop fields for a week; Komen killed 45 people in the country, some of whom due to illnesses spread by the storm. [CLS] [SEP] Later, floodwaters affected southeastern India, killing 103 people and damaging or destroying 476,046 houses. [CLS] [SEP]
