The 1989 Jamba Hercules crash was an air accident involving a Lockheed Hercules L-100 aircraft that crashed on final approach to Jamba, Cuando Cubango, Angola on 27 November 1989. [CLS] [SEP] The flight had originated at Kamina Airport, Zaire, and was attempting a low-level approach at night. [CLS] [SEP] The aircraft was owned by CIA front company Tepper Aviation; it was delivering arms to UNITA. [CLS] [SEP] The crash killed "Bud" Peddy, the head of Tepper Aviation, who was acting as the plane's pilot. [CLS] [SEP] On board were several Americans, two West Germans, and a Briton. [CLS] [SEP] All were killed in the crash. [CLS] [SEP]
