On December 28, 1997, United Airlines Flight 826 was operated by a Boeing 747-100 flying from New Tokyo Airport (which changed its name to Narita Airport in 2004), Japan to Honolulu International Airport, Hawaii. [CLS] [SEP] Two hours into the flight, at an altitude of 31,000 feet (9,448 m), the plane received reports of severe clear-air turbulence in the area and the seat belt sign was turned on. [CLS] [SEP] Moments later, the aircraft suddenly dropped around 100 feet (30 m), seriously injuring 15 passengers and 3 crew members. [CLS] [SEP] The plane turned around and landed safely back in Tokyo, but one passenger, a 32-year-old Japanese woman, died. [CLS] [SEP]
