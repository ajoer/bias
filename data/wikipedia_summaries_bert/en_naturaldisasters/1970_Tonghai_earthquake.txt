The 1970 Tonghai earthquake (Chinese: 1970年通海地震) occurred at 01:00:41 local time on January 5 with a moment magnitude of 7.1 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of X (Extreme). [CLS] [SEP] The strike-slip rupture originated on the Red River Fault, which had not experienced an earthquake above magnitude 7 since 1700, and affected Tonghai County, Yunnan province, China. [CLS] [SEP] At least 10,000 people were killed, making it one of the deadliest in its decade. [CLS] [SEP] The tremor caused between US$5 to $25 million in damage, felt over an area of 8,781 km2 (3,390 sq mi). [CLS] [SEP] In Hanoi, North Vietnam, almost 483 km (300 mi) from the epicenter, victims left their homes as the rupture rumbled through the city. [CLS] [SEP] Occurring during the height of the Cultural Revolution, it was not widely publicized by the Chinese government for well over a decade. [CLS] [SEP] The amount of aid and finances distributed was described by the Beijing Morning Post as "pathetically small". [CLS] [SEP] Much of the aid provided to survivors was in "spiritual" form, including Mao Zedong badges and condolence letters. [CLS] [SEP] Nevertheless, the earthquake was among the first to be studied over a long term by the Chinese government. [CLS] [SEP] It was cited as one of the reasons behind creating the largest earthquake monitoring system in China, 25 years later. [CLS] [SEP]
