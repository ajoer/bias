An earthquake occurred in Christchurch on 14 February 2016 at 1:13 p.m. [CLS] [SEP] local time (00:13 UTC) and initially recorded as 5.9 on the Richter scale, but subsequently reviewed as 5.7. [CLS] [SEP] Often referred to as the Valentine's Day earthquake, it was centred in the sea off New Brighton at a depth of 15 kilometres (9.3 mi). [CLS] [SEP] It was the first large earthquake that the Christchurch area had experienced since May 2012, and it was part of the earthquake sequence that started with the 4 September 2010 Canterbury earthquake.A number of cliffs collapsed at Scarborough (Godley Head and Whitewash Head), Richmond Hill, and Sumner (Peacock's Gallop). [CLS] [SEP] The shipping containers that were still placed along Peacock's Gallop on Main Road stopped falling rocks from hitting passing cyclists. [CLS] [SEP] Two surfers were below Whitewash Head and had car-size rocks crash into the sea around them. [CLS] [SEP]
