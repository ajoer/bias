[CLS] Luther Loide Blissett (born 1 February 1958) is a former professional footballer and manager who played for the England national team during the 1980s. [SEP] [CLS] Born in Jamaica, Blissett played as a striker, and is best known for his time at Watford, whom he helped win promotion from the Fourth Division to the First Division. [SEP] [CLS] As of 2010, Blissett holds Watford's all-time records for appearances and goals, having played 503 games and scored 186 goals. [SEP] [CLS] Blissett's other clubs included A.C. [SEP] [CLS] Milan, who paid £1m for him in 1983 before selling him back to Watford for £550,000 in 1984, and A.F.C. [SEP] [CLS] Bournemouth, for whom he had a goals-to-games ratio of nearly one goal in every two appearances. [SEP] [CLS] Blissett was capped 14 times by England, scoring a hat-trick on his debut. [SEP] [CLS] After retiring from playing, Blissett turned to coaching, initially under the management of Graham Taylor at Watford, and managed Chesham United from 2006 until 2007. [SEP] [CLS] Since the mid-1990s, Luther Blissett has frequently been used as a pseudonym, most notably by members of the Luther Blissett Project. [SEP] [CLS]
