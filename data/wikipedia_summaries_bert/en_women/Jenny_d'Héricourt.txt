[CLS] Jenny d'Héricourt (1809–1875), also Jenny P. [SEP] [CLS] d'Héricourt, was a feminist activist, writer, and a physician-midwife. [SEP] [CLS] She was born Jeanne-Marie-Fabienne Poinsard, in Besançon, France, to Protestant parents. [SEP] [CLS] Her father, Jean-Pierre Poinsard, a clock gilder, was born in the town of Héricourt, Haute-Saône, from which Jenny later took her pseudonym. [SEP] [CLS] After running a private girls' school, she married Gabriel Marie. [SEP] [CLS] However, they soon separated (divorce did not exist under French law at that time). [SEP] [CLS] D'Héricourt wrote her first novel, Le Fils du réprouvé ("The son of the reprobate")(1844), under the pseudonym Félix Lamb. [SEP] [CLS] She was an enthusiastic supporter of Étienne Cabet, the French socialist, and took part in the Revolution of 1848. [SEP] [CLS] She studied medicine privately in Paris in the 1850s, and later practiced midwifery in Paris and Chicago. [SEP] [CLS] She lived in the United States from 1863 to 1873 and was active in the feminist movement there also. [SEP] [CLS] D'Hericourt helped develop an unofficial international network of feminists during the first half of the 19th century. [SEP] [CLS] They provided each other with moral support and exchanged ideas. [SEP] [CLS] She also wrote an influential rebuttal to the sexist essays of the anarchist Pierre-Joseph Proudhon and historian Jules Michelet. [SEP] [CLS]
