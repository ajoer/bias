The Holmfirth floods refers to a number of instances when severe flooding had occurred in the Holme Valley, West Yorkshire, England affecting Holmfirth and other settlements in the valley. [CLS] [SEP] The earliest record dates from 1738 and the latest from 1944. [CLS] [SEP] The most severe flood occurred early on the morning of 5 February 1852, when the embankment of the Bilberry reservoir collapsed causing the deaths of 81 people. [CLS] [SEP] It is recorded as the 23rd most serious, worldwide, in terms of loss of life from floods and landslides in human history. [CLS] [SEP]
