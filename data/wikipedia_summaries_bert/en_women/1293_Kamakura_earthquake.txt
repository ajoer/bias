The 1293 Kamakura earthquake in Japan occurred at about 06:00 local time on 27 May 1293. [CLS] [SEP] It had an estimated magnitude of 7.1–7.5 and triggered a tsunami. [CLS] [SEP] The estimated death toll was 23,024. [CLS] [SEP] It occurred during the Kamakura period, and the city of Kamakura was seriously damaged. [CLS] [SEP] In the confusion following the quake, Hōjō Sadatoki, the Shikken of the Kamakura Shogunate, carried out a purge against his subordinate Taira no Yoritsuna. [CLS] [SEP] In what is referred to as the Heizen Gate Incident, Yoritsuna and 90 of his followers were killed. [CLS] [SEP] It has been suggested that the reference to a large tsunami may be incorrect, although a tsunami deposit has been found that is consistent with this age. [CLS] [SEP]
