Hurricane Waldo was a Pacific hurricane that caused significant flooding in Kansas during October 1985. [CLS] [SEP] After developing into a tropical depression on October 7, it steadily intensified, becoming a tropical storm within a day. [CLS] [SEP] Waldo reached hurricane intensity on October 8. [CLS] [SEP] After peaking as a moderate Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale, it re-curved to the east, making landfall at peak intensity near Culiacán. [CLS] [SEP] Afterward, it rapidly dissipated. [CLS] [SEP] In all, Waldo caused moderate damage in Sonora. [CLS] [SEP] The remnants of the storm combined with a cold front over the Great Plains. [CLS] [SEP] Significant flooding and one death was recorded in Kansas. [CLS] [SEP] Many rivers and creeks overflowed its banks. [CLS] [SEP]
