Michael Kenji "Mike" Shinoda (篠田 賢治, Shinoda Kenji) (, born February 11, 1977) is an American musician, singer, songwriter, rapper, record producer, and graphic designer. [CLS] [SEP] He co-founded Linkin Park in 1996 and is one of the band's vocalists, as well as rhythm guitarist, keyboardist, primary songwriter and producer. [CLS] [SEP] Shinoda later created a hip-hop-driven side project, Fort Minor, in 2004. [CLS] [SEP] He has also served as a producer for tracks and albums by Lupe Fiasco, Styles of Beyond and the X-Ecutioners. [CLS] [SEP] Born in Panorama City, California and raised in Agoura Hills, Shinoda formed Xero, which later became Linkin Park, with two of his high school friends: Brad Delson and Rob Bourdon in 1996, later joined by Joe Hahn, Dave Farrell and Mark Wakefield. [CLS] [SEP] Chester Bennington replaced Wakefield as the lead vocalist in 1999. [CLS] [SEP] The band later signed a record deal with Warner Bros Records. [CLS] [SEP] Shinoda is also the co-founder of Machine Shop Records, a California-based record label. [CLS] [SEP] Outside of music, Shinoda is an artist and graphic designer. [CLS] [SEP] He has painted several pieces of artwork, some of which have been featured in the Japanese American National Museum. [CLS] [SEP] On January 25, 2018, Shinoda released the Post Traumatic EP, which contained three songs about his own feelings at the aftermath of Chester Bennington's death on July 20, 2017. [CLS] [SEP] In March 2018, Shinoda announced through social media that he was working on a new solo album under the same name; the record was released on June 15, 2018. [CLS] [SEP]
