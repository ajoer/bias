[CLS] The Southeast Michigan Tornado Outbreak occurred on July 2, 1997, in the built-up area of Detroit, Michigan. [SEP] [CLS] There were 13 tornadoes in total, 3 dragged through neighborhoods and downtown, hitting northern Detroit between I-96 and Eight Mile Road, Hamtramck and Highland Park. [SEP] [CLS] The storms killed 7, caused local flooding, and destroyed houses. [SEP] [CLS] 5 of the fatalities were recorded in Grosse Pointe Farms, Michigan, due to straight-line winds of up to 100 mph that blew a gazebo full of people into Lake St. [SEP] [CLS] Clair. [SEP] [CLS] One tornado formed east of the Detroit River, in Essex County, Ontario, near Windsor, Ontario and caused damage in Windsor and Essex County. [SEP] [CLS] The strongest tornado was listed as an F3. [SEP] [CLS]
