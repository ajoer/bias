Typhoon Cecil, known in the Philippines as Typhoon Rubing, was one of two typhoons to strike Vietnam within a week in 1985. [CLS] [SEP] Cecil originated from an area of convection that tracked west-northwest and passed south of Palau late on October 9. [CLS] [SEP] The disturbance became increasingly defined as it moved through the southern Philippines. [CLS] [SEP] On October 12, a tropical depression developed, and the next day, the depression was upgraded into a tropical storm. [CLS] [SEP] Cecil turned northwest over the open waters of the South China Sea as it steadily strengthened, and was classified as a typhoon on October 14. [CLS] [SEP] After unexpectedly slowing down, Cecil continued to intensify and at noon of October 14, reached its peak intensity of 145 km/h (90 mph). [CLS] [SEP] Land interaction with Vietnam triggered a weakening trend. [CLS] [SEP] After turning west, Cecil moved onshore just north of Huáº¿ at 22:00 UTC on October 15.
