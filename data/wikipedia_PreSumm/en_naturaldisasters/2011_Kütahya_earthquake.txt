[CLS] The 2011 Kütahya earthquake struck near a populous region of western Turkey at 23:15 EEST (20:15 UTC) on 19 May with a moment magnitude of 5.8 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VII (Very strong). [SEP] [CLS] With an epicenter just to the east of Simav, it occurred at an estimated depth of 9.1 kilometers (5.7 mi), resulting in strong shaking in much of Kütahya. [SEP] [CLS] Many locals panicked and power was lost to most of Simav. [SEP] [CLS] A hospital in Simav reported an unknown number of injuries, and some buildings sustained damage. [SEP] [CLS] An elderly woman in İnegöl suffered a heart attack in the immediate aftermath of the tremor, and was later confirmed dead. [SEP] [CLS] In Simav, one person was killed after being struck by a concrete block. [SEP] [CLS] More than 450 weak aftershocks followed; the strongest registered at a magnitude of 4.6. [SEP] [CLS]
