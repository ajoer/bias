[CLS] Kathrin Neimke (18 July 1966 in Magdeburg, Saxony-Anhalt), is a German track and field athlete. [SEP] [CLS] During the 1980s and 1990s she was one of the world's best in the shot put. [SEP] [CLS] Until 1990 she represented East Germany. [SEP] [CLS] She won two Olympic medals, the first a silver medal at the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul, and the second a bronze at the 1992 Summer Olympics in Barcelona. [SEP] [CLS] Neimke represented SC Magdeburg and trained with Klaus Schneider. [SEP] [CLS] She is 1.80 meters tall and during her active career she weighed 95 kilograms. [SEP] [CLS] She has a degree in sales and at the end of her sporting career she had a job as reproduction photographer at a daily newspaper. [SEP] [CLS] After that she went to the Saxony-Anhalt police. [SEP] [CLS]
