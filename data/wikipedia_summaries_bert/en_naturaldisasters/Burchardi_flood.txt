[CLS] The Burchardi flood (also known as the second Grote Mandrenke) was a storm tide that struck the North Sea coast of North Frisia and Dithmarschen (in modern-day Germany) on the night between 11 and 12 October 1634. [SEP] [CLS] Overrunning dikes, it shattered the coastline and caused thousands of deaths (8,000 to 15,000 people drowned) and catastrophic material damage. [SEP] [CLS] Much of the island of Strand washed away, forming the islands Nordstrand, Pellworm and several halligen. [SEP] [CLS]
