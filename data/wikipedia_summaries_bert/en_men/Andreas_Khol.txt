[CLS] Andreas Khol (born 14 July 1941 in Bergen auf Rügen, Germany) is an Austrian politician of the centre-conservative Austrian People's Party, President of the National Council from 2002 to 2006.Andreas Khol was raised in the town of Sterzing in South Tyrol, Italy. [SEP] [CLS] His family subsequently relocated to Innsbruck, Austria, where Khol attended school (Akademisches Gymnasium Innsbruck) and served as a Boy Scout. [SEP] [CLS] Trained as a lawyer, Andreas Khol achieved a habilitation in constitutional law as a student of Felix Ermacora. [SEP] [CLS] One of the most senior politicians of his party, he held the position of the first president of the National Council of Austria between 2002 and 2006. [SEP] [CLS] Along with former chancellor Wolfgang Schüssel, he held considerable power within the Austrian People's Party. [SEP] [CLS] Andreas Khol assumed the post of head of the Senior Citizens Organisation in the Austrian People's Party. [SEP] [CLS] He resigned from the post after he accepted the nomination to stand as candidate for the post of Federal President in 2016. [SEP] [CLS] He immediately challenged the government's open door policy on accepting refugees and managed to bring about a U-turn both in his Party and the Government. [SEP] [CLS] Despite this he came one but last in the presidential race gaining just over 11 percent of the vote trailing behind the Social Democratic candidate. [SEP] [CLS] Internal squabbles in the ÖVP and a lack of engagement by the party machine were cited as reasons as well as a swell in support for the far right candidate Norbert Hofer (FPÖ) who topped the poll. [SEP] [CLS] Visibly shaken on election night, Khol announced he would no longer play an active role in politics. [SEP] [CLS] Khol is proficient in English and French and is editor of an annual political science book called the Austrian Politics Yearbook. [SEP] [CLS] A member of A.V. [SEP] [CLS] Raeto-Bavaria Innsbruck, a Catholic fraternity that belongs to the Cartellverband, Andreas Khol is known to be religious and has often voiced conservative opinions on social policy. [SEP] [CLS] He represents the Catholic-conservative wing of his party. [SEP] [CLS] His youngest son Julian is an internationally known male model and married with Turkish TV host Nazan Eckes. [SEP] [CLS] Andreas Khol has six children, three boys and three girls and 15 grandchildren. [SEP] [CLS]
