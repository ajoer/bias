Antonio Lucio Vivaldi (UK: , US: ; Italian: [anˈtɔːnjo ˈluːtʃo viˈvaldi] (listen); 4 March 1678 – 28 July 1741) was an Italian Baroque musical composer, virtuoso violinist, teacher, and Roman Catholic priest. [CLS] [SEP] Born in Venice, the capital of the Venetian Republic, he is regarded as one of the greatest Baroque composers, and his influence during his lifetime was widespread across Europe. [CLS] [SEP] He composed many instrumental concertos, for the violin and a variety of other musical instruments, as well as sacred choral works and more than forty operas. [CLS] [SEP] His best-known work is a series of violin concertos known as the Four Seasons. [CLS] [SEP] Many of his compositions were written for the all-female music ensemble of the Ospedale della Pietà, a home for abandoned children. [CLS] [SEP] Vivaldi had worked there as a Catholic priest for 1 1/2 years and was employed there from 1703 to 1715 and from 1723 to 1740. [CLS] [SEP] Vivaldi also had some success with expensive stagings of his operas in Venice, Mantua and Vienna. [CLS] [SEP] After meeting the Emperor Charles VI, Vivaldi moved to Vienna, hoping for royal support. [CLS] [SEP] However, the Emperor died soon after Vivaldi's arrival, and Vivaldi himself died in poverty less than a year later. [CLS] [SEP]
