[CLS] Johanna Maria Louise Loisinger (18 April 1865 – 20 July 1951) was an Austrian actress, pianist and operatic soprano singer. [SEP] [CLS] She was born in Preßburg, Austria (today Bratislava), the daughter of John Loisinger and Maria Meier. [SEP] [CLS] After she had completed her singing studies, Loisinger sang in Prague, Troppau (today Opava), Linz and at the court theatre in Darmstadt. [SEP] [CLS] She was a well-known singer of the works of Mozart. [SEP] [CLS] Loisinger married Prince Alexander of Battenberg (1857–1893) on 6 February 1889 in Menton, Alpes-Maritimes, France. [SEP] [CLS] The prince had resigned from the Bulgarian throne in 1886 and had assumed the style of Count von Hartenau, so Loisinger became the Countess von Hartenau. [SEP] [CLS] The couple settled in Graz, Austria, and had two children, Assen Ludwig Alexander (1890–1965) and Marie Therese Vera Zvetana (1893–1935). [SEP] [CLS] After her husband's early death, she moved to Vienna, where she was an active patron of musical organisations. [SEP] [CLS] Among other posts, she was president of the Vienna Symphony. [SEP] [CLS] Loisinger died on 20 July 1951 in Vienna. [SEP] [CLS] She was buried in St. [SEP] [CLS] Leonhard Cemetery in Graz where her daughter Zvetana was previously buried. [SEP] [CLS]
